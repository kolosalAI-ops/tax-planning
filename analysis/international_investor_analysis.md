## 5. Foreign Tax Credit Mechanisms

### 5.1 Comparison Table

| Parameter | US | Indonesia | Canada | Brazil | Mexico | Australia | Singapore |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Method** | Credit | Credit | Credit | Credit (reciprocity required) | Credit | Credit (FITO) | Credit (with pooling) |
| **Limitation** | Per-category basket | Per-country | Per-country (business vs. non-business) | Per-country | Per-country | One basket | Per-country (pooled) |
| **CF of excess** | 10 years forward, 1 year back | None | 10 years forward, 3 years back (business) | None | None | None | None |
| **Indirect credit** | Via deemed-paid (ss960) | Available | Via FAPI deduction (ss91(4)) | Available for CFC | Via CFC rules | Via FITO (Div. 770) | Available (ss50A) |
| **Unilateral relief** | Yes | Yes | Yes | Yes (with reciprocity) | Yes | Yes | Yes |
| **Legal citation** | IRC ss901-909; ss904 | UU 36/2008 Pasal 24 | ITA ss126; ss91(4) | RIR/2018 Art. 292-298 | LISR Art. 5 | ITAA 1997 Div. 770 | ITA ss50A |

### 5.2 Key Observations

- **US** has the most complex system with per-category baskets (general, passive, GILTI, branch, foreign branch) under IRC ss904(d). Excess credits can be carried forward 10 years / back 1 year.
- **Canada** separates business income FTC (10-year carry-forward, 3-year carry-back) from non-business income FTC (no carry-forward).
- **Singapore** allows FTC pooling — credits from multiple source countries can be pooled to maximize relief under ITA ss50A. However, there is no carry-forward of excess credits.
- **Brazil** requires reciprocity — FTC is available only where the foreign country would also grant credit for Brazilian tax, or where a tax treaty exists.
- **Indonesia, Mexico**: No carry-forward of unused foreign tax credits, increasing the risk of double taxation in low-profit years.

### 8.3 Australia - Diverted Profits Tax (DPT)

- Applies where there is insufficient economic substance in Australia
- Threshold: Group global revenue ≥ AUD 1B and Australian revenue ≥ AUD 25M
- **Legal citation**: ITAA 1936 Part IVB (ss177H-177R)

### 9.4 Key Considerations for International B2B Investors

4. **Exit Planning**: SG (0% CGT), US (QSBS 100% exclusion per IRC §1202), and AU (SBE 15-year exemption per ITAA 1997 Div. 152) offer the best exit tax profiles.
