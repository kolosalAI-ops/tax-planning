# Global R&D Tax Incentives — Comprehensive Analysis

> **Coverage**: 45 Countries | **Data Year**: 2024/2025
> **Regions**: Americas (7) · Europe (19) · Asia-Pacific (13) · Middle East & Africa (6)

---

## 1. Executive Summary

R&D tax incentives are among the most powerful tools governments deploy to stimulate innovation. This analysis covers 45 countries across four regions, examining credit rates, deduction enhancements, patent boxes, refundability, and effective benefit per $1M USD of qualifying R&D expenditure.

**Key Findings:**

- **43 of 45 countries** offer some form of R&D tax incentive (exceptions: UAE, Saudi Arabia)
- **14 countries** operate patent/IP box regimes with preferential rates on qualifying IP income
- **13 countries** offer refundable R&D credits, critical for pre-revenue startups
- **Effective benefit ranges** from $0 (UAE/Saudi) to $350,000 per $1M R&D spend (Chile)
- **Trend**: Enhanced deductions (200-400%) are increasingly common in Asia, while Europe favors direct tax credits and patent boxes

- **South Korea**: 10-year carry-forward

## 6. Patent Box / IP Box Deep Dive

Patent box regimes tax qualifying IP income at preferential rates, rewarding companies that commercialize R&D.

### 6.1 Nexus Approach (OECD Modified Nexus)

Since the OECD BEPS Action 5 recommendations, most patent boxes require a "nexus" between R&D expenditure and IP income. The formula:

```
Qualifying Income × (Qualifying Expenditure × 130%) / Overall Expenditure = Benefit Base
```

Countries compliant with modified nexus: UK, Netherlands, Belgium, Ireland, Italy, Spain, Portugal, France, Switzerland, Poland, Hungary, Australia, India.

## 11. Strategic Planning Considerations

### 11.1 Location Selection for R&D Operations

**For Pre-Revenue Startups (Refundability Priority):**
1. Canada (CCPC) — 35% cash refund
2. Australia — 43.5% refundable offset
3. France — 30% immediate refund for SMEs
4. Austria — 14% uncapped refundable premium
5. Germany — 25% refundable (capped at €1M)

**For Profitable Companies (Maximum Benefit):**
1. Chile — 35% credit, no cap
2. Portugal — 32.5% + 50% incremental
3. France — 30% (no cap below €100M)
4. Ireland — 30% + 6.25% KDB
5. South Korea — 25-50% for strategic tech

**For IP-Intensive Companies (Patent Box):**
1. Belgium — 3.75% effective rate
2. Portugal — 3.75% effective rate
3. Poland — 5% IP Box rate
4. Ireland — 6.25% KDB
5. Netherlands — 9% Innovation Box

### 11.2 Common Pitfalls

1. **Amortization traps**: US §174 requires 5-year amortization, reducing immediate benefit
2. **Profitability requirements**: Brazil's Lei do Bem requires taxable profit in the claim year
3. **Pre-approval deadlines**: Missing Norway/Mexico/Chile pre-approval deadlines = lost credits
4. **Transfer pricing**: R&D cost-sharing arrangements must be at arm's length in all jurisdictions
5. **Nexus requirements**: Patent box benefits require substantial R&D activity in-country (OECD modified nexus)
6. **Contractor limitations**: Many countries limit or exclude contracted R&D (often capped at 50-65%)
7. **Sunset provisions**: US QBI deduction (§199A) expires Dec 2025; Singapore EIS expires YA 2028
