Fundamentals

Territorial vs. Worldwide Tax Systems

  • Territorial system = only tax income earned domestically; foreign income generally exempt
    • Singapore is the only territorial system here — foreign income taxed only when remitted, with broad exemptions under ITA §13(8)
  • Worldwide system = tax all income regardless of where earned; foreign taxes may be credited
    • US, Canada, Australia, Brazil, Mexico, Indonesia all use worldwide systems
    • Each country has CFC rules that determine when and how they tax foreign subsidiaries
  • Singapore as parent = maximum flexibility (no CFC rules, no tax on foreign dividends/profits)
  • Brazil as parent = most restrictive (universal CFC — all foreign profits taxed annually, zero deferral)
  • US as parent = complex (GILTI imposes a 10.5% minimum tax on all CFC earnings)
Key takeaway: Before choosing where to set up subsidiaries, first understand what your parent country will do with the profits earned abroad.

Entity Structure

Use a Branch When

Loss Offset Strategy
Expecting initial losses in the new market. Branch losses flow through to offset HQ profits, reducing group tax immediately.
Example: Entering Australia for <2 years with no permanent office — branch losses offset Singapore parent profits at 17%.
Temporary / Project-Based
Short-term projects that may stay below PE thresholds. Avoid incorporation costs and exit complexity.
Example: 6-month consulting engagement in Canada — below 183-day service PE if managed carefully.
0% Branch Profits Tax
Brazil & Australia impose no additional branch profits tax on remittances. Singapore also charges 0% branch tax at 17% CIT.
Treaty Simplification
No WHT on branch profit remittances (vs 5-15% dividend WHT for subsidiaries). Simpler repatriation mechanics.
Decision rule: Choose a branch when losses are expected, presence is temporary (<2 years), or when the host country has 0% branch profits tax. Convert to subsidiary once profitable.
Entity Structure

Use a Subsidiary When

Profitable from Start
Access local incentives: Tax Holiday (ID: 0% for 5-20yr), Pioneer Status (SG: 0-5%), SBD (CA: 9% federal).
Example: Indonesian subsidiary qualifies for Tax Holiday on $50M+ investment in pioneer sectors — 0% CIT for up to 20 years.
Long-Term Presence
Asset protection, local credibility, ability to reinvest profits locally, and liability ring-fencing from parent.
Example: SG subsidiary with Pioneer Status reinvests profits at 5% CIT for 15 years, building local market share.
Treaty WHT Advantage
Treaty dividend WHT (5-15%) often lower than branch profits tax in some jurisdictions. Especially beneficial for US/CA parents.
R&D Credit Access
Only subsidiaries can claim local R&D credits: CA SR&ED 35% ITC, AU 43.5% offset, US IRC §41 credit. Branches cannot.
Decision rule: Choose a subsidiary when profitable from day one, planning long-term presence, or when local incentives (Tax Holiday, R&D credits, Pioneer) outweigh the simplicity of a branch.
