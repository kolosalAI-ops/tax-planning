# Indonesia - R&D Tax Incentives

## 1. Overview

| Feature | Details |
|---|---|
| **Primary Incentive** | Super Deduction up to 300% for R&D activities |
| **Tax Holiday** | Up to 100% CIT reduction for pioneer industries (5-20 years) |
| **Governing Authority** | Directorate General of Taxes (DGT); Ministry of Finance (MoF); BRIN (National Research Agency) |
| **Key Legislation** | Government Regulation (GR) 45/2019; Minister of Finance Regulation (PMK) 153/2020; Income Tax Law No. 36/2008 Art. 6 |
| **Corporate Tax Rate** | 22% (standard) |
| **Special Conditions** | R&D must be conducted in Indonesia |

## 2. Detailed Programs

### 2.1 Super Deduction for R&D - GR 45/2019

**Legal Basis:** Government Regulation No. 45 of 2019 on Tax Incentives for Business Activities in Certain Sectors; PMK 153/2020

The super deduction provides an additional deduction of up to 200% on top of the standard 100% deduction, yielding a total deduction of up to 300%.

| Parameter | Details |
|---|---|
| **Base Deduction** | 100% (standard cost deduction) |
| **Additional Deduction** | Up to 200% (super deduction) |
| **Total Deduction** | Up to 300% |
| **Eligible Sectors** | Priority sectors as defined by government regulation |
| **Location** | R&D activities must be conducted within Indonesia |
| **Approval** | Requires government approval from BRIN and Ministry of Finance |

**Super Deduction Tiers:**

| Tier | Additional Deduction | Criteria |
|---|---|---|
| **Tier 1** | 200% | R&D in priority sector, collaboration with Indonesian universities/research institutions |
| **Tier 2** | 150% | R&D in priority sector, conducted independently |
| **Tier 3** | 100% | General R&D meeting minimum criteria |

**Priority Sectors Include:**
- Food and beverage processing
- Textiles and garments
- Automotive and transportation
- Electronics and ICT
- Chemical and pharmaceutical
- Medical devices
- Energy (renewable)
- Agriculture and fisheries

### 2.2 Tax Holiday for Pioneer Industries - GR 45/2019, PMK 130/2020

**Legal Basis:** Government Regulation No. 45/2019; PMK 130/2020

| Parameter | Details |
|---|---|
| **Benefit** | 100% CIT reduction for 5-20 years (based on investment value) |
| **Investment ≥ Rp 500B** | 5-year tax holiday |
| **Investment ≥ Rp 1T** | 7-year tax holiday |
| **Investment ≥ Rp 5T** | 10-year tax holiday |
| **Investment ≥ Rp 15T** | 15-year tax holiday |
| **Investment ≥ Rp 30T** | 20-year tax holiday |
| **Transition Period** | 50% CIT reduction for 2 years after holiday ends |
| **Pioneer Industries** | 18 designated sectors including technology and digital economy |

### 2.3 Mini Tax Holiday - PMK 150/2018

| Parameter | Details |
|---|---|
| **Investment Threshold** | Rp 100B - Rp 500B |
| **Benefit** | 50% CIT reduction for 5 years |
| **Transition** | 25% CIT reduction for 2 years after |
| **Eligible Sectors** | Same pioneer industry list |

### 2.4 Special Economic Zone (SEZ) Incentives

| Parameter | Details |
|---|---|
| **CIT Reduction** | 10-100% for 5-25 years depending on investment |
| **Zones** | Multiple SEZs across Indonesia (e.g., Batam, Mandalika, Sei Mangkei) |
| **Additional Benefits** | Import duty exemption, VAT facilities |

## 3. Patent Box / IP Regime

Indonesia does not currently have a formal patent box or IP box regime.

| Aspect | Status |
|---|---|
| **Patent Box** | Not available |
| **IP Amortization** | Standard amortization rules apply (Art. 11A Income Tax Law) |
| **IP Transfer** | Subject to transfer pricing rules (PMK 172/2023) |
| **Royalty WHT** | 20% on payments to non-residents (reduced under tax treaties) |
| **Alternative** | Super deduction and tax holiday provide IP development support |

**Note:** While no formal patent box exists, the super deduction effectively provides IP development incentives. Companies may also structure IP holding through Indonesia's tax treaty network.

## 4. Effective Benefit Analysis (per $1M USD R&D Spend)

*Assuming IDR/USD exchange rate of 15,500; Rp 15.5B equivalent spend; 22% CIT rate*

| Scenario | Total Deduction | Tax Saving (IDR) | Tax Saving (USD) |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Tier 1 (300% total)** | Rp 46.5B | Rp 10.23B | ~$660,000 |
| **Tier 2 (250% total)** | Rp 38.75B | Rp 8.525B | ~$550,000 |
| **Tier 3 (200% total)** | Rp 31.0B | Rp 6.82B | ~$440,000 |
| **Standard (100%)** | Rp 15.5B | Rp 3.41B | ~$220,000 |
| **Tax Holiday (100% CIT reduction)** | N/A | Full CIT exemption | Up to $220,000 |

**Key Insight:** The Tier 1 super deduction of 300% provides the highest effective subsidy in the APAC region, but requires collaboration with Indonesian research institutions and operation in priority sectors.

## 5. Claim Process & Documentation

### 5.1 Filing Process

| Step | Action | Timeline |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Identify qualifying R&D and priority sector | Before project commencement |
| 2 | Apply to BRIN for R&D activity certification | Before or during R&D activities |
| 3 | Obtain BRIN certification of qualifying R&D | Processing time: 30-60 days |
| 4 | Apply to DGT/MoF for super deduction approval | With supporting BRIN certification |
| 5 | Claim deduction in annual CIT return (SPT) | By April 30 of following year |
| 6 | Retain documentation for audit | Minimum 10 years |

### 5.2 BRIN Certification Requirements

- **Research proposal:** Detailed project description and objectives
- **Innovation category:** Classification under priority sector taxonomy
- **Collaboration evidence:** MOU/agreements with Indonesian universities or research institutions (for Tier 1)
- **Personnel qualifications:** CVs of research staff
- **Budget breakdown:** Detailed cost allocation

### 5.3 Documentation Requirements

- **BRIN certification letter**
- **R&D activity reports:** Periodic progress reports
- **Financial records:** Separate accounting for R&D expenditure
- **Employment records:** Researcher qualifications and time allocation
- **Collaboration agreements:** For Tier 1 claims
- **Transfer pricing documentation:** For related-party R&D arrangements

## 6. Recent Changes & Sunset Provisions

| Change | Effective Date | Details |
|---|---|---|
| **GR 45/2019** | November 2019 | Introduction of super deduction framework |
| **PMK 153/2020** | October 2020 | Implementing regulation with detailed criteria |
| **BRIN Establishment** | 2021 | National Research and Innovation Agency (BRIN) consolidated research governance |
| **Omnibus Law (UU Cipta Kerja)** | 2020/2023 | Reinforced investment incentives including tax holidays |
| **PMK 172/2023** | December 2023 | Updated transfer pricing rules affecting R&D cost sharing |
| **Golden Visa Integration** | 2024 | Talent attraction provisions for R&D personnel |

**Sunset Provisions:**
- Super deduction has no explicit sunset date; regulated by government regulation
- Tax holidays are subject to periodic government review and sector list updates
- Pioneer industry list updated periodically by BKPM/Investment Ministry
