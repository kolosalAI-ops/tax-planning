# Indonesia - International Investors

> **Jurisdiction**: Direktorat Jenderal Pajak (DJP) / Badan Koordinasi Penanaman Modal (BKPM)
> **Governing Law**: UU No. 25/2007 (Investment), UU No. 36/2008 (Income Tax), UU No. 7/2021 (HPP), UU No. 11/2020 (Cipta Kerja)
> **Tax Year**: 2024/2025
> **Currency**: Indonesian Rupiah (IDR / Rp)

---

## 1. Foreign Ownership Restrictions & Investment Approvals

### BKPM/OSS-RBA Licensing System
> **Legal Basis**: UU No. 25/2007 (Investment Law); PP No. 5/2021 (Risk-Based Approach via OSS-RBA); UU No. 11/2020 (Cipta Kerja / Omnibus Law)

All foreign investment in Indonesia must be channeled through a **Penanaman Modal Asing (PMA)** company, which is a limited liability company (PT) established under Indonesian law with foreign shareholding. The Online Single Submission Risk-Based Approach (**OSS-RBA**) system, managed by BKPM (now part of the Ministry of Investment / BKPM), is the mandatory portal for obtaining business licenses.

| Parameter | Value |
|---|---|
| Licensing authority | **BKPM / Ministry of Investment** (Kementerian Investasi/BKPM) |
| Licensing system | **OSS-RBA** (Online Single Submission - Risk Based Approach) |
| Legal entity requirement | **PT PMA** (Perseroan Terbatas Penanaman Modal Asing) |
| Minimum invested capital | **IDR 10 billion** (approximately USD 625,000) — PP No. 5/2021 Pasal 12 |
| Minimum paid-up capital | **IDR 10 billion** (or more than 25% of authorized capital) |
| Business classification | **KBLI codes** (Klasifikasi Baku Lapangan Usaha Indonesia) required for all activities |
| Risk categories | Low (NIB only), Medium-Low (Standard Certificate), Medium-High (Standard Certificate), High (Permit required) |

The **Cipta Kerja (Omnibus Law)** — UU No. 11/2020 — fundamentally reformed Indonesia's investment framework by eliminating the Negative Investment List (DNI) and replacing it with a more permissive Positive Investment List, streamlining licensing through OSS-RBA, and liberalizing numerous sectors previously closed or restricted to foreign investors.

### Positive Investment List (Replaced DNI)
> **Legal Basis**: Perpres No. 10/2021 (Bidang Usaha Penanaman Modal)

The Positive Investment List categorizes all business sectors into four groups:

| Category | Description | Foreign Ownership |
|---|---|---|
| **Open** | No restrictions | **100%** foreign ownership permitted |
| **Priority** | Fiscal incentives available (tax holiday, tax allowance, etc.) | **100%** foreign ownership; additional incentives |
| **Allocated for MSMEs/Cooperatives** | Reserved for micro, small, and medium enterprises | Foreign investment allowed **only through partnership** with MSMEs |
| **Restricted with conditions** | Specific requirements or caps on foreign ownership | **Varies by sector** (see below) |

#### Key Sector-Specific Foreign Ownership Caps

| Sector | Maximum Foreign Ownership | Legal Basis |
|---|---|---|
| Most manufacturing | **100%** | Perpres No. 10/2021 |
| Telecommunications (basic services) | **67%** | Perpres No. 10/2021 Lampiran III |
| Broadcast media (free-to-air TV/radio) | **20%** | UU No. 32/2002 (Broadcasting Law) |
| Print media / online news | **49%** | Perpres No. 10/2021 Lampiran III |
| Private hospitals | **67%** | Perpres No. 10/2021 Lampiran III |
| Air transport (domestic scheduled) | **49%** | Perpres No. 10/2021 Lampiran III |
| Construction services | **67%** | Perpres No. 10/2021 Lampiran III |
| Banking | **Up to 99%** with OJK approval | POJK No. 56/POJK.03/2016 |
| Insurance | **80%** | UU No. 40/2014 |
| Education (higher education) | **49%** (joint venture with local institution) | Perpres No. 10/2021 Lampiran III |
| Plantation (smallholder crops) | **95%** | Perpres No. 10/2021 Lampiran III |
| Alcohol/liquor industry | **Closed** | Perpres No. 10/2021 Lampiran II |
| Cannabis cultivation | **Closed** | Perpres No. 10/2021 Lampiran II |
| Chemical weapons | **Closed** | Perpres No. 10/2021 Lampiran II |

### Land Rights Restrictions
> **Legal Basis**: UU No. 5/1960 (Undang-Undang Pokok Agraria / UUPA); PP No. 18/2021

Foreigners and PMA companies cannot hold **Hak Milik** (freehold title), which is reserved exclusively for Indonesian citizens. PMA companies may hold the following land rights:

| Land Right | Initial Term | Extension | Renewal | Total Maximum | Legal Basis |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| **HGB** (Hak Guna Bangunan / Right to Build) | **30 years** | **+20 years** | **+20 years** | **70 years** | UU 5/1960 Pasal 35; PP 18/2021 |
| **HGU** (Hak Guna Usaha / Right to Cultivate) | **35 years** | **+25 years** | **+25 years** | **85 years** | UU 5/1960 Pasal 29; PP 18/2021 |
| **Hak Pakai** (Right to Use) | **25 years** | **+20 years** | **+20 years** | **65 years** | UU 5/1960 Pasal 41; PP 18/2021 |

Individual foreigners domiciled in Indonesia may only hold **Hak Pakai** for residential purposes (PP No. 103/2015), not HGB or HGU.

### KBLI Code Requirements
> **Legal Basis**: Peraturan BPS No. 2/2020 (KBLI 2020)

Every business activity in Indonesia must be classified under a specific **KBLI** (Klasifikasi Baku Lapangan Usaha Indonesia) code. The KBLI code determines:

| Aspect | Detail |
|---|---|
| Risk classification | Low, medium-low, medium-high, or high risk |
| Foreign ownership limits | Whether the sector is open, restricted, or closed |
| Licensing requirements | Type of permit or certificate required |
| Sector-specific regulations | Additional ministry approvals (e.g., OJK for financial services, Kemenkes for health) |

PMA companies must ensure their articles of association and OSS registration accurately reflect the correct KBLI codes for all business activities. Operating outside registered KBLI codes can result in license revocation.

## 2. Non-Resident Investor Taxation

### Capital Gains on Shares (Unlisted)
> **Legal Basis**: UU No. 36/2008 Pasal 26 ayat (2); SE-04/PJ.34/2005

Non-resident investors selling shares in unlisted Indonesian companies are subject to a deemed capital gains tax:

| Parameter | Value |
|---|---|
| Tax mechanism | **20% WHT** on deemed gain of **25%** of gross sale proceeds |
| **Effective rate** | **5% of gross sale proceeds** |
| Tax basis | Gross transaction value (not actual gain) |
| Withholding agent | Buyer or other party making payment |
| Treaty override | If a DTA applies, actual gain may be used instead of deemed 25% |
| Notification | Seller must report to DJP within 20 days of transaction |

**Example**: A Singapore investor sells shares in an Indonesian PT PMA for IDR 50 billion. Without treaty relief, the tax is: IDR 50 billion x 25% (deemed gain) x 20% = **IDR 2.5 billion** (5% of gross).

### Capital Gains on Listed Shares
> **Legal Basis**: PP No. 14/1997; KMK No. 282/KMK.04/1997

| Parameter | Value |
|---|---|
| Tax rate | **0.1% of gross sale value** (final tax) |
| Tax mechanism | Withheld by securities broker |
| Additional tax on founders | **0.5%** of market value at IPO (one-time, optional election) |
| Applicability | All sellers (resident and non-resident alike) |

### Dividend Withholding Tax
> **Legal Basis**: UU No. 36/2008 Pasal 26 ayat (1); UU No. 7/2021 (HPP) Pasal 26

| Scenario | WHT Rate | Conditions |
|---|---|---|
| Default (no treaty) | **20%** of gross dividend | UU 36/2008 Pasal 26 ayat (1)(a) |
| Treaty rate | **5% - 15%** | Varies by treaty; requires DGT Form |
| Reinvested dividends | **0%** | UU 7/2021 (HPP); dividends reinvested in Indonesia within 3 tax years |
| Dividends from listed shares | **10% final** (individual resident) / **exempt** (corporate resident) | UU 7/2021 Pasal 4A |

The **0% rate on reinvested dividends** introduced by the HPP Law (UU No. 7/2021) applies when:
- The dividends are reinvested in Indonesia
- Investment is made within **3 tax years** from the year the dividend is received
- Qualifying investments include: financial instruments (government bonds, deposits, equity), investment in business activities in Indonesia, and other forms specified by regulation
- If the reinvestment condition is not met, the standard 20% WHT (or treaty rate) applies retroactively

### Branch Profits Tax
> **Legal Basis**: UU No. 36/2008 Pasal 26 ayat (4)

| Parameter | Value |
|---|---|
| Standard rate | **20%** on after-tax profit of the PE (BUT) |
| Treaty-reduced rates | Typically **10% - 15%** under most Indonesian DTAs |
| Calculation basis | Net after-tax income minus reinvestment in Indonesia |
| Exemption | If after-tax profit is **reinvested in Indonesia** within the same fiscal year, the branch profits tax is **not imposed** (PP No. 94/2010) |

### Deemed Profit on Permanent Establishment (PE)
> **Legal Basis**: UU No. 36/2008 Pasal 5

| Parameter | Value |
|---|---|
| Attribution principle | Income attributable to activities of the PE in Indonesia |
| Head office cost allocation | Allowed, subject to arm's length requirements |
| Force of attraction | PE may be taxed on income from similar activities in Indonesia, even if not conducted through the PE |
| Internal dealings | Deemed transactions between PE and head office must be at arm's length |
| Branch profits tax base | After-tax income of PE, not the remitted amount |

## 3. Repatriation of Profits

### Foreign Exchange System
> **Legal Basis**: UU No. 24/1999 (Lalu Lintas Devisa dan Sistem Nilai Tukar)

Indonesia maintains a **relatively liberal** foreign exchange regime. There are no general foreign exchange controls preventing the transfer of funds abroad, but several reporting obligations and sector-specific rules apply.

| Parameter | Detail |
|---|---|
| Exchange rate regime | **Free-floating** (with BI intervention band) |
| Capital account | **Open** — no general restrictions on capital inflows/outflows |
| Currency conversion | Free conversion of IDR to foreign currency |
| Transfer abroad | Permitted without prior approval (but subject to reporting) |
| Maximum cash transport | USD 100,000 equivalent (must declare to customs if exceeding) |

### Bank Indonesia Reporting Requirements
> **Legal Basis**: PBI No. 16/22/PBI/2014; PADG No. 21/12/PADG/2019

| Obligation | Detail |
|---|---|
| Foreign exchange reporting | **Mandatory** for all residents and non-residents conducting FX transactions |
| Reporting threshold | All FX transactions through domestic banks are automatically reported |
| Direct investment reporting | PMA companies must report foreign capital flows quarterly to BI |
| Loan reporting | All foreign loans (private sector) must be reported to BI — PBI No. 16/21/PBI/2014 |
| Penalties for non-reporting | Administrative sanctions, fines up to **IDR 500 million** |

### Export Proceeds Requirement
> **Legal Basis**: PP No. 1/2019; PP No. 36/2023 (amendment)

| Parameter | Value |
|---|---|
| Applicable sectors | **Natural resource exports** (mining, plantation, forestry, fisheries) |
| Requirement | Export proceeds must be **deposited in Indonesian banking system** |
| Deposit period | Minimum **30% of export proceeds** must remain in Indonesian bank accounts for **3 months** (PP 36/2023) |
| Currency | May be held in foreign currency (USD) in Indonesian banks |
| Incentive | Reduced final tax rates on deposit interest for compliant exporters |

### Dividend Remittance
> **Legal Basis**: UU No. 40/2007 (Company Law) Pasal 71-73; UU No. 36/2008 Pasal 26

| Parameter | Detail |
|---|---|
| Restrictions | **None** after proper tax withholding |
| Prerequisite | Company must have **positive retained earnings** |
| Mandatory reserve | **20% of paid-up capital** must be set aside as legal reserve before dividend distribution |
| WHT compliance | Withholding tax must be deducted at source (20% or treaty rate) |
| Board approval | Board of Directors declaration, approved by shareholders (RUPS) |
| Payment timing | Within **30 days** of RUPS resolution (unless otherwise agreed) |

### Transfer Pricing on Intercompany Payments
> **Legal Basis**: PMK No. 213/PMK.03/2016; PER-22/PJ/2013

| Payment Type | Transfer Pricing Requirement |
|---|---|
| Management fees | Must demonstrate actual services rendered; arm's length pricing required |
| Royalties | Must be for genuine IP; royalty rate must be benchmarked |
| Interest on related-party loans | Subject to DER 4:1 (PMK 169/2015) AND arm's length interest rate |
| Service fees | Must demonstrate benefit to Indonesian entity; no duplication with head office |
| Cost-sharing arrangements | Must be proportional to expected benefit |

## 4. Branch vs. Subsidiary

### Entity Options for Foreign Investors
> **Legal Basis**: UU No. 25/2007 (Investment Law); UU No. 40/2007 (Company Law); UU No. 36/2008 Pasal 2 ayat (5)

| Entity Type | Description | Can Earn Revenue? | Legal Basis |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Representative Office (KPPA)** | Kantor Perwakilan Perusahaan Asing | **No** — liaison, market research, coordination only | Permendag No. 10/2006 |
| **Branch (BUT / PE)** | Bentuk Usaha Tetap / Permanent Establishment | **Yes** — taxable in Indonesia | UU 36/2008 Pasal 2(5) |
| **Subsidiary (PT PMA)** | Perseroan Terbatas Penanaman Modal Asing | **Yes** — separate legal entity | UU 25/2007; UU 40/2007 |

### Comparative Tax Burden

| Parameter | Branch (BUT) | Subsidiary (PT PMA) |
|---|---|---|
| Corporate income tax rate | **22%** | **22%** |
| Additional tax on profits | **20% branch profits tax** (on after-tax profit) | **20% dividend WHT** (on distributed dividends) |
| Effective combined rate (full distribution) | **~37.6%** (22% + 20% of 78%) | **~37.6%** (22% + 20% of 78%) |
| Treaty reduction on secondary tax | Branch profits tax reduced to **10-15%** | Dividend WHT reduced to **5-15%** |
| Effective rate with typical treaty (15% secondary) | **~33.7%** (22% + 15% of 78%) | **~33.7%** (22% + 15% of 78%) |
| Effective rate with favorable treaty (10% secondary) | **~29.8%** (22% + 10% of 78%) | **~29.8%** (22% + 10% of 78%) |
| Tax holiday eligibility | **Not eligible** | **Eligible** (PMK 130/2020) |
| MSME concession eligibility | **Not eligible** | **Eligible** (Pasal 31E) |
| Loss utilization | Losses remain in Indonesian PE | Losses confined to Indonesian entity |
| Reinvestment exemption | Exempt from branch profits tax if reinvested (PP 94/2010) | 0% WHT if dividends reinvested (HPP Law) |

### Detailed Effective Tax Rate Comparison

| Scenario | Branch (BUT) | Subsidiary (PT PMA) |
|---|---|---|
| Pre-tax profit | **IDR 100 billion** | **IDR 100 billion** |
| CIT at 22% | **IDR 22 billion** | **IDR 22 billion** |
| After-tax profit | **IDR 78 billion** | **IDR 78 billion** |
| Branch profits tax / dividend WHT at 20% | **IDR 15.6 billion** | **IDR 15.6 billion** (if fully distributed) |
| **Total tax burden** | **IDR 37.6 billion (37.6%)** | **IDR 37.6 billion (37.6%)** |
| With 10% treaty rate | **IDR 29.8 billion (29.8%)** | **IDR 29.8 billion (29.8%)** |
| With reinvestment exemption | **IDR 22 billion (22%)** | **IDR 22 billion (22%)** |

### PE Definition
> **Legal Basis**: UU No. 36/2008 Pasal 2 ayat (5) — 18 categories of PE

Indonesia recognizes **18 categories** of permanent establishment, including:

| Category | Description |
|---|---|
| Place of management | Location where business decisions are regularly made |
| Branch office | A branch of a foreign entity |
| Factory | Manufacturing facility |
| Workshop | Production or repair facility |
| Warehouse | Storage used in business operations (if connected to delivery/distribution) |
| Mine/quarry/natural resource extraction | Any place for exploitation of natural resources |
| Construction site | Construction, installation, or assembly project (see Section 8) |
| Agent | Dependent agent acting on behalf of foreign entity |
| Service provision | Furnishing services for more than 60 days in 12 months |
| Insurance | Collecting premiums or insuring risks in Indonesia |
| Computer/electronic agent | Server or automated equipment facilitating transactions |
| Representative office earning income | If a KPPA engages in revenue-generating activities |

### Key Considerations for Choice of Structure

| Factor | Branch (BUT) | Subsidiary (PT PMA) |
|---|---|---|
| Minimum capital | No statutory minimum | **IDR 10 billion** invested capital |
| Legal liability | Head office fully liable | Limited to company assets |
| Incentive access | Limited | Full access to tax holidays, tax allowances, KEK incentives |
| Financing flexibility | Limited (no local equity) | Can raise local equity and debt |
| Regulatory perception | Less favorable | Preferred by regulators and business partners |
| Exit strategy | Wind down PE activities | Share sale or liquidation |

## 5. Foreign Tax Credit

### Credit Mechanism
> **Legal Basis**: UU No. 36/2008 Pasal 24 — Foreign Tax Credit

Indonesian resident taxpayers (including PT PMA companies) that earn income from abroad may claim a credit for foreign taxes paid against their Indonesian income tax liability.

| Parameter | Value |
|---|---|
| Method | **Ordinary credit method** (credit, not exemption) |
| Limitation | **Per-country limitation** (not overall/basket limitation) |
| Maximum credit | The **lesser of**: (a) actual foreign tax paid, or (b) Indonesian tax attributable to the foreign income |
| Carry-forward of excess credit | **Not allowed** (0 years) |
| Carry-back of excess credit | **Not allowed** (0 years) |
| Eligible taxes | Income taxes paid to foreign jurisdictions |
| Documentation | Tax payment receipts, tax returns from foreign jurisdiction |

### Calculation Method

The per-country credit limitation is calculated as:

**Maximum credit = (Foreign-source income from Country X / Worldwide taxable income) x Indonesian tax payable**

### Worked Example

| Item | Amount (IDR) |
|---|---|
| Worldwide taxable income | **100,000,000,000** |
| Indonesian-source income | **80,000,000,000** |
| Country A income | **15,000,000,000** |
| Country B income | **5,000,000,000** |
| Indonesian CIT at 22% | **22,000,000,000** |
| Country A tax paid (25%) | **3,750,000,000** |
| Country B tax paid (10%) | **500,000,000** |
| **Credit limit — Country A** | (15B / 100B) x 22B = **3,300,000,000** |
| **Credit limit — Country B** | (5B / 100B) x 22B = **1,100,000,000** |
| **Actual credit — Country A** | **3,300,000,000** (limited; IDR 450M excess is lost) |
| **Actual credit — Country B** | **500,000,000** (actual tax is lower than limit) |
| **Total credit claimed** | **3,800,000,000** |
| **Net Indonesian tax payable** | 22B - 3.8B = **18,200,000,000** |

Note: The IDR 450 million excess credit from Country A **cannot** be carried forward or used against Country B's credit limit. This is a significant disadvantage compared to jurisdictions that allow overall limitation or credit carry-forward.

### Timing Requirements
> **Legal Basis**: PP No. 94/2010 Pasal 1

| Parameter | Value |
|---|---|
| Claim period | Must be claimed in the **same tax year** the foreign income is reported |
| Filing deadline | Together with the annual tax return (SPT Tahunan) |
| Supporting documents | Proof of foreign tax payment, certified tax return, or tax assessment from foreign jurisdiction |
| Late claims | Cannot be claimed in subsequent years |

## 6. CFC Rules (Controlled Foreign Company)

### Deemed Dividend Regime
> **Legal Basis**: UU No. 36/2008 Pasal 18 ayat (2); PMK No. 107/PMK.03/2017

Indonesia's CFC rules deem dividends from controlled foreign entities to prevent deferral of Indonesian tax on passive income accumulated offshore.

| Parameter | Value |
|---|---|
| Regime name | **Deemed Dividend** (Dividen yang Dianggap Diperoleh) |
| Governing law | UU 36/2008 Pasal 18(2); PMK 107/PMK.03/2017 |
| Ownership threshold | **≥ 50%** (direct or indirect) of paid-up capital or voting rights |
| Active income exemption | **Not available** — applies to all CFC profits regardless of character |
| Applicable entities | All non-resident entities controlled by Indonesian taxpayers |

### Classification of Controlled Foreign Entities

| Classification | Definition | Deemed Dividend Timing | Basis |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Listed country entity** | Entity in a country with a DTA with Indonesia AND subject to tax in that country | Deemed received in the **4th month** after the entity's fiscal year ends | PMK 107/2017 Pasal 2(2) |
| **Non-listed country entity** | Entity in a country without a DTA with Indonesia OR not subject to tax | Deemed received based on **actual proportional profit** at fiscal year end | PMK 107/2017 Pasal 2(3) |

### Deemed Dividend Calculation

| Step | Description |
|---|---|
| 1. Determine ownership | Calculate direct + indirect ownership percentage |
| 2. Apply threshold | If ownership ≥ 50%, CFC rules apply |
| 3. Identify profit | Use after-tax profit of the foreign entity per its financial statements |
| 4. Calculate deemed dividend | Ownership % x After-tax profit of CFC |
| 5. Apply timing | Listed country: 4th month after FY end; Non-listed: at FY end |
| 6. Credit for actual dividends | When actual dividends are paid, they are offset against prior deemed dividends |

### Multi-Tier Ownership Rules

| Scenario | Treatment |
|---|---|
| Direct ownership ≥ 50% | CFC rules apply directly |
| Indirect ownership through chain | Calculated by multiplying ownership percentages through each tier |
| Combined direct + indirect ≥ 50% | CFC rules apply |
| Joint control with related parties | Related Indonesian taxpayers' holdings are aggregated |
| Multiple tiers of foreign entities | Deemed dividend applied at each tier where ≥ 50% threshold is met |

### Thresholds and Key Rules Summary

| Rule | Threshold / Value |
|---|---|
| Ownership threshold | **≥ 50%** (direct or indirect) |
| Active income exemption | **None** |
| Deemed dividend timing (DTA country) | **4th month** after CFC fiscal year end |
| Deemed dividend timing (non-DTA country) | **At fiscal year end** of CFC |
| Foreign tax credit on deemed dividend | **Available** (Pasal 24 credit applies) |
| Actual dividend received later | **Offset** against previously deemed dividend (no double taxation) |
| Documentation requirement | Indonesian taxpayer must maintain records of CFC financial statements |
| Penalties for non-compliance | Standard underpayment penalties (2% per month, max 48%) |

## 7. Tax Treaty Network

### Overview
> **Legal Basis**: UU No. 36/2008 Pasal 32A — Authority to enter into DTAs

Indonesia has one of the most extensive treaty networks among ASEAN nations, with over **70 comprehensive Double Tax Agreements (DTAs)** in force.

| Parameter | Value |
|---|---|
| Total comprehensive DTAs | **70+** |
| Treaty model | Primarily based on **UN Model** (with some OECD elements) |
| Limitation on Benefits (LOB) | **Present** in several newer/renegotiated treaties |
| Principal Purpose Test (PPT) | **Not yet adopted** in most treaties |
| MAP (Mutual Agreement Procedure) | Available in all DTAs |

### Key Treaty Withholding Tax Rates

| Treaty Partner | Dividend WHT (%) | Interest WHT (%) | Royalty WHT (%) | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Japan** | **10** | **10** | **10** | Major investor; stable rates |
| **Singapore** | **10** | **10** | **10** | Largest ASEAN treaty partner |
| **Netherlands** | **10** | **10** | **10** | Significant holding structures |
| **United States** | **10** | **10** | **10** | Broad treaty with LOB |
| **Australia** | **15** | **10** | **10-15** | 15% royalty for copyright; 10% for industrial |
| **China** | **10** | **10** | **10** | Growing bilateral investment |
| **South Korea** | **10** | **10** | **15** | Higher royalty rate |
| **United Kingdom** | **10** | **10** | **10** | Renegotiated in recent years |
| **Germany** | **10** | **10** | **10** | Long-standing DTA |
| **India** | **10** | **10** | **10** | Covers technical services |
| **Malaysia** | **10** | **10** | **10** | ASEAN neighbor |
| **Thailand** | **10** | **10** | **10** | ASEAN partner |
| **Hong Kong** | **5** | **10** | **5** | Favorable rates; signed 2010 |
| **Luxembourg** | **10** | **10** | **10** | Holding company structures |
| **Switzerland** | **10** | **10** | **10** | Includes LOB provisions |
| **UAE** | **10** | **5** | **5** | Favorable interest/royalty |
| **Mauritius** | **5** | **10** | **10** | Lower dividend rate |

Note: Rates shown are the maximum treaty rates. Lower rates may apply for qualifying shareholdings (e.g., 5% dividend rate for ≥25% ownership in some treaties). Always verify the specific treaty provisions.

### Treaty Benefit Requirements — DGT Form
> **Legal Basis**: PER-25/PJ/2018 (replaced PER-10/PJ/2017)

To claim reduced treaty rates, non-resident taxpayers must provide a **Certificate of Domicile** through the prescribed DGT Form:

| Form | Applicability | Validity |
|---|---|---|
| **DGT Form 1** | For non-resident taxpayers **other than** individuals claiming treaty benefits | **12 months** from date of certification by foreign tax authority |
| **DGT Form 2** | For individuals and specific cases (e.g., pension funds, governments) | **12 months** from date of certification |
| **DGT Form 1** Part II | Self-certification by the non-resident taxpayer (beneficial ownership declaration) | Submitted together with Part I |

**Requirements for valid DGT Form**:
- Must be certified (signed/stamped) by the **competent authority** of the treaty partner country
- Must be submitted **before the payment date** or at latest by the end of the month following the payment
- Must include a declaration that the recipient is the **beneficial owner** of the income
- Must not be a **conduit/nominee** arrangement

### Beneficial Ownership Requirements
> **Legal Basis**: PER-25/PJ/2018 Pasal 5

The Indonesian tax authority applies a strict beneficial ownership test. A recipient is **NOT** considered the beneficial owner if:

| Indicator | Description |
|---|---|
| Conduit/agent | Acts merely as a conduit, agent, or nominee |
| No control over income | Does not have the right to use or enjoy the income |
| Contractual obligation | Is contractually obligated to pass through more than **50%** of the income to another party |
| No substance | Has no economic substance — does not bear risk or have substantial activities in the treaty country |
| No employees/operations | Has no employees, no office, and conducts no real business activities |

### LOB Provisions in Indonesian Treaties

Several of Indonesia's newer or renegotiated treaties contain **Limitation on Benefits (LOB)** clauses designed to prevent treaty shopping:

| Treaty Partner | LOB Type | Key Features |
|---|---|---|
| **United States** | Comprehensive LOB | Qualified person test, active trade/business test, derivative benefits |
| **Switzerland** | Modified LOB | Anti-abuse provisions; substance requirements |
| **Japan** (renegotiated) | LOB article | Qualified person test; discretionary benefits |
| **India** (renegotiated) | LOB article | Beneficial ownership and substance tests |

## 8. PE Risk for Foreign Investors

### PE Definition under Indonesian Law
> **Legal Basis**: UU No. 36/2008 Pasal 2 ayat (5)

Indonesian domestic law defines **18 categories** of permanent establishment, making it one of the broadest PE definitions globally:

| No. | PE Category | Description |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Place of management | Where business decisions are regularly made |
| 2 | Branch | Branch office of a foreign company |
| 3 | Representative office | If it earns income (otherwise KPPA) |
| 4 | Office | Any office used for business |
| 5 | Factory | Manufacturing or processing facility |
| 6 | Workshop | Repair or assembly facility |
| 7 | Warehouse | Storage facility used in business |
| 8 | Space for promotion/sales | Showroom or sales point |
| 9 | Mine/quarry/natural resource extraction | Natural resource exploitation site |
| 10 | Fishery/animal husbandry/forestry/plantation/farm | Agricultural activities |
| 11 | Construction site | Construction, installation, or assembly project |
| 12 | Service provision | Furnishing services for >60 days in 12 months |
| 13 | Agent (dependent) | Person acting on behalf of foreign entity |
| 14 | Agent (insurance) | Collecting premiums or insuring risks in Indonesia |
| 15 | Computer/electronic agent/automated equipment | Digital presence or server facilitating transactions |
| 16 | Partner in a partnership | Partner in an Indonesian partnership (firma/CV) |
| 17 | Controlling interest in a company | Having a controlling stake used to conduct business |

### Dependent Agent PE
> **Legal Basis**: UU 36/2008 Pasal 2(5) huruf m; DTA Article 5(5) equivalents

| Parameter | Detail |
|---|---|
| Trigger | A person (other than an independent agent) who acts on behalf of a foreign enterprise and **habitually exercises authority** to conclude contracts in Indonesia |
| Contract authority | Includes signing contracts or playing a principal role in contract negotiation |
| Independent agent | Independent agent acting in the ordinary course of business does **not** create PE |
| Commission agent | May create PE if acting exclusively or almost exclusively for one foreign enterprise |

### PE Attribution Rules
> **Legal Basis**: PMK No. 35/PMK.03/2019 — PE Profit Attribution

| Parameter | Value |
|---|---|
| Attribution method | **Authorized OECD Approach (AOA)** adopted by Indonesia |
| Functional analysis | Required to determine functions, assets, and risks of the PE |
| Internal dealings | Recognized as notional transactions between PE and head office |
| Capital allocation | PE must be attributed free capital based on functions and risks |
| Documentation | Transfer pricing documentation required for PE profit attribution |
| Interest deduction | Interest attributable to the PE based on capital allocation |

### Practical PE Risk Mitigation

| Risk Factor | Mitigation Strategy |
|---|---|
| Employee presence in Indonesia | Limit visits to below treaty threshold; track days carefully |
| Contract conclusion | Ensure contracts are signed outside Indonesia |
| Fixed place of business | Avoid long-term office leases; use co-working spaces below threshold periods |
| Construction projects | Structure as subcontract with local entity; monitor duration |
| Digital presence | Monitor server location and automated transaction rules |
| Service provision | Break engagements into sub-60-day periods (under domestic law) or treaty thresholds |
| Agent activities | Use independent agents; avoid granting contract authority |
