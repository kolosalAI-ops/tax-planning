# Indonesia - Business Revenue Tax Codes

> **Jurisdiction**: Direktorat Jenderal Pajak (DJP) - Directorate General of Taxes
> **Governing Law**: UU No. 7/2021 (Harmonisasi Peraturan Perpajakan / HPP Law), UU No. 36/2008 (Income Tax), PP 55/2022
> **Tax Year**: 2024/2025
> **Currency**: Indonesian Rupiah (IDR / Rp)

---

## 1. Corporate Income Tax (PPh Badan)

### Standard Rate
> **Legal Basis**: UU No. 7/2021 (HPP) Pasal 17 ayat (1) huruf b

| Parameter | Value |
|---|---|
| Standard corporate tax rate | **22%** |
| Effective date | January 1, 2022 onwards (was 25% before, 22% from 2020) |

### Reduced Rate for Listed Companies
> **Legal Basis**: UU No. 36/2008 Pasal 17 ayat (2b)

| Parameter | Value |
|---|---|
| Discount | **3 percentage points** reduction |
| Effective rate | **19%** |
| Requirement | At least **40%** of total shares publicly traded on IDX |
| Minimum shareholders | ≥ 300 shareholders |
| Holding period | Each shareholder holds <5% for ≥ 183 days in a tax year |

### MSME/SME Tax Regimes

#### Final Income Tax for MSMEs
> **Legal Basis**: PP No. 55/2022 Pasal 56-63 (replacing PP 23/2018)

| Parameter | Value |
|---|---|
| Rate | **0.5%** of gross turnover (final tax) |
| Eligibility | Gross turnover ≤ **IDR 4.8 billion/year** |
| Duration limit (PT/Corporate) | **4 years** |
| Duration limit (CV, Firma, Koperasi) | **4 years** |
| Duration limit (Individual) | **7 years** |
| Non-taxable threshold (individual only) | First **IDR 500 million** of gross turnover exempt |

#### 50% Tax Reduction for Medium Enterprises
> **Legal Basis**: UU No. 36/2008 Pasal 31E

| Parameter | Value |
|---|---|
| Reduction | **50%** of the standard rate on proportional income |
| Applicable portion | On taxable income proportional to gross revenue up to **IDR 4.8 billion** |
| Effective rate on that portion | **11%** (50% × 22%) |
| Eligibility | Companies with gross turnover between IDR 4.8B - IDR 50B |
| Income above threshold | Taxed at full 22% rate |

**Example**: A company with IDR 30B gross revenue and IDR 3B taxable income:
- Proportional income at reduced rate: (IDR 4.8B / IDR 30B) × IDR 3B = IDR 480M → taxed at 11%
- Remaining: IDR 2.52B → taxed at 22%

## 2. Tax Holiday Program
> **Legal Basis**: PMK 130/2020 jo. PMK 31/2021 - Tax Holiday program

For pioneer industries investing in Indonesia:

| Investment Amount | Tax Holiday Duration | CIT Reduction |
|---|---|---|
| IDR 100 billion - IDR 500 billion | **5 years** | **100%** (full exemption) |
| IDR 500 billion - IDR 1 trillion | **7 years** | **100%** |
| IDR 1 trillion - IDR 5 trillion | **10 years** | **100%** |
| IDR 5 trillion - IDR 15 trillion | **15 years** | **100%** |
| > IDR 15 trillion | **20 years** | **100%** |

**Transition period**: After the holiday, 50% CIT reduction for **2 years**.

### Pioneer Industries Eligible

- Basic metals
- Oil refining and/or petrochemicals
- Organic/inorganic basic chemicals
- Machinery and industrial equipment
- Telecommunications, information, and communications
- Maritime transportation
- Processing of mineral/coal resources
- Economic infrastructure (other than government projects)
- Digital economy
- Pharmaceutical raw materials

## 6. Withholding Taxes

### PPh 21 - Employment Income
> **Legal Basis**: UU No. 36/2008 Pasal 21

Progressive rates on individual taxable income:

| Taxable Income Bracket (Annual) | Rate |
|---|---|
| Up to IDR 60,000,000 | **5%** |
| IDR 60,000,001 - IDR 250,000,000 | **15%** |
| IDR 250,000,001 - IDR 500,000,000 | **25%** |
| IDR 500,000,001 - IDR 5,000,000,000 | **30%** |
| Above IDR 5,000,000,000 | **35%** |

**Employer Social Security Obligations**:
> **Legal Basis**: UU No. 40/2007 Pasal 90 (BPJS employment); PP No. 44/2015, PP No. 46/2015 (BPJS Ketenagakerjaan rates)

Employer contributions to BPJS Ketenagakerjaan and BPJS Kesehatan are mandatory and interact with PPh 21 calculations (see deductions.md for detailed rates).

### PPh 22 - Import/Certain Purchases
> **Legal Basis**: UU No. 36/2008 Pasal 22

| Transaction | Rate |
|---|---|
| General imports (with API) | **2.5%** of import value |
| General imports (without API) | **7.5%** of import value |
| Import of certain goods (luxury) | **10%** of import value |
| Cement, steel, paper, automotive industry | **0.25% - 0.45%** of sales price |
| Government procurement | **1.5%** of payment |

### PPh 23 - Domestic Payments
> **Legal Basis**: UU No. 36/2008 Pasal 23 (dividends, interest, royalties, services)

| Payment Type | Rate |
|---|---|
| Dividends (to resident individuals) | **10%** final (or exempt under HPP) |
| Dividends (to resident companies) | **Exempt** |
| Interest (other than bank deposits) | **15%** |
| Royalties | **15%** |
| Technical/management/consulting services | **2%** |
| Rental of assets (not land/building) | **2%** |
| Construction services | **2% - 6%** |
| Other services (specified list) | **2%** |

### PPh 26 - Payments to Non-Residents
> **Legal Basis**: UU No. 36/2008 Pasal 26 (non-resident WHT, 20% default rate)

| Payment Type | Default Rate | Treaty Rate (varies) |
|---|---|---|
| Dividends | **20%** | 5-15% |
| Interest | **20%** | 0-15% |
| Royalties | **20%** | 5-15% |
| Services/fees | **20%** | Varies |
| Branch profit tax | **20%** | 10-15% |

### PPh 4(2) - Final Taxes
> **Legal Basis**: UU No. 36/2008 Pasal 4 ayat (2)

| Income Type | Rate |
|---|---|
| Bank deposit interest | **20%** |
| Land/building rental | **10%** |
| Construction services (small contractor) | **1.75%** |
| Construction services (medium contractor) | **2.65%** |
| Construction services (large contractor) | **4%** |
| Construction planning/supervision | **3.5% - 6%** |
| Land/building transfer | **2.5%** |
| Lottery/prizes | **25%** |

## 10. Income by Source Type

### Service Income (Domestic & Cross-Border)
> **Legal Basis**: UU 7/2021 Pasal 17, Pasal 23(1)(c), Pasal 26, PMK 172/2023

| Parameter | Treatment |
|---|---|
| Domestic service income | Subject to PPh Badan at **22%** (UU 7/2021 Art. 17) |
| PPh 23 on domestic service payments | **2%** of gross amount (Pasal 23(1)(c)) |
| Cross-border services (inbound to Indonesia) | PPh 26 at **20%** of gross, or reduced treaty rate (Pasal 26) |
| Technical, management, consulting services | Specific WHT treatment: **2%** domestic (PPh 23), **20%** non-resident (PPh 26) |
| Transfer pricing | Arm's length principle; documentation required under **PMK 172/2023** (Master File, Local File, CbCR) |
| Digital services by non-resident | PPh PMSE — non-resident digital providers may be appointed as withholding agents by DGT |

**Practical Notes**:
- Domestic service recipients must withhold PPh 23 at 2% of gross and remit to the state treasury before the 10th of the following month.
- Non-resident service providers without a tax treaty benefit face the full 20% PPh 26 rate. Treaty relief requires a valid Certificate of Domicile (SKD/DGT Form).
- Transfer pricing documentation must be prepared for related-party transactions exceeding the thresholds set under PMK 172/2023 (gross revenue >IDR 50B, related-party transactions >IDR 20B, or transactions with low-tax jurisdictions).

### Royalty & IP Licensing Income (Received)
> **Legal Basis**: Pasal 23(1)(a), Pasal 11A UU PPh, Pasal 26

| Parameter | Treatment |
|---|---|
| Domestic royalties received | Included in taxable income at **22%** CIT rate |
| PPh 23 on domestic royalty payments | **15%** of gross (Pasal 23(1)(a)) |
| Inbound royalties from abroad | Included in **worldwide income** and taxed at 22% CIT |
| IP amortization | Amortized under **Group 1-4** useful life categories per Pasal 11A UU PPh (4-20 years) |
| Patent box regime | **None** — Indonesia does not have a patent box or IP box regime |
| Tax treaty reduced rates on royalties | Typically **10-15%** depending on the applicable DTA |

**Practical Notes**:
- Royalty payments to non-residents are subject to PPh 26 at 20% unless reduced by a tax treaty.
- IP amortization follows the same grouping as tangible asset depreciation: Group 1 (4 years), Group 2 (8 years), Group 3 (16 years), Group 4 (20 years).
- Goodwill and other intangible assets from business acquisitions are amortizable under the same schedule.

### Rental & Lease Income
> **Legal Basis**: PP 34/2017, UU PPh Pasal 23, KMK 1169/KMK.01/1991

| Parameter | Treatment |
|---|---|
| Land/building rental income | PPh 4(2) **final tax at 10%** of gross rental amount (PP 34/2017) |
| Equipment/other asset rental income | PPh 23 at **2%** of gross (Pasal 23) |
| Operating lease | Lessee deducts lease payments as expenses; lessor recognizes rental income |
| Finance lease | Lessor recognizes **interest income**; lessee **depreciates the asset** (KMK 1169/KMK.01/1991) |

**Practical Notes**:
- The 10% final tax on land/building rental is withheld by the tenant and is not creditable against annual CIT — it is a final settlement.
- The distinction between operating and finance leases is governed by KMK 1169/KMK.01/1991. A finance lease requires: (a) lease term covers at least 75% of the asset's useful life, or (b) the lessee has an option to purchase the asset at the end of the term.
- Equipment rental to non-residents: PPh 26 at 20% (or treaty rate) applies.

### Capital Gains (Business Assets, Shares, Property)
> **Legal Basis**: PP 41/1994, PP 34/2016, PP 44/2022, Pasal 18 UU PPh

| Asset Type | Tax Treatment |
|---|---|
| Listed shares (IDX) | **0.1%** final tax on gross transaction value (PP 41/1994) |
| Founder shares | Additional **0.5%** final tax option on initial public offering shares |
| Non-listed shares | Capital gain included in taxable income at **22%** CIT |
| Land/building transfer | **2.5%** final tax of gross transfer value (PP 34/2016, updated by PP 44/2022) |
| Conduit/indirect share transfers | Taxable under **anti-avoidance provisions** (Pasal 18 UU PPh) |
| Merger/consolidation gains | **Book value transfer** possible with prior DGT approval (Pasal 10(3) UU PPh) |

**Practical Notes**:
- The 0.1% final tax on listed shares is withheld by the securities broker at the point of sale.
- For founder shares, the additional 0.5% is calculated on the acquisition cost at IPO and is optional — founders may choose to be taxed under the normal regime instead.
- Indirect transfers (e.g., offshore holding company share sales where the underlying value is Indonesian assets) may be challenged by DGT under Pasal 18 anti-avoidance rules, particularly for transactions involving special purpose vehicles.
- PP 44/2022 reduced the land/building transfer tax from the previous rates and confirmed the 2.5% rate applies to most transfers, with a 0% rate for certain government/social transfers.

### Dividend Income (Received by Company)
> **Legal Basis**: UU 7/2021 (HPP) Pasal 4(3)(f), PMK 18/2021, Pasal 18(2) UU PPh

| Parameter | Treatment |
|---|---|
| Domestic dividends | **EXEMPT** from tax for resident corporate shareholders — no minimum holding requirement (Pasal 4(3)(f) UU HPP 7/2021) |
| Foreign dividends (reinvested) | **EXEMPT** if reinvested in Indonesia within the specified timeframe (Pasal 4(3)(f) and PMK 18/2021) |
| Foreign dividends (not reinvested) | Subject to **22%** CIT as part of worldwide income |
| Dividends from tax haven subsidiaries | Potential **CFC deemed dividend** treatment under Pasal 18(2) UU PPh |

**Practical Notes**:
- The HPP Law (UU 7/2021) introduced a landmark change: domestic inter-corporate dividends are now fully exempt regardless of the ownership percentage, effective 2021.
- Foreign dividend exemption requires reinvestment in Indonesia within a specified period (generally within the tax year of receipt or by March of the following year) per PMK 18/2021. Eligible reinvestment instruments include: direct investment, government securities (SBN), financial sector investment, and other forms specified by MoF.
- The CFC regime under Pasal 18(2) allows DGT to deem dividends distributed from controlled foreign entities in low-tax jurisdictions (effective tax rate <10%) where the Indonesian shareholder holds ≥50% directly or indirectly.

### Interest Income (Received)
> **Legal Basis**: PP 131/2000, PP 9/2021, Pasal 23(1)(a), PMK 169/2015

| Parameter | Treatment |
|---|---|
| Bank deposit interest | PPh 4(2) **final tax at 20%** (PP 131/2000) |
| Domestic bond interest | PPh 4(2) **final tax at 10%** (PP 9/2021) |
| Government bonds (SBN) | PPh 4(2) **final tax at 10%** |
| Inter-company loan interest | PPh 23 at **15%** of gross (Pasal 23(1)(a)) |
| Interest from abroad | Included in **worldwide income** at 22% CIT |
| Thin capitalization rule | Debt-to-equity ratio (DER) **4:1** applies (PMK 169/2015) |

**Practical Notes**:
- Final taxes on deposit and bond interest are withheld at source and are not creditable against annual CIT.
- Deposits below IDR 7.5 million are exempt from the 20% final tax on interest (PP 131/2000).
- The DER 4:1 rule under PMK 169/2015 means interest expense on debt exceeding 4× equity is non-deductible. Exceptions apply to: banks, financing institutions, insurance/reinsurance companies, and mining/infrastructure companies under specific concession agreements.
- Interest paid to non-residents is subject to PPh 26 at 20% (or treaty rate).

## 11. Sector-Specific Revenue Taxes

### Digital Services Taxation
> **Legal Basis**: PER-12/PJ/2020, PMK 60/2022, PMK 68/2022, UU 7/2021 (HPP)

| Parameter | Treatment |
|---|---|
| PPh PMSE | Non-resident digital companies appointed as **VAT collectors** by DGT (PER-12/PJ/2020) |
| VAT on digital products/services (non-resident) | **11%** under the PMSE regime |
| Significant Economic Presence (SEP) | Concept introduced under **UU HPP** for future income tax nexus determination |
| Appointed PMSE entities | Over **160 entities** appointed, including Netflix, Spotify, Google, Amazon, Adobe, Microsoft, etc. |
| Crypto asset tax (PPh) | **0.1%** PPh final on each transaction (PMK 68/2022) |
| Crypto asset tax (VAT) | **0.11%** VAT final on each transaction (PMK 68/2022) |

**Practical Notes**:
- The PMSE regime currently applies only to VAT collection — the income tax (PPh) component for non-resident digital companies is still under regulatory development.
- SEP provisions in UU HPP allow the government to impose income tax on non-resident digital companies with significant economic presence in Indonesia, but implementing regulations have not yet been finalized.
- Crypto transactions through registered exchanges (Bappebti-licensed) are subject to the 0.1% PPh final + 0.11% VAT final rates. Transactions through unregistered exchanges face double rates: 0.2% PPh + 0.22% VAT.
- PMSE-appointed entities must collect and remit VAT quarterly via the DGT online system.

### Natural Resource / Extractive Taxes
> **Legal Basis**: PP 26/2022, PP 53/2017, UU 3/2020 (Minerba), PP 15/2022

| Resource | Tax/Royalty Treatment |
|---|---|
| **Coal** | Government royalties (PNBP) at **3-7%** depending on coal price tier (PP 26/2022) |
| **Gold** | Royalty at **3.75%** |
| **Copper** | Royalty at **4%** |
| **Nickel** | Royalty at **5%** |
| **Tin** | Royalty at **3%** |
| **Oil & Gas (Gross Split)** | Contractor share: **57% oil / 52% gas** (PP 53/2017) |
| **Oil & Gas (Cost Recovery PSC)** | Effective tax rate approximately **40%** under production sharing contract |
| Forestry | PSDH (reforestation fund) + IIUPH (forest utilization fee) based on timber volume/species |
| Fisheries | Levies under **PP 75/2015** based on catch volume and species |
| Mining concessions (IUP holders) | **Dead rent** (per hectare annual fee) + **royalty** on production |

**Practical Notes**:
- Coal royalty tiers under PP 26/2022 are linked to the Harga Batubara Acuan (HBA / coal reference price): lower prices attract lower royalties (3%), while higher prices attract higher rates (up to 7%).
- The Gross Split PSC regime (PP 53/2017) replaced the traditional Cost Recovery model for new oil & gas contracts. Under Gross Split, the contractor bears all costs and receives a base split of 57/43 (oil) or 52/48 (gas), with variable and progressive components adjusting the split.
- Mining companies with IUP (Izin Usaha Pertambangan) or IUPK must pay both dead rent and production royalties to the state (PNBP).
- The Domestic Market Obligation (DMO) requires coal miners to sell a minimum of 25% of production domestically at a capped price.

### Insurance Premium Taxes
> **Legal Basis**: UU PPh, Pasal 26, OJK Regulations

| Parameter | Treatment |
|---|---|
| Insurance premium tax | **None** — Indonesia does not impose a specific insurance premium tax |
| Insurance companies CIT | Subject to standard **22%** CIT |
| Investment income of insurers | Taxed under **normal rules** (interest, dividends, capital gains) |
| Reinsurance premiums to non-residents | PPh 26 at **20%** of premium (reducible by treaty) |
| OJK regulatory levies | Applicable — assessed by Otoritas Jasa Keuangan (Financial Services Authority) |

**Practical Notes**:
- While there is no dedicated insurance premium tax, insurance companies face regulatory levies imposed by OJK, which function as a cost of doing business.
- Reinsurance premiums paid to non-resident reinsurers are treated as services/payments subject to PPh 26 at 20%. Treaty relief may reduce this rate.
- Insurance reserves (technical provisions) are deductible for CIT purposes up to limits prescribed by MoF regulations.
- Life insurance premiums paid by employers for employees are considered taxable benefits under PPh 21 (effective 2025 under PMK on fringe benefits).

### Financial Transaction Taxes
> **Legal Basis**: PP 41/1994, UU 10/2020, PMK 68/2022

| Parameter | Treatment |
|---|---|
| Stock exchange transaction levy | **0.1%** of sales value — final tax on listed share transactions (PP 41/1994) |
| Broad-based FTT | **None** — Indonesia does not impose a general financial transaction tax |
| Stamp duty (Bea Meterai) | **IDR 10,000** on documents with value >IDR 5,000,000 (UU 10/2020) |
| Crypto transaction tax | **0.1%** PPh final per transaction (PMK 68/2022) |
| Large transaction reporting | Bank Indonesia reporting requirements for transactions ≥IDR 100M (cash) or ≥IDR 500M (transfer) |

**Practical Notes**:
- The stamp duty under UU 10/2020 (effective January 1, 2021) unified the previous IDR 3,000/6,000 tiers into a single IDR 10,000 rate. It applies to: agreements, notarial deeds, documents stating amounts >IDR 5M, securities transaction documents, and auction documents.
- Electronic stamp duty (e-Meterai) is available for digital documents through the authorized platform (e-meterai.co.id).
- The 0.1% stock transaction levy is separate from and in addition to any capital gains tax considerations. It applies to the seller only.
- There are no Tobin tax or similar broad-based financial transaction taxes under consideration.

### Gaming / Betting Revenue Taxes
> **Legal Basis**: KUHP (Criminal Code), UU PPh Pasal 4(2)

| Parameter | Treatment |
|---|---|
| Gambling legality | **Largely ILLEGAL** in Indonesia under KUHP (Criminal Code) provisions |
| Formal gaming tax regime | **None** — no dedicated gaming tax framework exists |
| Online gambling | **Strictly prohibited** and actively enforced |
| Lottery prizes (legal limited circumstances) | Subject to PPh 4(2) **final tax at 25%** of prize value |

**Practical Notes**:
- Indonesia's legal framework, rooted in both national law and societal/religious norms, prohibits most forms of gambling. KUHP Articles 303 and 303bis criminalize gambling activities.
- Limited exceptions historically existed for state-run lotteries, with prizes subject to the 25% final tax under PPh 4(2).
- Online gambling and offshore betting platforms are blocked by the Ministry of Communication and Informatics (Kominfo).
- There is no regulatory framework for casino operations, sports betting, or other gaming activities — hence no gaming-specific tax regime.

### Agricultural Income Taxation
> **Legal Basis**: PP 55/2022, BPDPKS Regulations, PMK on Export Levy, Permendag on CPO Export

| Parameter | Treatment |
|---|---|
| Palm oil CPO export levy (BPDPKS) | **$55-200/ton** depending on CPO reference price tiers |
| Palm oil export duty | Additional tiers based on CPO price — layered on top of BPDPKS levy |
| Agricultural income (general) | Standard **22%** CIT rate |
| MSME farmers | **0.5%** final tax if annual gross turnover <IDR 4.8B (PP 55/2022) |
| Subsidized fertilizer | Government program (pupuk bersubsidi) — not a tax incentive, but a direct subsidy |
| Estate crop R&D super deduction | **200-300%** super deduction for R&D activities in specified sectors/regions |

**Practical Notes**:
- The BPDPKS (Badan Pengelola Dana Perkebunan Kelapa Sawit) levy is a non-tax state revenue (PNBP) collected from palm oil exporters. Rates are tiered: e.g., $55/ton when CPO price is below $670/ton, escalating to $200/ton at higher price levels.
- Export duty on CPO and derivatives is separate from the BPDPKS levy and is administered by the Ministry of Trade.
- The 0.5% MSME final tax (PP 55/2022) benefits smallholder farmers and agricultural businesses with turnover below IDR 4.8B, providing significant simplification over normal CIT filing.
- The R&D super deduction of 200-300% applies to qualifying activities in priority sectors, including agriculture, under PMK implementing PP 45/2019. Companies must partner with domestic research institutions to qualify for the higher tiers.
- Plantation companies are subject to corporate social responsibility (CSR) obligations under UU 40/2007, including community development (plasma schemes).
