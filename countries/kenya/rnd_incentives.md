# Kenya - R&D Tax Incentives

## 1. Overview

| Feature | Details |
|---|---|
| **Primary Incentive** | 100% deduction for R&D expenditure |
| **Secondary Incentive** | 100% investment deduction for qualifying R&D buildings |
| **Deduction Rate** | 100% of qualifying R&D revenue expenditure |
| **Governing Law** | Income Tax Act, Cap 470 — Section 15(2)(e) |
| **Administering Body** | Kenya Revenue Authority (KRA) |
| **CIT Rate** | 30% (standard); 25% for newly listed companies (first 5 years) |
| **Patent Box / IP Regime** | None |
| **Currency** | Kenya Shilling (KES) |
| **Fiscal Year** | Calendar year (or approved accounting period) |
| **Key Consideration** | Growing tech ecosystem ("Silicon Savannah") with developing R&D incentive framework |

## 2. Detailed Programs

### 2.1 Section 15(2)(e) — R&D Revenue Deduction

**Legal Basis:** Income Tax Act, Cap 470, Section 15(2)(e)

Section 15(2)(e) provides for the deduction of expenditure incurred on scientific research or experiment related to the taxpayer's business.

| Parameter | Details |
|---|---|
| **Deduction Rate** | 100% of qualifying R&D revenue expenditure |
| **Eligible Activities** | Scientific research or experiment related to the taxpayer's business |
| **Eligible Expenditure** | Revenue (non-capital) expenditure on scientific research |
| **Capital R&D Expenditure** | Deductible under capital deduction provisions (Second Schedule) |
| **Pre-Approval Required** | No — claimed in annual tax return |
| **Carry-Forward of Losses** | Losses (including R&D) carried forward indefinitely (subject to conditions) |
| **Location Requirement** | Research should relate to business carried on in Kenya |

#### Qualifying Expenditure Categories

| Cost Category | Deduction | Legal Basis |
|---|---|---|
| **Employee salaries — R&D personnel** | 100% deductible | Section 15(2)(e) |
| **Materials and consumables** | 100% deductible | Section 15(2)(e) |
| **Subcontractor R&D payments** | 100% deductible | Section 15(2)(e), arm's length |
| **R&D equipment (capital)** | Capital deduction per Second Schedule | Second Schedule, Class 3/4 |
| **Payments to approved research institutions** | 100% deductible | Section 15(2)(e) |
| **Software and technology for R&D** | 100% deductible (revenue) | Section 15(2)(e) |
| **Pilot plant and prototypes (revenue costs)** | 100% deductible | Section 15(2)(e) |

### 2.2 Investment Deduction — R&D Buildings and Equipment

**Legal Basis:** Income Tax Act, Cap 470 — Second Schedule; Section 15(2)(h) — Investment Deduction

| Asset Type | Deduction Rate | Conditions |
|---|---|---|
| **New industrial building (including R&D facility)** | 100% investment deduction (in Nairobi, Mombasa, Kisumu) | Must be new building used for manufacturing or scientific research |
| **New building outside major cities** | 150% investment deduction | Promotes decentralization of R&D activities |
| **Machinery and equipment (R&D)** | 100% investment deduction | New equipment installed in qualifying building |
| **Existing building — wear and tear** | 10% per annum (straight-line) | Standard industrial building allowance |
| **Scientific research equipment** | 12.5% per annum (Class 3) or 100% investment deduction | Depends on classification and location |

**Investment Deduction Conditions:**
- Building or machinery must be new and unused
- Must be used for manufacturing, hotel, or qualifying scientific research purposes
- Must be placed in service during the year of assessment
- 100% deduction available in the year the asset is first used

### 2.3 Special Economic Zones (SEZ) and Export Processing Zones (EPZ)

**Legal Basis:** Special Economic Zones Act, 2015; Export Processing Zones Act, Cap 517

| Incentive | SEZ | EPZ |
|---|---|---|
| **CIT Rate** | 10% for first 10 years; 15% for next 10 years | 25% for first 10 years (0% on export income proposed) |
| **Investment Deduction** | 100% on buildings and machinery | Available |
| **Withholding Tax Exemption** | On dividends for first 10 years | On certain payments |
| **R&D Application** | R&D-intensive enterprises may qualify | Manufacturing with R&D component |
| **Minimum Investment** | USD 50 million (foreign) or USD 10 million (local) for SEZ | Varies |

### 2.4 Konza Technopolis and Innovation Hubs

| Program | Details | R&D Relevance |
|---|---|---|
| **Konza Technopolis** | Flagship smart city project; technology hub with SEZ benefits | Dedicated R&D zones; ICT and biotech focus |
| **iHub and Innovation Hubs** | Ecosystem support for tech startups | Networking, mentorship, limited direct tax benefits |
| **Kenya National Innovation Agency (KeNIA)** | Innovation commercialization support | Grant programs for applied research |
| **National Research Fund (NRF)** | Government research funding | Grants for university and industry R&D collaboration |
| **Kenya Industrial Research and Development Institute (KIRDI)** | Applied industrial R&D | Shared facilities and technical support |

## 3. Patent Box / IP Regime

Kenya does not currently have a patent box or preferential IP income regime.

| Feature | Details |
|---|---|
| **Patent Box** | Not available |
| **IP Royalty Income** | Taxed at standard 30% CIT rate |
| **Withholding Tax on Royalties — Resident** | 5% |
| **Withholding Tax on Royalties — Non-Resident** | 20% (subject to treaty relief) |
| **IP Disposal Gains** | Taxable as business income or capital gain |
| **IP Amortization** | Industrial property depreciation at applicable rates |
| **Transfer Pricing** | Section 18(3) of Income Tax Act; arm's length principle |
| **Tax Treaties** | Limited treaty network (~16 treaties in force) |

## 5. Claim Process & Documentation

### R&D Expense Deduction

| Step | Action | Timeline |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Maintain detailed records of R&D expenditure throughout the year | Ongoing |
| 2 | Classify R&D costs as revenue or capital | At incurrence |
| 3 | Include R&D deductions in annual CIT return | Within 6 months of year-end |
| 4 | File CIT return via KRA iTax portal | By June 30 (calendar year-end taxpayers) |
| 5 | Respond to KRA queries or audits | As required |
| 6 | Retain documentation for minimum 7 years | Ongoing |

### Investment Deduction Application

| Step | Action | Timeline |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Ensure building/equipment qualifies under Second Schedule | Pre-construction/purchase |
| 2 | Obtain necessary construction permits and certificates | Pre-construction |
| 3 | Place asset in service | Year of assessment |
| 4 | Claim investment deduction in CIT return | Annual filing |
| 5 | Provide certificate of completion and cost details | With tax return |

### Required Documentation

- **R&D Expenditure:** Detailed project descriptions linking expenditure to scientific research, employee contracts and payroll, subcontractor agreements, purchase invoices for materials
- **Investment Deduction:** Building plans, construction contracts, cost certificates, commissioning dates, location proof (for 150% outside cities)
- **Financial Records:** General ledger, trial balance, audited financial statements
- **Personnel Records:** Employment contracts, qualifications, time allocation for R&D activities
- **Transfer Pricing:** Documentation for related-party R&D transactions (Section 18(3))

## 6. Recent Changes & Sunset Provisions

| Change | Effective Date | Details |
|---|---|---|
| **Finance Act 2023** | July 2023 | Various tax changes; maintained R&D deduction provisions |
| **Digital Service Tax (Repealed)** | Replaced by CIT (Finance Act 2023) | DST at 1.5% replaced; digital services now subject to standard CIT |
| **Transfer Pricing Rules Enhanced** | 2022–2023 | KRA strengthened TP documentation requirements |
| **SEZ Act Amendments** | 2023 | Modifications to SEZ qualification criteria and incentive periods |
| **Minimum Tax (Section 12D)** | January 2024 | 1% of gross revenue as minimum tax; may reduce effective R&D deduction benefit |
| **Konza Technopolis Progress** | Ongoing | Phase 1 infrastructure development; SEZ status confirmed |
| **East African Community (EAC) Harmonization** | Ongoing | Potential harmonization of R&D incentives across EAC member states |
| **Tax Laws (Amendment) Act 2024** | 2024 | Various amendments; R&D deduction provisions maintained |

**Sunset Provisions:**
- Section 15(2)(e) R&D deduction is a permanent provision with no sunset date
- SEZ incentives have defined periods (10 + 10 years)
- EPZ incentives subject to the Export Processing Zones Act terms
- Investment deductions are permanent features of the Second Schedule

**Minimum Tax Impact:**
- Section 12D imposes a minimum tax of 1% of gross revenue
- Companies with high R&D expenditure and low profitability may still owe minimum tax
- R&D deductions cannot reduce tax below the minimum tax threshold
- Excess tax paid under minimum tax provisions can be offset against future CIT liability

**Future Outlook:**
- Kenya's growing tech ecosystem ("Silicon Savannah") may drive enhanced R&D incentives
- National Research Fund expansion under discussion
- Potential introduction of enhanced deduction (>100%) for R&D in priority sectors
- OECD BEPS implementation may influence future R&D incentive design
