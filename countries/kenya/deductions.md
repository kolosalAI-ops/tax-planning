# Kenya - Taxable Income Deductions

> **Jurisdiction**: National (Kenya Revenue Authority — KRA)
> **Governing Law**: Income Tax Act, Cap 470 (ITA)
> **Tax Year**: 2024/2025
> **Currency**: KES (KSh)

---

## 1. Depreciation & Capital Allowances

Kenya does not allow accounting depreciation as a tax deduction. Instead, the ITA provides **capital allowances** through the Second Schedule (Investment Allowance / Capital Deductions) and specific statutory provisions.

### Wear and Tear Allowance — Second Schedule, ITA Cap 470

| Asset Class | Rate | Method | Description |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Class I** — Heavy machinery | **25%** | Reducing balance | Heavy earth-moving equipment, tractors, combine harvesters, lorries ≥3 tonnes, buses, loaders, bulldozers, forklifts, cranes, tippers, graders, trains |
| **Class II** — Other self-propelling machinery | **12.5%** | Reducing balance | Motor vehicles <3 tonnes, light machinery |
| **Class III** — Other machinery | **12.5%** | Reducing balance | General plant and machinery, factory equipment |
| **Class IV** — Computers and electronic equipment | **12.5%** | Reducing balance | Computers, data handling equipment, photocopiers |
| **Furniture and fittings** | **12.5%** | Reducing balance | Office furniture, shelving, display units |
| **Telecommunication equipment** | **20%** | Reducing balance | Fibre optic cables, satellite dishes, transmission equipment |

> **Legal basis:** Second Schedule, Part II, ITA Cap 470 (as amended by Tax Laws (Amendment) Act, 2024).

### Investment Deduction — Second Schedule, Part I, ITA Cap 470

| Asset Type | Allowance | Conditions |
|---|---|---|
| Industrial buildings (hotel, manufacturing) | **100%** in year 1 | New building used for manufacture or hotel |
| Hospital buildings | **100%** in year 1 | New building used as a hospital |
| Educational buildings | **100%** in year 1 | New educational institution building |
| Petroleum/gas storage facilities | **100%** in year 1 | New facilities |
| Commercial buildings (outside Nairobi, Mombasa, Kisumu) | **100%** in year 1 | New commercial building in specified areas |
| Commercial buildings (Nairobi, Mombasa, Kisumu) | **50%** in year 1, balance at reducing balance (10% or 25%) | New commercial building |
| Machinery (first placed in use) | **50%** in year 1 | Residual depreciated at applicable class rate |
| Farm works | **100%** in year 1 | Dams, irrigation, fencing, farm buildings |

> **Legal basis:** Second Schedule, Part I, ITA Cap 470. The 2024 amendments rationalised capital deductions with a maximum of 100% and decelerated claims.

### Industrial Building Deduction (IBD)

| Parameter | Detail |
|---|---|
| Rate | **2.5%** per annum straight-line for industrial buildings (or as replaced by 100% investment deduction for new buildings) |
| Applicable to | Buildings used in manufacturing, processing, or hotel trade |
| Legal basis | Second Schedule, ITA Cap 470 |

### Pre-Trading Expenditure

| Parameter | Detail |
|---|---|
| Treatment | Pre-trading capital expenditure on buildings and machinery qualifies for investment deduction when the trade commences |
| Revenue expenditure | Pre-trading revenue expenditure may be amortised over 5 years from date trade commences [unverified — confirm with KRA practice] |

## 2. Business Operating Expenses

### General Deductibility (§15, ITA Cap 470)

All expenditure incurred **wholly and exclusively** in the production of income is deductible, provided it is not capital in nature (unless a capital allowance applies).

| Expense Category | Deductibility | Legal Basis |
|---|---|---|
| Salaries and wages | **100%** deductible | §15(1), ITA |
| Employer pension fund contributions | Deductible up to statutory limits | §15(2)(a), ITA |
| National Social Security Fund (NSSF) contributions | **100%** deductible (employer portion) | §15, ITA |
| National Hospital Insurance Fund (NHIF) | Employer contributions deductible | §15, ITA |
| Rent (business premises) | **100%** deductible | §15(1), ITA |
| Repairs and maintenance | **100%** deductible | §15(1), ITA |
| Insurance premiums (business) | **100%** deductible | §15(1), ITA |
| Professional fees | **100%** deductible | §15(1), ITA |
| Bad debts | Deductible when actually written off as irrecoverable | §15(2)(c), ITA |
| Advertising and marketing | **100%** deductible | §15(1), ITA |
| Training and staff development | **100%** deductible | §15(1), ITA |
| Charitable donations (approved institution) | Deductible up to **10%** of taxable income [unverified — confirm current cap] | §15(2)(l), ITA |
| Legal expenses (revenue nature) | **100%** deductible | §15(1), ITA |
| Entertainment | Deductible only if directly connected to business income generation; purely social entertainment is not deductible | §15(1) read with §16, ITA |

## 3. Interest & Financing Costs

### Interest Deduction (§15(2)(b), ITA Cap 470)

| Parameter | Detail |
|---|---|
| General rule | Interest on capital employed in the production of income is deductible |
| Arm's-length | Interest on related-party loans must be at arm's-length rates |

### Thin Capitalisation / Interest Restriction — §16(2)(j), ITA Cap 470

| Parameter | Detail |
|---|---|
| EBITDA restriction | Interest expense deductible up to a maximum of **30%** of earnings before interest, tax, depreciation, and amortisation (EBITDA) |
| Applicability | Applies to all companies (domestic and foreign-owned) |
| Carry-forward | Disallowed interest may be carried forward for up to **3 years** [unverified — confirm carry-forward period with KRA] |
| Legal basis | §16(2)(j), ITA Cap 470 (as amended by Finance Act, 2020) |

> **Note:** The earlier thin-capitalisation rule under §16(2)(j) limited interest deductions where loans from foreign-related parties exceeded 3:1 debt-to-equity. The 2020 amendment replaced this with the 30% EBITDA test, aligned with OECD BEPS Action 4. Confirm the transition provisions and current application with KRA.

## 4. Research & Development

R&D expenditure incurred in the production of income is deductible as a general business expense under §15(1), ITA Cap 470. Kenya does not offer an enhanced or super-deduction for R&D.

Capital expenditure on R&D assets qualifies for wear and tear or investment deduction under the Second Schedule.

See [R&D Incentives](rnd_incentives.md) for details on Kenya's R&D deduction treatment and any sector-specific incentives.

## 5. Loss Carry-Forward & Carry-Back

### Tax Losses — §15(4) & §15(5), ITA Cap 470

| Parameter | Detail |
|---|---|
| Carry-forward | Losses from a trade or business may be carried forward and set off against future income **indefinitely** [unverified — some sources cite 10 years; the 2024 amendments may have changed this from the earlier 4-year limit; confirm with current legislation] |
| Carry-back | **Not permitted** |
| Ring-fencing | Losses from rental income are ring-fenced and may only be offset against future rental income — §15(5), ITA |
| Farming losses | May be ring-fenced in certain circumstances |
| Change of ownership | KRA may disallow losses if the change of ownership was primarily for tax purposes [unverified — confirm anti-avoidance provisions] |

> **Historical note:** Prior to recent amendments, losses were limited to a 4-year carry-forward period. The Tax Laws (Amendment) Act, 2020 extended this, but subsequent amendments may have further modified these rules. Confirm the current carry-forward period with KRA or a local adviser.

## 6. Provisions & Reserves

### General Principles (§15, §16, ITA Cap 470)

| Item | Treatment |
|---|---|
| General provisions | **Not deductible** — only actual expenditure is deductible |
| Contingency reserves | **Not deductible** |
| Bad debt provisions | **Not deductible** as a general provision; specific bad debts written off are deductible under §15(2)(c) |
| Warranty provisions | **Not deductible** until the actual expenditure is incurred |
| Gratuity and end-of-service accruals | Deductible only when paid, or funded to an approved retirement benefits scheme |
| Insurance reserves (regulated insurers) | Subject to Insurance Regulatory Authority requirements and specific provisions |

## 7. Industry-Specific Deductions

### Export Processing Zones (EPZ) — EPZ Act, Cap 517

| Parameter | Detail |
|---|---|
| Corporate tax holiday | **10 years** tax exemption from the date of commencement, followed by **25%** corporate tax rate for 10 years (standard rate: 30%) |
| Withholding tax | Exempt during the tax holiday period |
| Capital allowances | Standard capital allowances apply to qualifying assets |
| Status | EPZ regime largely replaced by SEZ regime for new entrants |

### Special Economic Zones (SEZ) — SEZ Act, 2015

| Parameter | Detail |
|---|---|
| Corporate tax rate | **10%** for the first 10 years; **15%** for the next 10 years |
| Withholding tax on dividends | **0%** for the first 10 years |
| Capital allowances | 100% investment deduction in year 1 for capital expenditure on buildings and machinery within the SEZ |
| Customs | Customs duty exemption on imports for use within the SEZ |
| Legal basis | Special Economic Zones Act, 2015 (as amended) |

### Agriculture — Second Schedule, ITA

| Parameter | Detail |
|---|---|
| Farm works | **100%** deduction for capital expenditure on dams, irrigation, fencing, farm buildings |
| Livestock and crops | Valued at cost or market value; specific provisions |
| Farmland improvements | Investment deduction at 100% in year 1 |

### Extractive Industries (Mining and Petroleum)

| Parameter | Detail |
|---|---|
| Mining | Capital expenditure on mining deductible under Second Schedule — specific rates apply |
| Petroleum | Governed by the Petroleum Act and specific fiscal terms in production-sharing contracts |
| Depletion | No general depletion allowance; capital costs recovered through capital allowances |

### Financial Services

| Parameter | Detail |
|---|---|
| Banks | Specific bad debt provisions allowed per Central Bank of Kenya prudential guidelines |
| Insurance | Actuarial reserves deductible per Insurance Regulatory Authority rules |

## 8. Treaties & Transfer Pricing

### Transfer Pricing — §18(3), ITA Cap 470; Income Tax (Transfer Pricing) Rules, 2006

| Parameter | Detail |
|---|---|
| Standard | Arm's-length principle — §18(3), ITA |
| Methods | CUP, resale price, cost plus, TNMM, profit split — aligned with OECD/UN Guidelines |
| Documentation | Transfer pricing documentation required for related-party transactions exceeding KES 1B [unverified — confirm threshold] |
| Country-by-Country Reporting | Required for Kenyan-parented MNE groups with consolidated revenue ≥ KES 95B (~EUR 750M) |
| Penalties | Penalties for non-compliance including adjustments, interest, and administrative penalties |
| APA | Advance Pricing Agreements available under KRA practice |

### Treaty Network

Kenya has over 15 bilateral double taxation treaties. Standard treaty rates typically reduce withholding taxes on dividends, interest, and royalties.

## 9. Non-Deductible Items

The following are **NOT deductible** under the Kenya ITA:

| Expense | Legal Basis |
|---|---|
| Capital expenditure (unless capital allowance applies) | §16(1), ITA |
| Private or domestic expenses | §16(2)(a), ITA |
| Income tax (Kenyan or foreign) | §16(2)(b), ITA |
| Expenditure not wholly and exclusively for producing income | §16(2)(a), ITA |
| Fines and penalties for breach of any law | §16(2)(k), ITA |
| Contingent or future liabilities (provisions) | §16(2)(c), ITA |
| Entertainment expenses not directly connected to business | §16(2), ITA |
| Interest exceeding 30% EBITDA limit | §16(2)(j), ITA |
| Non-arm's-length portion of related-party payments | §18(3), ITA |
| Bribes and corrupt payments | §16(2), ITA |
| Club membership fees and subscriptions (personal) | §16(2)(a), ITA |
| Depreciation (accounting) — capital allowances must be claimed instead | §16(1), ITA |
