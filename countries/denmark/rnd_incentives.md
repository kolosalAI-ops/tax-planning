# Denmark - R&D Tax Incentives

## 1. Overview

| Feature | Details |
|---|---|
| **Headline Incentive** | Skattekreditordningen (Tax Credit Scheme) — 22% refundable R&D tax credit |
| **Type** | Refundable tax credit for loss-making R&D companies |
| **Rate** | 22% of qualifying R&D expenditure (matching the corporate tax rate) |
| **Refundable** | Yes — fully refundable; designed for pre-profit / loss-making R&D companies |
| **Patent Box** | No — Denmark does not operate a patent box or IP box regime |
| **Legal Basis** | Ligningsloven (LL) §8X (Skattekreditordningen); Ligningsloven §8B (R&D deductibility) |

---

## 2. Detailed Programs

### 2.1 Skattekreditordningen (Tax Credit Scheme)

**Legal Basis:** Ligningsloven (LL) §8X, introduced by Lov nr. 1379 af 28 December 2011. Effective from income year 2012.

| Parameter | Value |
|---|---|
| Credit Rate | 22% of qualifying R&D expenditure (equal to the standard corporate tax rate) |
| Maximum Qualifying Expenditure | DKK 25,000,000 per income year |
| Maximum Credit Amount | DKK 5,500,000 per income year (22% × DKK 25M) |
| Refundable | Yes — fully refundable; paid out in cash even if company has no tax liability |
| Target | Loss-making / pre-profit companies conducting R&D |
| Mechanism | Credit corresponds to the tax value of R&D deductions that cannot be utilized due to losses |
| Eligible Entities | All corporate taxpayers (selskaber) liable to Danish corporate tax (selskabsskat) |
| Payment Timing | Paid out in November of the year following the income year (e.g., for 2025 income year, paid November 2026) |

**Core Concept:**
The Skattekreditordningen effectively converts the tax value of unused R&D deductions into a cash refund. Since R&D costs are fully deductible under LL §8B, a loss-making company would otherwise carry forward these deductions until profitable. The tax credit scheme accelerates the benefit to cash.

### 2.2 Qualifying R&D Expenditure

**Legal Basis:** LL §8B (R&D deductibility) and §8X (tax credit).

| Category | Eligible |
|---|---|
| R&D personnel costs | Salaries, wages, and employer social contributions for employees engaged in R&D |
| Materials and consumables | Raw materials, prototyping materials, laboratory supplies used in R&D |
| R&D equipment (current expense) | Immediately deductible R&D equipment costs under LL §8B |
| Depreciation on R&D assets | Depreciation of capital assets used for R&D |
| Subcontracted R&D | Payments to third parties for R&D services (subject to conditions) |
| Patent costs | Costs of obtaining and maintaining patents related to R&D |
| Overhead | Overhead costs directly attributable to R&D activities |

**Qualifying R&D Activities (per LL §8B):**

| Activity | Eligible |
|---|---|
| Basic research (grundforskning) | Yes |
| Applied research (anvendt forskning) | Yes |
| Experimental development (eksperimentel udvikling) | Yes |
| Systematic, creative work | Must aim to increase knowledge or develop new applications |
| Novelty | Must involve development of new or significantly improved products, processes, or services |

**Exclusions:**

| Activity | Status |
|---|---|
| Routine product development | Not eligible |
| Market research | Not eligible |
| Quality control and testing | Not eligible |
| Production setup | Not eligible |
| Administrative activities | Not eligible |
| Prospecting and exploration | Not eligible |

### 2.3 R&D Full Deductibility (LL §8B)

**Legal Basis:** Ligningsloven §8B.

| Parameter | Value |
|---|---|
| Deductibility Rate | 100% of qualifying R&D expenditure is fully deductible in the year incurred |
| Historical Enhanced Deduction | 108% deduction applied for income years 2018–2019 (temporarily increased); 130% for 2020–2022 (as part of COVID recovery measures) |
| Current Rate | 100% (standard; check latest Finance Act for any enhancements) |
| Carry-Forward | Unused deductions (tax losses) carried forward indefinitely |
| Capital R&D Assets | Can be expensed immediately under LL §8B or depreciated under standard rules |

### 2.4 Cap and Group Rules

| Parameter | Value |
|---|---|
| DKK 25M Cap | Applies per legal entity; each company in a group can claim up to DKK 25M |
| Joint Taxation (Sambeskatning) | For jointly taxed groups, the DKK 25M cap applies per group (not per entity) |
| Consolidated Cap | In a jointly taxed group, the combined qualifying expenditure of all group companies is subject to a single DKK 25M cap |
| Allocation within Group | Group must designate which entities' R&D expenditure is included within the cap |
| Transfer of Credit | Credit is offset against the group's overall tax position; excess refunded to the administrative company |

### 2.5 Interaction with Loss Carry-Forward

| Scenario | Treatment |
|---|---|
| Company claims Skattekreditordningen | The corresponding R&D deductions reduce the available tax loss carry-forward |
| Effectively | The company "converts" loss carry-forward into immediate cash refund |
| If credit not claimed | R&D costs remain as tax losses, carried forward to offset future profits |
| Strategic Choice | Companies expecting near-term profitability may prefer to retain losses; pre-profit companies benefit from immediate cash |

## 3. Patent Box / IP Regime

**Denmark does not have a patent box or intellectual property box regime.**

| Aspect | Status |
|---|---|
| Patent Box | Not available |
| IP Box | Not available |
| License Box | Not available |
| Historical Discussions | Periodically discussed; Denmark has not adopted |
| Policy Preference | Denmark favors broad-based low corporate tax rate (22%) + direct R&D support |
| R&D Cost Deductibility | Full deductibility under LL §8B (100%); historically enhanced to 130% |
| IP Taxation | Royalties and IP income taxed at standard 22% corporate rate |
| Withholding Tax on Royalties | 22% WHT on royalties paid to non-residents (reduced by treaties) |
| Transfer Pricing | Arm's length principle; Danish TP rules follow OECD guidelines |

## 5. Claim Process & Documentation

### 5.1 Filing Process

| Step | Action | Deadline |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Conduct qualifying R&D activities during income year | During income year |
| 2 | Calculate qualifying R&D expenditure | After year end |
| 3 | File corporate tax return (selvangivelse) to Skattestyrelsen | 6 months after income year end (standard deadline) |
| 4 | Elect Skattekreditordningen on tax return | With tax return filing |
| 5 | Specify qualifying R&D expenditure amount (up to DKK 25M) | On tax return |
| 6 | Tax credit paid out | November of the year following the income year |

### 5.2 Documentation Requirements

| Document | Purpose |
|---|---|
| R&D project descriptions | Demonstrate qualifying R&D nature per LL §8B criteria |
| Personnel records | Identify R&D employees, time allocation, salary costs |
| Cost accounting | Detailed breakdown of R&D expenditure by category |
| Project plans and reports | Evidence of systematic, creative R&D work |
| Subcontractor agreements | Scope of contract R&D, costs, deliverables |
| Financial statements | Support expenditure claims; audited accounts |
| Time tracking records | Allocation of employee time to R&D vs. non-R&D activities |
| Joint taxation documentation | If part of a group: allocation of DKK 25M cap among entities |
| Board decisions / strategy documents | Evidence of R&D commitment and program design |

### 5.3 Audit & Compliance

| Aspect | Details |
|---|---|
| Skattestyrelsen Review | Danish Tax Agency may review Skattekreditordningen claims |
| Technical Assessment | Skattestyrelsen may seek external expert opinion on whether activities qualify as R&D |
| Burden of Proof | Taxpayer must substantiate qualifying R&D nature and expenditure amounts |
| Penalties | Standard tax penalty provisions apply (Skatteforvaltningsloven) |
| Statute of Limitations | 3 years (ordinary); extended periods for deliberate errors |
| Common Issues | Incorrectly classifying routine development as R&D; inadequate time records; exceeding group cap |
| Retention Period | 5 years (minimum Danish tax document retention) |

## 6. Recent Changes & Sunset Provisions

| Change | Effective Date | Details |
|---|---|---|
| Skattekreditordningen introduced | Income year 2012 | New refundable credit for loss-making R&D companies |
| Cap increased to DKK 25M | 2018 | Up from initial DKK 5M (2012), DKK 25M provides meaningful benefit |
| Enhanced deduction 108% (LL §8B) | 2018–2019 | Temporary 8% additional deduction |
| Enhanced deduction 130% (LL §8B) | 2020–2022 | COVID recovery; significantly boosted R&D deductibility |
| Return to 100% deduction | 2023+ | Enhanced deduction expired; back to standard 100% |
| Corporate tax rate stable at 22% | Since 2016 | No changes; directly determines credit rate |
| DKK 25M cap stable | Since 2018 | No further increases announced |

**Sunset Provisions:**
- The **Skattekreditordningen (LL §8X)** is permanent legislation; no sunset clause
- The DKK 25M cap is established by law; changes require legislative amendment
- **LL §8B** (full R&D deductibility) is permanent; enhanced deduction rates have been temporary measures
- No announced plans to eliminate the tax credit scheme
- Potential for future temporary enhanced deduction measures (as seen during COVID period)
- Cap increase discussions may arise in future fiscal policy debates

**Policy Context:**
- Denmark's R&D tax support is primarily targeted at loss-making/pre-profit companies through the Skattekreditordningen
- Profitable companies benefit from full deductibility at 22% but receive no additional tax credit
- Denmark emphasizes direct government R&D funding (Innovation Fund Denmark, Danish National Research Foundation) alongside the tax credit
- The absence of a patent box reflects Denmark's preference for broad-based, low corporate taxation
