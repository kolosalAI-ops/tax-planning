# Thailand - Taxable Income Deductions

> **Jurisdiction**: National (Revenue Department — RD)
> **Governing Law**: Revenue Code (RC)
> **Tax Year**: 2024/2025
> **Currency**: THB (฿)

---

## 1. Depreciation & Capital Allowances

### 1.1 General Depreciation Rules

> **Legal Basis**: RC §65 bis (2); RD Directive on Depreciation

Depreciation is computed on the cost of assets using rates prescribed by the Revenue Code. The **straight-line method** is the most common and default method, but any generally accepted method (declining balance, sum-of-years-digits, double declining) is permitted.

#### Maximum Depreciation Rates

| Asset Category | Maximum Annual Rate | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| **Buildings (durable)** | **5%** | 20-year write-off |
| **Buildings (temporary)** | **100%** | Immediate deduction if temporary structure |
| **Machinery & equipment** | **20%** | 5-year write-off |
| **Motor vehicles (passenger, max 5 seats)** | **20%** | Cost capped at **THB 1 million** (excess non-deductible) |
| **Motor vehicles (≥ 10 seats / commercial)** | **20%** | No cap on cost |
| **Furniture and fixtures** | **20%** | |
| **Computers and peripherals** | **33.3%** | 3-year write-off |
| **Computer software** | **33.3%** | 3-year write-off |
| **Tools and equipment** | **20%** | |
| **Goodwill** | **10%** | 10-year write-off |
| **Patents, copyrights** | **10%** or legal life | Whichever is shorter |

### 1.2 Initial Depreciation Allowance

> **Legal Basis**: Royal Decree on Initial Depreciation

| Parameter | Value |
|---|---|
| Factory buildings | **25%** initial depreciation in year of acquisition; remainder at max **5%** per year |
| Machinery & equipment (SMEs) | **40%** initial depreciation; remainder at max **20%** per year |
| Computer hardware/software (SMEs) | **40%** initial depreciation; remainder over 3 years |
| Eligibility for enhanced rates | SMEs with fixed assets (excl. land) ≤ THB 200 million and ≤ 200 employees |

### 1.3 Accelerated Depreciation (BOI-Promoted)

Companies granted promotion by the Board of Investment (BOI) may claim additional depreciation benefits, including potential immediate write-off of certain assets, subject to the terms of the BOI promotion certificate.

## 2. Business Operating Expenses

### 2.1 General Deductibility

> **Legal Basis**: RC §65 ter (general deduction rules)

Expenses incurred for the purposes of the business are deductible provided they are not prohibited under §65 ter.

| Expense Category | Deductibility | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Salaries, wages, bonuses | **100%** | Must be for actual services rendered |
| Social security fund (employer 5%) | **100%** | Statutory contribution |
| Provident fund (employer contribution) | **100%** | Within 15% of wages cap |
| Rent and lease payments | **100%** | Business premises |
| Repairs and maintenance | **100%** | Revenue nature |
| Insurance premiums | **100%** | Business-related |
| Travel expenses | **100%** | Business purpose; with documentation |
| Advertising and marketing | **100%** | |
| Bad debts written off | **100%** | Must meet Ministerial Regulation No. 186 conditions |
| Professional fees (legal, accounting) | **100%** | Revenue nature |
| Training expenses | **100%** | Job-related; double deduction may apply |

### 2.2 Entertainment Expenses

> **Legal Basis**: RC §65 ter (4)

| Parameter | Value |
|---|---|
| Deductible limit | **0.3%** of gross revenue or paid-up capital (whichever is higher) |
| Maximum cap | **THB 10 million** per accounting period |
| Documentation | Must have receipts and evidence of business purpose |
| Excess | **Non-deductible** |

### 2.3 Charitable Donations

> **Legal Basis**: RC §65 ter (3); various Royal Decrees

| Donation Type | Limit |
|---|---|
| Donations for education or sport | **2%** of net profit (some qualify for double deduction) |
| Donations to charitable institutions / public benefit | **2%** of net profit |
| Total cap | Combined charitable and educational donations: **2%** of net profit each |
| E-Donation requirement | From 1 January 2026, donations must be made through the e-Donation system to qualify [unverified — confirm implementation date] |

### 2.4 Double Deductions

> **Legal Basis**: Various Royal Decrees

| Item | Deduction |
|---|---|
| Employee training expenses (approved programs) | **200%** |
| Hiring of elderly workers (60+) | **200%** of wages (up to THB 15,000/month per person) |
| Hiring of disabled persons | **200%** of wages |
| R&D expenditure (approved by NSTDA) | **200%** (see Section 4) |
| Domestic travel expenses (certain periods) | **200%** (temporary stimulus measures) |

## 3. Interest & Financing Costs

### 3.1 General Deductibility

> **Legal Basis**: RC §65 ter (general); RC §65 bis (4)

| Parameter | Value |
|---|---|
| Business borrowings | Interest deductible if incurred for business purposes |
| Excessive interest | Revenue Department may adjust under §65 bis (4) if rate exceeds market rate |

### 3.2 Thin Capitalisation

> **Legal Basis**: No formal thin capitalisation statute

| Parameter | Value |
|---|---|
| Formal DER rule | **None** — Thailand has no statutory thin-cap ratio |
| Transfer pricing | Related-party interest must be at arm's length — Ministerial Regulation No. 161 |
| RD enforcement | RD may apply arm's length adjustments to excessive related-party interest |

### 3.3 Related-Party Transactions

> **Legal Basis**: RC §65 bis (4); Ministerial Regulation No. 161

The Revenue Department may adjust revenue, expenses, and interest to reflect arm's length pricing for related-party transactions. No formal advance pricing arrangement (APA) legislation exists, though the RD has stated it will consider APAs administratively.

## 4. Research & Development

Thailand provides a double deduction (200%) for qualifying R&D expenditure approved by the National Science and Technology Development Agency (NSTDA) under various Royal Decrees. Additionally, BOI-promoted R&D companies may receive corporate tax exemptions for 5–13 years. Training expenses for employee skill development also qualify for double deduction through the Department of Skill Development.

See [R&D Incentives](rnd_incentives.md) for full program detail.

## 5. Loss Carry-Forward & Carry-Back

> **Legal Basis**: RC §65 ter (12)

| Parameter | Value |
|---|---|
| Carry-forward period | **5 years** |
| Carry-back | **Not available** |
| Limitation | 100% of net profit (no percentage cap) |
| BOI-promoted companies | Losses incurred during BOI tax holiday period may be carried forward for an additional **5 years** beyond the normal 5-year period |

## 6. Provisions & Reserves

| Provision Type | Deductibility | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Specific bad debts written off | **Deductible** | Must meet Ministerial Regulation No. 186 conditions |
| General bad debt provisions | **Not deductible** | Until specifically written off |
| Doubtful debt reserves (financial institutions) | **Deductible** | Per Bank of Thailand regulations |
| Employee severance provisions | **Not deductible** until paid | Cash-basis for tax |
| Warranty provisions | **Not deductible** | Until liability crystallises |
| Insurance reserves | **Deductible** | For licensed insurers under OIC regulations |
| Stock obsolescence provisions | **Not deductible** | Until disposed of or destroyed |

## 7. Industry-Specific Deductions

### 7.1 BOI-Promoted Activities

> **Legal Basis**: Investment Promotion Act B.E. 2520 (1977), as amended; BOI announcements

| Parameter | Value |
|---|---|
| CIT exemption | **3–13 years** (depending on activity category and zone) |
| Additional benefits | Exemption from import duties on machinery; double deduction for utilities and transport; deduction of infrastructure installation costs |
| Zones | Enhanced benefits for activities in designated Special Economic Development Zones |

### 7.2 International Business Centre (IBC)

> **Legal Basis**: Royal Decree No. 674

| Parameter | Value |
|---|---|
| Reduced CIT rate | **8%** or **5%** or **3%** on qualifying income (based on spending/hiring thresholds) |
| Services | Management, technical support, treasury, international trading, procurement |

### 7.3 Eastern Economic Corridor (EEC)

Enhanced incentives for qualifying investments in the EEC, including extended tax holidays (up to 15 years CIT exemption for targeted industries) and additional deductions.

## 8. Treaties & Transfer Pricing

### 8.1 Transfer Pricing

> **Legal Basis**: RC §65 bis (4); Ministerial Regulation No. 161; Transfer Pricing Regulations (effective from 2019)

| Parameter | Value |
|---|---|
| Arm's length standard | Mandatory for related-party transactions — RC §71 bis |
| Methods | CUP, resale price, cost plus, TNMM, profit split, other reasonable methods |
| Documentation | Transfer pricing disclosure form (Form TPA) required with annual CIT return |
| Local File | Required for companies with related-party transactions ≥ THB 200 million per year |
| CbC reporting | Required for groups with consolidated revenue ≥ THB 28 billion |
| Penalties | 100% surcharge on TP adjustments; 1.5% interest per month |
| APA | No formal APA legislation; RD may consider on case-by-case basis |

### 8.2 Treaty Network

Thailand has over 60 double tax agreements. Thailand adopted several MLI provisions effective from 2021.
