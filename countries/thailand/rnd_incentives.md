# Thailand - R&D Tax Incentives

## 1. Overview

| Feature | Details |
|---|---|
| **Primary Incentive** | 200% Additional Deduction (300% total) for R&D expenditure |
| **BOI Incentives** | Up to 8-year CIT holiday for technology projects |
| **EEC Benefits** | Enhanced incentives in Eastern Economic Corridor zones |
| **Governing Authority** | Revenue Department (RD); National Science and Technology Development Agency (NSTDA); National Innovation Agency (NIA); Board of Investment (BOI) |
| **Key Legislation** | Revenue Code §65 ter; Royal Decree No. 598 (2016); BOI Act B.E. 2520 |
| **Corporate Tax Rate** | 20% (standard) |

## 2. Detailed Programs

### 2.1 Triple Deduction for R&D - Royal Decree No. 598

**Legal Basis:** Revenue Code §65 ter; Royal Decree No. 598 (B.E. 2559/2016); Ministerial Regulation

| Parameter | Details |
|---|---|
| **Base Deduction** | 100% (standard cost deduction) |
| **Additional Deduction** | 200% of qualifying R&D expenditure |
| **Total Deduction** | 300% of qualifying R&D expenditure |
| **Approval Required** | NSTDA or NIA project certification |
| **Location** | R&D activities must be conducted in Thailand |
| **Cap** | No statutory cap on qualifying expenditure |

**Qualifying R&D Activities:**
- Basic research
- Applied research
- Experimental development
- Must aim to create new knowledge, products, processes, or services
- Must involve technological uncertainty

**Qualifying Expenditure:**

| Category | Details |
|---|---|
| **Personnel costs** | Salaries of researchers and R&D support staff |
| **Materials and supplies** | Consumables used in R&D activities |
| **Equipment usage** | Depreciation of R&D equipment |
| **Outsourced R&D** | Payments to approved Thai research institutions |
| **IP costs** | Patent application and registration fees |
| **Testing and certification** | Product testing related to R&D outcomes |

### 2.2 BOI Investment Promotion - BOI Act B.E. 2520

**Legal Basis:** Investment Promotion Act B.E. 2520 (1977), as amended

| Category | CIT Exemption Period | Additional Benefits |
|---|---|---|
| **A1 (High Technology)** | 8 years, no cap | Import duty exemption on machinery |
| **A2 (Priority Activities)** | 8 years, capped | Import duty exemption on machinery |
| **A3 (Supportive Activities)** | 5 years, capped | Import duty exemption on machinery |
| **A4 (Basic Activities)** | 3 years, capped | Import duty reduction |
| **B1/B2** | No CIT exemption | Import duty and other non-tax benefits |

**Technology-Related A1 Activities Include:**
- Biotechnology (genetic engineering, molecular biology)
- Nanotechnology
- Advanced materials
- Digital technology and software development
- Automation and robotics
- Aerospace
- Medical devices and advanced pharmaceuticals
- Electric vehicles and components
- Semiconductor manufacturing

**Post-Holiday Benefits:**
- 50% CIT reduction for an additional 5 years (for certain categories)
- Double deduction for transportation, electricity, and water supply costs
- 25% deduction for facility installation/construction costs

### 2.3 Eastern Economic Corridor (EEC) Enhanced Incentives

**Legal Basis:** Eastern Special Development Zone Act B.E. 2561 (2018)

| Parameter | Details |
|---|---|
| **Location** | Chachoengsao, Chonburi, Rayong provinces |
| **CIT Exemption** | Up to 13 years for targeted industries |
| **Additional CIT Reduction** | 50% for 5 years after exemption period |
| **Personal Income Tax** | Flat 17% for foreign experts in EEC |
| **Target Industries** | 12 S-curve industries including digital, biotech, robotics |

### 2.4 NSTDA/NIA Approval Process for Triple Deduction

| Step | Details |
|---|---|
| **NSTDA Certification** | For R&D projects involving science and technology |
| **NIA Certification** | For innovation projects (broader than pure R&D) |
| **Processing Time** | 30-60 days for project review |
| **Annual Reporting** | Must submit progress reports to certifying agency |
| **Recertification** | May be required for multi-year projects |

### 2.5 Technology License Fee Deduction

| Parameter | Details |
|---|---|
| **Deduction** | 200% for payments for technology licensing to Thai institutions |
| **Conditions** | Technology must be approved by relevant government agency |
| **Scope** | Licensing fees for technology transfer into Thailand |

## 3. Patent Box / IP Regime

Thailand does not have a formal patent box or IP box regime.

| Aspect | Status |
|---|---|
| **Patent Box** | Not available |
| **IP Amortization** | Standard amortization over useful life |
| **IP Transfer** | Subject to normal CIT on gains |
| **Royalty WHT** | 15% on domestic payments; 15% on payments to non-residents (reduced under treaties) |
| **BOI Alternative** | BOI-promoted companies may receive CIT exemption on IP income during holiday period |
| **Alternative** | Triple deduction and BOI holidays effectively support IP creation |

**Note:** While no patent box exists, the combination of 300% deduction for R&D costs and up to 13-year CIT holidays (EEC) provides substantial IP development incentives. Thailand has over 60 tax treaties that may reduce royalty WHT.

## 4. Effective Benefit Analysis (per $1M USD R&D Spend)

*Assuming THB/USD exchange rate of 35; THB 35M equivalent spend; 20% CIT rate*

| Scenario | Total Deduction | Tax Saving (THB) | Tax Saving (USD) |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Triple Deduction (300%)** | THB 105M | THB 21M (on 200% additional) | ~$600,000 |
| **Standard Deduction (100%)** | THB 35M | THB 7M | ~$200,000 |
| **Additional Benefit of Triple** | THB 70M additional | THB 14M | ~$400,000 |
| **BOI A1 (8-year holiday)** | Full CIT exemption | Full CIT savings | Up to $200,000/year |
| **EEC (13-year holiday)** | Full CIT exemption | Full CIT savings | Up to $200,000/year |

**Combined Example (Triple Deduction + BOI):**
- During BOI holiday: CIT exempted, so triple deduction has no additional CIT benefit
- Post-holiday: Triple deduction provides THB 14M (~$400,000) additional saving per $1M R&D
- Companies should sequence incentives: BOI holiday first, then triple deduction post-holiday

**Key Insight:** The triple deduction is most valuable for companies not under BOI CIT holiday. For BOI-promoted companies, the R&D deduction becomes relevant after the holiday period ends.

## 5. Claim Process & Documentation

### 5.1 Filing Process

| Step | Action | Timeline |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Register R&D project with NSTDA or NIA | Before or during project |
| 2 | Obtain project certification from NSTDA/NIA | 30-60 days processing |
| 3 | Maintain project records and financial accounts | Ongoing |
| 4 | Submit annual progress report to NSTDA/NIA | Annually |
| 5 | Claim triple deduction on corporate tax return (PND.50) | Within 150 days of fiscal year-end |
| 6 | Retain documentation for audit | Minimum 5 years |

### 5.2 NSTDA/NIA Certification Requirements

- **Project proposal:** Technical description, objectives, methodology
- **R&D classification:** Basic research, applied research, or experimental development
- **Budget breakdown:** Detailed cost allocation by category
- **Personnel qualifications:** Researcher CVs and qualifications
- **Expected outcomes:** Anticipated results and commercialization plan
- **Thai content:** Evidence that R&D is conducted in Thailand

### 5.3 Documentation for Tax Filing

- **NSTDA/NIA certification letter**
- **R&D expenditure summary:** Categorized per Revenue Department requirements
- **Personnel records:** Time allocation to certified R&D projects
- **Financial records:** Invoices, receipts, payment records
- **Progress reports:** Submitted to NSTDA/NIA
- **BOI promotion certificate:** If claiming BOI benefits concurrently

## 6. Recent Changes & Sunset Provisions

| Change | Effective Date | Details |
|---|---|---|
| **Royal Decree No. 598** | 2016 | Established triple deduction framework (replacing prior Royal Decrees) |
| **EEC Act** | 2018 | Created enhanced incentive zone with up to 13-year CIT holiday |
| **BOI Competitiveness Enhancement** | 2023 | Updated BOI activity categories and added new technology areas |
| **Digital Asset Incentives** | 2024 | Additional benefits for blockchain and digital technology R&D |
| **Green Technology** | 2024 | Enhanced incentives for environmental and clean technology R&D |
| **EV Industry** | 2024 | Special BOI package for electric vehicle R&D and manufacturing |

**Sunset Provisions:**
- Triple deduction (Royal Decree No. 598): No explicit sunset; Royal Decree remains in force
- BOI incentives: Permanent statutory framework; specific activities reviewed periodically
- EEC incentives: Authorized under EEC Act through at least 2037
- Annual government policy statements may adjust scope and rates
- Thailand 4.0 policy framework supports continued innovation incentives
