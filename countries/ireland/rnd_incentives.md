# Ireland - R&D Tax Incentives

## 1. Overview

| Feature | Details |
|---|---|
| **Headline Incentive** | R&D Tax Credit — 30% (50% on first €400,000) |
| **Type** | Direct tax credit against corporation tax (CT) |
| **Rate** | 30% of qualifying R&D expenditure; 50% on first €400,000 (from 2024) |
| **Refundable** | Yes — 3-year installment refund mechanism; micro companies eligible for first-year refund |
| **Patent Box** | Yes — Knowledge Development Box (KDB) at 6.25% effective CT rate |
| **Legal Basis** | Taxes Consolidation Act (TCA) 1997 §766 (R&D Credit); TCA 1997 §769R (KDB) |

---

## 2. Detailed Programs

### 2.1 R&D Tax Credit

**Legal Basis:** TCA 1997 §766, as amended by Finance Act 2023 and Finance Act 2024.

| Parameter | Value |
|---|---|
| Standard Credit Rate | 30% of qualifying R&D expenditure (increased from 25% effective 2024) |
| Enhanced First-Year Rate | 50% on first €400,000 of qualifying expenditure (from 2024) |
| Credit on Excess (>€400k) | 30% on qualifying expenditure above €400,000 |
| Incremental vs. Volume | Volume-based (no incremental base; credit on full qualifying spend) |
| Qualifying Expenditure | Revenue expenditure on R&D activities: staff costs, materials, utilities, equipment |
| Buildings | 25% credit on qualifying R&D buildings expenditure (spread over 4 years) |
| Subcontracted R&D | Eligible up to the greater of €100,000 or 15% of total qualifying R&D expenditure; must be to universities/approved bodies for higher limits |
| Group Relief | Credit cannot be surrendered as group relief; must be used by the claimant company |

**Qualifying R&D Activities (per TCA 1997 §766 and Revenue Guidelines):**

| Criterion | Requirement |
|---|---|
| Scientific/Technological Advance | Activities must seek to achieve an advance in science or technology |
| Scientific/Technological Uncertainty | Must involve the resolution of uncertainty that is not readily deducible |
| Systematic Investigation | Must be carried out in a systematic, investigative manner |
| Field | Natural sciences, engineering, medical sciences, agricultural sciences, or technology |
| Exclusions | Social sciences, humanities, arts, routine testing, quality control, market research, cosmetic modifications |

### 2.2 Refund Mechanism (3-Year Installment)

**Legal Basis:** TCA 1997 §766(4B).

| Year | Refund Amount |
|---|---|
| Year 1 | Credit offset against current year CT liability; excess: refund of lesser of (a) €25,000 or (b) payroll tax liabilities |
| Year 2 | Further installment of balance (lesser of remaining credit or payroll taxes) |
| Year 3 | Full refund of any remaining balance |

| Special Provisions | Details |
|---|---|
| Micro Company Refund | Companies with qualifying R&D expenditure ≤€50,000 and CT liability <€25,000 can receive full refund in Year 1 |
| Pre-Profit Companies | Full 3-year mechanism applies; ensures pre-revenue R&D companies benefit |
| Carry-Back | Credit can be carried back against CT of the immediately preceding accounting period |
| No Time Limit on Carry-Forward | Unused credits carried forward indefinitely |

### 2.3 Key Employee Provision

**Legal Basis:** TCA 1997 §472D.

| Parameter | Value |
|---|---|
| Purpose | Allow qualifying R&D tax credit to be used to reduce income tax of key R&D employees |
| Eligible Employees | Employees who spend ≥50% of their time on qualifying R&D activities |
| Maximum Benefit | Effective reduction in employee's income tax; cannot exceed the corporation's R&D credit |
| Cap per Employee | Credit allocated to employee capped at €100,000 taxable pay per employee |
| Mechanism | Company surrenders portion of R&D credit; employee claims income tax reduction |
| Documentation | Company must maintain records of employee R&D time and credit allocation |

### 2.4 Pre-Approval / Advance Opinion

| Parameter | Value |
|---|---|
| Advance Opinion | Companies can seek an advance opinion from Revenue on whether proposed activities qualify |
| Non-Binding | Revenue advance opinions are not formally binding but provide comfort |
| Science Foundation Ireland (SFI) | SFI can provide technical opinions on the scientific nature of R&D activities |
| Revenue Guidance | Revenue published detailed guidance notes on R&D tax credit (Revenue eBrief) |

## 3. Patent Box / IP Regime

### Knowledge Development Box (KDB)

**Legal Basis:** TCA 1997 §769R–§769T, introduced by Finance Act 2015, effective 1 January 2016.

| Parameter | Value |
|---|---|
| Effective CT Rate | 6.25% on qualifying KDB profits (vs. standard 12.5% trading rate) |
| Tax Saving | 6.25 percentage points (12.5% – 6.25%) |
| Qualifying Assets | Patents (including short-term patents), copyrighted software, supplementary protection certificates |
| Nexus Approach | OECD BEPS Action 5 compliant; modified nexus ratio applies |
| Nexus Ratio | (Qualifying R&D expenditure × 1.3) / Overall R&D expenditure |
| Qualifying Expenditure (Numerator) | In-house R&D costs, arm's length payments to unconnected subcontractors |
| Overall Expenditure (Denominator) | All R&D expenditure including acquisitions, related-party costs |
| 30% Uplift | Qualifying expenditure uplifted by 30% (capped at total expenditure) |
| Income Covered | Royalties, license fees, embedded IP income from product/service sales, insurance/compensation for IP |
| Election | Annual election; can be made per qualifying asset |
| Documentation | Must maintain detailed records per qualifying asset; nexus calculation required |

**KDB Profit Calculation:**

| Step | Calculation |
|---|---|
| 1. Identify qualifying assets | Patents, copyrighted software developed in-house |
| 2. Calculate qualifying IP income | Gross income attributable to qualifying assets |
| 3. Deduct directly attributable costs | Costs directly related to earning the IP income |
| 4. Apply nexus fraction | Modified nexus ratio × net IP income |
| 5. Apply KDB rate | 6.25% on resulting KDB profit |
| 6. Standard rate on remainder | 12.5% on any non-qualifying portion |

## 4. Effective Benefit Analysis (per $1M USD R&D Spend)

*Assumes $1M USD ≈ €920,000 (illustrative rate); standard CT rate 12.5%.*

| Scenario | Calculation | Net Benefit |
|---|---|---|
| **R&D Credit — First €400k (50%)** | 50% × €400,000 = €200,000 | €200,000 |
| **R&D Credit — Remaining €520k (30%)** | 30% × €520,000 = €156,000 | €156,000 |
| **Total R&D Credit** | €200,000 + €156,000 | **€356,000** |
| **R&D Credit (pre-2024 at 25%)** | 25% × €920,000 = €230,000 | €230,000 |
| **KDB (on €920k IP profit)** | (12.5% – 6.25%) × €920,000 = €57,500 | **€57,500** |
| **Combined R&D Credit + KDB** | €356,000 + €57,500 | **€413,500** |
| **Full deductibility** | €920,000 × 12.5% = €115,000 (standard deduction) | Embedded |
| **Micro company (≤€50k spend at 50%)** | 50% × €50,000 = €25,000 (Year 1 full refund) | €25,000 |
| **Key employee provision** | Portion of €356,000 credit allocated to reduce employee IT | Cash-neutral to company |

> **Note:** Ireland's 12.5% CT rate is already low; the R&D credit and KDB provide benefits on top of this favorable base rate, making Ireland exceptionally competitive for R&D-intensive businesses.

## 5. Claim Process & Documentation

### 5.1 Filing Process

| Step | Action | Deadline |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Identify qualifying R&D expenditure | During accounting period |
| 2 | Prepare technical and financial documentation | Contemporaneous |
| 3 | Complete CT1 return with R&D credit claim (Section 766) | Within 12 months of accounting period end |
| 4 | Include KDB claim if applicable (Section 769R) | Same CT1 return |
| 5 | Revenue processing / refund | Installment basis or immediate (micro company) |
| 6 | Retain documentation for Revenue audit | Minimum 6 years |

### 5.2 Documentation Requirements

| Document | Purpose |
|---|---|
| Technical project reports | Describe advance sought, uncertainties, systematic approach |
| R&D activity logs | Contemporaneous records of R&D work performed |
| Staff time records | Time allocation between qualifying R&D and non-R&D activities |
| Financial records | Expenditure breakdown by project and cost category |
| Subcontractor agreements | Scope of work, R&D nature, costs |
| KDB nexus calculation | Per-asset tracking of qualifying vs. total R&D expenditure |
| IP ownership documentation | Evidence of IP ownership and self-development for KDB |

### 5.3 Revenue Audits

- Revenue has dedicated R&D audit teams
- Science Foundation Ireland (SFI) provides technical expertise for Revenue audits
- Audit focus: whether activities meet the science test and whether expenditure is properly apportioned
- Records should be maintained contemporaneously, not retrospectively
- Penalty regime applies for incorrect claims

## 6. Recent Changes & Sunset Provisions

| Change | Effective Date | Details |
|---|---|---|
| R&D credit rate increased to 30% | 1 January 2024 | Up from 25% (in place since 2004) |
| Enhanced 50% rate on first €400k | 1 January 2024 | New provision targeting SMEs and startups |
| Volume basis maintained | Ongoing | No incremental requirement; full spend qualifies |
| KDB rate unchanged at 6.25% | Stable since 2016 | No changes anticipated |
| Key employee provision continued | Ongoing | Regularly reviewed but maintained |
| Digital filing requirements | Ongoing | Revenue modernizing claim submission |
| Pillar Two impact | 2024+ | 15% global minimum tax may affect overall effective rate planning |
| Micro company refund provision | Ongoing | Immediate Year 1 refund for small claimants |

**Sunset Provisions:**
- **R&D Tax Credit (§766)** is permanent; no sunset clause
- **KDB (§769R)** is permanent; OECD-compliant
- Ireland's commitment to 12.5% CT rate is long-standing; now subject to Pillar Two minimum (15%)
- Rate adjustments possible through Finance Acts but no elimination anticipated
- Annual Finance Act may modify thresholds and rates
