# Ireland - Taxable Income Deductions

> **Jurisdiction**: National (Revenue Commissioners)
> **Governing Law**: Taxes Consolidation Act 1997 (TCA 1997)
> **Tax Year**: 2024/2025
> **Currency**: EUR (€)

---

## 1. Depreciation & Capital Allowances

Ireland does not permit book depreciation as a tax deduction. Instead, capital allowances (also called "wear and tear allowances") are provided under TCA 1997 for qualifying capital expenditure.

### Plant and Machinery — TCA 1997 §284

| Parameter | Value |
|---|---|
| Rate | **12.5%** straight-line over **8 years** |
| Eligible assets | Plant, machinery, equipment, fixtures, computer hardware/software, motor vehicles |
| Balancing allowances/charges | On disposal, excess relief clawed back or shortfall allowed — TCA 1997 §288 |

### Industrial Buildings — TCA 1997 §268–§272

| Building Type | Annual Rate | Period | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| Factory / manufacturing buildings | **4%** | 25 years | TCA 1997 §268(1)(a) |
| Market gardening buildings | **4%** | 25 years | |
| Laboratories used for R&D | **4%** | 25 years | |
| Hotels (limited) | 4% | 25 years | Restricted to pre-existing approvals |

### Accelerated Capital Allowances (ACA) — TCA 1997 §285A

| Parameter | Value |
|---|---|
| Rate | **100%** in year of acquisition |
| Eligible | Energy-efficient equipment (from specified list maintained by SEAI) |
| Cap | None (based on qualifying expenditure) |
| Note | Equipment must be from the Triple E Register |

### Intangible Assets — TCA 1997 §291A

| Parameter | Value |
|---|---|
| Rate | In line with accounting amortisation (IFRS/GAAP treatment followed for tax) or **7%** per annum over 15 years (election available) |
| Eligible | IP (patents, trademarks, copyrights, know-how, goodwill directly related to IP, customer lists, computer software) acquired for trade purposes |
| Cap | Aggregate allowances (capital allowances + related interest) capped at **80%** of income from the IP trade |
| Clawback | If disposed within specified period, balancing charge applies |

### Motor Vehicles — TCA 1997 §373–§380

| Parameter | Value |
|---|---|
| CO₂ categories | Category A/B/C (lower emissions) through category D/E (highest) |
| Capital allowances cap | Ranges from **€24,000** (Category A) to **€0** (Category E) based on CO₂ |
| Electric vehicles | Accelerated; higher cap |
| Rate | 12.5% over 8 years (on the lesser of cost and specified cap) |

## 2. Business Operating Expenses

### General Deductibility — TCA 1997 §81

Expenses are deductible only if incurred **"wholly and exclusively"** for the purposes of the trade or profession.

### Key Deductible Operating Expenses

| Expense Category | Deductibility | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Employee salaries and wages | Fully deductible | Must be reasonable; TCA 1997 §81 |
| Employer PRSI contributions | Fully deductible | |
| Employer pension contributions | Deductible within spreading rules | TCA 1997 §774 |
| Rent | Fully deductible | |
| Professional fees | Deductible if revenue nature | |
| Repairs and maintenance | Deductible (revenue, not capital) | TCA 1997 §81 |
| Bad debts | Deductible when specifically written off or provided for | TCA 1997 §81 |
| Insurance premiums | Fully deductible | |
| Training costs | Fully deductible | |
| Pre-trading expenses | Deductible if incurred within **3 years** before commencement | TCA 1997 §82 |

### Charitable Donations — TCA 1997 §848A

| Parameter | Value |
|---|---|
| Corporate donations | Treated as a deduction from trading income |
| Minimum | €250 per donation |
| Cap | Maximum 10% of total income in the accounting period [unverified — confirm with local counsel] |
| Eligible bodies | Charities, approved bodies, approved educational/research bodies |

## 3. Interest & Financing Costs

### General Interest Deductibility — TCA 1997 §247

| Parameter | Value |
|---|---|
| Trading interest | Deductible as a trading expense if for the purposes of the trade — TCA 1997 §81 |
| Non-trading interest (§247) | Deductible against total income if loan used for qualifying purposes (e.g., acquiring shares in/lending to a trading company) |
| Connected party restriction | Interest to certain connected non-resident lenders — TCA 1997 §817 |

### ATAD Interest Limitation Rule — TCA 1997 §835AZA (from 1 January 2022)

| Parameter | Value |
|---|---|
| Deductible limit | Net interest expense up to **30%** of tax-EBITDA |
| De minimis | **€3,000,000** net interest expense |
| Group ratio rule | Available (group net external interest / group EBITDA) |
| Carry-forward | Disallowed interest carried forward **indefinitely** |
| Carry-forward of unused EBITDA | **5 years** |
| Transitional | Applies to accounting periods commencing on or after 1 January 2022 |

### Anti-Avoidance — TCA 1997 §817A

| Parameter | Value |
|---|---|
| Scope | Restrictions on deduction of interest in certain intra-group financing arrangements |
| Effect | Interest non-deductible where loan does not fund a trading activity or the arrangement is primarily tax-motivated |

## 4. Research & Development

Ireland provides an R&D tax credit (from 2024 at 30% of qualifying expenditure, increased from 25%) and operates the Knowledge Development Box (KDB), which taxes qualifying IP income at an effective rate of **6.25%** (50% of the standard 12.5% trading rate). The R&D credit is available on a volume basis with no base-year calculation.

See [R&D Incentives](rnd_incentives.md) for full program detail.

## 5. Loss Carry-Forward & Carry-Back

### Trading Losses — TCA 1997 §396

| Parameter | Value |
|---|---|
| Current year offset | Against total profits of the same accounting period — TCA 1997 §396(1) |
| Carry-forward | **Indefinite** — against future trading profits of the **same trade** — TCA 1997 §396(1) |
| Carry-back | **Not available** (except terminal loss — see below) |

### Terminal Loss — TCA 1997 §397

| Parameter | Value |
|---|---|
| Availability | On permanent cessation of trade |
| Carry-back | Loss of final 12 months can be carried back against profits of the trade for the **preceding 3 years** (LIFO) |

### Group Relief — TCA 1997 §420

| Parameter | Value |
|---|---|
| Scope | Current-year trading losses, excess charges, and certain other losses may be surrendered to 75% group companies |
| Condition | Companies must be resident in Ireland (or EEA with comparable relief denied) |

### Value Basis

| Parameter | Value |
|---|---|
| Trading losses | Against same-trade income (12.5% rate) |
| Non-trading losses | Against non-trading income (25% rate) |
| Cross-rate loss relief | Special value-basis rules apply when losses at 12.5% offset income at 25% or vice versa — TCA 1997 §396A |

## 7. Industry-Specific Deductions

### Knowledge Development Box (KDB) — TCA 1997 §769R–§769T

| Parameter | Value |
|---|---|
| Effective rate | **6.25%** (50% of 12.5% trading rate) on qualifying IP income |
| Qualifying assets | Patented inventions, copyrighted software, supplementary protection certificates |
| Modified nexus approach | OECD BEPS Action 5 compliant — benefit proportional to in-house R&D |

### Film Relief — TCA 1997 §481

| Parameter | Value |
|---|---|
| Credit rate | **32%** of eligible expenditure |
| Cap | Maximum eligible expenditure **€70M** per project |
| Minimum spend | €250,000 |
| Eligible | Feature films, TV dramas, creative documentaries, animation |

### Real Estate Investment Trusts (REITs) — TCA 1997 §705A–§705N

| Parameter | Value |
|---|---|
| Tax treatment | Exempt from corporation tax on qualifying property rental income and gains |
| Distribution requirement | Minimum **85%** of property income distributed |
| Conditions | Listed on stock exchange; property rental business ≥75% of assets |

### Shipping (Tonnage Tax) — TCA 1997 §697A–§697Q

| Parameter | Value |
|---|---|
| Method | Lump-sum profit based on net tonnage |
| Election | **10 years** |

### Aviation Finance / Leasing

| Parameter | Value |
|---|---|
| Significance | Ireland is a major hub for aircraft leasing |
| Capital allowances | 12.5% over 8 years on aircraft — standard plant and machinery treatment |
| Interest deduction | Fully deductible for trading companies |

## 8. Treaties & Transfer Pricing

### Transfer Pricing — TCA 1997 §835A–§835H

| Parameter | Detail |
|---|---|
| Standard | Arm's-length principle (OECD compliant) — TCA 1997 §835C |
| Scope | All arrangements between associated persons (including domestic from 2020) |
| SME exemption | Small and medium enterprises (EU definition) exempt from TP documentation if arrangement value < €1M (or €5M for goods) [unverified — confirm with local counsel] |
| Documentation | Master file and local file required per OECD standard |
| Country-by-Country Reporting | Required for MNE groups with consolidated revenue ≥ €750M — TCA 1997 §891H |
| Penalties | Surcharge of 10% of tax underpaid (25% if deliberate) |
| APAs | Bilateral APAs available; no formal unilateral APA programme |

### Participation Exemption (from 2025)

| Parameter | Value |
|---|---|
| Status | Ireland is introducing a participation exemption for foreign dividends from 2025 (Finance Act 2024) [unverified — confirm with local counsel] |
| Current regime (2024) | Credit method — foreign tax credit against Irish tax on qualifying dividends |

### Treaty Network

Ireland has over **75** double taxation agreements.

| Income Type | Typical Treaty Rate |
|---|---|
| Dividends | 0–15% |
| Interest | 0–10% |
| Royalties | 0–10% |

## 9. Non-Deductible Items

| Expense | Statutory Reference |
|---|---|
| Corporation tax | Not deductible |
| Entertainment of clients | **Fully non-deductible** — TCA 1997 §840 |
| Capital expenditure (unless capital allowances apply) | TCA 1997 §81(2)(f) |
| Private or domestic expenses | TCA 1997 §81(2)(a) |
| Fines and penalties (criminal) | Not deductible — common law / TCA 1997 §81 |
| Dividends paid | Not an expense |
| General provisions / contingent liabilities | Not deductible until crystallised |
| Political donations | Not deductible |
| Depreciation (accounting) | Replaced by capital allowances |
| Non-trade interest (unless §247 relief applies) | Not deductible against trading income |
| Excessive directors' remuneration | Revenue may challenge under arm's-length — TCA 1997 §81 |
