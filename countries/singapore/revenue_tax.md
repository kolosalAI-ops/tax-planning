# Singapore - Revenue Tax (Tax on Business Revenue)

## 1. Corporate Income Tax (CIT)

### 1.1 Standard Rate

| Parameter | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Headline Rate** | **17%** (flat) — **ITA Section 43(1)(a)** |
| **Tax Year** | Year of Assessment (YA) - based on preceding financial year |
| **Residence Basis** | Resident if control & management exercised in Singapore |
| **Territorial System** | Yes - only Singapore-sourced income and foreign income remitted to Singapore taxed — **ITA Section 10(1)** (charge of income tax on income accruing in or derived from Singapore); **ITA Section 10(25)** (income received in Singapore from outside) |
| **Capital Gains Tax** | **None** |

### 1.2 Partial Tax Exemption (PTE) — ITA Section 43A

All companies (except those claiming the startup exemption) qualify:

| Chargeable Income | Exemption Rate | Effective Tax Rate |
|---|---|---|
| First S$10,000 | 75% exempt | **4.25%** |
| Next S$190,000 | 50% exempt | **8.50%** |
| Above S$200,000 | No exemption | **17.00%** |

**Maximum annual exemption:** S$102,500 (tax savings of S$17,425)

### 1.3 Startup Tax Exemption Scheme (SUTE) — ITA Section 43B

For qualifying new companies in their first 3 consecutive YAs:

| Chargeable Income | Exemption Rate | Effective Tax Rate |
|---|---|---|
| First S$100,000 | 75% exempt | **4.25%** |
| Next S$100,000 | 50% exempt | **8.50%** |
| Above S$200,000 | No exemption | **17.00%** |

**Eligibility:**
- Incorporated in Singapore
- Tax resident in Singapore for that YA
- Maximum 20 shareholders throughout the basis period
- At least 1 individual shareholder holding ≥10% of issued ordinary shares

**Excludes:** Property developers, investment holding companies

### 1.4 Corporate Income Tax Rebate — ITA Section 92

| Year of Assessment | Rebate | Cap |
|---|---|---|
| YA 2024 | 50% of tax payable | S$40,000 |
| YA 2025 | 50% of tax payable | S$40,000 |

### 1.5 BEPS Pillar 2 - Global Minimum Tax — Income Tax (Minimum Tax Rate) Act 2024

Effective **1 January 2025**:
- **Minimum effective rate:** 15%
- **Applies to:** MNE groups with consolidated annual revenue ≥ EUR 750M (S$1.125B)
- **Domestic Top-Up Tax (DTT):** Singapore will impose DTT to ensure 15% minimum — enacted via the **Income Tax (Minimum Tax Rate) Act 2024**
- Multinational Enterprise groups with excess profits in Singapore taxed at below 15% will face top-up

## 3. Goods and Services Tax (GST) — Goods and Services Tax Act 1993

### 3.1 Standard Rate — GST Act Section 7 (Imposition of GST); GST Act Section 16 (Rate of Tax)

| Parameter | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Current Rate** | **9%** (from 1 Jan 2024) — **GST Act Section 16** |
| **Previous Rate** | 8% (1 Jan 2023 - 31 Dec 2023) |
| **Before that** | 7% (2007 - 31 Dec 2022) |
| **Imposition** | Tax imposed on supply of goods and services in Singapore — **GST Act Section 7** |

### 3.2 Registration Thresholds — GST Act Section 25

| Type | Threshold |
|---|---|
| **Compulsory registration** | Taxable turnover > S$1M in past 12 months, or expected to exceed S$1M in next 12 months — **GST Act Section 25** |
| **Voluntary registration** | Available; must remain registered for minimum 2 years |
| **Retrospective basis** | Looking back at preceding 4 quarters |
| **Prospective basis** | Looking forward at next 12 months |

### 3.3 Supply Categories — GST Act Section 14 (Zero-rated); GST Act Section 22 & Fourth Schedule (Exempt)

| Category | Examples | Statutory Basis |
|---|---|---|
| **Standard-rated (9%)** | Most goods and services supplied in Singapore | **GST Act Section 16** |
| **Zero-rated (0%)** | Exports, international services | **GST Act Section 14** (zero-rated supplies) |
| **Exempt** | Sale/lease of residential property, financial services (specified), investment precious metals | **GST Act Section 22** read with the **Fourth Schedule** (exempt supplies) |
| **Out of scope** | Private transactions, transfer of going concern | |

### 3.4 Reverse Charge Mechanism

- Applies to **imported services** by GST-registered businesses
- Threshold: S$1M in global turnover **and** receiving imported services > S$1M per year
- Business must account for GST on imported services as if it were the supplier

### 3.5 Overseas Vendor Registration (OVR)

- Foreign vendors making > S$100K in B2C digital services to Singapore customers must register
- Applies to digital services, digital marketplace operators

## 4. Employer Contributions (CPF & Levies)

### 4.1 Central Provident Fund (CPF) — Central Provident Fund Act (Cap. 36)

CPF is Singapore's mandatory social security savings scheme. Employer obligation to contribute is established under **CPF Act Section 7(1)**. Contribution rates by age band are prescribed in **CPF Act Section 36, read with the First Schedule**.

| Employee Age | Employer Rate | Employee Rate | Total |
|---|---|---|---|
| ≤ 55 | **17.0%** | 20.0% | 37.0% |
| > 55 to 60 | **14.5%** | 15.0% | 29.5% |
| > 60 to 65 | **11.5%** | 9.5% | 21.0% |
| > 65 to 70 | **9.0%** | 7.0% | 16.0% |
| > 70 | **7.5%** | 5.0% | 12.5% |

**Wage Ceilings:**
- Ordinary Wage ceiling: S$6,800/month
- Additional Wage ceiling: S$102,000/year (less total ordinary wages subject to CPF)

**CPF Allocation (age ≤ 55):**
- Ordinary Account: 23% (of total 37%)
- Special Account: 6%
- MediSave Account: 8%

**Note:** CPF applies only to **Singapore citizens and Permanent Residents**. Foreign workers are not covered.

### 4.2 Skills Development Levy (SDL) — Skills Development Levy Act (Cap. 306)

| Parameter | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Rate** | 0.25% of monthly remuneration — **Skills Development Levy Act, Cap. 306** |
| **Minimum** | S$2 per employee per month |
| **Maximum** | S$11.25 per employee per month (on first S$4,500) |
| **Applies to** | All employees (including foreign workers) |

### 4.3 Foreign Worker Levy (FWL) — Employment of Foreign Manpower Act (Cap. 91A)

| Pass Type | Monthly Levy Range |
|---|---|
| S Pass | S$450 - S$650 |
| Work Permit (various sectors) | S$300 - S$950 |

- Levied under the **Employment of Foreign Manpower Act, Cap. 91A**
- Varies by sector (manufacturing, services, construction, marine, process)
- Tiered based on dependency ratio ceiling
- Higher levy for exceeding quota

## 8. International Tax Provisions

### 8.1 Territorial Tax System — ITA Section 10(1); ITA Section 10(25)

- Only income **sourced in Singapore** or **received in Singapore** from outside is taxable — **ITA Section 10(1)** (charge of income tax on income accruing in or derived from Singapore); **ITA Section 10(25)** (deemed receipt of foreign-sourced income in Singapore)
- Foreign-sourced income is taxable when remitted to Singapore, subject to exemptions

### 8.2 Foreign-Sourced Income Exemption (FSIE)

Foreign-sourced dividends, branch profits, and service income are exempt if:
1. Income was subject to tax in the source jurisdiction (headline rate ≥ 15%)
2. At the time income is received, the headline tax rate in the foreign jurisdiction is ≥ 15%
3. IRAS is satisfied that the exemption is beneficial

### 8.3 Transfer Pricing — ITA Sections 34D-34F; Income Tax (Transfer Pricing Documentation) Rules 2018

- **Arm's length principle** applies — **ITA Section 34D** (transfer pricing adjustments based on arm's length principle)
- **Documentation:** Must be prepared contemporaneously — **Income Tax (Transfer Pricing Documentation) Rules 2018**
- **CbCR filing:** Required if group revenue ≥ S$1.125B — **ITA Section 34F** (country-by-country reporting)
- **Surcharge:** 5% surcharge on TP adjustments — **ITA Section 34E** (surcharge on transfer pricing adjustments)
- **Advance Pricing Arrangements (APA):** Available (unilateral, bilateral, multilateral)
- **Penalties:** 5% surcharge on TP adjustments (**ITA Section 34E**); fines up to S$10,000 for non-compliance

### 8.4 Anti-Avoidance — ITA Section 33

- **General Anti-Avoidance Rule (GAAR):** **ITA Section 33** - IRAS can disregard arrangements with sole/main purpose of obtaining a tax advantage
- **No specific CFC rules** (but substance requirements for FSIE)
- **No specific interest limitation rules** (arm's length principle applies under **ITA Section 34D**)

## 11. Income by Source Type

### 11.1 Service Income (Domestic & Cross-Border)

**Domestic Services**

- Service income derived in Singapore is taxed as **trade income at 17% CIT** under **ITA Section 10(1)(a)** (gains or profits from any trade, business, profession, or vocation).
- GST applies at **9%** on taxable supplies of services in Singapore — **GST Act Section 8** (effective 1 January 2024).
- **Exported services** (international services listed in the Fourth Schedule of the GST Act) are **zero-rated for GST** under **GST Act Section 14**, provided the services qualify as international services.

**Cross-Border Services**

- Singapore applies a **territorial principle**: only Singapore-sourced income or foreign income **remitted** to Singapore is taxable — **ITA Section 10(25)**.
- **Foreign-sourced service income** is exempt from tax under **ITA Section 13(8)** if the following conditions are met:
  1. The income was subject to tax in the foreign jurisdiction.
  2. The headline tax rate in that foreign jurisdiction is **at least 15%**.
  3. The remitted income was subject to foreign tax.
- IRAS must be satisfied the exemption is beneficial to Singapore.

**Transfer Pricing**

- Related-party service fees must comply with the **arm's length principle** under **ITA Section 34D** and the IRAS Transfer Pricing Guidelines (7th edition, IRAS e-Tax Guide).

**Withholding Tax on Service Fees to Non-Residents**

| Payment Type | Default WHT Rate | Statutory Basis |
|---|---|---|
| **Management fees** | 17% (prevailing corporate rate) or applicable treaty rate | **ITA Section 45** |
| **Technical/professional fees (non-resident individuals)** | 15% of gross income or 22% of net income | **ITA Section 45** |
| **Director fees (non-resident)** | 22% | **ITA Section 45** |

---

### 11.2 Royalty & IP Licensing Income (Received)

**Domestic Royalties Received**

- Royalty income earned by a Singapore company is assessable as **trade income at 17% CIT** under **ITA Section 10(1)(a)**.

**IP Writing-Down Allowance (WDA)**

- Qualifying IP acquisition costs may be written down over **5, 10, or 15 years** under **ITA Section 19B**.
- Qualifying IP includes: patents, copyrights, trademarks, registered designs, know-how, and software.
- **Clawback provision:** If the IP is disposed of within the WDA period, previously claimed allowances may be subject to clawback.

**Concessionary IP Regimes**

| Incentive | Concessionary Rate | Administered By |
|---|---|---|
| **Approved Royalties Incentive (ARI)** | 5-10% on qualifying IP income | EDB |
| **IP Development Incentive (IDI)** | 5% or 10% on qualifying IP income (modified nexus approach) | EDB / **ITA Section 43C** |

- Singapore does not have a formal patent box regime, but the IDI and ARI serve a similar function.

**R&D Leading to IP**

- **250% enhanced deduction** for qualifying R&D expenditure under **ITA Section 14C**.
- Under the Enterprise Innovation Scheme (EIS), enhanced deduction of up to **400%** may apply (from YA 2024).

---

### 11.3 Rental & Lease Income

**Real Property Rental**

- Rental income from real property is assessable as **property income at 17% CIT** under **ITA Section 10(1)(f)** (rent, royalties, premiums, and any other profits arising from property).

**Property Tax Rates (Property Tax Act)**

| Property Type | Rate Range |
|---|---|
| Owner-occupied residential | 0% - 36% (progressive) |
| Non-owner-occupied residential | 10% - 36% |
| Non-residential (commercial/industrial) | 10% - 25% |

**Stamp Duty on Lease Agreements**

- **0.4% of total rent** for leases with a term of 4 years or less — **Stamp Duties Act**.

**Equipment Leasing**

- Income from equipment leasing is assessable as **trade income** at 17% CIT.

**S-REIT (Singapore Real Estate Investment Trust)**

- S-REITs are **tax-transparent** vehicles under **ITA Section 43(2A)**.
- Distributions to individuals: subject to **10% WHT**.
- Distributions to qualifying non-residents: **10% WHT**.

**Deductible Expenses Against Rental Income**

- Property tax, insurance premiums, repair and maintenance costs, mortgage interest, and agent commissions are deductible against rental income.

### 11.4 Capital Gains (Business Assets, Shares, Property)

**No Capital Gains Tax**

- Singapore does **not** impose capital gains tax. Capital gains are excluded from assessable income under **ITA Section 10(1)**, which only taxes income of a revenue nature.

**Revenue vs. Capital Distinction**

- The distinction between **revenue gains** (taxable) and **capital gains** (not taxable) is critical. Gains arising from trading activities are treated as revenue in nature and subject to tax; gains from the disposal of investments held on capital account are not taxable.

**IRAS Safe Harbour for Share Disposals**

- IRAS provides a safe harbour: gains from disposal of ordinary shares are **not taxable** if the divesting company holds **at least 20% of the shares** in the investee for a **continuous period of at least 24 months** prior to disposal — per the IRAS e-Tax Guide "Gains or Profits of an Income Nature".

**Stamp Duty on Share Transfers**

- **0.2%** of the higher of the consideration paid or the net asset value of the shares — **Stamp Duties Act Section 22**.

**Property Gains — Stamp Duty Implications**

| Duty | Rate | Trigger |
|---|---|---|
| **Seller's Stamp Duty (SSD) — Residential** | 12% / 8% / 4% | Sold within 1 / 2 / 3 years of acquisition |
| **Additional Buyer's Stamp Duty (ABSD) — Foreigners** | 20% | Any residential property purchase (note: current rate is 60% for foreigners as of latest revision) |
| **ABSD — PRs (2nd property)** | 12% (note: current rate is 30% as of latest revision) | Second residential property |
| **ABSD — Entities** | 30% (note: current rate is 65% as of latest revision) | Any residential property |

- Gains from sale of **foreign assets** are not taxable — they are of a capital nature and also foreign-sourced.

---

### 11.5 Dividend Income (Received by Company)

**One-Tier Corporate Tax System**

- Under Singapore's one-tier corporate tax system, dividends paid by a Singapore tax-resident company are **tax-exempt** for all shareholders — **ITA Section 13(1)(za)**.
  - **No dividend withholding tax.**
  - **No secondary tax** on distribution.
  - Foreign shareholders receive dividends **exempt from Singapore tax**.

**Foreign Dividends**

- Foreign-sourced dividends are exempt under **ITA Section 13(8)** if:
  1. The income was subject to tax in the foreign jurisdiction (headline rate **at least 15%**).
  2. The income was remitted to Singapore (or deemed remitted).
  3. IRAS is satisfied the exemption is beneficial to Singapore.

**Participation Exemption**

- Dividends from foreign subsidiaries may qualify for a participation exemption.

**FSIE (Foreign-Sourced Income Exemption)**

- The FSIE framework has been expanded under BEPS Pillar 2 compliance to ensure alignment with the global minimum tax of 15%.

---

### 11.6 Interest Income (Received)

**General Treatment**

- Interest income is taxable at **17% CIT** under **ITA Section 10(1)(d)** (interest, discount, charge, or annuity).

**Qualifying Debt Securities (QDS) Scheme**

- Interest income from qualifying debt securities enjoys a **10% concessionary rate** under **ITA Section 43N**.
- Interest from approved banks/finance companies may qualify for WHT exemption or reduced rates.

**Foreign-Sourced Interest**

- Exempt under **ITA Section 13(8)** if the standard FSIE conditions are met.

**GST on Financial Services**

- Interest is treated as an **exempt supply** (not zero-rated) — no GST is chargeable on interest income.

**Thin Capitalization**

- Singapore has **no thin capitalization rules**. The arm's length principle under **ITA Section 34D** (transfer pricing) applies instead.

**Related-Party Interest**

- Interest on related-party loans must be on **arm's length terms**; excess interest may be disallowed under **ITA Section 34D**.

## 12. Sector-Specific Revenue Taxes

### 12.1 Digital Services Taxation

**No Specific DST**

- Singapore does **not** impose a unilateral Digital Services Tax (DST).
- Singapore's position favours the **OECD Pillar 1** multilateral approach over unilateral DSTs.

**GST on Digital Services — Overseas Vendor Registration (OVR) Regime**

- Non-resident vendors supplying **B2C digital services** to customers in Singapore must register for GST under the OVR regime if:
  - **Global turnover** exceeds **S$1,000,000**, AND
  - **Digital supplies to Singapore** exceed **S$100,000**.
- Applicable GST rate: **9%** — **GST Act Section 33C** and OVR regime (effective 2020, rate updated to 9% from 1 January 2024).

**B2B Digital Services**

- Handled via the **reverse charge mechanism**: the Singapore-registered business recipient accounts for GST.

**E-Commerce / Marketplace Operators**

- Marketplace operator rules for GST apply under **GST Act Section 33C** — operators may be deemed the supplier for GST purposes.

**Digital PE**

- Singapore does **not** apply a digital permanent establishment concept.

---

### 12.2 Natural Resource / Extractive Taxes

**No Significant Natural Resources**

- Singapore has **no significant natural resources** and does not impose mining royalties, petroleum royalties, or a Petroleum Resource Rent Tax (PRRT).

**Petroleum & Commodity Trading Hub**

| Incentive | Concessionary Rate | Details |
|---|---|---|
| **Global Trader Programme (GTP)** | 5-10% on qualifying trading income | Administered by EDB/Enterprise Singapore |
| **Approved Oil Trader** | Similar concessionary treatment | For qualifying oil trading companies |

- Energy and commodity trading income is otherwise taxed at the standard **17% CIT**.

**Offshore Oil Services**

- Offshore oil services companies are taxed at the standard CIT rate or may qualify for concessionary maritime rates.

**Maritime Sector Incentives**

- **Approved International Shipping Enterprise (AIS):** qualifying shipping income taxed at **0%** — administered by the Maritime and Port Authority of Singapore (MPA).

---

### 12.3 Insurance Premium Taxes

**No Insurance Premium Tax (IPT)**

- Singapore does **not** impose an insurance premium tax.

**Insurance Company Taxation**

- Insurance companies are subject to standard **17% CIT** on underwriting profits.
- Insurance brokers: standard **17% CIT**.
- **Life insurance business:** Special tax rules apply under **ITA Section 26** (separate computation for Singapore fund and offshore fund).

**Concessionary Rates for Insurance Activities**

| Scheme | Rate | Details |
|---|---|---|
| **Insurance Business Development (IBD)** | 10% on offshore insurance/reinsurance income | Administered by EDB |
| **Approved Foreign Loan (AFL) scheme** | Reduced WHT on loans to qualifying insurers | Facilitates cheaper foreign borrowing |

- MAS (Monetary Authority of Singapore) imposes regulatory levies, but these are not taxes per se.

---

### 12.4 Financial Transaction Taxes

**No FTT / Securities Transaction Tax**

- Singapore does **not** impose a financial transaction tax or securities transaction tax.
- **No bank transaction tax.**

**Stamp Duty on Transfers**

| Transaction | Duty |
|---|---|
| **Share transfers** | 0.2% of value — **Stamp Duties Act Section 22** |
| **Buyer's Stamp Duty (BSD) — property** | Progressive 1-6% (up to 6% above S$1.5M) |
| **Additional Buyer's Stamp Duty (ABSD)** | 0-65% depending on buyer profile (citizens, PRs, foreigners, entities, trusts) |
| **Seller's Stamp Duty (SSD) — residential** | 4-12% if sold within 3 years of acquisition |

**GST on Financial Services**

- Financial services (including stock trades, insurance, lending) are **exempt supplies** — no GST applies.

**Financial Sector Incentive (FSI)**

- Concessionary tax rates of **5% to 13.5%** on qualifying income for approved financial institutions — administered by EDB under **ITA Section 43C**.

|---|---|

| **Mass market** | 22% of GGR |

- Rates were revised under the 2022 amendments to the Casino Control Act.
- **Entry levy** for Singapore citizens and Permanent Residents: **S$150 per visit** or **S$3,000 per year**.

**Betting Duty — Betting Act Section 11**

| Operator | Rate |
|---|---|
| Authorized operators (Singapore Pools, Singapore Turf Club) | **30% of gross betting revenue** |
| 4D / TOTO (operated by Singapore Pools) | **30% betting duty** |

**Private Lotteries**

- Regulated under the **Private Lotteries Act**; private lottery permits may be issued with minimal tax implications.

**Remote Gambling**

- Regulated under the **Remote Gambling Act 2014**.
- Only exempt operators (e.g., Singapore Pools online) are permitted to offer remote gambling services.

### 12.6 Agricultural Income Taxation

**Minimal Agriculture**

- Singapore is a **land-scarce city-state** with minimal agricultural activity. There is **no specific agricultural tax regime** or agricultural tax incentives.

**Tax Treatment**

- Agri-food tech companies are taxed at the standard **17% CIT**. Innovative agri-tech companies may qualify for **Pioneer** or **DEI** incentives if they meet the relevant criteria.
- Urban farming operations are taxed as standard business income.

**GST on Food Imports**

- Imported goods (including most food items) are subject to **9% GST**. Most food items are **not exempt** from GST.

**Regulatory Framework**

- The Singapore Food Agency (SFA) oversees food safety and security regulation — this is a regulatory function, not tax-related.

**"30 by 30" Food Security Goal**

- The government's target to produce 30% of nutritional needs locally by 2030 is supported through **government grants** (e.g., "30x30 Express" fund), not through tax incentives.
