# Singapore - Taxable Income Deductions

> **Jurisdiction**: National (Inland Revenue Authority of Singapore — IRAS)
> **Governing Law**: Income Tax Act (ITA Cap 134)
> **Tax Year**: 2024/2025
> **Currency**: SGD (S$)

---

## 1. Depreciation & Capital Allowances

Singapore does not use the term "depreciation" for tax purposes. Businesses claim **Capital Allowances (CA)** on qualifying capital expenditure on plant and machinery used for trade.

### 1.1 Section 19 — Standard 3-Year Write-Off

> **Legal Basis**: ITA §19; §19(2)

| Parameter | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Method** | Straight-line over 3 years |
| **Annual Rate** | 33.33% per year — §19(2) |
| **Applies to** | All qualifying plant and machinery |
| **No election required** | Default method |

### 1.2 Section 19A — Accelerated Write-Off

> **Legal Basis**: ITA §19A(1); §19A(2); §19A(10A)

| Option | Detail | Statutory Basis |
|---|---|---|
| **1-Year Write-Off** | 100% in year of acquisition | §19A(10A) |
| **2-Year Write-Off** | 75% in year 1, 25% in year 2 (specified automation equipment) | §19A(1) |
| **Prescribed Working Life** | 6–16 years depending on asset type | §19A(1) |

The election under §19A(2), once made, is **irrevocable** for that asset.

### 1.3 Key Asset Rates

| Asset Type | Standard (§19) | Accelerated (§19A) | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Plant & Machinery** | 3 years (33.3%) | 1 year (100%) | Most common claim |
| **Computers & IT Equipment** | 3 years (33.3%) | 1 year (100%) | Including peripherals |
| **Software** | 3 years (33.3%) | 1 year (100%) | Licensed or developed |
| **Motor Vehicles (Q-plate)** | 3 years (33.3%) | N/A | Capped at S$35,000 OMV — §19(8) |
| **Motor Vehicles (S-plate)** | **Not claimable** | N/A | Private cars non-deductible — §15(1)(ba) |
| **Furniture & Fittings** | 3 years (33.3%) | 1 year (100%) option | |
| **Renovation & Refurbishment** | 3 years (33.3%) | N/A | Cap: S$300,000 per 3-year period |
| **Industrial Buildings** | Initial 25% + 3%/year | N/A | Factory, warehouse, dock |
| **Low-value assets (≤ S$5,000)** | 1 year (100%) | N/A | Automatic full write-off |

### 1.4 Motor Vehicle Restrictions

> **Legal Basis**: ITA §15(1)(ba); §19(8)

| Vehicle Type | Deductibility | Statutory Basis |
|---|---|---|
| S-plated cars (private passenger) | **Not deductible** at all | §15(1)(ba) |
| Q-plated cars (business, company cars) | Deductible up to S$35,000 (OMV) | §19(8) |
| Commercial vehicles (goods vehicles) | **Fully deductible** | |
| Motorcycles | Deductible | |
| Vehicle running expenses (S-plate) | **Not deductible** | §15(1)(ba) |
| Vehicle running expenses (Q-plate/commercial) | Deductible | |

### 1.5 Renovation & Refurbishment Deduction

> **Legal Basis**: ITA §14Q

| Parameter | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Cap** | S$300,000 per relevant 3-consecutive-year period |
| **Write-off** | Equal instalments over 3 years |
| **Qualifying expenses** | Renovation, refurbishment, general electrical installation, general lighting, flooring, wall, door, window, plumbing, sanitary fittings |
| **Non-qualifying** | Antiques, paintings, designer furniture, equipment |

### 1.6 IP Writing-Down Allowance

> **Legal Basis**: ITA §19B; §19B(1)

| Parameter | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Write-off period** | 5 years (or elected 10 years) |
| **Qualifying IP** | Patents, copyrights, trademarks, designs, plant varieties, registered know-how |
| **Approval** | Must be approved by EDB |
| **Annual rate** | 20% (5-year) or 10% (10-year) |

### 1.7 Land Intensification Allowance (LIA)

| Parameter | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Rate** | Initial 25% + annual 5% |
| **Qualifying** | Capital expenditure on constructing buildings on qualifying land |
| **Condition** | Must achieve specified plot ratio |

## 2. Business Operating Expenses

### 2.1 General Deductibility

> **Legal Basis**: ITA §14(1); §14(1)(a)

All deductions must satisfy the general rule under §14(1)(a): expenses must be **wholly and exclusively** incurred in the production of income.

| Expense Category | Deductibility | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Employee salaries & bonuses | **100%** | Must be for services rendered |
| CPF employer contributions | **100%** | Mandatory and voluntary (within limits) |
| **Meals (business purpose)** | **100%** | Must be incurred for business — §14(1)(a) |
| **Entertainment** | **100%** | No specific restriction; must be business-related |
| Insurance premiums | **100%** | For business risks |
| Professional fees (legal, accounting, tax) | **100%** | Must be revenue in nature |
| Advertising & marketing | **100%** | Must be for trade purposes |
| Training (employees) | **100%** | Job-related training |
| Travelling expenses | **100%** | For business purposes |
| Utilities | **100%** | For business premises |
| Bad debts (specific) | **100%** | Must be specifically identified and written off — §14(1)(d) |

### 2.2 Partially Deductible / Capped

| Expense Category | Limit | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Medical expenses (staff) | **2%** of total remuneration | Uncapped under approved Transferable Medical Insurance Scheme |
| Motor vehicle (S-plate) | **0%** | Completely non-deductible — §15(1)(ba) |
| Motor vehicle (Q-plate) | CA on S$35,000 OMV | Plus running expenses — §19(8) |
| Renovation | S$300,000 per 3-year period | §14Q |
| Startup costs | S$15,000 | §14U |

### 2.3 Charitable Donations

> **Legal Basis**: ITA §14(1)(s); §37B

| Parameter | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Deduction Multiplier** | **2.5x** of donation value — §14(1)(s) |
| **Effective Through** | 31 December 2026 |
| **Qualifying Recipient** | Institutions of a Public Character (IPCs) — §37B |
| **Carry-Forward** | 5 years |
| **No Annual Cap** | No limit as percentage of income (but must have sufficient income) |

### 2.4 Startup Expense Deduction

> **Legal Basis**: ITA §14U

| Parameter | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Cap** | S$15,000 in total |
| **Categories** | Company incorporation fees, accounting, tax advisory, secretarial services |
| **Period** | Incurred before date of incorporation to 1 year after |
| **Write-off** | Deducted in the first YA |

### 2.5 Employee Compensation

#### CPF Contributions (Employer)

| Parameter | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Deductible** | Yes, up to statutory rate |
| **Voluntary contributions** | Deductible up to 37% of ordinary wages (for employees ≤ 55) |
| **Foreign employees** | No CPF; but FWL is deductible as a business expense |

#### Stock Options / Equity Compensation

| Type | Deductibility |
|---|---|
| ESOP/ESOS granted | Deduction when employee exercises and gain is taxable |
| Treasury share transfers | Cost of acquisition deductible |
| Restricted Stock Units (RSUs) | Deduction when vested and employee taxed |

## 3. Interest & Financing Costs

### 3.1 General Rules

> **Legal Basis**: ITA §14(1)(a); §15(1)(c)

| Parameter | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Thin Capitalisation Rules** | **None** (no specific statutory DER or EBITDA limit) |
| **Deductibility Test** | Interest must be incurred on capital employed to produce assessable income — §14(1)(a) |
| **Arm's Length** | Related-party interest must be at arm's length rates — §34D |
| **Non-deductible** | Interest on loans to acquire non-income-producing assets — §15(1) |

### 3.2 Transfer Pricing on Interest

> **Legal Basis**: ITA §34D; §34E

- Related-party loans must bear an arm's length interest rate — §34D
- IRAS will apply transfer pricing adjustments if intercompany loan rates are not commercial — §34E (5% surcharge on TP adjustments)
- Safe harbour: Interest on ordinary trade credit not challenged if within normal trade terms
- No fixed debt-to-equity ratio imposed

## 4. Research & Development

Singapore offers generous R&D incentives through multiple schemes. The Enterprise Innovation Scheme (EIS), effective YA 2024–YA 2028, provides 400% enhanced deduction on the first S$400,000 per qualifying activity (including R&D). Base R&D conducted in Singapore qualifies for 250% deduction under ITA §14C(1), while overseas R&D receives a 100% base deduction.

See [R&D Incentives](rnd_incentives.md) for full program detail.

## 5. Loss Carry-Forward & Carry-Back

### 5.1 Carry-Forward

> **Legal Basis**: ITA §37(1); §37(16)

| Parameter | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Duration** | **Indefinite** carry-forward — §37(1) |
| **Annual Limit** | **No limit** (100% of assessable income) |
| **Shareholder Test** | Must satisfy the shareholders' test (substantial change in shareholding may forfeit losses) — §37(16) |
| **Same Business Test** | Not required under Singapore law |

### 5.2 Carry-Back

> **Legal Basis**: ITA §37(3)

| Parameter | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Duration** | **1 year** carry-back — §37(3) |
| **Cap** | S$100,000 per YA |
| **Election** | Must be made at time of filing Estimated Chargeable Income (ECI) |
| **Applies to** | Current year unabsorbed CA, trade losses, and donations |

### 5.3 Group Relief

> **Legal Basis**: ITA §37(12)

| Parameter | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Available** | Yes, for Singapore-incorporated companies in a group |
| **Qualifying items** | Current year unabsorbed CA, trade losses, and donations |
| **Shareholding requirement** | ≥ 75% direct or indirect ownership |
| **Cap** | Amount transferable is per transferor company |
| **Same accounting period** | Required (or overlap period) |

## 6. Provisions & Reserves

| Provision Type | Deductibility | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Specific bad debts written off | **Deductible** | Must be specifically identified — §14(1)(d) |
| General provisions | **Not deductible** | Must be specific and crystallised |
| Employee leave accruals | **Deductible** when obligation is crystallised | Based on contractual entitlement |
| Warranty provisions (general) | **Not deductible** | Until specific liability arises |
| Insurance reserves | Subject to industry-specific rules | For licensed insurers |

### Forex Gains/Losses

| Type | Treatment |
|---|---|
| Revenue nature forex gains | Taxable |
| Revenue nature forex losses | Deductible |
| Capital nature forex gains/losses | Non-taxable / non-deductible |

## 7. Industry-Specific Deductions

### 7.1 Approved Royalty Payments

- Royalties to non-residents: Deductible but subject to WHT (10% or treaty rate)
- Royalties must be on arm's length terms
- No specific cap on royalty deductions

### 7.2 Pioneer / Development & Expansion Incentives

Qualifying companies approved by EDB may enjoy full or partial tax exemptions under the Economic Expansion Incentives (Relief from Income Tax) Act. Deductions incurred during pioneer periods interact with the exempt income rules.

### 7.3 Working Capital Deductions

> **Legal Basis**: ITA §14C; §14D

| Parameter | Detail |
|---|---|
| **§14C — Approved R&D** | Deduction for revenue expenditure on R&D undertaken in Singapore (see Section 4) |
| **§14D — Approved Automation** | Deduction for expenses on approved projects for automation of manufacturing |
| **§14E — Approved Innovations** | Double deduction for expenses on approved innovation or productivity projects |

### 7.4 Finance & Treasury Centre (FTC) Incentive

Approved FTC companies enjoy a concessionary tax rate on qualifying income (typically 8%). Deductions are apportioned between FTC and non-FTC income.

### 7.5 Maritime Sector

Approved International Shipping Enterprise (AIS) income from qualifying shipping operations is tax-exempt under §13A ITA.

## 8. Treaties & Transfer Pricing

### 8.1 Transfer Pricing Documentation

> **Legal Basis**: ITA §34D, §34E, §34F; Income Tax (Transfer Pricing Documentation) Rules 2018

| Parameter | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Arm's Length Principle** | Mandatory for all related-party transactions — §34D |
| **Documentation** | Contemporaneous TP documentation required |
| **Master File** | Required if group revenue ≥ S$1.125 billion |
| **Local File** | Required for material intercompany transactions |
| **Country-by-Country Report** | Required if group revenue ≥ S$1.125 billion — §34F |
| **Penalty** | 5% surcharge on TP adjustments — §34E; S$10,000 fine for non-compliance |

### 8.2 Accepted Methods

1. Comparable Uncontrolled Price (CUP)
2. Resale Price Method (RPM)
3. Cost Plus Method (CPM)
4. Transactional Net Margin Method (TNMM)
5. Profit Split Method (PSM)

### 8.3 Advance Pricing Arrangements

- Unilateral, bilateral, and multilateral APAs available
- Typical duration: 3–5 years
- Rollback possible

### 8.4 Treaty Network

Singapore has over 90 comprehensive Avoidance of Double Taxation Agreements (DTAs), providing reduced withholding tax rates and mutual agreement procedures.

| S-plated car expenses (all) | Statutory prohibition | §15(1)(ba) |
| Income tax paid | Not a business expense | §15(1) |
| Provisions (general) | Must be specific and crystallised | |

| Donations to non-IPCs | Not qualifying | |
