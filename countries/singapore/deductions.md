# Singapore - Taxable Income Deductions

## 1. Capital Allowances (Depreciation) — ITA Sections 19, 19A, 19B

Singapore does not use the term "depreciation" for tax purposes. Instead, businesses claim **Capital Allowances (CA)** on qualifying capital expenditure on plant and machinery used for trade. The general deductibility of expenses is governed by **ITA Section 14(1)** — deductions allowed against income, specifically **ITA Section 14(1)(a)** which allows deduction of expenses **wholly and exclusively** incurred in the production of income.

### 1.1 Section 19 - Standard 3-Year Write-Off — ITA Section 19; ITA Section 19(2)

| Parameter | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Method** | Straight-line over 3 years — **ITA Section 19** |
| **Annual Rate** | 33.33% per year — annual allowance calculation per **ITA Section 19(2)** |
| **Applies to** | All qualifying plant and machinery |
| **No election required** | Default method |

### 1.2 Section 19A - Accelerated Write-Off — ITA Section 19A(1); ITA Section 19A(2); ITA Section 19A(10A)

Taxpayer may **elect** one of the following under **ITA Section 19A(1)** (accelerated CA at 1-year or prescribed working life):

| Option | Detail | Statutory Basis |
|---|---|---|
| **1-Year Write-Off** | 100% in year of acquisition | **ITA Section 19A(10A)** (100% write-off in year of acquisition) |
| **2-Year Write-Off** | 75% in year 1, 25% in year 2 (for specified automation equipment) | **ITA Section 19A(1)** |
| **Prescribed Working Life** | 6-16 years depending on asset type | **ITA Section 19A(1)** |

**Note:** The election under **ITA Section 19A(2)** , once made, is **irrevocable** for that asset.

### 1.3 Key Asset Depreciation Rates

| Asset Type | Standard (S.19) | Accelerated (S.19A) | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Plant & Machinery** | 3 years (33.3%) | 1 year (100%) | Most common claim |
| **Computers & IT Equipment** | 3 years (33.3%) | 1 year (100%) | Including peripherals |
| **Software** | 3 years (33.3%) | 1 year (100%) | Licensed or developed |
| **Motor Vehicles (Q-plate)** | 3 years (33.3%) | N/A | Capped at S$35,000 OMV — **ITA Section 19(8)** |
| **Motor Vehicles (S-plate)** | **Not claimable** | N/A | Private cars non-deductible — **ITA Section 15(1)(ba)** |
| **Furniture & Fittings** | 3 years (33.3%) | 1 year (100%) option | |
| **Renovation & Refurbishment** | 3 years (33.3%) | N/A | Cap: S$300,000 per 3-year period |
| **Industrial Buildings** | Initial 25% + 3%/year | N/A | Factory, warehouse, dock |
| **Low-value assets (≤S$5,000)** | 1 year (100%) | N/A | Automatic full write-off |

### 1.4 Motor Vehicle Restrictions — ITA Section 15(1)(ba); ITA Section 19(8)

| Vehicle Type | Deductibility | Statutory Basis |
|---|---|---|
| S-plated cars (private passenger) | **Not deductible** at all | **ITA Section 15(1)(ba)** (S-plated motor car expenses non-deductible) |
| Q-plated cars (business, company cars) | Deductible up to S$35,000 (OMV) | **ITA Section 19(8)** (motor vehicle CA restricted to S$35,000 OMV cap for Q-plate) |
| Commercial vehicles (goods vehicles) | **Fully deductible** | |
| Motorcycles | Deductible | |
| Vehicle running expenses (S-plate) | **Not deductible** | **ITA Section 15(1)(ba)** |
| Vehicle running expenses (Q-plate/commercial) | Deductible | |

### 1.5 Renovation & Refurbishment Deduction — ITA Section 14Q

| Parameter | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Cap** | S$300,000 per relevant 3-consecutive-year period — **ITA Section 14Q** |
| **Write-off** | Equal instalments over 3 years |
| **Qualifying expenses** | Renovation, refurbishment, general electrical installation, general lighting, flooring, wall, door, window, plumbing, sanitary fittings |
| **Non-qualifying** | Antiques, paintings, designer furniture, equipment |

## 2. Net Operating Losses (NOL / Unabsorbed Losses) — ITA Section 37

### 2.1 Carry-Forward — ITA Section 37(1); ITA Section 37(16)

| Parameter | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Duration** | **Indefinite** carry-forward — **ITA Section 37(1)** (carry-forward of unabsorbed losses, indefinite) |
| **Annual Limit** | **No limit** (100% of assessable income) |
| **Shareholder Test** | Must satisfy the shareholders' test (substantial change in shareholding may forfeit losses) — **ITA Section 37(16)** (shareholder continuity test for loss carry-forward) |
| **Same Business Test** | Not required under Singapore law |

### 2.2 Carry-Back — ITA Section 37(3)

| Parameter | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Duration** | **1 year** carry-back — **ITA Section 37(3)** (loss carry-back relief, 1 year) |
| **Cap** | S$100,000 per YA — **ITA Section 37(3)** |
| **Election** | Must be made at time of filing ECI |
| **Applies to** | Current year unabsorbed CA, trade losses, and donations |

### 2.3 Group Relief — ITA Section 37(12)

| Parameter | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Available** | Yes, for Singapore-incorporated companies in a group — **ITA Section 37(12)** (group relief for losses) |
| **Qualifying items** | Current year unabsorbed CA, trade losses, and donations |
| **Shareholding requirement** | ≥ 75% direct or indirect ownership |
| **Cap** | Amount transferable is per transferor company |
| **Same accounting period** | Required (or overlap period) |

## 3. Interest Deductibility — ITA Section 14(1)(a); ITA Section 15(1)(c)

### 3.1 General Rules

| Parameter | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Thin Capitalization Rules** | **None** (no specific statutory DER or EBITDA limit) |
| **Deductibility Test** | Interest must be incurred on capital employed to produce assessable income — **ITA Section 14(1)(a)** (wholly and exclusively in the production of income) |
| **Arm's Length** | Related-party interest must be at arm's length rates — **ITA Section 34D** |
| **Non-deductible** | Interest on loans to acquire non-income-producing assets — **ITA Section 15(1)** |

### 3.2 Transfer Pricing on Interest — ITA Section 34D

- Related-party loans must bear an arm's length interest rate — **ITA Section 34D** (arm's length principle)
- IRAS will apply transfer pricing adjustments if intercompany loan rates are not commercial — **ITA Section 34E** (5% surcharge on TP adjustments)
- Safe harbour: Interest on ordinary trade credit not challenged if within normal trade terms
- No fixed debt-to-equity ratio imposed

## 4. R&D Tax Incentives

### 4.1 Section 14C - R&D Deduction (Enhanced) — ITA Section 14C(1); ITA Section 14C(2)

| Parameter | Detail |
|---|---|
| **R&D in Singapore** | **250% deduction** on qualifying R&D expenditure — **ITA Section 14C(1)** (enhanced R&D deduction) |
| **R&D outside Singapore** | **100% deduction** (base only) |
| **Qualifying expenditure** | Staff costs, consumables, outsourced R&D payments |
| **Condition** | R&D must be related to the company's trade or business — **ITA Section 14C(2)** (conditions for enhanced R&D deduction) |

### 4.2 Enterprise Innovation Scheme (EIS) - From YA 2024 — ITA Section 14D; Income Tax (Enterprise Innovation Scheme) Rules 2023

| Parameter | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Enhanced deduction** | **400%** on first S$400,000 per qualifying activity — **Income Tax (Enterprise Innovation Scheme) Rules 2023**; enhanced deductions for approved activities under **ITA Section 14D** |
| **Qualifying activities** | R&D, IP registration, training, innovation projects, licensing of IP |
| **Cap per activity** | S$400,000 of expenditure per YA |
| **Cash payout option** | 20% of up to S$100,000 qualifying expenditure (max S$20,000 payout) |
| **Cash payout eligibility** | Companies with ≤ S$100M revenue and ≥ 3 local employees |
| **Effective period** | YA 2024 to YA 2028 |

### 4.3 IP Writing-Down Allowance — ITA Section 19B; ITA Section 19B(1)

| Parameter | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Write-off period** | 5 years (or elected 10 years) — **ITA Section 19B** |
| **Qualifying IP** | Patents, copyrights, trademarks, designs, plant varieties, registered know-how — **ITA Section 19B(1)** (IP WDA qualifying expenditure) |
| **Approval** | Must be approved by EDB |
| **Annual rate** | 20% (5-year) or 10% (10-year) |

### 4.4 IP Registration - Enhanced Deduction

| Parameter | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Deduction** | 200% on first S$100,000 of qualifying IP registration costs |
| **Under EIS** | 400% on first S$400,000 |

## 5. Charitable Donations — ITA Section 14(1)(s); ITA Section 37B

| Parameter | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Deduction Multiplier** | **2.5x** of donation value — **ITA Section 14(1)(s)** (donations to IPCs) |
| **Effective Through** | 31 December 2026 |
| **Qualifying Recipient** | Institutions of a Public Character (IPCs) — **ITA Section 37B** (approved donations) |
| **Carry-Forward** | 5 years |
| **No Annual Cap** | No limit as percentage of income (but must have sufficient income) |

**Example:** A S$10,000 donation qualifies for S$25,000 deduction.

## 6. Business Expenses - General Deductibility — ITA Section 14(1); ITA Section 15(1)

All deductions must satisfy the general rule under **ITA Section 14(1)(a)**: expenses must be **wholly and exclusively** incurred in the production of income. Non-deductible items are specified in **ITA Section 15(1)**.

### 6.1 Fully Deductible Expenses — ITA Section 14(1)(a)

| Expense Category | Deductibility | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| **Meals (business purpose)** | **100%** | Must be incurred for business — **ITA Section 14(1)(a)** |
| **Entertainment** | **100%** | No specific restriction; must be business-related |
| **Employee salaries & bonuses** | 100% | Must be for services rendered |
| **CPF employer contributions** | 100% | Mandatory and voluntary (within limits) |
| **Insurance premiums** | 100% | For business risks |
| **Professional fees** | 100% | Legal, accounting, tax advisory |
| **Advertising & marketing** | 100% | Must be for trade purposes |
| **Training (employees)** | 100% | Job-related training |
| **Traveling expenses** | 100% | For business purposes |
| **Utilities** | 100% | For business premises |
| **Bad debts (specific)** | 100% | Must be specifically identified and written off — **ITA Section 14(1)(d)** (bad debt deduction) |

### 6.2 Partially Deductible / Capped

| Expense Category | Limit | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| **Medical expenses (staff)** | 2% of total remuneration | Uncapped under approved Transferable Medical Insurance Scheme |
| **Motor vehicle (S-plate)** | **0%** | Completely non-deductible — **ITA Section 15(1)(ba)** |
| **Motor vehicle (Q-plate)** | CA on S$35,000 OMV | Plus running expenses — **ITA Section 19(8)** |
| **Renovation** | S$300,000 per 3-year period | **ITA Section 14Q** |
| **Startup costs** | S$15,000 | **ITA Section 14U** |

### 6.3 Non-Deductible Expenses — ITA Section 15(1)

| Expense | Reason | Statutory Basis |
|---|---|---|
| Private/domestic expenses | Not incurred for trade | **ITA Section 15(1)(a)** (domestic/private expenses) |
| Capital expenditure (non-qualifying) | Must claim CA instead if applicable | **ITA Section 15(1)(c)** (capital expenditure, unless CA applies) |
| S-plated car expenses (all) | Statutory prohibition | **ITA Section 15(1)(ba)** (S-plated motor car expenses non-deductible) |
| Income tax paid | Not a business expense | **ITA Section 15(1)** |
| Provisions (general) | Must be specific and crystallised | |
| Fines and penalties | Not incurred for trade | |
| Donations to non-IPCs | Not qualifying | |
| Expenses to produce exempt income | No deduction for non-taxable income expenses | |
| Pre-incorporation expenses | Except under **ITA Section 14U** (S$15,000 cap) | **ITA Section 14U** |

## 9. Transfer Pricing Documentation — ITA Sections 34D, 34E, 34F; Income Tax (Transfer Pricing Documentation) Rules 2018

### 9.1 Requirements

| Parameter | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Arm's Length Principle** | Mandatory for all related-party transactions — **ITA Section 34D** (transfer pricing, arm's length) |
| **Documentation** | Contemporaneous TP documentation required — **Income Tax (Transfer Pricing Documentation) Rules 2018** |
| **Master File** | Required if group revenue ≥ S$1.125B |
| **Local File** | Required for material intercompany transactions |
| **Country-by-Country Report** | Required if group revenue ≥ S$1.125B — **ITA Section 34F** (country-by-country reporting) |
| **Penalty** | 5% surcharge on TP adjustments — **ITA Section 34E** (surcharge on transfer pricing adjustments); S$10,000 fine for non-compliance |

### 9.2 Accepted Methods

1. Comparable Uncontrolled Price (CUP)
2. Resale Price Method (RPM)
3. Cost Plus Method (CPM)
4. Transactional Net Margin Method (TNMM)
5. Profit Split Method (PSM)

### 9.3 Advance Pricing Arrangements

- Unilateral, bilateral, and multilateral APAs available
- Typical duration: 3-5 years
- Rollback possible

## 10. Miscellaneous Deductions

### 10.1 Land Intensification Allowance (LIA)

| Parameter | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Rate** | Initial 25% + annual 5% |
| **Qualifying** | Qualifying capital expenditure on constructing buildings on qualifying land |
| **Condition** | Must achieve specified plot ratio |

### 10.2 Approved Royalty Payments

- Royalties to non-residents: Deductible but subject to WHT (10% or treaty rate)
- Royalties must be on arm's length terms
- No specific cap on royalty deductions

### 10.3 Forex Gains/Losses

| Type | Treatment |
|---|---|
| Revenue nature forex gains | Taxable |
| Revenue nature forex losses | Deductible |
| Capital nature forex gains/losses | Non-taxable / non-deductible |

### 10.4 Intellectual Property Amortization — ITA Section 19B; ITA Section 19B(1)

| Parameter | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Section 19B** | Writing-down allowance on approved IP — **ITA Section 19B** |
| **Qualifying expenditure** | Capital expenditure on acquisition of qualifying IP rights — **ITA Section 19B(1)** (IP WDA qualifying expenditure) |
| **Period** | 5 or 10 years (taxpayer election) |
| **Qualifying IP** | Patents, copyrights, trademarks, designs, plant varieties |
| **EDB Approval** | Required for IP in-licensing from related party |
