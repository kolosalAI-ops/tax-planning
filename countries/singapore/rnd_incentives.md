# Singapore - R&D Tax Incentives

## 1. Overview

| Feature | Details |
|---|---|
| **Primary Incentive** | Enterprise Innovation Scheme (EIS) - 400% deduction |
| **Secondary Incentive** | Enhanced R&D Deduction (S.14C) - 250% deduction |
| **IP Regime** | Writing-Down Allowance (WDA) under ITA §19B |
| **Cash Payout Option** | 20% of up to S$100,000 qualifying expenditure for SMEs |
| **Governing Authority** | Inland Revenue Authority of Singapore (IRAS); Economic Development Board (EDB) |
| **Key Legislation** | Income Tax Act (ITA) §14C, §14D, §14E, §19B; EIS Rules 2023 |
| **Applicable Years** | YA2024 - YA2028 |
| **Corporate Tax Rate** | 17% (headline) |

## 2. Detailed Programs

### 2.1 Enterprise Innovation Scheme (EIS) - ITA §14D

**Legal Basis:** Income Tax Act §14D, Enterprise Innovation Scheme Rules 2023

The EIS was introduced in Budget 2023 as Singapore's flagship innovation incentive, replacing and expanding upon previous schemes.

| Parameter | Details |
|---|---|
| **Deduction Rate** | 400% on first S$400,000 of qualifying R&D expenditure |
| **Excess Expenditure** | 100% deduction for amounts above S$400,000 |
| **Eligible Activities** | R&D projects seeking scientific or technological advancement |
| **Cash Conversion** | Option to convert up to S$100,000 of qualifying expenditure into non-taxable cash payout at 20% |
| **Validity** | YA2024 to YA2028 |
| **Combined Cap** | S$400,000 across all qualifying EIS activities per YA |

**Qualifying Conditions:**
- R&D must be carried out in Singapore (for 400% rate)
- Must have at least 3 local employees with CPF contributions
- Company must not be dormant

**Qualifying Expenditure:**
- Staff costs directly engaged in R&D
- Consumable materials
- Payments to approved R&D organizations
- Costs of prototyping and testing

### 2.2 Enhanced R&D Deduction - ITA §14C

**Legal Basis:** Income Tax Act §14C

| Parameter | Details |
|---|---|
| **Deduction Rate** | 250% on qualifying R&D expenditure (for R&D conducted in Singapore) |
| **Overseas R&D** | 100% deduction under §14E |
| **Scope** | Broader than EIS; no expenditure cap for the 250% rate |
| **Approval** | Self-assessment; no prior approval required for §14C |

**Note:** Taxpayers may claim either EIS (§14D) or enhanced R&D deduction (§14C) but not both on the same expenditure.

### 2.3 Research Incentive Scheme for Companies (RISC)

**Legal Basis:** Administered by EDB

| Parameter | Details |
|---|---|
| **Benefit** | Co-funding of up to 50% of qualifying R&D project costs |
| **Target** | Companies performing R&D that will benefit Singapore's economy |
| **Approval** | Requires EDB application and project approval |
| **Duration** | Typically 2-3 years per project |

### 2.4 SME Cash Payout Option

| Parameter | Details |
|---|---|
| **Conversion Rate** | 20% of qualifying expenditure |
| **Maximum Qualifying Expenditure** | S$100,000 per YA |
| **Maximum Cash Payout** | S$20,000 per YA |
| **Conditions** | Revenue ≤ S$100M or employment ≤ 200 employees |
| **Taxability** | Non-taxable |

## 3. Patent Box / IP Regime

### 3.1 IP Development Incentive (IDI)

**Legal Basis:** ITA §43X; Economic Expansion Incentives (Relief from Income Tax) Act

| Parameter | Details |
|---|---|
| **Concessionary Rate** | 5% or 10% on qualifying IP income (negotiated with EDB) |
| **Standard CIT Rate** | 17% |
| **Qualifying IP** | Patents, software copyrights, and other IP approved by EDB |
| **Nexus Requirement** | Modified nexus approach consistent with OECD BEPS Action 5 |
| **Minimum Commitment** | Significant economic activities in Singapore |

### 3.2 Writing-Down Allowance (WDA) for IP - ITA §19B

| Parameter | Details |
|---|---|
| **Allowance Period** | 5 years (accelerated) or 10 years |
| **Qualifying IP** | Patents, trademarks, copyrights, designs, plant varieties, layout designs |
| **Cap** | No statutory cap, but must be incurred for trade purposes |
| **Approval** | Prior approval from IRAS for §19B WDA |

## 4. Effective Benefit Analysis (per $1M USD R&D Spend)

*Assuming SGD/USD exchange rate of 1.35; S$1,350,000 equivalent spend; 17% CIT rate*

| Scenario | Calculation | Net Tax Benefit (SGD) | Net Tax Benefit (USD) |
|---|---|---|---|
| **EIS (400% on first S$400k)** | (S$400k × 400% + S$950k × 100%) × 17% | S$433,500 | ~$321,100 |
| **§14C Enhanced (250%)** | S$1,350,000 × 250% × 17% | S$573,750 | ~$425,000 |
| **SME Cash Payout** | S$100,000 × 20% (additional) | S$20,000 | ~$14,800 |
| **IDI Patent Box** | Income × (17% - 5%) = 12% savings | Varies by IP income | Varies |
| **Combined (EIS + Cash Payout)** | S$433,500 + S$20,000 | S$453,500 | ~$335,900 |

**Key Insight:** The §14C enhanced deduction at 250% on the full amount may yield a higher benefit than EIS when R&D expenditure significantly exceeds S$400,000. Companies should model both scenarios.

## 5. Claim Process & Documentation

### 5.1 Filing Process

| Step | Action | Timeline |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Identify qualifying R&D expenditure | Ongoing during financial year |
| 2 | Determine optimal scheme (EIS vs §14C) | Before filing |
| 3 | File Form C/C-S with IRAS | Within 30 Nov of following YA |
| 4 | Submit R&D claim form (Appendix) | With tax return |
| 5 | EDB approval (if required for RISC/IDI) | Prior to project commencement |

### 5.2 Documentation Requirements

- **Project documentation:** Description of R&D activities, objectives, scientific/technological uncertainties
- **Financial records:** Detailed breakdown of qualifying expenditure by project
- **Staff records:** Time sheets for personnel involved in R&D
- **Contemporaneous records:** Must be maintained at the time expenditure is incurred
- **EDB correspondence:** Approval letters for schemes requiring EDB sign-off

### 5.3 Audit Considerations

- IRAS may request a technical review of R&D claims
- Record retention: 5 years from the relevant YA
- Transfer pricing documentation required for related-party R&D arrangements

## 6. Recent Changes & Sunset Provisions

| Change | Effective Date | Details |
|---|---|---|
| **EIS Introduction** | YA2024 | Replaced Productivity & Innovation Credit (PIC); 400% deduction |
| **EIS Validity Period** | YA2024 - YA2028 | 5-year scheme; review expected in Budget 2028 |
| **§14C Enhancement** | Ongoing | 250% rate currently has no sunset; subject to annual Budget review |
| **IP WDA Expansion** | YA2017 onwards | Expanded list of qualifying IP assets |
| **IDI Refinement** | 2018 onwards | Aligned with OECD BEPS modified nexus approach |

**Upcoming Considerations:**
- Budget 2026 may announce adjustments to EIS parameters
- OECD Pillar Two (15% global minimum tax) effective from 2025 may impact IDI concessionary rates
- Singapore has introduced Domestic Top-Up Tax (DTT) from YA2025 for MNEs in scope of Pillar Two
