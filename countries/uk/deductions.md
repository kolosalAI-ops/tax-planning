# United Kingdom - Taxable Income Deductions

> **Jurisdiction**: National (HMRC)
> **Governing Law**: Corporation Tax Act 2009 (CTA 2009); Corporation Tax Act 2010 (CTA 2010); Capital Allowances Act 2001 (CAA 2001); Taxation (International and Other Provisions) Act 2010 (TIOPA 2010)
> **Tax Year**: 2024/2025
> **Currency**: GBP (£)

---

## 1. Depreciation & Capital Allowances

UK corporation tax does not permit book depreciation as a deduction. Instead, capital allowances under CAA 2001 provide tax relief on qualifying capital expenditure.

### Annual Investment Allowance (AIA) — CAA 2001 §51A

| Parameter | Value |
|---|---|
| Maximum deduction | **£1,000,000** per annum |
| Eligible assets | Plant and machinery (including fixtures, equipment, commercial vehicles) |
| Not eligible | Cars, land, buildings (structure) |
| Shared limit | Associated companies share the £1M AIA |

### Full Expensing — CAA 2001 §45DA (from April 2023)

| Parameter | Value |
|---|---|
| Rate | **100%** first-year allowance |
| Eligible assets | New (unused) main rate plant and machinery |
| 50% first-year allowance | New special rate (long-life, integral features) assets |
| Effective from | 1 April 2023 (permanent from Autumn Statement 2023) |
| Exclusions | Cars, assets for leasing, second-hand assets |

### Writing Down Allowances (WDA) — CAA 2001 §56

| Pool | WDA Rate | Method | Typical Assets |
|---|---|---|---|
| Main rate pool | **18%** | Reducing balance | General plant, machinery, equipment, cars with CO₂ ≤50 g/km |
| Special rate pool | **6%** | Reducing balance | Long-life assets (>25 years), integral features, thermal insulation, cars with CO₂ >50 g/km |
| Single asset pools | **18% or 6%** | Reducing balance | Short-life assets (election), cars with private use |

### Structures and Buildings Allowance (SBA) — CAA 2001 §270AA

| Parameter | Value |
|---|---|
| Rate | **3%** straight-line |
| Period | Over 33⅓ years |
| Eligible | New commercial structures and buildings (construction or renovation contracts entered into on or after 29 October 2018) |
| Land | Not eligible |

### First-Year Allowances — Special Categories

| Category | Rate | Statutory Basis |
|---|---|---|
| Zero-emission cars | **100%** | CAA 2001 §45DA |
| Zero-emission goods vehicles | **100%** | CAA 2001 §45DA |
| Electric vehicle charge points | **100%** | CAA 2001 §45EA (until 31 March 2025) |
| Energy-saving / water-saving plant | **100%** (ended 31 March 2020) | CAA 2001 §45A/45H (historical) |

## 2. Business Operating Expenses

### General Deductibility Rule — CTA 2009 §54

Expenditure is deductible only if incurred **"wholly and exclusively"** for the purposes of the trade. Mixed-purpose expenditure must be apportioned where a identifiable trade element exists.

### Key Deductible Business Expenses

| Expense Category | Deductibility | Statutory Reference |
|---|---|---|
| Employee salaries and wages | Fully deductible if reasonable | CTA 2009 §54 |
| Employer NIC contributions | Fully deductible | CTA 2009 §54 |
| Employer pension contributions | Deductible when paid (spreading for large one-off contributions) | CTA 2009 §196 |
| Rent and business rates | Fully deductible | CTA 2009 §54 |
| Professional fees (legal, accounting) | Deductible if revenue nature | CTA 2009 §54 |
| Repairs and maintenance | Deductible (must be revenue, not improvement) | CTA 2009 §54; BIM46900 |
| Bad debts | Deductible when specifically provided for or written off | CTA 2009 §55 |
| Insurance premiums | Fully deductible | CTA 2009 §54 |
| Travel and subsistence | Deductible if wholly and exclusively for trade | CTA 2009 §54; BIM37600 |
| Staff training | Fully deductible | CTA 2009 §54 |
| Pre-trading expenses | Deductible if incurred within 7 years before trading commences | CTA 2009 §61 |

### Charitable Donations — CTA 2010 §189

| Parameter | Value |
|---|---|
| Qualifying donations | Payments to registered charities and CASCs |
| Treatment | Deducted as a charge on income (paid gross) |
| Limit | Cannot create or augment a loss |
| Gift Aid | Corporation tax relief at marginal rate |

## 3. Interest & Financing Costs

### Loan Relationships — CTA 2009 Part 5 (§292–§476)

All interest income and expense of a company is accounted for under the loan relationship rules. Debits (interest expense) and credits (interest income) are computed on an accounting basis (generally GAAP/IFRS).

| Parameter | Detail |
|---|---|
| Basis | Generally follows accounting treatment |
| Trading debits | Deducted against trading profits — CTA 2009 §297 |
| Non-trading debits | Pooled as non-trading deficit — CTA 2009 §301; may offset total profits, carry back 1 year, or carry forward |
| Connected party rules | Late-paid interest to connected persons — deductible only when actually paid — CTA 2009 §373 |

### Corporate Interest Restriction (CIR) — TIOPA 2010 Part 10

| Parameter | Value |
|---|---|
| Fixed ratio limit | **30%** of tax-EBITDA |
| De minimis | **£2,000,000** net interest deduction (group-wide) |
| Group ratio rule | Alternative: proportion of group's net external interest expense to group EBITDA |
| Carry-forward | Disallowed amounts may be carried forward and reactivated in future periods (subject to capacity) |
| Interest reactivation cap | **£2M** de minimis plus 30% of excess tax-EBITDA |
| Reporting | Full CIR return required if net interest exceeds £2M |

### Late-Paid Interest — CTA 2009 §373

Interest payable to connected companies not paid within 12 months of the period end is non-deductible until actually paid.

## 4. Research & Development

The UK operates a merged R&D expenditure credit scheme (effective from 1 April 2024) replacing the prior separate SME and RDEC regimes. The merged scheme provides an above-the-line taxable credit on qualifying R&D expenditure. An enhanced scheme with higher rates is available for R&D-intensive loss-making SMEs.

See [R&D Incentives](rnd_incentives.md) for full program detail.

## 5. Loss Carry-Forward & Carry-Back

### Trading Losses — CTA 2010 §37, §45

| Parameter | Value |
|---|---|
| Current year offset | Against total profits of the same accounting period — CTA 2010 §37 |
| Carry-back | **1 year** (same-length preceding period) — CTA 2010 §37(3)(b) |
| Extended carry-back | 3 years for losses in accounting periods ending 1 April 2020–31 March 2022 (COVID measure, now expired) |
| Carry-forward | **Indefinite** — CTA 2010 §45 |

### Loss Restriction — CTA 2010 §269ZA–§269ZF (from 1 April 2017)

| Parameter | Value |
|---|---|
| Deductions allowance | **£5,000,000** (group-wide) |
| Restriction | Carried-forward losses above the £5M allowance limited to **50%** of remaining profits |
| Applies to | Trading losses, non-trading loan relationship deficits, management expenses, UK property losses carried forward |
| Does not apply | Current-year losses or 1-year carry-back claims |

### Capital Losses — TCGA 1992 §8

| Parameter | Value |
|---|---|
| Carry-forward | Indefinite |
| Carry-back | Not permitted (except terminal loss on cessation) |
| Restriction | Cannot offset against income profits; only against capital gains |

## 6. Provisions & Reserves

| Provision Type | Deductibility | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Specific bad debt provisions | Deductible | Must relate to specific identified debts — CTA 2009 §55 |
| General bad debt provisions | **Not deductible** | Too uncertain — BIM42700 |
| Accounting provisions (IAS 37) | Deductible only if meet "wholly and exclusively" test and represent actual liability | Must meet revenue recognition timing — CTA 2009 §54 |
| Warranty provisions | Generally deductible | If specific and estimated with reasonable accuracy |
| Restructuring provisions | Generally **not deductible** until expenditure actually incurred | Timing — BIM46500 |
| Pension deficit contributions | Deductible when paid | CTA 2009 §196; spread if >210% of regular contributions |
| Decommissioning / environmental provisions | Deductible on accruals basis if specific statutory obligation exists | Site restoration costs — BIM46900 |

## 7. Industry-Specific Deductions

### Creative Industries Tax Reliefs

| Relief | Rate (Expenditure Credit) | Statutory Basis |
|---|---|---|
| Audio-Visual Expenditure Credit (AVEC) | **34%** for film, high-end TV, animation, children's TV | CTA 2009 Part 14A (from 1 January 2024) |
| Video Games Expenditure Credit (VGEC) | **34%** | CTA 2009 Part 14B |
| Theatrical Productions | **45%** (touring) / **30%** (non-touring) | CTA 2009 Part 15C [unverified — confirm with local counsel] |
| Orchestra / Museums & Galleries | Various rates | CTA 2009 Parts 15D–15E |

### Extractive Industries — CAA 2001 Part 5

| Parameter | Value |
|---|---|
| Mineral extraction allowance | 10% or 25% reducing balance depending on asset type |
| Ring fence trades | Oil & gas profits ring-fenced; supplementary charge at 35% (Energy Profits Levy applies 2023–2028) |

### Real Estate Investment Trusts (REITs)

| Parameter | Value |
|---|---|
| Property rental profits | Exempt from corporation tax if 90% distributed |
| Conditions | At least 75% of assets/income from property rental business |
| Entry charge | None (abolished) |

### Financial Services

| Item | Treatment |
|---|---|
| Bank surcharge | Additional **3%** on profits over £100M (from 1 April 2023) — CTA 2010 §269DA |
| Bank loss restriction | Further 25% restriction on pre-2015 carried-forward losses |

## 8. Treaties & Transfer Pricing

### Transfer Pricing — TIOPA 2010 Part 4

| Parameter | Detail |
|---|---|
| Standard | Arm's-length principle — TIOPA 2010 §147 |
| Scope | All cross-border transactions and certain large domestic transactions |
| Documentation | Requirement to maintain contemporaneous transfer pricing documentation |
| OECD Guidelines | UK follows OECD Transfer Pricing Guidelines — TIOPA 2010 §164 |
| Country-by-Country Reporting | Required for MNE groups with consolidated revenue ≥€750M |
| Advance Pricing Agreements | Available through HMRC (unilateral, bilateral, multilateral) |

### SME Exemption

| Parameter | Value |
|---|---|
| Small enterprise exemption | Companies meeting the small company criteria (broadly <50 employees and either turnover <€10M or balance sheet <€10M) are exempt from TP rules |
| Medium enterprise exemption | Medium companies exempt unless HMRC issues a transfer pricing notice |

### Diverted Profits Tax (DPT) — Finance Act 2015 §77

| Parameter | Value |
|---|---|
| Rate | **25%** |
| Scope | Profits diverted from UK through entities/transactions lacking economic substance |
| Threshold | UK-related sales ≥£10M |
| Note | Being subsumed into Pillar Two framework from 2025 |

### Tax Treaties

The UK has one of the world's largest treaty networks with over **130** double taxation agreements. Standard WHT rates under treaties typically reduce to:

| Income Type | Treaty Rate (typical) |
|---|---|
| Dividends | 0–15% |
| Interest | 0–10% |
| Royalties | 0–10% |

## 9. Non-Deductible Items

The following expenses are **not deductible** for UK corporation tax purposes:

| Expense | Statutory Reference |
|---|---|
| Depreciation (accounting) | CAA 2001 — replaced by capital allowances |
| Entertainment of clients/customers | CTA 2009 §1298 |
| Staff entertainment (not wholly and exclusively for trade) | CTA 2009 §1298 (exception: annual staff event up to £150/head) |
| Capital expenditure (unless capital allowances apply) | CTA 2009 §53 |
| Fines and penalties (criminal/regulatory) | CTA 2009 §1303–1304 |
| Dividends paid | Not an expense; distribution of profits |
| UK corporation tax | Not deductible |
| Political donations | CTA 2009 §1298 |
| Provisions for future or contingent liabilities (general) | CTA 2009 §54; must be specific and incurred |
| Personal/non-trade expenses | CTA 2009 §54 — "wholly and exclusively" rule |
| Lease payments — land element | Restricted under CTA 2009 §54 |
| Car leasing — CO₂ restriction | 15% disallowance for cars with CO₂ >50 g/km — CAA 2001 §268B |
