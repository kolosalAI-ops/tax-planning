# United Kingdom - R&D Tax Incentives

## 1. Overview

| Feature | Details |
|---|---|
| **Headline Incentive** | R&D Expenditure Credit (RDEC) — Merged Scheme |
| **Type** | Above-the-line taxable credit |
| **Rate** | 20% of qualifying R&D expenditure |
| **Refundable** | Yes (net of tax, effective ~15% for profitable companies at 25% CT rate) |
| **Patent Box** | Yes — 10% effective corporation tax rate on qualifying IP profits |
| **Legal Basis** | Corporation Tax Act (CTA) 2009 Part 13; Finance Act 2023; CTA 2010 Part 8A (Patent Box) |

---

## 2. Detailed Programs

### 2.1 Merged R&D Expenditure Credit (RDEC) Scheme

**Legal Basis:** CTA 2009 Part 13 as amended by Finance Act 2023, effective 1 April 2024.

The merged RDEC scheme replaced both the former SME R&D Relief and the large company RDEC scheme from 1 April 2024. All companies now claim under a single unified mechanism.

| Parameter | Value |
|---|---|
| Credit Rate | 20% of qualifying R&D expenditure |
| Effective Benefit (25% CT payer) | ~15% net (20% credit less 25% CT on the credit) |
| Effective Benefit (19% CT payer) | ~16.2% net |
| Qualifying Expenditure | Staff costs, consumables, software, externally provided workers, subcontracted R&D, clinical trial volunteers |
| Subcontracted R&D | 65% of payment to unconnected parties qualifies |
| Overseas Restrictions | Externally provided workers and subcontracted activities must be UK-based unless conditions unavailable in the UK are met |
| Accounting Treatment | Above-the-line credit, recognized in operating profit |
| Cap on Payable Credit | PAYE/NIC cap applies (£20,000 + 300% of total PAYE/NIC liability) |

**Qualifying R&D Activities:**
- Must seek an advance in science or technology
- Must overcome scientific or technological uncertainty
- Cannot be readily deducible by a competent professional in the field
- Follows the BIS Guidelines (2004, updated 2023)

### 2.2 Enhanced R&D Intensive Support (ERIS)

**Legal Basis:** CTA 2009 Part 13 Chapter 1A, Finance Act 2023.

| Parameter | Value |
|---|---|
| Eligibility | Companies where qualifying R&D expenditure is ≥30% of total expenditure |
| Additional Deduction | 86% enhanced deduction on qualifying expenditure |
| Payable Credit Rate | 14.5% of surrenderable loss |
| Effective Benefit (loss-making R&D intensive) | Up to ~27% cash benefit |
| PAYE/NIC Cap | Applies (same formula as RDEC) |
| Threshold Test | Assessed on a rolling basis; intensity measured per period |

### 2.3 Former SME R&D Relief (Historical — Pre-April 2024)

**Legal Basis:** CTA 2009 Part 13 (pre-amendment). No longer available for periods starting on or after 1 April 2024.

| Parameter | Value |
|---|---|
| Enhanced Deduction | 86% (reduced from 130% from April 2023) |
| Payable Credit (loss-making) | 10% of surrenderable loss |
| SME Threshold | <500 employees, <€100M turnover or <€86M gross assets |

> **Note:** This scheme is fully abolished for accounting periods beginning on or after 1 April 2024. Transitional rules apply for straddling periods.

### 2.4 Claim Notification Requirement

**Legal Basis:** Finance Act 2023 Schedule 1.

| Requirement | Details |
|---|---|
| Who Must Notify | Companies claiming R&D relief for the first time, or those that have not claimed in the previous 3 accounting periods |
| Deadline | Within 6 months of the end of the accounting period |
| Method | Digital notification via HMRC online portal |
| Consequence of Failure | Claim will be invalid; no R&D relief for the period |

### 2.5 Additional Information Form (AIF)

| Requirement | Details |
|---|---|
| Mandatory From | 8 August 2023 (all claims) |
| Contents | Named senior officer, description of R&D projects, qualifying expenditure breakdown by category, agent details |
| Filing | Must be submitted before or at the same time as the CT600 |
| Consequence of Failure | Claim will not be processed |

## 3. Patent Box / IP Regime

**Legal Basis:** CTA 2010 Part 8A, introduced by Finance Act 2012, amended by Finance Act 2016 (nexus approach).

| Parameter | Value |
|---|---|
| Effective Tax Rate | 10% on qualifying IP profits (vs. standard 25% CT rate) |
| Qualifying IP | Patents granted by UK IPO, EPO, or specified EEA patent offices |
| Nexus Fraction | Qualifying expenditure / overall expenditure × qualifying IP profits |
| Qualifying Expenditure | In-house R&D, subcontracted R&D to unconnected parties (capped at 65%), externally provided workers |
| Streaming | Must calculate relevant IP income per IP right or product grouping |
| Election | Must elect into Patent Box; election applies from start of accounting period |
| Interaction with RDEC | Can claim both RDEC and Patent Box simultaneously on the same IP |

**Qualifying IP Income Includes:**
- Royalties and license fees
- Sales income attributable to patented products/processes
- Infringement income
- Damages and insurance proceeds relating to IP
- Notional royalties for in-house use of qualifying IP

**Routine Return Deductions:**
A routine return of 10% must be deducted from qualifying residual profit before applying the Patent Box rate.

## 4. Effective Benefit Analysis (per $1M USD R&D Spend)

*Assumes $1M USD ≈ £790,000 (illustrative rate); 25% CT rate.*

| Scenario | Mechanism | Gross Benefit | Tax on Credit | Net Cash Benefit | Effective Rate |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Profitable company (RDEC) | 20% credit | £158,000 | £39,500 (25% CT) | £118,500 | ~15.0% |
| Loss-making (non-intensive) | RDEC payable credit | £158,000 | £39,500 | £118,500 | ~15.0% |
| Loss-making (R&D intensive, ≥30%) | ERIS | £680,140 enhanced loss + 14.5% payable | — | ~£213,000 | ~27.0% |
| Patent Box (on £790k IP profit) | 10% effective rate | £118,500 saved (vs. 25%) | — | £118,500 | 15% saving |
| Combined RDEC + Patent Box | Both on same spend | ~£237,000 | — | ~£237,000 | ~30.0% |

## 5. Claim Process & Documentation

### 5.1 Filing Process

| Step | Action | Deadline |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Submit Claim Notification (if required) | 6 months after period end |
| 2 | Complete Additional Information Form (AIF) | Before or with CT600 |
| 3 | Calculate qualifying expenditure | — |
| 4 | Complete CT600 with R&D boxes (CT600L for RDEC) | 12 months after period end |
| 5 | Amend previous returns if needed | Within amendment window (12 months from filing deadline) |
| 6 | HMRC review/enquiry | Within 12 months of filing; up to 4 years if discovery assessment |

### 5.2 Documentation Requirements

- **Project descriptions** — Technical narratives explaining the baseline, advance sought, and uncertainties encountered
- **Competent professional test** — Evidence that the advance was not readily deducible
- **Expenditure records** — Detailed breakdown by category (staff, subcontractors, consumables, software, EPWs)
- **Time allocation records** — Staff time apportioned between qualifying R&D and non-R&D activities
- **Contemporaneous records** — HMRC expects records kept in real time, not retrospectively compiled
- **Technical reports, lab notebooks, project plans, test results**
- **Financial records** linking expenditure to specific R&D projects

### 5.3 HMRC Compliance & Enquiry

- HMRC has significantly increased R&D compliance activity since 2023
- Dedicated R&D compliance teams review claims
- Average enquiry takes 6–18 months
- Penalties apply for incorrect claims (up to 100% of overclaimed amount in cases of deliberate error)

## 6. Recent Changes & Sunset Provisions

| Change | Effective Date | Details |
|---|---|---|
| Merged RDEC scheme introduced | 1 April 2024 | Replaces separate SME and large company schemes |
| RDEC rate increased to 20% | 1 April 2023 | Up from 13% |
| SME additional deduction reduced to 86% | 1 April 2023 | Down from 130% |
| SME payable credit reduced to 10% | 1 April 2023 | Down from 14.5% |
| ERIS introduced | 1 April 2023 | For R&D intensive loss-making companies |
| Claim Notification requirement | 1 April 2023 | New pre-filing requirement |
| Additional Information Form (AIF) | 8 August 2023 | Mandatory for all claims |
| Overseas expenditure restrictions | 1 April 2024 | UK-nexus requirement for subcontracted R&D and EPWs |
| PAYE/NIC cap expanded | 1 April 2024 | Now applies to merged RDEC scheme |
| Grace period for R&D intensity | 1 April 2024 | One-year grace if intensity drops below 30% |

**Planned/Anticipated Changes:**
- HMRC continues to consult on further simplification
- Pre-approval pilot scheme under consideration
- Potential expansion of qualifying costs (e.g., datasets, cloud computing confirmed from April 2023)
- No sunset clause on RDEC or Patent Box; both are permanent features of UK tax law
