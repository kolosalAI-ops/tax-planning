# Saudi Arabia - Taxable Income Deductions

> **Jurisdiction**: Federal (Zakat, Tax and Customs Authority — ZATCA)
> **Governing Law**: Income Tax Law (Royal Decree No. M/1, 15/1/1425H — 2004); Implementing Regulations (Ministerial Resolution No. 1535 of 2004, as amended)
> **Tax Year**: 2024/2025
> **Currency**: SAR (﷼)

---

## 1. Depreciation & Capital Allowances

### General Framework (Art. 17, Income Tax Law; Art. 23, Implementing Regulations)

Depreciation is calculated using the **declining-balance method** applied to pools of assets grouped by category. Only 50% of the acquisition cost of an asset added during the year is included in the asset pool for that year; the remaining 50% is added in the following year. The same 50/50 rule applies to disposal proceeds.

| Asset Group | Description | Declining-Balance Rate |
|---|---|---|
| **Group 1** | Buildings (permanent, non-portable) | **5%** |
| **Group 2** | Portable buildings, machinery, equipment, vehicles, computer hardware and software | **25%** |
| **Group 3** | Geological surveying, drilling, exploration, and other preliminary work for natural resources | **20%** |
| **Group 4** | All other tangible and intangible depreciable assets (furniture, aircraft, ships, trains, goodwill) | **10%** |

> **Legal basis:** Art. 17(a) Income Tax Law; Art. 23 Implementing Regulations (ITR).

### Maintenance and Improvement Expenses (Art. 23, ITR)

| Parameter | Detail |
|---|---|
| Threshold | Enhancement and improvement expenses ≤ **4%** of the closing balance of the asset group are fully deductible as expenses |
| Excess | Amounts exceeding 4% are added to the asset group pool and depreciated at the group rate |

### Non-Depreciable Items

| Item | Treatment |
|---|---|
| Land | Not depreciable — Art. 17(a) ITR |
| Assets for resale (inventory) | Not depreciable; deducted as cost of goods sold |
| Assets that do not lose value over time | Not depreciable |

### Pre-Operating Expenditure

| Parameter | Detail |
|---|---|
| Treatment | Pre-operating costs incurred before the commencement of business are amortised on a straight-line basis over a reasonable period |
| Legal basis | Art. 17, Income Tax Law |

## 2. Business Operating Expenses

### General Deductibility (Art. 7 & Art. 12, Income Tax Law; Art. 5, ITR)

To be deductible, an expense must be:
1. Actually incurred during the tax year
2. Connected to the generation of taxable income
3. Properly documented
4. At arm's-length (for related-party transactions)

| Expense Category | Deductibility | Legal Basis |
|---|---|---|
| Salaries and wages | **100%** deductible (arm's-length for related parties) | Art. 7, Income Tax Law |
| Employee end-of-service benefits (funded) | Deductible if fund notified to ZATCA | Art. 12(c), Income Tax Law |
| Retirement / social insurance contributions | **100%** deductible (with ZATCA notification) | Art. 12(c), ITR |
| Medical insurance for employees | **100%** deductible | Art. 7, Income Tax Law |
| Rent and utilities | **100%** deductible | Art. 7 |
| Professional fees | **100%** deductible | Art. 7 |
| Travel and business expenses | **100%** deductible (must be reasonable and documented) | Art. 7 |
| Advertising and marketing | **100%** deductible | Art. 7 |
| Bad debts | Deductible when actually written off and proven uncollectible | Art. 12(a), Income Tax Law |
| Head office expenses (allocated to PE) | Deductible up to arm's-length allocation | Art. 7 |

### Zakat vs. Income Tax Distinction

| Taxpayer Category | Tax/Levy | Rate | Deduction Framework |
|---|---|---|---|
| Saudi/GCC nationals (individuals and entities) | Zakat | **2.5%** on Zakat base (approximate net worth) | Zakat Regulations (separate framework) |
| Foreign-owned entities | Income Tax | **20%** on adjusted profit | Income Tax Law deductions apply |
| Mixed ownership | Proportional Zakat + Income Tax | Pro-rata based on ownership shares | Respective rules apply to each portion |

## 3. Interest & Financing Costs

### Interest Deduction (Art. 12(b), Income Tax Law; Art. 7, ITR)

| Parameter | Detail |
|---|---|
| General rule | Interest on loans used for income-generating activities is deductible |
| Limitation formula | Deductible interest is the lower of: (a) actual loan charges incurred, or (b) loan income plus **50%** of taxable income excluding loan income |
| Carry-forward | Disallowed interest may be carried forward to subsequent years |

### Related-Party Debt

| Parameter | Detail |
|---|---|
| Arm's-length | Interest on related-party loans must be at arm's-length rates — Art. 13, Income Tax Law |
| Excess interest | Interest exceeding arm's-length rate is treated as a dividend and subject to withholding tax |

### Islamic Finance

| Parameter | Detail |
|---|---|
| Treatment | Profit-sharing payments under Sharia-compliant arrangements (Murabaha, Mudaraba, Ijara, Sukuk) are treated as economically equivalent to interest |
| Deductibility | Same rules and limitations as conventional interest apply |

> **Note:** The task instructions reference a 3:1 thin capitalisation ratio under Art. 11 ITR. Based on current research, the Saudi Income Tax Law does not prescribe a fixed debt-to-equity ratio but instead uses the formula-based limitation above. ## 4. Research & Development

R&D expenditure incurred during the tax year in connection with the generation of taxable income is fully deductible as an ordinary expense — Art. 12(d), Income Tax Law. There is no specific R&D tax credit or enhanced deduction in Saudi Arabia.

Capital expenditure on R&D assets is added to the relevant depreciation group and depreciated at the applicable declining-balance rate.

See [R&D Incentives](rnd_incentives.md) for full details on the R&D deduction framework, KACST grants, and Vision 2030 programs.

## 5. Loss Carry-Forward & Carry-Back

### Net Operating Losses (Art. 16, Income Tax Law; Art. 14, ITR)

| Parameter | Detail |
|---|---|
| Carry-forward | **Indefinite** (no time limit) — Art. 16, Income Tax Law |
| Annual utilisation cap | Maximum **25%** of annual taxable profit may be offset by brought-forward losses |
| Carry-back | **Not permitted** |
| Change of ownership | Losses may be forfeited upon a change of ownership exceeding 50% if there is also a change in the nature of the business [unverified — confirm specific ZATCA guidance] |

### Zakat Losses

| Parameter | Detail |
|---|---|
| Treatment | Losses are not relevant to the Zakat calculation (Zakat is based on net worth, not profit) |
| Impact | Saudi/GCC-owned businesses do not carry forward losses for Zakat purposes |

## 6. Provisions & Reserves

### General Principles (Art. 8, Income Tax Law; Art. 5, ITR)

| Item | Treatment |
|---|---|
| General provisions | **Not deductible** — only actual expenses are deductible |
| Contingency reserves | **Not deductible** |
| Bad debt provisions | **Not deductible** as a provision; deductible only when actually written off |
| Warranty provisions | **Not deductible** until payment is made |
| Employee end-of-service accruals | Deductible when paid or funded to an approved fund (with ZATCA notification) |
| Insurance reserves (regulated entities) | Subject to ZATCA regulations for insurance companies |

## 7. Industry-Specific Deductions

### Petroleum / Natural Gas Sector

| Parameter | Detail |
|---|---|
| Tax rate — oil | **50%–85%** (graduated based on internal rate of return under concession agreement) |
| Tax rate — natural gas | **20%** on adjusted net income |
| Depreciation | Separate rules under petroleum fiscal terms; geological/exploration costs: Group 3 at 20% declining balance |
| Deductions | Governed by individual concession agreements and the Income Tax Law provisions for hydrocarbons |
| Legal basis | Art. 4, Income Tax Law; Ministerial Resolutions for petroleum companies |

### Regional Headquarters (RHQ) Programme

| Parameter | Detail |
|---|---|
| Incentive | Companies establishing their regional HQ in Saudi Arabia may benefit from reduced withholding tax rates and other incentives |
| Condition | Effective from 1 January 2024, government contracts require an RHQ in Saudi Arabia |
| Deductions | Standard income tax deductions apply; operating expenses of the RHQ are fully deductible |

### Special Economic Zones

| Parameter | Detail |
|---|---|
| Zones | King Abdullah Economic City (KAEC), NEOM, Jazan City for Primary and Downstream Industries (JCPDI), Ras Al-Khair Special Economic Zone, Cloud Computing SEZ |
| Benefits | Competitive tax incentives including 0% income tax for up to 50 years (varying by zone), and customs duty exemptions [unverified — confirm specific zone terms with ZATCA] |

## 8. Treaties & Transfer Pricing

### Transfer Pricing (Art. 13, Income Tax Law; Art. 9, ITR)

| Parameter | Detail |
|---|---|
| Standard | Arm's-length principle — Art. 13, Income Tax Law |
| Related parties | Defined as parties with direct or indirect ownership ≥ 50%, or common control |
| Methods | CUP, resale price, cost plus, TNMM, profit split — aligned with OECD Guidelines |
| Documentation | Transfer pricing documentation required for related-party transactions |
| Country-by-Country Reporting | Required for MNE groups with consolidated revenue ≥ SAR 3.2B (~EUR 750M) |
| Penalties | Penalties for non-compliance with TP documentation requirements |
| APA | Advance Pricing Agreements available through ZATCA |

### Treaty Network

Saudi Arabia has over 50 bilateral double taxation treaties. Treaty benefits may reduce withholding tax rates on dividends (typically 5%–10%), interest, and royalties.

## 9. Non-Deductible Items

The following are **NOT deductible** under the Saudi Income Tax Law:

| Expense | Legal Basis |
|---|---|
| Income tax itself (Saudi or foreign) | Art. 8(a), Income Tax Law |
| Fines and penalties | Art. 8(b), Income Tax Law |
| Personal expenses of the business owner | Art. 8(c), Income Tax Law |
| Bribes and similar illicit payments | Art. 8(d), Income Tax Law |
| Undisclosed or undocumented expenses | Art. 8, Income Tax Law |
| Provisions and contingency reserves | Art. 8, Income Tax Law; Art. 5, ITR |
| Interest exceeding the limitation formula | Art. 12(b), Income Tax Law |
| Non-arm's-length portion of related-party expenses | Art. 13, Income Tax Law |
| Capital expenditure (unless depreciated through asset groups) | Art. 17, Income Tax Law |
| Dividends or profit distributions | Art. 8, Income Tax Law |
| Expenses not connected to generating taxable income | Art. 7, Income Tax Law |
