# Saudi Arabia - R&D Tax Incentives

## 1. Overview

| Feature | Details |
|---|---|
| **Primary Incentive** | No specific R&D tax credit or enhanced deduction |
| **CIT Rate (Foreign-Owned)** | 20% on foreign-owned entities |
| **Zakat Rate (Saudi/GCC-Owned)** | 2.5% on Zakat base (Saudi and GCC nationals) |
| **Mixed Ownership** | Pro-rata CIT and Zakat based on ownership shares |
| **R&D Expense Treatment** | Fully deductible as ordinary business expense |
| **Patent Box / IP Regime** | None |
| **Governing Law** | Income Tax Law (Royal Decree No. M/1, 2004); Zakat Collection Regulations |
| **Administering Body** | Zakat, Tax and Customs Authority (ZATCA) |
| **R&D Grants** | Available through KACST and Vision 2030 sectoral programs |
| **Withholding Tax on Royalties** | 15% (subject to treaty relief) |

## 2. Detailed Programs

### 2.1 Corporate Tax Framework

**Legal Basis:** Income Tax Law, issued by Royal Decree No. M/1 dated 15/1/1425H (2004); Implementing Regulations issued by Ministerial Resolution No. 1535 of 2004 (as amended)

| Parameter | Saudi/GCC Shareholders | Foreign Shareholders |
|---|---|---|
| **Tax Type** | Zakat | Corporate Income Tax |
| **Rate** | 2.5% on Zakat base | 20% on adjusted profit |
| **R&D Deduction** | 100% deductible expense | 100% deductible expense |
| **Loss Carry-Forward** | Not applicable for Zakat | Unlimited carry-forward (max 25% of annual profit) |
| **Loss Carry-Back** | Not available | Not available |
| **Capital Gains** | Included in Zakat base | 20% CIT |
| **Withholding Tax (WHT) on Royalties** | 15% | 15% (treaty may reduce) |

### 2.2 R&D Expense Deductibility

**Legal Basis:** Article 12 of the Income Tax Law; Articles 9–16 of the Implementing Regulations

| Eligible R&D Costs | Deductibility | Conditions |
|---|---|---|
| Employee salaries (R&D personnel) | 100% deductible | Must relate to business activities |
| Materials and consumables | 100% deductible | Consumed in R&D process |
| Subcontractor R&D payments | 100% deductible | Arm's length pricing required |
| Equipment and machinery | Depreciated per standard rules | 25% declining balance for machinery |
| Software and IT costs | 100% deductible (revenue) or amortized | Depends on classification |
| Overhead allocation | 100% deductible | Reasonable and proportionate |
| Patent and IP registration | 100% deductible | Revenue expenditure |
| R&D building costs | Depreciated at standard rates | 5% straight-line for buildings |

**Conditions for Deductibility (Article 12):**
- Expenditure must be incurred in the production of income
- Must be actual, documented, and reasonable in amount
- Capital expenditure must be depreciated under standard rules
- Related-party transactions subject to transfer pricing rules (Article 63)

### 2.3 Special Economic Zones and Incentive Programs

**Legal Basis:** Special Economic Zones Regulations (2021); various sector-specific royal decrees

Saudi Arabia has introduced special economic zones with potential tax benefits:

| Zone / Program | Tax Incentive | R&D Relevance |
|---|---|---|
| **NEOM** | 0% CIT for up to 50 years (announced) | Advanced technology, biotech, energy |
| **King Abdullah Economic City (KAEC)** | Potential reduced CIT (negotiable) | Manufacturing, logistics R&D |
| **Ras Al-Khair Special Zone** | Mining and industrial incentives | Materials science, mining technology |
| **Jazan City for Primary and Downstream Industries** | Industrial incentives | Energy and chemical R&D |
| **Special Integrated Logistics Zone (SILZ) — Riyadh** | 5% CIT, 0% WHT, 0% personal income tax for 50 years | Technology, logistics |
| **Cloud Computing Special Zone** | Incentives under development | Cloud and AI R&D |

> **Note:** SEZ incentives are typically negotiated on a case-by-case basis and may require minimum investment and employment commitments. Not all zones have finalized their regulatory frameworks.

### 2.4 Government R&D Grants and Vision 2030 Programs

While not tax incentives, Saudi Arabia offers significant government-funded R&D support:

| Program | Administering Body | Details |
|---|---|---|
| **KACST (King Abdulaziz City for Science & Technology)** | KACST | National R&D grants; technology transfer programs; lab access |
| **Saudi Technology Development and Investment Company (TAQNIA)** | PIF subsidiary | Technology commercialization and investment |
| **National Industrial Development and Logistics Program (NIDLP)** | Vision 2030 | Industrial R&D support; 4IR technology adoption |
| **Saudi Data and AI Authority (SDAIA)** | SDAIA | AI and data science R&D programs |
| **Research, Development, and Innovation Authority (RDIA)** | RDIA (est. 2021) | Coordinates national R&D strategy; grant programs |
| **Wa'ed (Aramco Entrepreneurship Center)** | Saudi Aramco | Startup loans and equity for technology companies |
| **KAUST Innovation Fund** | KAUST | Deep tech and science-based startup funding |
| **Monsha'at (SME Authority)** | Monsha'at | SME innovation support and incubation |

**Vision 2030 R&D Targets:**
- Increase R&D spending to 1.6% of GDP by 2030 (from ~0.8%)
- Establish Saudi Arabia as a regional innovation hub
- RDIA established by Royal Decree in 2021 to coordinate national R&D ecosystem

## 3. Patent Box / IP Regime

Saudi Arabia does not currently have a patent box or preferential IP income regime.

| Feature | Details |
|---|---|
| **Patent Box** | Not available |
| **IP Royalty Income** | Taxed at standard 20% CIT (foreign) or included in Zakat base |
| **Withholding Tax on Royalties — Outbound** | 15% (Article 68 of the Income Tax Law) |
| **Withholding Tax — Treaty Reduced** | 5–10% under select treaties (e.g., France 7%, UK 5–8%) |
| **IP Disposal Gains** | 20% CIT (foreign shareholders) or Zakat |
| **IP Amortization** | Amortized over useful life; intangible assets depreciated at 10% declining balance |
| **Transfer Pricing on IP** | Article 63; ZATCA has adopted OECD-aligned transfer pricing rules |
| **Tax Treaties** | 40+ treaties in force; expanding network |

**Withholding Tax Rates on Royalties (Selected Treaties):**

| Treaty Partner | WHT on Royalties |
|---|---|
| France | 7% |
| United Kingdom | 5–8% |
| Japan | 5–10% |
| South Korea | 5–10% |
| India | 10% |
| Malaysia | 8% |
| China | 10% |
| No treaty | 15% (domestic rate) |

## 5. Claim Process & Documentation

### R&D Expense Deduction

| Step | Action | Timeline |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Maintain detailed records of R&D expenditure | Ongoing |
| 2 | Classify costs as revenue or capital in accounting records | At incurrence |
| 3 | Prepare transfer pricing documentation for related-party R&D | Annually |
| 4 | Include R&D deductions in annual CIT return (or Zakat return) | Within 120 days of fiscal year-end |
| 5 | File return and pay tax/Zakat via ZATCA portal (ERAD) | Annual deadline |
| 6 | Retain supporting documentation for ZATCA audit | Minimum 10 years |

### Government Grant Applications

| Step | Action | Timeline |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Identify relevant grant program (KACST, RDIA, sector programs) | Pre-project |
| 2 | Submit proposal with R&D plan, budget, and team credentials | Per program deadlines |
| 3 | Grant committee review and site visits | 2–6 months |
| 4 | Negotiate terms and grant agreement | Upon approval |
| 5 | Submit periodic progress and financial reports | Per agreement |
| 6 | Grant disbursement upon milestone completion | Per agreement |

### Required Documentation

- **Tax Filing:** Annual CIT return (ZATCA Form 100); Zakat return; audited financial statements
- **R&D Records:** Project descriptions, budgets, progress reports, personnel records
- **Employment Records:** Contracts, payroll, time allocation for R&D employees
- **Financial Records:** General ledger, invoices, payment vouchers, bank statements
- **Transfer Pricing:** Master file, local file, CbCR (if applicable, for groups with revenue > SAR 3.2B)
- **Grant Documentation:** Application, approval letter, progress reports, disbursement records

## 6. Recent Changes & Sunset Provisions

| Change | Effective Date | Details |
|---|---|---|
| **Transfer Pricing Bylaws** | January 1, 2019 | ZATCA adopted OECD-aligned transfer pricing rules (Article 63) |
| **RDIA Establishment** | 2021 (Royal Decree) | Research, Development, and Innovation Authority created to coordinate national R&D |
| **Special Economic Zones Regulations** | 2021–2022 | SEZs with significant tax incentives launched (NEOM, SILZ, others) |
| **SILZ Riyadh Announced** | 2023 | 5% CIT, 0% WHT for 50 years in Special Integrated Logistics Zone |
| **Loss Carry-Forward Limitation** | Effective 2019 | Annual utilization capped at 25% of current year taxable income |
| **Vision 2030 R&D Spending Target** | 2016 (target: 2030) | Goal to increase R&D expenditure to 1.6% of GDP |
| **Pillar Two Monitoring** | Ongoing | ZATCA monitoring OECD Pillar Two; may impact SEZ incentives for large MNEs |
| **Proposed R&D Tax Incentive** | Under study (2025–2026) | RDIA and ZATCA reportedly studying enhanced R&D deduction or credit mechanism |

**Sunset Provisions:**
- No specific R&D incentive sunset dates (as no specific R&D incentive currently exists)
- SEZ incentives (NEOM, SILZ) are typically granted for 50-year periods from establishment
- Government grant programs are subject to annual budgetary allocation
- Vision 2030 programs are reviewed periodically with 2030 as the target year

**Future Outlook:**
- Saudi Arabia's RDIA is actively developing a national R&D incentive framework
- Potential introduction of enhanced R&D deduction (150%+) or tax credit under study
- SEZ expansion with R&D-specific zones under consideration
- Growing bilateral R&D cooperation agreements with technology-leading countries
