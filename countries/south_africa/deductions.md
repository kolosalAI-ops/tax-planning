# South Africa - Taxable Income Deductions

> **Jurisdiction**: National (South African Revenue Service — SARS)
> **Governing Law**: Income Tax Act 58 of 1962 (ITA)
> **Tax Year**: 2024/2025
> **Currency**: ZAR (R)

---

## 1. Depreciation & Capital Allowances

South Africa does not allow accounting depreciation as a tax deduction. Instead, the ITA provides specific **capital allowance** provisions that replace depreciation for tax purposes.

### Wear and Tear / Depreciation Allowance — §11(e), ITA; SARS Interpretation Note 47 (Issue 5); Binding General Ruling (BGR) 7 (Issue 4)

| Asset Category | Write-Off Period | Method | Legal Basis |
|---|---|---|---|
| Machinery and plant — general | **5 years** (20% per annum) | Straight-line | §11(e); IN 47 |
| Computers and computer equipment | **3 years** (33.3% per annum) | Straight-line | §11(e); IN 47 |
| Motor vehicles | **5 years** (20% per annum) | Straight-line | §11(e); IN 47 |
| Office furniture and fittings | **6 years** (16.7% per annum) | Straight-line | §11(e); IN 47 |
| Air conditioning units | **6 years** | Straight-line | §11(e); IN 47 |
| Office equipment (photocopiers, printers) | **5 years** | Straight-line | §11(e); IN 47 |
| Tools and implements | **3 years** | Straight-line | §11(e); IN 47 |

> IN 47 and BGR 7 provide a comprehensive list of write-off periods accepted by SARS. Assets not listed may be depreciated over their estimated useful life.

### Manufacturing Asset Allowance — §12C, ITA

| Parameter | Detail |
|---|---|
| Applicable assets | New or used machinery, plant, implements, utensils, or articles used in a process of manufacture or similar process |
| Allowance — new assets | **40%** in year 1, then **20%** each in years 2–4 (40/20/20/20) |
| Allowance — used assets | **40%** in year 1, then **20%** each in years 2–4 |
| Also applicable to | Hotelkeepers (new assets used in hotel trade), aircraft, ships |
| Legal basis | §12C, ITA |

### Small Business Corporation (SBC) Accelerated Depreciation — §12E, ITA

| Parameter | Detail |
|---|---|
| Qualifying businesses | Turnover ≤ ZAR 20M; natural persons hold all shares; ≤20% of income from personal services |
| Manufacturing assets | **100%** write-off in year 1 |
| Non-manufacturing plant & machinery | **50/30/20** over 3 years |
| Legal basis | §12E, ITA |

### Industrial / Commercial Buildings — §13quin, ITA

| Parameter | Detail |
|---|---|
| New buildings (non-residential) | **5%** per annum over 20 years |
| New low-cost residential units | **5%** per annum over 20 years — §13sex, ITA |
| Urban Development Zone buildings | **20%** in year 1 + **8%** per annum for years 2–11 (new buildings); **20%** in year 1 + **8%** per annum for years 2–6 (renovations) — §13quat, ITA |
| Legal basis | §13quin, §13sex, §13quat, ITA |

### Renewable Energy Assets — §12B, ITA

| Parameter | Detail |
|---|---|
| Standard allowance | **50/30/20** over 3 years |
| Enhanced incentive (temporary) | **125%** deduction in year 1 for qualifying renewable energy assets brought into use between 1 March 2023 and 28 February 2025 — §12BA, ITA |
| Extension | Budget 2025 proposes extending certain building/energy incentives to 31 March 2030 [unverified — confirm enactment] |

### Pre-Trading Expenditure — §11A, ITA

| Parameter | Detail |
|---|---|
| Scope | Expenditure and losses incurred before commencement of trade |
| Treatment | Deductible in the year trade commences, to the extent the expenditure would have been deductible if trade had already commenced |
| Legal basis | §11A, ITA |

## 2. Business Operating Expenses

### General Deductibility (§11(a), ITA)

Expenditure and losses actually incurred in the production of income, provided they are not of a capital nature, are deductible.

| Expense Category | Deductibility | Legal Basis |
|---|---|---|
| Salaries, wages, and bonuses | **100%** deductible | §11(a), ITA |
| Employer pension/provident/retirement fund contributions | **100%** deductible (employee contributions are limited) | §11(l), ITA |
| Employee training | **100%** deductible; additional learnership allowances available (§12H, ITA) | §11(a), §12H, ITA |
| Rent (premises) | **100%** deductible | §11(a), ITA |
| Repairs and maintenance | **100%** deductible (improvements are capital — not deductible under §11(a)) | §11(d), ITA |
| Insurance premiums (business) | **100%** deductible | §11(a), ITA |
| Professional and legal fees | **100%** deductible (if revenue, not capital) | §11(a), ITA |
| Bad debts | Deductible when actually written off as irrecoverable — §11(i), ITA | §11(i), ITA |
| Doubtful debts | Allowance per §11(j), ITA — face value of debts owed that are doubtful (SARS may accept IFRS 9 expected credit loss method for financial institutions) | §11(j), ITA |
| Travel and subsistence | Deductible subject to SARS rates (published annually) | §11(a), ITA |
| Entertainment | Deductible if directly connected to trade and not of a private nature | §11(a) read with §23(a), ITA |
| Donations | **Not deductible** as business expense; but donations to approved §18A organisations qualify for a deduction limited to 10% of taxable income | §18A, ITA |

### Foreign Currency Translation — §25D, ITA

| Parameter | Detail |
|---|---|
| Scope | Exchange differences on foreign-currency monetary items |
| Treatment | Gains and losses translated at year-end spot rate are included in / deducted from taxable income |
| Legal basis | §25D, ITA |

## 3. Interest & Financing Costs

### General Interest Deduction — §24J, ITA

| Parameter | Detail |
|---|---|
| General rule | Interest incurred in the production of income is deductible — §11(a), ITA |
| Accrual basis | Interest accrued on "instruments" (as defined in §24J) is brought to account on a yield-to-maturity basis |

### Interest Limitation — §23M, ITA

| Parameter | Detail |
|---|---|
| Scope | Applies to interest on debts owed to persons in a **controlling relationship** with the debtor, where the interest is **not subject to South African tax** in the hands of the creditor |
| Limitation | Deductible interest limited to the higher of: (a) interest received/accrued by the debtor, or (b) **30%** of "adjusted taxable income" (analogous to EBITDA) |
| Carry-forward | Disallowed interest carried forward **indefinitely** for deduction in subsequent years |
| Adjusted taxable income | Taxable income before interest, assessed losses, and capital allowances are added back |
| Exempt entities | Banks, insurers, and certain regulated financial institutions |
| Legal basis | §23M, ITA (as amended by Taxation Laws Amendment Act, 2021) |

> **Note:** The original §23M used a 40% of adjusted taxable income formula adjusted by a repo rate factor. The 2021/2022 amendments tightened this to align with the OECD BEPS Action 4 recommendation (30% of EBITDA equivalent). Confirm the exact version in force for the relevant tax year with SARS guidance.

### §23N, ITA — Acquisition of Controlling Interest

| Parameter | Detail |
|---|---|
| Scope | Interest on debt used to acquire a controlling share (≥50%) in a company, or to fund a connected person's acquisition |
| Limitation | Deduction limited to the taxable income of the acquired company attributable to the acquiring company |
| Legal basis | §23N, ITA |

## 4. Research & Development

### §11D, ITA — R&D Deduction

| Parameter | Detail |
|---|---|
| Enhanced deduction | **150%** of qualifying R&D expenditure (i.e., 50% additional deduction over the normal 100%) |
| Qualifying activities | Systematic investigative or experimental activities for the discovery of novel, practical, and non-obvious information; approval required from Department of Science and Innovation (DSI) |
| Capital R&D expenditure | **50/30/20** accelerated allowance for R&D machinery |
| Approval | Pre-approval from DSI required before claiming the additional 50% |
| Legal basis | §11D, ITA |

See [R&D Incentives](rnd_incentives.md) for full programme details including the approval process, qualifying criteria, and interaction with other incentives.

## 5. Loss Carry-Forward & Carry-Back

### Assessed Losses — §20, ITA

| Parameter | Detail |
|---|---|
| Carry-forward | **Indefinite** — §20(1), ITA |
| Annual utilisation cap | **80%** of taxable income (for years of assessment commencing on or after 1 April 2023) — §20(1)(a)(ii), ITA |
| Minimum deduction | At least ZAR 1,000,000 of assessed losses can always be utilised regardless of the 80% cap |
| Carry-back | **Not permitted** |
| Ring-fencing | Companies that do not trade or whose income is predominantly investment income may have losses ring-fenced — §20(2A), ITA |
| Cessation of trade | If a company ceases to trade, its assessed loss is forfeited unless trade resumes within a reasonable period |

### Change of Ownership — §103(2), ITA

| Parameter | Detail |
|---|---|
| Anti-avoidance | SARS may disallow assessed loss utilisation where there is a change in shareholders and the sole or main purpose of the share acquisition was to utilise the assessed loss |

## 6. Provisions & Reserves

### General Principles (§11(a), §23, ITA)

| Item | Treatment |
|---|---|
| General provisions | **Not deductible** — expenditure must be actually incurred |
| Contingency reserves | **Not deductible** |
| Bad debt provisions | Deductible under §11(j) — doubtful debt allowance (requires specific identification or SARS-approved methodology) |
| Warranty provisions | **Not deductible** until claim is paid (no general provision allowed) |
| Leave pay accruals | Deductible under §11(l) — limited to leave accrued but not yet paid at year-end |
| Retirement fund contributions | Employer contributions deductible under §11(l) |
| Insurance reserves (long-term insurers) | Specific provisions in §28 and §29, ITA |

## 7. Industry-Specific Deductions

### Mining — §36, ITA

| Parameter | Detail |
|---|---|
| Capital expenditure | **100%** deduction in the year incurred for mining capital expenditure (shaft sinking, development, machinery, buildings) — §36(7C), ITA |
| Gold mining formula | Taxable income subject to a special formula: `y = 34 – 170/x` where x = ratio of taxable income to gross mining income (expressed as %) |
| Pre-production expenditure | May be capitalised and deducted from future mining income — §36(7F), ITA |
| Rehabilitation | Environmental rehabilitation funds: deductible contributions to approved funds — §37A, ITA |
| Legal basis | §36, §37, §37A, ITA |

### Agriculture — §12, ITA

| Parameter | Detail |
|---|---|
| Capital development expenditure | Deductible — dams, irrigation, fencing, plantations, soil conservation |
| Livestock | Special provisions for valuation of livestock (standard value, market value, or specific) — §22, ITA (farming) |

### Film and Entertainment — §12O, ITA

| Parameter | Detail |
|---|---|
| Allowance | Accelerated deduction for qualifying South African film productions |
| Legal basis | §12O, ITA |

### Special Economic Zones — §12R, §12S, ITA

| Parameter | Detail |
|---|---|
| Reduced CIT rate | **15%** for qualifying companies in designated SEZs (vs. standard 27%) |
| Building allowance | **10%** per annum on cost of new buildings — §12S, ITA |
| Conditions | Must carry on qualifying activities within a designated SEZ |

### Learnership and Employment Incentives — §12H, ITA

| Parameter | Detail |
|---|---|
| Learnership allowance | Annual deduction of ZAR 30,000 (non-disabled) or ZAR 50,000 (disabled) per learner; completion allowance of same amounts |
| Employment Tax Incentive (ETI) | Not a deduction but a credit against PAYE; reduces effective cost of employing young workers (18–29 years) |

## 8. Treaties & Transfer Pricing

### Transfer Pricing — §31, ITA

| Parameter | Detail |
|---|---|
| Standard | Arm's-length principle — §31(2), ITA |
| Scope | Transactions between connected persons where one is a non-resident |
| Adjustment | SARS may adjust the transaction to reflect arm's-length terms; excess treated as deemed dividend (20% dividend withholding tax) |
| Methods | CUP, resale price, cost plus, TNMM, profit split — aligned with OECD Guidelines |
| Documentation | Master File and Local File required for transactions with connected parties |
| Country-by-Country Reporting | Required for SA-headquartered MNE groups with consolidated revenue ≥ ZAR 10B |
| APA | Advance Pricing Agreements available through SARS |
| Penalties | Understatement penalties: 10%–200% depending on behaviour and disclosure |

### Treaty Network

South Africa has over 80 bilateral double taxation treaties. Standard treaty withholding rates typically reduce dividends to 5%–15%, interest to 0%–10%, and royalties to 0%–10%.

## 9. Non-Deductible Items

The following are **NOT deductible** under the South African ITA:

| Expense | Legal Basis |
|---|---|
| Capital expenditure (unless specific allowance applies) | §23(f), ITA |
| Expenditure not incurred in the production of income | §23(g), ITA |
| Private or domestic expenses | §23(a), ITA |
| Income tax (South African or foreign — except as a credit) | §23(d), ITA |
| Provisions and contingent reserves (unless specifically permitted) | §23(e), ITA |
| Interest exceeding §23M / §23N limitations | §23M, §23N, ITA |
| Fines and penalties for unlawful activities | §23(o), ITA |
| Bribes and corrupt payments | §23(o), ITA |
| Expenditure relating to exempt income | §23(f), ITA |
| Entertainment expenditure not directly connected to trade | §23(a), ITA |
| Contributions to unapproved retirement funds | §23(m), ITA |
| Transfer pricing adjustments (excess over arm's-length) | §31, ITA |
