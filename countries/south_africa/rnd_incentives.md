# South Africa - R&D Tax Incentives

## 1. Overview

| Feature | Details |
|---|---|
| **Primary Incentive** | 150% Super Deduction on qualifying R&D expenditure |
| **Deduction Rate** | 150% of qualifying R&D expenditure (100% base + 50% additional) |
| **Governing Law** | Section 11D of the Income Tax Act, No. 58 of 1962 |
| **Administering Bodies** | Department of Science and Innovation (DSI); South African Revenue Service (SARS) |
| **CIT Rate** | 27% (reduced from 28% for years of assessment ending on or after March 31, 2023) |
| **Pre-Approval Required** | Yes — mandatory DSI pre-approval before claiming the deduction |
| **Patent Box / IP Regime** | None |
| **Fiscal Year** | Varies by company; assessment year typically ends February/March |
| **Currency** | South African Rand (ZAR) |

## 2. Detailed Programs

### 2.1 Section 11D — R&D Super Deduction (150%)

**Legal Basis:** Section 11D of the Income Tax Act, No. 58 of 1962 (as amended by the Taxation Laws Amendment Act, 2012; reintroduced effective October 2, 2012)

The Section 11D incentive provides a 150% deduction for qualifying R&D expenditure, meaning companies receive an additional 50% deduction above the standard 100% business expense deduction.

| Parameter | Details |
|---|---|
| **Deduction Rate** | 150% of qualifying R&D expenditure |
| **Effective Additional Benefit** | 50% × 27% CIT = 13.5% cash tax saving on qualifying spend |
| **Pre-Approval** | Mandatory — must be obtained from DSI before expenditure is incurred |
| **Qualifying Activities** | Systematic investigative or experimental activities with technological uncertainty |
| **Eligible Entity** | Any company registered as a taxpayer in South Africa |
| **Carry-Forward** | Assessed losses (including R&D deductions) carry forward indefinitely |
| **Retrospective Claims** | Not permitted — pre-approval must precede the expenditure |

#### Qualifying R&D Activities

Section 11D requires that R&D activities meet all of the following criteria:

| Criterion | Description |
|---|---|
| **Systematic** | Activities must be planned, structured, and methodologically sound |
| **Investigative or Experimental** | Must involve investigation, experimentation, or search for new knowledge |
| **Technological Uncertainty** | The outcome must not be determinable in advance by a competent professional |
| **Novelty** | Must aim to achieve scientific or technological advancement |
| **Conducted in South Africa** | The R&D must be performed in South Africa (with limited exceptions) |

#### Excluded Activities

| Excluded Activity | Notes |
|---|---|
| Market research and sales promotion | Not qualifying |
| Quality control or routine testing | Not qualifying |
| Cosmetic or stylistic changes | Not qualifying |
| Social science, arts, or humanities research | Not qualifying |
| Management studies or efficiency surveys | Not qualifying |
| Oil and gas exploration | Covered under separate provisions |
| Activities to comply with statutory requirements | Not qualifying |

### 2.2 Eligible Expenditure Categories

**Legal Basis:** Section 11D(2)–(5) of the Income Tax Act

| Cost Category | Deduction Rate | Conditions |
|---|---|---|
| **Compensation — Employees directly engaged in R&D** | 150% | Must be directly and solely engaged in qualifying R&D |
| **Compensation — Employees partially engaged in R&D** | 150% (pro rata) | Apportioned based on time spent on qualifying R&D |
| **Materials and consumables** | 150% | Consumed or transformed in the R&D process |
| **Contract R&D payments** | 150% | To South African entities; subject to agreement provisions |
| **Computer software for R&D** | 150% | Used directly in R&D activities |
| **Equipment depreciation (Section 11(e))** | 100% (standard) | Depreciation at standard rates; no 150% on capital items |
| **Pilot plant and prototypes** | 150% (operating costs) | Capital costs depreciated at standard rates |
| **Overhead (allocated)** | 150% | Must be directly attributable and reasonably allocated |

**Important:** The 150% super deduction applies only to revenue (non-capital) R&D expenditure. Capital expenditure on R&D assets is deducted under normal depreciation provisions (Section 11(e) or Section 12C).

### 2.3 Accelerated Depreciation for R&D Buildings and Equipment

| Asset Type | Provision | Rate |
|---|---|---|
| **R&D machinery and equipment** | Section 12C | 40:20:20:20 (4-year write-off) |
| **R&D buildings** | Section 13(1) | 5% per annum (20 years) |
| **New or unused manufacturing assets used in R&D** | Section 12C | 40:20:20:20 |

## 3. Patent Box / IP Regime

South Africa does not currently have a patent box or preferential IP income regime.

| Feature | Details |
|---|---|
| **Patent Box** | Not available |
| **IP Royalty Income** | Taxed at standard 27% CIT rate |
| **Withholding Tax on Royalties** | 15% on royalties paid to non-residents (subject to treaty relief) |
| **IP Disposal Gains** | Taxed as capital gains (effective rate: 21.6% for companies) |
| **IP Amortization** | Section 11(gC) — expenditure on acquisition of IP amortized over useful life |
| **Tax Treaties** | Extensive treaty network (80+ treaties) may reduce withholding taxes |

**Note on Capital Gains:**
- Companies: 80% inclusion rate × 27% CIT = 21.6% effective rate on capital gains
- Disposal of IP assets (patents, trademarks, designs) may qualify for capital gains treatment

## 5. Claim Process & Documentation

### DSI Pre-Approval Process

| Step | Action | Timeline |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Submit application to DSI using prescribed form (Form A) | Before R&D expenditure is incurred |
| 2 | DSI assigns R&D project reference number | 2–4 weeks |
| 3 | DSI adjudication committee reviews application | 4–12 weeks (target: 90 days) |
| 4 | DSI issues approval letter with conditions | Upon committee decision |
| 5 | Company commences/continues approved R&D | Per approval terms |
| 6 | Submit annual progress report to DSI (Form B) | Within 12 months of prior reporting |
| 7 | Claim 150% deduction in annual CIT return filed with SARS | Annual tax return deadline |

### Annual Reporting to DSI

| Report Element | Details |
|---|---|
| **Form B — Annual Report** | Detailed progress update on approved R&D project |
| **Expenditure Report** | Actual R&D expenditure breakdown by category |
| **Progress Report** | Technical progress against stated R&D objectives |
| **Personnel Report** | R&D employees involved, qualifications, time allocation |
| **Variation Requests** | Any changes to project scope, budget, or timeline |
| **Filing Deadline** | Within the period specified in the approval letter |

### SARS Filing Requirements

| Requirement | Details |
|---|---|
| **ITR14 Return** | Include Section 11D deduction in annual corporate tax return |
| **Supporting Schedule** | Section 11D schedule with DSI approval reference number |
| **Reconciliation** | Reconcile R&D expenditure to financial statements |
| **Record Retention** | Minimum 5 years from date of assessment (Section 29 of Tax Administration Act) |

### Required Documentation

- **DSI Application (Form A):** Project description, technological uncertainty, expected outcomes, budget breakdown, key R&D personnel, timeline
- **Annual Reports (Form B):** Progress against milestones, actual vs. budgeted expenditure, personnel changes, publications/patents
- **Financial Records:** General ledger coding for R&D costs, payroll records, time sheets, procurement records
- **Technical Records:** Lab notebooks, experimental records, design documents, prototype testing results
- **Personnel Records:** Employment contracts, qualifications, time allocation records for R&D staff

## 6. Recent Changes & Sunset Provisions

| Change | Effective Date | Details |
|---|---|---|
| **CIT Rate Reduction to 27%** | Years ending on or after March 31, 2023 | Reduces the cash value of the 150% deduction (from 14% to 13.5% incremental) |
| **Section 11D Reintroduction** | October 2, 2012 | Current version of Section 11D replaced the previous (2006) version |
| **DSI Streamlined Process** | 2021 | DSI implemented online application portal and reduced processing times |
| **Broadened R&D Definition** | 2023 Amendment | Clarification on software development and digital innovation qualifying activities |
| **Section 12BA — Renewable Energy** | March 2023 | 125% deduction for qualifying renewable energy assets (complements R&D for clean tech) |
| **Proposed Enhancement (2025 Budget)** | Under consideration | National Treasury exploring increase to 175% or 200% deduction to boost innovation |
| **Pillar Two Considerations** | Monitoring | Limited impact as 27% CIT exceeds 15% minimum; R&D deductions are standard timing differences |

**Sunset Provisions:**
- Section 11D has no explicit sunset date; it is a permanent provision of the Income Tax Act
- However, the provision is subject to periodic amendment through the annual Taxation Laws Amendment Act
- DSI approval is project-specific and typically valid for 1–3 years, renewable upon application
- The approved R&D project must be renewed or extended if it continues beyond the approved period

**Potential Risks:**
- Retroactive disallowance if DSI approval is not obtained before R&D expenditure is incurred
- Annual reporting failure may result in DSI withdrawing approval
- SARS audit focus on Section 11D claims has increased in recent years
