# Colombia - Taxable Income Deductions

> **Jurisdiction**: National (DIAN — Dirección de Impuestos y Aduanas Nacionales)
> **Governing Law**: Estatuto Tributario (ET), Decreto 624/1989, as amended (notably by Ley 2277/2022 — Tax Reform)
> **Tax Year**: 2024/2025
> **Currency**: COP ($)

---

## 1. Depreciation & Capital Allowances

> **Legal Basis**: ET Art. 128 (deduction for depreciation); ET Art. 137 (limitation on depreciation deduction — useful life and maximum rates); ET Art. 140 (accelerated depreciation)

### Standard Depreciation Rates — ET Art. 137

| Asset Category | Useful Life | Max Annual Rate | Legal Reference |
|---|---|---|---|
| Buildings and constructions | **45 years** | **2.22%** | ET Art. 137 |
| Aqueducts, sewage, communication networks | **10–25 years** | **4–10%** | ET Art. 137 |
| Roads, railways, bridges | **10–20 years** | **5–10%** | ET Art. 137 |
| Machinery and equipment | **10 years** | **10%** | ET Art. 137 |
| Furniture and fixtures | **10 years** | **10%** | ET Art. 137 |
| Land and water transport equipment | **10 years** | **10%** | ET Art. 137 (default rate in absence of specific regulation) |
| Air transport equipment | **5 years** | **20%** | ET Art. 137 |
| Computers and IT equipment | **5 years** | **20%** | ET Art. 137 |
| Scientific research equipment | **5 years** | **20%** | ET Art. 137 |
| Software | **3–5 years** | **20–33%** | ET Art. 137 |
| Trees (commercial plantations) | Varies | Amortised per productive cycle | ET Art. 137 |

> Assets acquired after December 31, 2016 are depreciated in accordance with IFRS (NIIF) principles, considering the economic benefits expected. Assets acquired before that date continue under the fiscal methods then in force.

### Depreciation Methods — ET Art. 134

| Method | Availability |
|---|---|
| Straight-line | Default and most common |
| Declining balance | Permitted |
| Units of production | Permitted for specific assets |
| Technical estimate | Permitted if reflecting economic useful life |

### Accelerated Depreciation — ET Art. 140

| Parameter | Value | Legal Reference |
|---|---|---|
| Additional shift allowance | Depreciation rate increased by **25%** for every additional 8-hour shift (or pro rata for fractions) | ET Art. 140 |
| Exclusion | Real property (buildings/immovables) is **not** eligible for accelerated depreciation | ET Art. 140 |
| Example | Machine used 16 hours/day: standard 10% + 25% = **12.5%** annual depreciation rate | |

### Amortisation of Intangibles — ET Art. 142

| Parameter | Value | Legal Reference |
|---|---|---|
| Eligible intangibles | Acquired intangible assets with finite useful life (patents, licences, franchises, trademarks, software) | ET Art. 142 |
| Method | Straight-line over legal or economic useful life | ET Art. 142 |
| Goodwill | Generally **not deductible** unless from a qualifying business combination | ET Art. 143 |

## 2. Business Operating Expenses

> **Legal Basis**: ET Art. 107 (general deductibility — relation to income-producing activity, proportionality, necessity)

### General Deductibility — ET Art. 107

| Parameter | Value | Legal Reference |
|---|---|---|
| Standard | Expenses are deductible if they are **necessary, proportional, and bear a cause-and-effect relationship** with the income-producing activity | ET Art. 107 |
| Accrual method | Expenses must be realised during the relevant tax year under the accrual method | ET Art. 105 |
| Documentation | Must be supported by invoices or equivalent documents | ET Art. 771-2 |
| Electronic invoicing | Mandatory for most taxpayers (factura electrónica) | ET Art. 616-1 |

### Common Deductible Expenses

| Expense Type | Deductibility | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Employee salaries and wages | **100%** | Subject to proper withholding |
| Employer social security contributions | **100%** | Pension (12%), health (8.5%), ARL (variable) |
| Severance (cesantías) and interest on severance | **100%** | |
| Rent and lease payments | **100%** | Arm's length for related parties |
| Utilities (electricity, water, telecom) | **100%** | Business use |
| Professional services | **100%** | |
| Insurance premiums | **100%** | Business-related |
| Advertising and marketing | **100%** | |
| Maintenance and repairs | **100%** | Current-year expenses; improvements capitalised |
| Travel expenses | **100%** | Business-related with documentation |
| Non-income taxes (industry and commerce tax — ICA) | **100%** | ET Art. 115 — deductible or creditable (50%) |
| GMF (financial transactions tax — 4 per thousand) | **50%** deductible | ET Art. 115 |

### Charitable Donations — ET Art. 257

| Parameter | Value | Legal Reference |
|---|---|---|
| Tax discount | **25%** of donation value as a tax discount (credit against tax) | ET Art. 257 |
| Eligible recipients | Registered non-profit entities (entidades sin ánimo de lucro — ESAL) under special tax regime | ET Art. 257 |
| Documentation | Must have certification from donee entity | ET Art. 125-1 |

### Meals & Entertainment

| Expense Type | Deductibility |
|---|---|
| Business meals | Deductible if meeting ET Art. 107 criteria (necessary, proportional, related to income) |
| Entertainment | Deductible to the extent related to income-producing activity |
| Representational expenses | Deductible if reasonable and documented |

## 3. Interest & Financing Costs

> **Legal Basis**: ET Art. 117 (interest deduction); ET Art. 118-1 (thin capitalization and interest limitation)

### General Interest Deduction — ET Art. 117

| Parameter | Value | Legal Reference |
|---|---|---|
| Basic rule | Interest paid on debts is deductible if related to income-producing activity | ET Art. 117 |
| Presumptive interest | Loans between related parties must bear interest at least at the market rate (DTF or IBR reference); if interest-free, DIAN may impute income | ET Art. 35 |

### Thin Capitalization and Interest Limitation — ET Art. 118-1

| Parameter | Value | Legal Reference |
|---|---|---|
| Debt-to-equity ratio | **2:1** (total average debt vs. net equity) | ET Art. 118-1 (as amended by Ley 2277/2022) |
| Special purpose vehicles (social housing) | **4:1** ratio | ET Art. 118-1, parágrafo 1 |
| Interest limitation | Interest exceeding the ratio is **non-deductible** | ET Art. 118-1 |
| EBITDA-based limitation | Net interest expense limited to **30% of tax-EBITDA** | ET Art. 118-1 (introduced by Ley 2277/2022, Art. 56) |
| Carry-forward of denied interest | **5 years** | ET Art. 118-1 |
| Scope | Applies to all debt (both related-party and third-party) | ET Art. 118-1 |
| Exemptions | Financial institutions; special purpose vehicles for social housing (4:1 ratio) | ET Art. 118-1 |

> The thin capitalization rule and the EBITDA-based interest limitation operate concurrently. Taxpayers must satisfy both tests — whichever produces the lower deductible amount prevails.

## 4. Research & Development

Colombia provides R&D tax incentives under ET Art. 256 (as amended by Ley 2277/2022). Taxpayers investing in qualified R&D projects certified by the National Council of Tax Benefits in Science, Technology and Innovation (CNBT) may claim a **tax discount of 30%** of the invested value against their income tax liability. The former additional deduction under ET Art. 158-1 was repealed by Ley 2277/2022 for tax years beginning 2023.

See [R&D Incentives](rnd_incentives.md) for full program detail.

## 5. Loss Carry-Forward & Carry-Back

> **Legal Basis**: ET Art. 147 (loss carry-forward — 12 years)

| Parameter | Value | Legal Reference |
|---|---|---|
| Carry-forward period | **12 years** | ET Art. 147 |
| Carry-back | **Not allowed** | ET Art. 147 (no carry-back provision) |
| Annual limitation | **100%** of taxable income (no percentage cap) | ET Art. 147 |
| Losses from exempt income | Cannot be offset against ordinary taxable income | ET Art. 147 |
| Merger/absorption | Losses of absorbed entity may be carried forward by surviving entity (subject to conditions) | ET Art. 147 |
| Losses from prior regime changes | Tax losses generated before 2017 can be carried forward for 12 years from the year they arose | ET Art. 147 |

## 6. Provisions & Reserves

> **Legal Basis**: ET Art. 105 (realisation of expenses); ET Art. 145 (bad debt deduction)

| Provision Type | Deductibility | Legal Reference |
|---|---|---|
| Bad debts (specific provision) | **Deductible** — individual provision for debts with more than one year overdue and demonstrated collection efforts | ET Art. 145 |
| Bad debts (general provision) | **Not deductible** for tax purposes | ET Art. 145 |
| Employee severance (cesantías) accrual | **Deductible** when provisioned per legal requirements | ET Art. 109 |
| Vacation accruals | **Deductible** when accrued | ET Art. 109 |
| General contingency reserves | **Not deductible** until liability is crystallised and paid | ET Art. 105 |
| Warranty provisions | **Not deductible** until actual claim is paid | ET Art. 105 |
| Environmental reserves | **Not deductible** until payment | ET Art. 105 |
| Financial institution loan-loss provisions | Special rules apply — deductible per Superintendencia Financiera guidelines | ET Art. 145 |

## 7. Industry-Specific Deductions

### Oil & Gas (Petroleum)

| Parameter | Value | Legal Reference |
|---|---|---|
| Exploration expenses | **100%** deductible in year incurred (or capitalised and amortised) | ET Art. 142; Decreto 2820/2010 |
| Development costs | Capitalised and amortised based on units of production or straight-line | ET Art. 142 |
| Depletion allowance | Available for natural resource extraction based on proven reserves | ET Art. 159 |
| Royalties (government) | Oil royalties paid to the Colombian state are **deductible** as an expense | ET Art. 115; Ley 756/2002 |
| Abandonment/remediation costs | Deductible when incurred | |

### Agriculture

| Parameter | Value |
|---|---|
| Plantation establishment costs | Capitalised and amortised over productive life |
| Annual crop costs | Deductible in the harvest year |
| Rural property tax | Deductible as operating expense |

### Free Trade Zones (Zonas Francas) — Ley 2277/2022

| Parameter | Value |
|---|---|
| Income tax rate (qualifying users) | **20%** (reduced from general 35%) |
| Requirement | Must meet investment and employment commitments |
| Depreciation | Standard rules apply within the zone |

### Tourism — Ley 2068/2020

| Parameter | Value |
|---|---|
| Incentive | Reduced income tax rate for qualifying hotel and tourism investments |
| Duration | For investments made 2003–2027 in new/expanded hotel infrastructure |

## 8. Treaties & Transfer Pricing

### Transfer Pricing — ET Arts. 260-1 through 260-11

| Parameter | Value | Legal Reference |
|---|---|---|
| Standard | Arm's length principle | ET Art. 260-1 |
| Methods | CUP, RPM, CPM, TNMM, PSM, residual PSM | ET Art. 260-3 |
| Documentation | Transfer pricing study (Informe de Precios de Transferencia) required for related-party transactions | ET Art. 260-5 |
| Informative return | Annual filing (Declaración Informativa) for operations with related parties | ET Art. 260-9 |
| Country-by-Country reporting | Required for MNE groups with consolidated revenue ≥ 81,000 UVT (~COP 3.7 trillion for 2024) | ET Art. 260-5, parágrafo 2 |
| Master File / Local File | Required per OECD BEPS Action 13 guidelines | ET Art. 260-5 |
| Penalties | Fines for late/incomplete filing: up to 20,000 UVT; adjustment penalties per standard rules | ET Art. 260-11 |

### Treaty Network

Colombia has over 15 bilateral tax treaties in force (including with Spain, Switzerland, Canada, Mexico, Chile, UK, France, India, Japan, Italy). Treaties generally follow the OECD model with some UN model provisions.
