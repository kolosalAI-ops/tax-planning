# Colombia - R&D Tax Incentives

## 1. Overview

| Parameter | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Headline Incentive** | R&D Tax Discount and Additional Deduction |
| **Mechanism Type** | Tax discount (credit against income tax) and super-deduction |
| **Headline Rate** | 25% tax discount on R&D investment (Art. 256-1 ET) + 100% additional deduction (Art. 158-1 ET) |
| **Refundable** | No — non-refundable discount against income tax; carry-forward 4 years |
| **Patent Box** | No formal patent box, but preferential treatment for software copyright income exists |
| **Legal Basis** | Estatuto Tributario (ET) Art. 256-1 (tax discount); ET Art. 158-1 (additional deduction); Law 1819/2016; Law 2277/2022; Decree 1011/2020; CONPES Document 4069/2022 |

## 2. Detailed Programs

### 2.1 R&D Tax Discount (ET Art. 256-1)

| Parameter | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Legal Basis** | Estatuto Tributario Art. 256-1 (introduced by Law 1819/2016, Art. 170; modified by Law 2277/2022) |
| **Discount Rate** | 25% of investments in science, technology, and innovation (CTI) projects |
| **Mechanism** | Direct discount against income tax liability (functions as a tax credit) |
| **Application** | Applied after calculating income tax but before surtaxes |
| **Cap** | Cannot exceed 25% of total income tax liability in any given year |
| **Carry-Forward** | Unused discount may be carried forward for 4 years |
| **Qualifying Investments** | Investments in qualified R&D projects, technology development, and innovation approved by CNBT (National Council for Tax Benefits in CTI) |
| **Approval Requirement** | Projects must be registered and approved by MinCiencias (Ministry of Science, Technology and Innovation) through CNBT |
| **Eligible Taxpayers** | All income tax taxpayers making qualifying investments |

### 2.2 Additional Deduction for R&D (ET Art. 158-1)

| Parameter | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Legal Basis** | Estatuto Tributario Art. 158-1 (modified by Law 1819/2016 and Law 2277/2022) |
| **Deduction Rate** | 100% additional deduction on investments in CTI projects (on top of the ordinary expense deduction) |
| **Effective Treatment** | Total deduction = 200% of qualifying expenditure (100% ordinary + 100% additional) |
| **Cap** | The additional deduction is limited and cannot be combined with the Art. 256-1 discount on the same expenditure |
| **Choice** | Taxpayer may choose either the 25% tax discount (Art. 256-1) OR the 100% additional deduction (Art. 158-1) for each qualifying project — but not both on the same investment |
| **Approval Requirement** | Same CNBT/MinCiencias approval required |
| **Carry-Forward** | Deduction applies in the year the investment is made; general loss carry-forward rules apply |

### 2.3 Combined Benefit Structure

| Parameter | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Option A — Tax Discount** | 25% of R&D investment as direct tax credit (Art. 256-1) + 100% ordinary deduction of the expense |
| **Option B — Super-Deduction** | 200% total deduction (100% ordinary + 100% additional per Art. 158-1) |
| **Optimal Choice** | Depends on taxpayer's effective tax rate, profitability, and available tax liability; generally Option A is more beneficial for profitable companies |
| **Cannot Stack** | The same expenditure cannot benefit from both Art. 256-1 and Art. 158-1 simultaneously |

### 2.4 CNBT and MinCiencias Approval

| Parameter | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Governing Body** | Consejo Nacional de Beneficios Tributarios en Ciencia, Tecnología e Innovación (CNBT) |
| **Administrative Authority** | MinCiencias (Ministerio de Ciencia, Tecnología e Innovación) |
| **Regulation** | Decree 1011/2020; CONPES Document 4069/2022 |
| **Project Registration** | Projects must be registered on the MinCiencias SIGP (Sistema Integrado de Gestión de Proyectos) platform |
| **Evaluation Criteria** | Scientific merit, technological innovation, potential economic impact, alignment with national CTI policy |
| **Approval Timeline** | Typically 60–90 days from complete submission |
| **Annual Cupo (Budget)** | CNBT allocates an annual national budget (*cupo*) for tax benefits; total benefits are capped at the national level |
| **CONPES Allocation** | CONPES (National Council for Economic and Social Policy) determines the overall annual fiscal allocation for CTI tax benefits |

### 2.5 Other Related Incentives

| Parameter | Detail |
|---|---|
| **VAT Exemption on R&D Equipment** | ET Art. 428-1 — VAT exemption on imports of equipment and materials for qualified CTI projects |
| **Income Exclusion for Software** | ET Art. 206-1 — preferential treatment for income from new software developed in Colombia (7-year exclusion — modified by recent reforms) |
| **Free Trade Zones** | Special Economic Zones offering 20% CIT rate (vs. 35% standard) for qualifying industrial activities, including technology |
| **Orange Economy** | Law 1834/2017 — incentives for creative and cultural industries, including technology-driven content creation |

## 3. Patent Box / IP Regime

**No formal patent box.** Colombia does not have a traditional patent box or IP box regime. However, certain preferential treatments exist for IP-derived income:

| Parameter | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Software Income Exclusion** | ET Art. 206-1 (as modified) — new software developed and registered in Colombia may benefit from partial income exclusion (subject to conditions and recent legislative changes under Law 2277/2022) |
| **Duration** | Previously 7 years; subject to modification under recent tax reforms |
| **Conditions** | Software must be new, developed in Colombia, and registered with the National Copyright Directorate (*Dirección Nacional de Derecho de Autor*) |
| **Standard CIT Rate** | 35% (ET Art. 240; set by Law 2277/2022) |
| **OECD Status** | Colombia is an OECD member (joined 2020); subject to BEPS Action 5 commitments |

## 4. Effective Benefit Analysis

**Assumptions:** COP equivalent of US$1,000,000 R&D spend, 35% CIT rate, project approved by CNBT/MinCiencias, profitable entity.

### Option A: Tax Discount (Art. 256-1)

| Component | Calculation | Tax Benefit |
|---|---|---|
| **Ordinary Deduction (100%)** | US$1,000,000 × 100% × 35% | **US$350,000** |
| **25% Tax Discount** | US$1,000,000 × 25% | **US$250,000** |
| **Total Tax Benefit** | US$350,000 + US$250,000 | **US$600,000** |
| **Effective Benefit Rate** | US$600,000 ÷ US$1,000,000 | **60.0%** |

### Option B: Additional Deduction (Art. 158-1)

| Component | Calculation | Tax Benefit |
|---|---|---|
| **Total Deduction (200%)** | US$1,000,000 × 200% × 35% | **US$700,000** |
| **Effective Benefit Rate** | US$700,000 ÷ US$1,000,000 | **70.0%** |

### Comparison

| Option | Tax Benefit (US$) | Effective Rate | Best For |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Option A (Tax Discount)** | US$600,000 | 60.0% | Companies with sufficient tax liability; provides direct cash benefit |
| **Option B (Super-Deduction)** | US$700,000 | 70.0% | Companies with high taxable income; higher total benefit but dependent on profitability |

> **Important Caveats:** (1) The Art. 256-1 discount is capped at 25% of total income tax liability per year; (2) CNBT approval is mandatory and subject to annual national budget (*cupo*) limits; (3) the same expenditure cannot benefit from both options; (4) carry-forward of 4 years applies to the tax discount.

## 5. Claim Process & Documentation

### Filing Process

| Step | Detail |
|---|---|
| **1. Design CTI Project** | Develop a qualifying science, technology, and innovation project aligned with CNBT criteria |
| **2. Register on SIGP** | Register the project on the MinCiencias SIGP platform (Sistema Integrado de Gestión de Proyectos) |
| **3. CNBT Evaluation** | CNBT evaluates the project for scientific merit, innovation, and compliance with CTI policy |
| **4. Obtain CNBT Approval** | Receive approval and certified amount of qualifying investment (*cupo*) |
| **5. Execute Project** | Conduct the R&D activities, maintaining detailed expenditure records |
| **6. Select Benefit Option** | Choose between Art. 256-1 (25% tax discount) or Art. 158-1 (100% additional deduction) |
| **7. File Tax Return** | Apply the selected benefit on the annual income tax return filed with DIAN (Dirección de Impuestos y Aduanas Nacionales) |
| **8. Submit Completion Report** | File project completion and expenditure report with MinCiencias |

### Key Deadlines

| Item | Deadline |
|---|---|
| **CNBT Application** | Annual application window (typically Q1–Q2; specific dates published by MinCiencias) |
| **CNBT Evaluation** | 60–90 days from submission |
| **Annual Income Tax Return** | April (for most corporate taxpayers; exact date set annually by DIAN) |
| **Completion Report** | Within deadlines established by MinCiencias (typically 3 months after project completion) |
| **Pre-Approval** | **Mandatory** — CNBT certification required before claiming benefits |
| **Carry-Forward Claim** | Within 4 years of the year the discount was generated |

### Documentation Requirements

- Detailed project proposal with scientific objectives, methodology, and expected outcomes
- Budget breakdown by qualifying expense category
- Researcher qualifications and CVs
- Time allocation records for R&D personnel
- Financial records (invoices, contracts, payroll) supporting claimed expenditures
- CNBT approval documentation and certified *cupo*
- Project progress and completion reports
- Contracts with external R&D collaborators (if applicable)
- Annual tax return supporting schedules (DIAN forms)
- MinCiencias SIGP registration confirmation

## 6. Recent Changes & Sunset Provisions

| Change | Effective Date | Detail |
|---|---|---|
| **Law 1819/2016** | January 2017 | Restructured R&D tax incentives; established Art. 256-1 (25% discount) and modified Art. 158-1 |
| **Law 1955/2019 (National Development Plan)** | May 2019 | Reaffirmed CTI tax benefits; increased CONPES annual allocation |
| **Decree 1011/2020** | July 2020 | Detailed regulations for CNBT operation and project evaluation criteria |
| **CONPES 4069/2022** | 2022 | Policy document increasing the annual national *cupo* for CTI tax benefits |
| **Law 2277/2022 (Tax Reform)** | January 2023 | Increased CIT rate to 35%; confirmed continuation of CTI tax benefits; modified some conditions |
| **CIT Rate Increase** | 2023 | CIT rate increased from 31% to 35% (Law 2277/2022, Art. 10); enhances value of deduction-based benefits |
| **MinCiencias Digitalization** | 2021–ongoing | SIGP platform improvements; streamlined application and evaluation process |
| **Sunset Provisions** | None explicit | CTI tax benefits are embedded in the Estatuto Tributario; no sunset clause, but subject to annual *cupo* allocations |
