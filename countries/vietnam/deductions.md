# Vietnam - Taxable Income Deductions

> **Jurisdiction**: National (General Department of Taxation — GDT)
> **Governing Law**: Corporate Income Tax Law No. 14/2008/QH12 (CIT Law); Decree 218/2013/ND-CP; Circular 78/2014/TT-BTC; Circular 96/2015/TT-BTC; Decree 132/2020/ND-CP
> **Tax Year**: 2024/2025
> **Currency**: VND (VND)

---

## 1. Depreciation & Capital Allowances

### 1.1 General Depreciation Rules

> **Legal Basis**: Circular 45/2013/TT-BTC (as amended by Circular 147/2016/TT-BTC)

The default method is **straight-line depreciation**. Accelerated depreciation and units-of-production methods are also permitted with conditions.

#### Useful Life Ranges (Circular 45/2013, Appendix 1)

| Asset Category | Minimum Life | Maximum Life | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Buildings and structures** | **25 years** | **50 years** | Depending on construction type |
| **Machinery and equipment (general)** | **7 years** | **15 years** | |
| **Machinery (specialised / heavy industry)** | **10 years** | **20 years** | |
| **Transport vehicles** | **6 years** | **10 years** | |
| **Office equipment and tools** | **3 years** | **8 years** | |
| **Computers and IT equipment** | **3 years** | **5 years** | |
| **Software** | **3 years** | **5 years** | |
| **Livestock (working animals)** | **4 years** | **15 years** | |
| **Perennial plants** | **6 years** | **40 years** | Depending on crop type |

### 1.2 Accelerated Depreciation

> **Legal Basis**: Circular 45/2013 Art. 13

| Parameter | Value |
|---|---|
| Maximum acceleration | **2x** the straight-line rate (shortened useful life to no less than 50% of minimum) |
| Eligibility | Assets subject to rapid technological change, heavy use, or corrosion |
| Approval | Must register with local tax authority |
| Condition | Enterprise must be profitable (accelerated depreciation must not cause a tax loss) [unverified — confirm current enforcement practice] |

### 1.3 Intangible Assets

> **Legal Basis**: Circular 45/2013 Art. 9

| Asset Type | Amortisation Period |
|---|---|
| Land use rights (definite term) | Over the term of the right |
| Land use rights (indefinite) | Not amortised; recoverable on disposal |
| Software | 3–5 years |
| Patents, licences | Contract/legal term (max 20 years if no defined term) |
| Goodwill | Not more than **20 years** (if no defined term) |
| Brand names, trademarks | Legal/contractual term |

## 2. Business Operating Expenses

### 2.1 General Deductibility

> **Legal Basis**: CIT Law Art. 9; Decree 218/2013 Art. 6; Circular 78/2014 Art. 6

Expenses are deductible if they are:
1. Actually incurred and related to business activities
2. Supported by legitimate invoices and documents
3. Paid via non-cash methods for transactions ≥ **VND 20 million** (proposed to change to VND 5 million under new CIT Law) [unverified — confirm current threshold]

| Expense Category | Deductibility | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Salaries, wages, bonuses | **100%** | Must be actually paid; accruals not yet paid are non-deductible |
| Social insurance (employer: ~17.5%) | **100%** | Pension (14%), sickness/maternity (3%), occupational accident (0.5%) |
| Health insurance (employer 3%) | **100%** | |
| Unemployment insurance (employer 1%) | **100%** | |
| Rent and lease payments | **100%** | With valid invoice |
| Repairs and maintenance | **100%** | Revenue nature |
| Insurance premiums (business) | **100%** | Property, liability |
| Travel expenses (business) | **100%** | Per company policy; documented |
| Advertising and marketing | **100%** | No cap (since 2015); must have invoice |
| Bad debts written off | **100%** | Must meet conditions (2 years overdue, documented collection efforts) |
| Professional fees | **100%** | With valid invoice |

### 2.2 Employee Welfare

> **Legal Basis**: Circular 78/2014 Art. 6(2.30)

| Item | Limit |
|---|---|
| Total employee welfare expenses | Capped at 1 month's average actual salary per year |
| Includes | Mid-shift meals, health check-ups, employee events, employee family support |

### 2.3 Charitable Donations

> **Legal Basis**: CIT Law Art. 9(1); Decree 218/2013 Art. 6

| Parameter | Value |
|---|---|
| Donations to education, health care, natural disasters, social housing | **Deductible** (through authorised organisations) |
| Donations to poor/disadvantaged areas | **Deductible** |
| Non-qualifying donations | **Non-deductible** |
| Cap | No specific percentage cap, but must be for qualifying purposes and properly documented |

## 3. Interest & Financing Costs

### 3.1 General Deductibility

> **Legal Basis**: Circular 78/2014 Art. 6; Decree 132/2020

| Parameter | Value |
|---|---|
| Bank loans | Interest deductible at commercial bank rate |
| Non-bank loans (individual/enterprise) | Deductible up to **150%** of the State Bank base rate at the time of borrowing |
| Related-party loans | Subject to thin-cap and TP rules |

### 3.2 Thin Capitalisation / Interest Limitation

> **Legal Basis**: Decree 132/2020/ND-CP Art. 16 (as amended by Decree 20/2025/ND-CP)

| Parameter | Value |
|---|---|
| Interest deduction cap | **30%** of EBITDA (net interest) |
| Application | All enterprises with related-party transactions |
| Carry-forward of disallowed interest | **5 years** |
| EBITDA definition | Net profit + net interest expense + depreciation/amortisation |
| Retroactive effect | Applied from fiscal year 2019 |
| Related-party definition | 25% capital/voting threshold; various other criteria under Decree 132 |

> Note: No formal debt-to-equity ratio test. The 30% EBITDA cap is the primary interest limitation mechanism.

## 4. Research & Development

Vietnam provides preferential CIT rates (10% for 15 years) and tax holidays (up to 4-year exemption + 9-year 50% reduction) for enterprises engaged in scientific research and technological development in areas encouraged by the state. R&D expenditure is deductible as a business expense. There is no separate super deduction mechanism.

See [R&D Incentives](rnd_incentives.md) for full program detail.

## 5. Loss Carry-Forward & Carry-Back

> **Legal Basis**: CIT Law Art. 9(1); Circular 78/2014 Art. 7

| Parameter | Value |
|---|---|
| Carry-forward period | **5 years** from the year of the loss |
| Carry-back | **Not available** |
| Limitation | 100% of taxable income (no percentage cap) |
| Transferred losses | In mergers/acquisitions, the acquired entity's losses may be carried forward by the surviving entity |
| Assignment | Losses must be fully carried forward in the earliest possible year; cannot selectively skip years |

## 6. Provisions & Reserves

> **Legal Basis**: Circular 228/2009/TT-BTC (as amended)

| Provision Type | Deductibility | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Bad debt provisions (doubtful debts) | **Deductible** | Specific rates: 30% (6 months–1 year overdue), 50% (1–2 years), 70% (2–3 years), 100% (3+ years) |
| Inventory obsolescence provisions | **Deductible** | Based on evidence of decline in value |
| Investment loss provisions | **Deductible** | For long-term financial investments with value decline |
| Warranty provisions | **Not deductible** | General warranty accruals not recognised |
| Employee severance provisions | **Deductible** | Under labour law requirements |
| General contingent provisions | **Not deductible** | Until liability crystallises |

## 7. Industry-Specific Deductions

### 7.1 CIT Incentive Zones

> **Legal Basis**: CIT Law Art. 13–14; Decree 218/2013

| Zone / Activity | CIT Rate | Tax Holiday |
|---|---|---|
| **Special economic zones, difficult areas** | **10%** for 15 years | 4-year exemption + 9-year 50% reduction |
| **Industrial parks in difficult areas** | **10%** for 15 years | 2-year exemption + 4-year 50% reduction |
| **Encouraged sectors (education, health, environment)** | **10%** for entire life | Varies |
| **High-tech enterprises** | **10%** for 15 years | 4 + 9 |
| **Standard incentive zones** | **17%** for 10 years | 2 + 4 |

### 7.2 Software / IT Services

| Parameter | Value |
|---|---|
| CIT rate | **10%** for 15 years |
| Tax holiday | 4-year exemption + 9-year 50% reduction |
| Condition | Must be certified software enterprise |

### 7.3 Export Processing Enterprises (EPEs)

EPEs in industrial/export processing zones benefit from CIT incentives based on location. Import duty exemptions on raw materials and equipment complement the CIT deduction regime.

## 8. Treaties & Transfer Pricing

### 8.1 Transfer Pricing

> **Legal Basis**: Decree 132/2020/ND-CP (as amended by Decree 20/2025/ND-CP)

| Parameter | Value |
|---|---|
| Arm's length standard | Mandatory for all related-party transactions |
| Methods | CUP, resale price, cost plus, comparable profit, profit split |
| Documentation | TP disclosure form (Appendix I–IV of Decree 132) filed with annual CIT return |
| Local File | Required for enterprises with related-party transactions |
| Master File | Required if part of a group with consolidated revenue ≥ VND 18 trillion |
| CbC reporting | Required if group consolidated revenue ≥ VND 18 trillion |
| Penalties | Tax arrears + 0.03%/day interest; understatement penalties of 20% |
| APA | Available — unilateral and bilateral (Circular 45/2021/TT-BTC) |

### 8.2 Related-Party Definition

Under Decree 132/2020, related parties include entities with:
- ≥ 25% capital contribution / voting rights
- Guaranteed debt ≥ 25% of equity
- ≥ 50% of management appointed by the other party
- Various other criteria (loans > 25% of equity, same management control, etc.)

### 8.3 Treaty Network

Vietnam has over 80 double tax agreements. Treaties reduce withholding taxes and provide dispute resolution mechanisms.
