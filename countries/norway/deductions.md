# Norway - Taxable Income Deductions

> **Jurisdiction**: National (Skatteetaten)
> **Governing Law**: Lov om skatt av formue og inntekt 1999 — Skatteloven (Sktl.)
> **Tax Year**: 2024/2025
> **Currency**: NOK (kr)

---

## 1. Depreciation & Capital Allowances

Norway uses a declining balance (saldometode) pooled depreciation system for most tangible assets. The system is codified in Skatteloven §14-40 through §14-48.

### 1.1 Depreciation Groups — Sktl. §14-41

All depreciable business assets are assigned to one of eight groups (saldogrupper):

| Group | Asset Type | Maximum Rate | Statutory Basis |
|---|---|---|---|
| **A** | Office equipment, tools | 30% | Sktl. §14-43 (1) a |
| **B** | Vehicles, trailers, machinery for construction | 20% (24% for vans/trucks under 7,500 kg) | Sktl. §14-43 (1) b |
| **C** | Vessels, drilling rigs | 14% | Sktl. §14-43 (1) c |
| **D** | Machinery, instruments, fixtures | 20% | Sktl. §14-43 (1) d |
| **E** | Ships, vessels (larger) | 14% | Sktl. §14-43 (1) e |
| **F** | Aircraft, helicopters | 12% | Sktl. §14-43 (1) f |
| **G** | Technical installations in buildings | 10% (or 6% for certain structures) | Sktl. §14-43 (1) g |
| **H** | Buildings, hotels, warehouses | 4% (6% for agricultural buildings, 10% for temporary structures) | Sktl. §14-43 (1) h |

### 1.2 Key Depreciation Rules

| Parameter | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Method** | Declining balance (saldometode) — Sktl. §14-40 |
| **Pooling** | Groups A-D: pooled (all assets in group combined). Groups E-H: individual asset basis — Sktl. §14-41 |
| **Residual value** | When pool balance falls below NOK 15,000, the remaining amount may be fully expensed — Sktl. §14-47 |
| **Improvement costs** | Added to the depreciation pool — Sktl. §14-40 (3) |
| **Disposal** | Proceeds reduce the pool balance; negative balance taxed as gain — Sktl. §14-44 |
| **Land** | Not depreciable |

### 1.3 Intangible Assets

| Asset Type | Treatment | Statutory Basis |
|---|---|---|
| **Goodwill (acquired)** | 20% declining balance (Group B treatment) | Sktl. §14-41 (1) b [unverified — confirm with local counsel] |
| **Patents, licenses** | Amortized over useful life; or pooled if no determinable life | Sktl. §14-50 |
| **Self-developed intangibles** | Expensed as incurred | Sktl. §6-25 |
| **R&D costs** | Immediately deductible | Sktl. §6-25 |

### 1.4 Initial Allowance for Climate-Friendly Investments

| Parameter | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Extra depreciation** | 10% initial deduction for qualifying climate-friendly machinery — Sktl. §14-43 (4) [unverified — confirm with local counsel] |
| **Scope** | Zero-emission vehicles and specific green technology |

## 2. Business Operating Expenses

General deductibility is governed by Sktl. §6-1: expenses incurred to acquire, secure, or maintain taxable income are deductible.

### 2.1 Fully Deductible Expenses — Sktl. §6-1

| Expense Category | Deductibility | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| **Employee salaries & wages** | 100% | Including bonuses and holiday pay |
| **Employer social security (arbeidsgiveravgift)** | 100% | 14.1% standard rate (zone-based rates from 0% to 14.1%) — Folketrygdloven §23-2 |
| **Rent (business premises)** | 100% | Must be business-related |
| **Insurance premiums** | 100% | Business-related risks |
| **Professional fees** | 100% | Legal, audit, consulting |
| **Advertising & marketing** | 100% | Business purpose required |
| **Travel expenses** | 100% | Business travel (rate-based deductions for subsistence) |
| **Training & education** | 100% | Job-related employee training — Sktl. §6-1 |
| **Bad debts (realized)** | 100% | Must be definitively established as uncollectible — Sktl. §6-2 (2) |

### 2.2 Partially Deductible / Restricted

| Expense Category | Limit | Statutory Basis |
|---|---|---|
| **Representation & entertainment** | Non-deductible for income tax purposes | Sktl. §6-21 |
| **Gifts to business contacts** | NOK 298 per gift [unverified — confirm with local counsel] | Skatteetaten administrative guidance |
| **Pension contributions** | Deductible within statutory limits (OTP, mandatory occupational pension) | Obligatorisk tjenestepensjonsloven (OTP-loven) |
| **Company cars** | Benefit-in-kind taxed to employee; employer cost deductible | Sktl. §5-13 |

## 3. Interest & Financing Costs

### 3.1 General Deductibility — Sktl. §6-40

Interest on debt is generally deductible if incurred in connection with business activities — Sktl. §6-40.

### 3.2 EBITDA-Based Interest Limitation — Sktl. §6-41

| Parameter | Detail |
|---|---|
| **General rule** | Net interest expenses deductible up to 25% of tax EBITDA — Sktl. §6-41 (3) |
| **De minimis** | NOK 25M threshold — net interest expenses up to NOK 25M are always deductible — Sktl. §6-41 (4) |
| **Scope** | Applies to both related-party and third-party interest — Sktl. §6-41 (1) |
| **Carry-forward** | Non-deductible interest may be carried forward for up to 10 years — Sktl. §6-41 (8) |
| **Equity escape rule** | If the entity's equity ratio equals or exceeds the consolidated group's equity ratio (±2 percentage points), the limitation does not apply — Sktl. §6-41 (5) |
| **Financial sector** | Modified rules for banks and financial institutions — Sktl. §6-41 (9) |

### 3.3 Related-Party Debt — Sktl. §13-1

| Parameter | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Arm's length** | Related-party transactions must be at arm's length — Sktl. §13-1 |
| **Recharacterization** | Tax authorities may recharacterize excessive debt as equity |

## 4. Research & Development

Norway offers the SkatteFUNN tax credit scheme (Sktl. §16-40 and §16-41) providing a tax credit of 19% on qualifying R&D expenditures for all companies, regardless of size. The credit is refundable if exceeding tax liability. Qualifying costs are capped at NOK 25M for in-house R&D and NOK 50M for R&D conducted with approved research institutions.

See [R&D Incentives](rnd_incentives.md) for full program detail.

## 5. Loss Carry-Forward & Carry-Back

### 5.1 Loss Carry-Forward — Sktl. §14-6

| Parameter | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Duration** | **Indefinite** carry-forward — Sktl. §14-6 |
| **Annual cap** | **No cap** — 100% of taxable income may be offset |
| **Ownership change restriction** | Anti-avoidance rule: losses may be denied if acquisition of company was primarily for the purpose of utilizing losses — Sktl. §14-90 |
| **Merger/demerger** | Losses may transfer in qualifying tax-free reorganizations — Sktl. §11-7 |

### 5.2 Loss Carry-Back

| Parameter | Detail |
|---|---|
| **General** | **Not permitted** under general Norwegian tax law |
| **Petroleum companies** | 2-year carry-back available under the Petroleum Tax Act — Petroleumsskatteloven §3c (4) |
| **COVID-19 temporary measure** | A temporary carry-back of 2020 losses against 2018/2019 income was available (now expired) |

### 5.3 Group Contributions (Konsernbidrag) — Sktl. §10-2 to §10-4

| Parameter | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Availability** | Between Norwegian companies with ≥90% ownership — Sktl. §10-2 |
| **Effect** | Deductible for payer, taxable for recipient |
| **Cross-border** | Limited availability for EEA-resident group companies (final loss doctrine) — Sktl. §10-4a |
| **Requirement** | Group relationship must have existed for the entire year — Sktl. §10-4 |

## 7. Industry-Specific Deductions

### 7.1 Petroleum Sector — Petroleumsskatteloven

Petroleumsskatteloven §3 establishes the general deductibility framework for companies engaged in petroleum activities on the Norwegian continental shelf, providing that costs incurred in connection with petroleum production are deductible. Specific deduction categories in §§3b, 3c, and 5 derive from this foundational provision.

| Deduction | Detail | Statutory Basis |
|---|---|---|
| **General deductibility** | Costs incurred in connection with petroleum production are deductible | Petroleumsskatteloven §3 |
| **Special petroleum tax** | 71.8% total marginal rate (22% corporate + 49.8% special tax [unverified — confirm with local counsel]) | Petroleumsskatteloven §5 |
| **Uplift (friinntekt)** | Extra deduction on investment costs (currently 0% after 2024 reform [unverified — confirm with local counsel]) | Petroleumsskatteloven §5 (previously 5.2%/year over 4 years) |
| **Immediate expensing** | Investment costs may be expensed in year of incurrence against special tax base | Petroleumsskatteloven §3b |
| **Exploration costs** | Fully deductible; refundable tax value for companies not yet in tax position — Petroleumsskatteloven §3c | Petroleumsskatteloven §3c |
| **Loss carry-back** | 2-year carry-back available for petroleum losses — Petroleumsskatteloven §3c (4) | Petroleumsskatteloven §3c (4) |

### 7.2 Shipping (Tonnage Tax Regime) — Sktl. §8-10 to §8-20

| Parameter | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Regime** | Optional tonnage-based taxation — Sktl. §8-10 |
| **Tax base** | Tonnage-based notional income (effectively zero tax on shipping income) |
| **Ring-fencing** | Shipping income/losses ring-fenced from other activities — Sktl. §8-15 |
| **Qualifying activities** | Operation of qualifying vessels in international traffic |
| **Financial assets** | Financial income within the regime taxed at ordinary rate |

### 7.3 Hydropower — Skatteloven §18-3

| Deduction | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Resource rent tax** | 45% resource rent tax (grunnrenteskatt) in addition to 22% corporate tax — Sktl. §18-3 |
| **Standard deduction** | A standard deduction (friinntekt) in the resource rent tax base |
| **Natural resource tax** | NOK 0.013/kWh to municipalities — Sktl. §18-2 [unverified — confirm with local counsel] |

## 9. Non-Deductible Items

### 9.1 Expressly Non-Deductible — Sktl. §6-22; §6-51

| Expense | Reason | Statutory Basis |
|---|---|---|
| **Fines and penalties** | Not incurred for business purposes | Sktl. §6-22 |
| **Bribes and corrupt payments** | Prohibited under Straffeloven | Sktl. §6-22 |
| **Income taxes** | Not a deductible business expense | Sktl. §6-15 |
| **Cash payments exceeding NOK 10,000** | Non-deductible if paid in cash to the same payee per transaction | Sktl. §6-51 |
| **Representation and entertainment** | Non-deductible | Sktl. §6-21 |
| **Private/personal expenses** | Not business-related | Sktl. §6-1 (negative inference) |
| **Dividends paid** | Distribution of profits | N/A |
| **Capital losses on qualifying shares** | Participation exemption applies | Sktl. §2-38 |
| **General provisions** | Only specific, realized losses deductible | Sktl. §6-2 |
| **Excessive interest** | Interest exceeding EBITDA limitation | Sktl. §6-41 |

### 9.2 Participation Exemption (Fritaksmetoden) — Sktl. §2-38

| Parameter | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Scope** | Dividends and capital gains on shares in Norwegian and EEA companies — Sktl. §2-38 |
| **3% inclusion** | 3% of exempt dividends are included in taxable income (effective rate 0.66%) — Sktl. §2-38 (6) |
| **Exclusion** | Does not apply to portfolio investments in companies resident in low-tax jurisdictions |
| **Effect on losses** | Capital losses on qualifying shares are non-deductible |
