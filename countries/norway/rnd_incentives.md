# Norway - R&D Tax Incentives

## 1. Overview

| Feature | Details |
|---|---|
| **Primary Incentive** | SkatteFUNN Tax Credit |
| **Type** | Refundable tax credit |
| **Base Rate** | 19% of qualifying R&D expenditure |
| **Maximum In-House R&D** | NOK 25 million per year |
| **Maximum Contract R&D** | NOK 50 million per year (combined with in-house) |
| **Legal Basis** | Skatteloven (Tax Act) §16-40 |
| **Administering Body** | Research Council of Norway (Norges forskningsråd) |
| **Refundability** | Fully refundable — excess credit paid as cash |
| **Eligibility** | All companies regardless of size, sector, or profitability |
| **Patent Box** | None — Norway does not operate a patent box regime |

## 2. Detailed Programs

### 2.1 SkatteFUNN Tax Credit Scheme

**Legal Basis:** Skatteloven (Norwegian Tax Act) §16-40, supplemented by FSFIN §16-40 (regulation)

The SkatteFUNN scheme is Norway's primary R&D incentive, providing a direct tax credit for approved research and development projects. It is designed to be broadly accessible to all Norwegian businesses.

#### Credit Rates & Expenditure Caps

| Parameter | Amount |
|---|---|
| Credit rate (all companies) | 19% |
| In-house R&D expenditure cap | NOK 25,000,000 per annum |
| Contract R&D expenditure cap (combined total) | NOK 50,000,000 per annum |
| Maximum credit (in-house only) | NOK 4,750,000 |
| Maximum credit (with contract R&D) | NOK 9,500,000 |
| Hourly rate cap for personnel | NOK 1.2× per hour (based on actual costs) |

**Note:** Prior to 2020, SMEs received a higher rate (20%) vs. large enterprises (18%). The rate was unified to 19% from the 2020 tax year onward.

#### Qualifying R&D Activities

- Systematic and organized research or development work
- Projects aimed at acquiring new knowledge, information, or experience
- Development of new or improved products, services, or processes
- Must have a clear element of novelty and scientific/technical uncertainty

#### Qualifying Expenditure

| Cost Category | Eligible |
|---|---|
| Personnel costs (direct R&D staff) | Yes |
| Purchased R&D services (from approved institutions) | Yes |
| Materials and supplies for R&D | Yes (if directly consumed) |
| Equipment costs | Depreciation portion used in R&D |
| Indirect costs / overhead | Limited — must be directly attributable |
| Patent filing costs | Yes |
| Prototype and pilot plant costs | Yes |

#### Contract R&D Requirements

- The contractor must be an approved R&D institution (university, research institute, or approved entity listed by the Research Council)
- The company must retain IP ownership and bear the financial risk
- Combined in-house and contract R&D cannot exceed NOK 50 million

### 2.2 Project Pre-Approval Process

All SkatteFUNN projects require **pre-approval** from the Research Council of Norway before claiming the credit:

1. **Application Submission:** Filed via the SkatteFUNN online portal (skattefunn.no)
2. **Project Assessment:** The Research Council evaluates the R&D content, novelty, and scientific merit
3. **Approval Duration:** Projects can be approved for up to 3 years
4. **Approval Deadline:** Applications can be submitted year-round; projects approved by year-end qualify for that tax year
5. **Annual Reporting:** Companies must submit annual project reports to the Research Council

### 2.3 Refundability Mechanism

A key feature of SkatteFUNN is its **full refundability**:

- If the tax credit exceeds the company's tax liability, the excess is refunded as a direct cash payment
- This makes the scheme particularly valuable for **startups and pre-revenue companies**
- The refund is paid together with the ordinary tax settlement (typically October following the tax year)
- The refund can also be assigned/pledged to a bank as collateral for financing

## 3. Patent Box / IP Regime

Norway **does not** currently operate a patent box or preferential IP income regime.

- There is no reduced tax rate for income derived from patents or qualifying intellectual property
- Standard corporate income tax rate of **22%** applies to all income, including royalties and IP licensing income
- Norway has historically relied on direct R&D support (SkatteFUNN and grant programs) rather than output-based IP incentives
- No legislative proposals for a patent box regime are currently under active consideration

### Alternative IP Considerations

| Feature | Details |
|---|---|
| Standard CIT rate on IP income | 22% |
| Withholding tax on outbound royalties | 0% (Norway generally does not impose WHT on royalties) |
| R&D grant programs | Innovation Norway, Research Council grants |
| Tax treatment of IP sale gains | Taxed at standard 22% CIT rate |

## 4. Effective Benefit Analysis (per $1M USD R&D Spend)

*Assumptions: Exchange rate ~NOK 10.5/USD; spend within SkatteFUNN caps; company is tax-paying at 22% CIT*

| Benefit Component | Calculation | Value (USD) |
|---|---|---|
| Base R&D expenditure | — | $1,000,000 |
| SkatteFUNN credit (19%) | $1,000,000 × 19% | $190,000 |
| Regular CIT deduction (22%) | $1,000,000 × 22% | $220,000 |
| **Total combined benefit** | Credit + deduction | **$410,000** |
| **Effective rate of support** | Total / spend | **41.0%** |

### Scenario Comparison

| Company Type | CIT Deduction | SkatteFUNN Credit | Total Benefit | Effective Rate |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Profitable company (22% CIT) | $220,000 | $190,000 | $410,000 | 41.0% |
| Loss-making / startup (0% CIT) | $0 (carried forward) | $190,000 (refunded) | $190,000 | 19.0% |
| Startup with contract R&D ($2M) | $0 | $380,000 (refunded) | $380,000 | 19.0% |

**Key Advantage:** The refundable nature ensures startups receive immediate cash benefit regardless of profitability.

## 5. Claim Process & Documentation

### Step-by-Step Claim Process

1. **Project Application** — Submit R&D project application to the Research Council of Norway via skattefunn.no
2. **Pre-Approval** — Obtain formal approval (typically 4-8 weeks processing time)
3. **Conduct R&D** — Execute approved project activities during the tax year
4. **Annual Project Report** — Submit to the Research Council by March 1 following the tax year
5. **Tax Return Filing** — Include SkatteFUNN claim in the corporate tax return (RF-1053 form)
6. **Auditor Attestation** — The claim must be verified by the company's auditor (required)
7. **Tax Assessment** — Norwegian Tax Administration (Skatteetaten) assesses the claim
8. **Credit/Refund** — Applied against tax liability or refunded (October settlement)

### Documentation Requirements

| Requirement | Details |
|---|---|
| Project description | Must describe R&D content, objectives, and novelty |
| Time records | Hour-by-hour tracking per employee per project |
| Cost allocation records | Detailed breakdown of all qualifying expenditure |
| Technical progress reports | Documenting scientific/technical advancement |
| Contract R&D agreements | Contracts with approved research institutions |
| Auditor's attestation (RF-1053) | Mandatory annual certification |
| Separation of R&D vs. routine activities | Clear delineation required |

### Common Pitfalls

- Failing to obtain pre-approval before starting the project
- Inadequate timekeeping records for personnel costs
- Including routine/non-R&D activities in the claim
- Exceeding the expenditure caps without adjusting the claim
- Late submission of annual project reports

## 6. Recent Changes & Sunset Provisions

| Year | Change | Impact |
|---|---|---|
| 2020 | Unified credit rate to 19% (previously 18%/20% split) | Simplified; slight reduction for SMEs |
| 2020 | Increased contract R&D cap from NOK 25M to NOK 50M | Doubled maximum benefit for outsourced R&D |
| 2020 | Increased in-house cap from NOK 25M (maintained) | No change |
| 2023 | Tightened documentation and audit requirements | Greater scrutiny on time records |
| 2024 | Enhanced digital reporting requirements | Electronic submission of supporting documents |
| 2025 | No significant structural changes announced | Scheme continues with existing parameters |

### Sunset Provisions

- SkatteFUNN has **no formal sunset date** — it is a permanent feature of the Norwegian tax code
- The scheme has been operational since 2002 and has broad political support
- Parameters (rates, caps) are subject to annual budget adjustments
- The scheme is reviewed periodically by the Ministry of Finance

### Upcoming Considerations

- Ongoing discussions about increasing expenditure caps to keep pace with inflation
- Potential alignment with EU state aid rules (Norway participates via EEA Agreement)
- Continued focus on audit quality and compliance enforcement
