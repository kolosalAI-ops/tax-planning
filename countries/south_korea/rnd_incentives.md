# South Korea - R&D Tax Incentives

## 1. Overview

| Feature | Details |
|---|---|
| **Primary Incentive** | R&D Tax Credit (RSTA Art. 10) |
| **National Strategic Tech** | SME 50%, Large 40% credit rates |
| **General R&D** | SME 25%, Large up to 2% volume + 40% incremental |
| **Carry-Forward** | 10 years |
| **Strategic Technologies** | Semiconductors, batteries, vaccines, display, hydrogen |
| **Governing Authority** | National Tax Service (NTS); Ministry of Economy and Finance (MOEF) |
| **Key Legislation** | Restriction of Special Taxation Act (RSTA) Art. 10; Enforcement Decree |
| **Corporate Tax Rate** | 9-24% progressive (24% on income > KRW 300B) |

## 2. Detailed Programs

### 2.1 National Strategic Technology R&D Credit - RSTA Art. 10(1)

**Legal Basis:** Restriction of Special Taxation Act (Josae Teukrye Jehan Beop) Article 10, Paragraph 1

Introduced as part of the "K-Chips Act" and expanded in 2023 to provide world-leading R&D credits for designated national strategic technologies.

| Company Size | Credit Rate |
|---|---|
| **SME** | 50% of qualifying R&D expenditure |
| **Mid-sized Company** | 40% of qualifying R&D expenditure |
| **Large Company** | 40% of qualifying R&D expenditure |

**National Strategic Technologies (12 Categories):**

| Category | Examples |
|---|---|
| **Semiconductors** | Design, manufacturing, advanced packaging, equipment |
| **Secondary Batteries** | Next-gen batteries, solid-state, materials |
| **Display** | OLED, micro-LED, advanced display technologies |
| **Vaccines/Biopharmaceuticals** | mRNA platforms, cell/gene therapy, biosimilars |
| **Hydrogen** | Production, storage, fuel cells |
| **Future Mobility** | Autonomous driving, electric vehicles |
| **Robotics** | Industrial/service robots, AI integration |
| **Aerospace** | Satellites, launch vehicles, UAM |
| **Quantum Computing** | Quantum processors, algorithms |
| **AI** | Foundation models, AI chips |
| **Cybersecurity** | Zero-trust, encryption technologies |
| **Advanced Materials** | Carbon fiber, rare earth processing |

### 2.2 New Growth Engine & Source Technology Credit - RSTA Art. 10(1)

**Legal Basis:** RSTA Art. 10(1); Enforcement Decree Annex

| Company Size | Credit Rate |
|---|---|
| **SME** | 40% of qualifying R&D expenditure |
| **Mid-sized Company** | 30% of qualifying R&D expenditure |
| **Large Company** | 30% of qualifying R&D expenditure |

**Qualifying Areas:** 12th-generation new growth engine technologies including AI, IoT, blockchain, autonomous vehicles, biotechnology, and advanced manufacturing.

### 2.3 General R&D Tax Credit - RSTA Art. 10(1)

**Legal Basis:** RSTA Art. 10(1)

Companies can choose the higher of the volume-based or incremental methods:

**Method A: Volume-Based**

| Company Size | Credit Rate |
|---|---|
| **SME** | 25% of total qualifying R&D expenditure |
| **Mid-sized** | 8% of total qualifying R&D expenditure |
| **Large** | 0-2% of total qualifying R&D expenditure (based on R&D ratio) |

*Large company volume rate = max(0%, revenue-based R&D ratio × 50%), capped at 2%*

**Method B: Incremental**

| Company Size | Credit Rate |
|---|---|
| **SME** | 50% of incremental R&D expenditure |
| **Mid-sized** | 40% of incremental R&D expenditure |
| **Large** | 40% of incremental R&D expenditure |

*Incremental = current year R&D expenditure minus average of prior 4 years*

### 2.4 Facility Investment Tax Credit - RSTA Art. 24

| Parameter | Details |
|---|---|
| **National Strategic** | SME 25%, Large 15% |
| **New Growth** | SME 12%, Large 3% |
| **General** | SME 10%, Large 1% |
| **Scope** | Capital expenditure on R&D facilities and equipment |

### 2.5 Carry-Forward and Cap

| Parameter | Details |
|---|---|
| **Carry-Forward** | 10 years for unused R&D tax credits |
| **Minimum Tax** | R&D credits can reduce tax to minimum tax level |
| **Minimum Tax Rate** | SME: 7% of taxable income; Large: 10-17% |
| **Stacking** | Multiple credits can be combined within minimum tax constraints |

## 3. Patent Box / IP Regime

South Korea does not have a formal patent box regime.

| Aspect | Status |
|---|---|
| **Patent Box** | Not available |
| **IP Amortization** | Patents: 7 years; Utility models: 5 years; Software: 5 years |
| **IP Transfer** | Subject to transfer pricing rules (International Tax Coordination Act) |
| **Royalty WHT** | 20% (reduced under tax treaties; many treaties provide 0-15%) |
| **Technology Transfer Deduction** | Income from technology transfer to SMEs may receive deductions |
| **Alternative** | Extremely generous R&D credit rates (up to 50%) serve as IP creation incentive |

**Note:** Korea's approach is to front-load incentives through R&D credits rather than back-load through patent boxes. The 50% SME credit for national strategic technologies is among the world's most generous.

## 4. Effective Benefit Analysis (per $1M USD R&D Spend)

*Assuming KRW/USD exchange rate of 1,300; KRW 1.3B equivalent spend; 24% max CIT rate*

| Scenario | Credit Rate | Tax Credit (KRW) | Tax Credit (USD) | Effective Subsidy |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **National Strategic (SME)** | 50% | KRW 650M | ~$500,000 | 50.0% |
| **National Strategic (Large)** | 40% | KRW 520M | ~$400,000 | 40.0% |
| **New Growth (SME)** | 40% | KRW 520M | ~$400,000 | 40.0% |
| **New Growth (Large)** | 30% | KRW 390M | ~$300,000 | 30.0% |
| **General (SME volume)** | 25% | KRW 325M | ~$250,000 | 25.0% |
| **General (Large volume)** | 2% (max) | KRW 26M | ~$20,000 | 2.0% |
| **General (Large incremental)** | 40% of increase | Varies | Varies | Varies |

**Key Insight:** South Korea's national strategic technology credit at 50% for SMEs is one of the most generous R&D incentives globally. The 10-year carry-forward ensures startups and pre-profit companies can eventually utilize credits.

## 5. Claim Process & Documentation

### 5.1 Filing Process

| Step | Action | Timeline |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Classify R&D as national strategic, new growth, or general | Before or during R&D activities |
| 2 | Maintain contemporaneous records | Ongoing |
| 3 | Calculate credit using volume or incremental method | Year-end |
| 4 | File corporate tax return with R&D credit schedule | Within 3 months of fiscal year-end |
| 5 | NTS may request verification or audit | Post-filing |

### 5.2 Documentation Requirements

- **Technology classification:** Documentation linking R&D to strategic/new growth technology categories
- **Project records:** Technical descriptions, objectives, uncertainties addressed
- **Expenditure records:** Detailed breakdown by qualifying category
- **Personnel records:** Researcher qualifications, dedicated R&D personnel list
- **Prior year data:** 4-year R&D expenditure history for incremental method
- **Facility records:** For R&D facility investment credit claims

### 5.3 Qualifying Expenditure Categories

| Category | Included |
|---|---|
| **Personnel costs** | Researchers, R&D support staff salaries |
| **Materials** | Raw materials, reagents, consumables |
| **Outsourced R&D** | Payments to external research organizations |
| **Depreciation** | R&D-dedicated equipment and facilities |
| **IP costs** | Patent application and registration fees |
| **Testing/certification** | Product testing and quality certification |

## 6. Recent Changes & Sunset Provisions

| Change | Effective Date | Details |
|---|---|---|
| **K-Chips Act** | 2023 | Expanded national strategic tech categories to 12; increased credit rates |
| **Credit Rate Increases** | 2023 | Large company strategic: 30% → 40%; SME: 40% → 50% |
| **Facility Credit Enhancement** | 2023 | Large company strategic facility: 8% → 15% |
| **10-Year Carry-Forward** | 2023 | Extended from 5 years to 10 years |
| **New Growth Expansion** | 2024 | Added additional qualifying technology areas |
| **AI-Specific Credit** | 2024 | AI included in national strategic technology list |

**Sunset Provisions:**
- National strategic technology credits: Currently authorized through 2025 tax year; extension expected
- General R&D credits: Permanent feature of RSTA, reviewed in annual tax reforms
- Carry-forward: 10-year period is a permanent change
- Annual tax reform (December) by MOEF determines extensions and modifications
- Strong political support for semiconductor and battery R&D suggests continued generosity
