# South Korea - Taxable Income Deductions

> **Jurisdiction**: National (National Tax Service — NTS)
> **Governing Law**: Corporate Tax Act (법인세법 — CTA); Restriction of Special Taxation Act (조세특례제한법 — RSTA); Act for Coordination of International Tax Affairs (국제조세조정에관한법률 — ACITA)
> **Tax Year**: 2024/2025
> **Currency**: KRW (₩)

---

## 1. Depreciation & Capital Allowances

### 1.1 General Depreciation Methods

> **Legal Basis**: CTA Art. 23; CTA Enforcement Decree Art. 26

| Method | Description | Applicability |
|---|---|---|
| **Declining Balance (정률법)** | Accelerated method; default for tangible assets other than buildings | Machinery, vehicles, furniture |
| **Straight-Line (정액법)** | Equal annual deductions over useful life | Buildings (mandatory); intangibles |
| **Units of Production** | Output-based depreciation | Mining assets, quarries |

#### Key Asset Useful Lives (CTA Enforcement Decree, Appendix 5 & 6)

| Asset Category | Standard Useful Life | Range Allowed | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Reinforced concrete buildings** | **40 years** | 20–60 years | SL only |
| **Steel-frame buildings** | **30 years** | 15–45 years | SL only |
| **Wooden/other buildings** | **20 years** | 10–30 years | SL only |
| **General machinery & equipment** | **8–12 years** | ±25% of standard life | DB or SL |
| **Motor vehicles (passenger)** | **5 years** | 4–6 years | DB or SL |
| **Computers & electronics** | **4 years** | 3–5 years | DB or SL |
| **Furniture & fixtures** | **5–10 years** | ±25% | DB or SL |
| **Software (purchased)** | **5 years** | 3–7 years | SL |
| **Patents** | Legal remaining life | — | SL |
| **Goodwill** | **5 years** | — | SL; CTA Art. 24 |

Taxpayers may select useful lives within the ±25% range of the standard life specified by the NTS for each asset class.

### 1.2 Immediate Expensing for SMEs

> **Legal Basis**: RSTA Art. 28 (SME investment tax deduction)

| Parameter | Value |
|---|---|
| Eligible | SMEs (중소기업) as defined under RSTA |
| Investment tax credit | **10%** of qualifying asset investment (instead of depreciation) for facilities contributing to productivity |
| Alternative | 3% for medium enterprises, 1% for large enterprises (RSTA Art. 25) |

## 2. Business Operating Expenses

### 2.1 General Deductibility

> **Legal Basis**: CTA Art. 19 (general deduction rules for expenses)

| Expense Category | Deductibility | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Salaries, wages, bonuses | **100%** | Must be ordinary and necessary |
| Social insurance (employer share) | **100%** | National Pension (~4.5%), Health Insurance (~3.5%), Employment Insurance (~1.15%) |
| Rent and lease payments | **100%** | Operating leases |
| Repairs and maintenance | **100%** | Revenue nature |
| Insurance premiums | **100%** | Business-related |
| Travel expenses | **100%** | Business-related; reasonable amounts |
| Advertising and marketing | **100%** | |
| Bad debts (specific write-off) | **100%** | Meeting NTS criteria — CTA Art. 19-2 |
| Legal and professional fees | **100%** | Revenue nature |
| Training expenses | **100%** | Job-related; enhanced deduction available for SMEs |

### 2.2 Entertainment Expenses (접대비)

> **Legal Basis**: CTA Art. 25

| Parameter | Value |
|---|---|
| Basic limit | **KRW 36 million** per year (KRW 24 million for capital > KRW 10 billion) [unverified — confirm with local counsel] |
| Additional limit | Revenue-based: 0.03%–0.2% of revenue (sliding scale by revenue bracket) |
| Per-event limit | Meals with proper documentation: no per-event cap; must have credit card/tax receipt |
| Non-deductible | Amounts exceeding the aggregate annual limit |

### 2.3 Charitable Donations (기부금)

> **Legal Basis**: CTA Art. 24

| Donation Type | Limit |
|---|---|
| Statutory donations (법정기부금) — government, disaster relief | **100%** of taxable income |
| Designated donations (지정기부금) — approved nonprofits | **10%** of taxable income (before donation deduction) |
| Non-qualifying donations | **Non-deductible** |
| Carry-forward (excess designated donations) | **10 years** |

## 3. Interest & Financing Costs

### 3.1 General Deductibility

> **Legal Basis**: CTA Art. 28

Interest on business debt is generally deductible. Related-party interest must satisfy arm's length requirements.

### 3.2 Thin Capitalisation

> **Legal Basis**: ACITA Art. 14 (국외특수관계인에 대한 과소자본세제)

| Parameter | Value |
|---|---|
| Debt-to-equity ratio | **2:1** for related-party overseas loans |
| Excess interest | **Non-deductible** |
| Application | Loans from overseas related parties only |
| Exempt | Domestic related-party loans not subject to thin-cap rules |

### 3.3 EBITDA Interest Limitation

> **Legal Basis**: ACITA Art. 22-4 (국외특수관계인 이자비용 한도)

| Parameter | Value |
|---|---|
| Limitation | Net interest paid to overseas related parties limited to **30%** of adjusted EBITDA |
| De minimis | Not explicitly specified; applies alongside thin-cap rules |
| Carry-forward of disallowed interest | **7 years** [unverified — confirm with local counsel] |
| Interaction | Taxpayers must satisfy both the 2:1 DER test and the 30% EBITDA test |

### 3.4 Non-Business Interest

Interest on loans not connected to business income generation (e.g., loans to shareholders for personal use) is non-deductible.

## 4. Research & Development

South Korea offers substantial R&D tax incentives through the RSTA. The general R&D tax credit under RSTA Art. 10 provides 0%–25% credit for qualifying R&D expenditure depending on company size and R&D growth rate. SMEs benefit from a higher base rate (25%). National strategic technology R&D receives 30%–40% credits for SMEs and 20%–30% for large enterprises.

See [R&D Incentives](rnd_incentives.md) for full program detail.

## 5. Loss Carry-Forward & Carry-Back

### 5.1 Loss Carry-Forward

> **Legal Basis**: CTA Art. 13 (이월결손금)

| Parameter | Value |
|---|---|
| Duration | **15 years** forward |
| Utilisation limit (large companies) | **60%** of taxable income per year |
| Utilisation limit (SMEs) | **100%** of taxable income |
| Requirement | Must file blue-return (blue-return system similar to Japan's) |

### 5.2 Loss Carry-Back

> **Legal Basis**: CTA Art. 72

| Parameter | Value |
|---|---|
| Duration | **1 year** carry-back |
| Availability | **SMEs only** — CTA Art. 72(1) |
| Large companies | **Not available** |
| Mechanism | Refund of prior year tax paid |

## 6. Provisions & Reserves

> **Legal Basis**: CTA Art. 19-2; CTA Art. 33–35

| Provision Type | Deductibility | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Allowance for doubtful accounts (대손충당금) | **Deductible** | Limited to 1% of receivables or historical bad-debt ratio (whichever is higher) — CTA Art. 34 |
| Retirement benefit reserves | **Deductible** | Severance pay reserve up to statutory minimum — CTA Art. 33 |
| Warranty provisions | **Not deductible** | Until actual expenditure incurred |
| General reserves | **Not deductible** | |
| Insurance/financial reserves | **Deductible** | Industry-specific for banks, insurers |

## 7. Industry-Specific Deductions

### 7.1 SME Investment Tax Credits

> **Legal Basis**: RSTA Art. 5–Art. 28 (various provisions)

| Incentive | Benefit |
|---|---|
| Integrated investment tax credit (통합투자세액공제) | 10% for SMEs, 3% for medium, 1% for large (RSTA Art. 24) |
| Additional credit for increased investment | Extra 3–4% on incremental investment above baseline |
| National strategic technology facilities | 15% SME, 8% large enterprise |

### 7.2 Free Economic Zones / Special Zones

Companies in designated Free Economic Zones (FEZs) or Foreign Investment Zones may qualify for:
- 3–7 year corporate tax reduction (50%–100%)
- Local tax exemptions

### 7.3 Startup / Venture Company Incentives

> **Legal Basis**: RSTA Art. 6

Qualifying startup SMEs in technology sectors may receive a 50%–100% corporate tax reduction for the first 5 years.

## 8. Treaties & Transfer Pricing

### 8.1 Transfer Pricing

> **Legal Basis**: ACITA Art. 4–Art. 14

| Parameter | Value |
|---|---|
| Arm's length standard | Mandatory for all international related-party transactions |
| Methods | CUP, resale price, cost plus, TNMM, profit split, and other reasonable methods |
| Documentation | Contemporaneous TP documentation required — ACITA Art. 11 |
| Master File / Local File | Required for groups with cross-border transactions above thresholds |
| CbC reporting | Required for groups with consolidated revenue ≥ KRW 1 trillion |
| Penalties | 10%–40% understatement penalties; additional penalties for non-documentation |
| APA | Available — unilateral, bilateral, multilateral |

### 8.2 Treaty Network

South Korea has over 90 double tax agreements. The MLI was ratified and entered into force in 2020.
