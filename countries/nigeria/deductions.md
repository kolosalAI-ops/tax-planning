# Nigeria - Taxable Income Deductions

> **Jurisdiction**: Federal (Federal Inland Revenue Service — FIRS)
> **Governing Law**: Companies Income Tax Act (CITA), Cap C21 LFN 2004 (as amended); Nigeria Tax Act (NTA), 2025
> **Tax Year**: 2024/2025
> **Currency**: NGN (₦)

---

## 1. Depreciation & Capital Allowances

Accounting depreciation is **not deductible** for Nigerian tax purposes. Instead, the CITA (and now the NTA, 2025) provides capital allowances consisting of an **initial allowance** (claimed in the year the asset is first used) and an **annual allowance** (claimed in subsequent years on a straight-line basis until the asset is fully written down to a notional residual value of 1%).

### Capital Allowance Rates — Second Schedule, CITA (as amended by Finance Act 2023 and NTA 2025)

| Asset Class | Initial Allowance | Annual Allowance | Residual Value | Legal Basis |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Plant and machinery** | **50%** | **25%** | 1% (notional) | Second Schedule, CITA |
| **Motor vehicles** — general | **50%** | **25%** | 1% | Second Schedule, CITA |
| **Motor vehicles** — public transportation (buses, taxis) | **95%** | Nil | 5% | Second Schedule, CITA |
| **Furniture and fittings** | **25%** | **20%** | 1% | Second Schedule, CITA |
| **Buildings** — industrial | **15%** | **10%** | 1% | Second Schedule, CITA |
| **Buildings** — non-industrial | **15%** | **10%** | 1% | Second Schedule, CITA |
| **Agricultural plant** | **95%** | Nil | 5% | Second Schedule, CITA |
| **Mining** — exploration and drilling | **95%** | Nil | 5% | Second Schedule, CITA |
| **Ranching and plantation** | **30%** | **50%** | 1% | Second Schedule, CITA |
| **Housing estate** | **50%** | **25%** | 1% | Second Schedule, CITA |
| **Research equipment** | **50%** | **25%** | 1% | Second Schedule, CITA |
| **Containers** | **25%** | **33.33%** | 1% | Second Schedule, CITA |

### Investment Allowance (Abolished)

| Parameter | Detail |
|---|---|
| Former rate | **10%** one-off allowance on qualifying capital expenditure |
| Status | **Deleted** by Finance Act 2023 (effective for accounting periods ending on or after 1 September 2023) |
| NTA 2025 | The NTA replaces the variable initial + annual allowance system with uniform annual rates (10%, 20%, or 25%) and requires retention of 1% notional value until disposal |

### NTA 2025 — New Capital Allowance Regime

| Parameter | Detail |
|---|---|
| New system | Uniform annual depreciation rates: **10%**, **20%**, or **25%** depending on asset class |
| Effective date | For accounting periods commencing on or after 1 January 2026 (transitional rules apply) |
| Residual value | 1% notional value retained until disposal |
| Note | The existing CITA Second Schedule rates remain applicable for periods up to 31 December 2025 |

### Pre-Operating Expenditure

| Parameter | Detail |
|---|---|
| Treatment | Pre-production capital expenditure qualifies for capital allowances when the business commences operations |
| Revenue pre-operating costs | May be amortised over 5 years [unverified — confirm with FIRS guidance] |

## 2. Business Operating Expenses

### General Deductibility (§24, CITA)

Deductions are permitted for expenses incurred **wholly, reasonably, exclusively, and necessarily** for the purpose of producing the assessable profits, including:

| Expense Category | Deductibility | Legal Basis |
|---|---|---|
| Salaries, wages, and bonuses | **100%** deductible (arm's-length for related parties) | §24(a), CITA |
| Employer pension contributions | Deductible up to statutory limits (Pension Reform Act, 2014) | §24, CITA |
| Rent (business premises) | **100%** deductible | §24(a), CITA |
| Repairs and maintenance | **100%** deductible | §24(c), CITA |
| Insurance premiums (business) | **100%** deductible | §24(a), CITA |
| Professional fees | **100%** deductible | §24(a), CITA |
| Bad debts | Deductible when actually written off as irrecoverable and previously included in income | §24(d), CITA |
| Training and staff development | **100%** deductible | §24, CITA |
| Advertising and marketing | **100%** deductible | §24(a), CITA |
| Charitable donations (approved organisation) | Deductible up to **10%** of assessable profit | §24(g), CITA |
| Entertainment | Deductible if wholly and exclusively for business purposes; excessive amounts may be disallowed | §24(a) read with §25, CITA |

### Small Company Relief (NTA 2025)

| Parameter | Detail |
|---|---|
| Definition | Gross turnover ≤ NGN 50,000,000 and total fixed assets ≤ NGN 250,000,000 |
| Exclusion | Professional services companies do not qualify |
| Tax rate | **0%** corporate income tax |
| Legal basis | NTA, 2025 |

## 3. Interest & Financing Costs

### Interest Deduction (§24(b), CITA)

| Parameter | Detail |
|---|---|
| General rule | Interest on borrowed capital employed in the production of profits is deductible |
| Arm's-length | Interest on related-party loans must be at arm's-length rates |

### Thin Capitalisation — §16, CITA (as amended)

| Parameter | Detail |
|---|---|
| Original rule | Total debt not to exceed **70%** of total assets (70:100 debt-to-assets ratio, approximately 2.33:1 debt-to-equity); interest on excess non-deductible |
| NTA 2025 update | Interest deduction limited to **30%** of earnings before interest, depreciation, and amortisation (EBITDA); aligned with OECD BEPS Action 4 |
| Carry-forward | Disallowed interest may be carried forward for up to **5 years** [unverified — confirm carry-forward period under NTA] |
| Exempt entities | Banks, insurance companies, and other regulated financial institutions |
| Legal basis | §16, CITA; NTA, 2025 |

### VAT Compliance Linkage (NTA 2025)

| Parameter | Detail |
|---|---|
| New rule | Companies may no longer claim capital allowances or operating deductions for assets or services where VAT or import duty was evaded |
| Impact | Reinforces procurement compliance; all invoices must show VAT registration number |

## 4. Research & Development

R&D expenditure is deductible as a general business expense under §24(a), CITA, provided it is incurred wholly, reasonably, exclusively, and necessarily for the purpose of producing assessable profits. Capital expenditure on R&D equipment qualifies for capital allowances (50% initial + 25% annual, or under the NTA regime).

Nigeria does not offer an enhanced or super-deduction for R&D expenditure.

See [R&D Incentives](rnd_incentives.md) for full details on R&D deduction treatment and sector-specific incentives.

## 5. Loss Carry-Forward & Carry-Back

### Tax Losses — §27, CITA

| Parameter | Detail |
|---|---|
| Carry-forward | **4 years** (or **6 years** for losses from agricultural trade) — §27, CITA |
| NTA 2025 | Losses may be carried forward **indefinitely**, but utilisation limited to **50%** of taxable profit in any year [unverified — confirm the exact terms under NTA transitional provisions] |
| Carry-back | **Not permitted** |
| Ring-fencing | Losses from one trade may generally be offset against profits from another trade within the same company |
| Change of ownership | Losses may be forfeited if there is a change of ownership primarily for tax loss utilisation purposes |

### Commencement and Cessation

| Parameter | Detail |
|---|---|
| New businesses | Losses incurred in the first 4 years of trade may be carried forward without the normal 4-year time limit [unverified — confirm this relief under current legislation] |
| Cessation of trade | Unrelieved losses are forfeited upon cessation |

## 6. Provisions & Reserves

### General Principles (§24, §25, CITA)

| Item | Treatment |
|---|---|
| General provisions | **Not deductible** — only actual expenditure is deductible |
| Contingency reserves | **Not deductible** — §25(c), CITA |
| Bad debt provisions | **Not deductible** as a general provision; specific bad debts written off are deductible under §24(d), CITA |
| Warranty provisions | **Not deductible** until actual payment |
| Employee gratuity accruals | Deductible only when paid |
| Insurance reserves (regulated insurers) | Subject to National Insurance Commission (NAICOM) and FIRS-specific provisions |

## 7. Industry-Specific Deductions

### Petroleum Upstream — Petroleum Profits Tax Act (PPTA), Cap P13

| Parameter | Detail |
|---|---|
| Tax rate | **50%** for production-sharing contracts (PSCs); **65.75%** for joint ventures (JVs) under the old regime; **85%** for concession holders |
| Capital allowances | Specific rates: 20% initial on qualifying building; 10% annual; drilling costs: 100% immediate write-off |
| Intangible drilling costs | **100%** deductible in the year incurred |
| Production allowance | Various allowances for investment tax credit, gas utilisation, etc. |
| NTA 2025 | Petroleum profits tax rates and allowances were restructured; graduated rate structure based on cumulative production [unverified — confirm specific NTA petroleum provisions] |
| Legal basis | PPTA, Cap P13; Petroleum Industry Act, 2021; NTA, 2025 |

### Pioneer Status — Industrial Development (Income Tax Relief) Act

| Parameter | Detail |
|---|---|
| Benefit | **100%** tax holiday for 3 years (extendable to 5 years) for qualifying pioneer industries |
| Qualifying industries | Agriculture, mining, manufacturing, infrastructure, ICT, and other designated sectors |
| Conditions | Must be engaged in a "pioneer" industry as designated by the Nigerian Investment Promotion Commission (NIPC) |
| Status under NTA 2025 | Pioneer status regime retained but modified; new tax incentives framework introduced |

### Gas Utilisation — §39, CITA

| Parameter | Detail |
|---|---|
| Incentive | 3-year tax holiday + additional allowances for companies engaged in gas utilisation (downstream gas) |
| Capital allowances | Accelerated — 90% first year on gas plant and equipment |

### Export Incentives

| Parameter | Detail |
|---|---|
| Export Expansion Grant (EEG) | Grant of 5%–15% of export proceeds for non-oil exporters [unverified — confirm current EEG rates] |
| Export Free Zones | Companies in designated free zones enjoy tax holidays and duty-free imports — NEPZA Act, Cap N107 |

### Agriculture

| Parameter | Detail |
|---|---|
| Capital allowances | Agricultural plant and machinery: 95% initial allowance |
| Loss carry-forward | Extended to **6 years** for agricultural losses — §27, CITA |
| Incentives | Tax holiday of 5 years for agricultural companies (under Pioneer Status or special provisions) |

## 8. Treaties & Transfer Pricing

### Transfer Pricing — §22, CITA; Income Tax (Transfer Pricing) Regulations, 2018

| Parameter | Detail |
|---|---|
| Standard | Arm's-length principle — §22, CITA |
| Methods | CUP, resale price, cost plus, TNMM, profit split — aligned with OECD Guidelines |
| Documentation | Transfer pricing documentation (Master File, Local File) required for connected transactions |
| Connected persons | Defined as parties with ≥50% ownership or common control |
| Country-by-Country Reporting | Required for Nigerian-parented MNE groups with consolidated revenue ≥ NGN 160B (~EUR 750M) |
| Penalties | Penalties for non-compliance: up to 1% of the value of the transaction + interest |
| APA | Advance Pricing Agreements available under FIRS regulations |

### Treaty Network

Nigeria has over 15 bilateral double taxation treaties. Standard treaty rates typically reduce withholding taxes on dividends (7.5%–10%), interest (7.5%–10%), and royalties (7.5%–10%).

## 9. Non-Deductible Items

The following are **NOT deductible** under CITA:

| Expense | Legal Basis |
|---|---|
| Capital expenditure (unless capital allowance applies) | §25(a), CITA |
| Income tax (Nigerian or foreign, except as allowed credit) | §25(b), CITA |
| Provisions, reserves, and contingent liabilities | §25(c), CITA |
| Fines and penalties for breach of any law | §25(d), CITA |
| Expenditure not wholly and exclusively for producing profits | §25(a), CITA |
| Non-arm's-length portion of related-party payments | §22, CITA |
| Excessive management, technical, or consultancy fees to non-residents | §25, CITA (FIRS may apply benchmarks) |
| Depreciation (accounting) — capital allowances claimed instead | §25(a), CITA |
| Dividends or profit distributions | §25, CITA |
| Bribes and corrupt payments | General anti-avoidance / public policy |
| Assets/services acquired without proper VAT/customs compliance (NTA 2025) | NTA, 2025 |
