# Nigeria - R&D Tax Incentives

## 1. Overview

| Feature | Details |
|---|---|
| **Primary Incentive** | 120% enhanced deduction for R&D expenditure |
| **Secondary Incentive** | Pioneer Status — 3 to 5 year CIT holiday |
| **Deduction Rate** | 120% of qualifying R&D expenditure |
| **Governing Law** | Companies Income Tax Act (CITA), Cap C21, LFN 2004 — Section 26 |
| **Pioneer Status Law** | Industrial Development (Income Tax Relief) Act, Cap I7, LFN 2004 |
| **Administering Bodies** | Federal Inland Revenue Service (FIRS); Nigerian Investment Promotion Commission (NIPC) |
| **CIT Rate** | 30% (large companies); 20% (medium); 0% (small, turnover ≤ NGN 25M) |
| **Patent Box / IP Regime** | None |

| **Fiscal Year** | As determined by the company (12-month period) |
| **NOTAP** | National Office for Technology Acquisition and Promotion — required for technology transfer |

## 2. Detailed Programs

### 2.1 Section 26 CITA — 120% R&D Deduction

**Legal Basis:** Companies Income Tax Act (CITA), Cap C21, LFN 2004, Section 26

Section 26 provides for an enhanced deduction of 120% for R&D expenditure, offering a 20% super deduction above the standard 100% business expense deduction.

| Parameter | Details |
|---|---|
| **Deduction Rate** | 120% of qualifying R&D expenditure |
| **Effective Additional Benefit** | 20% × 30% CIT = 6% additional tax saving on qualifying spend |
| **Pre-Approval Required** | No — claimed in annual CIT return; must meet qualifying criteria |
| **Qualifying Activities** | R&D activities related to the company's business and carried on in Nigeria |
| **Eligible Entity** | Nigerian-incorporated companies liable to CIT under CITA |
| **Carry-Forward** | Tax losses (including R&D deductions) carried forward for 4 years |
| **Location Requirement** | R&D must be carried out in Nigeria |

#### Qualifying R&D Activities

| Activity Type | Qualifying | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Scientific research and experiment | Yes | Core qualifying activity |
| Product development and testing | Yes | Must advance technological knowledge |
| Process improvement and innovation | Yes | Must involve systematic investigation |
| Software development (novel) | Yes | New algorithms, platforms, architectures |
| Clinical trials and pharmaceutical R&D | Yes | Subject to NAFDAC regulations |
| Agricultural research | Yes | Crop science, livestock improvement |
| Market research | No | Not qualifying under Section 26 |
| Quality control (routine) | No | Not qualifying |
| Cosmetic/aesthetic changes | No | Not qualifying |
| Adaptation of existing technology without novelty | No | Not qualifying |

#### Eligible Expenditure Categories

| Cost Category | Deduction Rate | Conditions |
|---|---|---|
| **Employee compensation — R&D staff** | 120% | Directly engaged in qualifying R&D |
| **Materials and consumables** | 120% | Consumed in R&D activities |
| **Subcontractor R&D (Nigerian entities)** | 120% | Must be conducted in Nigeria |
| **Payments to approved research institutes** | 120% | Recognized by the Federal Government |
| **Equipment and machinery (capital)** | Standard capital allowances | Initial 50%, annual 25% (plant and machinery) |
| **R&D software and technology** | 120% (revenue) | Revenue expenditure on software |
| **Overhead allocation** | 120% | Directly attributable to R&D |
| **Patent filing and registration** | 120% | Revenue expenditure |

### 2.2 Pioneer Status — CIT Holiday

**Legal Basis:** Industrial Development (Income Tax Relief) Act, Cap I7, LFN 2004; NIPC Guidelines on Pioneer Status

Pioneer status grants a complete CIT exemption for qualifying companies engaged in designated pioneer industries, including technology and R&D-intensive sectors.

| Parameter | Details |
|---|---|
| **Tax Relief Period** | 3 years (initial), extendable to 5 years |
| **CIT Rate During Pioneer Period** | 0% on qualifying pioneer income |
| **Qualifying Industries** | Listed in the Industrial Development Act Schedule; includes technology, manufacturing, agriculture |
| **Application Body** | Nigerian Investment Promotion Commission (NIPC) |
| **Minimum Investment** | Varies by sector; typically NGN 100M+ for manufacturing |
| **Employment Requirement** | Must create minimum employment thresholds |
| **Commencement** | From "production day" as certified by NIPC |

**Pioneer Industries Relevant to R&D:**

| Industry | Pioneer Status Eligibility |
|---|---|
| Information and communications technology | Yes |
| Pharmaceutical manufacturing | Yes |
| Biotechnology | Yes |
| Automotive manufacturing | Yes |
| Renewable energy equipment manufacturing | Yes |
| Agricultural processing | Yes |
| Electronics and semiconductor | Yes |
| Software development and data centers | Under review / selectively granted |

### 2.3 NOTAP Registration — Technology Transfer

**Legal Basis:** National Office for Technology Acquisition and Promotion Act, Cap N62, LFN 2004

| Parameter | Details |
|---|---|
| **Purpose** | Registration of technology transfer agreements for tax deductibility |
| **Registration Required For** | Royalty payments, technical service fees, management fees to foreign entities |
| **Tax Deduction** | Only NOTAP-registered agreements qualify for tax deduction of royalty/service payments |
| **WHT on Royalties** | 10% WHT on registered royalty payments to non-residents |
| **Maximum Royalty Rate** | NOTAP may limit royalty rates (typically 1–5% of net sales) |
| **Validity** | Registration valid for the agreement term; renewable |

### 2.4 Other Relevant Incentives

| Incentive | Legal Basis | Details |
|---|---|---|
| **Export Expansion Grant (EEG)** | Export (Incentives and Miscellaneous Provisions) Act | Up to 30% of export revenue for manufactured goods with R&D component |
| **Tax-Free Bonds** | CITA Section 23 | Interest on government science and technology bonds exempt |
| **Free Trade Zone** | NEPZA Act, 1992 | 100% CIT exemption within free trade zones |
| **Import Duty Exemption** | Various | Exemption on import of R&D equipment (case-by-case) |
| **Rural Investment Allowance** | CITA Section 34 | 15–100% additional deduction for investments in rural areas |
| **Infrastructure Tax Credit** | Executive Order 007 (2019) | Tax credit for private sector infrastructure investment |

## 3. Patent Box / IP Regime

Nigeria does not currently have a patent box or preferential IP income regime.

| Feature | Details |
|---|---|
| **Patent Box** | Not available |
| **IP Royalty Income** | Taxed at standard CIT rate (30%/20%/0%) |
| **Withholding Tax on Royalties — Outbound** | 10% (subject to treaty relief) |
| **IP Registration** | Patents and Designs Act, Cap P2, LFN 2004; Trademarks Act, Cap T13 |
| **IP Disposal Gains** | Taxable as business income under CITA |
| **IP Amortization** | No specific IP amortization provision; capital allowances may apply |
| **Transfer Pricing** | FIRS Transfer Pricing Regulations, 2018 (Income Tax (Transfer Pricing) Regulations) |
| **Tax Treaties** | Limited treaty network (~15 treaties in force) |

## 5. Claim Process & Documentation

### 120% R&D Deduction

| Step | Action | Timeline |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Identify and document qualifying R&D activities | Ongoing |
| 2 | Maintain segregated R&D cost accounts in general ledger | Ongoing |
| 3 | Prepare R&D expenditure schedule for tax return | Year-end |
| 4 | Include 120% deduction in annual CIT return (FIRS Form) | Within 6 months of year-end |
| 5 | File CIT return via FIRS TaxPro Max portal | Annual deadline |
| 6 | Respond to FIRS queries or desk review | As required |
| 7 | Retain documentation for minimum 6 years | Ongoing |

### Pioneer Status Application

| Step | Action | Timeline |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Confirm business falls within pioneer industry list | Pre-application |
| 2 | Submit application to NIPC with required documentation | Pre-commencement |
| 3 | NIPC review and processing | 3–6 months |
| 4 | Publication in Federal Gazette (if approved) | Upon approval |
| 5 | Obtain Pioneer Certificate from NIPC | Upon publication |
| 6 | Apply for extension (Year 3) if desired | Before initial period expires |
| 7 | File annual returns with NIPC during pioneer period | Annually |
| 8 | Transition to standard CIT regime upon expiry | End of pioneer period |

### NOTAP Registration

| Step | Action | Timeline |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Draft technology transfer agreement | Pre-execution |
| 2 | Submit agreement to NOTAP for registration | Before payments commence |
| 3 | NOTAP review and negotiation of terms | 4–8 weeks |
| 4 | Issuance of Certificate of Registration | Upon approval |
| 5 | Claim tax deduction for registered royalty/service payments | Annual CIT return |

### Required Documentation

- **R&D Deduction:** Project descriptions, technical reports, employee records, expenditure breakdown, invoices, laboratory records
- **Pioneer Status:** Company incorporation documents, feasibility study, business plan, projected employment figures, capital investment evidence
- **NOTAP:** Original technology transfer agreement, company profiles of both parties, technical justification for technology transfer
- **Financial Records:** Audited financial statements, general ledger, trial balance, tax computations
- **Transfer Pricing:** Master file, local file, CbCR (if applicable, for groups with revenue > NGN 300M)

## 6. Recent Changes & Sunset Provisions

| Change | Effective Date | Details |
|---|---|---|
| **Finance Act 2019** | January 2020 | Introduced graduated CIT rates (0%/20%/30%); reduced burden on SMEs |
| **Finance Act 2020** | January 2021 | Expanded pioneer status industries; streamlined application |
| **Finance Act 2021** | January 2022 | Transfer pricing and thin capitalization rules enhanced |
| **Finance Act 2023** | 2023 | Various amendments; R&D provisions maintained |
| **FIRS Transfer Pricing Regulations** | 2018 (amended 2022) | Mandatory TP documentation; advance pricing agreements |
| **Startup Act (Nigeria Startup Bill)** | October 2022 | Tax incentives for qualifying tech startups including pioneer status fast-track |
| **Executive Order 007** | 2019 | Infrastructure tax credit for road and bridge construction |
| **Pillar Two Monitoring** | Ongoing | FIRS engaging with OECD framework; limited immediate impact due to revenue thresholds |
| **Proposed R&D Enhancement** | Under discussion (2025–2026) | FIRS exploring increase to 150% deduction; no legislation yet |

**Nigeria Startup Act (NSA) 2022 — Key Provisions:**

| Feature | Details |
|---|---|
| **Qualifying Startup** | Registered in Nigeria; < 10 years old; technology-focused |
| **Tax Incentive** | Pioneer status fast-track for labeled startups |
| **Pioneer Period** | Up to 5 years CIT holiday |
| **Labeling Body** | National Information Technology Development Agency (NITDA) |
| **R&D Relevance** | Technology startups with R&D activities can access CIT holiday |

**Sunset Provisions:**
- Section 26 (120% R&D deduction) is a permanent provision of CITA with no sunset date
- Pioneer status is granted for fixed periods (3 + 2 years); requires reapplication for new projects
- Startup Act incentives subject to startup "labeling" renewal
- Free Trade Zone incentives are indefinite for qualifying entities

**Future Outlook:**
- Nigeria's growing tech ecosystem (Lagos as Africa's startup capital) driving policy interest in enhanced R&D incentives
- FIRS modernization (TaxPro Max) improving compliance and audit capacity
- Potential increase in R&D deduction rate to 150% under discussion
- African Continental Free Trade Area (AfCFTA) may influence regional R&D incentive harmonization
