# Switzerland - R&D Tax Incentives

## 1. Overview

| Feature | Details |
|---|---|
| **Headline Incentive** | R&D Super Deduction — up to 150% of qualifying R&D expenditure |
| **Type** | Enhanced deduction at cantonal/communal level; patent box at cantonal level |
| **Rate** | Up to 150% super deduction (50% additional deduction); patent box up to 90% relief |
| **Refundable** | No — deduction-based; reduces taxable profit only; no refund if in loss position |
| **Patent Box** | Yes — cantonal patent box with up to 90% reduction of qualifying IP income |
| **Legal Basis** | Bundesgesetz über die direkte Bundessteuer (DBG) Art. 25a (R&D super deduction); TRAF/STAF (Federal Act on Tax Reform and AHV Financing) 2020; cantonal tax laws |

## 2. Detailed Programs

### 2.1 R&D Super Deduction

**Legal Basis:** DBG Art. 25a (federal); cantonal tax laws implementing TRAF/STAF provisions. Effective 1 January 2020 (TRAF/STAF implementation).

| Parameter | Value |
|---|---|
| Federal Level | 150% deduction of qualifying in-house R&D personnel costs (50% additional deduction) |
| Cantonal Level | Up to 150% deduction; exact rate varies by canton |
| Qualifying Costs — In-House | Direct R&D personnel costs (salaries, social charges) + 35% flat-rate surcharge for other R&D costs |
| Qualifying Costs — Contract R&D | 80% of payments to third parties for R&D performed in Switzerland |
| In-House R&D Definition | Research and development performed by the taxpayer's own employees in Switzerland |
| Geographic Requirement | R&D must be performed in Switzerland |
| Interaction with Patent Box | Both available; subject to combined relief limitation |
| Total Relief Cap | Combined relief from patent box + R&D super deduction cannot exceed 70% of taxable profit (before application of these measures) |

**Cantonal R&D Super Deduction Rates (Selected):**

| Canton | Super Deduction Rate | Additional Deduction |
|---|---|---|
| Zurich | 150% | 50% additional |
| Zug | 150% | 50% additional |

| Basel-Landschaft | 150% | 50% additional |

> **Note:** Most cantons have adopted the maximum 150% rate, but some may apply lower rates. Verify with cantonal tax authority.

### 2.2 Patent Box

**Legal Basis:** DBG Art. 24b (federal); cantonal implementations under TRAF/STAF.

| Parameter | Value |
|---|---|
| Federal Framework | Up to 90% reduction of net qualifying IP income at cantonal/communal level |
| Federal Direct Tax | Patent box does **not** apply at federal level (8.5% federal CIT rate applies fully) |
| Cantonal Application | Each canton implements patent box independently; most allow up to 90% reduction |
| Qualifying IP | Patents (Swiss, European, foreign), comparable rights (plant variety, topographies), supplementary protection certificates |
| Nexus Approach | OECD BEPS Action 5 compliant; modified nexus fraction required |
| Nexus Fraction | Qualifying R&D expenditure / Total R&D expenditure × qualifying net IP income |
| Net IP Income | Gross IP income minus directly attributable costs minus routine return |
| Entry Ticket | Must hold qualifying IP right |
| Self-Development | IP must be (at least partly) self-developed |

**Cantonal Patent Box — Effective Rates (Illustrative):**

| Canton | Standard Combined Rate | Patent Box Combined Rate (approx.) | Effective Saving |
|---|---|---|---|
| Zug | ~11.9% | ~3.5–4.5% | ~7.5–8.5 pp |
| Lucerne | ~12.2% | ~3.5–4.5% | ~7.5–8.5 pp |
| Vaud | ~14.0% | ~4.0–5.0% | ~9.0–10.0 pp |
| Basel-Stadt | ~13.0% | ~4.0–5.0% | ~8.0–9.0 pp |
| Zurich | ~19.7% | ~5.0–7.0% | ~12.0–14.0 pp |
| Geneva | ~14.0% | ~4.0–5.5% | ~8.5–10.0 pp |

> **Note:** Combined rates include federal (8.5%), cantonal, and communal taxes. Exact rates depend on commune within the canton.

### 2.3 TRAF/STAF Relief Limitation (70% Cap)

**Legal Basis:** DBG Art. 25b; cantonal equivalents.

| Parameter | Value |
|---|---|
| Combined Relief Cap | Total tax reduction from patent box + R&D super deduction + IP-related depreciation cannot exceed 70% of taxable profit |
| Calculation Base | Taxable profit before application of patent box, R&D super deduction, and step-up depreciation |
| Purpose | Ensures minimum taxation; prevents complete elimination of cantonal/communal tax |
| Application | Applied at cantonal/communal level only; federal tax unaffected |
| Excess | If combined relief would exceed 70%, relief is proportionally reduced |

### 2.4 Step-Up / Entry Provisions

**Legal Basis:** TRAF/STAF transitional provisions; cantonal implementations.

| Parameter | Value |
|---|---|
| Step-Up on Hidden Reserves | Companies transitioning from former special status regimes (holding, domicile, mixed company) can step up IP values to fair market value |
| Amortization Period | Up to 10 years for stepped-up values |
| Purpose | Smooth transition from abolished cantonal special regimes to new TRAF/STAF tools |
| Deadline | Transitional provisions; generally available for companies that held special status before TRAF/STAF |
| Impact on 70% Cap | Step-up depreciation counts toward the 70% relief cap |

## 3. Patent Box / IP Regime

See **Section 2.2** above for full details.

**Summary:**

| Feature | Swiss Patent Box |
|---|---|
| Level | Cantonal/communal (not federal) |
| Maximum Reduction | 90% of net qualifying IP income |
| Federal Tax | Still applies at 8.5% (no patent box relief) |
| Effective Combined Rate | ~3.5%–7% depending on canton/commune |
| Nexus Compliant | Yes (OECD BEPS Action 5) |
| Qualifying IP | Patents, comparable rights, SPCs |
| Process IP | Only if patented |
| Software | Not separately; only if protected by qualifying patent |

## 4. Effective Benefit Analysis (per $1M USD R&D Spend)

*Assumes $1M USD ≈ CHF 880,000 (illustrative rate); Zurich combined rate ~19.7%, Zug combined rate ~11.9%.*

| Scenario (Zurich) | Calculation | Net Benefit |
|---|---|---|
| **R&D Super Deduction (150%)** | Additional 50% deduction: CHF 880,000 × 50% = CHF 440,000 additional deduction × ~11.2% cantonal/communal rate = CHF 49,280 saving | **CHF 49,280** |
| **Patent Box (on CHF 880k IP income)** | 90% reduction of IP income at cantonal level: CHF 880,000 × 90% × ~11.2% = CHF 88,704 saving | **CHF 88,704** |
| **Combined (subject to 70% cap)** | CHF 49,280 + CHF 88,704 = CHF 137,984 (subject to cap) | **Up to CHF 137,984** |
| **Full deductibility (standard)** | CHF 880,000 × 19.7% = CHF 173,360 | Embedded |

| Scenario (Zug) | Calculation | Net Benefit |
|---|---|---|
| **R&D Super Deduction (150%)** | CHF 440,000 additional × ~3.4% cantonal/communal = CHF 14,960 | **CHF 14,960** |
| **Patent Box (on CHF 880k IP income)** | CHF 880,000 × 90% × ~3.4% = CHF 26,928 | **CHF 26,928** |
| **Combined** | CHF 14,960 + CHF 26,928 = CHF 41,888 | **Up to CHF 41,888** |

> **Note:** The R&D super deduction and patent box provide greater absolute savings in higher-tax cantons (e.g., Zurich, Geneva) than in low-tax cantons (e.g., Zug, Lucerne) where the base rate is already low. Canton selection involves trade-offs beyond tax incentives.

## 5. Claim Process & Documentation

### 5.1 Filing Process

| Step | Action |
|---|---|
| 1 | Identify qualifying R&D expenditure (in-house personnel + 35% surcharge; contract R&D at 80%) |
| 2 | Determine patent box eligible IP assets and calculate net IP income |
| 3 | Compute nexus fraction per qualifying IP asset |
| 4 | Apply 70% combined relief cap |
| 5 | Claim on cantonal/communal tax return |
| 6 | Claim R&D super deduction on federal tax return (DBG Art. 25a) |
| 7 | Maintain supporting documentation |

### 5.2 Documentation Requirements

| Document | Purpose |
|---|---|
| R&D project descriptions | Demonstrate qualifying R&D activities |
| Personnel records | Identify R&D employees, roles, time allocation |
| Cost accounting | Separate tracking of R&D costs (personnel, materials, equipment) |
| Contract R&D agreements | Subcontractor contracts specifying R&D scope; Swiss nexus |
| Patent portfolio | Register of qualifying IP rights (patents, SPCs, comparable rights) |
| Nexus fraction workpapers | Per-asset tracking of qualifying vs. total R&D expenditure |
| IP income allocation | Transfer pricing documentation for embedded IP income |
| 70% cap calculation | Workpapers demonstrating compliance with relief limitation |
| Cantonal ruling (if obtained) | Advance tax ruling from cantonal tax authority |

### 5.3 Advance Tax Rulings

| Feature | Details |
|---|---|
| Availability | Swiss cantons routinely issue advance tax rulings |
| Scope | Can cover patent box eligibility, super deduction application, 70% cap calculations |
| Process | Written application to cantonal tax authority; describe facts and proposed treatment |
| Binding | Binding on the tax authority for the covered period, provided facts remain as described |
| Cost | Generally free (government service); some cantons charge for complex rulings |
| Timeline | Typically 4–12 weeks |
| Recommended | Strongly recommended for patent box and combined relief cap structuring |

## 6. Recent Changes & Sunset Provisions

| Change | Effective Date | Details |
|---|---|---|
| TRAF/STAF enacted | 1 January 2020 | Introduced patent box, R&D super deduction, 70% cap at federal level |
| Cantonal implementations | 2020–2021 | All cantons implemented TRAF/STAF tools; timing varied |
| Abolition of special status regimes | 1 January 2020 | Holding, domicile, mixed company, and principal company statuses eliminated |
| Step-up transitional provisions | 2020+ | Available for former special status companies |
| OECD Pillar Two considerations | 2024+ | 15% global minimum tax may impact effective rates in low-tax cantons |
| Cantonal rate reductions | 2020–2023 | Many cantons reduced headline CIT rates alongside TRAF/STAF (e.g., Basel-Stadt, Vaud, Geneva) |

**Sunset Provisions:**
- **R&D super deduction** (DBG Art. 25a) is permanent federal legislation; no sunset clause
- **Patent box** (DBG Art. 24b) is permanent; no sunset clause
- **Step-up provisions** are transitional; limited availability
- **70% cap** is permanent structural limitation
- Cantonal implementations are permanent but rates may be adjusted by cantonal parliaments
