# Switzerland - Taxable Income Deductions

> **Jurisdiction**: Federal (ESTV / FTA — Eidgenössische Steuerverwaltung) + Cantonal/Communal
> **Governing Law**: Bundesgesetz über die direkte Bundessteuer (DBG); Steuerharmonisierungsgesetz (StHG); Cantonal tax laws
> **Tax Year**: 2024/2025
> **Currency**: CHF (CHF)

---

## 1. Depreciation & Capital Allowances

### General Principles — DBG Art. 62 / Art. 28 StHG

Tax depreciation (Abschreibungen) is based on actual wear and obsolescence. The ESTV publishes safe-harbour rates in its official circular (Merkblatt A 1995) that are presumed to reflect arm's-length depreciation. Both straight-line and declining balance methods are accepted.

### ESTV Safe-Harbour Depreciation Rates (Merkblatt A 1995)

| Asset Category | Declining Balance Rate | Straight-Line Rate | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| Commercial buildings (masonry) | **4%** | **2%** | |
| Commercial buildings (other) | **6–8%** | **3–4%** | |
| Industrial buildings | **8%** | **4%** | |
| Factory buildings (light construction) | **10–15%** | **5–7%** | |
| Machinery and equipment | **30%** | **15%** | |
| Motor vehicles | **40%** | **20%** | |
| Computer equipment | **40%** | **33%** | |
| Office equipment and furniture | **25%** | **12.5%** | |
| Tools | **40–50%** | **20–25%** | |
| Goodwill | **40%** | **20%** | |
| Patents, licences | **40%** | **20%** | |
| Inventories (lump-sum write-down) | Up to **33.3%** (one-third) of cost | — | Traditional Swiss allowance |

### Key Notes

| Parameter | Detail |
|---|---|
| Method preference | Declining balance preferred in practice (higher initial deductions) |
| Pro rata first year | Full-year rate generally applied in year of acquisition (no half-year rule) |
| Land | Not depreciable |
| Immediate write-off | Small items may be expensed immediately per commercial practice |
| Hidden reserves | Swiss tax law permits the maintenance of hidden reserves (stille Reserven) within the depreciation framework |

### Cantonal Variations

| Canton | Notes |
|---|---|
| Zurich | Generally follows ESTV rates |
| Zug | Follows ESTV rates; competitive overall environment |
| Geneva | Follows ESTV rates but may scrutinise aggressive depreciation claims |

## 2. Business Operating Expenses

### General Deductibility — DBG Art. 59

Business expenses (geschäftsmässig begründeter Aufwand) are deductible if commercially justified and documented.

### Key Deductible Operating Expenses

| Expense Category | Deductibility | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Employee salaries and wages | Fully deductible | Must be arm's-length for related parties |
| Employer social insurance contributions (AHV/IV/EO/ALV) | Fully deductible | |
| BVG/pension fund employer contributions | Fully deductible within limits | |
| Rent and lease payments | Fully deductible | |
| Professional fees | Fully deductible | |
| Repairs and maintenance | Deductible if revenue nature | |
| Bad debts | Deductible when specifically provided for or written off | |
| Insurance premiums | Fully deductible | |
| Training and education | Fully deductible | |
| Start-up and organisation costs | Deductible in year incurred or amortised | |

### Charitable Donations — DBG Art. 59 Abs. 1 Buchst. c

| Parameter | Value |
|---|---|
| Deductible | Donations to tax-exempt public-benefit entities |
| Cap | Up to **20%** of net profit (after deducting the donation itself) |
| Minimum | Generally none for corporate donors |

## 3. Interest & Financing Costs

### Safe-Harbour Interest Rates — ESTV Rundschreiben (Annual Circular)

| Parameter | Value |
|---|---|
| Approach | ESTV publishes annual safe-harbour interest rates for related-party loans (CHF and foreign currency) |
| CHF loans to shareholders (2024) | Typically published each February (e.g., 1.5% for operating loans, 2.0% for investment loans — 2023 rates) [unverified — confirm with local counsel] |
| Foreign currency loans | Separate rates per currency |
| Consequence of exceeding | Excess interest recharacterised as hidden profit distribution (geldwerte Leistung) |

### Thin Capitalisation — ESTV Circular 6/1997

| Parameter | Value |
|---|---|
| Formal rule | No statutory thin-cap ratio, but ESTV applies substance-based debt/equity tests |
| Safe-harbour ratios | Total third-party and related-party debt should not exceed specified percentages of asset categories (e.g., 70% of real estate, 85% of receivables, 60% of inventories, 70% of other assets) |
| Typical effective ratio | Approximately **6:1** debt-to-equity overall |
| Consequence | Interest on excessive related-party debt is treated as hidden profit distribution (non-deductible + subject to withholding tax) |

### ATAD Interest Limitation

| Parameter | Value |
|---|---|
| Status | Switzerland has **not implemented** the EU ATAD interest limitation rules (not an EU member) |
| Instead | Relies on safe-harbour interest rates and thin-cap substance tests |

## 4. Research & Development

Switzerland provides R&D deductions at both federal and cantonal levels. Under the STAF (Federal Act on Tax Reform and AHV Financing) reform effective from 2020, cantons may allow an additional deduction of up to **50%** of qualifying R&D expenditure (primarily personnel costs for in-house R&D, plus 80% of contracted R&D from domestic third parties). The federal level does not provide a separate R&D credit.

See [R&D Incentives](rnd_incentives.md) for full program detail.

## 5. Loss Carry-Forward & Carry-Back

### Loss Carry-Forward — DBG Art. 67

| Parameter | Value |
|---|---|
| Duration | **7 years** |
| Restriction | Losses offset against **100%** of taxable income (no percentage cap) |
| Cantonal | Cantons follow the 7-year rule per StHG Art. 25 Abs. 2 |

### Loss Carry-Back

| Parameter | Value |
|---|---|
| Status | **Not available** under Swiss tax law (federal or cantonal) |

### Change of Ownership

| Parameter | Value |
|---|---|
| Mantelhandel (shell trading) | Losses forfeited if company is economically liquidated and then re-activated with a new business (substance-over-form approach) |
| Tax authority scrutiny | Change of >50% ownership combined with fundamental change in business activity may lead to loss disallowance |

## 6. Provisions & Reserves

### Tax-Deductible Provisions — DBG Art. 63

| Provision Type | Deductibility | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Bad debt provisions | Deductible | Individually assessed; blanket provisions on portfolio also accepted (e.g., 5–10% of receivables) |
| Warranty provisions | Deductible | Based on historical data |
| Litigation provisions | Deductible | If probable and estimable |
| Environmental provisions | Deductible | If legal obligation |
| Pension provisions | Deductible | Employer contributions to BVG pension funds |
| Inventory write-downs | **33.3%** blanket write-down accepted (Warendrittel) | Long-standing Swiss practice |
| Restructuring provisions | Deductible | If specific plan exists |
| General reserves | Deductible to extent commercially justified | Swiss practice is relatively permissive |

### Hidden Reserves (Stille Reserven) — DBG Art. 62

| Parameter | Value |
|---|---|
| Concept | Swiss tax law permits companies to maintain hidden reserves via conservative depreciation and provisions |
| Release | Hidden reserves are taxed upon realisation (disposal, dissolution) |
| Replacement asset deferral | Hidden reserves may be rolled over to replacement assets if reinvested within a reasonable period — DBG Art. 64 |

### Holding Company Regime (Historical)

| Parameter | Value |
|---|---|
| Status | **Abolished** under STAF (effective 1 January 2020) |
| Replacement | Participation deduction (Beteiligungsabzug — DBG Art. 69–70) remains for qualifying dividend and capital gains |

### Participation Deduction — DBG Art. 69–70

| Parameter | Value |
|---|---|
| Exemption | Tax reduction proportional to net participation income relative to total net profit |
| Threshold | Dividends: ≥10% holding or CHF 1M fair market value; capital gains: ≥10% holding held ≥1 year |

### Shipping / Commodities Trading

| Parameter | Value |
|---|---|
| Significance | Switzerland (Geneva, Zug) is a major hub for commodity trading |
| Tax treatment | No special regime; standard deduction rules apply |
| Principal structure | Common; TP documentation essential |

## 8. Treaties & Transfer Pricing

### Transfer Pricing — DBG Art. 58 / ESTV Practice

| Parameter | Detail |
|---|---|
| Standard | Arm's-length principle (based on OECD Guidelines; no specific TP statute, derived from general anti-abuse principles) |
| Documentation | No formal statutory documentation requirement, but recommended per ESTV circular |
| OECD BEPS | Switzerland adopted CbCR from 2018 for MNE groups with consolidated revenue ≥ CHF 900M |
| Penalties | Profit adjustments treated as hidden profit distributions (subject to withholding tax on excess) |
| APAs | Bilateral APAs available (typically coordinated with tax treaty partner) |

### Withholding Tax (Verrechnungssteuer) — VStG

| Parameter | Value |
|---|---|
| Dividend WHT | **35%** (credited/refunded to treaty-eligible recipients) |
| Interest on bonds/bank deposits | **35%** |
| Treaty reduction | Typically to 0–15% under DTAs |

### Treaty Network

Switzerland has over **100** double taxation agreements.

| Income Type | Typical Treaty Rate |
|---|---|
| Dividends (substantial holding) | 0–5% |
| Dividends (portfolio) | 15% |
| Interest | 0–10% |
| Royalties | 0–5% |

## 9. Non-Deductible Items

| Expense | Statutory Reference |
|---|---|
| Federal and cantonal income taxes | Not deductible — DBG Art. 59 (by exclusion) |
| Capital taxes (Kapitalsteuer) | **Deductible** (unlike income taxes) |
| Withholding tax (Verrechnungssteuer) borne by company | Not deductible (creditable/refundable) |
| Hidden profit distributions (verdeckte Gewinnausschüttungen) | Not deductible — DBG Art. 58 Abs. 1 Buchst. b |
| Excessive interest to shareholders (above safe-harbour) | Recharacterised as distribution — not deductible |
| Fines and penalties (criminal) | Not deductible |
| Bribes | Not deductible — DBG Art. 59 Abs. 2 |
| Private expenditure | Not deductible — DBG Art. 59 |
| Capital expenditure | Must be depreciated |
| Dividends paid | Not an expense |
| Donations exceeding 20% of net profit | Not deductible |
| Non-arm's-length management fees | Non-deductible to extent of excess |
