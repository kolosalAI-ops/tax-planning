## 9. Income by Source Type

### 9.1 Service Income (Domestic & Cross-Border)

> **Legal Basis**: ITA §125 (small business deduction), ITA §212(1)(a) (WHT on management/technical fees), ITA §247 (transfer pricing), ITA §245 (GAAR)

| Parameter | Treatment | Statutory Reference |
|---|---|---|
| Domestic service income | Taxed as **active business income** at **15%** federal (general) or **9%** federal (SBD for CCPCs on first $500K) | ITA §125 |
| Cross-border services rendered in Canada | May create a permanent establishment (PE), subjecting non-resident to **Part I tax** on Canadian-source income | ITA §2(3), §253 |
| Management/technical fees to non-residents | **Part XIII WHT at 25%** (default) or reduced treaty rate (e.g., 0% under Canada-US Treaty for independent services) | ITA §212(1)(a) |
| Transfer pricing | Arm's length principle applies; **contemporaneous documentation** required; penalties for non-compliance (10% of adjustment) | ITA §247 |
| GAAR application | Anti-avoidance rule applies to service arrangements that result in misuse or abuse of ITA provisions | ITA §245 |

**Practical Notes:**
- CCPCs providing services must ensure income qualifies as "active business income" (not specified investment business or personal services business) to access the SBD rate.
- Non-resident corporations providing services in Canada for extended periods (generally >12 months for construction, >183 days for services) may trigger PE under Canada's tax treaties.
- Management fee arrangements between related parties are subject to close CRA scrutiny under transfer pricing rules (ITA §247). Contemporaneous documentation must demonstrate arm's length pricing.

### 9.2 Royalty & IP Licensing Income (Received)

> **Legal Basis**: ITA §20(1)(a) (CCA deduction), Income Tax Regulations §1100 (CCA rates), ITA §127 (SR&ED ITC)

| Parameter | Treatment | Statutory Reference |
|---|---|---|
| Domestic royalties received | Included in **business income** at standard CIT rates (15% federal general / 9% SBD) | ITA §9 |
| Inbound royalties from abroad | Included in **worldwide income**; foreign tax credits (FTC) available for foreign WHT paid | ITA §126 |
| CCA Class 14 (patents, franchises with limited life) | **5% declining balance** or straight-line over life of property (whichever is applicable) | ITA §20(1)(a), Reg. 1100 |
| CCA Class 14.1 (goodwill, other intangibles) | **5% declining balance** (post-2017 rules; replaces former eligible capital property regime) | ITA §20(1)(a), Reg. 1100, Sched. II Class 14.1 |
| Patent box regime | **None** — Canada does not offer a patent box or IP box regime | N/A |
| SR&ED on IP development | **35% refundable ITC** for CCPCs (on first $3M of qualified expenditures); 15% non-refundable for others | ITA §127, §127.1 |

**Practical Notes:**
- Royalties received from foreign affiliates may be subject to FAPI rules if the paying entity is a controlled foreign affiliate.
- The transition from eligible capital property (ECP) to CCA Class 14.1 (effective January 1, 2017) means goodwill and customer lists are now depreciated at 5% declining balance.
- SR&ED claims on IP development are one of the most significant incentives available to CCPCs developing proprietary technology in Canada.

### 9.3 Rental & Lease Income

> **Legal Basis**: ITA §9 (income from business or property), ITA §12(1)(j) (rent included in income), ITA §12(1)(x) (lease inducements), Reg. §1100(11) (rental CCA restriction)

| Parameter | Treatment | Statutory Reference |
|---|---|---|
| Rental income from real property | Taxed as **property income** (not active business income unless significant ancillary services provided) | ITA §9, §12(1)(j) |
| CCA on rental buildings | **Class 1: 4%** declining balance for buildings acquired after 1987 | Reg. Sched. II, Class 1 |
| Rental loss limitation | CCA **cannot create or increase** a net rental loss (CCA restricted to amount that reduces rental income to zero) | Reg. §1100(11) |
| REIT provisions | Mutual fund trust regime: 100% distribution of income → **no entity-level tax**; flow-through to unitholders | ITA §104, §122 |
| Lease inducements | Included in income of recipient; amortized over lease term | ITA §12(1)(x) |

**Practical Notes:**
- The rental CCA restriction (Reg. §1100(11)) prevents taxpayers from using CCA on rental properties to shelter non-rental income. CCA can only reduce rental income to nil, not create a loss.
- REITs that qualify as "mutual fund trusts" and meet the "REIT conditions" (ITA §122.1(1)) are not subject to the SIFT rules and can distribute income tax-free at the entity level.
- Lease inducements received by tenants (e.g., tenant improvement allowances, rent-free periods) must be included in income under ITA §12(1)(x) and can be amortized over the lease term.

### 9.4 Capital Gains (Business Assets, Shares, Property)

> **Legal Basis**: ITA §38 (taxable capital gains inclusion rate), ITA §110.6 (lifetime capital gains exemption), ITA §40 (capital gains computation and reserves)

| Parameter | Treatment | Statutory Reference |
|---|---|---|
| Capital gains inclusion rate (individuals) | **50%** on first $250,000; **66.67%** above $250,000 (effective June 25, 2024) | ITA §38 |
| Corporate capital gains | **50% inclusion**, taxed at regular CIT rate on the taxable portion | ITA §38 |
| Lifetime Capital Gains Exemption (LCGE) | **$1,016,836** (2024, indexed) on qualified small business corporation (QSBC) shares | ITA §110.6 |
| Principal residence exemption | Available to **individuals only** (not corporations); eliminates gain on qualifying principal residence | ITA §40(2)(b) |
| Capital gains reserve | Gain may be spread over up to **5 years** (10 years for qualifying transfers to children) when proceeds not fully received | ITA §40(1)(a)(iii) |
| Eligible capital property | Transitioned to **CCA Class 14.1** (post-2017 rules); 5% declining balance | ITA §13, Sched. II Class 14.1 |
| Superficial loss rules | Loss denied if identical property reacquired within **30 days** before or after disposition | ITA §54 "superficial loss" |
| Allowable business investment loss (ABIL) | **50% deductible** against all sources of income (applies to losses on shares/debt of SBCs) | ITA §38(c), §39(1)(c) |

**Practical Notes:**
- The two-tier inclusion rate (50% / 66.67%) effective June 25, 2024 applies to individuals; corporations and trusts use the 66.67% rate on all capital gains above the threshold.
- The LCGE is available only for dispositions of QSBC shares, qualified farm property, or qualified fishing property. Shares must meet the 90% active asset test at disposition and the 50% asset test for the 24-month holding period.
- The capital gains reserve allows deferral where proceeds are receivable over time, but at least 20% of the gain must be recognized each year (minimum 5 equal installments).

### 9.5 Dividend Income (Received by Company)

> **Legal Basis**: ITA §112(1) (inter-corporate dividend deduction), ITA §186 (Part IV refundable tax), ITA §113 (foreign affiliate dividends), ITA §83(2) (capital dividends)

| Parameter | Treatment | Statutory Reference |
|---|---|---|
| Inter-corporate dividends | Generally **deductible** under §112(1) — effectively no tax on dividends between private Canadian corporations | ITA §112(1) |
| Connected corporation dividends | **Fully deductible**; no Part IV tax (unless payer received portfolio dividends generating its own Part IV tax) | ITA §112(1), §186(4) |
| Part IV refundable tax | **38.33%** on portfolio dividends from non-connected corporations | ITA §186(1) |
| RDTOH refund mechanism | Refunded at **$1 per $3** of taxable dividends paid (Refundable Dividend Tax On Hand) | ITA §129(1) |
| Eligible vs. non-eligible dividends | **Eligible**: grossed up 38%, federal credit 15.0198%; **Non-eligible**: grossed up 15%, credit 9.0301% (individual shareholder mechanism) | ITA §82(1), §121 |
| Foreign affiliate dividends | **Exempt surplus**: tax-free; **Taxable surplus**: grossed up with underlying FTC | ITA §113(1)(a) (exempt), §113(1)(b) (taxable) |
| Capital dividends | **Tax-free** distribution from Capital Dividend Account (CDA) — funded by non-taxable portion of capital gains | ITA §83(2) |

**Practical Notes:**
- The inter-corporate dividend deduction (ITA §112(1)) is one of the key mechanisms preventing double taxation in Canada's corporate system.
- Part IV tax at 38.33% is a refundable tax designed to prevent deferral of tax by holding portfolio investments inside a private corporation. It is fully refunded when the corporation pays taxable dividends to its shareholders.
- Foreign affiliate dividends from exempt surplus (generally active business income earned in treaty countries) are received tax-free in Canada, making the exempt surplus regime a key planning tool for Canadian multinationals.

### 9.6 Interest Income (Received)

> **Legal Basis**: ITA §12(1)(c) (interest income inclusion), ITA §12(4) (accrual rule), ITA §18(4) (thin capitalization), ITA §18.2 (EIFEL rules), ITA §80.4 (prescribed rate loans)

| Parameter | Treatment | Statutory Reference |
|---|---|---|
| Interest income | **Fully taxable** as property income at regular CIT rates (no preferential rate) | ITA §12(1)(c) |
| Accrual rules | Must recognize interest income on **anniversary date** of debt obligation (not just on receipt) | ITA §12(4) |
| Prescribed rate loans | CRA quarterly prescribed rate (used for shareholder/employee loans; 2024 Q4: 5%) | ITA §80.4, §80.5 |
| EIFEL rules (effective June 2024) | Net interest deductions limited to **30% of tax-EBITDA**; denied interest carried forward 20 years; excess capacity carried forward 3 years | ITA §18.2 |
| Foreign affiliate interest | May constitute FAPI if earned by CFA from non-arm's length parties | ITA §95(1), §95(2)(a) |
| Thin capitalization | Debt-to-equity ratio of **1.5:1** for debt owed to specified non-resident shareholders (25%+ ownership); excess interest reclassified as non-deductible dividends | ITA §18(4), §18(5) |

**Practical Notes:**
- Interest income earned by a CCPC is treated as aggregate investment income and taxed at 38.67% federally (including refundable portion), with the refundable portion recoverable through the RDTOH mechanism when dividends are paid.
- The EIFEL (Excessive Interest and Financing Expenses Limitation) rules, effective for tax years beginning on or after June 2024, represent a significant change aligned with OECD BEPS Action 4. A de minimis threshold of $250,000 in net interest expense exempts smaller taxpayers.
- Thin capitalization rules apply only to debt from specified non-resident shareholders (or persons not dealing at arm's length with such shareholders). Interest on excess debt is recharacterized as a dividend (subject to Part XIII WHT).

## 10. Sector-Specific Revenue Taxes

### 10.1 Digital Services Taxation

> **Legal Basis**: Digital Services Tax Act (2024), Excise Tax Act Division II (GST/HST on digital supplies)

| Parameter | Treatment | Legal Reference |
|---|---|---|
| Digital Services Tax (DST) rate | **3%** on Canadian-source digital services revenue | Digital Services Tax Act §10 |
| Global revenue threshold | Consolidated global revenue **>€750 million** | Digital Services Tax Act §5 |
| Canadian digital revenue threshold | Canadian digital services revenue **>$20 million** | Digital Services Tax Act §5 |
| Covered services | Online marketplace services, social media services, online advertising services, user data sales | Digital Services Tax Act §2 |
| Retroactive application | Retroactive to **January 1, 2022** | Digital Services Tax Act §4 |
| GST/HST on digital products | Non-resident vendors supplying digital products/services to Canadian consumers must register and collect **GST/HST** | Excise Tax Act, Division II, §240.1 |

**Practical Notes:**
- The DST was enacted in response to delays in OECD Pillar One implementation. It applies to large multinational enterprises with significant digital revenues from Canadian users.
- The retroactive application to 2022 means affected businesses must file returns and remit tax for the 2022 and 2023 calendar years as well.
- The GST/HST registration requirement for non-resident digital vendors (effective July 1, 2021) is separate from the DST and applies to a broader set of digital economy participants.

### 10.2 Natural Resource / Extractive Taxes

> **Legal Basis**: ITA §66.1 (CEE), §66.2 (CDE), §66.4 (COGPE), §66 (flow-through shares); various provincial statutes

| Parameter | Treatment | Legal Reference |
|---|---|---|
| Federal mining royalty/tax | **None** — mining royalties are under **provincial jurisdiction** | Provincial statutes |
| Resource allowance | Eliminated; replaced by deduction for **actual provincial royalties/taxes paid** | ITA §20(1)(v.1) |
| Ontario mining tax | **10%** on mining profits | Ontario Mining Tax Act |
| BC mineral tax | **2%** on net current proceeds; **13%** on net revenue | BC Mineral Tax Act |
| Saskatchewan potash tax | Potash production tax + base payment; varies by production volume | Saskatchewan Potash Production Tax |
| Alberta freehold mineral tax | Tax on freehold mineral rights production | Alberta Freehold Mineral Rights Tax Act |
| Alberta oil & gas royalties | **1-40%** sliding scale based on commodity price and production levels | Alberta Mines and Minerals Act |
| CCA Class 41/41.2 | Accelerated depreciation for mining/oil sands equipment: **25% DB** | Reg. Sched. II, Class 41/41.2 |
| Canadian Exploration Expense (CEE) | **100% deduction** in the year incurred | ITA §66.1 |
| Canadian Development Expense (CDE) | **30% declining balance** | ITA §66.2 |
| Canadian Oil & Gas Property Expense (COGPE) | **10% declining balance** | ITA §66.4 |
| Flow-through shares | Transfer CEE/CDE deductions to investors (share purchasers claim the deductions) | ITA §66(12.6), §66(12.601) |

**Practical Notes:**
- Canada's resource taxation operates on a two-tier system: federal CIT applies to all resource income, and provinces levy additional royalties and mining taxes which are then deductible for federal purposes.
- Flow-through shares are a unique Canadian mechanism allowing junior exploration companies to renounce exploration/development expenses to investors, providing immediate tax deductions to the investors.
- The accelerated CCA rates for resource sector equipment (Class 41/41.2) combined with CEE and CDE provide significant front-loaded tax deductions for resource companies.

### 10.3 Insurance Premium Taxes

> **Legal Basis**: ITA §211.1 (Part XII.3 — investment income of life insurers), ITA §212(1)(d.1) (WHT on insurance premiums to non-residents), various provincial insurance premium tax statutes

| Parameter | Treatment | Legal Reference |
|---|---|---|
| Federal: Part XII.3 tax | Tax on **investment income of life insurers** | ITA §211.1 |
| Ontario premium tax | **2%** life insurance; **3%** P&C insurance | Ontario Insurance Act (premium tax provisions) |
| Quebec premium tax | **3.48%** on gross premiums | Quebec Insurers Tax Act |
| British Columbia premium tax | **2-4.4%** depending on class of insurance | BC Insurance Premium Tax Act |
| General provincial range | **2-4%** on gross premiums (varies by province and class) | Various provincial statutes |
| Non-resident insurer WHT | **25%** on insurance premium payments to non-resident insurers | ITA §212(1)(d.1) |
| Insurance corporation special rules | Specific computation rules for life insurers including policy reserves and segregated funds | ITA Part XII.3 |

**Practical Notes:**
- Provincial insurance premium taxes are levied in lieu of provincial income tax for insurance companies in some provinces, while others levy both.
- The Part XII.3 tax on investment income of life insurers is designed to approximate the tax that would be paid by policyholders if they held the investments directly.
- Non-resident insurers writing Canadian risks are subject to Part XIII WHT on premiums, which may be reduced under applicable tax treaties.

### 10.4 Financial Transaction Taxes

> **Legal Basis**: Various provincial land transfer tax statutes

| Parameter | Treatment | Legal Reference |
|---|---|---|
| Federal FTT | **None** — Canada does not impose a federal financial transaction tax | N/A |
| Provincial FTT | **None** — no province imposes a securities transaction tax | N/A |
| Ontario (proposed FTT) | Proposed but **not implemented** | N/A |
| Securities commissions | Levy regulatory **fees** (not classified as taxes) | Provincial Securities Acts |
| Ontario land transfer tax | **0.5-2.5%** on property value (tiered); additional 25% non-resident speculation tax in specified areas | Ontario Land Transfer Tax Act |
| BC property transfer tax | **1-3%** on fair market value (tiered); additional **20% foreign buyers tax** in specified areas | BC Property Transfer Tax Act |

**Practical Notes:**
- While Canada has no securities transaction tax, various provincial land transfer taxes apply on real property transactions and can represent a significant cost, particularly in Ontario and British Columbia.
- The non-resident/foreign buyer surcharges (Ontario: 25%, BC: 20%) in designated areas are designed to address housing affordability concerns and apply in addition to the base land transfer tax.
- Toronto imposes a municipal land transfer tax in addition to Ontario's provincial land transfer tax, resulting in double land transfer tax on Toronto property purchases.

### 10.5 Gaming / Betting Revenue Taxes

> **Legal Basis**: Provincial gaming legislation; ITA §40(2)(f) (windfall gains exemption)

| Parameter | Treatment | Legal Reference |
|---|---|---|
| Jurisdiction | **Provincial** — gaming is operated and regulated by provincial Crown corporations | Provincial gaming statutes |
| Provincial gaming revenue | Retained by province as Crown corporation revenue; **not taxed as corporate income** | Provincial gaming acts |
| Private operators | Subject to provincial gaming **regulations and licensing fees** | Provincial gaming regulations |
| Online gaming | Regulated provincially; Ontario iGaming market **launched April 2022** | Ontario iGaming (Alcohol and Gaming Commission) |
| Prizes/winnings to individuals | Generally **NOT taxable** to individual winners in Canada (treated as windfall) | ITA §40(2)(f); CRA administrative position |
| Professional gamblers | **May be taxed as business income** if gambling constitutes a business activity (systematic, profit-seeking) | ITA §9; case law (e.g., *Luprypa v. The Queen*) |

**Practical Notes:**
- Canada's approach to gaming taxation is fundamentally different from most jurisdictions. Provincial governments operate gaming through Crown corporations (e.g., Ontario Lottery and Gaming Corporation, BCLC) and retain the revenue directly rather than taxing private operators.
- The non-taxability of gambling winnings for individuals is a longstanding CRA administrative position based on the characterization of winnings as a windfall. However, professional or systematic gambling may be recharacterized as business income.
- Ontario's iGaming market (launched 2022) allows private operators to offer online gaming to Ontario residents, subject to regulatory oversight and revenue-sharing agreements with the provincial government.

### 10.6 Agricultural Income Taxation

> **Legal Basis**: ITA §28 (cash method for farming), ITA §31 (restricted farm losses), ITA §73(3)-(4) (intergenerational farm transfers), ITA §110.6 (LCGE for qualified farm property)

| Parameter | Treatment | Statutory Reference |
|---|---|---|
| Cash method of accounting | Available for farming businesses (**not available** for other businesses) | ITA §28 |
| Restricted farm loss | Limited to **$2,500 + 50% of next $30,000** ($17,500 max) if farming is not chief source of income | ITA §31 |
| Farm loss carry-forward | Same as regular NOL: **20 years forward, 3 years back** | ITA §111(1)(a) |
| Intergenerational farm transfers | Rollover at **tax cost** (no immediate tax on transfer to child/grandchild) | ITA §73(3), §73(4) |
| Grain deferral | Can defer income on grain delivered to the Canadian Wheat Board (or designated agent) | ITA §76 |
| Supply management quota | CCA **Class 14.1** (5% declining balance for quota and other intangible farm assets) | Reg. Sched. II, Class 14.1 |
| AgriStability / AgriInvest | Government income stabilization programs (**not tax provisions**; funded through federal-provincial agreements) | Agriculture and Agri-Food Administrative Monetary Penalties Act |
| Qualified farm property LCGE | **$1,016,836** (2024, indexed) capital gains exemption on disposition of qualified farm property | ITA §110.6(2) |

**Practical Notes:**
- The cash method of accounting under ITA §28 allows farmers to defer income by controlling the timing of grain and livestock sales, making it a significant tax planning tool.
- The restricted farm loss rule (ITA §31) applies to taxpayers whose chief source of income is neither farming nor a combination of farming and another source. Recent case law (*Craig v. Canada*, 2012 SCC 43) clarified that a "combination" source can include non-farming income that is subordinate to farming.
- The intergenerational farm rollover provisions (ITA §73(3)-(4)) were expanded in 2024 to include transfers to farming corporations and partnerships controlled by the taxpayer's children, facilitating succession planning.
- The qualified farm property LCGE ($1,016,836 in 2024) is in addition to the QSBC share exemption and can be claimed by individuals on the disposition of qualifying farm land, buildings, and quota.
