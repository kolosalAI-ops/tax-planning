# Canada - International Investors

> **Jurisdiction**: Federal (CRA) + Provincial/Territorial
> **Governing Law**: Income Tax Act (ITA), R.S.C. 1985, c. 1 (5th Supp.); Investment Canada Act (ICA), R.S.C. 1985, c. 28 (1st Supp.)
> **Tax Year**: 2024/2025
> **Currency**: Canadian Dollar (CAD / C$)

## 1. Foreign Ownership Restrictions & Investment Approvals

> **Legal Basis**: Investment Canada Act (ICA), R.S.C. 1985, c. 28 (1st Supp.); ICA §14-14.11 (net benefit review thresholds), ICA §25.1-25.4 (national security review)

### Investment Canada Act (ICA) Overview

Canada screens foreign investments through the Investment Canada Act, administered by Innovation, Science and Economic Development Canada (ISED). Two separate review processes apply: the **net benefit review** (threshold-based, for large acquisitions) and the **national security review** (no threshold, any transaction).

### Net Benefit Review Thresholds (2024)

| Investor Type | Threshold (Enterprise Value) | Statutory Reference |
|---|---|---|
| WTO private-sector investors | **CAD 1.326 billion** | ICA §14.1(1), adjusted annually |
| State-owned enterprise (SOE) investors | **CAD 512 million** | ICA §14.1(1.1) |
| Non-WTO investors | **CAD 5 million** (direct), **CAD 50 million** (indirect) | ICA §14(3)-(4) |
| Cultural businesses | **CAD 5 million** (direct), **CAD 50 million** (indirect) regardless of WTO status | ICA §14.1(6) |

The threshold is based on **enterprise value** (equity value plus debt minus cash) for WTO investors since 2015. The Minister has **75 days** (extendable by 30 days, plus further extensions with investor consent) to complete the net benefit determination under ICA §21-23.

### National Security Review

| Parameter | Value | Statutory Reference |
|---|---|---|
| Threshold | **None** -- any investment may be reviewed | ICA §25.1(1) |
| Initial assessment period | **45 days** after filing | ICA §25.2(1) |
| Order for review | Governor in Council may order a full review | ICA §25.3(1) |
| Remedies | Block, unwind, impose conditions, or accept undertakings | ICA §25.4(1) |
| Applies to | Investments, establishments, acquisitions (including minority stakes and joint ventures) | ICA §25.1(1)-(3) |

### Sector-Specific Foreign Ownership Restrictions

| Sector | Ownership Limit | Legal Basis |
|---|---|---|
| **Banking (large Schedule I banks)** | 20% per person, 65% total non-resident ownership | Bank Act, §372-374 |
| **Banking (small/medium Schedule I banks)** | 65% individual ownership permitted | Bank Act, §372(2) |
| **Telecommunications** | 46.7% effective indirect foreign ownership (20% direct in licensee, 33.3% in holding company) | Telecommunications Act, §16; Canadian Telecommunications Common Carrier Ownership and Control Regulations |
| **Airlines (domestic)** | 49% voting interests (25% single non-resident) | Canada Transportation Act, §55(1) |
| **Broadcasting & media** | 20% direct voting, 33.3% of holding company | Broadcasting Act; Direction to the CRTC (Ineligibility of Non-Canadians) |
| **Cultural industries** | Subject to review at any value; net benefit review applies at CAD 5 million | ICA §14.1(6); Investment Canada Act cultural industry definition |
| **Uranium mining** | 49% maximum non-Canadian ownership | Non-Resident Ownership Policy (NRCan administrative policy) |
| **Fishing** | Canadian control required for licenses | Fisheries Act; Coastal Fisheries Protection Act |
| **Book publishing & distribution** | New foreign investment reviewed; indirect acquisitions must ensure Canadian control | Investment Canada Act (cultural sector policy) |

## 2. Non-Resident Investor Taxation

> **Legal Basis**: ITA §2(3) (non-resident taxable income), ITA §115(1)(b) (taxable Canadian property gains), ITA §116 (compliance certificate), ITA §212 (Part XIII withholding tax), ITA §219 (branch tax)

### Capital Gains Tax on Shares

Non-residents are subject to Canadian tax on dispositions of **Taxable Canadian Property (TCP)** as defined in ITA §248(1).

| TCP Category | Description | Statutory Reference |
|---|---|---|
| Canadian real property | Real or immovable property situated in Canada | ITA §248(1) "taxable Canadian property" (a) |
| Resource property | Canadian resource properties, timber resource properties | ITA §248(1) "taxable Canadian property" (b)-(c) |
| Private corporation shares | Shares of a corporation (not listed on a designated stock exchange) if >50% of fair market value derived from Canadian real property, resource property, or timber at any time in the preceding 60 months | ITA §248(1) "taxable Canadian property" (d) |
| Listed shares | Shares listed on a designated stock exchange are **not TCP** (exempt under most treaties even if they otherwise qualify) | ITA §248(1) "taxable Canadian property" (d); Treaty Art. XIII(4)-(5) |
| Partnership interests | If >50% of FMV derived from Canadian real property | ITA §248(1) "taxable Canadian property" (e) |

### ITA Section 116 Compliance Certificate

| Parameter | Value | Statutory Reference |
|---|---|---|
| Purpose | Non-resident must obtain a certificate of compliance before or after disposing of TCP | ITA §116(1)-(2) |
| Withholding by purchaser | **25%** of purchase price must be withheld if no certificate obtained | ITA §116(5) |
| Treaty-exempt property | If gains exempt under treaty (e.g., listed shares), purchaser may rely on treaty but should obtain certificate | ITA §116(5.2) |
| Filing deadline | Within 10 days of disposition | ITA §116(3) |
| Penalty for non-compliance | Purchaser liable for 25% of purchase price | ITA §116(5) |

## 3. Repatriation of Profits

> **Legal Basis**: ITA §212 (Part XIII withholding tax); Bank of Canada Act; Currency Act; no exchange control legislation

### Foreign Exchange Controls

Canada maintains **no foreign exchange controls**. The Canadian dollar is fully convertible, and there are no restrictions on:

| Area | Status | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Currency convertibility | **Fully convertible** | No restrictions on purchase or sale of foreign currencies |
| Capital account transactions | **No restrictions** | No central bank approval required for investments or repatriation |
| Current account transactions | **No restrictions** | No approvals needed for trade payments |
| Central bank registration | **Not required** | No requirement to register foreign investments with the Bank of Canada |
| Repatriation of capital | **No restrictions** | Principal and profits may be freely repatriated |
| Cross-border payments | **No restrictions** | Subject only to withholding tax obligations under ITA Part XIII |

### Repatriation Channels and Tax Treatment

| Repatriation Method | WHT Rate (Default) | Treaty Rate (Typical) | Statutory Reference |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Dividends** | 25% | 5-15% (depending on ownership) | ITA §212(2) |
| **Interest (arm's length)** | **0%** | 0% | ITA §212(1)(b)(vii) -- exempt since 2008 for arm's length |
| **Interest (non-arm's length)** | 25% | 0-15% | ITA §212(1)(b) |
| **Royalties** | 25% | 0-10% | ITA §212(1)(d) |
| **Management/technical fees** | 25% | 0-25% (many treaties do not have a specific article; taxed as business profits or other income) | ITA §212(1)(a) |
| **Branch profits** | 25% branch tax | 5% | ITA §219(1) |
| **Return of capital** | 0% (tax-free to extent of paid-up capital) | N/A | ITA §84(4), §90(2) |

### Key Considerations

- **No thin capitalization WHT surcharge**: Canada does not impose additional withholding on excess interest beyond the re-characterization rules under ITA §18(4) and EIFEL rules (ITA §18.2). Denied interest deductions may be re-characterized as dividends for WHT purposes under ITA §214(16).
- **Transfer pricing**: All cross-border payments between related parties must be at arm's length prices under ITA §247. Failure to comply can result in adjustments plus a **10% penalty** on the adjustment under ITA §247(3).
- **Anti-avoidance**: The General Anti-Avoidance Rule (GAAR) under ITA §245 may apply to repatriation structures that are primarily tax-motivated.

## 4. Branch vs. Subsidiary

> **Legal Basis**: ITA §2(3) (non-resident taxation), ITA §219 (branch tax), ITA §212(2) (dividend WHT), Bank Act Part XII.1 (foreign bank branches)

### Structural Comparison

| Feature | Branch | Subsidiary |
|---|---|---|
| **Legal status** | Extension of foreign parent; no separate legal entity | Separate Canadian legal entity (corporation) |
| **Corporate income tax** | Federal 15% + provincial (same as subsidiary) | Federal 15% + provincial |
| **Profit repatriation tax** | **Branch tax: 25%** (ITA §219) on undistributed profits not reinvested in Canada | **Dividend WHT: 25%** (ITA §212(2)) on declared dividends |
| **Treaty-reduced repatriation tax** | Typically **5%** branch tax | **5-15%** dividend WHT |
| **First CAD 500,000 exemption** | Available under most treaties (branch tax) | Not available (WHT applies from first dollar) |
| **Loss utilization** | Losses offset against foreign parent's worldwide income (subject to foreign rules) | Losses trapped in Canadian subsidiary |
| **Limited liability** | Parent fully liable for branch obligations | Parent liability limited to investment in subsidiary |
| **Thin cap / EIFEL** | Applicable (ITA §18(4), §18.2) | Applicable (ITA §18(4), §18.2) |
| **Transfer pricing** | ITA §247 applies to branch allocations | ITA §247 applies to intercompany transactions |
| **GST/HST** | Must register and collect | Must register and collect |

### Canadian Branch of a Foreign Bank

| Parameter | Value | Statutory Reference |
|---|---|---|
| Authorization | Must obtain Order to commence/carry on business in Canada | Bank Act §524 |
| Regulation | OSFI-regulated under Bank Act Part XII.1 | Bank Act §510-588 |
| Capital requirements | Must maintain adequate assets in Canada | Bank Act §534 |
| Lending restrictions | **Full-service branch**: lending powers similar to domestic banks; **Lending branch**: limited to loans > $5 million, no retail deposits | Bank Act §545-545.1 |
| Deposit insurance | Not eligible for CDIC deposit insurance | Canada Deposit Insurance Corporation Act |

### Effective Tax Rate Comparison (Ontario)

| Component | Branch (Ontario) | Subsidiary (Ontario) |
|---|---|---|
| Pre-tax profit | CAD 1,000,000 | CAD 1,000,000 |
| Federal CIT (15%) | 150,000 | 150,000 |
| Provincial CIT (Ontario 11.5%) | 115,000 | 115,000 |
| **Total CIT** | **265,000** | **265,000** |
| After-tax profit | 735,000 | 735,000 |
| Branch tax (25% default) | 183,750 | N/A |
| Branch tax (5% treaty) | 36,750 | N/A |
| Dividend WHT (25% default) | N/A | 183,750 |
| Dividend WHT (5% treaty, 10%+ ownership) | N/A | 36,750 |
| **Total tax (default)** | **448,750 (44.9%)** | **448,750 (44.9%)** |
| **Total tax (US treaty)** | **301,750 (30.2%)** | **301,750 (30.2%)** |
| **Total tax (US treaty, 15% other dividend)** | **301,750 (30.2%)** | **375,250 (37.5%)** |

> **Note**: For a US parent with 10%+ ownership, the effective rates are equivalent. However, the branch may benefit from the **CAD 500,000 cumulative exemption** for branch profits tax under many treaties. For parents in non-treaty countries, the branch and subsidiary produce identical tax outcomes (25% additional tax in both cases).

### Hybrid Entities and Check-the-Box Issues

Under US tax rules (Treas. Reg. §301.7701-3), a Canadian unlimited liability company (ULC) may be treated as a disregarded entity for US tax purposes while being a separate taxable entity in Canada. This creates potential for:

- **Double non-taxation** of certain income streams (addressed by the Fifth Protocol to the Canada-US Tax Convention, Art. IV(7)(b))
- **Hybrid mismatch** issues under Canadian EIFEL rules (ITA §18.2) and proposed hybrid mismatch rules
- **Treaty benefits denial** where the hybrid entity's income is not subject to tax in the residence state

## 5. Foreign Tax Credit

> **Legal Basis**: ITA §126(1) (non-business income FTC), ITA §126(2) (business income FTC), ITA §126(7) (definitions)

### Foreign Tax Credit Mechanism

Canada provides foreign tax credits under ITA §126 to eliminate double taxation on income earned abroad by Canadian resident corporations. Credits are computed on a **per-country** basis.

### Credit Types and Rules

| Parameter | Non-Business Income FTC | Business Income FTC | Statutory Reference |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Provision** | ITA §126(1) | ITA §126(2) | |
| **Limitation** | Lesser of: (a) foreign tax paid, or (b) Canadian tax attributable to foreign-source income | Lesser of: (a) foreign tax paid, or (b) Canadian tax attributable to foreign business income | ITA §126(1)(b), §126(2)(b) |
| **Carry-forward** | **None** (use it or lose it) | **10 years** | ITA §126(2.3) |
| **Carry-back** | **None** | **3 years** | ITA §126(2.3) |
| **Per-country limitation** | Yes -- separate calculation for each country | Yes -- separate calculation for each country | ITA §126(1), §126(2) |
| **Provincial FTC** | Most provinces provide a provincial FTC on business income | Varies by province | Provincial tax legislation |
| **Ordering** | Non-business FTC claimed first, then business FTC | Business FTC claimed after non-business FTC | ITA §126(1)-(2) ordering |

### Limitation Formula

The foreign tax credit for each type is the **lesser of**:

1. The foreign tax paid or payable to a particular country; and
2. The Canadian tax otherwise payable, multiplied by: (Foreign-source income from that country) / (Worldwide income)

### Foreign Tax Definitions

| Term | Definition | Statutory Reference |
|---|---|---|
| Non-business income tax | Tax paid to a foreign government that is not a business income tax (e.g., withholding tax on passive income) | ITA §126(7)(a) |
| Business income tax | Tax paid to a foreign government on business income earned in that country | ITA §126(7)(b) |
| Qualifying incomes | Income from sources in the foreign country included in Canadian taxable income | ITA §126(7)(d) |
| Tax-exempt income | Income exempt under a tax treaty does not generate FTC entitlement | ITA §126(1)(b)(i) |

### Foreign Tax Credit Summary Table

| Credit Type | Maximum Claim | Carry-Forward | Carry-Back | Limitation |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Non-business income FTC | Canadian tax on foreign non-business income | **0 years** | **0 years** | Per-country; no excess carry |
| Business income FTC | Canadian tax on foreign business income | **10 years** | **3 years** | Per-country |
| Foreign tax deduction (alternative) | Deduction under ITA §20(12) for non-creditable foreign non-business tax | N/A | N/A | Available when FTC is insufficient |
| Provincial FTC (business) | Provincial tax on foreign business income | Varies | Varies | Per province |

> **Note**: Where the foreign tax exceeds the Canadian FTC limit for non-business income, the excess may be claimed as a deduction under ITA §20(12) or §20(11) against income. This provides partial relief but at a lower effective rate than a credit.

## 6. CFC Rules (Foreign Accrual Property Income - FAPI)

> **Legal Basis**: ITA §91-95 (foreign accrual property income), ITA §113(1) (deduction for dividends from foreign affiliates), ITA §95(1) (definitions: controlled foreign affiliate, foreign affiliate)

### Overview

Canada's controlled foreign corporation (CFC) regime is known as the **Foreign Accrual Property Income (FAPI)** regime. It applies to Canadian resident taxpayers that hold interests in controlled foreign affiliates (CFAs). The regime requires that passive income earned by a CFA be included in the Canadian taxpayer's income on a current basis, regardless of whether it is distributed.

### Key Definitions

| Term | Definition | Statutory Reference |
|---|---|---|
| **Foreign Affiliate (FA)** | A non-resident corporation in which a Canadian taxpayer has an **equity percentage of 10% or more** | ITA §95(1) "foreign affiliate" |
| **Controlled Foreign Affiliate (CFA)** | A foreign affiliate that is **controlled** by the Canadian taxpayer (alone or together with up to 4 other Canadian residents), with an equity percentage of **50% or more** | ITA §95(1) "controlled foreign affiliate"; ITA §95(2)(a.1)-(a.2) |
| **Equity percentage** | Ownership interest determined by reference to votes, value, and distributions on liquidation | ITA §95(4) |
| **FAPI** | Passive income of a CFA that is included in the Canadian shareholder's income | ITA §95(1) "foreign accrual property income" |
| **Active business income** | Income from an active business carried on by the FA; generally **exempt** from FAPI | ITA §95(1) "income from an active business" |

### FAPI Income Categories

| Category | Treatment | Statutory Reference |
|---|---|---|
| **Interest and dividends** | Included in FAPI | ITA §95(1) "foreign accrual property income" (a) |
| **Rents and royalties (passive)** | Included in FAPI | ITA §95(1) "foreign accrual property income" (a) |
| **Capital gains** | 50% taxable capital gain portion included in FAPI | ITA §95(1) "foreign accrual property income" (b); ITA §95(2)(f) |
| **Income from property** | Included in FAPI unless from active business | ITA §95(1) "foreign accrual property income" (a) |
| **Income from investment business** | Included in FAPI (defined as principally earning income from property) | ITA §95(1) "investment business" |
| **Active business income** | **Excluded** from FAPI -- taxed only when dividends are paid to Canada (if at all) | ITA §95(1) "income from an active business" |
| **Income from non-qualifying business** | Included in FAPI unless the business employs more than 5 full-time employees | ITA §95(1) "investment business" (exception for 5+ employees) |

### Surplus Regime (Dividends from Foreign Affiliates)

When a foreign affiliate pays dividends to a Canadian corporate shareholder, the tax treatment depends on the **surplus pool** from which the dividend is paid.

| Surplus Pool | Description | Tax Treatment on Dividend | Statutory Reference |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Exempt surplus** | Active business income earned in a **treaty country** or **TIEA country**; certain inter-affiliate dividends | **Tax-free**: Full deduction under ITA §113(1)(a) | ITA §5907(1) "exempt surplus" (ITR); ITA §113(1)(a) |
| **Hybrid surplus** | Capital gains earned in a treaty/TIEA country on shares of other foreign affiliates | **50% deduction** under ITA §113(1)(a.1) | ITA §5907(1) "hybrid surplus" (ITR); ITA §113(1)(a.1) |
| **Taxable surplus** | Income that is not exempt or hybrid surplus (e.g., active business income from non-treaty countries, FAPI previously included) | **Grossed-up and taxed** with underlying foreign tax credit under ITA §91(4) or deduction under ITA §113(1)(b)-(d) | ITA §5907(1) "taxable surplus" (ITR); ITA §113(1)(b)-(d) |
| **Pre-acquisition surplus** | Surplus existing at time of acquisition of FA | Deemed return of capital (reduces ACB) | ITA §5907(1) "pre-acquisition surplus" (ITR); ITA §92(2) |

### FAPI Inclusion Mechanism

| Step | Description | Statutory Reference |
|---|---|---|
| 1. Calculate FAPI | Determine CFA's FAPI for the taxation year | ITA §91(1), §95(1) |
| 2. Include in income | Canadian shareholder includes its share of FAPI (based on equity percentage) | ITA §91(1) |
| 3. Add to ACB | FAPI inclusion added to the adjusted cost base of CFA shares | ITA §92(1)(a) |
| 4. Deduction for foreign taxes | Deduction for "foreign accrual tax" applicable to FAPI | ITA §91(4) |
| 5. Subsequent dividend | Dividend paid from taxable surplus (previously taxed FAPI) receives a deduction to prevent double taxation | ITA §113(1)(b)-(d) |
| 6. Reduce ACB | Deductible dividends from FA reduce ACB of shares | ITA §92(1)(b) |

### Canada-US Tax Convention (Fifth Protocol, 2007)

The Fifth Protocol to the Canada-US Tax Convention introduced significant changes:

| Feature | Description | Treaty Article |
|---|---|---|
| **Limitation on Benefits (LOB)** | Comprehensive LOB provision to prevent treaty shopping; requires qualified person status or derivative benefits test | Art. XXIX-A |
| **Interest withholding elimination** | Phased elimination of WHT on cross-border interest (0% since Jan 1, 2008) | Art. XI |
| **Hybrid entities** | Rules addressing fiscally transparent entities and hybrid mismatches | Art. IV(6)-(7) |
| **Arbitration** | Mandatory binding arbitration for unresolved MAP cases | Art. XXVI(6)-(7) |
| **Stock options** | Source-country taxation rules for stock option benefits | Art. XV(2)(b) |
| **Withholding on pension/RRSP** | Cross-border pension distributions subject to source country WHT (generally 15%) | Art. XVIII |
| **Services PE** | No specific services PE provision (follows OECD model) | Art. V |

### Multilateral Instrument (MLI) and Principal Purpose Test (PPT)

| Parameter | Description |
|---|---|
| **MLI ratification** | Canada ratified the MLI on June 21, 2019 (in force September 1, 2019) |
| **PPT adoption** | Canada adopted the **Principal Purpose Test (PPT)** as the minimum standard for treaty abuse prevention |
| **LOB election** | Canada did not adopt the detailed LOB under the MLI (has bilateral LOB with the US only) |
| **Arbitration under MLI** | Canada opted into mandatory binding arbitration under Part VI of the MLI |
| **Treaties modified** | Most of Canada's 90+ treaties now include PPT through the MLI |
| **PE modifications** | Canada adopted expanded PE definitions under Art. 12-15 of the MLI for some treaties |

## 8. Permanent Establishment (PE) Risk

> **Legal Basis**: ITA §253 (extended meaning of carrying on business in Canada); Treaty Art. 5 (PE definition, OECD Model); ITA §2(3)(b) (non-resident taxation of business income)

### Domestic Law: Carrying on Business in Canada

ITA §253 provides an **extended meaning** of "carrying on business in Canada" for non-residents:

| Activity | PE Trigger | Statutory Reference |
|---|---|---|
| Producing, growing, mining, creating, manufacturing, fabricating, improving, preserving, or constructing anything in Canada | **Yes** | ITA §253(a) |
| Soliciting orders or offering anything for sale in Canada through an agent or servant | **Yes** | ITA §253(b) |
| Disposing of Canadian real property, resource property, or timber resource property | **Yes** (deems carrying on business) | ITA §253(c) |
| Having an inventory of goods in Canada for delivery | **Yes** (deemed carrying on business) | ITA §253(b) |

> **Important**: ITA §253 is broader than the treaty PE definition. Where a tax treaty applies, the treaty PE definition takes precedence under ITA §2(3). A non-resident with treaty protection can only be taxed on business income attributable to a PE as defined in the applicable treaty.

### Treaty PE Definition (OECD Model Art. 5)

| PE Type | Threshold / Criteria | Typical Treaty Provision |
|---|---|---|
| **Fixed place of business** | A fixed place through which the business of the enterprise is wholly or partly carried on (office, branch, factory, workshop, mine, quarry, etc.) | Art. 5(1)-(2) |
| **Construction PE** | Building site, construction, or installation project lasting more than **12 months** | Art. 5(3) |
| **Service PE** | Services performed in Canada for more than **183 days in any 12-month period** (included in some treaties, e.g., Canada-India, Canada-China) | Art. 5(3)(b) (where applicable) |
| **Dependent agent PE** | A person acting on behalf of the enterprise who habitually exercises authority to conclude contracts in the name of the enterprise | Art. 5(5) |
| **Independent agent PE exclusion** | An agent of independent status acting in the ordinary course of business is **not** a PE | Art. 5(6) |

### Activities That Do Not Constitute a PE (Treaty-Protected)

| Activity | Treaty Provision |
|---|---|
| Storage, display, or delivery of goods | Art. 5(4)(a)-(b) |
| Maintaining stock of goods for storage, display, or delivery | Art. 5(4)(a) |
| Maintaining stock of goods for processing by another enterprise | Art. 5(4)(b) |
| Purchasing goods or merchandise, or collecting information | Art. 5(4)(d) |
| Advertising, supplying information, or conducting scientific research | Art. 5(4)(d)-(e) |
| Any combination of the above, if the overall activity is preparatory or auxiliary | Art. 5(4)(f) |

### Specific PE Considerations for Canada

| Issue | Details |
|---|---|
| **Construction PE (12 months)** | Most Canadian treaties specify a 12-month threshold. The period runs from the date the contractor first begins work (including preparatory activities). Interruptions generally do not stop the clock. |
| **Service PE (183 days)** | Found in several Canadian treaties (e.g., Canada-India Art. 5(3)(b), Canada-China Art. 5(3)(b)). The 183-day test is computed over any 12-month period. Days of all connected employees are aggregated. |
| **Dependent agent PE** | Under the MLI modifications adopted by Canada, this includes persons who habitually play the principal role leading to the conclusion of contracts (even if they do not formally sign). |
| **Digital PE** | Canada does **not** currently have a digital PE concept in its domestic law or treaties. However, Canada has implemented the Digital Services Tax Act (effective January 1, 2024) imposing a **3% tax on revenue** from digital services (separate from PE rules). |
| **Server / equipment PE** | A server located in Canada may constitute a PE if business functions are carried out through it (consistent with OECD commentary on Art. 5). |
| **Warehousing and fulfillment** | If a non-resident uses a Canadian warehouse to store goods for delivery to customers and the warehouse staff exercise authority to conclude sales, a PE may exist. Mere storage is protected under Art. 5(4). |
| **Anti-fragmentation** | Under the MLI Art. 13 (adopted by Canada for some treaties), the preparatory/auxiliary exception does not apply if the activities form part of a cohesive business operation by closely related enterprises. |

### PE Risk Mitigation Strategies

| Strategy | Description |
|---|---|
| Limit employee activities | Ensure employees in Canada perform only preparatory/auxiliary activities |
| Use independent contractors | Engage arm's length Canadian service providers rather than dependent agents |
| Avoid contract conclusion authority | Ensure that contracts are concluded outside Canada by non-Canadian personnel |
| Monitor construction timelines | For construction projects, plan to complete within 12 months or structure separate contracts |
| Track service days | Maintain records of employee days in Canada to remain below 183-day thresholds |
| Commission agent structures | Use commissionnaire arrangements (though effectiveness reduced post-MLI) |
