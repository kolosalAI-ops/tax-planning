# Italy - Taxable Income Deductions

> **Jurisdiction**: National (Agenzia delle Entrate)
> **Governing Law**: Testo Unico delle Imposte sui Redditi (TUIR — DPR 917/1986); IRAP (D.Lgs. 446/1997)
> **Tax Year**: 2024/2025
> **Currency**: EUR (€)

---

## 1. Depreciation & Capital Allowances

### General Principles — Art. 102 TUIR

Depreciation (ammortamento) is allowed on tangible and intangible fixed assets used in the business. Rates are set by Ministerial Decree (DM 31 December 1988, as updated) and represent maximum annual allowances.

### Key Depreciation Rates — Ministerial Decree Tables

| Asset Category | Maximum Rate | Method | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| Industrial buildings | **3%** | Straight-line | Art. 102 TUIR |
| Commercial buildings | **3%** | Straight-line | |
| Light commercial construction | **4–6%** | Straight-line | Sector-specific tables |
| General machinery and equipment | **10–15%** | Straight-line | Industry-specific rates apply |
| Specialised machinery | **15–25%** | Straight-line | |
| Office furniture | **12%** | Straight-line | |
| Computer equipment | **20%** | Straight-line | |
| Motor vehicles | **25%** | Straight-line | Subject to deductibility caps — Art. 164 TUIR |
| Patents and know-how | **50%** (or contractual life) | Straight-line | Art. 103 TUIR |
| Goodwill | **5.56%** (1/18th) | Straight-line | Art. 103 TUIR |
| Trademarks | **5.56%** (1/18th) | Straight-line | Art. 103 TUIR |

### First-Year Rule

| Parameter | Value |
|---|---|
| Year of acquisition | Depreciation limited to **50%** of the normal rate | Art. 102 Abs. 1 TUIR |
| Year of disposal | No depreciation in year of sale |

### Super-Depreciation and Hyper-Depreciation (Historical)

| Incentive | Status | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Super-ammortamento (130–140%) | **Expired** — last applicable to assets acquired by 30 June 2020 | L. 208/2015 |
| Iper-ammortamento (150–270%) | **Expired** — replaced by Credito d'Imposta Beni Strumentali | L. 232/2016 |
| Tax credit for capital goods (Industria 4.0) | **Active** — 20% credit for ordinary assets in 2024; 20% for 4.0 tangible assets; 5–10% for 4.0 intangibles | Art. 1 para. 1051–1063 L. 178/2020 (as amended) [unverified — confirm with local counsel] |

### Low-Value Assets — Art. 102 Abs. 5 TUIR

| Parameter | Value |
|---|---|
| Immediate expensing threshold | Assets with unit cost ≤ **€516.46** |

## 2. Business Operating Expenses

### General Deductibility — Art. 109 TUIR

Business expenses (costi e oneri) are deductible in the fiscal year in which they are incurred, provided they are inherent to the business activity and adequately documented.

### Key Deductible Operating Expenses

| Expense Category | Deductibility | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Employee salaries and wages | Fully deductible for IRES | Art. 95 TUIR; subject to arm's-length for related parties |
| Employer social contributions (INPS, INAIL) | Fully deductible | |
| Rent and lease payments | Fully deductible | Arm's-length for related parties |
| Professional fees | Fully deductible | Withholding obligation at source (20%) |
| Repairs and maintenance | Deductible up to **5%** of depreciable asset value per year; excess carried forward over 5 years | Art. 102 Abs. 6 TUIR |
| Bad debts | Deductible when debtor is subject to insolvency proceedings or loss is certain and definite | Art. 101 Abs. 5 TUIR |
| Insurance premiums | Fully deductible | |
| Start-up and organisation costs | Deductible in year incurred or amortised over 5 years | Art. 108 TUIR |

### Charitable Donations — Art. 100 TUIR

| Parameter | Value |
|---|---|
| General donations | Deductible up to **2%** of declared business income |
| Donations to ONLUS and Third Sector entities | Deductible up to **10%** of total declared income or **€70,000** |
| Food donations | Fully deductible if meeting conditions of L. 166/2016 |

### Entertainment and Hospitality — Art. 108 Abs. 2 TUIR

| Revenue Bracket | Deductibility Cap |
|---|---|
| Up to €10M revenue | **1.5%** of revenue |
| €10M–€50M | **0.6%** of revenue |
| Above €50M | **0.4%** of revenue |

## 3. Interest & Financing Costs

### Interest Limitation — Art. 96 TUIR (ATAD-Aligned)

| Parameter | Value |
|---|---|
| Deductible limit | Net interest expense up to **30%** of tax-EBITDA |
| De minimis | None (Italy does not apply a de minimis; however net interest income is excluded from the computation) |
| Carry-forward of excess interest | **Indefinite** — disallowed net interest may be carried forward and deducted in future years when EBITDA capacity is available |
| EBITDA carry-forward | Unused EBITDA carried forward **5 years** (for entities existing before 2019) or indefinitely (for entities from 2019) [unverified — confirm with local counsel] |
| Group election (Consolidato Fiscale) | Excess EBITDA of one group member can be transferred to offset disallowed interest of another |

### ACE (Aiuto alla Crescita Economica) — REPEALED

| Parameter | Value |
|---|---|
| Status | **Repealed effective 1 January 2024** — Legge di Bilancio 2024 (L. 213/2023, Art. 1, para. 137) |
| Historical mechanism | Notional deduction on incremental equity (rate varied: 1.3% for 2023) |
| Transitional | Unused ACE surplus from prior years can still be carried forward and used as a deduction or converted to a tax credit |
| Replacement | No direct replacement has been enacted for 2024 |

## 4. Research & Development

Italy provides tax credits for R&D activities (Credito d'Imposta per Ricerca e Sviluppo) under the Industria 4.0 / Transizione 4.0 framework. The credit applies to qualifying R&D expenditure, including personnel costs, materials, and contracted research, and is offset against income tax liabilities.

See [R&D Incentives](rnd_incentives.md) for full program detail.

## 5. Loss Carry-Forward & Carry-Back

### Loss Carry-Forward — Art. 84 TUIR

| Parameter | Value |
|---|---|
| Duration | **Indefinite** |
| Restriction | Losses offset up to **80%** of taxable income per year |
| First 3 years | Losses from the first three fiscal years of activity may offset **100%** of income (no 80% cap) — Art. 84 Abs. 2 TUIR |
| Change of control | Losses forfeited upon change of majority ownership unless vitality test (revenue and employee thresholds) passed — Art. 84 Abs. 3 TUIR |

### Loss Carry-Back

| Parameter | Value |
|---|---|
| Status | **Not available** under Italian tax law |

### IRAP Losses

| Parameter | Value |
|---|---|
| Carry-forward | Indefinite; subject to **80%** annual cap |
| Treatment | IRAP losses are separate from IRES losses |

## 6. Provisions & Reserves

### Tax-Deductible Provisions

| Provision Type | Deductibility | Statutory Reference |
|---|---|---|
| Bad debt provisions | Deductible up to **0.5%** of receivables per year (max reserve 5% of receivables) | Art. 106 TUIR |
| Warranty provisions (product guarantees) | Deductible | Art. 107 TUIR |
| Employee severance indemnity (TFR) | Deductible — annual accrual | Art. 105 TUIR |
| Pension fund contributions | Deductible within limits | Art. 105 TUIR |
| Environmental provisions | Deductible if legal obligation exists | General principles |
| Litigation provisions | Generally **not deductible** until loss crystallises | Art. 107 TUIR |

### Non-Deductible Reserves

| Type | Notes |
|---|---|
| General risk reserves | Not deductible |
| Provisions for future costs without specific legal obligation | Not deductible |
| Revaluation reserves | Not deductible (unless tax realignment elected under specific laws) |

## 8. Treaties & Transfer Pricing

### Transfer Pricing — Art. 110 Abs. 7 TUIR / Art. 31-quater DPR 600/1973

| Parameter | Detail |
|---|---|
| Standard | Arm's-length principle (OECD compliant) |
| Documentation | Master file and local file required — Provvedimento Agenzia delle Entrate 23 November 2020 |
| Penalty protection | Adequate documentation provides protection from transfer pricing penalties (100–200% sanctions reduced) |
| Country-by-Country Reporting | Required for MNE groups with consolidated revenue ≥ €750M — Art. 1 para. 145 L. 208/2015 |
| APAs | Bilateral and unilateral available — Art. 31-ter DPR 600/1973 |

### Participation Exemption (PEX) — Art. 87 TUIR

| Parameter | Value |
|---|---|
| Exemption | **95%** of capital gains on qualifying participations exempt from IRES |
| Conditions | Holding ≥12 months; participation classified as financial fixed asset; subsidiary in a non-tax-haven jurisdiction; subsidiary carries on commercial activity |
| Dividends | 95% exempt if from qualifying subsidiaries (5% taxed) — Art. 89 TUIR |

### Treaty Network

Italy has over **100** double taxation agreements.

| Income Type | Typical Treaty Rate |
|---|---|
| Dividends (substantial holding) | 5–10% |
| Dividends (portfolio) | 15% |
| Interest | 0–10% |
| Royalties | 0–10% |
