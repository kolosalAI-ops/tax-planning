# Italy - R&D Tax Incentives

## 1. Overview

| Feature | Details |
|---|---|
| **Primary Incentive** | R&D Tax Credit (Credito d'imposta R&S) |
| **R&D Credit Rate** | 10% (cap €5 million per year) |
| **Innovation Credit Rate** | 5% (cap €2 million per year) |
| **Green/Digital Innovation Rate** | 10% (cap €2 million per year) |
| **Patent Box** | 110% super-deduction on R&D costs for qualifying IP |
| **Legal Basis (R&D Credit)** | Legge 160/2019, Art. 1, commi 198-209 |
| **Legal Basis (Patent Box)** | Decreto Legge 146/2021 (converted by L. 215/2021) |
| **Certification** | Certificazione required from 2024 for new claims |
| **Carry-Forward** | 3 years for unused credits |
| **Refundability** | Not refundable — offset against various taxes |

## 2. Detailed Programs

### 2.1 R&D Tax Credit (Credito d'Imposta per Ricerca e Sviluppo)

**Legal Basis:** L. 160/2019, Art. 1, commi 198-209; as modified by L. 178/2020 (Legge di Bilancio 2021) and subsequent budget laws

#### Credit Rates and Caps (Current)

| Activity Type | Credit Rate | Annual Cap | Period |
|---|---|---|---|
| Fundamental research & applied R&D | 10% | €5,000,000 | 2023–2031 |
| Technological innovation | 5% | €2,000,000 | 2024–2025 |
| Green transition & digital innovation (4.0) | 10% | €2,000,000 | 2024–2025 |
| Design & aesthetic innovation | 5% | €2,000,000 | 2024–2025 |

**Note:** Rates have been progressively reduced from earlier, more generous levels (20% R&D credit in 2020-2022).

#### Historical Rate Evolution

| Period | R&D Rate | Innovation Rate | Green/Digital Rate |
|---|---|---|---|
| 2020 | 12% (cap €3M) | 6% (cap €1.5M) | 10% (cap €1.5M) |
| 2021–2022 | 20% (cap €4M) | 10% (cap €2M) | 15% (cap €2M) |
| 2023 | 10% (cap €5M) | 5% (cap €2M) | 10% (cap €4M) |
| 2024–2025 | 10% (cap €5M) | 5% (cap €2M) | 10% (cap €2M) |

#### Qualifying R&D Activities

Under Italian law, R&D is defined in alignment with the OECD Frascati Manual:

- Fundamental research (ricerca fondamentale)
- Industrial/applied research (ricerca industriale)
- Experimental development (sviluppo sperimentale)
- Activities with elements of novelty, creativity, uncertainty, systematicness, and transferability/reproducibility

#### Qualifying Expenditure

| Cost Category | Eligible | Credit Basis |
|---|---|---|
| Personnel costs (R&D employees) | Yes | Actual cost to employer |
| Personnel costs (employees under 35, first hire for R&D) | Yes | 150% of actual cost |
| Contract R&D (universities/research bodies) | Yes | 150% of actual cost |
| Contract R&D (other entities) | Yes | Actual cost |
| Materials and supplies | Yes | If directly consumed in R&D |
| Depreciation of R&D instruments/equipment | Yes | Proportional to R&D use |
| Patent maintenance costs | Yes | For patents used in R&D |
| Consulting and equivalent services | Yes | Related to R&D activities |

### 2.2 Technological Innovation Credit

**Legal Basis:** L. 160/2019, Art. 1, comma 201

| Parameter | Details |
|---|---|
| Standard innovation rate | 5% |
| Green/digital innovation rate | 10% |
| Cap (standard) | €2,000,000 |
| Cap (green/digital) | €2,000,000 |
| Definition | Activities aimed at producing new or substantially improved products or processes |

#### Green Transition & Digital 4.0 Innovation

The enhanced 10% rate applies to technological innovation activities aimed at:

- Environmental sustainability objectives (ecological transition)
- Digital transformation and Industry 4.0 objectives
- Must be certified as meeting these specific objectives

### 2.3 Patent Box — 110% Super-Deduction

**Legal Basis:** DL 146/2021 (Art. 6), converted with modifications by L. 215/2021

Italy replaced its former patent box regime (reduced taxation of IP income) with a new **cost-based super-deduction** model:

| Parameter | Details |
|---|---|
| Super-deduction rate | 110% of qualifying R&D costs |
| Total deduction | 210% (100% standard + 110% additional) |
| Qualifying IP | Patents, industrial designs, utility models, software copyright, legally protected know-how |
| Qualifying costs | R&D costs directly connected to development/maintenance of qualifying IP |
| Election | Irrevocable for 5 tax years |
| Nexus requirement | OECD nexus approach applies |

#### How the New Patent Box Works

Instead of reducing tax on IP income, Italy now provides a **super-deduction on R&D costs** related to qualifying IP:

1. Company identifies qualifying IP assets
2. Company incurs R&D costs related to creating, developing, or maintaining those IP assets
3. Those R&D costs are deductible at **210%** (100% regular deduction + 110% super-deduction)
4. The election is made in the tax return and is irrevocable for 5 years
5. Nexus ratio applies to determine the qualifying portion

#### Comparison: Old vs. New Patent Box

| Feature | Old Patent Box (pre-2022) | New Patent Box (2022+) |
|---|---|---|
| Mechanism | 50% exemption of IP income | 110% super-deduction of R&D costs |
| Focus | Output-based (income) | Input-based (costs) |
| Benefit timing | When IP generates income | When R&D costs are incurred |
| Interaction with R&D credit | Separate | Can be combined (cumulative benefit) |
| OECD compliance | Required reform | Fully nexus-compliant |

### 2.4 Certificazione Requirement (from 2024)

**Legal Basis:** DL 73/2022, Art. 23; Implementing Decree DPCM 15.09.2023

From 2024, companies claiming R&D tax credits must obtain a **certificazione** (certification):

| Parameter | Details |
|---|---|
| Effective from | Tax periods starting from 2024 |
| Certifying bodies | Accredited entities listed by MIMIT (Ministry of Enterprises) |
| Scope | Confirms qualifying nature of R&D activities and eligible expenditure |
| Legal effect | Provides safe harbor — certification is binding on tax authorities for penalty purposes |
| Cost | Borne by the company; varies by certifying body |
| Voluntary for prior years | Companies can retroactively certify 2020-2023 claims |

## 3. Patent Box / IP Regime

*(Covered in detail under Section 2.3 above)*

### Summary of Current Patent Box

| Feature | Details |
|---|---|
| Type | 110% super-deduction on R&D costs related to qualifying IP |
| Effective tax benefit | At 24% IRES + 3.9% IRAP, the 110% super-deduction yields ~30.7% benefit on qualifying R&D costs |
| Qualifying IP | Patents, industrial designs, utility models, software copyright, trade secrets |
| Excluded IP | Trademarks, literary/artistic works (non-software) |
| Nexus approach | OECD BEPS Action 5 compliant |
| Cumulation | Can be combined with R&D tax credits (significant combined benefit) |
| Election period | 5 years, irrevocable |
| Penalty protection | Available through ruling or certificazione process |

### Combined Benefit: R&D Credit + Patent Box

For R&D costs related to qualifying IP, companies can claim **both**:

1. **10% R&D tax credit** on qualifying expenditure
2. **110% super-deduction** (Patent Box) on the same costs

This creates a substantial cumulative benefit for IP-intensive R&D activities.

## 5. Claim Process & Documentation

### Step-by-Step Claim Process

1. **Identify Qualifying Activities** — Classify as R&D, innovation, green/digital, or design
2. **Track and Segregate Costs** — Detailed cost accounting per project and activity type
3. **Prepare Technical Documentation** — Mandatory technical report per project
4. **Obtain Certificazione** — Engage accredited certifying body (mandatory from 2024)
5. **Calculate Credits** — Apply rates and caps per activity category
6. **Tax Return Filing (Modello Redditi SC)** — Report credits in annual corporate tax return
7. **Offset Credits** — Use via F24 form against IRES, IRAP, INPS, VAT, and other taxes
8. **Patent Box Election** — Separate election in tax return; irrevocable for 5 years

### Offset Mechanism

Unlike many countries, Italy's R&D credit is offset via the **F24 compensation mechanism**:

| Tax | Offsettable |
|---|---|
| IRES (corporate income tax) | Yes |
| IRAP (regional production tax) | Yes |
| INPS (social contributions) | Yes |
| VAT | Yes |
| Withholding taxes | Yes |

Credits are available for offset from the tax period following the year of expenditure.

### Documentation Requirements

| Requirement | Details |
|---|---|
| Technical report per project | Mandatory — describing R&D content, objectives, novelty, and results |
| Certificazione (from 2024) | Certification from accredited body confirming R&D qualification |
| Cost accounting records | Detailed allocation of costs to qualifying activities |
| Personnel time sheets | Hours allocated to R&D vs. other activities |
| Subcontracting agreements | Contracts with research providers |
| IP documentation (Patent Box) | Evidence of qualifying IP assets and nexus calculation |
| Statutory auditor certification | Confirmation of expenditure amounts (for companies with statutory auditor) |

## 6. Recent Changes & Sunset Provisions

| Year | Change | Impact |
|---|---|---|
| 2020 | L. 160/2019 restructured credits into four categories | Replaced single R&D credit with differentiated rates |
| 2021 | L. 178/2020 increased rates significantly | R&D up to 20%, innovation up to 10%, green/digital up to 15% |
| 2022 | DL 146/2021 replaced patent box with super-deduction | Shift from income-based to cost-based IP regime |
| 2023 | Rates reduced — R&D to 10%, innovation to 5% | Progressive phase-down from 2021–2022 peak rates |
| 2024 | Certificazione becomes mandatory for new claims | Increased compliance burden; greater legal certainty |
| 2024 | Voluntary certification available for 2020–2023 claims | Retroactive safe harbor option |
| 2025 | Innovation and green/digital credits set to expire (check extension) | May be renewed in annual budget law |

### Sunset Provisions

- **R&D credit (10%):** Extended through 2031 under current legislation
- **Innovation credit (5%):** Currently authorized through 2025; renewal expected but not guaranteed
- **Green/digital innovation credit (10%):** Through 2025; renewal under discussion
- **Design credit (5%):** Through 2025
- **Patent Box super-deduction:** No sunset date — permanent feature
- Annual budget laws (Legge di Bilancio) regularly modify rates and caps

### Upcoming Considerations

- Potential further reduction of R&D credit rate after 2025
- EU Minimum Tax Directive (Pillar Two) implementation may affect credit utilization
- Enhanced audit activity by Agenzia delle Entrate on historical R&D credit claims (2015–2019 period)
- Ongoing "voluntary regularization" program for potentially incorrect past claims
