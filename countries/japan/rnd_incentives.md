# Japan - R&D Tax Incentives

## 1. Overview

| Feature | Details |
|---|---|
| **Primary Incentive** | R&D Tax Credit (volume-based) 2-14% of qualifying R&D expenditure |
| **Open Innovation Credit** | 20% for collaborative R&D with universities/startups |
| **SME Enhanced Credit** | 12% base rate for SMEs |
| **Tax Credit Cap** | 25% of corporate tax liability (30% for high-growth companies) |
| **Special Credits** | DX (Digital Transformation) and GX (Green Transformation) credits |
| **Carry-forward** | 1 year |
| **Governing Authority** | National Tax Agency (NTA); Ministry of Economy, Trade and Industry (METI) |
| **Key Legislation** | Special Taxation and Collections Law (SCTL) Art. 42-4; Corporation Tax Act |
| **Corporate Tax Rate** | ~30.62% effective (national 23.2% + local taxes) |

## 2. Detailed Programs

### 2.1 Volume-Based R&D Tax Credit - SCTL Art. 42-4

**Legal Basis:** Special Taxation and Collections Law (Sozei Tokubetsu Sochi Ho) Article 42-4

The volume-based credit is the primary R&D incentive, providing a tax credit based on a percentage of total R&D expenditure. The credit rate varies based on the company's R&D ratio (R&D expenditure / revenue).

**Credit Rate Formula (Large Companies):**

| R&D Ratio | Credit Rate |
|---|---|
| **Below 0%** (decline) | 2% (floor) |
| **0% - 10%** | 2% + (R&D ratio - 0%) × 0.12 |
| **Above 10%** | Up to 14% (ceiling) |

*The rate adjusts based on the increase/decrease ratio of R&D expenditure compared to the average of the prior 3 years.*

**Enhanced Rate for Increased R&D:**

| Increase Ratio | Additional Benefit |
|---|---|
| **> 4% year-on-year increase** | Credit rate enhanced by up to additional percentage points |
| **> 2% increase** | Partial enhancement |
| **Decrease** | Rate reduced toward floor |

### 2.2 SME R&D Tax Credit

**Legal Basis:** SCTL Art. 42-4 (SME provisions)

| Parameter | Details |
|---|---|
| **Base Credit Rate** | 12% of qualifying R&D expenditure |
| **Enhanced Rate** | Up to 17% for SMEs with increased R&D spending |
| **Definition of SME** | Capitalization ≤ ¥100M |
| **Tax Credit Cap** | 25% of corporate tax liability |

### 2.3 Open Innovation Tax Credit - SCTL Art. 42-4-2

**Legal Basis:** SCTL Article 42-4-2

| Parameter | Details |
|---|---|
| **Credit Rate** | 20% of qualifying collaborative R&D expenditure |
| **Partners** | Universities, national research institutions, startups |
| **Startup Collaboration** | Enhanced 25% credit for R&D with certified startups |
| **Minimum Threshold** | Contract R&D payments of ¥300K or more per partner |
| **Cap** | Combined with volume credit, subject to overall cap |

### 2.4 DX (Digital Transformation) Investment Credit

**Legal Basis:** SCTL (DX Investment Promotion Tax System)

| Parameter | Details |
|---|---|
| **Credit Rate** | 3% or 5% of qualifying DX investment |
| **Alternative** | 30% special depreciation |
| **Qualifying Investment** | Cloud computing, AI, IoT, data analytics systems |
| **Cap** | Included within overall R&D credit cap |
| **Validity** | Extended through FY2026 |

### 2.5 GX (Green Transformation) Investment Credit

**Legal Basis:** GX Promotion Act; SCTL provisions

| Parameter | Details |
|---|---|
| **Credit Rate** | Up to 10% for qualifying GX investments |
| **Scope** | Carbon neutral technologies, renewable energy, hydrogen |
| **Period** | FY2024-FY2033 (10-year framework) |
| **Budget** | ¥20 trillion GX transition bonds to support investment |

### 2.6 Tax Credit Cap and Carry-Forward

| Parameter | Details |
|---|---|
| **Standard Cap** | 25% of corporate tax liability |
| **High-Growth Cap** | 30% of corporate tax liability (R&D increase > 4%) |
| **Carry-Forward** | 1 year (unused credits from current year) |
| **Stacking** | Volume + Open Innovation credits share the same cap |

## 3. Patent Box / IP Regime

Japan does not have a formal patent box regime.

| Aspect | Status |
|---|---|
| **Patent Box** | Not available |
| **IP Amortization** | Patents: 8 years; Software: 3-5 years; Know-how: 5 years |
| **IP Transfer** | Subject to transfer pricing rules (SCTL Art. 66-4) |
| **Royalty WHT** | 20.42% (reduced under tax treaties) |
| **Innovation Box Discussion** | Under consideration; METI has studied introduction |
| **Alternative** | R&D tax credit system effectively incentivizes IP creation |

**Note:** Japan relies on its generous R&D tax credit system rather than a patent box to incentivize innovation. The absence of a patent box is partially offset by the high credit rates and broad qualifying expenditure base.

## 4. Effective Benefit Analysis (per $1M USD R&D Spend)

*Assuming JPY/USD exchange rate of 150; ¥150M equivalent spend; effective CIT rate 30.62%*

| Scenario | Credit Rate | Tax Credit (JPY) | Tax Credit (USD) | Effective Subsidy Rate |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Large (low R&D ratio)** | 2% | ¥3.0M | ~$20,000 | 2.0% |
| **Large (moderate ratio)** | 8% | ¥12.0M | ~$80,000 | 8.0% |
| **Large (high ratio)** | 14% | ¥21.0M | ~$140,000 | 14.0% |
| **SME (base)** | 12% | ¥18.0M | ~$120,000 | 12.0% |
| **SME (enhanced)** | 17% | ¥25.5M | ~$170,000 | 17.0% |
| **Open Innovation** | 20% | ¥30.0M | ~$200,000 | 20.0% |

**Cap Impact:** At 30.62% effective rate on ¥150M income, tax liability would be ~¥45.9M. The 25% cap means maximum credit is ~¥11.5M regardless of calculated credit amount. Companies need sufficient taxable income to fully utilize credits.

**Key Insight:** The sliding scale based on R&D ratio rewards companies that invest heavily in R&D relative to revenue. The open innovation credit at 20% is among the highest collaborative R&D incentives globally.

## 5. Claim Process & Documentation

### 5.1 Filing Process

| Step | Action | Timeline |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Identify and categorize qualifying R&D expenditure | During fiscal year |
| 2 | Calculate R&D ratio and determine applicable credit rate | Year-end |
| 3 | Complete Schedule 6(1) (R&D Tax Credit form) | With corporate tax return |
| 4 | File corporate tax return with NTA | Within 2 months after fiscal year-end |
| 5 | Extension available | Up to 1 additional month with application |

### 5.2 Documentation Requirements

- **R&D project descriptions:** Objectives, methodology, and technical uncertainties
- **Expenditure records:** Itemized R&D costs by project
- **Personnel records:** Researcher time allocation and qualifications
- **R&D ratio calculation:** Supporting workpapers for credit rate determination
- **Open innovation records:** Contracts with universities/research partners; payment evidence
- **Prior year comparatives:** 3-year R&D expenditure history for ratio calculation

### 5.3 Qualifying Expenditure Categories

| Category | Examples |
|---|---|
| **Labor costs** | Salaries of dedicated R&D personnel |
| **Raw materials** | Consumables used in experimentation |
| **Depreciation** | R&D equipment and facilities |
| **Contract research** | Payments to external research providers |
| **Patent costs** | Application and maintenance fees for R&D-generated IP |
| **Design costs** | Product/process design directly related to R&D |

## 6. Recent Changes & Sunset Provisions

| Change | Effective Date | Details |
|---|---|---|
| **Credit Rate Enhancement** | FY2023 | Increased ceiling from 10% to 14% for large companies |
| **High-Growth Cap Increase** | FY2023 | Cap raised from 25% to 30% for companies with R&D growth > 4% |
| **Open Innovation Expansion** | FY2023 | Startup collaboration credit increased to 25% |
| **DX Credit Extension** | FY2024 | Extended through FY2026 |
| **GX Credit Introduction** | FY2024 | 10-year framework for green transformation |
| **1-Year Carry-Forward** | FY2023 | Introduction of limited carry-forward (previously no carry-forward) |

**Sunset Provisions:**
- Volume-based credit: Permanent feature, but rates are reviewed in annual tax reforms
- Open innovation credit: Subject to periodic review and extension
- DX credit: Current authorization through FY2026
- GX credit: 10-year authorization through FY2033
- Annual tax reform (Zeisei Kaisei) by ruling coalition determines extensions
