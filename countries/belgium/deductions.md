# Belgium - Taxable Income Deductions

> **Jurisdiction**: Federal (FOD Financiën / SPF Finances)
> **Governing Law**: Wetboek van de Inkomstenbelastingen 1992 (WIB 92) / Code des Impôts sur les Revenus 1992 (CIR 92)
> **Tax Year**: 2024/2025
> **Currency**: EUR (€)

---

## 1. Depreciation & Capital Allowances

### General Principles — Art. 61–64 WIB 92

Depreciation is allowed on tangible and intangible fixed assets using either straight-line or declining balance methods. Rates must correspond to the economic useful life of the asset.

### Depreciation Methods

| Method | Availability | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Straight-line (lineair) | Default; available for all assets | Art. 61 WIB 92 |
| Declining balance (degressief) | Available for tangible assets acquired new (not buildings); rate = 2× straight-line rate, max 40% | Art. 64 WIB 92 |

### Key Depreciation Rates

| Asset Category | Typical Rate (Straight-Line) | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Industrial buildings | **3–5%** | Art. 61 WIB 92 |
| Commercial buildings | **3%** | |
| Machinery and equipment | **10–20%** | |
| Vehicles | **20–25%** | |
| Computer equipment | **33%** | |
| Office furniture | **10%** | |
| Software | **33%** | |
| Goodwill | **Varies** — typically over contractual or useful life | |
| Patents and licences | Over contractual life or max **5 years** for indefinite-life | |

### SME First-Year Depreciation

| Parameter | Value |
|---|---|
| Eligible | SMEs (small companies under Art. 15 Companies Code) |
| Benefit | From tax year 2024, **SMEs are no longer required** to apply the pro rata temporis (half-year) rule in the first year — full-year depreciation allowed for assets acquired during the year |
| Large companies | Must apply pro rata temporis first-year rule |

## 2. Business Operating Expenses

### General Deductibility — Art. 49 WIB 92

Expenses are deductible if they are incurred or borne during the taxable period in order to acquire or maintain taxable income, and are justified by documentation.

### Key Deductible Operating Expenses

| Expense Category | Deductibility | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Employee salaries and wages | Fully deductible | Must be arm's-length for related parties |
| Employer social security contributions (ONSS/RSZ) | Fully deductible | |
| Rent and lease payments | Fully deductible | Arm's-length for related parties |
| Professional fees | Fully deductible | |
| Repairs and maintenance | Deductible if revenue nature | |
| Bad debts | Deductible when specifically provided for and probable | Art. 48 WIB 92 |
| Insurance premiums | Fully deductible | |
| Training expenses | Fully deductible | |

### Restaurant and Hospitality Expenses — Art. 53 WIB 92

| Expense Type | Deductible Portion |
|---|---|
| Restaurant meals (business) | **69%** deductible |
| Gifts to clients | **50%** deductible |
| Entertainment / reception costs | **50%** deductible |
| Company events for employees (max 2/year) | **100%** deductible |

### Charitable Donations — Art. 14 §1 5° WIB 92

| Parameter | Value |
|---|---|
| Deduction | Donations to approved institutions |
| Cap | **5%** of net taxable income; maximum **€500,000** per year |
| Minimum donation | **€40** |

## 3. Interest & Financing Costs

### ATAD Interest Limitation — Art. 198/1 WIB 92 (from 2019)

| Parameter | Value |
|---|---|
| Deductible limit | Net interest expense (exceeding interest income) up to **30%** of tax-EBITDA |
| De minimis | **€3,000,000** net interest expense |
| Carry-forward | Disallowed interest carried forward **indefinitely** |
| EBITDA carry-forward | Unused EBITDA carried forward **5 years** |
| Group | Computed at entity level (not consolidated) |

### Thin Capitalisation — Art. 55 WIB 92 / Art. 198 §1 11° WIB 92

| Parameter | Value |
|---|---|
| Debt/equity ratio | **5:1** thin cap rule for interest paid to certain related-party creditors or tax-haven entities |
| Effect | Interest exceeding arm's-length on excessive portion is reclassified as dividend (non-deductible) |
| Interaction with ATAD | Both rules may apply; the more restrictive prevails |

### 1:1 Rule for Directors' Loans — Art. 18 Abs. 4 WIB 92

| Parameter | Value |
|---|---|
| Scope | Loans from directors/shareholders where interest exceeds market rate or total debt exceeds equity |
| Effect | Excess interest recharacterised as dividend |

## 4. Research & Development

Belgium provides the Innovation Income Deduction (IID — innovatie-inkomensaftrek), allowing an **85%** deduction on net qualifying IP income, resulting in an effective tax rate of approximately **3.75%** on IP income. Additionally, partial exemption from payroll withholding tax applies to qualifying R&D personnel (80% exemption for researchers with qualifying degrees).

See [R&D Incentives](rnd_incentives.md) for full program detail.

## 5. Loss Carry-Forward & Carry-Back

### Loss Carry-Forward — Art. 206 WIB 92

| Parameter | Value |
|---|---|
| Duration | **Indefinite** |
| Restriction (from tax year 2019) | Losses offset freely up to **€1,000,000**; above that, only **70%** of remaining taxable profit |
| Note | The 70% cap was reduced from the previous unlimited offset |

### Loss Carry-Back

| Parameter | Value |
|---|---|
| Status | **Not available** under Belgian tax law (except for a temporary COVID-19 measure that has expired) |

### Change of Control — Art. 207 WIB 92

| Parameter | Value |
|---|---|
| Effect | Losses forfeited if change of control occurs and the change does not meet legitimate financial or economic needs |
| Anti-abuse | Tax authorities may deny losses if change of ownership is tax-motivated |

## 6. Provisions & Reserves

### Tax-Deductible Provisions — Art. 48 WIB 92

| Provision Type | Deductibility | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Specific bad debt provisions | Deductible | Must be individually assessed and probable |
| General bad debt provisions | **Not deductible** | |
| Warranty provisions | Deductible | If based on reliable experience |
| Litigation provisions | Deductible | If probable and estimable |
| Environmental / decommissioning | Deductible | If legal obligation |
| Pension provisions | Generally externalised; employer contributions to external pension funds deductible within limits — Art. 59 WIB 92 |
| Restructuring provisions | Deductible if plan is detailed and announced |

### Tax-Free Reserves

| Reserve | Detail |
|---|---|
| Investment reserve (investeringsreserve) — Art. 194quater WIB 92 | SMEs may set aside a tax-free reserve equal to **50%** of taxable profit (max €37,500) if reinvested within 3 years [unverified — confirm with local counsel] |

## 7. Industry-Specific Deductions

### Investment Deduction (Investeringsaftrek) — Art. 68–77 WIB 92

| Category | Rate (2024) | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| General (SMEs only) | **8%** of qualifying new tangible/intangible assets | Art. 69 WIB 92 |
| Energy-saving investments | **13.5%** | Art. 69 WIB 92 |
| Patents | **13.5%** | |
| R&D environmentally friendly investments | **13.5%** | |
| Reusable packaging | **3%** | |
| Digital investments (SMEs) | **13.5%** | |
| Shipping investments | **30%** | |
| Enhanced deduction for carbon-neutral investments | Up to **20.5%** (from 2025) [unverified — confirm with local counsel] |

### Notional Interest Deduction (NID / Déduction pour Capital à Risque — DCR) — Art. 205bis–205novies WIB 92

| Parameter | Value |
|---|---|
| Status | **Significantly reformed** — from tax year 2019, only the **incremental** equity qualifies |
| Basis | Deduction on the 5-year average increase in qualifying equity |
| Rate (2024) | Published annually; typically very low (~0.2–0.4% for large companies; SMEs receive 0.5% additional) [unverified — confirm with local counsel] |
| Purpose | Reduce tax bias toward debt financing |

### Tonnage Tax — Art. 115–121 WIB 92

| Parameter | Value |
|---|---|
| Method | Lump-sum profit based on net tonnage |
| Eligible | Shipping companies with qualifying vessels |

### Film Tax Shelter — Art. 194ter WIB 92

| Parameter | Value |
|---|---|
| Deduction | Tax shelter for qualifying audiovisual works and performing arts |
| Rate | **421%** of amounts invested (provisional tax exemption — this is the fiscal leverage ratio: a €1 investment yields a provisional CIT-base exemption of ~€4.21, not a 421% cash return) — Art. 194ter WIB 92 |
| Cap | Lesser of €750,000 per work or 50% of taxable profit |

### Diamond Industry — Art. 67ter WIB 92

| Parameter | Value |
|---|---|
| Flat-rate taxation | 0.55% of turnover for qualifying diamond traders |

## 8. Treaties & Transfer Pricing

### Transfer Pricing — Art. 26 / Art. 185 §2 WIB 92

| Parameter | Detail |
|---|---|
| Standard | Arm's-length principle — Art. 185 §2 WIB 92 (OECD aligned) |
| Documentation | Master file and local file required for qualifying entities — Royal Decree of 28 October 2016 |
| Thresholds | Part of MNE group + Belgian entity exceeds one of: revenue €50M, total assets €1B, or ≥100 FTE |
| Country-by-Country Reporting | Required for MNE groups with consolidated revenue ≥ €750M |
| Penalties | Administrative fines up to €25,000 for documentation failures |
| APAs | Bilateral and unilateral available through Service des Décisions Anticipées |

### Participation Exemption (DBI — Definitief Belaste Inkomsten) — Art. 202–204 WIB 92

| Parameter | Value |
|---|---|
| Exemption | **100%** deduction of qualifying dividends received |
| Conditions | Minimum **10%** holding or participation with acquisition value ≥ **€2,500,000**; held ≥ 1 year |
| Subsidiary | Must be subject to normal tax regime (no tax-haven exclusion) |
| Capital gains on shares | **100%** exempt if DBI conditions met — Art. 192 WIB 92 |

### Treaty Network

Belgium has over **95** double taxation agreements.

| Income Type | Typical Treaty Rate |
|---|---|
| Dividends (substantial holding) | 0–5% |
| Dividends (portfolio) | 15% |
| Interest | 0–10% |
| Royalties | 0–10% |

## 9. Non-Deductible Items

| Expense | Statutory Reference |
|---|---|
| Corporate income tax (Ven.B / I.Soc.) | Not deductible — Art. 198 §1 1° WIB 92 |
| Regional surcharges and local taxes | Partially non-deductible |
| Fines and penalties (criminal, administrative) | **Fully non-deductible** — Art. 53 10° WIB 92 |
| Bribes | **Fully non-deductible** — Art. 53 11° WIB 92 |
| 31% of restaurant expenses | Art. 53 8° WIB 92 |
| 50% of entertainment / gifts | Art. 53 8° WIB 92 |
| 50% of client reception costs | Art. 53 8° WIB 92 |
| Clothing (non-specific work clothing) | Art. 53 7° WIB 92 |
| Excessive remuneration (above arm's-length) | Reclassified / non-deductible |
| Hidden profit distributions (abnormale voordelen) | Non-deductible — Art. 26 WIB 92 |
| Non-documented commissions / fees (fiches 281.50) | **100% non-deductible** + additional 100% "secret commissions" tax (50% from 2020 [unverified — confirm with local counsel]) if form 281.50 not filed — Art. 219 WIB 92 |
| Capital expenditure | Must be depreciated |
| Car expenses exceeding CO₂-based deduction limits | Deductibility ranges from 50%–100% based on CO₂ emissions; electric vehicles 100% (transitioning from 2026) — Art. 66 WIB 92 |
