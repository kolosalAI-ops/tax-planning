# Belgium - R&D Tax Incentives

## 1. Overview

| Feature | Details |
|---|---|
| **Headline Incentive** | Partial Withholding Tax (WHT) Exemption for R&D Researchers — 80% |
| **Type** | Multiple: WHT exemption, investment deduction, tax credit, Innovation Income Deduction |
| **Rate** | 80% WHT exemption on researcher salaries; 13.5% one-off / 20.5% spread investment deduction; IID 85% |
| **Refundable** | Partial — WHT exemption is immediate cash flow benefit; tax credit alternative is partly refundable |
| **Patent Box** | Yes — Innovation Income Deduction (IID) at 85% deduction of qualifying IP income |
| **Legal Basis** | CIR/WIB 1992 Art. 275/3 (WHT exemption); CIR/WIB Art. 68–77 (investment deduction); CIR/WIB Art. 205/1–205/4 (IID) |

## 2. Detailed Programs

### 2.1 Partial Withholding Tax Exemption for R&D Researchers

**Legal Basis:** CIR/WIB 1992 Art. 275/3, introduced 2003, progressively expanded.

This is Belgium's most significant R&D incentive in terms of cash flow impact.

| Parameter | Value |
|---|---|
| Exemption Rate | 80% of professional withholding tax (précompte professionnel / bedrijfsvoorheffing) on qualifying researcher salaries |
| Mechanism | Employer withholds full salary tax from employee, but retains 80% instead of remitting to tax authorities |
| Cash Flow Benefit | Immediate; reduces effective employer cost of R&D personnel |
| Eligible Employers | Companies, universities, research institutions, and recognized scientific institutions |
| Eligible Employees | Researchers with qualifying diploma (Master's or PhD in specified fields) engaged in R&D programs |
| Qualifying Diplomas | Master's in exact/applied sciences, medicine, veterinary science, pharmaceutical sciences, applied biological sciences, civil engineering, biotechnology, etc. |
| Bachelor Diploma Extension | 80% exemption also applies to certain Bachelor-level researchers (since 2018) involved in R&D programs |
| PhD Holders | PhD holders benefit from 80% exemption even if working in broader innovation roles |
| Young Innovative Companies | Additional category; see Section 2.5 |

**Qualifying Activities:**

| Category | Eligible |
|---|---|
| Fundamental research | Yes — systematic study to acquire new scientific knowledge |
| Industrial/applied research | Yes — planned research to acquire knowledge for new products/processes |
| Experimental development | Yes — combining existing knowledge to produce new/improved products/processes |
| R&D programs | Must be formally documented and notified to BELSPO (Belgian Science Policy Office) |
| BELSPO Notification | Mandatory registration of R&D programs/projects with BELSPO |

### 2.2 Investment Deduction for R&D

**Legal Basis:** CIR/WIB 1992 Art. 68–77; Royal Decree on qualifying R&D investments.

| Parameter | One-Off Deduction | Spread Deduction |
|---|---|---|
| **Rate** | 13.5% of qualifying investment | 20.5% of annual depreciation charges |
| **Base** | Acquisition value of R&D fixed assets | Annual depreciation amount |
| **Duration** | Applied in year of investment | Applied over depreciation period |
| **Qualifying Assets** | Tangible fixed assets used for R&D; patents; environmentally friendly R&D investments | Same |
| **Conditions** | Assets must be used exclusively for R&D in Belgium; BELSPO attestation required for R&D nature | Same |
| **Carry-Forward** | Unused deduction carried forward indefinitely | Unused deduction carried forward indefinitely |

### 2.3 R&D Tax Credit (Alternative to Investment Deduction)

**Legal Basis:** CIR/WIB 1992 Art. 289bis and Art. 292bis.

| Parameter | Value |
|---|---|
| Alternative To | Investment deduction (taxpayer elects one or the other, not both) |
| One-Off Credit Rate | ~13.5% × qualifying investment amount, converted to credit at corporate tax rate |
| Spread Credit Rate | ~20.5% × annual depreciation, converted to credit |
| Refundable Element | If credit not fully used within 4 assessment years, the balance is refundable in the 5th year |
| Advantage over Deduction | Refundability; useful for loss-making or low-profit R&D companies |
| Election | Irrevocable for each investment; must choose between deduction and credit |

### 2.4 Innovation Income Deduction (IID)

**Legal Basis:** CIR/WIB 1992 Art. 205/1–205/4, introduced by Loi du 9 février 2017, effective from assessment year 2017.

| Parameter | Value |
|---|---|
| Deduction Rate | 85% of net qualifying IP income deducted from taxable base |
| Effective Tax Rate | ~3.75% on qualifying IP income (25% × 15% remaining income) |
| Qualifying IP | Patents, supplementary protection certificates, plant breeder's rights, orphan drug designations, copyrighted software (data protection or marketing exclusivity from regulatory authorities) |
| Nexus Fraction | OECD BEPS Action 5 compliant; qualifying expenditure / overall expenditure (with 30% uplift) |
| Net Income Calculation | Gross IP income minus directly linked IP costs minus nexus-adjusted amount |
| Carry-Forward | Unused IID can be carried forward indefinitely |
| Interaction with WHT Exemption | Both can apply simultaneously; WHT exemption on researchers + IID on resulting IP income |
| Self-Development Requirement | IP must be (at least partly) self-developed |

**IID Income Categories:**

| Income Type | Eligible |
|---|---|
| License fees and royalties | Yes |
| Embedded IP income (product sales) | Yes — portion attributable to qualifying IP |
| IP damages and compensation | Yes |
| Process IP savings | Yes — notional income from cost savings due to qualifying IP |
| Capital gains on IP | Not under IID; separate provisions may apply |

### 2.5 Young Innovative Company (YIC) Status

**Legal Basis:** CIR/WIB 1992 Art. 275/3 §1, 4°; Programme Law of 10 February 2015.

| Parameter | Value |
|---|---|
| WHT Exemption | 80% for qualifying researchers (same as general R&D exemption) |
| Eligibility — Age | Company less than 10 years old |
| Eligibility — Size | SME (EU definition) |
| Eligibility — R&D Intensity | R&D expenditure ≥15% of total operating costs |
| Eligibility — Not from Restructuring | Must not have been formed through a restructuring of existing businesses |
| Benefit | WHT exemption applies to all R&D personnel (broader application); simplified BELSPO requirements |

## 3. Patent Box / IP Regime

See **Section 2.4 — Innovation Income Deduction (IID)** above for full details.

**Key Highlights:**

| Feature | IID |
|---|---|
| Effective Rate | ~3.75% (one of the lowest in Europe) |
| Standard CIT Rate | 25% |
| Tax Saving | ~21.25 percentage points |
| Nexus Compliant | Yes |
| Scope of IP | Broad — includes software copyright (with regulatory exclusivity) |
| Self-Development | Required |
| Process IP | Eligible (notional income from cost savings) |
| Interaction with Other Incentives | Combinable with WHT exemption and investment deduction |

## 4. Effective Benefit Analysis (per $1M USD R&D Spend)

*Assumes $1M USD ≈ €920,000 (illustrative rate); standard CIT rate 25%.*

| Scenario | Calculation | Net Benefit |
|---|---|---|
| **WHT Exemption (80% on researchers)** | Assume 70% of spend is personnel = €644,000 wages; avg WHT ~40% = €257,600; 80% exemption = €206,080 retained | **€206,080** |
| **Investment Deduction (one-off 13.5%)** | On €200,000 R&D equipment: 13.5% × €200,000 = €27,000 deduction × 25% CIT = €6,750 tax saving | **€6,750** |
| **Investment Deduction (spread 20.5%)** | 20.5% × annual depreciation; higher total over asset life | Higher over time |
| **R&D Tax Credit (refundable alternative)** | Similar to investment deduction but refundable after 5 years | Valuable for losses |
| **IID (on €920k IP income)** | 85% × €920,000 = €782,000 deducted; tax on remaining €138,000 = €34,500 | **€195,500 saving** vs. full tax |
| **Combined WHT + IID** | €206,080 + €195,500 | **€401,580** |
| **Full stack (all applicable)** | WHT + Investment Deduction + IID | **~€410,000+** |

> **Note:** Belgium's combination of WHT exemption (immediate cash flow) + IID (profit-phase benefit) creates one of Europe's most attractive R&D tax environments when all incentives are stacked.

## 5. Claim Process & Documentation

### 5.1 WHT Exemption Process

| Step | Action | Details |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | BELSPO Notification | Register R&D programs/projects with Belgian Science Policy Office |
| 2 | Employee Qualification | Verify qualifying diplomas (Master's/PhD in eligible fields) |
| 3 | Monthly Payroll | Withhold full salary tax from employee; retain 80% as employer benefit |
| 4 | WHT Return (Précompte Professionnel) | Declare exemption on monthly/quarterly WHT return (Code 33 / specific declaration codes) |
| 5 | Annual Reconciliation | Ensure consistent application; audit trail maintained |
| 6 | Documentation | Maintain employee CVs, diplomas, R&D project descriptions, time allocation records |

### 5.2 Investment Deduction / Tax Credit Process

| Step | Action |
|---|---|
| 1 | Identify qualifying R&D fixed asset investments |
| 2 | Obtain BELSPO attestation confirming R&D nature of investment |
| 3 | Elect between investment deduction and tax credit (irrevocable per investment) |
| 4 | Claim on corporate tax return (Form 275U) |
| 5 | Maintain asset registers, depreciation schedules, BELSPO certificates |

### 5.3 IID Process

| Step | Action |
|---|---|
| 1 | Identify qualifying IP assets (patents, software, etc.) |
| 2 | Calculate net qualifying IP income per asset |
| 3 | Compute nexus fraction per asset |
| 4 | Apply 85% deduction to nexus-adjusted net IP income |
| 5 | Claim on corporate tax return |
| 6 | Maintain transfer pricing documentation, nexus calculations, IP registers |

### 5.4 Documentation & Compliance

| Document | Requirement |
|---|---|
| BELSPO registration | Mandatory for WHT exemption and investment deduction |
| Employee diplomas | Must be on file for all researchers claiming WHT exemption |
| R&D project descriptions | Technical documentation of R&D nature, objectives, uncertainties |
| Time allocation records | Hours per employee on R&D vs. non-R&D activities |
| Nexus fraction workpapers | Per-asset tracking for IID |
| Transfer pricing documentation | Arm's length IP income allocation |
| Asset registers | For investment deduction claims |
| Retention | 7 years minimum |

## 6. Recent Changes & Sunset Provisions

| Change | Effective Date | Details |
|---|---|---|
| WHT exemption increased to 80% | 2020 | Up from 40% (2003), 65% (2006), 75% (2013), 80% (2020) |
| Bachelor-level researchers included | 2018 | Expanded eligibility beyond Master's/PhD |
| IID replaced former Patent Income Deduction | 2016 (assessment year 2017) | OECD BEPS Action 5 compliance; broader scope |
| CIT rate reduced to 25% | 2020 | Down from 33.99%; increases relative value of deductions |
| SME reduced CIT rate 20% | 2020 | On first €100,000 profit; enhances R&D benefits for SMEs |
| BELSPO registration requirements tightened | Ongoing | More detailed project descriptions required |
| Digital payroll reporting | Ongoing | Enhanced digital reporting for WHT exemption claims |

**Sunset Provisions:**
- **WHT exemption (Art. 275/3)** is permanent legislation; no sunset clause
- **Investment deduction** is permanent; rates adjusted periodically
- **IID** is permanent; OECD-compliant
- Belgium is committed to maintaining its R&D incentive framework
- Rates may be adjusted in annual budget laws
