# India - Taxable Income Deductions

> **Jurisdiction**: National (Income Tax Department — ITD)
> **Governing Law**: Income Tax Act 1961 (ITA 1961); Income Tax Rules 1962
> **Tax Year**: 2024/2025 (AY 2025-26)
> **Currency**: INR (₹)

---

## 1. Depreciation & Capital Allowances

### 1.1 General Depreciation (§32 ITA 1961)

> **Legal Basis**: §32(1) ITA 1961; Income Tax Rules 1962, Appendix I

Depreciation is computed on the **Written Down Value (WDV)** of each block of assets. Assets are grouped into blocks by prescribed depreciation rates.

#### Key Depreciation Rates (Appendix I, Income Tax Rules 1962)

| Block / Asset Category | WDV Rate | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| **Buildings (residential)** | **5%** | |
| **Buildings (non-residential / factory)** | **10%** | |
| **Buildings (temporary / purely temporary)** | **40%** / **100%** | Temporary wooden structures |
| **Furniture and fittings** | **10%** | |
| **Plant and machinery (general)** | **15%** | Default rate |
| **Plant and machinery (motor vehicles)** | **15%** | Excluding those used in hire business |
| **Plant and machinery (motor vehicles for hire)** | **30%** | |
| **Plant and machinery (specified)** | **30%** | Certain manufacturing equipment |
| **Computers and computer software** | **40%** | Including peripherals |
| **Ships and vessels** | **20%** | |
| **Aircraft (aeroengines)** | **40%** | |
| **Intangible assets** | **25%** | Know-how, patents, copyrights, trademarks, licences, franchises |
| **Renewable energy devices** | **40%** | Solar, wind, biogas equipment |

#### Half-Year Convention

| Parameter | Value |
|---|---|
| Asset put to use > 180 days in the year | Full rate depreciation |
| Asset put to use ≤ 180 days (after 3 October) | **50%** of applicable rate |

### 1.2 Additional Depreciation

> **Legal Basis**: §32(1)(iia) ITA 1961

| Parameter | Value |
|---|---|
| Rate | **20%** additional depreciation on new plant and machinery |
| Eligible | Manufacturing or power generation businesses |
| Exclusions | Ships, aircraft, second-hand assets, office equipment |
| Half-year rule | If used < 180 days, 50% of additional depreciation; balance in next year |

### 1.3 New Tax Regime (§115BAC)

> **Legal Basis**: §115BAC ITA 1961 (as amended by Finance Act 2023)

Under the new tax regime applicable from AY 2024-25 (default regime for individuals; optional for companies under §115BAA/§115BAB), certain deductions including additional depreciation under §32(1)(iia) are not available. Standard depreciation under §32(1)(ii) continues to apply.

## 2. Business Operating Expenses

### 2.1 General Deductibility

> **Legal Basis**: §37(1) ITA 1961

Any expenditure (not being of a capital nature or personal expenditure) laid out wholly and exclusively for the purposes of the business or profession is deductible under the residual §37(1).

| Expense Category | Deductibility | Legal Basis |
|---|---|---|
| Salaries, wages, bonuses | **100%** | §36(1)(ii) |
| Employer contribution to PF | **100%** (within limits) | §36(1)(iv); subject to §43B |
| Employer contribution to approved superannuation fund | Up to **₹1.5 lakh** per employee | §36(1)(iv-a) |
| Employer contribution to gratuity fund | **100%** (approved fund) | §36(1)(v); subject to §43B |
| Insurance premiums (business) | **100%** | §37(1) |
| Rent and lease payments | **100%** | §37(1); TDS under §194-I |
| Repairs and maintenance | **100%** | Revenue nature — §37(1) |
| Legal and professional fees | **100%** | §37(1); TDS under §194J |
| Advertising and marketing | **100%** | §37(1) |
| Travel expenses (business) | **100%** | §37(1) |
| Bad debts written off | **100%** | §36(1)(vii); must have been credited to income previously — §36(2) |
| STT paid on transactions | **100%** | §36(1)(xv) — where income from such transactions is treated as business income |

### 2.2 Payments Deductible Only on Actual Payment (§43B)

> **Legal Basis**: §43B ITA 1961

Certain liabilities are deductible only in the year of actual payment (not accrual):

| Item | Reference |
|---|---|
| Any sum payable as tax, duty, cess, or fee | §43B(a) |
| Employer contribution to PF, superannuation, gratuity | §43B(b) |
| Bonus or commission to employees | §43B(c) |
| Interest on loans from scheduled banks / public financial institutions | §43B(d) |
| Leave encashment to employees | §43B(f) |

> Exception: Payment made before the due date of filing the return (§139(1)) is allowed as deduction in the year of accrual.

### 2.3 Charitable Donations

> **Legal Basis**: §80G ITA 1961

Companies under the old tax regime may claim deductions under §80G for donations to approved funds/institutions. The deduction ranges from 50%–100% of the donation, subject to a cap of 10% of adjusted gross total income for certain categories. Under the new regime (§115BAA/BAB), §80G deductions are **not available**.

### 2.4 CSR Expenditure

> **Legal Basis**: §37(1), proviso (inserted by Finance Act 2014)

| Parameter | Value |
|---|---|
| CSR expenditure | **Not deductible** from AY 2015-16 onward |
| Rationale | CSR is a Companies Act obligation, not a business expense |

## 3. Interest & Financing Costs

### 3.1 General Deductibility

> **Legal Basis**: §36(1)(iii) ITA 1961

| Parameter | Value |
|---|---|
| Interest on borrowed capital | Deductible if capital is borrowed for the purposes of the business |
| Pre-commencement interest | Deductible in 5 equal instalments beginning in the year business commences |
| Capital asset acquisition | Interest capitalised until asset is put to use; deductible as depreciation thereafter |

### 3.2 Thin Capitalisation (§94B)

> **Legal Basis**: §94B ITA 1961 (effective from AY 2018-19)

| Parameter | Value |
|---|---|
| Limitation | Interest paid to associated enterprises (AE) limited to **30%** of EBITDA |
| De minimis | **₹1 crore** (₹10 million) — interest below this threshold exempt from the rule |
| Carry-forward of disallowed interest | **8 assessment years** |
| Excluded entities | Banking and insurance companies |
| EBITDA definition | Profit before interest, tax, depreciation, and amortisation per books of account |
| Application | Applies to interest paid/payable to an AE (as defined in §92A) or to a third party where the AE provides an implicit/explicit guarantee |

### 3.3 Interest Deduction Limits (§94B vs §36(1)(iii))

Both provisions may apply simultaneously. §36(1)(iii) provides the general deductibility rule; §94B adds an additional cap for related-party interest.

## 4. Research & Development

India offers several R&D incentives. Under §35(1)(i), revenue R&D expenditure is 100% deductible. Under §35(1)(iv), capital R&D expenditure is 100% deductible. The erstwhile 150%/200% weighted deduction under §35(2AB) for in-house R&D facilities was reduced to 100% from AY 2021-22. Contributions to approved scientific research associations under §35(1)(ii) qualify for 100% deduction.

See [R&D Incentives](rnd_incentives.md) for full program detail.

## 5. Loss Carry-Forward & Carry-Back

### 5.1 Business Losses

> **Legal Basis**: §72 ITA 1961

| Parameter | Value |
|---|---|
| Carry-forward period | **8 assessment years** |
| Carry-back | **Not available** |
| Set-off | Against income from business or profession only (non-speculative losses) |
| Filing requirement | Return must be filed within due date under §139(1) to carry forward |

### 5.2 Unabsorbed Depreciation

> **Legal Basis**: §32(2) ITA 1961

| Parameter | Value |
|---|---|
| Carry-forward period | **Indefinite** (no time limit) |
| Set-off | Against any head of income (not limited to business income) |
| Priority | Set off after current year's depreciation; after carried-forward business losses under §72 |

### 5.3 Speculative Business Losses

| Parameter | Value |
|---|---|
| Carry-forward | **4 assessment years** — §73 |
| Set-off | Only against speculative business income |

### 5.4 Capital Losses

| Parameter | Short-Term | Long-Term |
|---|---|---|
| Carry-forward | **8 years** — §74 | **8 years** — §74 |
| Set-off | Against any capital gain | Against long-term capital gains only |

## 6. Provisions & Reserves

> **Legal Basis**: §36(1)(viia); §36(1)(vii)

| Provision Type | Deductibility | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Specific bad debts written off | **Deductible** | §36(1)(vii); must have been credited to income — §36(2) |
| Provision for doubtful debts (banks) | **Deductible** | Up to 8.5% of total income + 10% of aggregate average advances — §36(1)(viia) |
| Provision for doubtful debts (non-banking) | **Not deductible** | Only actual write-off allowed |
| Employee gratuity fund provision | **Deductible** when funded through approved fund | §36(1)(v); §43B |
| Leave encashment provision | **Not deductible** until paid | §43B(f) |
| Warranty provisions | **Not deductible** | Until crystallised and paid |
| General contingent provisions | **Not deductible** | Must be ascertained liability |

## 7. Industry-Specific Deductions

### 7.1 Special Economic Zones (SEZ)

> **Legal Basis**: §10AA ITA 1961

| Parameter | Value |
|---|---|
| Tax holiday | 100% deduction of export profits for first 5 years; 50% for next 5 years; 50% of ploughed-back profits for next 5 years |
| Status | Available for units commencing operations before 1 April 2020 (sunset); existing units continue to claim |

### 7.2 Infrastructure / Power / Specified Businesses

> **Legal Basis**: §80-IA; §80-IAB; §80-IAC ITA 1961

Various deductions for profits from infrastructure development, power generation, and approved startup businesses. Most provisions have sunset dates and are not available under the new tax regime (§115BAA/BAB).

### 7.3 New Manufacturing Companies

> **Legal Basis**: §115BAB ITA 1961

| Parameter | Value |
|---|---|
| CIT rate | **15%** (effective ~17.16% with surcharge and cess) |
| Eligible | New manufacturing companies incorporated on/after 1 October 2019, commencing manufacturing by 31 March 2024 |
| Trade-off | Cannot claim most deductions (§80-IA, §80-IAB, additional depreciation, etc.) |

### 7.4 Existing Domestic Companies (Optional Regime)

> **Legal Basis**: §115BAA ITA 1961

| Parameter | Value |
|---|---|
| CIT rate | **22%** (effective ~25.17% with surcharge and cess) |
| Trade-off | Cannot claim most incentive deductions; MAT does not apply |

## 8. Treaties & Transfer Pricing

### 8.1 Transfer Pricing

> **Legal Basis**: §92A–92F ITA 1961; Income Tax Rules 10A–10E

| Parameter | Value |
|---|---|
| Arm's length standard | Mandatory for all international transactions with AE — §92(1) |
| Specified domestic transactions | TP rules also apply to certain domestic transactions with related parties (if aggregate > ₹20 crore) — §92BA |
| Methods | CUP, resale price, cost plus, profit split, TNMM, or other prescribed method |
| Documentation | §92D — TP study report (Form 3CEB); maintained and furnished with return |
| Master File | Required if group turnover > ₹500 crore — §92D(4) |
| CbC reporting | Required if consolidated group revenue > ₹5,500 crore — §286 |
| Penalties | §271G — 2% of value of international transaction for non-documentation; §270A for under-reporting |
| APA | Available — unilateral (4 years rollback), bilateral, multilateral — §92CC–92CE |
| Safe Harbour Rules | Specified margins for IT/ITES, KPO, contract R&D, financial transactions — §92CB |

### 8.2 Treaty Network

India has over 95 double tax agreements. India has not signed the MLI but has renegotiated many treaties bilaterally with BEPS-aligned provisions.
