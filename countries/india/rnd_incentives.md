# India - R&D Tax Incentives

## 1. Overview

| Feature | Details |
|---|---|
| **Primary Incentive** | 100% deduction for in-house R&D (§35(1)(iv) and §35(2AB)) |
| **Weighted Deduction** | 200% weighted deduction expired March 31, 2020 |
| **Patent Box** | 10% concessionary rate on patent royalties (§115BBF) |
| **Contributions** | Deductions for contributions to approved research institutions |
| **Governing Authority** | Central Board of Direct Taxes (CBDT); Department of Scientific and Industrial Research (DSIR) |
| **Key Legislation** | Income Tax Act 1961 - §35, §115BBF; Income Tax Rules |
| **Corporate Tax Rate** | 25.17% (new regime); 22% + surcharge (§115BAA); 15% + surcharge for new manufacturing (§115BAB) |

## 2. Detailed Programs

### 2.1 In-House R&D Deduction - §35(2AB)

**Legal Basis:** Income Tax Act 1961, Section 35(2AB)

| Parameter | Details |
|---|---|
| **Current Deduction** | 100% of qualifying R&D expenditure (capital and revenue) |
| **Previous Deduction** | 200% weighted deduction (expired March 31, 2020) |
| **Eligible Entities** | Companies engaged in the business of biotechnology or manufacturing |
| **Approval** | DSIR approval required for in-house R&D facility |
| **Capital Expenditure** | 100% deduction on R&D capital expenditure (excluding land and buildings) |
| **Revenue Expenditure** | 100% deduction on R&D revenue expenditure |

**Historical Weighted Deduction Rates:**

| Period | Weighted Deduction |
|---|---|
| Before April 1, 2017 | 200% |
| April 1, 2017 - March 31, 2020 | 150% (transition) |
| April 1, 2020 onwards | 100% (standard deduction only) |

### 2.2 Contributions to Research Institutions - §35(1)

**Legal Basis:** Income Tax Act 1961, Section 35(1)

| Provision | Deduction | Recipient |
|---|---|---|
| **§35(1)(ii)** | 100% | Approved research associations/universities for scientific research |
| **§35(1)(iia)** | 100% | Approved research associations for social science/statistical research |
| **§35(1)(iii)** | 100% | Approved research associations/universities/institutions for research in social science or statistical research |

### 2.3 Contributions to National Laboratories - §35(2AA)

**Legal Basis:** Income Tax Act 1961, Section 35(2AA)

| Parameter | Details |
|---|---|
| **Current Deduction** | 100% of contributions (weighted deduction of 150% expired April 1, 2020) |
| **Eligible Recipients** | National laboratories, universities, IITs, specified institutions |
| **Approval** | Prescribed authority must approve the recipient institution |
| **Condition** | Contributions must be for approved scientific research programs |

### 2.4 New Manufacturing Company Regime - §115BAB

| Parameter | Details |
|---|---|
| **CIT Rate** | 15% + 10% surcharge + 4% cess = 17.16% effective |
| **Eligibility** | New manufacturing companies incorporated and commencing production by March 31, 2024 |
| **Condition** | Cannot claim §35(2AB) weighted deduction under this regime |
| **R&D Treatment** | Standard 100% deduction available; no additional weighted benefit |

### 2.5 Concessional Tax Regime - §115BAA

| Parameter | Details |
|---|---|
| **CIT Rate** | 22% + 10% surcharge + 4% cess = 25.17% effective |
| **Key Trade-off** | Must forgo certain deductions and exemptions |
| **R&D Treatment** | Standard deduction available; §35(2AB) weighted deduction not available |

## 3. Patent Box / IP Regime

### Patent Box - §115BBF

**Legal Basis:** Income Tax Act 1961, Section 115BBF (introduced by Finance Act 2016)

| Parameter | Details |
|---|---|
| **Concessionary Rate** | 10% on gross royalty income from patents |
| **Effective Rate** | ~10.92% (including surcharge and cess) vs. 25.17% standard |
| **Qualifying Income** | Royalty income from patents developed and registered in India |
| **Patent Requirement** | Patent must be granted under the Patents Act, 1970 (Indian patent) |
| **Nexus Requirement** | Patent must be developed through in-house R&D (DSIR-approved facility) |
| **Expense Deduction** | No deduction for any expenditure related to royalty income |
| **Effective Date** | Assessment Year 2017-18 onwards |

**Key Limitations:**
- Only applies to patents registered in India (not foreign patents)
- Royalty must be received by the true and first inventor or their assignee
- No deduction for expenses against the 10% taxed income
- Does not apply to embedded IP income from product sales (only royalties/licensing)

## 4. Effective Benefit Analysis (per $1M USD R&D Spend)

*Assuming INR/USD exchange rate of 83; INR 83M equivalent spend*

| Scenario | Treatment | Tax Saving (INR) | Tax Saving (USD) |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Standard Deduction (25.17% regime)** | 100% × 25.17% | INR 20.89M | ~$251,700 |
| **Standard Deduction (17.16% new mfg)** | 100% × 17.16% | INR 14.24M | ~$171,600 |
| **Historical Weighted (200%, expired)** | 200% × 25.17% | INR 41.78M | ~$503,400 |
| **Patent Box (per $1M royalty)** | 14.25% rate differential | INR 11.83M | ~$142,500 |
| **Contribution to National Lab** | 100% × 25.17% | INR 20.89M | ~$251,700 |

**Key Insight:** The expiration of the weighted deduction in 2020 significantly reduced India's R&D tax competitiveness. The patent box at 10% provides meaningful benefit but is limited to Indian-registered patents and royalty income only.

## 5. Claim Process & Documentation

### 5.1 DSIR Approval Process

| Step | Action | Timeline |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Establish in-house R&D facility | Before claiming deduction |
| 2 | Apply to DSIR for recognition | Form 3CL application |
| 3 | DSIR inspection and evaluation | 3-6 months processing |
| 4 | DSIR issues recognition certificate | Valid for specified period |
| 5 | Annual renewal/reporting to DSIR | Before March 31 each year |

### 5.2 Filing Process

| Step | Action | Timeline |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Obtain DSIR approval for R&D facility | Prior to first claim |
| 2 | File Form 3CLA with DSIR (annual) | Electronically on DSIR portal |
| 3 | Obtain Form 3CL from DSIR (quantification of R&D expenditure) | DSIR issues to CBDT |
| 4 | Claim deduction in income tax return | By due date (generally October 31 for audit cases) |
| 5 | Maintain prescribed records | Minimum 8 years |

### 5.3 Documentation Requirements

- **DSIR recognition certificate:** Valid in-house R&D facility approval
- **Form 3CLA:** Annual return of R&D expenditure filed with DSIR
- **Form 3CL:** DSIR's quantification report sent to Income Tax authorities
- **Project records:** R&D project descriptions, objectives, outcomes
- **Financial records:** Audited accounts with R&D expenditure breakdowns
- **Personnel records:** R&D staff qualifications and time allocation
- **Capital asset register:** R&D equipment and assets
- **Patent documentation:** For §115BBF claims, Indian patent certificates and royalty agreements

### 5.4 Audit Considerations

- Transfer pricing scrutiny on R&D cost-sharing arrangements
- DSIR may audit R&D facility and activities
- Tax authorities may challenge classification of expenditure as R&D
- Thin capitalization rules may apply to R&D funding from related parties

## 6. Recent Changes & Sunset Provisions

| Change | Effective Date | Details |
|---|---|---|
| **Weighted Deduction Expiry** | April 1, 2020 | 200%/150% weighted deduction under §35(2AB) expired |
| **§115BAA Introduction** | FY2019-20 | 22% CIT option; trade-off with R&D deductions |
| **§115BAB Introduction** | FY2019-20 | 15% CIT for new manufacturing; trade-off with R&D deductions |
| **New Manufacturing Deadline** | March 31, 2024 | Extended deadline for §115BAB eligibility |
| **Patent Box Unchanged** | Ongoing | §115BBF remains at 10% with no announced changes |
| **Digital Economy** | 2020 onwards | Equalization levy may impact cross-border R&D structures |

**Sunset Provisions:**
- Weighted deduction (§35(2AB)): Already expired; no indication of reinstatement
- Patent box (§115BBF): No sunset clause; permanent feature
- §115BAB (15% new manufacturing): Incorporated by March 31, 2024 deadline
- Government has signaled preference for lower rates over targeted deductions
- Union Budget 2025-26 may introduce new innovation incentives

**Policy Direction:**
India's approach has shifted from weighted deductions to lower headline CIT rates. The government's stated position is that a simple, low-rate tax system is preferable to sector-specific incentives.
