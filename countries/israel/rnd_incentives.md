# Israel - R&D Tax Incentives

## 1. Overview

| Feature | Details |
|---|---|
| **Primary Incentive** | IIA (Israel Innovation Authority) R&D Grants |
| **Secondary Incentive** | Preferred Technology Enterprise Reduced CIT |
| **Grant Rate** | 20–50% of approved R&D expenditure |
| **Preferred Tech CIT** | 12% standard; 6% in Development Zone A |
| **Special Preferred Tech CIT** | 6% standard; 6% in Zone A (revenue > ILS 10B) |
| **IP Box Effective Rate** | 6–12% on qualifying IP income |
| **Governing Law** | Encouragement of R&D in Industry Law 5744-1984; Capital Investments Law (Amendment 73) |
| **Administering Body** | Israel Innovation Authority (IIA, formerly OCS) |
| **Standard CIT Rate** | 23% |
| **Fiscal Year** | Calendar year (January–December) |

## 2. Detailed Programs

### 2.1 IIA (Innovation Authority) R&D Grants

**Legal Basis:** Encouragement of Research and Development in Industry Law, 5744-1984 (as amended)

The Israel Innovation Authority provides direct, non-dilutive grants covering a percentage of approved R&D expenditures. Unlike tax credits, these are cash grants disbursed during the R&D phase.

| Parameter | Details |
|---|---|
| **Grant Rate — Standard** | 20–30% of approved R&D budget |
| **Grant Rate — Priority Areas** | Up to 40% of approved R&D budget |
| **Grant Rate — Peripheral Regions** | Up to 50% of approved R&D budget |
| **Eligible Costs** | Salaries, subcontractors (up to 30%), materials, equipment, overhead |
| **Repayment Obligation** | Royalties of 3–5% on revenues from products developed with grant funding |
| **IP Restrictions** | IP developed with grant funding must remain in Israel; transfer requires IIA approval and payment of up to 6x grant amount |
| **Application Deadline** | Rolling, but typically reviewed in biannual cycles |
| **Decision Timeline** | 3–6 months from submission |

#### Key IIA Programs

| Program | Target | Grant Rate |
|---|---|---|
| **R&D Fund (Flagship)** | Established companies with R&D in Israel | 20–50% |
| **Tnufa (Incubator Seed)** | Early-stage startups, pre-seed | Up to 85% (max ILS 800K) |
| **Binational Funds (BIRD, CIIRDF)** | Israel + US/Canada/other bilateral R&D | 50% (up to $1M per project) |
| **Magnet/MAGNETON** | Industry-academia consortia | 66% for academic; 50% for industry |
| **Nofar** | Applied academic research transitioning to industry | Up to 90% of academic R&D costs |

### 2.2 Preferred Technology Enterprise (PTE)

**Legal Basis:** Law for the Encouragement of Capital Investments, 5719-1959 — Amendment 73 (effective January 1, 2017)

| Parameter | Standard Zone | Development Zone A |
|---|---|---|
| **CIT on Technology Income** | 12% | 6% |
| **Withholding Tax on Dividends** | 20% | 20% |
| **Minimum R&D Expenditure** | 7% of revenue (3-year average) or annual R&D spend > ILS 75M | Same |
| **Minimum IP Ownership** | Must own qualifying IP (patents, software, know-how) | Same |
| **Minimum Employees** | Not specified, but substance required | Same |
| **Qualifying Income** | Income from IP-embedded products/services | Same |

**Eligibility Criteria for Preferred Technology Enterprise:**

1. Company must be part of a group with consolidated annual revenue ≥ ILS 75 million, OR
2. Company must have consolidated annual R&D expenditure ≥ 7% of total revenue (average of last 3 years)
3. At least one of the following IP conditions:
   - Owns a patent or patentable technology registered in Israel
   - Owns software-based IP
   - Owns qualifying know-how developed in Israel
4. At least 20% of workforce (or 200+ employees) are R&D employees

### 2.3 Special Preferred Technology Enterprise (SPTE)

**Legal Basis:** Amendment 73 to the Capital Investments Law (2017)

| Parameter | Standard Zone | Development Zone A |
|---|---|---|
| **CIT on Technology Income** | 6% | 6% |
| **Withholding Tax on Dividends** | 4% (to foreign parent) | 4% |
| **Revenue Threshold** | Group consolidated revenue > ILS 10 billion | Same |
| **IP Sale Capital Gains** | 6% | 6% |

This tier is designed for large multinational enterprises with significant Israeli operations.

## 3. Patent Box / IP Regime

Israel's IP regime operates through the Preferred Technology Enterprise framework rather than a standalone patent box.

| Feature | Details |
|---|---|
| **Regime Type** | Integrated IP/Technology Enterprise regime |
| **Qualifying IP** | Patents, software IP, know-how developed in Israel |
| **Effective Tax Rate on IP Income** | 6–12% depending on zone and enterprise tier |
| **Capital Gains on IP Disposition** | 6% for SPTE; 12% for PTE (vs. 23% standard) |
| **Nexus Requirement** | IP must be developed substantially in Israel; R&D workforce requirements |
| **Transfer Pricing** | Must comply with OECD guidelines; Section 85A of the Income Tax Ordinance |
| **DEMPE Alignment** | Yes — development, enhancement, maintenance, protection, and exploitation must occur in Israel |
| **Ruling Availability** | Advance rulings available from Israel Tax Authority |

**Capital Gains on IP Sales:**

| Enterprise Tier | Rate on IP Capital Gains |
|---|---|
| Standard Company | 23% CIT |
| Preferred Technology Enterprise | 12% |
| Special Preferred Technology Enterprise | 6% |

## 4. Effective Benefit Analysis (per $1M USD R&D Spend)

### Scenario A: IIA Grant + Standard CIT (23%)

| Component | Amount (USD) |
|---|---|
| R&D Expenditure | $1,000,000 |
| IIA Grant (30% average) | $300,000 |
| Net R&D Cost After Grant | $700,000 |
| Tax Deduction on Net R&D (23%) | $161,000 |
| **Total Benefit** | **$461,000** |
| **Effective R&D Cost** | **$539,000** |
| **Net Benefit Rate** | **46.1%** |

### Scenario B: No Grant + Preferred Technology Enterprise (12% CIT)

| Component | Amount (USD) |
|---|---|
| R&D Expenditure | $1,000,000 |
| Tax Deduction on R&D (12% rate) | $120,000 |
| CIT Savings on Revenue (23% → 12%) | $110,000 per $1M qualifying income |
| **Tax Deduction Benefit** | **$120,000** |
| **Note** | Primary benefit is the reduced ongoing CIT rate on technology income |

### Scenario C: IIA Grant + PTE Zone A (6% CIT)

| Component | Amount (USD) |
|---|---|
| R&D Expenditure | $1,000,000 |
| IIA Grant (40% peripheral region) | $400,000 |
| Net R&D Cost After Grant | $600,000 |
| Tax Deduction on Net R&D (6%) | $36,000 |
| CIT Savings on Revenue (23% → 6%) | $170,000 per $1M qualifying income |
| **Total Direct R&D Benefit** | **$436,000** |
| **Effective R&D Cost** | **$564,000** |

### Comparative Summary

| Scenario | Effective Cost per $1M R&D | Benefit Rate |
|---|---|---|
| IIA Grant + Standard CIT | $539,000 | 46.1% |
| No Grant + PTE (12%) | $880,000 | 12.0% |
| IIA Grant + PTE Zone A (6%) | $564,000 | 43.6% |
| IIA Grant (50%) + SPTE (6%) | $464,000 | 53.6% |

> **Note:** IIA grants and PTE status are not mutually exclusive. A company may receive IIA grants and simultaneously benefit from reduced CIT as a Preferred Technology Enterprise.

## 5. Claim Process & Documentation

### IIA Grant Application

| Step | Action | Timeline |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Register on IIA online portal (innovationisrael.org.il) | Ongoing |
| 2 | Submit detailed R&D program with budget breakdown | Before project start |
| 3 | IIA review committee evaluation | 3–6 months |
| 4 | Approval letter with terms and conditions | Upon approval |
| 5 | Submit periodic progress and financial reports | Semi-annual |
| 6 | Grant disbursement | Upon report approval |
| 7 | Royalty payments commence upon commercialization | Ongoing (3–5% of revenue) |

### PTE/SPTE Application

| Step | Action | Timeline |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Self-assessment of eligibility criteria | Annual |
| 2 | Engage Israeli tax advisor for qualification review | Pre-filing |
| 3 | File annual CIT return claiming PTE/SPTE rates | Annual deadline |
| 4 | Maintain contemporaneous documentation of R&D activities and IP ownership | Ongoing |
| 5 | Optional: request advance ruling from ITA | 3–12 months |

### Required Documentation

- **IIA Grants:** Detailed R&D plan, budget breakdown by cost category, milestone schedule, team qualifications, IP ownership declaration, previous grant history
- **PTE/SPTE:** R&D expenditure calculations, employee headcounts, IP ownership documentation, consolidated group revenue figures, Zone A certification (if applicable), transfer pricing documentation
- **General:** Payroll records for R&D staff, subcontractor agreements, equipment purchase invoices, lab notebooks or development logs

## 6. Recent Changes & Sunset Provisions

| Change | Effective Date | Details |
|---|---|---|
| **Amendment 73 to Capital Investments Law** | January 1, 2017 | Introduced PTE and SPTE tiers; replaced prior Preferred/Benefited Enterprise regimes |
| **IIA Strategic Plan 2022–2027** | 2022 | Increased focus on deep tech, climate tech, and digital health; higher grant rates for these sectors |
| **Pillar Two Impact** | Expected 2026 | Israel monitoring OECD Pillar Two (15% global minimum tax); may reduce effectiveness of 6% SPTE rate for large MNEs |
| **Angel's Law Extension** | Extended through 2025 | Tax deduction for individual investors in qualifying R&D companies (up to ILS 5M investment) |
| **IIA Budget Increase** | 2024 | Annual IIA budget increased to ~ILS 4.5 billion |
| **Zone A Expansion** | Under review | Potential expansion of Development Zone A boundaries |

**Sunset Provisions:**
- PTE/SPTE regimes under Amendment 73 have no explicit sunset date but are subject to legislative amendment
- IIA grants are approved on a per-project basis (typically 1–3 years)
- Angel's Law (Section 20A of Income Tax Ordinance) was most recently extended through December 31, 2025; further extensions pending

**OECD Pillar Two Considerations:**
- Companies within scope (consolidated revenue > EUR 750M) may face a top-up tax reducing the effective benefit of the 6% SPTE rate
- Israel is actively monitoring developments and may introduce a Qualified Domestic Minimum Top-up Tax (QDMTT)
