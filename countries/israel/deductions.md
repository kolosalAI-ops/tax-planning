# Israel - Taxable Income Deductions

> **Jurisdiction**: National (Israel Tax Authority — ITA)
> **Governing Law**: Income Tax Ordinance [New Version] 5721-1961 (ITO)
> **Tax Year**: 2024/2025
> **Currency**: ILS (₪)

---

## 1. Depreciation & Capital Allowances

### General Framework (§21, ITO; Income Tax Regulations — Depreciation Rates — 5746-1985)

Depreciation is permitted on a **straight-line basis** at prescribed rates for assets used in the production of taxable income. Land is not depreciable. Rates are set by regulation and depend on asset type and, in certain cases, the number of shifts the asset is used.

| Asset Category | Annual Depreciation Rate | Legal Basis |
|---|---|---|
| Buildings — residential | **2%** | Depreciation Regulations 5746-1985 |
| Buildings — industrial/commercial | **4%** | Depreciation Regulations 5746-1985 |
| Buildings — temporary structures | **5%–10%** | Depreciation Regulations 5746-1985 |
| Machinery and equipment — general | **7%–15%** | Depreciation Regulations 5746-1985 |
| Machinery — industrial (certified enterprise) | **20%–40%** (accelerated) | Depreciation Regulations; Capital Investment Encouragement Law |
| Motor vehicles | **15%** | Depreciation Regulations 5746-1985 |
| Computers and peripherals | **33%** | Depreciation Regulations 5746-1985 |
| Office furniture | **7%** | Depreciation Regulations 5746-1985 |
| Air conditioners, photocopiers | **15%** | Depreciation Regulations 5746-1985 |
| Intangible assets — general | Per useful life | §21, ITO |
| Goodwill (acquired in a transaction) | Over useful life (typically **10%–20%** per year) | §21, ITO |
| Patents and know-how | Over legal/economic life | §21, ITO |

### Accelerated Depreciation

| Programme | Rate | Conditions | Legal Basis |
|---|---|---|---|
| Industrial enterprises (approved/certified) | **20%–40%** straight-line | Equipment first used in Israel; enterprise approved under Capital Investment Encouragement Law | Capital Investment Encouragement Law 5719-1959 (Innovation Law, as amended) |
| National Priority Areas | Enhanced rates may apply | Assets in designated development areas | ITO + Investment Encouragement Law |
| Shift work | Additional depreciation for multi-shift use | Two or three shifts; rates increase by 50% or 100% respectively | Depreciation Regulations 5746-1985 |

### Pre-Trading Expenditure

Pre-operating expenses are generally amortised over the period to which they relate, not to exceed 5 years from commencement — §17, ITO.

## 2. Business Operating Expenses

### General Deductibility (§17, ITO)

Expenses incurred wholly and exclusively in the production of taxable income during the tax year are deductible. The expense must not be capital in nature.

| Expense Category | Deductibility | Legal Basis |
|---|---|---|
| Salaries and wages | **100%** deductible (arm's-length for related parties) | §17(1), ITO |
| Employee social contributions (Bituach Leumi — National Insurance, pension) | **100%** deductible | §17(5), ITO |
| Rent and utilities | **100%** deductible | §17(1), ITO |
| Professional fees (legal, accounting) | **100%** deductible | §17(1), ITO |
| Insurance premiums (business) | **100%** deductible | §17(1), ITO |
| Repairs and maintenance | **100%** deductible (if not capital improvement) | §17(3), ITO |
| Bad debts | Deductible when actually written off; doubtful debts may be provisioned (per ITA guidance) | §17(4), ITO |
| Travel and accommodation (business) | **100%** deductible (subject to documentation) | §17(1), ITO |
| Vehicle expenses (company car) | Limited deductibility based on value and use | §31, ITO; Income Tax Regulations (Vehicle Benefits) |
| Entertainment and hospitality | **80%** of the expense is **non-deductible** (only **20%** deductible) [unverified — confirm exact percentage with local counsel; some sources cite different limitations based on type] | §32(1a), ITO |
| Gifts | Limited to ILS 220 per recipient per year [unverified — indexed amount] | §32, ITO |
| Charitable donations (approved institutions) | Deductible up to **35%** of taxable income; minimum ILS 190 per donation | §46, ITO |

### Withholding on Payments to Non-Residents

Payments to non-residents are subject to withholding tax, which may affect the timing and deductibility of cross-border expenses. Treaty relief may apply.

## 3. Interest & Financing Costs

### Interest Deduction (§17(1), ITO)

| Parameter | Detail |
|---|---|
| General rule | Interest incurred in the production of taxable income is deductible |
| Personal interest | Not deductible — §32, ITO |
| Capitalised interest | Must be capitalised into the asset cost where required by accounting standards |
| Inflationary adjustment | Israel's tax law includes an inflation adjustment regime (§3, Inflationary Adjustment Law — Income Tax) that may adjust the real cost of interest in periods of significant inflation |

### Thin Capitalisation

| Parameter | Detail |
|---|---|
| Statutory ratio | No formal thin-capitalisation statute exists in Israel |
| Transfer pricing override | The ITA may apply §85A (transfer pricing) to recharacterise excessive related-party debt as equity and disallow interest accordingly |
| Practice | ITA has challenged excessive debt in audit using general anti-avoidance and transfer pricing provisions |

### Controlled Foreign Corporation (CFC) Rules (§75B, ITO)

| Parameter | Detail |
|---|---|
| Application | Israeli residents holding ≥50% (or ≥40% with one Israeli holder controlling ≥10%) of a foreign company in a low-tax jurisdiction (≤15% effective rate) |
| Effect | Undistributed profits attributed to Israeli shareholders; interest deductions of the CFC may be scrutinised |

## 4. Research & Development

Israel provides substantial R&D incentives through two main frameworks: (1) the Capital Investment Encouragement Law 5719-1959 (the "Innovation Law"), which offers preferential corporate tax rates for Preferred and Preferred Technological Enterprises earning income from IP developed in Israel, and (2) the Encouragement of Research, Development and Technological Innovation in Industry Law 5744-1984, which provides grants via the Israel Innovation Authority (IIA) and allows 100% current expensing of qualifying R&D expenditure under §20A ITO. Benefits are conditional on compliance with restrictions on the transfer of know-how and IP developed with IIA support.

See [R&D Incentives](rnd_incentives.md) for full programme details, including Israel Innovation Authority grants and patent box regime.

## 5. Loss Carry-Forward & Carry-Back

### Net Operating Losses (§28, ITO)

| Parameter | Detail |
|---|---|
| Carry-forward | **Indefinite** (no time limit) — §28(a), ITO |
| Utilisation | Losses from a trade or business may offset any income in the same year; carried-forward losses offset business income only |
| Carry-back | **Not permitted** for business losses |
| Capital losses | May be carried forward **indefinitely**; may only offset capital gains — §92, ITO |
| Capital loss carry-back | 2 years carry-back permitted for capital losses only [unverified — confirm whether this remains in effect] — §92, ITO |
| Change of ownership | No automatic forfeiture rule, but ITA may challenge loss utilisation under anti-avoidance provisions |
| Real estate losses | May be ring-fenced under certain circumstances |

### Losses in Approved/Preferred Enterprises

Losses from Preferred Enterprise activities may be offset against other income of the company, but special rules apply to the ordering of losses where different tax rates apply to different income streams.

## 6. Provisions & Reserves

### General Principles (§17, §32, ITO)

| Item | Treatment |
|---|---|
| General provisions | **Not deductible** unless specifically permitted by the ITO |
| Bad debt provisions | Deductible under §17(4) ITO when the debt is proven to be doubtful or bad; ITA may accept systematic provisions per IFRS 9 in certain circumstances |
| Warranty reserves | **Not deductible** until actual expenditure |
| Contingent liabilities | **Not deductible** |
| Employee termination accruals | Deductible when funded to a severance pay fund (Kupat Gemel) — §17(5A), ITO |
| Vacation and recreation pay accruals | Deductible when the liability is certain and quantifiable |

## 7. Industry-Specific Deductions

### High-Tech / Technology Sector

| Parameter | Detail |
|---|---|
| Preferred Technological Enterprise | Reduced 12% (or 7.5%) tax rate on qualifying IP income — §51D, Capital Investment Encouragement Law |
| R&D personnel | R&D wages and related costs fully deductible; may also benefit from IIA grants |
| IP sale | Gain on sale of IP developed in Israel may qualify for reduced rates |
| Recent circular (Nov 2025) | New guidance on attribution of income to R&D centres; safe harbour rules for cost-plus arrangements |

### Real Estate and Construction

| Parameter | Detail |
|---|---|
| Land betterment tax | Separate levy under Land Taxation Law 5723-1963 |
| Building depreciation | 2%–5% per year depending on building type |
| Rental income | Deductions available for expenses related to rental income production |

### Financial Institutions

| Parameter | Detail |
|---|---|
| Bad debt reserves | Banks and financial institutions may claim provisions per central bank/regulatory guidance |
| Interest expense | Fully deductible in the ordinary course of banking business |

### Agriculture

| Parameter | Detail |
|---|---|
| Planting costs | Deductible over the economic life of the plantation |
| Agricultural depreciation | Specific rates for agricultural machinery and structures |

## 8. Treaties & Transfer Pricing

### Transfer Pricing (§85A, ITO; Income Tax Regulations — Arm's Length Conditions — 5767-2006)

| Parameter | Detail |
|---|---|
| Standard | Arm's-length principle — §85A, ITO |
| Methods | CUP, resale price, cost plus, TNMM, profit split — aligned with OECD Guidelines |
| Documentation | Transfer pricing study required for transactions with related parties; contemporaneous documentation |
| Annual filing | Form 1385 (TP disclosure) filed with annual tax return |
| Country-by-Country Reporting | Required for Israeli-parented MNE groups with consolidated revenue ≥ ILS 3.4B (~EUR 750M) |
| Penalties | 5%–20% addition on reassessed amounts |
| APA | Advance rulings available; formal APA programme under development |

### Treaty Network

Israel has over 55 bilateral double taxation treaties. The standard treaty withholding rates typically reduce dividends to 5%–15%, interest to 5%–10%, and royalties to 5%–10%.

## 9. Non-Deductible Items

The following are **NOT deductible** under Israeli tax law:

| Expense | Legal Basis |
|---|---|
| Capital expenditure (unless depreciable) | §32(3), ITO |
| Appreciation in asset value (capital improvement) | §32(4), ITO |
| Private or domestic expenses | §32(1), ITO |
| Income tax (Israeli or foreign) | §32(2), ITO |
| 80% of entertainment and hospitality expenses | §32(1a), ITO [unverified — confirm exact limitation] |
| Voluntary payments not connected to income production | §32, ITO |
| Fines and penalties | §32, ITO (implied by "wholly and exclusively" requirement) |
| Excessive or non-arm's-length related-party payments | §85A, ITO |
| Interest not incurred for income production | §32, ITO |
| Gifts exceeding ILS 220 per recipient per year | §32, ITO |
| Vehicle expenses beyond permitted ceilings | §31, ITO; Vehicle Benefit Regulations |
