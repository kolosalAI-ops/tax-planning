# Mexico - R&D Tax Incentives

## 1. Overview

| Parameter | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Headline Incentive** | R&D Tax Credit (*Estímulo Fiscal a la Investigación y Desarrollo de Tecnología* — EFIDT) |
| **Mechanism Type** | Tax credit on incremental R&D expenditures |
| **Headline Rate** | 30% tax credit on incremental R&D spending |
| **Refundable** | No — non-refundable credit against ISR (income tax); unused credit cannot be carried forward |
| **Patent Box** | No |
| **Legal Basis** | Ley del Impuesto sobre la Renta (LISR) Articles 202–203; Decree published in DOF 28/12/2016; CONACYT Rules of Operation |

## 2. Detailed Programs

### 2.1 R&D Tax Credit — EFIDT (LISR Art. 202–203)

| Parameter | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Legal Basis** | LISR Articles 202–203 (introduced by 2017 tax reform); Annual Rules of Operation published by CONACYT/CONAHCYT |
| **Credit Rate** | 30% of incremental R&D expenditures |
| **Incremental Basis** | Credit applies to R&D expenditures exceeding the average of R&D expenditures in the three preceding fiscal years |
| **Annual Cap** | MXN 50,000,000 (approximately US$2.8 million) per taxpayer per year |
| **National Budget Cap** | Total annual fiscal budget allocated for the program (set annually; historically ~MXN 1,500 million total) |
| **Qualifying Expenses** | Investments in technological research and development (*investigación y desarrollo de tecnología*): salaries of researchers, materials, equipment used in R&D, subcontracted R&D with Mexican institutions |
| **Exclusions** | Routine testing, quality control, market research, software acquisition (non-developmental), social science research |
| **Eligible Taxpayers** | All ISR taxpayers (corporate and individuals with business income) conducting qualifying R&D in Mexico |
| **Carry-Forward** | Not available — credit must be applied in the year earned |
| **Carry-Back** | Not available |

### 2.2 CONACYT/CONAHCYT Approval Process

| Parameter | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Legal Basis** | LISR Art. 202; CONACYT General Rules (*Reglas Generales*) published annually in the DOF |
| **Approval Body** | Consejo Nacional de Humanidades, Ciencias y Tecnologías (CONAHCYT, formerly CONACYT) — Interinstitutional Committee |
| **Application Period** | Annual window (typically opens in the first quarter of the fiscal year) |
| **Project Evaluation** | Technical evaluation by CONAHCYT peer reviewers; financial evaluation by SAT (Tax Administration Service) |
| **Approval Timeline** | Approximately 3–6 months from application submission |
| **Certification Required** | Yes — projects must be pre-approved by CONAHCYT before the credit can be claimed |
| **Annual Reporting** | Approved taxpayers must file an annual progress and expenditure report with CONAHCYT |

### 2.3 Maquiladora R&D Considerations

| Parameter | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Legal Basis** | LISR Art. 181–182; Maquiladora Decree (IMMEX) |
| **Relevance** | Maquiladoras operating under IMMEX may qualify for EFIDT if conducting qualifying R&D beyond routine manufacturing |
| **Transfer Pricing** | R&D expenditures must comply with arm's-length transfer pricing standards (LISR Art. 179–184) |
| **Safe Harbor** | Maquiladoras using the safe harbor must ensure R&D expenditures are properly allocated |

## 3. Patent Box / IP Regime

**Not applicable.** Mexico does not have a patent box or IP box regime. Income derived from patents, trademarks, or other intellectual property is taxed at the standard corporate income tax rate of 30%.

| Parameter | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Status** | No patent box regime |
| **Standard CIT Rate** | 30% (LISR Art. 9) |
| **Withholding on IP Royalties** | 25%–35% depending on type (LISR Art. 167); reduced by applicable tax treaties |
| **Treaty Network** | Mexico has 60+ tax treaties that may reduce withholding rates on royalty payments |

## 4. Effective Benefit Analysis

**Assumptions:** MXN 50,000,000 (~US$2,800,000) incremental R&D spend, ISR rate 30%, project approved by CONAHCYT, within annual cap.

| Component | Calculation | Tax Benefit (MXN) |
|---|---|---|
| **30% Credit on Incremental R&D** | MXN 50,000,000 × 30% | **MXN 15,000,000** |
| **Ordinary Deduction of R&D Expenses** | MXN 50,000,000 × 30% ISR rate (separate from credit) | **MXN 15,000,000** |
| **Total Tax Benefit** | Credit + deduction | **MXN 30,000,000** |
| **Effective Benefit Rate** | MXN 30,000,000 ÷ MXN 50,000,000 | **60%** |

**Per US$1,000,000 R&D Spend (incremental, within cap):**

| Component | Tax Benefit (USD Equivalent) |
|---|---|
| **R&D Tax Credit** | ~US$300,000 |
| **Ordinary Deduction** | ~US$300,000 |
| **Total** | ~US$600,000 |
| **Effective Rate** | **~60%** |

> **Important Caveats:** (1) Only *incremental* expenditures qualify for the credit; (2) the MXN 50M cap significantly limits the benefit for large R&D spenders; (3) the total national budget cap means not all applicants may receive approval; (4) CONAHCYT pre-approval is mandatory and not guaranteed.

## 5. Claim Process & Documentation

### Filing Process

| Step | Detail |
|---|---|
| **1. Identify Qualifying R&D Projects** | Ensure projects meet the definition of technological research and development under LISR Art. 202 |
| **2. Submit Application to CONAHCYT** | File project application during the annual application window via CONAHCYT online portal |
| **3. Technical Evaluation** | CONAHCYT peer review committee evaluates the scientific/technological merit of the project |
| **4. Financial Evaluation** | SAT evaluates the taxpayer's fiscal compliance and financial standing |
| **5. Obtain Approval Certificate** | Receive CONAHCYT approval and certified amount of eligible R&D credit |
| **6. Claim Credit on Annual ISR Return** | Apply the approved credit amount against ISR liability in the annual tax return |
| **7. File Annual Progress Report** | Submit progress and expenditure report to CONAHCYT per the Rules of Operation |

### Key Deadlines

| Item | Deadline |
|---|---|
| **CONAHCYT Application Window** | Typically Q1 of the fiscal year (specific dates published in DOF) |
| **Annual ISR Return** | March 31 following the fiscal year (for corporations) |
| **Annual Progress Report** | As specified in CONAHCYT Rules of Operation (typically within 3 months of project completion) |
| **Pre-Approval** | **Mandatory** — credit cannot be claimed without prior CONAHCYT certification |
| **SAT Fiscal Compliance** | Must be current on all tax obligations (*opinión de cumplimiento positiva*) |

### Documentation Requirements

- Detailed project description with technological objectives and methodology
- Budget breakdown of qualifying R&D expenditures
- Personnel qualifications and time allocation to R&D projects
- Evidence of technological uncertainty and innovation
- Financial records supporting claimed expenditures
- CONAHCYT application forms and supporting technical documentation
- Prior-year R&D expenditure records (for incremental calculation)
- Annual progress reports and deliverables

## 6. Recent Changes & Sunset Provisions

| Change | Effective Date | Detail |
|---|---|---|
| **EFIDT Introduction** | January 1, 2017 | 2017 tax reform reintroduced R&D tax credit after previous incentive was repealed in 2009 |
| **Previous R&D Incentive Repeal** | 2009 | The previous R&D tax credit (LISR Art. 219, up to MXN 500M) was eliminated in the 2009 reform |
| **CONACYT → CONAHCYT Renaming** | May 2023 | General Law of Humanities, Science, Technology and Innovation renamed CONACYT to CONAHCYT |
| **Annual Cap Maintained** | Ongoing | MXN 50M per taxpayer cap has remained unchanged since 2017 |
| **Budget Allocation Variability** | Annual | Total national budget for the program varies annually; some years have seen reduced allocations |
| **Sunset Provisions** | None explicit | Program is part of permanent LISR legislation but subject to annual budget appropriations and CONAHCYT rule changes |
| **Potential Reforms** | Under discussion | Proposals to increase the cap and expand qualifying activities have been discussed but not enacted |
