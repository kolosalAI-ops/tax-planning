# Mexico - International Investors

> **Jurisdiction**: Servicio de Administracion Tributaria (SAT)
> **Governing Law**: Ley de Inversion Extranjera (LIE), Ley del Impuesto Sobre la Renta (LISR), Codigo Fiscal de la Federacion (CFF)
> **Tax Year**: 2024/2025
> **Currency**: Mexican Peso (MXN / $)

## 1. Foreign Ownership Restrictions & Investment Approvals

> **Legal basis**: Ley de Inversion Extranjera (LIE) Arts. 5-11, 32; Constitucion Politica de los Estados Unidos Mexicanos (CF) Art. 27

Mexico's foreign investment framework is governed primarily by the **Ley de Inversion Extranjera (LIE)**, enacted in 1993 and substantially reformed following the 2013 energy reform. The LIE classifies economic activities into tiers of restriction, ranging from activities reserved exclusively to the State to those fully open to foreign participation.

### Activities Reserved Exclusively to the Mexican State

| Activity | Legal Reference |
|---|---|
| Exploration and extraction of petroleum and other hydrocarbons | CF Art. 27; LIE Art. 5 fraccion I |
| Planning and control of the national electric system (transmission/distribution) | CF Art. 27; LIE Art. 5 fraccion II |
| Generation of nuclear energy | LIE Art. 5 fraccion III |
| Radioactive minerals | LIE Art. 5 fraccion IV |
| Telegraphic and postal services | LIE Art. 5 fraccion V |
| Coinage and printing of currency | LIE Art. 5 fraccion VI |
| Control, supervision, and surveillance of ports, airports, and heliports | LIE Art. 5 fraccion VII |

> **Note**: The 2013 energy reform (CF Arts. 25, 27, 28 as amended) opened upstream hydrocarbons and electricity generation to private and foreign investment via license contracts, production-sharing contracts, profit-sharing contracts, and service contracts, administered by the Comision Nacional de Hidrocarburos (CNH). While the State retains ownership of subsoil resources, foreign companies may now participate through these contractual arrangements.

### Activities Reserved to Mexican Nationals or Mexican Companies with Exclusion-of-Foreigners Clause

| Activity | Legal Reference |
|---|---|
| National passenger ground transportation (excluding tourism and freight) | LIE Art. 6 fraccion I |
| Retail gasoline and LPG distribution | LIE Art. 6 fraccion II |
| Radio broadcasting services (beyond 49% foreign cap) | LIE Art. 6 fraccion III |
| Credit unions (Uniones de Credito) | LIE Art. 6 fraccion IV |
| Development banking institutions | LIE Art. 6 fraccion V |
| Professional and technical services expressly provided by law | LIE Art. 6 fraccion VI |

### Activities Subject to Foreign Investment Caps (Requiring CNIE Approval Above Thresholds)

| Activity | Maximum Foreign Ownership | Legal Reference |
|---|---|---|
| Domestic air transportation | **25%** | LIE Art. 7 fraccion I |
| Air taxi transportation | **25%** | LIE Art. 7 fraccion II |
| Manufacture and sale of explosives, firearms, cartridges, ammunition, and fireworks | **49%** | LIE Art. 7 fraccion III |
| Printing and publication of newspapers (for domestic circulation only) | **49%** | LIE Art. 7 fraccion IV |
| Series "T" shares in agricultural, livestock, and forestry companies | **49%** | LIE Art. 7 fraccion V |
| Cable television | **49%** | LIE Art. 7 fraccion VI |
| Basic telephony services | **49%** | LIE Art. 7 fraccion VII |
| Shares of financial holding companies, multiple banking institutions, securities brokerage houses, stock exchange specialists | **49%** | LIE Art. 7 fraccion VIII |
| Insurance and bonding institutions | **49%** | LIE Art. 7 fraccion IX |

> **CNIE approval**: For investments exceeding the above thresholds, or for certain large-scale acquisitions, approval from the Comision Nacional de Inversiones Extranjeras (CNIE) is required under LIE Arts. 10-11.

### Restricted Zone (Zona Restringida)

| Parameter | Value | Legal Reference |
|---|---|---|
| Border strip | **50 km** from national borders | LIE Art. 8; CF Art. 27 fraccion I |
| Coastal strip | **100 km** from coastlines | LIE Art. 8; CF Art. 27 fraccion I |

### National Foreign Investment Commission (CNIE) & Registration

| Parameter | Detail | Legal Reference |
|---|---|---|

## 2. Non-Resident Investor Taxation

> **Legal basis**: LISR Titulo V (Artículos 153-175) — Non-resident taxation

### Capital Gains Tax on Shares

| Parameter | Value | Legal Reference |
|---|---|---|
| Standard CGT rate (non-resident) | **10%** on net gain from alienation of shares | LISR Art. 161 |
| Alternative rate (documented cost basis) | **35%** on net gain, if cost basis is properly determined per LISR Art. 22 rules | LISR Art. 161 parrafo 2 |
| Stock exchange transactions | **10%** on gross consideration | LISR Art. 161 bis |
| Real property-rich companies | Treated as real property disposition — **25%** of gross or **35%** of net gain | LISR Art. 160 |

## 4. Branch vs. Subsidiary

> **Legal basis**: Ley General de Sociedades Mercantiles (LGSM); LIE Arts. 17-A, 32; LISR Arts. 9, 164-165

### Common Mexican Corporate Structures for Foreign Investors

| Structure | Description | Legal Reference |
|---|---|---|
| **S.A. de C.V.** | Sociedad Anonima de Capital Variable — similar to a corporation; variable capital feature allows share capital changes without charter amendment | LGSM Arts. 87-206 |
| **S. de R.L. de C.V.** | Sociedad de Responsabilidad Limitada de Capital Variable — LLC equivalent; popular for US investors due to check-the-box planning | LGSM Arts. 58-86 |
| **S.A.P.I. de C.V.** | Sociedad Anonima Promotora de Inversion — similar to S.A. but with additional flexibility for minority shareholder protections and governance provisions | LGSM; Ley del Mercado de Valores |
| **Branch (Sucursal)** | Permanent establishment of a foreign entity; must register with RNIE | LIE Art. 17-A; LISR Art. 168-169 |

### Tax Comparison: Branch vs. Subsidiary

| Parameter | Branch | Subsidiary (S.A. de C.V.) | Subsidiary (S. de R.L. de C.V.) |
|---|---|---|---|
| CIT rate | **30%** | **30%** | **30%** |
| Repatriation tax | **10%** branch remittance (LISR Art. 165) | **10%** dividend WHT on post-2014 CUFIN (LISR Art. 164) | **10%** dividend WHT on post-2014 CUFIN (LISR Art. 164) |
| Effective combined rate | **~37%** (30% + 10% of 70%) | **~37%** (30% + 10% of 70%) | **~37%** (30% + 10% of 70%) |
| Treaty dividend reduction | Limited (some treaties reduce branch tax) | Yes — treaty dividend rates apply | Yes — treaty dividend rates apply |
| Liability protection | **None** — head office fully liable | **Yes** — separate legal entity | **Yes** — separate legal entity |
| Corporate formalities | Fewer — no board, no shareholders' meetings | Annual shareholders' meeting, board of directors, statutory auditor | Members' meeting, manager(s), fewer formalities than S.A. |
| RNIE registration | **Required** | **Required** (if foreign-invested) | **Required** (if foreign-invested) |
| US check-the-box planning | **N/A** | Not available (treated as corporation for US tax) | **Available** — can elect to be treated as disregarded entity or partnership for US tax |
| PTU (profit sharing) | **10%** of taxable profit | **10%** of taxable profit | **10%** of taxable profit |
| Thin capitalization rules | Apply (3:1 debt-to-equity) | Apply (3:1 debt-to-equity) | Apply (3:1 debt-to-equity) |

### Check-the-Box Planning (US Investors)

The **S. de R.L. de C.V.** structure is widely used by US-parented multinationals because:

- Under US Treasury Regulations section 301.7701-3, the S. de R.L. is an "eligible entity" that can elect classification as a disregarded entity (single member) or partnership (multiple members) for US federal tax purposes.
- This allows US parents to claim **direct foreign tax credits** for Mexican taxes paid, without the need for the subpart F or GILTI indirect credit mechanism.
- Mexican law treats the S. de R.L. as a separate taxable entity regardless of US classification.
- Commonly used in **maquiladora** operations under the IMMEX program.

## 5. Foreign Tax Credit

> **Legal basis**: LISR Articulo 5; CFF Articulo 6

### Credit Methodology

| Parameter | Detail | Legal Reference |
|---|---|---|
| Method | **Credit method** (not exemption) | LISR Art. 5 |
| Eligible taxes | Income taxes paid or accrued in a foreign jurisdiction | LISR Art. 5 parrafo 1 |
| Limitation | Credit limited to Mexican tax attributable to the foreign-source income (proportional method) | LISR Art. 5 parrafo 2 |
| Per-country limitation | **Yes** — credit computed separately for each source country | LISR Art. 5 parrafo 3 |
| Carry-forward of excess credits | **Not permitted** (use-it-or-lose-it) | LISR Art. 5 |
| Carry-back of excess credits | **Not permitted** | LISR Art. 5 |
| Direct credits | Available for taxes directly paid by the Mexican taxpayer on foreign income | LISR Art. 5 |
| Deemed-paid (indirect) credits | Available for corporate-level taxes paid by foreign subsidiaries on distributed dividends | LISR Art. 5 parrafo 4 |
| Documentation | Must provide proof of foreign tax paid (tax returns, payment receipts, certificates) | LISR Art. 5 parrafo 5 |

### Credit Calculation Example

| Element | Amount (MXN) |
|---|---|
| Foreign-source income | 10,000,000 |
| Mexican tax on total income (30%) | 15,000,000 (assuming 50M total income) |
| Proportion of foreign income to total income | 20% (10M / 50M) |
| Maximum creditable amount | **3,000,000** (15M x 20%) |
| Foreign tax actually paid | 2,500,000 |
| Credit allowed | **2,500,000** (lower of actual vs. maximum) |
| Excess credit (if foreign tax = 4,000,000) | **1,000,000** — lost (no carry-forward) |

### Key Considerations

| Issue | Detail |
|---|---|
| Treaty interaction | Credits apply regardless of treaty; treaty may reduce source-country tax, improving credit utilization |
| Indirect credits | Mexican parent can credit underlying corporate tax of a foreign subsidiary (minimum 10% ownership) on dividend distributions |
| Timing | Credit taken in the fiscal year the foreign tax is paid or accrued |
| Currency conversion | Foreign tax converted to MXN at the exchange rate on the date paid or accrued |

## 6. CFC Rules (REFIPRE - Regimen Fiscal Preferente)

> **Legal basis**: LISR Articulos 176-178

### Overview

Mexico's CFC rules, known as **REFIPRE** (Regimen Fiscal Preferente), require Mexican residents who control or have significant influence over foreign entities in low-tax jurisdictions to include the foreign entity's passive income in their Mexican taxable base on a current-year basis.

### Applicability Thresholds

| Parameter | Detail | Legal Reference |
|---|---|---|
| Low-tax jurisdiction threshold | Foreign entity subject to effective income tax rate **less than 75%** of Mexican rate | LISR Art. 176 parrafo 1 |
| Effective rate threshold | **< 22.5%** (75% x 30% Mexican CIT rate) | LISR Art. 176 |
| Control definition | Direct or indirect ownership of **>= 50%** of voting rights or equity | LISR Art. 176 parrafo 2 |
| Significant influence | Direct or indirect ownership of **>= 10%** of voting rights or equity | LISR Art. 176 parrafo 3 |
| Per-entity determination | Analysis performed entity-by-entity for each foreign investment | LISR Art. 176 |

### Income Subject to REFIPRE

| Income Type | Treatment | Legal Reference |
|---|---|---|
| **Passive income** — dividends, interest, royalties, capital gains, rental income | **Included** in Mexican resident's taxable base in the year earned | LISR Art. 176 fraccion I |
| **Active business income** from non-tax-haven jurisdictions | **Generally excluded** from current inclusion | LISR Art. 176 fraccion II |
| **Active income from tax-haven jurisdictions** | **Included** regardless of nature | LISR Art. 176 fraccion III |
| Income from transparent entities | **Included** if the entity is in a low-tax jurisdiction | LISR Art. 176 |

### Substance Exception

| Parameter | Detail | Legal Reference |
|---|---|---|
| Exemption available | Yes — if the foreign entity has genuine economic substance | LISR Art. 176 parrafo 6 |
| Requirements | Real operations with: dedicated employees, physical assets, decision-making occurring locally | LISR Art. 176 |
| Documentation | Must demonstrate that the entity is not merely a conduit or shell company | LISR Art. 176 |
| Burden of proof | On the Mexican taxpayer claiming the exemption | LISR Art. 176 |

### Reporting & Compliance

| Parameter | Detail | Legal Reference |
|---|---|---|
| Annual informative return | Required for all Mexican residents with investments in REFIPRE jurisdictions | LISR Art. 178 |
| Content | Details of foreign entities, income earned, taxes paid, ownership percentages | LISR Art. 178 |
| Filing deadline | Same as annual income tax return (March 31 for legal entities) | LISR Art. 178; CFF Art. 76 |
| Foreign tax credit | Taxes paid by the CFC in its jurisdiction may be credited against the Mexican tax on the included income | LISR Art. 177 |
| Penalties for non-compliance | Fines of **MXN 93,330 to MXN 133,340** for failure to file informative return; potential criminal penalties for tax evasion | CFF Arts. 81-82; CFF Art. 108 |
| Blacklisted jurisdictions | SAT publishes list of jurisdictions considered preferential tax regimes (updated periodically via Annex 10 of the Miscellaneous Fiscal Resolution) | LISR Art. 176; RMF Annex 10 |

## 7. Tax Treaty Network

> **Legal basis**: CF Art. 133 (treaty supremacy); LISR Art. 4 (treaty application); Convencion Multilateral (MLI)

### Overview

Mexico maintains a broad network of **60+ comprehensive double taxation agreements (DTAs)**, primarily based on the OECD Model Tax Convention. Mexico has signed the **Multilateral Convention to Implement Tax Treaty Related Measures to Prevent BEPS (MLI)**, which modifies the application of covered bilateral treaties.

### MLI & Treaty Anti-Abuse

| Parameter | Detail |
|---|---|
| MLI signed | **Yes** — Mexico signed the MLI on June 7, 2017; ratified and deposited |

| Mutual Agreement Procedure (MAP) | Available under all treaties; Mexico participates in OECD MAP peer reviews |
| Arbitration | Mexico has **not** adopted mandatory binding arbitration under the MLI |

## 8. PE Risk

> **Legal basis**: CFF Articulo 9 (tax residence); LISR Articulos 168-169 (PE definition); LISR Articulos 181-182 (maquiladora safe harbor)

### Domestic PE Definition

| PE Type | Threshold / Trigger | Legal Reference |
|---|---|---|
| Fixed place of business | Place of management, branch, office, factory, workshop, mine, quarry, installation, or any place of extraction of natural resources | LISR Art. 168 |
| Construction PE | Activities lasting **more than 183 days** in any 12-month period (domestic rule) | LISR Art. 168 parrafo 2 |
| Service PE | Furnishing of services for **more than 183 days** in any 12-month period (domestic rule) | LISR Art. 168 parrafo 3 |
| Dependent agent PE | Agent habitually exercising authority to conclude contracts on behalf of the non-resident | LISR Art. 169 |
| Insurance PE | Collection of premiums or insurance of risks in Mexico | LISR Art. 168 parrafo 4 |

### Treaty PE (OECD Model Art. 5 Based)

| Treaty Partner | Construction PE Threshold | Service PE Threshold | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| **United States** | **6 months** (183 days) | **183 days in any 12-month period** | US-Mexico DTA Art. 5(3) |
| **Canada** | **6 months** | **183 days in any 12-month period** | Canada-Mexico DTA Art. 5(3) |
| **Spain** | **12 months** | **183 days in any 12-month period** | Spain-Mexico DTA Art. 5(3) |
| **Germany** | **6 months** | **183 days in any 12-month period** | Germany-Mexico DTA Art. 5(3) |
| **United Kingdom** | **6 months** | **183 days in any 12-month period** | UK-Mexico DTA Art. 5(3) |
| **Japan** | **6 months** | **183 days in any 12-month period** | Japan-Mexico DTA Art. 5(3) |
| **Netherlands** | **6 months** | **183 days in any 12-month period** | Netherlands-Mexico DTA Art. 5(3) |

### Agent PE Risk

| Parameter | Detail | Legal Reference |
|---|---|---|
| Dependent agent | Agent with authority to **conclude contracts** in the name of the non-resident creates a PE | LISR Art. 169; OECD Model Art. 5(5) |
| Independent agent | Does not create a PE if acting in the ordinary course of independent business | LISR Art. 169; OECD Model Art. 5(6) |
| Post-MLI modification | MLI Art. 12 expands agent PE to include agents who **habitually play the principal role** leading to conclusion of contracts, even without formal authority | MLI Art. 12 |
| Commissionaire arrangements | SAT may re-characterize commissionaire structures to find PE under substance-over-form rules | CFF Art. 5-A |

### Digital PE

| Parameter | Detail |
|---|---|
| Current status | **No digital PE** in existing treaties or domestic law |
| Digital services tax | Non-resident digital service providers subject to **16% IVA** withholding (not income tax PE) |
| OECD Pillar One | Mexico is an Inclusive Framework member; Amount A reallocation rules under negotiation |
| Proposed domestic rules | Legislative proposals have been discussed but not enacted as of 2024/2025 |

### Maquiladora Safe Harbor (IMMEX Program)

| Parameter | Detail | Legal Reference |
|---|---|---|
| IMMEX program | Industria Manufacturera, Maquiladora y de Servicios de Exportacion — allows temporary import of materials for manufacturing and re-export | Decreto IMMEX (DOF 2006, amended) |
| PE protection | Non-resident principal is **deemed not to have a PE** in Mexico through the maquiladora, provided safe harbor conditions are met | LISR Art. 181; LISR Art. 182 |
| Safe harbor pricing | Maquiladora must report taxable income of **at least the greater of**: 6.9% of total assets used in the operation, or 6.5% of total costs and expenses | LISR Art. 182 |
| APA alternative | Maquiladoras may enter into Advance Pricing Agreements (APAs) with SAT as alternative to safe harbor | LISR Art. 182 parrafo 3 |
| Transfer pricing compliance | Full transfer pricing documentation required regardless of safe harbor election | LISR Art. 76 fracciones IX, XII |
| Shelter maquiladora | Third-party operator managing maquiladora for foreign companies; foreign company protected from PE for up to **4 fiscal years** | LISR Art. 183 |
| Post-shelter transition | After 4 years, foreign company must establish its own Mexican entity or PE | LISR Art. 183 |
