# Portugal - R&D Tax Incentives

## 1. Overview

| Feature | Details |
|---|---|
| **Primary Incentive** | SIFIDE II (Sistema de Incentivos Fiscais à I&D Empresarial) |
| **Base Rate** | 32.5% tax credit on qualifying R&D expenditure |
| **Incremental Rate** | 50% on increase over prior 2-year average (cap €1.5M) |
| **Patent Box** | 85% exemption on qualifying IP income |
| **Legal Basis** | Lei 40/2005 (SIFIDE); Código do IRC Art. 50-A (Patent Box) |
| **Carry-Forward** | 8 tax years |
| **Administering Body** | ANI (Agência Nacional de Inovação) |
| **Refundability** | Not refundable — credit against IRC (corporate income tax) |
| **Combined Generosity** | Among the highest R&D tax incentive rates in the EU |

## 2. Detailed Programs

### 2.1 SIFIDE II — R&D Tax Credit

**Legal Basis:** Lei 40/2005, as subsequently amended (most recently by the annual State Budget laws); Decree-Law 82/2023

SIFIDE II is Portugal's primary R&D fiscal incentive and one of the most generous in Europe, combining a high base rate with a substantial incremental component.

#### Credit Rates and Caps

| Component | Rate | Cap |
|---|---|---|
| Base rate | 32.5% of qualifying R&D expenditure | No explicit cap on base credit |
| Incremental rate | 50% on expenditure increase vs. 2-year average | €1,500,000 per year |
| Total potential credit | 32.5% + up to 50% incremental | Effectively uncapped base + €1.5M incremental |

#### Qualifying R&D Activities

SIFIDE II follows definitions aligned with the OECD Frascati Manual:

- Fundamental/basic research (investigação fundamental)
- Applied research (investigação aplicada)
- Experimental development (desenvolvimento experimental)
- Must be systematic and aimed at increasing scientific or technical knowledge
- Must involve novelty and resolution of uncertainty

#### Qualifying Expenditure

| Cost Category | Eligible | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Personnel costs — researchers with PhD | Yes | 120% weighted (20% bonus) |
| Personnel costs — other R&D staff | Yes | 100% of cost |
| Operating costs (materials, supplies) | Yes | Directly consumed in R&D |
| Acquisition of R&D equipment | Yes | Depreciable assets used primarily for R&D |
| Contract R&D services | Yes | From recognized R&D entities |
| Patent registration and maintenance | Yes | Including international filings |
| R&D audit and certification costs | Yes | Costs of SIFIDE II compliance |
| Technology acquisition costs | Yes | Licenses, know-how for R&D use |
| Participation in R&D institutions | Yes | Contributions to collective R&D bodies |

#### PhD Researcher Bonus

Personnel costs for researchers holding a **doctoral degree (PhD)** are weighted at **120%** for SIFIDE II calculation purposes:

| Researcher Type | Weighting |
|---|---|
| PhD-holding researchers | 120% of actual cost |
| Other qualified R&D personnel | 100% of actual cost |
| R&D technicians | 100% of actual cost |

### 2.2 Application Limits and Carry-Forward

| Rule | Details |
|---|---|
| Annual application limit | Credit applied against IRC liability for the year |
| Carry-forward | 8 tax years (from the year the credit was generated) |
| Interaction with other incentives | Can coexist with certain EU-funded R&D grants |
| Minimum spend threshold | No minimum threshold |
| Company size restrictions | All companies eligible (SMEs and large enterprises) |

### 2.3 ANI Pre-Validation Process

All SIFIDE II claims must be validated by **ANI (Agência Nacional de Inovação)**:

1. **Application Submission** — Filed electronically to ANI after the tax year closes
2. **Technical Assessment** — ANI evaluates the R&D content and qualifying nature of activities
3. **Expenditure Review** — ANI verifies that claimed costs meet eligibility criteria
4. **Validation Certificate** — ANI issues a certificate confirming the qualifying amount
5. **Tax Return Claim** — Company includes the validated credit in the IRC return
6. **Deadline** — Applications must be submitted by the end of the 5th month after the tax year

### 2.4 Additional R&D Support Programs

| Program | Description | Administering Body |
|---|---|---|
| SIFIDE II | Primary R&D tax credit (described above) | ANI |

## 3. Patent Box / IP Regime

### Portuguese Patent Box

**Legal Basis:** Código do IRC, Artigo 50-A; Portaria implementing regulations

| Feature | Details |
|---|---|
| Exemption rate | 85% of qualifying IP income |
| Effective tax rate | 3.15% (85% exemption on 21% IRC rate) |
| Qualifying IP | Patents, industrial designs, utility models, plant breeders' rights, software copyright |
| Qualifying income | Royalties, licensing income, embedded IP income |
| Nexus approach | Modified nexus approach (OECD BEPS compliant) |
| Self-development requirement | IP must be substantially developed by the taxpayer |

#### Nexus Fraction

The 85% exemption applies to "qualifying income" calculated via the OECD nexus approach:

```
Qualifying Income = Gross IP Income × (Qualifying Expenditure × 1.3) / Total Expenditure
```

| Component | Included |
|---|---|
| Qualifying expenditure (numerator) | In-house R&D, unrelated-party outsourcing, 30% uplift |
| Total expenditure (denominator) | All R&D costs including related-party outsourcing and acquisitions |
| 30% uplift | Capped so numerator does not exceed denominator |

#### Excluded IP

- Trademarks
- Brand names and marketing intangibles
- Literary and artistic works (non-software)
- IP acquired from related parties (unless further substantially developed)

#### Combined SIFIDE II + Patent Box Strategy

Portugal allows companies to benefit from **both** SIFIDE II (input-based) and the Patent Box (output-based):

1. Claim SIFIDE II credits on R&D expenditure during the development phase
2. Elect Patent Box treatment once the IP generates income
3. No anti-stacking rules prevent this combination
4. Combined effective benefit can exceed 70% of R&D investment for IP-generating activities

*Assumptions: Exchange rate ~€0.92/USD; IRC rate 21%; expenditure exceeds 2-year average by 30%; no PhD researchers*

| Benefit Component | Calculation | Value (USD) |

| Incremental credit (50% on 30% increase) | $300,000 × 50% | $150,000 |
| **Total benefit** | | **$685,000** |
| **Effective rate** | | **68.5%** |

| Benefit Component | Calculation | Value (USD) |
|---|---|---|

| Patent Box: Assuming $500K IP income | $500,000 × 85% × 21% | $89,250 |

## 5. Claim Process & Documentation

### Step-by-Step Claim Process

1. **Conduct R&D Activities** — Execute qualifying projects during the tax year
2. **Track Qualifying Expenditure** — Maintain detailed cost records per project
3. **Submit ANI Application** — File electronically within 5 months of tax year-end
4. **ANI Technical Review** — Agency evaluates R&D nature and qualifying costs
5. **Receive ANI Validation** — Certificate issued confirming eligible credit amount
6. **File IRC Return (Modelo 22)** — Include SIFIDE II credit in corporate tax return
7. **Apply Credit** — Offset against IRC liability
8. **Carry Forward** — Unused credits carried forward for up to 8 years

### Documentation Requirements

| Requirement | Details |
|---|---|
| Technical project reports | Description of R&D objectives, methodology, and results |
| Personnel records | Time allocation to R&D activities; qualifications (PhD status) |
| Financial records | Detailed cost breakdown per qualifying category |
| Subcontracting agreements | Contracts with R&D service providers |
| Equipment records | Documentation of assets acquired for R&D use |
| Patent documentation | Filings and maintenance records |
| ANI application and validation | Complete application package and resulting certificate |
| Certified accountant statement | Confirmation of expenditure figures |

### Common Pitfalls

- Missing the ANI application deadline (5 months post-year-end)
- Inadequate documentation of R&D novelty and uncertainty
- Failing to segregate R&D costs from routine production activities
- Not tracking the 2-year expenditure history for incremental calculation
- Claiming costs already fully covered by non-repayable grants

## 6. Recent Changes & Sunset Provisions

| Year | Change | Impact |
|---|---|---|
| 2014 | SIFIDE II enacted (replacing original SIFIDE) | Modernized framework; enhanced rates |
| 2020 | Patent box reformed to OECD nexus approach | Compliance with BEPS Action 5 |
| 2022 | Increased carry-forward from 6 to 8 years | Extended benefit window for loss-making companies |
| 2023 | DL 82/2023 — enhanced rates confirmed for budget cycle | Maintained 32.5% base + 50% incremental |
| 2024 | Enhanced ANI digital application process | Streamlined filing |
| 2025 | State Budget — scheme renewed with existing parameters | Continuity confirmed |

### Sunset Provisions

- SIFIDE II is **renewed annually** through the State Budget Law (Orçamento do Estado)
- No permanent legislation — requires annual legislative confirmation
- Historically, the scheme has been consistently renewed since 2014
- Strong cross-party political support for maintaining R&D incentives
- The Patent Box (IRC Art. 50-A) is a permanent feature of the tax code
- Carry-forward rights survive even if the scheme is not renewed (credits already generated remain valid)

### Future Outlook

- Government commitment to maintaining Portugal's competitive R&D tax position
- Discussions on potential refundability for startups and SMEs
- Consideration of expanding qualifying cost categories
- EU Minimum Tax Directive (Pillar Two) implementation may affect credit utilization strategy
