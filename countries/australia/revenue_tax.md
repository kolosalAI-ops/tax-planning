# Australia - Business Revenue Tax Codes

> **Jurisdiction**: Australian Taxation Office (ATO) + State/Territory Revenue Offices
> **Governing Law**: Income Tax Assessment Act 1997 (ITAA 1997), Income Tax Assessment Act 1936
> **Tax Year**: 2024-25 (July 1, 2024 - June 30, 2025)
> **Currency**: Australian Dollar (AUD / A$)

---

## 1. Corporate Income Tax

> **Legal Basis**: ITAA 1997 Section 4-10 (Charge of income tax)

### Standard Rates

| Entity Type | Rate | Conditions | Legal Reference |
|---|---|---|---|
| **General company** | **30%** | Companies not qualifying as base rate entities | ITAA 1997 Section 23-5, Schedule 1 |
| **Base rate entity** | **25%** | Aggregated turnover < $50M AND ≤80% base rate entity passive income | ITAA 1997 Section 23-10 (base rate entity definition) |

### Base Rate Entity Requirements

> **Legal Basis**: ITAA 1997 Section 23-12 (Base rate entity passive income test)

| Parameter | Value |
|---|---|
| Aggregated turnover threshold | **< $50 million** (ITAA 1997 Section 23-10) |
| Passive income test | ≤ **80%** of assessable income is base rate entity passive income (ITAA 1997 Section 23-12) |
| Passive income includes | Interest, dividends (non-franked), rent, royalties, net capital gains, partnership/trust distributions of passive income |
| Effective date | 25% rate from 2021-22 onwards |

### Historical Corporate Rates

| Year | General Rate | Small Business Rate |
|---|---|---|
| Pre-2015 | 30% | 30% |
| 2015-16 | 30% | 28.5% |
| 2016-17 | 30% | 27.5% |
| 2017-2021 | 30% | 27.5% (turnover <$50M) |
| 2021-22+ | 30% | **25%** |

## 2. Imputation System (Franking Credits)

> **Legal Basis**: ITAA 1997 Division 202 (Franking/imputation system)

Australia operates a **full imputation system** to prevent double taxation of corporate profits:

| Parameter | Value | Legal Reference |
|---|---|---|
| Maximum franking rate | **30%** (or 25% for base rate entities) | ITAA 1997 Division 202 |
| Franking account | Tracks franking credits and debits | ITAA 1997 Section 202-5 |
| Franking credit formula | Dividend × (tax rate / (1 - tax rate)) | |
| Franking credit at 30% | $0.4286 per $1 of franked dividend | |
| Franking credit at 25% | $0.3333 per $1 of franked dividend | |
| Refundable credits | Yes - excess franking credits refundable to individuals and complying super funds | ITAA 1997 Section 207-20 (franking credit tax offset - refundable) |
| Benchmark rule | Must frank at same rate within a franking period | ITAA 1997 Division 203 |

## 3. Capital Gains Tax (CGT)

> **Legal Basis**: ITAA 1997 Parts 3-1 and 3-3 (Capital gains and losses)

### Companies

| Parameter | Value | Legal Reference |
|---|---|---|
| CGT rate | Same as income tax rate (**30%** or **25%**) | |
| 50% discount | **NOT available** to companies (available to individuals/trusts only) | ITAA 1997 Division 115, Subdivision 115-A |
| Indexation method | Available for assets held before Sept 21, 1999 | ITAA 1997 Division 114 |
| Net capital loss | Carry forward indefinitely (cannot offset other income) | ITAA 1997 Division 102 |

### Small Business CGT Concessions (Division 152)

> **Legal Basis**: ITAA 1997 Subdivisions 152-A to 152-E

| Concession | Benefit | Conditions | Legal Reference |
|---|---|---|---|
| 15-year exemption | **100% exempt** | Asset held ≥15 years, owner retiring/permanently incapacitated | Subdivision 152-B |
| 50% active asset reduction | **50%** reduction in capital gain | Active asset, aggregated turnover <$2M or net assets <$6M | Subdivision 152-C |
| Retirement exemption | Exempt up to **$500,000** lifetime limit | | Subdivision 152-D |
| Small business rollover | Defer gain for **2 years** (or longer if replacement asset acquired) | | Subdivision 152-E |

### Eligibility for Small Business CGT

| Test | Threshold |
|---|---|
| Maximum net asset value test | **$6 million** (net assets of related entities) |
| Aggregated turnover test | **< $2 million** |
| Active asset test | Asset must be an active asset (used in business) |
| Significant individual test | ≥20% ownership by individual (companies with <$2M turnover) |

## 4. R&D Tax Incentive (Division 355)

> **Legal Basis**: ITAA 1997 Division 355 (R&D tax incentive); ITAA 1997 Section 355-100 (R&D entities)

### Refundable Offset (Small Companies)

| Parameter | Value |
|---|---|
| Eligibility | Aggregated turnover **< $20 million** (ITAA 1997 Section 355-100) |
| Offset rate | Company tax rate **+ 18.5 percentage points** |
| Effective rate (25% base entity) | **43.5%** refundable offset |
| Effective rate (30% entity) | **48.5%** refundable offset |
| Annual expenditure cap | **$150 million** (for the intensity premium) |
| Refundable | **Yes** (cash back even if no tax payable) |
| $4M refund cap | Maximum **$4 million** refundable per year (excess carried forward) |

### Non-Refundable Offset (Larger Companies)

| Parameter | Value |
|---|---|
| Eligibility | Aggregated turnover **≥ $20 million** |
| Base offset rate | Company tax rate **+ 8.5 percentage points** |
| Effective rate (30% entity) | **38.5%** |
| Intensity premium | Additional **8.5%** for R&D expenditure exceeding 2% of total expenses |
| Intensity premium rate | Company tax rate **+ 16.5 percentage points** = **46.5%** |
| Refundable | **No** (carry forward as tax offset) |
| Annual cap | **$150 million** in R&D expenditure |

### Eligible R&D Activities

| Category | Description |
|---|---|
| Core R&D | Experimental activities to generate new knowledge, where outcome cannot be known in advance |
| Supporting R&D | Activities directly related to core R&D |
| Exclusions | Market research, commercial testing, routine data collection, management studies |

## 15. Income by Source Type

> **Legal Basis**: ITAA 1997, ITAA 1936, GST Act 1999

### 15.1 Service Income (Domestic & Cross-Border)

| Parameter | Detail | Legal Reference |
|---|---|---|
| **Domestic services** | Taxed as ordinary assessable income at **30%** CIT (or **25%** for base rate entities with turnover <$50M) | ITAA 1997 §4-15 |
| **GST on services** | **10%** standard rate on taxable supplies | GST Act 1999 |
| **Cross-border services (non-resident)** | Non-residents taxed only on Australian-sourced income | ITAA 1936 §6-5 |
| **Royalty WHT vs. service fees** | Services generally **not** subject to WHT unless a treaty reclassifies fees as royalties — distinction is critical for cross-border structuring | ITAA 1997 §12-280; relevant DTAs |
| **Transfer pricing** | Arm's length principle (OECD-aligned) governs cross-border related-party service pricing | Subdivision 815-B ITAA 1997 |
| **MAAL (Multinational Anti-Avoidance Law)** | Targets foreign income shifting through Australian service activities by SGEs (≥$1B global revenue) | Division 177DA ITAA 1936 |
| **DPT (Diverted Profits Tax)** | **40%** penalty rate on profits diverted from Australia through contrived arrangements | Division 177J ITAA 1936 |

**Practical notes:**
- The distinction between service fees and royalties is especially relevant for software-related payments and technical/management fees under Australia's treaty network.
- Transfer pricing documentation (local file, master file, CbCR) is mandatory for SGEs with ≥$1B global revenue.
- MAAL and DPT apply concurrently; the ATO may pursue either or both.

### 15.2 Royalty & IP Licensing Income (Received)

| Parameter | Detail | Legal Reference |
|---|---|---|
| **Domestic royalties** | Assessable as ordinary income at **30%** CIT (or **25%** for base rate entities) | ITAA 1997 §6-5 |
| **Inbound royalties from abroad** | Assessable as part of worldwide income; foreign tax credit (FITO) available | ITAA 1997 Division 770 |
| **IP depreciation** | Effective life method under Division 40; software effective life **2.5 years** (200% diminishing value) | Division 40 ITAA 1997 |
| **Mining/quarrying rights** | Capital allowances under Division 40 for exploration and prospecting rights | Division 40 ITAA 1997 |
| **Patent box regime** | **None** — Australia does not have a formal patent box regime | N/A |
| **R&D tax incentive** | **48.5%** refundable offset for turnover <$20M; **38.5%** non-refundable offset for larger companies | Division 355 ITAA 1997 |

**Practical notes:**
- No patent box means IP income is taxed at the standard CIT rate with no preferential treatment.
- The R&D tax incentive effectively subsidises R&D expenditure, not IP income — it operates as an offset on eligible expenditure.
- Inbound royalties should be analysed under the relevant DTA to determine FITO availability and any limitation.

### 15.3 Rental & Lease Income

| Parameter | Detail | Legal Reference |
|---|---|---|
| **Rental income from real property** | Assessable income taxed at standard CIT rate (**30%** or **25%**) | ITAA 1997 §6-5 |
| **Division 43 capital works deductions** | **2.5%** for commercial buildings; **4%** for certain industrial/manufacturing buildings | Division 43 ITAA 1997 |
| **Negative gearing** | Rental losses deductible against other income for individuals (proposed changes not yet enacted into law) | ITAA 1997 general deduction rules |
| **Equipment leasing** | Decline-in-value deductions under Division 40 for leased plant and equipment | Division 40 ITAA 1997 |
| **MIT (Managed Investment Trust)** | Concessionary **15%** WHT for foreign investors on fund income from MITs | Subdivision 12-H ITAA 1997 |
| **A-REIT** | Tax-transparent if all taxable income is distributed; **no entity-level tax** | Various (trust law + tax law) |

**Practical notes:**
- Division 43 applies to construction expenditure on buildings, not to the land itself.
- The MIT regime offers a significant concession for foreign investors compared to the standard 30% non-treaty WHT rate.
- A-REITs are the primary vehicle for institutional real estate investment in Australia, offering flow-through tax treatment.

### 15.4 Capital Gains (Business Assets, Shares, Property)

| Parameter | Detail | Legal Reference |
|---|---|---|
| **CGT regime** | Comprehensive CGT regime under Parts 3-1 and 3-3 | ITAA 1997 Parts 3-1, 3-3 |
| **Companies** | **No CGT discount**; gains taxed at **30%** CIT (or **25%** for base rate entities) | ITAA 1997 Division 115 |
| **Individuals** | **50% CGT discount** if asset held for >12 months | Division 115 ITAA 1997 |
| **Small business CGT concessions** | Up to **100% exemption** for assets where net asset value <$6M | Division 152 ITAA 1997 |
| — 15-year exemption | Full exemption if held ≥15 years and owner retiring/incapacitated | §152-105 |
| — 50% active asset reduction | 50% reduction in capital gain for active business assets | §152-205 |
| — Retirement exemption | Exempt up to **$500,000** lifetime limit | §152-305 |
| — Rollover relief | Defer gain for 2+ years if replacement asset acquired | §152-400 |
| **Goodwill** | Subject to CGT (not depreciable); qualifies as active asset for Division 152 concessions | ITAA 1997 §108-5 |
| **Rollover relief (restructures)** | Same-asset roll-over available for company restructures | Subdivision 124-M ITAA 1997 |
| **Key CGT events** | A1 (disposal), C1 (loss/destruction), E1 (trust creation), K6 (pre-CGT shares) | ITAA 1997 Part 3-1 |

**Practical notes:**
- Companies do not receive the 50% CGT discount — this is only available to individuals, trusts, and complying superannuation funds.
- The small business CGT concessions can be stacked (e.g., 50% active asset reduction + retirement exemption) for significant tax savings.
- Subdivision 124-M rollover is commonly used for scrip-for-scrip transactions in M&A.

### 15.5 Dividend Income (Received by Company)

| Parameter | Detail | Legal Reference |
|---|---|---|
| **Franked dividends** | No additional tax if company tax rate matches franking rate; franking credit offsets tax | Division 207 ITAA 1997 |
| **Unfranked dividends** | Assessable at full CIT rate (**30%** or **25%**) | ITAA 1997 §6-5 |
| **Inter-corporate dividend rebate** | Effectively achieved through the franking/imputation system (no separate rebate) | Division 207 ITAA 1997 |
| **Franking deficit tax** | Anti-avoidance measure to prevent manipulation of franking accounts | Part IIIAA ITAA 1936 |
| **Non-resident dividends** | Assessable as part of worldwide income; FITO available for foreign WHT paid | Division 770 ITAA 1997 |
| **Conduit foreign income (CFI)** | Can flow through as **tax-free** to non-resident shareholders | Division 802 ITAA 1997 |
| **Streaming of franked dividends** | Anti-avoidance rules prevent selective streaming of franked dividends to preferred shareholders | §207-145 ITAA 1997 |

**Practical notes:**
- The imputation system eliminates double taxation on fully franked dividends for Australian resident shareholders.
- Conduit foreign income is a key feature for Australian holding companies distributing foreign-sourced income to non-resident investors.
- Franking deficit tax applies where a company's franking account goes into deficit — designed to prevent "over-franking."

### 15.6 Interest Income (Received)

| Parameter | Detail | Legal Reference |
|---|---|---|
| **Assessability** | Fully assessable as statutory income at CIT rate (**30%** or **25%**) | ITAA 1997 §6-5 |
| **TOFA (Taxation of Financial Arrangements)** | Compulsory for certain taxpayers with financial assets **>$100M**; accrual/realisation methods available | Division 230 ITAA 1997 |
| **Tax-exempt interest** | Very limited — certain government securities for non-residents may be exempt | Specific provisions |
| **OID equivalent** | Accruals methodology under Division 230 applies to discount/premium on debt instruments | Division 230 ITAA 1997 |
| **Thin capitalisation** | Safe harbour: **60%** debt (1.5:1 DER); fixed ratio: **30% of EBITDA** | Division 820 ITAA 1997 |
| **Related-party interest** | Must be at arm's length under transfer pricing rules | Subdivision 815-B ITAA 1997 |

**Practical notes:**
- Division 230 TOFA is complex and applies compulsorily to large taxpayers; smaller taxpayers may elect in.
- The thin capitalisation rules were reformed in 2023-24 to introduce a fixed ratio test (30% of tax EBITDA) alongside the existing safe harbour.
- Denied interest deductions under thin capitalisation can be carried forward for up to 15 years.

## 16. Sector-Specific Revenue Taxes

> **Legal Basis**: Various Commonwealth and State/Territory legislation

### 16.1 Digital Services Taxation

| Parameter | Detail | Legal Reference |
|---|---|---|
| **Specific DST** | **Not enacted** — Australia opted for the OECD Pillar 1 approach rather than a unilateral DST | N/A |
| **GST on digital supplies by non-residents** | **10%** GST applies to digital products/services supplied to Australian consumers ("Netflix tax") | GST Act 1999 Division 83 |
| **Low-value imported goods** | Goods <AUD 1,000 subject to GST from **July 2018** | GST Act 1999 |
| **MAAL and DPT** | Target profit-shifting by digital multinationals through Australian operations | Division 177DA, Division 177J ITAA 1936 |
| **SGE reporting** | "Significant global entity" reporting required for entities with **$1B** global revenue | TAA §3CA |
| **Country-by-country reporting** | Required for SGEs — lodged with ATO, exchanged internationally | TAA Schedule 1, Subdivision 815-E ITAA 1997 |

**Practical notes:**
- Australia has been at the forefront of taxing digital economy participants through MAAL, DPT, and the GST on digital supplies.
- The ATO actively audits digital multinationals and has secured significant settlements from major technology companies.
- CbCR and local file requirements apply to SGEs regardless of whether they have a taxable presence in Australia.

### 16.2 Natural Resource / Extractive Taxes

| Parameter | Detail | Legal Reference |
|---|---|---|

| **PRRT transferability** | Expenditure transferable between projects in the same title area | PRRT Act 1987 |
| **WA mining royalties** | Iron ore **7.5%** FOB; gold **2.5%**; lithium **5%** | Mining Act 1978 (WA) |

| **SA mining royalties** | Copper/gold **3.5%** | Mining Act 1971 (SA) |

**Practical notes:**
- State mining royalties are **not** deductible for PRRT purposes but are deductible for income tax.
- The PRRT uplift mechanism means exploration expenditure compounds over time, creating substantial deductions against future profits.
- The Safeguard Mechanism requires facilities emitting >100,000 tonnes CO2-e to keep emissions below a baseline or purchase carbon credits.

- The bank levy applies only to the five largest banks (CBA, Westpac, ANZ, NAB, Macquarie) and raises approximately $1.6B annually.

| **WA casino tax** | **1-20%** (casino gaming only; no EGMs outside casino) | Casino Control Act 1984 (WA) |
| **Point of consumption (POC) tax** | **15%** on net wagering revenue in most states/territories | State wagering legislation |
| **Lottery taxes** | Vary by state — typically **60-80%** of revenue returned as prizes; operator taxed on remainder | State lottery legislation |
| **Online gambling** | Regulated under the Interactive Gambling Act 2001; offshore operators prohibited from offering services to Australians | Interactive Gambling Act 2001 |
| **Sports betting** | Major operators (Sportsbet, TAB) subject to state licensing + POC tax | State wagering/betting legislation |

**Practical notes:**
- Western Australia is unique in prohibiting electronic gaming machines outside the Crown Perth casino.
- The point of consumption tax was introduced to capture tax revenue from wagering operators based in low-tax jurisdictions (primarily the NT).
- Gaming tax rates are among the highest effective tax rates on any industry in Australia.

### 16.6 Agricultural Income Taxation

| Parameter | Detail | Legal Reference |
|---|---|---|
| **Farm Management Deposits (FMD)** | Defer up to **$800,000** of pre-tax income; deduction in year of deposit, assessable in year of withdrawal | Division 393 ITAA 1997 |
| **FMD holding period** | Must be held for **12 months** for full tax benefit | Division 393 ITAA 1997 |
| **Primary producer averaging** | Average taxable income over **5 years** to smooth tax burden from volatile farming income | Division 392 ITAA 1997 |
| **Water infrastructure** | Immediate deduction for expenditure on fencing and water facilities | §40-545, §40-555 ITAA 1997 |
| **Landcare operations** | Immediate deduction for landcare operations (soil conservation, prevention of land degradation) | §40-630 ITAA 1997 |
| **Horticultural plants** | Capital allowances based on effective life of plants | Division 40 ITAA 1997 |
| **Drought/disaster relief** | Tax-free government grants for natural disaster relief | §53-10 ITAA 1997 |
| **Forestry managed investment schemes** | **100%** deduction in year of planting for qualifying forestry schemes | Division 394 ITAA 1997 |
| **Livestock** | Cost base rules, natural increase, and forced disposal special rules (e.g., insurance election, replacement livestock) | §70-110 ITAA 1997 |

**Practical notes:**
- Farm Management Deposits are a key tax planning tool for primary producers, allowing income smoothing across good and bad years.
- Primary producer averaging only applies to individuals (not companies or trusts).
- The forced disposal provisions allow deferral of income when livestock are disposed of due to drought, flood, or other natural disasters.
- Forestry MIS deductions are only available where the scheme is registered with ASIC and meets specific requirements.
