# Australia - R&D Tax Incentive

## 1. Overview

| Feature | Details |
|---|---|
| **Primary Incentive** | R&D Tax Incentive (RDTI) |
| **SME Benefit** | 43.5% refundable tax offset (turnover < A$20M) |
| **Large Company Benefit** | 38.5% non-refundable offset + intensity premium |
| **Expenditure Cap** | A$150M per annum on notional deductions |
| **IP Regime** | Biotech patent box at 17% effective rate |
| **Governing Authority** | AusIndustry (DISR); Australian Taxation Office (ATO) |
| **Key Legislation** | Income Tax Assessment Act 1997 (ITAA 1997) Division 355; Industry Research and Development Act 1986 |
| **Corporate Tax Rate** | 30% (standard); 25% (base rate entities with turnover < A$50M) |

## 2. Detailed Programs

### 2.1 R&D Tax Incentive - ITAA 1997 Division 355

**Legal Basis:** Income Tax Assessment Act 1997, Division 355; Industry Research and Development Act 1986

#### SME Entities (Aggregated Turnover < A$20M)

| Parameter | Details |
|---|---|
| **Offset Rate** | 43.5% refundable tax offset (company tax rate + 18.5% premium) |
| **Refundability** | Fully refundable (cash back if in tax loss) |
| **Refund Cap** | A$4M cash refund per annum; excess carried forward |
| **Clawback** | Claw-back provisions apply on disposal of R&D results |
| **Expenditure Cap** | A$150M notional deduction cap |

#### Large Entities (Aggregated Turnover ≥ A$20M)

| Parameter | Details |
|---|---|
| **Base Offset Rate** | 38.5% non-refundable tax offset (company tax rate + 8.5% premium) |
| **Intensity Premium** | Additional offset for R&D intensity above baseline |
| **Refundability** | Non-refundable; excess carried forward as tax offset |
| **Expenditure Cap** | A$150M notional deduction cap |

**R&D Intensity Premium (Large Companies):**

| R&D Intensity | Marginal Offset Rate |
|---|---|
| 0% to 2% | Company tax rate + 8.5% |
| Above 2% | Company tax rate + 16.5% |

*R&D intensity = notional R&D deductions / total assessable income*

#### Qualifying Activities

**Core R&D Activities (§355-25):**
- Experimental activities conducted for generating new knowledge
- Outcome cannot be known or determined in advance on basis of current knowledge
- Must involve systematic progression of work based on principles of established science

**Supporting R&D Activities (§355-30):**
- Directly related to core R&D activities
- Must be undertaken for the dominant purpose of supporting core R&D

#### Qualifying Expenditure

| Category | Examples |
|---|---|
| **Salary & wages** | Employees directly engaged in R&D |
| **Contractor costs** | Payments to contractors performing R&D |
| **Consumables** | Materials consumed in R&D activities |
| **Depreciation** | Assets used in R&D (pro-rata basis) |
| **Overhead** | Utilities, rent attributable to R&D |

### 2.2 Biotech Patent Box

**Legal Basis:** Treasury Laws Amendment (Patent Box) Act 2022; ITAA 1997 Division 357 (proposed)

| Parameter | Details |
|---|---|
| **Effective Rate** | 17% on qualifying patent income |
| **Standard Rate** | 30% / 25% |
| **Qualifying Patents** | Patents granted on pharmaceutical and biotech innovations |
| **Nexus Requirement** | R&D must be conducted in Australia |
| **Effective Date** | Income years commencing on or after 1 July 2022 |
| **Status** | Enacted for medical/biotech; agricultural expansion announced |

### 2.3 Export Market Development Grant (EMDG)

| Parameter | Details |
|---|---|
| **Benefit** | Reimbursement up to 50% of eligible export promotion costs |
| **Relevance** | Complements R&D incentive for commercialization of IP offshore |
| **Administered by** | Austrade |

## 3. Patent Box / IP Regime

### Australia Biotech Patent Box - Division 357

| Parameter | Details |
|---|---|
| **Concessionary Rate** | 17% effective tax rate on qualifying patent income |
| **Tax Saving** | Up to 13 percentage points (from 30% to 17%) |
| **Qualifying IP** | Australian-granted patents for pharmaceutical and biotech innovations |
| **Qualifying Income** | Royalties, licensing fees, embedded patent income from product sales |
| **Nexus Approach** | Modified nexus approach aligned with OECD BEPS Action 5 |
| **R&D Requirement** | Underlying R&D must have been conducted substantially in Australia |
| **Interaction with RDTI** | Can claim both RDTI for R&D phase and patent box for commercialization |
| **Extension** | Agricultural sector patent box announced in 2023-24 Budget |

## 4. Effective Benefit Analysis (per $1M USD R&D Spend)

*Assuming AUD/USD exchange rate of 0.65; A$1,538,462 equivalent spend; R&D intensity above 2%*

| Scenario | Offset Rate | Gross Offset (AUD) | Less: Base Deduction (AUD) | Net Benefit (AUD) | Net Benefit (USD) |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| **SME (refundable)** | 43.5% | A$669,231 | A$461,538 (at 30%) | A$207,692 | ~$135,000 |
| **SME (25% base rate entity)** | 43.5% | A$669,231 | A$384,615 (at 25%) | A$284,615 | ~$185,000 |
| **Large (base intensity)** | 38.5% | A$592,308 | A$461,538 (at 30%) | A$130,769 | ~$85,000 |
| **Large (high intensity)** | 46.5% | A$715,385 | A$461,538 (at 30%) | A$253,846 | ~$165,000 |
| **Biotech Patent Box** | 17% effective | Varies by income | N/A | Up to 13% CIT saving | Varies |

**Key Insight:** SME refundable offset is particularly valuable for pre-revenue companies as it provides a direct cash refund, effectively subsidizing R&D by approximately 18.5 cents per dollar spent.

## 5. Claim Process & Documentation

### 5.1 Filing Process

| Step | Action | Timeline |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Conduct qualifying R&D activities | During income year |
| 2 | Register R&D activities with AusIndustry | Within 10 months after end of income year |
| 3 | Complete R&D Tax Incentive Schedule (Form) | With company tax return |
| 4 | Lodge company tax return with ATO | Standard lodgment due date |
| 5 | ATO processes offset/refund | 30-60 days after lodgment |

### 5.2 AusIndustry Registration

- **Mandatory:** Must register activities before claiming
- **Application:** Via AusIndustry online portal (R&DTI Customer Portal)
- **Deadline:** 10 months after end of the income year
- **Late registration:** Possible with ATO discretion but not guaranteed
- **Review:** AusIndustry may issue findings on eligibility of registered activities

### 5.3 Documentation Requirements

- **Project records:** Technical descriptions, hypotheses, experimental methodology
- **Activity records:** Evidence of systematic progression from hypothesis through experiment
- **Financial records:** Expenditure attributed to specific R&D activities
- **Time records:** Staff allocation records showing time on R&D activities
- **Contemporaneous records:** Created at or near the time the activities are conducted
- **Feedstock adjustment records:** Where R&D produces marketable products

### 5.4 Advance/Overseas Finding

- **Advance Finding:** Apply to AusIndustry for certainty on activity eligibility
- **Overseas Finding:** Required for R&D conducted overseas (max 50% of total R&D expenditure)

## 6. Recent Changes & Sunset Provisions

| Change | Effective Date | Details |
|---|---|---|
| **Intensity Premium** | 1 July 2021 | Replaced flat 38.5% for large entities with intensity-based premium |
| **A$150M Expenditure Cap** | 1 July 2021 | Replaced unlimited expenditure with A$150M cap |
| **A$4M Refund Cap** | 1 July 2021 | Cash refund capped at A$4M per annum for SMEs |
| **Biotech Patent Box** | 1 July 2022 | 17% rate for qualifying pharmaceutical/biotech patent income |
| **Clinical Trial Uplift** | 1 July 2022 | Additional support for clinical trials conducted in Australia |
| **Agricultural Patent Box** | Announced 2023-24 | Extension to agricultural innovations (legislation pending) |
| **OECD Pillar Two** | 1 January 2024 | 15% global minimum tax may reduce benefit of patent box for large MNEs |

**Sunset Provisions:**
- The R&D Tax Incentive has no sunset date; it is a permanent feature of the tax law
- The Biotech Patent Box is also enacted without a sunset clause
- Periodic government reviews (e.g., 2016 Ferris Review, 2023 Treasury Review) may lead to parameter changes
