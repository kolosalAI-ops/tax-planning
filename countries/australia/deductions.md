> **Jurisdiction**: Federal (Australian Taxation Office — ATO)
> **Governing Law**: Income Tax Assessment Act 1997 (ITAA 1997); Income Tax Assessment Act 1936 (ITAA 1936)
> **Tax Year**: 2024/2025 (FY2025)
> **Currency**: AUD (A$)

## 1. Depreciation & Capital Allowances

### 1.1 Uniform Capital Allowance — Decline in Value (Division 40, ITAA 1997)

> **Legal Basis**: ITAA 1997 Division 40

#### Methods Available

| Method | Description | Legal Reference |
|---|---|---|
| **Diminishing Value** | (Base value x (days held / 365)) x (200% / effective life) | ITAA 1997 §40-105 |
| **Prime Cost** | (Cost x (days held / 365)) x (100% / effective life) | ITAA 1997 §40-100 |

> Taxpayer can choose either method for each asset. Once chosen, method cannot be changed for that asset.

> **Legal Basis**: ITAA 1997 Division 70; §25-100

## 3. Interest & Financing Costs

### 3.1 General Rules (§8-1)

> **Legal Basis**: ITAA 1997 §8-1

| Parameter | Value |
|---|---|
| Basic rule | Interest deductible if borrowed money used to produce assessable income or for business purposes |
| Prepaid interest | Generally deductible over the period to which it relates (12-month rule for SBEs) |
| Capitalised interest | Deductible if underlying borrowing is deductible |

### 3.2 Thin Capitalisation Rules (Division 820)

> **Legal Basis**: ITAA 1997 Division 820

#### Old Rules (pre-1 July 2023)

| Parameter | Value |
|---|---|
| Safe harbour debt amount | **60%** of adjusted average assets (inward investing entities) — §820-37 |
| Outward investing entities | 60% of adjusted average assets |
| Financial entities | **6:1** debt-to-equity ratio (effective 85.71% of assets) |
| De minimis threshold | **A$2 million** in debt deductions (entities below exempt) |
| Arm's length test | Alternative to safe harbour — prove actual debt is arm's length |
| Worldwide gearing ratio | Alternative test — Australian gearing ≤ 100% of worldwide gearing |

#### New Rules from 1 July 2023 (Treasury Laws Amendment — Making Multinationals Pay Their Fair Share)

> **Legal Basis**: ITAA 1997 §820-46 (applicable to income years commencing on or after 1 July 2023)

| Parameter | Value |
|---|---|
| Fixed ratio test | **30%** of tax EBITDA — §820-46 |
| Group ratio test | Can use worldwide group's net interest-to-EBITDA ratio (if higher) |
| Third-party debt test | For entities funded primarily by third-party debt (conditions apply) |
| Tax EBITDA calculation | Taxable income + net debt deductions + Div 40 (depreciation) + Div 43 (capital works) deductions |
| Carry-forward of denied deductions | **15 years** |
| De minimis | A$2 million of net debt deductions |

### 3.3 Debt Deduction Creation Rules

> **Legal Basis**: ITAA 1997 §820-423A (from 1 July 2023)

Anti-avoidance rules deny deductions for debt used in certain related-party schemes designed to shift profits out of Australia. The rules target arrangements where debt deductions arise from schemes with a principal purpose of obtaining an Australian tax benefit, including cross-border related-party financing structures lacking economic substance.

## 4. Research & Development

Australia's R&D Tax Incentive (Division 355, ITAA 1997) provides a refundable tax offset of the company tax rate plus 18.5% for entities with aggregated turnover under A$20M, and a non-refundable offset of the company tax rate plus 8.5% (or 16.5% at intensity above 2%) for larger entities. The R&D must be registered with the Department of Industry, Science and Resources.

See [R&D Incentives](rnd_incentives.md) for full program detail.

## 5. Loss Carry-Forward & Carry-Back

### 5.1 Revenue Losses (Non-Capital)

> **Legal Basis**: ITAA 1997 Division 36

| Parameter | Companies | Trusts |
|---|---|---|
| Carry-forward | **Indefinite** | **Indefinite** |
| Carry-back | **Yes** (see below) | Not available |
| Continuity of Ownership Test (COT) | Same persons hold majority voting/dividend/capital rights throughout ownership test period | N/A |
| Business Continuity Test (BCT) | Same business test if COT fails — must carry on same business, no new business | Income injection test |
| Similar Business Test (SBT) | Extended test available from 2015 — similar business sufficient | |

### 5.2 Loss Carry-Back (Companies Only)

> **Legal Basis**: ITAA 1997 Division 160 (§160-5)

| Parameter | Value |
|---|---|
| Available for | Companies (not trusts, partnerships, or individuals) |
| Carry-back period | **1 year** (to the immediately prior income year) |
| Maximum offset | Prior year tax liability (cannot generate refund beyond prior tax paid) |
| Eligible losses | 2019–20 and later income year losses |
| Franking account impact | Reduces franking account balance |
| Aggregated turnover requirement | **< A$5 billion** |

### 5.3 Capital Losses

| Parameter | Value |
|---|---|
| Carry-forward | **Indefinite** |
| Carry-back | **Not allowed** |
| Usage | Can only offset capital gains (never ordinary income) |
| Application order | Current year capital losses first, then prior year losses (oldest first) |
| COT/BCT | Same rules apply as revenue losses |

## 8. Treaties & Transfer Pricing

### 8.1 Transfer Pricing (Division 815)

> **Legal Basis**: ITAA 1997 Division 815

#### Documentation Requirements

| Document | Required For | Content |
|---|---|---|
| **Local File** | Entities with cross-border related-party dealings | Detailed transaction analysis, benchmarking |
| **Master File** | Part of a group with CbCR obligations | Group structure, business descriptions, TP policies |
| **Country-by-Country Report** | Significant Global Entities (global income ≥ A$1 billion) | Revenue, profit, tax, employees by jurisdiction |
| Simplified record keeping | Small entities (< A$2M related-party dealings) | Simplified documentation |

#### Methods Accepted

1. Comparable Uncontrolled Price (CUP)
2. Resale Price Method (RPM)
3. Cost Plus Method (CPM)
4. Transactional Net Margin Method (TNMM)
5. Transactional Profit Split Method (PSM)

#### Penalties for Non-Compliance

| Scenario | Penalty |
|---|---|
| TP adjustment (reasonably arguable position) | **25%** of tax shortfall |
| TP adjustment (no reasonably arguable position) | **50%** of tax shortfall |
| Failure to lodge CbCR | Up to **A$782,500** (per report) |
| Scheme penalties (Part IVA + DPT) | Up to **100%** of tax shortfall |

### 8.2 Diverted Profits Tax (DPT)

> **Legal Basis**: ITAA 1997 Division 177J (from 1 July 2017)

| Parameter | Value |
|---|---|
| Rate | **40%** penalty rate |
| Applies to | Significant Global Entities (income ≥ A$1 billion) with diverted profits |
| Assessment | Commissioner may issue DPT assessment where arrangements lack economic substance |

### 8.3 Treaty Network

Australia has over 45 comprehensive double tax agreements. Treaty relief generally reduces withholding taxes on dividends, interest, and royalties paid to non-residents.
