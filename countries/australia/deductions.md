# Australia - Taxable Income Deductions

> **Jurisdiction**: Australian Taxation Office (ATO)
> **Governing Law**: Income Tax Assessment Act 1997 (ITAA 1997), Income Tax Assessment Act 1936 (ITAA 1936)
> **Tax Year**: 2024-25 (July 1, 2024 - June 30, 2025)
> **Currency**: Australian Dollar (AUD / A$)

## 1. Capital Allowances - Decline in Value (Division 40, ITAA 1997)

> **Legal Basis**: ITAA 1997 Division 40 (Capital allowances - decline in value)

### Methods Available

| Method | Description | Legal Reference |
|---|---|---|
| **Diminishing Value** | (Base value × (days held / 365)) × (200% / effective life) | ITAA 1997 Section 40-105 |
| **Prime Cost** | (Cost × (days held / 365)) × (100% / effective life) | ITAA 1997 Section 40-100 |

> Taxpayer can choose either method for each asset. Once chosen, method cannot be changed for that asset.

## 2. Small Business Depreciation Concessions (Division 328)

> **Legal Basis**: ITAA 1997 Division 328 (Small business entity concessions)

### Eligibility

| Parameter | Value |
|---|---|
| Aggregated turnover threshold | **< $10 million** (ITAA 1997 Section 328-110) |
| Must be a small business entity | Yes (ITAA 1997 Division 328) |

### Instant Asset Write-Off

> **Legal Basis**: ITAA 1997 Section 328-180

| Parameter | 2024-25 Value |
|---|---|
| Threshold per asset | **$20,000** (each asset costing less than this) (ITAA 1997 Section 328-180) |
| Eligible assets | New and used depreciating assets |
| Application | Each asset assessed individually |
| Effective period | July 1, 2024 - June 30, 2025 (subject to extension) |
| Previous thresholds | $150,000 (COVID), $30,000 (2019-20), $20,000 (2024-25) |

### Small Business Pool

> **Legal Basis**: ITAA 1997 Section 328-175 (General small business pool)

| Parameter | Value |
|---|---|
| Pool depreciation rate (general) | **15%** in first year, **30%** thereafter (ITAA 1997 Section 328-175) |
| Closing pool balance <$20,000 | **Write off entirely** |
| Applies to | All assets not individually written off |

### Temporary Full Expensing (Ended June 30, 2023)

| Parameter | Value |
|---|---|
| Status | **Expired** - was available from Oct 6, 2020 to June 30, 2023 |
| Benefit | 100% deduction in year of first use/installation |
| Eligible | Businesses with aggregated turnover < $5 billion |
| Assets | New depreciating assets (and second-hand for turnover < $50M) |

## 3. Loss Rules

> **Legal Basis**: ITAA 1997 Division 36 (Tax losses - carry-forward, indefinite)

### Revenue Losses (Non-Capital)

| Parameter | Companies | Trusts |
|---|---|---|
| Carry-forward | **Indefinite** (ITAA 1997 Division 36) | **Indefinite** |
| Carry-back | **Yes** (see below) | Not available |
| Continuity of Ownership Test (COT) | Same persons hold majority voting/dividend/capital rights throughout ownership test period | N/A |
| Business Continuity Test (BCT) | Same business test if COT fails - must carry on same business, no new business | Income injection test |
| Similar Business Test (SBT) | Extended test available from 2015 - similar business sufficient | |

### Loss Carry-Back (Companies Only)

> **Legal Basis**: ITAA 1997 Division 160 (Loss carry-back tax offset); ITAA 1997 Section 160-5 (Eligibility - turnover < $5 billion)

| Parameter | Value |
|---|---|
| Available for | Companies (not trusts, partnerships, or individuals) (ITAA 1997 Division 160) |
| Carry-back period | **1 year** (to the immediately prior income year) |
| Maximum offset | Prior year tax liability (cannot generate refund beyond prior tax paid) |
| Eligible losses | 2019-20 and later income year losses |
| Franking account impact | Reduces franking account balance |
| Aggregated turnover requirement | **< $5 billion** (ITAA 1997 Section 160-5) |

### Capital Losses

| Parameter | Value |
|---|---|
| Carry-forward | **Indefinite** |
| Carry-back | **Not allowed** |
| Usage | Can only offset capital gains (never ordinary income) |
| Application order | Current year capital losses first, then prior year losses (oldest first) |
| COT/BCT | Same rules apply as revenue losses |

## 4. Interest Deductibility

### General Rules (Section 8-1)

> **Legal Basis**: ITAA 1997 Section 8-1 (General deduction provision)

| Parameter | Value |
|---|---|
| Basic rule | Interest deductible if borrowed money used to produce assessable income or for business purposes (ITAA 1997 Section 8-1) |
| Prepaid interest | Generally deductible over the period to which it relates (12-month rule for SBEs) |
| Capitalised interest | Deductible if underlying borrowing is deductible |

### Thin Capitalisation Rules (Division 820)

> **Legal Basis**: ITAA 1997 Division 820 (Thin capitalisation); ITAA 1997 Section 820-37 (Safe harbour debt test - 60% of assets, old rule)

| Parameter | Value |
|---|---|
| Safe harbour debt amount | **60%** of adjusted average assets (inward investing entities) (ITAA 1997 Section 820-37, old rule) |
| Outward investing entities | 60% of adjusted average assets |
| Financial entities | **6:1** debt-to-equity ratio (effective 85.71% of assets) |
| De minimis threshold | **$2 million** in debt deductions (entities below exempt) |
| Arm's length test | Alternative to safe harbour - prove actual debt is arm's length |
| Worldwide gearing ratio | Alternative test - Australian gearing ≤ 100% of worldwide gearing |

### New Rules from July 1, 2024 (Treasury Laws Amendment)

> **Legal Basis**: ITAA 1997 Section 820-46 (Fixed ratio test - 30% EBITDA, new from 1 July 2024)

| Parameter | Value |
|---|---|
| Fixed ratio test | **30%** of tax EBITDA (ITAA 1997 Section 820-46) |
| Group ratio test | Can use worldwide group's net interest-to-EBITDA ratio (if higher) |
| External debt test | For outward investors using external debt funding |
| Third-party debt exclusion | Pure third-party debt may be excluded (conditions apply) |
| Carry-forward of denied deductions | **15 years** |
| De minimis | $2 million of net interest deductions |

## 7. R&D Tax Incentive Deductions (Division 355)

> **Legal Basis**: ITAA 1997 Division 355 (R&D tax incentive); ITAA 1997 Section 355-100 (R&D entities)

### Eligible R&D Expenditure

| Expenditure Type | Treatment |
|---|---|
| R&D salaries (employees) | **100%** base deduction + R&D tax offset |
| R&D contractor payments | **100%** base deduction + offset (on amounts paid to Australian entities) |
| R&D materials consumed | **100%** deductible |
| R&D plant depreciation | Normal CCA + R&D offset rate applied |
| R&D overhead (proportion) | Deductible to extent directly related |
| Feedstock adjustments | Reduce R&D deduction by market value of output produced |

### Offset Rates (Recap)

| Turnover | Rate |
|---|---|
| < $20M (refundable) | Company tax rate + **18.5%** |
| ≥ $20M (non-refundable, base) | Company tax rate + **8.5%** |
| ≥ $20M (non-refundable, intensity >2%) | Company tax rate + **16.5%** |

## 8. Charitable Donations (Division 30)

> **Legal Basis**: ITAA 1997 Division 30 (Gifts and contributions); ITAA 1997 Section 30-15 (Deductible gift recipients - DGR)

### Deductible Gift Recipients (DGR)

| Parameter | Value |
|---|---|
| Minimum gift | **$2** (ITAA 1997 Section 30-15) |
| Maximum deduction | No cap (companies can deduct up to 100% of taxable income) |
| Carry-forward | **5 years** (or as specified for particular funds) |
| DGR requirement | Recipient must be endorsed as DGR by ATO (ITAA 1997 Section 30-15) |
| Valuation (property gifts >$5,000) | Must have qualified independent valuation |
| Gifts of property | Deductible at market value (may trigger CGT) |

### Eligible Gift Types

| Gift Type | Deductibility |
|---|---|
| Cash donations | **100%** deductible (to DGR) |
| Property gifts (valued >$5,000) | Market value; independent valuation needed |
| Trading stock | Cost or market value (taxpayer's choice) |
| Cultural gifts (under Cultural Gifts Program) | **100%** deductible; exempt from CGT |
| Environmental property | **100%** deductible under specific provisions |
| Political contributions | **Not deductible** for companies (from 2008-09) |

## 14. Transfer Pricing (Division 815)

> **Legal Basis**: ITAA 1997 Division 815 (Transfer pricing - arm's length principle)

### Documentation Requirements

| Document | Required For | Content |
|---|---|---|
| **Local File** | Entities with cross-border related-party dealings | Detailed transaction analysis, benchmarking |
| **Master File** | Part of a group with CbCR obligations | Group structure, business descriptions, TP policies |
| **Country-by-Country Report** | Significant Global Entities (global income ≥A$1 billion) | Revenue, profit, tax, employees by jurisdiction |
| Simplified record keeping | Small entities (< $2M related-party dealings) | Simplified documentation |

### Methods Accepted

1. Comparable Uncontrolled Price (CUP)
2. Resale Price Method (RPM)
3. Cost Plus Method (CPM)
4. Transactional Net Margin Method (TNMM)
5. Transactional Profit Split Method (PSM)

### Penalties for Non-Compliance

| Scenario | Penalty |
|---|---|
| Transfer pricing adjustment (reasonably arguable position) | **25%** of tax shortfall |
| Transfer pricing adjustment (no reasonably arguable position) | **50%** of tax shortfall |
| Failure to lodge CbCR | Up to **$782,500** (per report) |
| Scheme penalties (Part IVA + DPT) | Up to **100%** of tax shortfall |

## 15. Primary Producer Concessions

### Farm Management Deposits (FMD)

| Parameter | Value |
|---|---|
| Maximum deposit | **$800,000** |
| Deductibility | **100%** deductible in year of deposit |
| Withdrawal | Assessable in year of withdrawal |
| Minimum holding | **12 months** to maintain deduction |
| Eligibility | Primary producers with non-primary production income ≤ $100,000 |

### Averaging for Primary Producers

| Parameter | Value |
|---|---|
| Available to | Individuals who are primary producers |
| Mechanism | Tax calculated on average of current year and up to 4 prior years' taxable income |
| Benefit | Smooths income tax liability in volatile income years |
| Tax offset | If average income < current income, tax saving applied |

### Water Infrastructure Improvement

| Parameter | Value |
|---|---|
| Deduction rate | **100%** immediate deduction |
| Eligible expenditure | Capital expenditure on water facilities used in primary production |
| Includes | Dams, bores, irrigation channels, pumps, water storage |

### Horticulture/Forestry

| Parameter | Value |
|---|---|
| Establishment costs | Deductible over the period of the plantation |
| Carbon sink forests | Deductible over **14 years** |
