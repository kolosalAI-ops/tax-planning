# Malaysia - R&D Tax Incentives

## 1. Overview

| Feature | Details |
|---|---|
| **Primary Incentive** | Double Deduction (200%) for R&D expenditure |
| **Pioneer Status** | 100% income tax exemption for 5-10 years |
| **Investment Tax Allowance** | 60% on qualifying capital expenditure |
| **Governing Authority** | Inland Revenue Board (LHDN); Malaysian Investment Development Authority (MIDA); Ministry of Science, Technology and Innovation (MOSTI) |
| **Key Legislation** | Income Tax Act 1967 (ITA 1967) Schedule 4B; Promotion of Investments Act 1986 (PIA 1986) |
| **Corporate Tax Rate** | 24% (standard); 17% on first RM 600,000 for SMEs |

## 2. Detailed Programs

### 2.1 Double Deduction for R&D - ITA 1967 Schedule 4B

**Legal Basis:** Income Tax Act 1967, Section 34A read with Schedule 4B

| Parameter | Details |
|---|---|
| **Deduction Rate** | 200% of qualifying R&D expenditure |
| **Base Deduction** | 100% (standard expense deduction) |
| **Additional Deduction** | 100% (double deduction component) |
| **Total Deduction** | 200% of qualifying R&D expenditure |
| **Approval** | Research must be approved by MOSTI or conducted at approved research institutions |
| **Location** | R&D must be conducted in Malaysia |

**Qualifying R&D Activities:**
- Scientific or technological research related to the company's business
- Research for discovering new knowledge
- Product or process improvement research
- Must not be market research, quality control, or routine testing

**Qualifying Expenditure:**

| Category | Deduction |
|---|---|
| **Revenue expenditure** | 200% double deduction |
| **Capital expenditure** | Standard capital allowance (not doubled) |
| **Payments to approved research institutions** | 200% double deduction |
| **R&D company payments** | 200% for payments to MOSTI-approved R&D companies |
| **Cash contributions** | 200% for contributions to approved research institutes |

### 2.2 Pioneer Status - PIA 1986

**Legal Basis:** Promotion of Investments Act 1986, Sections 5-21

| Parameter | Details |
|---|---|
| **Tax Exemption** | 100% of statutory income |
| **Duration** | 5 years (standard); up to 10 years (strategic/high-tech) |
| **Exemption Scope** | Income from promoted product or activity |
| **Unabsorbed Losses** | Carried forward post-pioneer period |
| **Capital Allowance** | Unabsorbed capital allowances carried forward |
| **Application** | Through MIDA |

**Enhanced Pioneer Status:**

| Category | Exemption Rate | Duration |
|---|---|---|
| **High Technology** | 100% | 5+5 years |
| **Strategic Projects** | 100% | 10 years |
| **Multimedia Super Corridor (MSC)** | 100% | 5+5 years |
| **BioNexus Status** | 100% | 10 years |
| **Green Technology** | 70-100% | 5-10 years |

### 2.3 Investment Tax Allowance (ITA) - PIA 1986

**Legal Basis:** Promotion of Investments Act 1986, Sections 26-29

| Parameter | Details |
|---|---|
| **Allowance Rate** | 60% on qualifying capital expenditure |
| **Set-Off** | Against 70% of statutory income |
| **Duration** | 5 years from first qualifying expenditure |
| **Alternative to Pioneer** | Companies choose either Pioneer Status OR ITA (not both) |
| **Qualifying Expenditure** | Factory, plant, machinery, equipment for promoted activity |

**Enhanced ITA Rates:**

| Category | Allowance Rate | Set-Off Rate |
|---|---|---|
| **High Technology** | 60% | 100% of statutory income |
| **Strategic Projects** | 100% | 100% of statutory income |
| **MSC Status** | 100% | 100% of statutory income |
| **R&D Companies** | 100% | 70% of statutory income |

### 2.4 MSC Malaysia Status (for ICT/Digital Companies)

**Legal Basis:** MSC Malaysia Bill of Guarantees; MIDA administration

| Parameter | Details |
|---|---|
| **Pioneer Status** | 100% income tax exemption for 5+5 years |
| **Alternative: ITA** | 100% ITA on qualifying expenditure |
| **Knowledge Workers** | Flat 15% personal tax rate for qualifying knowledge workers |
| **Duty Exemption** | Import duty exemption on multimedia equipment |
| **Eligibility** | ICT, software development, digital content, shared services |

### 2.5 R&D Status Company Incentives

| Parameter | Details |
|---|---|
| **Contract R&D Company** | ITA of 100% on qualifying capex, set-off against 70% of statutory income |
| **R&D Company (Own Products)** | Pioneer Status 100% for 5 years or ITA 100% |
| **Approved Research Institute** | Income tax exemption on income from R&D activities |
| **MOSTI Approval** | Required for R&D company status |

## 3. Patent Box / IP Regime

Malaysia does not have a formal patent box regime.

| Aspect | Status |
|---|---|
| **Patent Box** | Not available |
| **IP Amortization** | Capital allowance over useful life per ITA 1967 Schedule 3 |
| **IP Acquisition** | Qualifying plant expenditure may attract capital allowance |
| **Royalty WHT** | 10% on payments to non-residents (reduced under treaties) |
| **Labuan IBFC** | Labuan entities may benefit from 3% preferential rate on IP trading income (subject to substance requirements) |
| **Alternative** | Pioneer Status and ITA provide effective IP development support |

**Labuan International Business and Financial Centre (IBFC):**

| Parameter | Details |
|---|---|
| **Trading Income** | 3% of net audited profit or RM 20,000 flat (election) |
| **Non-Trading Income** | 0% (exempt) |
| **Substance Requirements** | Must meet minimum number of employees and operating expenditure in Labuan |
| **IP Relevance** | IP licensing income may qualify as trading income at 3% |
| **OECD Compliance** | Subject to EU/OECD scrutiny on substance requirements |

## 4. Effective Benefit Analysis (per $1M USD R&D Spend)

*Assuming MYR/USD exchange rate of 4.5; RM 4.5M equivalent spend; 24% CIT rate*

| Scenario | Treatment | Tax Saving (MYR) | Tax Saving (USD) |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Double Deduction (200%)** | RM 4.5M × 200% × 24% | RM 2.16M (total); RM 1.08M (additional) | ~$240,000 additional |
| **Pioneer Status (100% exempt)** | Full income exemption | Full CIT saving on promoted income | Up to full CIT |
| **ITA (60% standard)** | RM 4.5M capex × 60% | RM 2.7M allowance × 24% = RM 648K | ~$144,000 |
| **ITA (100% enhanced)** | RM 4.5M capex × 100% | RM 4.5M allowance × 24% = RM 1.08M | ~$240,000 |
| **Labuan (3%)** | 3% on IP trading income | 21% rate differential | Varies by income |

**Key Insight:** Pioneer Status offers the most valuable incentive for R&D-intensive companies as it provides complete income tax exemption on promoted activities. However, it requires MIDA approval and commitment to promoted activities. Double deduction is available without the same restrictions but provides a lower benefit.

## 5. Claim Process & Documentation

### 5.1 Double Deduction Claim Process

| Step | Action | Timeline |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Ensure R&D is approved by MOSTI or conducted at approved institution | Before claiming |
| 2 | Maintain separate R&D expenditure records | Ongoing |
| 3 | Obtain MOSTI approval letter for in-house R&D | Before or during R&D |
| 4 | Claim double deduction in annual tax return | Within 7 months of year-end |
| 5 | Retain documentation for audit | Minimum 7 years |

### 5.2 Pioneer Status / ITA Application

| Step | Action | Timeline |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Determine eligibility and select Pioneer or ITA | Before applying |
| 2 | Submit application to MIDA | Before commencement of promoted activity |
| 3 | MIDA evaluation and recommendation | 3-6 months |
| 4 | Minister of Finance approval | Following MIDA recommendation |
| 5 | Compliance with promotion conditions | Throughout incentive period |

### 5.3 Documentation Requirements

- **MOSTI approval:** R&D project approval letter
- **MIDA promotion certificate:** For Pioneer Status or ITA
- **R&D project records:** Technical descriptions, objectives, outcomes
- **Expenditure records:** Detailed breakdown of qualifying costs
- **Personnel records:** R&D staff qualifications and allocation
- **Financial statements:** Audited accounts with R&D expenditure disclosure
- **Capital allowance schedules:** For ITA claims
- **Compliance reports:** Annual reporting to MIDA on promoted activities

## 6. Recent Changes & Sunset Provisions

| Change | Effective Date | Details |
|---|---|---|
| **National Investment Aspirations (NIA)** | 2023 | New investment policy framework; revised MIDA incentive criteria |
| **New Industrial Master Plan 2030** | 2023 | Focus on high-value manufacturing and technology |
| **Global Minimum Tax** | Under review | Malaysia studying OECD Pillar Two implementation |
| **Green Technology Incentives** | 2024 | Enhanced Pioneer/ITA for green technology R&D |
| **Digital Economy Incentives** | 2024 | Additional incentives for AI, cloud computing, data centers |
| **MIDA Process Streamlining** | 2024 | Simplified application for R&D-related incentives |

**Sunset Provisions:**
- Double deduction: Permanent feature of ITA 1967; no sunset clause
- Pioneer Status/ITA: Permanent features of PIA 1986; specific application windows may apply
- MSC Malaysia Status: Under review as Malaysia transitions to digital economy framework
- Labuan IBFC: Subject to ongoing OECD/EU review of substance requirements
- Budget 2025-2026 may introduce patent box aligned with OECD guidelines
