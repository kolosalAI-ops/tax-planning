# Malaysia - Taxable Income Deductions

> **Jurisdiction**: National (Inland Revenue Board — LHDN / IRB)
> **Governing Law**: Income Tax Act 1967 (ITA 1967)
> **Tax Year**: 2024/2025
> **Currency**: MYR (RM)

---

## 1. Depreciation & Capital Allowances

### 1.1 General Rules

> **Legal Basis**: ITA 1967 Schedule 3

Malaysia does not allow accounting depreciation as a tax deduction. Instead, **Capital Allowances (CA)** are claimed on qualifying capital expenditure on plant and machinery used for business purposes.

#### Capital Allowance Rates (Schedule 3, ITA 1967)

| Asset Category | Initial Allowance (IA) | Annual Allowance (AA) | Effective Write-Off |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Industrial buildings** | **10%** | **3%** | ~30 years |
| **Plant and machinery (general)** | **20%** | **14%** | ~6 years |
| **Heavy machinery** | **20%** | **20%** | ~4.5 years |
| **Motor vehicles** | **20%** | **20%** | ~4.5 years |
| **Motor vehicles (on-the-road price cap: RM150,000)** | **20%** | **20%** | Cap on qualifying expenditure |
| **Office equipment** | **20%** | **10%** | ~8 years |
| **Furniture and fittings** | **20%** | **10%** | ~8 years |
| **Computers and IT equipment** | **20%** | **40%** | ~2.5 years |
| **Software (purchased)** | **20%** | **40%** | ~2.5 years |
| **Environmental protection equipment** | **40%** | **20%** | ~3 years |
| **Small value assets (≤ RM2,000 each)** | **100%** | N/A | Immediate (capped at RM20,000/year total; uncapped for SMEs) |

### 1.2 Accelerated Capital Allowance (ACA)

> **Legal Basis**: Various Orders under ITA 1967

| Incentive | IA | AA | Effective |
|---|---|---|---|
| **ICT equipment / software** | **20%** | **80%** | 1 year |
| **Environmental control equipment** | **40%** | **60%** | 1 year |
| **Automation equipment (manufacturing)** | **20%** | **80%** | 1 year |
| **ACA for digital technology adoption (2025–2026)** | **40%** | **60%** | 1 year [unverified — confirm effective dates with local counsel] |

### 1.3 Buildings — Industrial Building Allowance (IBA)

> **Legal Basis**: ITA 1967 Schedule 3, Para 37–41

| Parameter | Value |
|---|---|
| Qualifying buildings | Factories, docks, warehouses, research buildings |
| Initial allowance | **10%** of qualifying building expenditure |
| Annual allowance | **3%** |
| Non-qualifying | Office buildings, showrooms, residential buildings |

### 1.4 Agriculture Allowances

> **Legal Basis**: ITA 1967 Schedule 3, Para 5–8

| Item | IA | AA |
|---|---|---|
| Land clearing and preparation | **50%** | **10%** |
| Planting expenditure | **50%** | **10%** |
| Construction of access roads | **50%** | **10%** |

## 2. Business Operating Expenses

### 2.1 General Deductibility

> **Legal Basis**: ITA 1967 §33(1)

Expenses must be **wholly and exclusively** incurred in the production of income to be deductible.

| Expense Category | Deductibility | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Salaries, wages, bonuses | **100%** | Must be for services rendered |
| EPF employer contribution | **100%** | Statutory rate (12%–13% of wages) |
| SOCSO employer contribution | **100%** | |
| EIS employer contribution | **100%** | Employment Insurance System |
| Rent and lease payments | **100%** | Business premises |
| Repairs and maintenance | **100%** | Revenue nature; improvements are capital |
| Insurance premiums (business) | **100%** | |
| Legal and professional fees | **100%** | Revenue nature |
| Advertising and marketing | **100%** | |
| Travel expenses (business) | **100%** | With documentation |
| Bad debts written off | **100%** | Specific debts; must have been included in income — §34(2) |
| Entertainment expenses | **50%** | General entertainment — §33(1) proviso |

### 2.2 Entertainment Expenses

> **Legal Basis**: ITA 1967 §33(1) proviso

| Type | Deductibility |
|---|---|
| Entertainment (general) | **50%** deductible |
| Promotional entertainment (gifts with company logo ≤ RM500) | **100%** deductible |
| Entertainment for staff events (annual dinner, etc.) | **100%** deductible |
| Entertainment for product launches (open to public) | **100%** deductible |
| Entertainment for foreign buyers/trade delegations | **100%** deductible |

### 2.3 Charitable Donations

> **Legal Basis**: ITA 1967 §44(6); §44(11C)

| Parameter | Value |
|---|---|
| Cash donations to approved institutions | Deductible up to **10%** of aggregate income |
| Donations to government / approved bodies | **100%** deductible (no cap) |
| Artefacts, manuscripts, paintings to government | **100%** deductible (approved value) |

## 3. Interest & Financing Costs

### 3.1 General Deductibility

> **Legal Basis**: ITA 1967 §33(1)

| Parameter | Value |
|---|---|
| Business borrowings | Interest deductible if wholly and exclusively for income production |
| Capitalisation | Interest on construction-period borrowings may be capitalised |
| Non-business loans | Interest non-deductible |

### 3.2 Thin Capitalisation

> **Legal Basis**: No formal thin capitalisation statute

| Parameter | Value |
|---|---|
| Formal DER rule | **None** — Malaysia has no statutory thin-cap ratio |
| Transfer pricing | Related-party interest must be at arm's length — §140A |
| IRB guidelines | IRB may challenge excessive related-party debt under TP rules |
| Labuan entities | Different rules apply for transactions with Labuan entities |

### 3.3 Interest Restriction Rule (§140C)

> **Legal Basis**: ITA 1967 §140C (effective from YA 2019)

| Parameter | Value |
|---|---|
| Application | Financial assistance between associated persons |
| Rule | Deduction denied if interest is not at arm's length or if the financial arrangement is excessive |
| Interaction | Works alongside §140A transfer pricing rules |

## 4. Research & Development

Malaysia provides a double deduction for qualifying R&D expenditure under ITA 1967 §34A, with conditions including R&D being approved by and conducted in Malaysia. Companies may also claim 100% deduction for contract R&D payments to approved research institutes. The new National Technology and Innovation Sandbox provides enhanced incentives for qualifying technology companies.

See [R&D Incentives](rnd_incentives.md) for full program detail.

## 5. Loss Carry-Forward & Carry-Back

### 5.1 Loss Carry-Forward

> **Legal Basis**: ITA 1967 §44(2)

| Parameter | Value |
|---|---|
| Duration | **7 consecutive years** of assessment (from YA 2019 onward) |
| Previous rule | Indefinite carry-forward (for losses incurred before YA 2019) |
| Carry-back | **Not available** |
| Limitation | 100% of income from the same business source |
| Dormant company | Losses forfeited if company is dormant for certain periods |

### 5.2 Capital Allowance Carry-Forward

> **Legal Basis**: ITA 1967 Schedule 3

| Parameter | Value |
|---|---|
| Unabsorbed CA | Carried forward **indefinitely** (no time limit) |
| Set-off | Against income from the same business source |

### 5.3 Group Relief

> **Legal Basis**: ITA 1967 §44A

| Parameter | Value |
|---|---|
| Available | Yes, for Malaysian-incorporated companies |
| Ownership | ≥ 70% direct holding |
| Losses transferable | Up to 70% of current-year adjusted loss |
| Conditions | Both companies must have same accounting period; 12-month minimum operation |

## 6. Provisions & Reserves

| Provision Type | Deductibility | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Specific bad debts written off | **Deductible** | Must be specific and previously included in income — §34(2) |
| General bad debt provisions | **Not deductible** | Until specifically identified and written off |
| Employee leave provisions | **Deductible** when obligation crystallises | Based on contractual entitlement |
| Warranty provisions | **Not deductible** until paid | Contingent liability |
| Insurance reserves | **Deductible** | For licensed insurers per industry-specific rules |
| Stock obsolescence provisions | **Not deductible** | Until stock is disposed of or destroyed |

## 7. Industry-Specific Deductions

### 7.1 Reinvestment Allowance (RA)

> **Legal Basis**: ITA 1967 Schedule 7A

| Parameter | Value |
|---|---|
| Rate | **60%** of qualifying capital expenditure (QCE) |
| Utilisation | Against **70%** of statutory income from the qualifying project |
| Duration | **15 consecutive years** from first claim year |
| Eligible activities | Expansion, modernisation, automation, diversification of existing manufacturing/agricultural business |
| Minimum operation period | Company must have been in operation for ≥ 36 months |

### 7.2 Pioneer Status / Investment Tax Allowance

> **Legal Basis**: Promotion of Investments Act 1986 (PIA 1986)

| Incentive | Benefit |
|---|---|
| Pioneer Status | 70%–100% tax exemption on statutory income for 5–10 years |
| Investment Tax Allowance (ITA) | 60%–100% of QCE to set off against 70%–100% of statutory income for 5–10 years |
| New key industries | Enhanced 100% exemption for qualifying sectors |

### 7.3 Principal Hub Incentive

| Parameter | Value |
|---|---|
| CIT rate | **0%** (Tier 1) or **5%** (Tier 2) or **10%** (Tier 3) for 5+5 years |
| Qualifying activities | Regional management, treasury, strategic planning for multinational groups |

### 7.4 Green Technology

| Parameter | Value |
|---|---|
| Green Investment Tax Allowance | 100% of QCE on qualifying green assets |
| Green Income Tax Exemption | 70% of statutory income from qualifying green activities |
| Duration | Typically 5 years |

## 8. Treaties & Transfer Pricing

### 8.1 Transfer Pricing

> **Legal Basis**: ITA 1967 §140A; Income Tax (Transfer Pricing) Rules 2012

| Parameter | Value |
|---|---|
| Arm's length standard | Mandatory for all controlled transactions — §140A |
| Methods | CUP, resale price, cost plus, profit split, TNMM |
| Documentation | Contemporaneous TP documentation required — Income Tax (Transfer Pricing) Rules 2012 |
| Master File / Local File | Required for companies with cross-border related-party transactions |
| CbC reporting | Required for groups with consolidated revenue ≥ RM3 billion |
| Penalties | Surcharge on TP adjustments; penalty of 25%–100% of additional tax |
| APA | Available — unilateral, bilateral |

### 8.2 Labuan Entity Transactions

> **Legal Basis**: Labuan Business Activity Tax Act 1990 (LBATA)

Transactions between Malaysian-resident entities and Labuan entities are subject to special rules. Payments to Labuan entities may be denied as deductions if conditions are not met (ITA 1967 §2C, §39(1A)).

### 8.3 Treaty Network

Malaysia has over 75 double tax agreements. Treaties generally reduce withholding taxes on dividends, interest, and royalties.
