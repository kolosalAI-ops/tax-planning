# Poland - Taxable Income Deductions

> **Jurisdiction**: National (Krajowa Administracja Skarbowa — KAS)
> **Governing Law**: Ustawa o podatku dochodowym od osob prawnych (CIT Act) — Act of 15 February 1992
> **Tax Year**: 2024/2025
> **Currency**: PLN (zl)

---

## 1. Depreciation & Capital Allowances

Poland's tax depreciation rules are set out in Articles 16a-16m and Annex 1 of the CIT Act. Depreciation is mandatory for fixed assets exceeding PLN 10,000 in value.

### 1.1 Standard Depreciation Rates — CIT Art. 16i; Annex 1

| Asset Type | Annual Rate | Statutory Basis |
|---|---|---|
| **Residential buildings** | 1.5% | CIT Annex 1, Group 1 |
| **Commercial/office buildings** | 2.5% | CIT Annex 1, Group 1 |
| **Industrial buildings** | 2.5% | CIT Annex 1, Group 1 |
| **Civil engineering structures** | 4.5% | CIT Annex 1, Group 2 |
| **Boilers & power machinery** | 7% | CIT Annex 1, Group 3 |
| **General machinery** | 10-14% | CIT Annex 1, Group 4-5 |
| **Technical equipment** | 10% | CIT Annex 1, Group 6 |
| **Vehicles (passenger cars)** | 20% | CIT Annex 1, Group 7 |
| **Vehicles (trucks)** | 20% | CIT Annex 1, Group 7 |
| **Computers & IT equipment** | 30% | CIT Annex 1, Group 4 (491) |
| **Tools and instruments** | 20% | CIT Annex 1, Group 8 |
| **Furniture & fixtures** | 20% | CIT Annex 1, Group 8 |

### 1.2 Depreciation Methods — CIT Art. 16h-16k

| Method | Detail | Statutory Basis |
|---|---|---|
| **Straight-line** | Default method; rates from Annex 1 | CIT Art. 16i |
| **Declining balance** | Up to 2x the straight-line rate; switches to straight-line when advantageous | CIT Art. 16k |
| **One-time depreciation** | Up to PLN 100,000 per year for "small taxpayers" and startup companies | CIT Art. 16f (3) |

### 1.3 Accelerated Depreciation

| Scenario | Multiplier | Statutory Basis |
|---|---|---|
| **Machinery used in aggressive conditions** | 1.4x standard rate | CIT Art. 16i (2) |
| **R&D equipment** | One-time depreciation allowed | CIT Art. 16f (3) |
| **Used assets** | Higher rates possible based on remaining useful life | CIT Art. 16j |
| **Buildings in deteriorating areas** | Up to 10% rate for specific municipalities | CIT Art. 16i (2) |

### 1.4 Low-Value Assets

| Parameter | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Threshold** | Assets with acquisition cost ≤PLN 10,000 may be fully expensed in the year of acquisition — CIT Art. 16d (1) |
| **Classification** | Not classified as fixed assets for depreciation purposes |

### 1.5 Intangible Assets — CIT Art. 16b

| Asset Type | Minimum Period | Statutory Basis |
|---|---|---|
| **Goodwill (acquired)** | 60 months | CIT Art. 16m (1) 4 |
| **Licenses (software)** | 24 months | CIT Art. 16m (1) 1 |
| **Patents** | 60 months | CIT Art. 16m (1) 3 |
| **Trademarks** | 60 months | CIT Art. 16m (1) 3 |
| **Know-how** | 60 months | CIT Art. 16m (1) 3 |
| **Copyright (software)** | 24 months | CIT Art. 16m (1) 1 |

### 1.6 Passenger Car Restrictions — CIT Art. 16 (1) 4

| Parameter | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Depreciation cap** | PLN 150,000 for electric vehicles; PLN 150,000 for other vehicles — CIT Art. 16 (1) 4 |
| **Leasing cap** | Operating lease payments deductible proportionally to the PLN 150,000 cap — CIT Art. 16 (1) 49a |
| **Insurance cap** | Insurance deductible up to PLN 150,000 of vehicle value — CIT Art. 16 (1) 49 |

## 2. Business Operating Expenses

General deductibility is governed by CIT Art. 15 (1) — expenses incurred for the purpose of generating revenue or maintaining/securing the source of revenue are deductible, except those listed in Art. 16.

### 2.1 Fully Deductible Expenses — CIT Art. 15 (1)

| Expense Category | Deductibility | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| **Employee salaries & wages** | 100% | Including bonuses, holiday pay |
| **Employer social security (ZUS)** | 100% | Employer portion of pension, disability, accident, labour fund contributions |
| **Rent (business premises)** | 100% | Must be business use |
| **Insurance premiums** | 100% | Business-related risks |
| **Professional fees** | 100% | Legal, audit, consulting |
| **Advertising & marketing** | 100% | Business purpose; representation costs limited (see below) |
| **Travel expenses** | 100% | Business travel within per diem limits |
| **Training expenses** | 100% | Job-related employee training |
| **Bad debts (realized)** | 100% | Must be definitively uncollectible; previously recognized as revenue — CIT Art. 16 (1) 25 |

### 2.2 Partially Deductible / Restricted

| Expense Category | Limit | Statutory Basis |
|---|---|---|
| **Representation** | Non-deductible | CIT Art. 16 (1) 28 |
| **Meals (with clients, representation)** | Non-deductible (classified as representation) | CIT Art. 16 (1) 28 |
| **Charitable donations** | Deductible up to 10% of income before donation deduction | CIT Art. 18 (1) |
| **Passenger car operating costs** | 75% deductible if car also used privately; 100% if exclusively business use with mileage log — CIT Art. 16 (1) 51 | CIT Art. 16 (1) 51 |

## 3. Interest & Financing Costs

### 3.1 General Deductibility — CIT Art. 15 (1)

Interest expenses are deductible on a cash basis (when paid) — CIT Art. 16 (1) 11.

### 3.2 EBITDA-Based Interest Limitation — CIT Art. 15c

| Parameter | Detail |
|---|---|
| **General rule** | Excess borrowing costs deductible up to 30% of tax EBITDA — CIT Art. 15c (1) |
| **De minimis** | PLN 3M — excess borrowing costs up to PLN 3M are always deductible — CIT Art. 15c (14) 1 |
| **Definition** | Excess borrowing costs = interest expense minus interest income — CIT Art. 15c (3) |
| **Carry-forward** | Non-deductible interest may be carried forward for up to 5 years — CIT Art. 15c (18) |
| **Scope** | Applies to all borrowing costs (related and unrelated party) — CIT Art. 15c (1) |
| **Financial sector** | Modified rules for banks and financial institutions — CIT Art. 15c (16) |

### 3.3 Hidden Profit Distribution — CIT Art. 16 (1) 13f (Estonian CIT)

| Parameter | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Scope** | Under Estonian CIT regime, certain payments to shareholders (including excessive interest) may be recharacterized as hidden profit distribution |
| **Effect** | Subject to 20%/25% Estonian CIT rate |

## 4. Research & Development

Poland offers an R&D relief (ulga B+R) under CIT Art. 18d allowing companies to claim an additional deduction of up to 200% of qualifying R&D costs (100% base deduction + 100% extra deduction for R&D centres, or 100% + varying percentages for other companies). Additionally, the IP Box regime (CIT Art. 24d) provides a preferential 5% rate on qualifying IP income.

See [R&D Incentives](rnd_incentives.md) for full program detail.

## 5. Loss Carry-Forward & Carry-Back

### 5.1 Loss Carry-Forward — CIT Art. 7 (5)

| Parameter | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Duration** | **5 years** — CIT Art. 7 (5) |
| **Annual cap** | Maximum 50% of the loss may be used in any single year — CIT Art. 7 (5) 1 |
| **Alternative** | From 2022: up to PLN 5M of losses may be used in full in a single year — CIT Art. 7 (5) 2 |
| **Combined method** | Taxpayer may combine both methods for losses exceeding PLN 5M |
| **Ownership change** | No specific loss forfeiture on ownership change (general anti-avoidance may apply) |

### 5.2 Loss Carry-Back

| Parameter | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Carry-back** | **Not permitted** under Polish law (temporary COVID provisions have expired) |

### 5.3 Estonian CIT (Ryczalt od dochodow spolek) — CIT Art. 28c-28t

| Parameter | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Regime** | CIT on distributed profit only — CIT Art. 28c |
| **Tax rate** | 20% on distributions (10% for small taxpayers) — CIT Art. 28o |
| **Effective rate** | Approximately 20% when combined with shareholder PIT credit (effectively ~25% total or ~20% for small taxpayers) |
| **Eligibility** | Revenue <PLN 100M; shareholders are natural persons only; limited passive income — CIT Art. 28j |
| **Retained earnings** | Not taxed until distributed |
| **Effect on losses** | Pre-existing losses cannot be used under Estonian CIT regime |

## 6. Provisions & Reserves

### 6.1 Bad Debt Provisions — CIT Art. 16 (1) 25-26a

| Parameter | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Realized bad debts** | Deductible if previously recognized as revenue and definitively uncollectible — CIT Art. 16 (1) 25 |
| **Write-off conditions** | Debt must meet specific conditions of uncollectibility (e.g., debtor bankruptcy, enforcement failure) — CIT Art. 16 (2) |
| **Bad debt relief (ulga na zle dlugi)** | Creditor may increase taxable income deduction after 90 days overdue; debtor must increase taxable income — CIT Art. 18f |

### 6.2 Inventory Valuation

| Parameter | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Method** | Lower of cost or market — Accounting Act (Ustawa o rachunkowosci) Art. 28 |
| **Write-down** | Permitted for obsolete or damaged inventory |
| **Tax alignment** | Tax generally follows accounting treatment for inventory |

### 6.3 Warranty Provisions

| Parameter | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Deductibility** | Warranty provisions are not deductible until realized as actual costs — CIT Art. 16 (1) 27 |
| **Actuarial provisions** | Not deductible for tax purposes |

### 6.4 Restructuring Provisions

| Parameter | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Deductibility** | Restructuring provisions are generally not deductible until realized |

## 7. Industry-Specific Deductions

### 7.1 Special Economic Zones (SEZ) / Polish Investment Zone

| Parameter | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Regime** | Tax exemption for income from qualifying investments — CIT Art. 17 (1) 34a |
| **Benefit** | CIT exemption based on qualifying investment costs or 2-year employment costs |
| **Duration** | 10-15 years depending on location |
| **Regional aid** | Aid intensity varies by region: 15-50% of qualifying costs |

### 7.2 Mining & Extractive Industries

| Deduction | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Extraction tax (copper, silver)** | Separate extraction tax applies; deductible as business expense |
| **Exploration costs** | Deductible when incurred |
| **Geological and mining law** | Specific provisions under Prawo geologiczne i gornicze |

### 7.3 Agriculture

| Deduction | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Agricultural income** | Generally exempt from CIT (subject to agricultural tax instead) |
| **Mixed activities** | Agricultural income separated from business income |

### 7.4 Holding Company Relief — CIT Art. 24n-24p

| Parameter | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Dividend exemption** | 95% exemption on dividends received from qualifying subsidiaries — CIT Art. 24n |
| **Capital gains exemption** | Full exemption on gains from disposal of qualifying subsidiary shares — CIT Art. 24o |
| **Conditions** | Polish holding company; 10%+ holding for 2+ years; subsidiary in treaty country; substance requirements — CIT Art. 24p |

## 8. Treaties & Transfer Pricing

### 8.1 Transfer Pricing — CIT Art. 11a-11t

| Parameter | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Arm's length principle** | Mandatory for all related-party transactions — CIT Art. 11c |
| **Documentation** | Local TP documentation required for transactions exceeding thresholds — CIT Art. 11k |
| **Thresholds** | PLN 10M (goods/financial) or PLN 2M (services/intangibles) — CIT Art. 11k (2) |
| **Country-by-Country Reporting** | Required for groups with consolidated revenue ≥ PLN 3.25Bn — CIT Art. 11p |
| **Master File** | Required if group revenue ≥ PLN 200M — CIT Art. 11s |
| **Benchmark study** | Required as part of local documentation — CIT Art. 11q |
| **Penalties** | Tax surcharge on TP adjustments; additional penalty of 10% / 20% / 30% for undisclosed transactions — CIT Art. 11o |

### 8.2 Accepted Methods

1. Comparable Uncontrolled Price (CUP)
2. Resale Price Method
3. Cost Plus Method
4. Transactional Net Margin Method (TNMM)
5. Profit Split Method

### 8.3 Tax Treaties

| Parameter | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Treaty network** | Poland has 90+ double taxation treaties |
| **Model** | Based on OECD Model Tax Convention |
| **MAP** | Mutual Agreement Procedure available |
| **EU Arbitration Convention** | Applicable for intra-EU disputes |

### 8.4 Withholding Tax (Outbound)

| Payment Type | Domestic Rate | Treaty-Reduced Rate |
|---|---|---|
| **Dividends** | 19% (or 0% under EU Parent-Subsidiary Directive) | Typically 5-15% |
| **Interest** | 20% | Typically 0-10% |
| **Royalties** | 20% | Typically 0-10% |

**Note:** Poland applies a withholding tax "pay-and-refund" mechanism for payments exceeding PLN 2M per year to one recipient — CIT Art. 26 (2e). The payer must withhold at domestic rate and the recipient must apply for refund.
