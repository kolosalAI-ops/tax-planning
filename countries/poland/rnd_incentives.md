# Poland - R&D Tax Incentives

## 1. Overview

| Feature | Details |
|---|---|
| **Primary Incentive** | R&D Relief (Ulga B+R) — Enhanced Deduction |
| **Standard Rate** | 200% deduction of qualifying R&D costs |
| **R&D Centre Rate** | 300% deduction for certified R&D centres (CBR) |
| **IP Box** | 5% preferential tax rate on qualifying IP income |
| **Legal Basis (R&D Relief)** | CIT Act (Ustawa o CIT), Art. 18d |
| **Legal Basis (IP Box)** | CIT Act, Art. 24d |
| **Carry-Forward** | 6 years (for R&D relief losses) |
| **Startup Refund Option** | Loss-making startups can claim cash refund |
| **Standard CIT Rate** | 19% (9% for small taxpayers up to €2M revenue) |

## 2. Detailed Programs

### 2.1 R&D Relief (Ulga Badawczo-Rozwojowa / Ulga B+R)

**Legal Basis:** Ustawa o podatku dochodowym od osób prawnych (CIT Act), Art. 18d

The R&D Relief allows companies to deduct qualifying R&D costs **twice**: once as a standard business expense and again as an additional R&D deduction.

#### Deduction Rates

| Company Type | Additional Deduction Rate | Total Deduction |
|---|---|---|
| Standard companies | 100% additional (200% total) | 200% of qualifying R&D costs |
| Certified R&D centres (CBR) | 200% additional (300% total) | 300% of qualifying R&D costs |

#### Effective Tax Benefit

| Company Type | CIT Rate | Deduction | Tax Benefit per €1 of R&D |
|---|---|---|---|
| Standard company (19% CIT) | 19% | 200% | €0.38 (19% × 200%) |
| Small taxpayer (9% CIT) | 9% | 200% | €0.18 (9% × 200%) |
| R&D centre (19% CIT) | 19% | 300% | €0.57 (19% × 300%) |
| R&D centre (9% CIT) | 9% | 300% | €0.27 (9% × 300%) |

#### Qualifying R&D Activities

Under Polish law, R&D (działalność badawczo-rozwojowa) includes:

- Scientific research (badania naukowe) — basic and applied
- Development work (prace rozwojowe) — using existing knowledge to create new products, processes, or services
- Must be creative, systematic, and aimed at increasing knowledge or new applications
- Aligned with OECD Frascati Manual definitions

#### Qualifying Expenditure (Koszty Kwalifikowane)

| Cost Category | Standard Companies | R&D Centres (CBR) |
|---|---|---|
| Employee remuneration (wages, social contributions) | 200% | 300% |
| Materials and raw materials for R&D | 200% | 300% |
| Expert opinions, advisory services | 200% | 300% |
| Use of R&D equipment (excluding passenger vehicles) | 200% | 300% |
| Patent costs (application, maintenance) | 200% | 300% |
| Depreciation of R&D fixed assets and intangibles | 200% | 300% |
| Equipment and apparatus depreciation | 200% | 300% |

### 2.2 Certified R&D Centre (Centrum Badawczo-Rozwojowe / CBR)

**Legal Basis:** Ustawa o niektórych formach wspierania działalności innowacyjnej

| Requirement | Details |
|---|---|
| R&D revenue threshold | At least 20% of total revenue from R&D activities |
| Minimum revenue | PLN 2.5 million per year from R&D or total revenue of PLN 5 million |
| No tax arrears | Company must be current on all tax obligations |
| Certification body | Ministry of Economic Development and Technology |
| Enhanced deduction | 300% (vs. 200% for standard companies) |
| Additional benefits | Exemption from property tax, right to create innovation fund |

### 2.3 Startup Loss Refund (Zwrot Gotówkowy)

**Legal Basis:** CIT Act, Art. 18da

Loss-making companies (including startups) can claim a **cash refund** of the R&D relief:

| Parameter | Details |
|---|---|
| Eligibility | Companies with CIT losses or insufficient taxable income |
| Refund amount | R&D relief amount × applicable CIT rate (19% or 9%) |
| Company age requirement | Established within the prior 2 fiscal years (startup condition) |
| Revenue cap | Below threshold for small taxpayer status |
| Form | Refund claimed in annual CIT return |
| Payment | Refunded by tax office within standard processing time |

### 2.4 IP Box (Innovation Box)

**Legal Basis:** CIT Act, Art. 24d-24e

| Feature | Details |
|---|---|
| Preferential rate | 5% CIT on qualifying IP income |
| Standard CIT rate | 19% (reduction of 14 percentage points) |
| Qualifying IP | Patents, utility models, registered designs, supplementary protection certificates, plant variety rights, integrated circuit topographies, software copyright |
| Qualifying income | Licensing revenue, sale of IP, IP income embedded in product/service prices |
| Nexus approach | OECD BEPS Action 5 compliant |
| Record-keeping | Separate books for each qualifying IP asset required |

#### Nexus Fraction

```
Qualifying Income = Total IP Income × (Qualifying R&D × 1.3) / Total R&D Expenditure
```

| Numerator | Denominator |
|---|---|
| Own R&D expenditure | All R&D costs for the IP |
| Unrelated-party outsourcing | Including related-party outsourcing |
| 30% uplift (capped) | Including IP acquisition costs |

#### Combining R&D Relief and IP Box

Poland explicitly allows combining the R&D Relief (Ulga B+R) with the IP Box:

1. **Development Phase:** Claim 200%/300% R&D deduction on qualifying R&D costs
2. **Commercialization Phase:** Apply 5% IP Box rate on income from resulting IP
3. **No anti-stacking provision** — both can apply simultaneously
4. **Combined effective rate** can approach near-zero taxation on IP income

### 2.5 Polish Investment Zone (Polska Strefa Inwestycji)

**Legal Basis:** Ustawa o wspieraniu nowych inwestycji (2018)

| Feature | Details |
|---|---|
| CIT exemption | Up to 10-15 years depending on location and investment size |
| Qualifying activities | Including R&D-intensive investments |
| Regional aid intensity | 10-50% depending on region |
| Combination with R&D Relief | Can be combined, subject to state aid limits |
| Minimum investment | Varies by region and company size |

## 3. Patent Box / IP Regime

*(Covered in detail under Section 2.4 above)*

### Summary

| Feature | Details |
|---|---|
| Regime name | IP Box (Innovation Box) |
| Tax rate | 5% on qualifying IP income |
| Qualifying IP | Patents, utility models, designs, software copyright, and others |
| Nexus compliant | Yes — OECD BEPS Action 5 |
| Separate accounting | Required — per IP asset |
| Excluded IP | Trademarks, brand names, know-how (unless patented) |
| Effective since | January 1, 2019 |
| Combination with R&D Relief | Permitted |

## 5. Claim Process & Documentation

### Step-by-Step Claim Process

1. **Identify Qualifying R&D** — Assess activities against Polish statutory definitions
2. **Isolate Qualifying Costs** — Separate eligible costs per Art. 18d categories
3. **Maintain R&D Records** — Contemporaneous project documentation
4. **Annual CIT Return (CIT-8)** — Report R&D relief in the annual corporate tax return
5. **Attachment CIT-BR** — Complete the specific R&D relief form with cost breakdown
6. **IP Box (if applicable)** — Separate CIT-IP attachment with per-asset accounting
7. **No Pre-Approval Required** — Self-assessed claim; no advance certification needed
8. **Optional: Binding Tax Ruling** — Request advance ruling from the Director of National Tax Information

### Documentation Requirements

| Requirement | Details |
|---|---|
| R&D project records | Description of each project, objectives, methodology, results |
| Personnel time records | Allocation of employee time to R&D activities |
| Cost records per category | Detailed breakdown matching Art. 18d cost categories |
| IP Box: Separate ledger per IP | Mandatory separate accounting for each qualifying IP asset |
| R&D Centre certificate | CBR status documentation (if applicable) |
| Grant funding records | Costs covered by grants must be excluded |
| Expert opinions (if claimed) | Documentation of advisory services |

### Binding Tax Ruling Option

- Companies can request a **binding individual interpretation** (interpretacja indywidualna) from the Director of National Tax Information (Dyrektor KIS)
- Useful for confirming eligibility of specific activities or cost types
- Free of charge (PLN 40 fee per question)
- Response within 3 months
- Binding on tax authorities for the requesting taxpayer

## 6. Recent Changes & Sunset Provisions

| Year | Change | Impact |
|---|---|---|
| 2016 | R&D Relief (Ulga B+R) introduced | Initial deduction rates of 130%-150% |
| 2018 | Rates increased to 200% (100% additional) | Significant enhancement |
| 2019 | IP Box introduced (5% rate) | New output-based incentive |
| 2022 | "Polish Deal" (Polski Ład) reforms | R&D Centre deduction increased to 300%; startup refund expanded |
| 2022 | Explicit combination of R&D Relief + IP Box allowed | Legislative clarity on stacking |
| 2023 | Global minimum tax discussions | Impact analysis for large MNEs |
| 2024 | Enhanced guidance on software R&D qualification | KIS interpretations |
| 2025 | Pillar Two implementation | May affect benefit for large groups |

### Sunset Provisions

- The R&D Relief (Art. 18d) is a **permanent provision** of the CIT Act — no sunset date
- The IP Box (Art. 24d) is also permanent
- The startup refund (Art. 18da) is permanent
- CBR enhanced rates are tied to certification status (renewable)
- Parameters are subject to legislative amendment via annual or special tax reform acts
- Poland has progressively enhanced (not reduced) R&D incentives since 2016
