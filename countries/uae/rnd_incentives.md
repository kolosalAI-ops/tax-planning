# United Arab Emirates - R&D Tax Incentives

## 1. Overview

| Feature | Details |
|---|---|
| **Primary Incentive** | No specific R&D tax credit or enhanced deduction |
| **CIT Rate** | 9% on taxable income exceeding AED 375,000 (effective June 1, 2023) |
| **Free Zone Rate** | 0% on qualifying income for qualifying free zone persons |
| **R&D Expense Treatment** | Fully deductible as ordinary business expense |
| **Patent Box / IP Regime** | None |
| **Governing Law** | Federal Decree-Law No. 47 of 2022 on the Taxation of Corporations and Businesses |
| **Administering Body** | Federal Tax Authority (FTA) |
| **R&D Grants** | Sector-specific grants through government innovation programs |
| **Key Consideration** | Low base CIT rate reduces the need for specific R&D incentives |

## 2. Detailed Programs

### 2.1 Corporate Income Tax Framework

**Legal Basis:** Federal Decree-Law No. 47 of 2022 on the Taxation of Corporations and Businesses (UAE CT Law), effective for financial years starting on or after June 1, 2023.

| Parameter | Details |
|---|---|
| **CIT Rate — Below Threshold** | 0% on taxable income up to AED 375,000 |
| **CIT Rate — Standard** | 9% on taxable income exceeding AED 375,000 |
| **CIT Rate — Large MNEs (Pillar Two)** | 15% minimum tax under consideration for entities in scope |
| **R&D Deduction** | 100% deduction of R&D expenditure as ordinary business expense |
| **Capital Expenditure on R&D** | Depreciable under standard asset depreciation rules |
| **Fiscal Year** | 12-month period as elected by the taxpayer |

Prior to June 2023, the UAE had no federal corporate income tax (except for oil and gas companies and foreign bank branches). The introduction of CIT at 9% remains one of the lowest rates globally.

### 2.2 R&D Expense Deductibility

**Legal Basis:** Articles 28–33 of Federal Decree-Law No. 47/2022; Ministerial Decision No. 126 of 2023 on Deductible Expenditure

| Eligible R&D Costs | Deductibility |
|---|---|
| Employee salaries and benefits (R&D personnel) | 100% deductible |
| Consumable materials and supplies | 100% deductible |
| Subcontractor and outsourced R&D costs | 100% deductible (arm's length) |
| Equipment and machinery (capital) | Depreciated per standard rules |
| Software and IT costs for R&D | 100% deductible (or amortized if capital) |
| Overhead allocated to R&D | 100% deductible (reasonable allocation) |
| Patent filing and registration costs | 100% deductible |

**Conditions for Deductibility:**
- Expenditure must be incurred wholly and exclusively for business purposes (Article 28)
- Must not be capital in nature (unless depreciation applies)
- Must be supported by adequate documentation and records
- Transfer pricing rules apply to related-party R&D transactions (Articles 34–36)

### 2.3 Free Zone Qualifying Persons

**Legal Basis:** Articles 18–19 of Federal Decree-Law No. 47/2022; Cabinet Decision No. 55 of 2023 on Qualifying Income

| Parameter | Details |
|---|---|
| **Tax Rate on Qualifying Income** | 0% |
| **Tax Rate on Non-Qualifying Income** | 9% |
| **Qualifying Activity — R&D Relevant** | Manufacturing, processing, holding IP, certain HQ activities |
| **Substance Requirements** | Adequate employees, expenditure, and assets in the free zone |
| **De Minimis Threshold** | Non-qualifying revenue must not exceed the lower of AED 5M or 5% of total revenue |
| **Qualifying Income Sources** | Transactions with other free zone persons; certain mainland and foreign income |

**Key Free Zones for R&D Activities:**

| Free Zone | R&D Focus |
|---|---|
| **Dubai Internet City (DIC)** | Technology, software development |
| **Dubai Silicon Oasis (DSO)** | Technology startups, electronics |
| **Abu Dhabi Global Market (ADGM)** | Fintech, financial innovation |
| **Masdar City Free Zone** | Clean energy, sustainability R&D |
| **Khalifa Industrial Zone (KIZAD)** | Industrial R&D, manufacturing |
| **Sharjah Research, Technology & Innovation Park** | Multi-sector R&D |
| **twofour54 (Abu Dhabi)** | Media technology |

### 2.4 Government R&D Support Programs

While not tax incentives, the UAE offers various government-funded programs supporting R&D activities:

| Program | Administering Body | Details |
|---|---|---|
| **Mohammed bin Rashid Innovation Fund (MBRIF)** | Ministry of Finance | Loan guarantees for innovative companies |
| **Abu Dhabi Investment Office (ADIO) R&D Program** | ADIO | Cash rebates and incentives for R&D-intensive companies establishing in Abu Dhabi |
| **Technology Innovation Pioneers (TIP)** | TRA | Grants for telecom and ICT innovation |
| **Hub71 (Abu Dhabi)** | Mubadala / Hub71 | Startup incentives including housing, cloud credits, and health insurance |
| **Dubai Future Accelerators** | Dubai Future Foundation | Partnership programs with government entities |
| **ASPIRE (Abu Dhabi)** | ATRC | Defense and security technology R&D funding |

## 3. Patent Box / IP Regime

The UAE does not currently operate a patent box or specific IP income regime.

| Feature | Details |
|---|---|
| **Patent Box** | Not available |
| **IP Income Treatment** | Taxed at standard 9% CIT rate |
| **Free Zone Alternative** | IP holding as qualifying activity in free zones may achieve 0% on qualifying income |
| **IP Amortization** | Intangible assets amortized over useful life per accounting standards |
| **IP Transfer Pricing** | Articles 34–36 of CT Law; arm's length pricing required |
| **Withholding Tax on Royalties** | 0% (UAE imposes no withholding tax on any payments) |

**Strategic Consideration:** While no formal patent box exists, the combination of 0% free zone rate for qualifying IP holding activities and 0% withholding tax on royalty payments makes the UAE an attractive jurisdiction for IP structuring. Companies should ensure adequate substance to support any IP arrangements.

## 4. Effective Benefit Analysis (per $1M USD R&D Spend)

### Scenario A: Mainland Company (9% CIT)

| Component | Amount (USD) |
|---|---|
| R&D Expenditure | $1,000,000 |
| Tax Deduction (100% × 9%) | $90,000 |
| **Total Tax Benefit** | **$90,000** |
| **Effective R&D Cost** | **$910,000** |
| **Net Benefit Rate** | **9.0%** |

### Scenario B: Free Zone Qualifying Person (0% on Qualifying Income)

| Component | Amount (USD) |
|---|---|
| R&D Expenditure | $1,000,000 |
| Tax Deduction (100% × 0%) | $0 |
| **Total Tax Benefit from Deduction** | **$0** |
| **Effective R&D Cost** | **$1,000,000** |
| **Note** | No deduction benefit because income is already at 0%; the benefit is the 0% rate on income generated from R&D activities |

### Scenario C: Mainland + ADIO R&D Rebate

| Component | Amount (USD) |
|---|---|
| R&D Expenditure | $1,000,000 |
| ADIO Cash Rebate (illustrative, varies) | $150,000 – $300,000 |
| Tax Deduction (100% × 9% on net cost) | $63,000 – $76,500 |
| **Total Benefit** | **$213,000 – $376,500** |
| **Effective R&D Cost** | **$623,500 – $787,000** |

### Comparative Summary

| Scenario | Effective Cost per $1M R&D | Benefit Rate |
|---|---|---|
| Mainland (9% CIT) | $910,000 | 9.0% |
| Free Zone (0% on qualifying income) | $1,000,000 (benefit in 0% income rate) | 0% direct |
| Mainland + ADIO Rebate (est.) | $623,500 – $787,000 | 21.3–37.7% |
| Free Zone + Government Grant | Varies by program | Case-specific |

> **Note:** The UAE's primary R&D advantage is its low 9% CIT rate (or 0% free zone rate) rather than specific R&D tax incentives. The absence of withholding taxes and personal income tax further enhances the overall attractiveness for R&D operations.

## 5. Claim Process & Documentation

### R&D Expense Deduction (Standard)

| Step | Action | Timeline |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Maintain records of R&D expenditure with supporting invoices | Ongoing |
| 2 | Classify R&D costs appropriately (revenue vs. capital) | At incurrence |
| 3 | Prepare transfer pricing documentation for related-party R&D | Annually |
| 4 | Include R&D deductions in annual CIT return | Within 9 months of fiscal year-end |
| 5 | File CIT return via EmaraTax portal | Annual deadline |
| 6 | Retain records for minimum 7 years | Ongoing |

### Free Zone Qualification

| Step | Action | Timeline |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Register and operate in a qualifying free zone | At establishment |
| 2 | Ensure qualifying activity status (e.g., IP holding, manufacturing) | Ongoing |
| 3 | Maintain adequate substance (employees, assets, expenditure) | Ongoing |
| 4 | Track qualifying vs. non-qualifying income | Ongoing |
| 5 | File CIT return claiming free zone person status | Annual |
| 6 | Prepare and maintain transfer pricing documentation | Annually |

### Required Documentation

- **Financial Records:** General ledger entries, invoices, payment records for all R&D expenditure
- **Employment Records:** Contracts, payroll records, and time allocation for R&D personnel
- **Project Documentation:** R&D project descriptions, objectives, and outcomes
- **Transfer Pricing:** Master file, local file, and CbCR (if applicable) per Ministerial Decision No. 97 of 2023
- **Free Zone:** Substance evidence, lease agreements, employee visas, board meeting minutes in the free zone

## 6. Recent Changes & Sunset Provisions

| Change | Effective Date | Details |
|---|---|---|
| **Introduction of Federal CIT** | June 1, 2023 | Federal Decree-Law No. 47/2022 — 9% CIT introduced for the first time |
| **Free Zone Regime Clarification** | June 1, 2023 | Cabinet Decision No. 55/2023 defining qualifying activities and income |
| **Transfer Pricing Rules** | June 1, 2023 | Arm's length principle and documentation requirements introduced |
| **Ministerial Decision No. 265 of 2023** | 2023 | Clarification on deductible expenditure including R&D costs |
| **Pillar Two Domestic Minimum Tax** | Under consideration | UAE may implement a 15% DMTT for MNEs with revenue > EUR 750M; timing TBD |
| **R&D Tax Credit Proposal** | Under discussion (2025–2026) | Ministry of Finance exploring introduction of specific R&D tax credits to diversify economy; no legislation yet |
| **Small Business Relief** | June 1, 2023 | Revenue ≤ AED 3M eligible for simplified regime (Ministerial Decision No. 73/2023) |

**Sunset Provisions:**
- The free zone 0% qualifying income rate is available for a period determined by each free zone's regulations, but the CT Law provides that qualifying free zone persons benefit from the 0% rate
- No specific R&D incentive sunset dates as no specific R&D regime currently exists
- Government grant programs (ADIO, MBRIF, etc.) are subject to periodic renewal and budget allocation

**Future Outlook:**
- The UAE government has signaled interest in introducing targeted R&D incentives as part of economic diversification
- The National Innovation Strategy 2030 may lead to new legislative measures
- OECD Pillar Two implementation could impact the attractiveness of the 0% free zone rate for large MNEs
