# United Arab Emirates - Taxable Income Deductions

> **Jurisdiction**: Federal (Federal Tax Authority — FTA)
> **Governing Law**: Federal Decree-Law No. 47 of 2022 on the Taxation of Corporations and Businesses (CT Law)
> **Tax Year**: 2024/2025
> **Currency**: AED (د.إ)

---

## 1. Depreciation & Capital Allowances

### General Framework (Art. 20, CT Law)

The UAE CT Law does not prescribe specific depreciation rates or asset-life tables. Depreciation and amortisation follow the taxpayer's accounting treatment under IFRS (or IFRS for SMEs), provided the accounting standards are applied consistently.

| Parameter | Detail |
|---|---|
| Method | Per applicable accounting standard (IFRS / IFRS for SMEs) — Art. 20 CT Law |
| Prescribed rates | None — follows accounting useful life |
| Land | Not depreciable |
| Goodwill | Amortised per IFRS 3 / IAS 38 (no separate tax schedule) |
| Impairment | Recognised for tax purposes where accounting standards require it |

### Capitalised Interest

Interest capitalised into the cost of an asset under IAS 23 is subject to the General Interest Deduction Limitation Rule (GIDLR) separately from the depreciation charge — Art. 30 CT Law; FTA Guide CTGIDL1 (April 2025).

### Investment Property Election (Ministerial Decision No. 173 of 2025)

| Parameter | Detail |
|---|---|
| Scope | Taxable persons who elected realisation basis for gains/losses |
| Benefit | Irrevocable election to claim deemed depreciation on investment property carried at fair value under IFRS |
| Legal basis | Ministerial Decision No. 173 of 2025 |

### Pre-Trading Expenditure (Art. 27, CT Law)

Capital expenditure incurred before the business generates taxable income may be capitalised and depreciated from the date the business commences, provided it relates to the preparation of the business for trade.

## 2. Business Operating Expenses

### General Deductibility (Art. 28, CT Law)

All expenditure incurred wholly and exclusively for the purposes of the taxable person's business is deductible, provided it is not capital in nature (unless depreciable) and is incurred during the relevant tax period.

| Expense Category | Deductibility | Legal Basis |
|---|---|---|
| Salaries, wages, and employee benefits | **100%** deductible | Art. 28 CT Law |
| Rent, utilities, and office expenses | **100%** deductible | Art. 28 CT Law |
| Professional fees (legal, accounting, consulting) | **100%** deductible | Art. 28 CT Law |
| Insurance premiums (business-related) | **100%** deductible | Art. 28 CT Law |
| Travel and accommodation (business purpose) | **100%** deductible | Art. 28 CT Law |
| Entertainment, amusement, recreation | **50%** deductible | Art. 32 CT Law; Ministerial Decision No. 114/2023 |
| Donations to qualifying public benefit entities | **100%** deductible (up to 10% of taxable income prior to the deduction) | Art. 31 CT Law |
| Donations to non-qualifying entities | **0%** — non-deductible | Art. 33(1) CT Law |
| Start-up costs | Deductible or amortised per IFRS treatment | Art. 28 CT Law |

### Entertainment Expenses (Art. 32, CT Law; Ministerial Decision No. 114/2023)

| Parameter | Detail |
|---|---|
| Deduction limit | **50%** of entertainment, amusement, or recreation expenditure |
| Qualifying expenses | Meals, accommodation, transportation, admission fees, facilities, and equipment for entertainment |
| Condition | Must be incurred for business purposes |

### Small Business Relief (Ministerial Decision No. 73/2023)

| Parameter | Detail |
|---|---|
| Revenue threshold | AED 3,000,000 or less |
| Benefit | Treated as having no taxable income (effectively 0% tax) |
| Conditions | Must be a resident taxable person; not part of a multinational enterprise group; must elect annually |

## 3. Interest & Financing Costs

### General Interest Deduction Limitation Rule — GIDLR (Art. 30, CT Law; Ministerial Decision No. 126/2023)

| Parameter | Detail |
|---|---|
| Cap | Net interest expenditure (NIE) deductible up to **30%** of tax-adjusted EBITDA |
| De minimis threshold | **AED 12,000,000** — if NIE does not exceed this, GIDLR does not apply |
| Deduction | Higher of AED 12M de minimis or 30% of tax-adjusted EBITDA |
| Carry-forward | Disallowed net interest carried forward for up to **10 tax periods** |
| Exempt entities | Banks, insurance providers, and certain regulated financial services entities |

### Specific Interest Deduction Limitation Rule (Art. 31, CT Law)

Interest or economically equivalent returns on a loan obtained from a related party to finance income that is exempt from CT (e.g., dividends from a participation) is **non-deductible**.

### Islamic Finance (Art. 5, CT Law)

| Parameter | Detail |
|---|---|
| Treatment | Payments under Sharia-compliant financing arrangements (e.g., Murabaha profit, Ijara rent, Sukuk distributions) are treated as economically equivalent to interest |
| GIDLR application | Same 30% EBITDA / AED 12M de minimis rules apply |
| Legal basis | Art. 5 CT Law — economic substance over legal form for Islamic finance instruments |

## 4. Research & Development

The UAE does not offer a specific R&D tax credit or enhanced deduction. R&D expenditure is fully deductible as an ordinary business expense under Art. 28 of the CT Law, subject to general deductibility requirements. Capital expenditure on R&D assets is depreciated per IFRS.

See [R&D Incentives](rnd_incentives.md) for full programme details, including Free Zone benefits and government grant programmes.

## 5. Loss Carry-Forward & Carry-Back

### Tax Loss Relief (Art. 37–39, CT Law)

| Parameter | Detail |
|---|---|
| Carry-forward | **Indefinite** — Art. 37 CT Law |
| Annual utilisation cap | **75%** of taxable income for the relevant tax period — Art. 38 CT Law |
| Carry-back | **Not permitted** |
| Change of ownership | Tax losses may be forfeited if there is a change in ownership of more than 50% **and** a change in the nature or conduct of the business — Art. 39 CT Law |
| Transfer of losses | Available within a Tax Group or between commonly owned entities (≥75% ownership) under specific conditions — Art. 40 CT Law |

### Free Zone Losses

| Parameter | Detail |
|---|---|
| QFZP losses | Losses from qualifying activities (taxed at 0%) cannot offset income from non-qualifying activities (taxed at 9%), and vice versa |
| Carry-forward | Qualifying losses carry forward indefinitely but remain ring-fenced |

## 6. Provisions & Reserves

### Accounting Provisions (Art. 20, CT Law)

| Item | Treatment |
|---|---|
| General rule | Provisions recognised under IFRS are deductible if they meet the recognition criteria of IAS 37 (probable outflow, reliable estimate) |
| Bad debt provisions | Deductible when calculated per IFRS 9 expected credit loss model |
| Warranty provisions | Deductible if recognised under IAS 37 |
| Contingent liabilities | **Not deductible** — disclosed only; no recognition under IAS 37 |
| Employee end-of-service gratuity | Deductible when accrued per IAS 19 |

### Realisation Basis Election (Art. 20(2), CT Law; Ministerial Decision No. 134/2023)

Taxable persons may elect to recognise gains and losses on an actual realisation basis for financial assets and liabilities measured at fair value through profit or loss, and for foreign currency hedging instruments. This election is **irrevocable** and requires adjustments to be made upon disposal.

## 7. Industry-Specific Deductions

### Oil & Gas / Natural Resources

| Parameter | Detail |
|---|---|
| Regime | Separate fiscal regime under individual Emirate-level concession agreements |
| CT Law scope | Persons engaged in the extraction of natural resources are subject to Emirate-level taxation and are **outside the scope** of Federal Decree-Law No. 47/2022, unless the relevant Emirate elects otherwise — Art. 7 CT Law |
| Deductions | Governed by individual concession terms (typically 55%–85% effective tax rate) |

### Qualifying Free Zone Persons — QFZP (Art. 18, CT Law; Ministerial Decision No. 265/2023; MD 229 & 230/2025)

| Parameter | Detail |
|---|---|
| Tax rate — qualifying income | **0%** |
| Tax rate — non-qualifying income | **9%** |
| Conditions | Adequate substance in the Free Zone; derives qualifying income; maintains adequate TP documentation; has not elected standard CT regime |
| Qualifying activities | Manufacturing, processing, holding of shares, IP licensing (if created in FZ), treasury and financing, fund management, wealth management, HQ services, shipping, reinsurance, distribution from/to FZ |
| TP requirement | Arm's-length for all related-party transactions; disclosure threshold: AED 4M; TP documentation threshold: AED 40M — Ministerial Decision No. 97/2023 |

### Banking & Insurance

Banks and insurance companies are **exempt from the GIDLR** interest limitation, but are otherwise subject to the standard 9% CT rate and all other deduction rules.

## 8. Treaties & Transfer Pricing

### Transfer Pricing (Art. 34–36, CT Law; Ministerial Decision No. 97/2023)

| Parameter | Detail |
|---|---|
| Standard | Arm's-length principle — Art. 34 CT Law |
| Methods | CUP, resale price, cost plus, TNMM, profit split — consistent with OECD Guidelines |
| Documentation | Master File and Local File required where UAE revenue > AED 200M or part of MNE group with consolidated revenue > AED 3.15B — MD 97/2023 |
| Related-party disclosure | Transactions > AED 4M must be disclosed in the CT return |
| Country-by-Country Reporting | Required for UAE-headquartered MNE groups with consolidated revenue > AED 3.15B (~EUR 750M) |
| Penalties | AED 500,000 for failure to maintain TP documentation |
| Advance Pricing Agreements | Not yet formally available (under consideration) |

### Treaty Network

The UAE has over 130 bilateral double taxation treaties (DTTs). Treaty benefits may reduce withholding taxes on cross-border payments and provide for mutual agreement procedures.

## 9. Non-Deductible Items

The following are **NOT deductible** under UAE CT Law:

| Expense | Legal Basis |
|---|---|
| 50% of entertainment, amusement, recreation expenses | Art. 32 CT Law |
| Donations, grants, or gifts to non-qualifying entities | Art. 33(1) CT Law |
| Fines and penalties (imposed by a government or regulatory authority) | Art. 33(2) CT Law (except contractual damages/compensation) |
| Bribes and similar illicit payments | Art. 33(3) CT Law |
| Dividends, profit distributions, or similar payments to owners | Art. 33 CT Law |
| Corporate income tax itself (UAE CT and foreign income tax) | Art. 33 CT Law |
| Interest on loans to finance exempt income (e.g., participation dividends) | Art. 31 CT Law |
| Net interest exceeding 30% EBITDA / AED 12M de minimis | Art. 30 CT Law |
| Expenditure not incurred wholly and exclusively for business purposes | Art. 28 CT Law |
| Expenditure with no supporting documentation | General principle |
| Withdrawals and personal expenses of the business owner | Art. 33 CT Law |
