# New Zealand - Taxable Income Deductions

> **Jurisdiction**: National (Inland Revenue — IR)
> **Governing Law**: Income Tax Act 2007 (ITA 2007)
> **Tax Year**: 2024/2025
> **Currency**: NZD (NZ$)

---

## 1. Depreciation & Capital Allowances

### 1.1 General Depreciation Rules

> **Legal Basis**: ITA 2007 Subpart EE

New Zealand allows depreciation on assets with a limited useful life that decline in value. Inland Revenue publishes binding depreciation rate tables (DEP) by asset type.

#### Methods Available

| Method | Description | Legal Reference |
|---|---|---|
| **Diminishing Value (DV)** | Percentage applied to adjusted tax value each year | ITA 2007 §EE 12 |
| **Straight-Line (SL)** | Equal annual deductions based on cost | ITA 2007 §EE 12 |

Taxpayers may choose either method for each asset. The DV rate is generally 1.5x the SL rate.

#### Key Asset Rates (IRD Depreciation Tables)

| Asset Category | DV Rate | SL Rate | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Buildings** | **0%** | **0%** | Non-depreciable from 2011 income year (ITA 2007 §EE 33) |
| Commercial fit-out | **8–20%** DV | **5.5–13.5%** SL | Depending on component |
| **Motor vehicles** | **30%** DV | **21%** SL | General rate |
| **Computers (laptops/desktops)** | **50%** DV | **40%** SL | |
| **Computer servers** | **30–40%** DV | **21–30%** SL | |
| **Office furniture** | **12–20%** DV | **8–13.5%** SL | |
| **Plant & machinery (general)** | **10–30%** DV | **7–21%** SL | Varies by industry |
| **Software (purchased)** | **50%** DV | **40%** SL | |
| **Software (in-house, >NZ$500K)** | **36%** DV | **25.2%** SL | Capitalised |
| **Patents** | Cost / legal life | Cost / legal life | Over remaining patent life |

#### Buildings — 0% Depreciation

> **Legal Basis**: ITA 2007 §EE 33; Taxation (Annual Rates for 2024–25) Act 2024

| Period | Commercial/Industrial Buildings (≥ 50-year life) | Residential Buildings |
|---|---|---|
| 2011–12 to 2019–20 | **0%** | **0%** |
| 2020–21 to 2023–24 | **2% DV / 1.5% SL** (temporarily reinstated) | **0%** |
| From 2024–25 onward | **0%** (removed again) | **0%** |

Building fit-out and fixed plant within buildings remain depreciable at standard rates regardless of the building rate.

### 1.2 Low-Value Assets

> **Legal Basis**: ITA 2007 §EE 38

| Parameter | Value |
|---|---|
| Threshold | **NZ$1,000** (cost exclusive of GST, if GST-registered) |
| Treatment | Immediate deduction in year of purchase |
| Pooling option | Assets NZ$1,000–NZ$5,000 may be pooled at a single DV rate |

### 1.3 Provisional Depreciation Rates

If an asset is not listed in the IRD tables, taxpayers may apply to the Commissioner for a provisional rate (ITA 2007 §EE 29).

## 2. Business Operating Expenses

### 2.1 General Deduction Rule

> **Legal Basis**: ITA 2007 §DA 1

A person is allowed a deduction for an amount of expenditure or loss to the extent that it is incurred in deriving their assessable income or in carrying on a business for the purpose of deriving assessable income.

| Expense Category | Deductibility | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Salaries, wages, bonuses | **100%** | Must be arm's length for related parties |
| KiwiSaver employer contributions | **100%** | Compulsory employer contribution 3% |
| ACC levies (employer) | **100%** | Accident Compensation Corporation |
| Rent and lease payments | **100%** | Business premises |
| Repairs and maintenance | **100%** | Revenue in nature; improvements are capital |
| Insurance premiums | **100%** | Business-related |
| Legal and accounting fees | **100%** | Revenue nature; not capital |
| Advertising and marketing | **100%** | |
| Travel expenses (business) | **100%** | Domestic and international; FBT may apply to motor vehicles |
| Bad debts written off | **100%** | Must have been previously returned as income — §DB 31 |
| Trading stock (cost of goods sold) | **100%** | Cost, market selling price, or replacement cost — Subpart EB |
| Motor vehicle expenses | **100%** or apportioned | Business-use proportion; logbook or IRD mileage rate |

### 2.2 Charitable Donations

> **Legal Basis**: ITA 2007 §DV 12

| Parameter | Value |
|---|---|
| Companies | Deductible donations to approved donee organisations |
| Limit | Total donations must not exceed company's net income for the year |
| Carry-forward | Not available for companies |
| Qualifying recipients | Organisations on the IRD approved donee list |

### 2.3 Entertainment Expenses

> **Legal Basis**: ITA 2007 §DD 1–DD 4

| Type | Deductibility |
|---|---|
| Client entertainment (meals, hospitality) | **50%** deductible |
| Staff entertainment (Christmas function etc.) | **50%** deductible |
| Employer-provided light refreshments at workplace | **100%** deductible |
| Conference/seminar costs | **100%** deductible (if principally business) |

## 3. Interest & Financing Costs

### 3.1 General Interest Deductibility

> **Legal Basis**: ITA 2007 §DB 6–DB 8

Interest is deductible to the extent it relates to income-earning activity. The nexus test requires a direct connection between the borrowing and the derivation of assessable income.

### 3.2 Thin Capitalisation

> **Legal Basis**: ITA 2007 §FE 1–FE 18

| Parameter | Value |
|---|---|
| **Inbound investment** | Maximum debt percentage: **60%** of group assets — §FE 6 |
| **Outbound investment** | Maximum 110% of worldwide group debt ratio — §FE 12 |
| **De minimis** | NZ$10 million of total group debt (entities below exempt) |
| **Excess interest** | Non-deductible |
| **Measurement** | Based on New Zealand group's assets and liabilities |

### 3.3 Interest Limitation Rules (Residential Property)

> **Legal Basis**: ITA 2007 Subpart DH

From 1 October 2021, interest on residential investment property loans is no longer deductible for properties acquired on or after 27 March 2021. For properties acquired before that date, deductibility is being phased out (50% in 2024–25, 0% from 2025–26). New builds are exempt from the restriction.

### 3.4 Related-Party Interest

Interest paid to associated persons must be at arm's length rates. The transfer pricing rules in Subpart GC apply to cross-border related-party financing.

## 4. Research & Development

New Zealand provides a 15% R&D tax credit under the Research and Development Tax Incentive (RDTI) for eligible R&D expenditure incurred in New Zealand, administered by Callaghan Innovation. The minimum spend threshold is NZ$50,000 per year and the maximum eligible expenditure is NZ$120 million.

See [R&D Incentives](rnd_incentives.md) for full program detail.

## 5. Loss Carry-Forward & Carry-Back

### 5.1 Loss Carry-Forward

> **Legal Basis**: ITA 2007 §IA 3–IA 5

| Parameter | Value |
|---|---|
| Duration | **Indefinite** carry-forward |
| Continuity requirement | At least **49%** commonality of ownership must be maintained — §IA 5 |
| Business continuity test | Available from 2020–21 if ownership test fails — §IB 3 |
| Ring-fencing | Certain losses (e.g., rental property from 2019–20) ring-fenced to that activity |

### 5.2 Loss Carry-Back

> **Legal Basis**: ITA 2007 §IZ 8 (temporary COVID measure — expired)

| Parameter | Value |
|---|---|
| Status | **Not currently available** |
| History | Temporary loss carry-back was available for 2019–20 losses carried back to 2018–19 (COVID-19 response); not extended |

### 5.3 Group Loss Offsets

> **Legal Basis**: ITA 2007 §IC 1–IC 10

| Parameter | Value |
|---|---|
| Available | Yes, between companies in same 66% common ownership group |
| Current year only | Losses can only be offset in the year incurred |
| Subvention payment | The profit company must make a subvention payment (actual or notional) to the loss company |

## 7. Industry-Specific Deductions

### 7.1 Farming & Agriculture

> **Legal Basis**: ITA 2007 Subpart DO

| Item | Treatment |
|---|---|
| Fertiliser expenditure | Deductible in year incurred — §DO 1 |
| Drainage expenditure | Deductible in year incurred — §DO 1 |
| Planting & development (farming) | Deductible over 5 years (certain improvements) |

### 7.2 Forestry

> **Legal Basis**: ITA 2007 Subpart DP

| Item | Treatment |
|---|---|
| Planting expenditure | Deductible when trees harvested or forest sold |
| Costs of maintaining immature forest | Deductible currently |

### 7.3 Petroleum Mining

> **Legal Basis**: ITA 2007 Subpart DT

| Item | Treatment |
|---|---|

## 8. Treaties & Transfer Pricing

### 8.1 Transfer Pricing

> **Legal Basis**: ITA 2007 Subpart GC (§GC 6–GC 14)

| Parameter | Value |
|---|---|
| Arm's length principle | Mandatory for all cross-border associated-party transactions |
| Methods accepted | CUP, resale price, cost plus, TNMM, profit split |
| Documentation | Contemporaneous documentation expected; no prescribed format |
| Penalties | Shortfall penalties (20%–150%) for incorrect tax positions |

### 8.2 Country-by-Country Reporting

| Parameter | Value |
|---|---|
| Threshold | Consolidated group revenue ≥ EUR 750 million |
| Filing | CbC report filed with Inland Revenue |
| Exchange | Exchanged under Multilateral Competent Authority Agreement |

### 8.3 Treaty Network

New Zealand has over 40 double tax agreements. The Multilateral Instrument (MLI) applies to modify existing treaties with BEPS-related provisions.
