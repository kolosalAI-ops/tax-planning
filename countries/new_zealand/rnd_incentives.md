# New Zealand - R&D Tax Incentives

## 1. Overview

| Feature | Details |
|---|---|
| **Primary Incentive** | Research and Development Tax Incentive (RDTI) - 15% tax credit |
| **Minimum Spend** | NZ$50,000 per annum |
| **Refundability** | Refundable up to NZ$255,000 (for R&D intensity > 20%) |
| **Grants** | Callaghan Innovation growth grants and project grants |
| **Governing Authority** | Inland Revenue (IR); Callaghan Innovation; Ministry of Business, Innovation and Employment (MBIE) |
| **Key Legislation** | Income Tax Act 2007 (ITA 2007) Subpart LY; Tax Administration Act 1994 |
| **Corporate Tax Rate** | 28% |

## 2. Detailed Programs

### 2.1 R&D Tax Incentive (RDTI) - ITA 2007 Subpart LY

**Legal Basis:** Income Tax Act 2007, Subpart LY (Sections LY 1-LY 7)

| Parameter | Details |
|---|---|
| **Credit Rate** | 15% of eligible R&D expenditure |
| **Minimum Spend** | NZ$50,000 per annum (aggregated group basis) |
| **Maximum Spend** | No statutory cap (previously NZ$120M; cap removed) |
| **Refundability** | Refundable up to NZ$255,000 per annum if R&D intensity > 20% |
| **Non-Refundable** | Credits exceeding refund cap carried forward to offset future tax |
| **Carry-Forward** | Indefinite carry-forward of unused non-refundable credits |

**R&D Intensity Test for Refundability:**

| Parameter | Details |
|---|---|
| **R&D Intensity** | Eligible R&D expenditure / total operating expenditure |
| **Threshold** | ≥ 20% R&D intensity required for refundability |
| **Maximum Refund** | NZ$255,000 per year |
| **Purpose** | Support pre-revenue R&D-intensive startups |

### 2.2 Eligible R&D Activities

**Core R&D Activities:**
- Activities conducted using a systematic approach
- Purpose: resolving scientific or technological uncertainty
- Must involve creating new knowledge or new/improved processes, services, or products

**Supporting Activities:**
- Activities that are required for, and integral to, core R&D activities
- Must be closely connected to core activities

**Excluded Activities:**

| Excluded Activity | Reason |
|---|---|
| Market research | Commercial, not scientific/technological |
| Quality control | Routine activity |
| Social science research | Not scientific/technological |
| Routine data collection | No uncertainty |
| Cosmetic modifications | Not innovation |
| Mineral exploration | Separate tax regime |
| Pre-production activities | After uncertainty resolved |

### 2.3 Eligible R&D Expenditure

| Category | Eligibility |
|---|---|
| **Employee costs** | Salary/wages for time spent on eligible R&D |
| **Contractor costs** | Payments to contractors for R&D services |
| **Consumables** | Materials consumed in R&D activities |
| **Depreciation** | Assets used primarily for R&D |
| **Overhead** | Proportionate overhead allocable to R&D |
| **Software** | Software used primarily in R&D activities |
| **IP costs** | Patent costs related to R&D outcomes |

**Expenditure Limitations:**
- Feedstock: only net cost (after subtracting value of outputs)
- Internal software development: eligible if meeting core R&D criteria
- Overseas expenditure: capped at 10% of total eligible expenditure

### 2.4 Callaghan Innovation Grants

**Administered by:** Callaghan Innovation (Crown entity)

| Grant Type | Details |
|---|---|
| **Growth Grant** | 20% co-funding for R&D expenditure (for firms with > NZ$300K R&D spend) |
| **Project Grant** | 40-50% co-funding for specific R&D projects |
| **Student Grant** | Funding for graduate/postgraduate researchers in business |
| **Getting Started Grant** | Up to NZ$5,000 for R&D feasibility studies |
| **Eligibility** | New Zealand-based businesses |

**Note:** Companies cannot claim both RDTI and Callaghan Innovation Growth Grant on the same expenditure. Must choose one or the other. Project grants can be combined with RDTI on non-overlapping expenditure.

### 2.5 General Approval vs. Criteria and Methodology Approach

| Approach | Details |
|---|---|
| **General Approval** | Apply to Callaghan Innovation for advance approval of R&D activities |
| **Criteria and Methodology** | Self-assess eligibility using IR criteria; no advance approval |
| **Recommendation** | General approval provides certainty; C&M suitable for experienced claimants |
| **Threshold** | Claims > NZ$2M encouraged to seek general approval |

## 3. Patent Box / IP Regime

New Zealand does not have a patent box or IP box regime.

| Aspect | Status |
|---|---|
| **Patent Box** | Not available |
| **IP Amortization** | No specific IP amortization regime |
| **IP Transfer** | Subject to income tax on gains |
| **Royalty WHT** | 15% NRWT on payments to non-residents (reduced under treaties) |
| **R&D IP** | IP generated from RDTI-supported R&D has no special tax treatment |
| **Policy Position** | Government has not announced plans for a patent box |

**Note:** New Zealand's tax policy focuses on broad-base, low-rate principles. The RDTI at 15% is relatively modest compared to other APAC countries, reflecting this philosophy. IP income is taxed at the standard 28% corporate rate.

## 4. Effective Benefit Analysis (per $1M USD R&D Spend)

*Assuming NZD/USD exchange rate of 0.60; NZ$1,666,667 equivalent spend; 28% CIT rate*

| Scenario | Credit/Grant | Benefit (NZD) | Benefit (USD) | Effective Subsidy |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **RDTI (15% credit)** | 15% tax credit | NZ$250,000 | ~$150,000 | 15.0% |
| **RDTI (refundable portion)** | Up to NZ$255,000 | NZ$255,000 max | ~$153,000 max | N/A |
| **Callaghan Growth Grant** | 20% co-funding | NZ$333,333 | ~$200,000 | 20.0% |
| **Callaghan Project Grant** | 40-50% co-funding | NZ$666,667-833,333 | ~$400,000-500,000 | 40-50% |
| **Net Position (RDTI only)** | 15% credit on expenses already deducted at 28% | NZ$250,000 | ~$150,000 | 15.0% |

**RDTI vs. Callaghan Innovation Comparison:**

| Factor | RDTI | Callaghan Growth Grant |
|---|---|---|
| **Rate** | 15% | 20% |
| **Certainty** | Self-assessed or pre-approved | Application-based |
| **Cap** | No cap | Budget-constrained |
| **Administration** | Tax return claim | Separate grant application |
| **Best For** | Large-scale R&D | Companies meeting grant criteria |

**Key Insight:** Callaghan Innovation Growth Grant (20%) is more generous than RDTI (15%) but is subject to budget constraints and application approval. RDTI provides greater certainty and scalability. Companies with > NZ$300K R&D spend should compare both options.

## 5. Claim Process & Documentation

### 5.1 RDTI Filing Process

| Step | Action | Timeline |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Determine eligible R&D activities | During income year |
| 2 | Optional: seek general approval from Callaghan Innovation | Before or during income year |
| 3 | Track eligible expenditure separately | Ongoing |
| 4 | Complete R&D supplementary return | With income tax return |
| 5 | File income tax return (IR4) with R&D tax credit claim | Standard filing date (typically 7 July or extension) |
| 6 | IR processes credit/refund | 30-60 days post-filing |

### 5.2 Documentation Requirements

- **R&D activity records:** Description of core and supporting R&D activities
- **Scientific/technological uncertainty:** Documentation of the uncertainty being addressed
- **Systematic approach:** Evidence of hypothesis, experimentation, analysis
- **Expenditure records:** Detailed allocation by eligible category and activity
- **Time records:** Employee time spent on R&D activities (contemporaneous)
- **Contractor records:** Contracts and deliverables for outsourced R&D
- **Overseas R&D records:** Tracking to ensure within 10% cap
- **General approval documentation:** If obtained from Callaghan Innovation

### 5.3 In-Year Payments

| Parameter | Details |
|---|---|
| **In-Year Payments** | RDTI can be claimed via reduced provisional tax payments |
| **Benefit** | Cash flow advantage for R&D-intensive companies |
| **Requirement** | Must have reasonable expectation of R&D credit entitlement |

## 6. Recent Changes & Sunset Provisions

| Change | Effective Date | Details |
|---|---|---|
| **RDTI Introduction** | April 1, 2019 | 15% R&D tax credit introduced |
| **Cap Removal** | 2021 | NZ$120M expenditure cap removed |
| **Refundability Enhancement** | 2022 | Refundable amount increased to NZ$255,000 |
| **Overseas R&D Cap** | 2020 | 10% cap on overseas R&D expenditure |
| **In-Year Payments** | 2021 | Ability to reduce provisional tax for expected R&D credits |
| **Criteria Refinement** | Ongoing | IR continues to refine eligible activity criteria |

**Sunset Provisions:**
- RDTI: **No sunset clause** - permanent feature of Income Tax Act 2007
- Callaghan Innovation grants: Subject to annual government budget appropriation
- 15% rate: No announced changes; rate is set in legislation
- NZ$50,000 minimum: Under periodic review
- Government has signaled continued support for R&D investment
