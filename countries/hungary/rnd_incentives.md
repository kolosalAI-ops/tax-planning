# Hungary - R&D Tax Incentives

## 1. Overview

| Feature | Details |
|---|---|
| **Primary Incentive** | R&D Super-Deduction (200% deduction) |
| **Deduction Rate** | 200% of qualifying R&D costs |
| **IP Royalty Exemption** | 50% exemption on qualifying IP royalty income |
| **Researcher Social Contribution** | 50% exemption on social contribution tax for R&D staff |
| **CIT Rate** | 9% (lowest in the EU) |
| **Legal Basis** | Act LXXXI of 1996 on Corporate Tax (Tao.), §7(1)(t) and §7(1)(s) |
| **NRDI Office** | National Research, Development and Innovation Office certification |
| **Carry-Forward** | 5 years |
| **Refundability** | Not refundable |

## 2. Detailed Programs

### 2.1 R&D Super-Deduction (200%)

**Legal Basis:** Act LXXXI of 1996 on Corporate Tax and Dividend Tax (Társasági adóról és az osztalékadóról szóló törvény / Tao.), §7(1)(t)

Hungarian companies can deduct **200%** of qualifying R&D costs from their corporate tax base — the full cost as a normal business expense plus an additional 100% as an R&D incentive.

#### Deduction Structure

| Component | Rate | Description |
|---|---|---|
| Standard business deduction | 100% | Normal deductibility of R&D as business expense |
| Additional R&D deduction | 100% | Extra deduction under §7(1)(t) |
| **Total deduction** | **200%** | Combined effect |

#### Effective Tax Benefit

Given Hungary's exceptionally low CIT rate of **9%**, the absolute tax benefit per unit of R&D spend is lower than in higher-CIT countries:

| CIT Rate | Deduction | Tax Benefit per €1 of R&D |
|---|---|---|
| Hungary (9%) | 200% | €0.18 |
| Comparison: Poland (19%) | 200% | €0.38 |
| Comparison: France (25%) | 200% | €0.50 |

**While the deduction percentage is high, the low CIT rate limits the absolute benefit.**

#### Qualifying R&D Activities

Under Hungarian law, R&D is defined in alignment with the OECD Frascati Manual:

- Basic research (alapkutatás)
- Applied research (alkalmazott kutatás)
- Experimental development (kísérleti fejlesztés)
- Must be creative, systematic work aimed at increasing knowledge or new applications
- Must involve novelty and scientific/technical uncertainty

#### Qualifying Expenditure

| Cost Category | Eligible | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Personnel costs (R&D employees) | Yes | Direct wages, salaries, social contributions |
| Materials and supplies for R&D | Yes | Directly consumed in R&D |
| Subcontracted R&D services | Yes | From both domestic and foreign providers |
| Depreciation of R&D equipment | Yes | Proportional to R&D use |
| Overhead directly allocable to R&D | Yes | Reasonable allocation |
| Instruments and equipment (under HUF 200K) | Yes | Expensed directly |
| Patent and IP registration costs | Yes | Related to R&D outcomes |

### 2.2 IP Royalty Income Exemption (50%)

**Legal Basis:** Tao. §7(1)(s)

| Parameter | Details |
|---|---|
| Exemption rate | 50% of qualifying royalty income deducted from tax base |
| Qualifying income | Royalties from licensing patents, know-how, copyright, trade secrets |
| Effective CIT rate on royalties | 4.5% (50% exemption on 9% CIT rate) |
| Nexus requirement | No formal OECD nexus approach (grandfathered/domestic regime) |
| Combined with R&D deduction | Yes — input-side deduction and output-side exemption can coexist |

#### Qualifying IP for Royalty Exemption

| IP Type | Eligible |
|---|---|
| Patents | Yes |
| Know-how | Yes |
| Copyright (including software) | Yes |
| Trade secrets | Yes |
| Trademarks | Partially (limited) |
| Industrial designs | Yes |

### 2.3 Social Contribution Tax Exemption for Researchers

**Legal Basis:** Act LII of 2018 on Social Contribution Tax (Szociális hozzájárulási adó / Szocho), related provisions

| Parameter | Details |
|---|---|
| Exemption rate | 50% of social contribution tax for qualifying R&D employees |
| Social contribution tax rate | 13% (reduced from previous levels) |
| Effective rate for R&D staff | 6.5% (50% of 13%) |
| Qualifying employees | Researchers and R&D staff directly involved in R&D projects |
| NRDI certification | May be required to confirm R&D nature of activities |

#### Combined Employment Cost Benefit

For R&D researchers, the social contribution exemption reduces the overall employment cost:

| Cost Component | Standard Rate | R&D Staff Rate |
|---|---|---|
| Gross salary | 100% | 100% |
| Social contribution tax (Szocho) | 13% | 6.5% (50% exempt) |
| **Total employer cost** | **113%** of gross | **106.5%** of gross |
| **Saving per employee** | — | **6.5%** of gross salary |

### 2.4 NRDI Office Certification

**Legal Basis:** Act CXXXIV of 2004 on Research, Development and Innovation

The **National Research, Development and Innovation Office (NKFI Hivatal / NRDI Office)** provides certification services:

| Service | Details |
|---|---|
| R&D project certification | Confirms that activities qualify as R&D for tax purposes |
| Binding effect | Certification is binding on the tax authority |
| Voluntary | Not mandatory, but provides legal certainty |
| Processing time | Typically 60-90 days |
| Validity | For the certified project and tax years specified |
| Cost | Administrative fee applies |

### 2.5 Additional Innovation Incentives

| Incentive | Details | Legal Basis |
|---|---|---|
| Innovation contribution exemption | R&D centres can be exempt from the innovation contribution (0.3% of adjusted net revenue) | Act CXXXIV of 2004 |
| Local business tax deduction | 50% of direct R&D costs deductible from local business tax base | Act C of 1990 |
| Development tax incentive | CIT credit for large investment projects including R&D facilities | Tao. §22/B |

## 3. Patent Box / IP Regime

### Hungarian IP Regime Summary

Hungary's IP regime operates through the **50% royalty income exemption** under Tao. §7(1)(s), rather than a formal "patent box" with nexus requirements.

| Feature | Details |
|---|---|
| Mechanism | 50% deduction of royalty income from CIT base |
| Effective tax rate | 4.5% on qualifying royalties (50% × 9% CIT) |
| OECD nexus compliance | Not fully nexus-compliant in the BEPS Action 5 sense |
| Qualifying income | Royalties, licensing fees, embedded IP income |
| IP transfer gains | 50% exemption may also apply to IP disposal gains |
| Anti-abuse rules | Transfer pricing rules apply; substance requirements |

### Comparison with Formal Patent Box Regimes

| Feature | Hungary | Netherlands | Ireland |
|---|---|---|---|
| Effective IP rate | 4.5% | ~9% | 6.25% |
| Nexus approach | Limited | Full OECD nexus | Full OECD nexus |
| Qualifying IP | Broad | Patents, software | Patents, software, IP |
| CIT rate | 9% | 25.8% | 12.5% |
| EU Code of Conduct | Under review | Compliant | Compliant |

## 4. Effective Benefit Analysis (per $1M USD R&D Spend)

*Assumptions: Exchange rate ~HUF 370/USD; CIT rate 9%; company is profitable; social contribution savings estimated*

### Scenario A: R&D Deduction Only

| Benefit Component | Calculation | Value (USD) |
|---|---|---|
| Base R&D expenditure | — | $1,000,000 |
| Standard CIT deduction (9%) | $1,000,000 × 9% | $90,000 |
| Additional R&D deduction (100% × 9%) | $1,000,000 × 100% × 9% | $90,000 |
| **Total CIT benefit** | | **$180,000** |
| **Effective CIT rate of support** | | **18.0%** |

### Scenario B: R&D Deduction + Social Contribution Savings

| Benefit Component | Calculation | Value (USD) |
|---|---|---|
| CIT benefit (from above) | | $180,000 |
| Assume 60% of R&D is personnel ($600K) | | |
| Social contribution saving (6.5% of gross) | $600,000 × 6.5% | $39,000 |
| **Total benefit** | | **$219,000** |
| **Effective rate** | | **21.9%** |

### Scenario C: R&D Deduction + IP Royalty Exemption

| Benefit Component | Calculation | Value (USD) |
|---|---|---|
| R&D deduction CIT benefit | | $180,000 |
| Social contribution saving | | $39,000 |
| IP royalty exemption: $500K royalties × 50% × 9% | | $22,500 |
| **Total benefit** | | **$241,500** |

### Scenario Comparison

| Scenario | CIT Benefit | Szocho Saving | IP Exemption | Total | Effective Rate |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| R&D deduction only | $180,000 | — | — | $180,000 | 18.0% |
| R&D + social contribution | $180,000 | $39,000 | — | $219,000 | 21.9% |
| R&D + social + IP royalties | $180,000 | $39,000 | $22,500 | $241,500 | 24.2%* |
| Loss-making company | $0 (carry fwd) | $39,000 | — | $39,000 | 3.9% |

*IP exemption calculated on assumed $500K royalty income, not directly additive to $1M R&D spend.

**Key Insight:** Hungary's absolute tax benefit is limited by the 9% CIT rate. The real advantage is the low overall tax burden — Hungary's base CIT rate already provides a significant cost advantage over higher-tax jurisdictions.

## 5. Claim Process & Documentation

### Step-by-Step Claim Process

1. **Identify Qualifying R&D** — Assess against Frascati Manual and Hungarian statutory definitions
2. **Optional: NRDI Certification** — Request binding certification from the NRDI Office
3. **Track Qualifying Costs** — Segregate R&D expenditure by category
4. **Maintain Project Records** — Contemporaneous documentation of R&D activities
5. **File CIT Return (Társasági adóbevallás)** — Claim R&D deduction in annual return
6. **Social Contribution Claim** — Apply exemption through payroll reporting
7. **IP Royalty Exemption** — Declare qualifying royalty income in CIT return
8. **Carry Forward** — Unused R&D deductions carried forward up to 5 years

### Documentation Requirements

| Requirement | Details |
|---|---|
| R&D project descriptions | Objectives, methodology, novelty, uncertainty |
| NRDI certificate (optional) | Binding confirmation of R&D qualification |
| Personnel records | Time sheets allocating hours to R&D projects |
| Cost records | Detailed expenditure breakdown per project |
| Subcontracting agreements | Contracts for outsourced R&D services |
| IP documentation | Records of qualifying IP for royalty exemption |
| Social contribution records | Payroll documentation for researcher exemption |
| Results documentation | Technical outcomes and progress reports |

## 6. Recent Changes & Sunset Provisions

| Year | Change | Impact |
|---|---|---|
| 2017 | CIT rate reduced to 9% (from 19%) | Reduced absolute R&D benefit but improved overall competitiveness |
| 2019 | Social contribution tax reduced (gradually to 13%) | Lower base rate reduces absolute exemption value |
| 2022 | Enhanced guidance on NRDI certification | Greater clarity on qualifying activities |
| 2023 | Global minimum tax discussions intensified | Hungary initially resisted Pillar Two implementation |
| 2024 | Pillar Two implementation enacted (delayed) | 15% top-up tax for qualifying large MNEs |
| 2025 | R&D deduction parameters maintained | No changes to 200% deduction or IP exemption |

### Sunset Provisions

- The R&D deduction under Tao. §7(1)(t) is a **permanent provision** — no sunset date
- The IP royalty exemption under §7(1)(s) is also permanent
- The social contribution exemption is permanent but rates are subject to change
- Hungary's Pillar Two implementation may create a minimum 15% effective rate for large MNEs, partially offsetting benefits
- Political commitment to maintaining Hungary's tax competitiveness remains strong

### Pillar Two Impact

- For groups within scope of Pillar Two (€750M+ consolidated revenue), the effective benefit of Hungary's low CIT rate is reduced
- Top-up tax may apply to bring effective rate to 15%
- R&D deductions reduce the effective tax rate, potentially triggering higher top-up tax
- QDMTT (Qualified Domestic Minimum Top-Up Tax) introduced to capture top-up tax domestically
- Detailed modeling needed for large MNEs to assess net benefit
