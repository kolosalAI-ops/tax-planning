## 1. Federal Corporate Income Tax (IRC §11)

### Current Rate (Post-TCJA, effective 2018+)

| Taxable Income | Rate |
|---|---|
| All taxable income | **21% flat rate** (IRC §11(b)) |

The TCJA of 2017 replaced the previous graduated rate structure with a single flat 21% rate effective January 1, 2018, per IRC §11(b). This applies to all C-corporations regardless of income level.

## 9. Tax Residence & Effectively Connected Income

### Tax Residence Definition (IRC §7701(a)(30))

A corporation is treated as a **domestic corporation** if it is created or organized in the United States or under the law of the United States or of any State (IRC §7701(a)(4)). A **foreign corporation** is any corporation that is not a domestic corporation (IRC §7701(a)(5)). The term "United States person" is defined under IRC §7701(a)(30) and includes any domestic corporation.

### Effectively Connected Income (IRC §882)

A foreign corporation engaged in a trade or business within the United States is taxed at regular corporate rates on income that is **effectively connected** with that U.S. trade or business (IRC §882(a)). Key provisions:

| Parameter | Value |
|---|---|
| Tax rate on ECI | Standard 21% corporate rate (IRC §11(b)) |
| Branch profits tax | **30%** on dividend equivalent amount (IRC §884) |
| Treaty override | Reduced or eliminated under applicable tax treaties |
| Force of attraction rule | Certain FDAP income may be treated as ECI (IRC §882(a)(2)) |

## 10. Income by Source Type

### 10.1 Service Income (Domestic & Cross-Border)

#### Domestic Service Income

Domestic service income is taxed as ordinary business income at the federal corporate income tax rate.

| Parameter | Value | Legal Basis |
|---|---|---|
| Federal CIT rate | **21%** flat | IRC §11(b) |
| State CIT | Varies (0%-11.5%) | State statutes |
| QBI deduction (pass-throughs) | 20% deduction on qualified business income | IRC §199A |

#### Cross-Border Service Income

The tax treatment of cross-border services depends on whether the income constitutes **Effectively Connected Income (ECI)** or **Fixed, Determinable, Annual, Periodical (FDAP)** income.

| Classification | Tax Treatment | Legal Basis |
|---|---|---|
| ECI (trade or business in US) | Taxed at graduated/flat 21% CIT rate on net basis | IRC §871(b), §882 |
| FDAP (no US trade or business) | 30% gross-basis withholding (or treaty rate) | IRC §871(a), §881 |
| Services PE risk under treaties | If a PE exists, income attributable to PE is taxed as ECI | US Model Treaty Art. 7 |

#### FDII Deduction for Service Income

US corporations providing services to foreign persons for foreign use may qualify for the **Foreign-Derived Intangible Income (FDII)** deduction:

| Parameter | Value | Legal Basis |
|---|---|---|
| FDII deduction | **37.5%** of FDII amount | IRC §250(a)(1)(A) |
| Effective tax rate | **13.125%** (21% × (1 - 37.5%)) | IRC §250 |
| Post-2025 deduction | 21.875% (effective rate 16.406%) | IRC §250(a)(2) |

```
Calculation Example:
  Revenue from foreign services:         $10,000,000
  Allocable deductions:                  ($2,000,000)
  Deemed Intangible Income (DII):         $8,000,000
  Less: 10% × QBAI (e.g., $20M):        ($2,000,000)
  Excess DII:                             $6,000,000
  Foreign-derived ratio:                  70%
  FDII:                                   $4,200,000
  §250 deduction (37.5%):               ($1,575,000)
  Taxable FDII:                           $2,625,000
  Tax at 21%:                              $551,250
  Effective rate on FDII:                  13.125%
```

#### Transfer Pricing for Intercompany Services

| Requirement | Details | Legal Basis |
|---|---|---|
| Arm's length standard | Intercompany service charges must reflect arm's length pricing | IRC §482 |
| Acceptable methods | Services Cost Method (SCM), Comparable Uncontrolled Services Price, Cost of Services Plus, etc. | Treas. Reg. §1.482-9 |
| Low-margin services safe harbor | SCM: cost with no markup for qualifying low-margin services | Treas. Reg. §1.482-9(b) |
| Documentation | Contemporaneous documentation required; penalties for non-compliance | IRC §6662(e) |

### 10.5 Dividend Income (Received by Company)

#### Dividends-Received Deduction (DRD)

The DRD reduces the effective tax on intercorporate dividends to avoid triple taxation:

| Ownership Level | DRD Percentage | Effective Tax Rate | Legal Basis |
|---|---|---|---|
| Less than 20% | **50%** | 10.5% (21% × 50%) | IRC §243(a)(1) |
| 20% to less than 80% | **65%** | 7.35% (21% × 35%) | IRC §243(c) |
| 80% or more (affiliated) | **100%** | 0% | IRC §243(a)(3) |

```
DRD Calculation Example (< 20% ownership):
  Dividend received:           $1,000,000
  DRD (50%):                  ($500,000)
  Taxable dividend:             $500,000
  Tax at 21%:                   $105,000
  Effective rate:               10.5%
```

> **Limitation**: The DRD cannot exceed the corresponding percentage of the corporation's taxable income (computed without the DRD, NOL deduction, and certain other items), unless it creates or increases an NOL (IRC §246(b)).

#### Section 245A Participation Exemption

| Parameter | Value | Legal Basis |
|---|---|---|
| Deduction | **100%** of foreign-source portion of dividends | IRC §245A(a) |
| Eligibility | US corporate shareholder owning **10%+** of CFC (by vote or value) | IRC §245A(b) |
| Holding period | 365+ days during 731-day period | IRC §246(c) modified |
| Hybrid dividends | §245A deduction **not available** for hybrid dividends | IRC §245A(e) |
| Effective date | Post-TCJA (tax years after 12/31/2017) | P.L. 115-97 §14101 |

> **Note**: §245A represents the US shift to a **territorial-style** system for foreign earnings, replacing the prior worldwide system with deferral.

#### Special Rules

| Rule | Details | Legal Basis |
|---|---|---|
| Extraordinary dividends | Basis reduction required if dividend exceeds 10% (preferred) or 5% (other) of basis | IRC §1059 |
| REIT dividends | Generally **no DRD** available | IRC §243(d)(1) |
| Debt-financed portfolio stock | DRD reduced proportionally to debt financing | IRC §246A |
| Holding period requirement | Must hold stock 46+ days (91 days for preferred) during 91-day (181-day) window | IRC §246(c) |

| Resource | Percentage Depletion Rate | Legal Basis |

#### Windfall Profit Tax (Historical)

The Crude Oil Windfall Profit Tax Act of 1980 imposed an excise tax on domestically produced crude oil based on the difference between market price and a statutory base price. The tax was **repealed in 1988** (P.L. 100-418). No current federal windfall profit tax exists, though proposals have surfaced periodically.

### 11.4 Financial Transaction Taxes

#### Federal Status

The United States **does not impose** a federal financial transaction tax (FTT).

| Parameter | Status | Details |
|---|---|---|
| Federal FTT | **None** | No enacted FTT |
| Proposed legislation | Various bills introduced (not enacted) | E.g., Wall Street Tax Act, Inclusive Prosperity Act |
| SEC §31 fee | **De minimis** regulatory fee | Securities Exchange Act §31 |

#### SEC Section 31 Transaction Fees

| Parameter | Value | Legal Basis |
|---|---|---|
| Fee rate (FY 2024) | **$8.00 per $1,000,000** of covered sales | §31 Securities Exchange Act of 1934 |
| Covered transactions | Sales of securities on exchanges and certain OTC transactions | §31(b) |
| Paid by | Exchanges and FINRA (passed through to broker-dealers) | §31(d) |
| Purpose | Fund SEC operations | Not a tax; regulatory fee |

> **Note**: The SEC §31 fee is adjusted periodically (typically annually) based on SEC's congressionally appropriated budget. It is **not a tax** in the traditional sense but rather a regulatory cost-recovery fee.

#### State-Level Financial Transaction Taxes

No US state currently imposes a significant financial transaction tax. New York briefly considered an FTT proposal but did not enact it. The historical New York State Stock Transfer Tax (Tax Law §270) still technically exists but has been 100% rebated since 1981.

### 11.6 Agricultural Income Taxation

#### General Treatment

Farm income is taxed as **ordinary business income** under the general provisions of the Internal Revenue Code. However, numerous special provisions apply to agricultural operations.

| Parameter | Value | Legal Basis |
|---|---|---|
| Tax treatment | Ordinary business income | IRC §61 |
| Cash method available | **Yes** — farmers may use cash method even if otherwise required to use accrual | IRC §447 exception, IRC §448(d)(1) |
| Fiscal year | Farm corporations with ≤$25M gross receipts may use fiscal year | IRC §441, §447(c) |

#### Special Agricultural Provisions

| Provision | Details | Legal Basis |
|---|---|---|
| Crop insurance proceeds deferral | May defer crop insurance proceeds to following year if income would normally be reported then | IRC §451(f) |
| Disaster payments | May also be deferred under same rules | IRC §451(f) |
| Conservation Reserve Program (CRP) | CRP payments are **taxable income** but subject to self-employment tax | IRC §1402; CRP per USDA |
| USDA subsidies | Generally **taxable** as ordinary income | IRC §61 |
| Conservation expenses | Deductible: soil/water conservation, endangered species recovery, farmland preservation | IRC §175 |
| Land clearing expenses | Must be capitalized (no current deduction since 1986) | IRC §182 (repealed) |

#### Farm Net Operating Loss (NOL) Rules

| Parameter | Value | Legal Basis |
|---|---|---|
| Carry-back | **2-year** carry-back for farm NOLs | IRC §172(b)(1)(B) |
| Carry-forward | **Indefinite** | IRC §172(b)(1)(A) |
| 80% limitation | Farm NOLs subject to 80% of taxable income limitation for carry-forwards (post-TCJA) | IRC §172(a) |
| Election to waive carry-back | Farmer may elect to forgo 2-year carry-back | IRC §172(b)(3) |

#### Commodity Credit Corporation (CCC) Loans

| Parameter | Value | Legal Basis |
|---|---|---|
| Default treatment | Loan proceeds are **not income** until commodity sold or forfeited | General tax principles |
| §77 election | Farmer may elect to treat CCC loan as **income in year received** | IRC §77 |
| Benefit of election | Smooths income recognition; basis in commodity increases by amount included | IRC §77(a) |

#### Section 1231 Treatment for Livestock

| Parameter | Value | Legal Basis |
|---|---|---|
| Eligible livestock | Cattle, horses, hogs, sheep, goats, mink, other fur-bearing animals | IRC §1231(b)(3) |
| Purpose | Held for **draft, breeding, dairy, or sporting purposes** | IRC §1231(b)(3) |
| Holding period — cattle/horses | **24 months** or more | IRC §1231(b)(3)(A) |
| Holding period — other livestock | **12 months** or more | IRC §1231(b)(3)(B) |
| Treatment | Net gains = long-term capital gains; net losses = ordinary losses | IRC §1231(a) |
