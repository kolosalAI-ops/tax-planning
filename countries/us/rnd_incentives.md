# United States - R&D Tax Incentives

## 1. Overview

| Parameter | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Headline Incentive** | Federal R&D Tax Credit (Research & Experimentation Credit) |
| **Mechanism Type** | Tax credit (regular and alternative simplified), super-deduction amortization, payroll tax offset |
| **Headline Rate** | 20% (Regular Credit) / 14% (Alternative Simplified Credit) |
| **Refundable** | No (but payroll tax offset available for qualifying small businesses up to $500,000) |
| **Patent Box** | No federal patent box regime |
| **Legal Basis** | Internal Revenue Code §41, §174, §45C, §48D; Treasury Regulations §1.41-1 through §1.41-9 |

## 2. Detailed Programs

### 2.1 Federal R&D Tax Credit (IRC §41)

| Parameter | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Legal Basis** | IRC §41; Treas. Reg. §1.41-1 through §1.41-9 |
| **Credit Type** | Non-refundable income tax credit (permanent, made permanent by PATH Act of 2015, P.L. 114-113) |
| **Regular Credit Rate (§41(a)(1))** | 20% of qualified research expenses (QREs) exceeding a base amount |
| **Alternative Simplified Credit Rate (§41(c)(5))** | 14% of QREs exceeding 50% of the average QREs for the prior 3 tax years |
| **Base Amount Calculation** | Fixed-base percentage × average annual gross receipts for 4 preceding years (minimum base amount = 50% of current year QREs) |
| **Qualifying Expenses** | Wages for qualified research, supplies used in research, 65% of contract research expenses, cloud computing costs for qualified research |
| **Four-Part Test** | (1) IRC §174 test — expenditures eligible for §174 treatment; (2) Technological in nature; (3) Elimination of uncertainty; (4) Process of experimentation |
| **Carry-Forward** | 20 years forward, 1 year back (IRC §39) |
| **Cap** | No statutory cap on credit amount |
| **Stacking** | Reduces §174 deduction by amount of credit unless taxpayer elects reduced credit under §280C(c)(3) |

### 2.2 IRC §174 R&E Amortization (Post-2022)

| Parameter | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Legal Basis** | IRC §174 as amended by Tax Cuts and Jobs Act (TCJA), P.L. 115-97, §13206 |
| **Effective Date** | Tax years beginning after December 31, 2021 |
| **Domestic Research** | Mandatory capitalization and amortization over 5 years (60-month period), mid-year convention |
| **Foreign Research** | Mandatory capitalization and amortization over 15 years (180-month period), mid-year convention |
| **Prior Law** | Full expensing of R&E expenditures was allowed (immediate deduction) |
| **Impact** | Significant cash flow impact; taxpayers can no longer immediately expense R&D costs |
| **Pending Legislation** | Multiple bipartisan proposals to restore immediate expensing (e.g., American Innovation and Jobs Act) |

### 2.3 Orphan Drug Credit (IRC §45C)

| Parameter | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Legal Basis** | IRC §45C; Orphan Drug Act, 21 U.S.C. §360bb |
| **Credit Rate** | 25% of qualified clinical testing expenses (reduced from 50% by TCJA, effective for tax years after 2017) |
| **Qualifying Expenses** | Clinical testing expenses for drugs designated as orphan drugs under §526 of the Federal Food, Drug, and Cosmetic Act |
| **Conditions** | Drug must be designated as an orphan drug before clinical testing begins; testing must be conducted for rare diseases (fewer than 200,000 affected individuals in the U.S.) |
| **Cap** | No statutory cap |

### 2.4 Payroll Tax Offset for Qualified Small Businesses (IRC §41(h))

| Parameter | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Legal Basis** | IRC §41(h); added by PATH Act of 2015, expanded by Inflation Reduction Act of 2022 |
| **Maximum Offset** | $500,000 per year against employer FICA payroll tax liability (expanded from $250,000 by IRA 2022) |
| **Eligibility** | Gross receipts < $5 million for the current tax year AND no gross receipts for any tax year preceding the 5-tax-year period ending with the current year |
| **Effective Date** | Available for tax years beginning after December 31, 2015; $500,000 limit for tax years beginning after December 31, 2022 |
| **Claim Method** | Elected on Form 6765, applied on Form 941 (quarterly payroll tax return) |

### 2.5 CHIPS Act Investment Tax Credit (IRC §48D)

| Parameter | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Legal Basis** | IRC §48D; CHIPS and Science Act of 2022 (P.L. 117-167) |
| **Credit Rate** | 25% of qualified investment in semiconductor manufacturing property |
| **Qualifying Property** | Tangible property integral to manufacturing of semiconductors or semiconductor manufacturing equipment |
| **Conditions** | Facility must be located in the U.S.; subject to applicable wage and apprenticeship requirements; clawback provisions apply |
| **Cap** | No per-taxpayer cap; subject to guardrails on foreign entity of concern restrictions |
| **Sunset** | Property placed in service before January 1, 2027 |

### 2.6 Bonus Depreciation Phasedown (IRC §168(k))

| Parameter | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Legal Basis** | IRC §168(k) as amended by TCJA |
| **2023** | 80% first-year bonus depreciation |
| **2024** | 60% first-year bonus depreciation |
| **2025** | 40% first-year bonus depreciation |
| **2026** | 20% first-year bonus depreciation |
| **2027+** | 0% (fully phased out) |
| **Qualifying Property** | R&D equipment and other qualifying depreciable assets with recovery period of 20 years or less |

## 3. Patent Box / IP Regime

**Not applicable.** The United States does not have a federal patent box or IP box regime. The Foreign-Derived Intangible Income (FDII) deduction under IRC §250 provides a reduced effective tax rate of 13.125% (rising to 16.406% after 2025) on income derived from serving foreign markets, which functions as a partial IP incentive for exports but is not a traditional patent box.

| Parameter | Detail |
|---|---|
| **FDII Provision** | IRC §250(a)(1)(A) |
| **Deduction Rate** | 37.5% of FDII (21% × 62.5% = 13.125% effective rate) |
| **Post-2025 Rate** | 21.875% deduction (21% × 78.125% = 16.406% effective rate) |
| **Nexus Requirement** | None (not OECD-compliant modified nexus approach) |

## 4. Effective Benefit Analysis

**Assumptions:** $1,000,000 USD R&D spend, 21% federal CIT rate, profitable C-corporation, using ASC method.

| Component | Calculation | Tax Benefit |
|---|---|---|
| **ASC R&D Credit (§41)** | $1,000,000 × 14% (simplified; assumes 50% base) = $70,000 credit | $70,000 |
| **Reduced Credit Election (§280C(c)(3))** | $70,000 × (1 - 21%) = $55,300 net credit | $55,300 |
| **§174 Amortization (Year 1, domestic)** | $1,000,000 ÷ 5 years × 50% (mid-year) = $100,000 deduction × 21% | $21,000 |
| **Year 1 Combined Benefit (with reduced credit)** | $55,300 + $21,000 | **$76,300** |
| **Year 1 Combined Benefit (full credit, reduced deduction)** | $70,000 + ($1,000,000 - $70,000) ÷ 5 × 50% × 21% | **$89,530** |
| **Effective Benefit Rate (Year 1)** | | **7.6% – 9.0%** |
| **Lifetime Aggregate Benefit** | Credit + full amortization deduction | **~$265,300 – $280,000** |

> **Note:** State R&D credits can add an additional 1.5% to 24% depending on jurisdiction. California (24%), Massachusetts (10%), and New York (up to 8%) offer some of the most generous state credits.

## 5. Claim Process & Documentation

### Filing Process

| Step | Detail |
|---|---|
| **1. Identify QREs** | Catalog all wages, supplies, and contract research expenses meeting the four-part test |
| **2. Contemporaneous Documentation** | Maintain project records, timesheets, technical narratives, financial records (Treas. Reg. §1.41-4) |
| **3. Complete Form 6765** | "Credit for Increasing Research Activities" — select Regular or ASC method |
| **4. Attach to Tax Return** | File Form 6765 with Form 1120 (corporate) or relevant return |
| **5. Payroll Tax Offset** | If applicable, file election on Form 6765, Part III; apply on Form 941 |
| **6. §174 Capitalization** | Report amortization on Form 4562 or applicable schedule |

### Key Deadlines

| Item | Deadline |
|---|---|
| **Tax Return Filing** | 15th day of 4th month after year-end (April 15 for calendar year); extensions available |
| **Amended Claims** | Within statute of limitations (generally 3 years from filing or 2 years from payment) |
| **Payroll Tax Offset** | First quarter after income tax return is filed |
| **Pre-Approval** | Not required (self-assessment system) |

### Documentation Requirements

- Technical project descriptions and uncertainty narratives
- Employee time allocation records (contemporaneous preferred; estimates accepted per *United States v. McFerrin*, 570 F.3d 672 (5th Cir. 2009))
- Financial ledgers linking expenses to qualified activities
- Contract research agreements identifying scope of qualified work
- Supply expense documentation

## 6. Recent Changes & Sunset Provisions

| Change | Effective Date | Detail |
|---|---|---|
| **§174 Mandatory Amortization** | Tax years after 12/31/2021 | TCJA §13206; R&E expenses must be capitalized and amortized (5-year domestic, 15-year foreign) |
| **Bonus Depreciation Phasedown** | 2023–2027 | IRC §168(k); declining from 100% to 0% |
| **Payroll Tax Offset Increase** | Tax years after 12/31/2022 | IRA 2022; increased from $250,000 to $500,000 |
| **CHIPS Act §48D Credit** | 2022–2026 | 25% ITC for semiconductor manufacturing; sunsets for property placed in service after 12/31/2026 |
| **Orphan Drug Credit Reduction** | Tax years after 12/31/2017 | TCJA reduced from 50% to 25% |
| **R&D Credit Made Permanent** | 12/18/2015 | PATH Act (P.L. 114-113) eliminated sunset provisions for §41 |
| **FDII Rate Change** | Tax years after 12/31/2025 | Deduction decreases from 37.5% to 21.875%, increasing effective rate |
