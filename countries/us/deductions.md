> **Jurisdiction**: Federal (IRS)
> **Governing Law**: Internal Revenue Code (IRC)
> **Tax Year**: 2024/2025

## 1. Depreciation & Capital Allowances

### Section 179 Immediate Expensing (IRC §179)

| Parameter | 2024 Value | 2025 Value (OBBBA) |
|---|---|---|
| Maximum deduction | **$1,220,000** | **$2,500,000** |
| Phase-out threshold (begins) | $3,050,000 | $4,000,000 |
| Phase-out (complete) | $4,270,000 | $6,500,000 |
| SUV limitation | $28,900 (for vehicles >6,000 lbs GVW) | Indexed for inflation |
| Eligible property | Tangible personal property, off-the-shelf software, qualified improvement property | Same |
| NOT eligible | Real property (buildings), land, inventory | Same |

> The One Big Beautiful Bill Act (OBBBA, P.L. 119-21, signed July 4, 2025) raised the Section 179 limit to $2.5M and the phase-out threshold to $4M for tax years beginning after December 31, 2024, with both amounts indexed for inflation going forward.

### Bonus Depreciation (IRC §168(k))

| Tax Year | Bonus Depreciation Rate |
|---|---|
| 2017–2022 | **100%** |
| 2023 | **80%** |
| 2024 | **60%** |
| 2025 (property acquired before Jan 20, 2025) | **40%** |
| 2025+ (property acquired after Jan 19, 2025) | **100%** (permanent, per OBBBA) |

**Eligible property**: New and used property with a recovery period of 20 years or less, computer software, water utility property, qualified film/TV/live theatrical productions.

> OBBBA permanently reinstated 100% bonus depreciation for qualified property acquired and placed in service after January 19, 2025. Property acquired under a binding written contract before this date remains subject to the TCJA phase-down schedule.

### Qualified Improvement Property (QIP)

| Parameter | Value |
|---|---|
| Recovery period | **15 years** |
| Method | 150% Declining Balance |
| Bonus depreciation eligible | Yes (at current year rate) |
| Definition | Interior improvements to nonresidential real property (excludes enlargement, elevators, escalators, internal structural framework) |

### Qualified Production Property — IRC §168(n) (NEW — OBBBA)

| Parameter | Value |
|---|---|
| Provision | 100% expensing for certain nonresidential real property used in qualified production activities |
| Eligible property | Manufacturing facilities and other qualified production property placed in service after OBBBA enactment |
| Method | Full expensing in year placed in service |
| Legal basis | IRC §168(n), added by OBBBA (P.L. 119-21) |

> OBBBA introduced new IRC §168(n), which allows 100% expensing for certain nonresidential real property used in qualified production activities (e.g., manufacturing facilities), subject to specific requirements and timeframes. This represents a significant expansion beyond existing bonus depreciation rules, which generally exclude real property.

## 2. Business Operating Expenses

### General Deductibility (IRC §162)

All ordinary and necessary business expenses are deductible under IRC §162(a), including salaries, rent, supplies, utilities, and professional services.

### Employee Compensation Deductions

| Parameter | Value |
|---|---|
| General rule | Deductible if "reasonable" (IRC §162(a)(1)) |
| IRC §162(m) limitation | **$1,000,000** per "covered employee" for publicly traded companies |
| Covered employees | CEO, CFO, 3 other highest-paid officers, plus anyone previously covered |
| Performance-based exception | **Eliminated** by TCJA (no longer exempt from $1M cap) |

### Employee Benefits

| Benefit | Deductibility |
|---|---|
| Health insurance premiums | **100%** deductible |
| Retirement plan contributions | Deductible up to plan limits |
| 401(k) employer match | Deductible; total contributions ≤25% of eligible compensation |
| Group term life insurance | Deductible up to $50,000 coverage per employee |
| Educational assistance | Deductible up to $5,250 per employee per year |
| Dependent care assistance | Deductible up to $5,000 per employee |
| HSA contributions | Deductible: $4,150 (self) / $8,300 (family) for 2024 |

### Meals & Entertainment (IRC §274)

| Expense Type | Deductibility |
|---|---|
| Business meals (with business discussion) | **50%** deductible (IRC §274(k), §274(n)(1)) |
| Entertainment expenses | **0%** deductible (eliminated by TCJA) (IRC §274(a)(1)) |
| Employer-provided meals (on premises, convenience of employer) | **50%** deductible |
| Holiday/annual parties for employees | **100%** deductible |
| De minimis meals (snacks, coffee) | **50%** deductible |
| Transportation fringe benefits | **0%** deductible (qualified parking, transit) |

### Charitable Contributions (IRC §170)

| Type | Limit |
|---|---|
| General cash contributions | **10%** of taxable income (computed before charitable deduction) (IRC §170(b)(2)(A)) |
| Food inventory donations | **15%** of taxable income (IRC §170(e)(3)(C)) |
| Conservation easements | **Not subject** to percentage limitation for qualified farmers/ranchers |
| Carry-forward | Excess contributions carry forward **5 years** |

### Bad Debt Deduction (IRC §166)

| Method | Details |
|---|---|
| Specific charge-off method | Required for all businesses (direct write-off when debt becomes worthless) |
| Reserve method | **Not allowed** for tax purposes (except certain financial institutions) |
| Partially worthless debts | Can deduct amount charged off, with IRS consent |
| Business vs. non-business | Business bad debts = ordinary deduction; non-business = short-term capital loss |
| Documentation required | Evidence debt is bona fide, efforts to collect, determination of worthlessness |

### Insurance Premium Deductions

| Type | Deductibility |
|---|---|
| Business property & casualty insurance | **100%** deductible |
| Professional liability / E&O | **100%** deductible |
| Workers' compensation | **100%** deductible |
| Key-person life insurance | **Not deductible** if business is beneficiary |
| Self-employed health insurance | **100%** deductible (above-the-line for individuals) |

### Start-Up & Organizational Costs (IRC §195, §248)

| Parameter | Start-Up Costs (IRC §195) | Organizational Costs (IRC §248) |
|---|---|---|
| Immediate deduction | Up to **$5,000** | Up to **$5,000** |
| Phase-out | Reduced dollar-for-dollar when total costs exceed **$50,000** | Reduced dollar-for-dollar when total costs exceed **$50,000** |
| Remaining costs | Amortized over **180 months** (15 years) | Amortized over **180 months** |

### Vehicle & Travel Deductions

**Standard Mileage Rate (2024):**

| Purpose | Rate per Mile |
|---|---|
| Business | **$0.67** |
| Medical/moving (military only) | $0.21 |
| Charitable | $0.14 |

**Per Diem Rates (2024):**

| Component | Rate |
|---|---|
| Lodging | Varies by locality ($98–$394/night) |
| M&IE (Meals & Incidental Expenses) | $59–$79/day |
| High-low method - high cost | $319/day ($79 M&IE) |

### Home Office Deduction (IRC §280A)

| Method | Calculation |
|---|---|
| Regular method | Actual expenses x (office sq ft / total home sq ft) |
| Simplified method | **$5.00 per sq ft**, max 300 sq ft = **$1,500 maximum** |
| Requirements | Regular & exclusive use, principal place of business |
| Limitation | Cannot exceed gross income from business |

## 3. Interest & Financing Costs

### Business Interest Limitation — IRC §163(j)

| Parameter | 2022–2024 Value | 2025+ Value (OBBBA) |
|---|---|---|
| Deduction limit | **30%** of Adjusted Taxable Income (ATI) (IRC §163(j)(1)) | **30%** of ATI |
| ATI definition | Roughly equivalent to EBIT (no D&A add-back) (IRC §163(j)(8)) | EBITDA-based (D&A add-back restored by OBBBA) |
| Carry-forward | Disallowed interest carries forward indefinitely (IRC §163(j)(2)) | Same |
| Exemption - small business | Avg. annual gross receipts ≤ **$29M** (2024, indexed) for 3 prior years (IRC §163(j)(3)) | ≤ **$31M** (2025, per OBBBA) |
| Exempt industries | Electing real property trades, electing farming businesses, regulated utilities (IRC §163(j)(7)) | Same |

> For tax years beginning after December 31, 2024, OBBBA restored the EBITDA-based method of calculating ATI by allowing businesses to add back depreciation, amortization, and depletion. This significantly increases the 30% limitation threshold.

### Qualified Business Income Deduction — IRC §199A

*Applies to pass-through entities (S-corps, partnerships, sole proprietors)*

| Parameter | Value |
|---|---|
| Deduction rate | **20%** of Qualified Business Income (QBI) (IRC §199A(a)) |
| Taxable income threshold (Single) | $191,950 (2024) |
| Taxable income threshold (MFJ) | $383,900 (2024) |
| Phase-out range | $50,000 (Single) / $100,000 (MFJ) |
| Specified Service Trade/Business (SSTB) | Fully phased out above threshold + phase-out (IRC §199A(d)) |
| W-2 wage / UBIA limitation | Greater of: 50% of W-2 wages OR 25% of W-2 wages + 2.5% of UBIA of qualified property (IRC §199A(b)(2)) |
| Sunset | Scheduled to expire after **December 31, 2025** |

## 4. Research & Development

The United States provides an R&D tax credit under IRC §41 (regular credit rate of 20% on qualified research expenses exceeding a base amount, or an Alternative Simplified Credit of 14%). Since tax years beginning after December 31, 2021, IRC §174 requires domestic R&D expenditures to be amortized over 5 years and foreign R&D over 15 years — immediate expensing is no longer permitted.

See [R&D Incentives](rnd_incentives.md) for full program detail.

## 5. Loss Carry-Forward & Carry-Back

### Net Operating Loss (NOL) — IRC §172

| Parameter | Value |
|---|---|
| Deduction allowance | IRC §172(a) allows a deduction for the aggregate NOLs carried to that year |
| Carry-forward | **Indefinite** (IRC §172(b)(1)(A)) |
| Carry-back | **Not allowed** (for losses arising in tax years after 2020) (IRC §172(b)(1)(A)) |
| Limitation | **80%** of taxable income (for losses arising after Dec 31, 2017) (IRC §172(a)(2)) |
| Pre-TCJA losses | 100% offset allowed, 2-year carry-back, 20-year carry-forward |
| Farming losses | 2-year carry-back allowed (IRC §172(b)(1)(B)) |

## 6. Provisions & Reserves

### Accrual Methods (IRC §461)

| Item | Treatment |
|---|---|
| General rule | Deductible when all events have occurred establishing the liability and the amount can be determined with reasonable accuracy — IRC §461(h) (economic performance test) |
| Warranty reserves | **Not deductible** until actual payment (no general provision allowed) |
| Contingency reserves | **Not deductible** until liability is fixed and determinable |
| Vacation accruals | Deductible if paid within 2.5 months of year-end (IRC §461) |
| Bad debt reserves | **Not allowed** for tax purposes (except financial institutions) — IRC §166 |
| Insurance reserves | Regulated insurance companies may deduct reserves per IRC §807, §832 |

## 8. Treaties & Transfer Pricing

### Transfer Pricing (IRC §482)

| Parameter | Value |
|---|---|
| Standard | Arm's length standard — IRC §482 |
| Methods | CUP, Resale Price, Cost Plus, CPM, Profit Split — Treas. Reg. §1.482-1 through §1.482-9 |
| Documentation | Contemporaneous documentation required — IRC §6662(e) |
| Penalties | 20% accuracy-related penalty; 40% for gross valuation misstatement — IRC §6662(e)–(h) |
| Country-by-Country reporting | Required for US MNEs with consolidated revenue ≥ $850M — IRC §6038(a) |
| APA program | IRS Revenue Procedure 2015-41 (advance pricing agreements) |

### Treaty Network

The US has over 60 bilateral tax treaties. Treaty benefits may reduce or eliminate withholding tax on cross-border payments and may provide for mutual agreement procedures and exchange of information.
