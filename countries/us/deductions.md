# United States - Taxable Income Deductions

> **Jurisdiction**: Federal (IRS)
> **Governing Law**: Internal Revenue Code (IRC)
> **Tax Year**: 2024/2025
> **Currency**: USD ($)

## 1. Depreciation & Capital Expensing

### Section 179 Immediate Expensing (IRC Section 179)

| Parameter | 2024 Value |
|---|---|
| Maximum deduction | **$1,220,000** |
| Phase-out threshold (begins) | $3,050,000 |
| Phase-out (complete) | $4,270,000 |
| SUV limitation | $28,900 (for vehicles >6,000 lbs GVW) |
| Eligible property | Tangible personal property, off-the-shelf software, qualified improvement property |
| NOT eligible | Real property (buildings), land, inventory |

### Bonus Depreciation (IRC Section 168(k))

| Tax Year | Bonus Depreciation Rate |
|---|---|
| 2017-2022 | **100%** |
| 2023 | **80%** |
| 2024 | **60%** |
| 2025 | **40%** |
| 2026 | **20%** |
| 2027+ | **0%** |

**Eligible property**: New and used property with a recovery period of 20 years or less, computer software, water utility property, qualified film/TV/live theatrical productions.

### Qualified Improvement Property (QIP)

| Parameter | Value |
|---|---|
| Recovery period | **15 years** |
| Method | 150% Declining Balance |

| Definition | Interior improvements to nonresidential real property (excludes enlargement, elevators, escalators, internal structural framework) |

## 2. Net Operating Loss (NOL) - IRC §172

| Parameter | Value |
|---|---|
| Deduction allowance | IRC §172(a) allows a deduction for the aggregate NOLs carried to that year |
| Carry-forward | **Indefinite** (IRC §172(b)(1)(A)) |
| Carry-back | **Not allowed** (for losses arising in tax years after 2020) (IRC §172(b)(1)(A)) |
| Limitation | **80%** of taxable income (for losses arising after Dec 31, 2017) (IRC §172(a)(2)) |
| Pre-TCJA losses | 100% offset allowed, 2-year carry-back, 20-year carry-forward |
| Farming losses | 2-year carry-back allowed (IRC §172(b)(1)(B)) |

## 3. Business Interest Limitation - IRC §163(j)

| Parameter | Value |
|---|---|
| Deduction limit | **30%** of Adjusted Taxable Income (ATI) (IRC §163(j)(1)) |
| ATI definition (2022+) | Roughly equivalent to EBIT (no longer adds back D&A) (IRC §163(j)(8)) |
| Carry-forward | Disallowed interest carries forward indefinitely (IRC §163(j)(2)) |
| Exemption - small business | Businesses with avg. annual gross receipts ≤ **$29 million** (2024, indexed) for 3 prior years (IRC §163(j)(3)) |
| Exempt industries | Electing real property trades, electing farming businesses, regulated utilities (IRC §163(j)(7)) |

## 4. Research & Development

### R&D Tax Credit (IRC Section 41)

| Parameter | Value |
|---|---|
| Regular credit rate | **20%** of qualified research expenses (QREs) exceeding base amount |
| Alternative Simplified Credit (ASC) | **14%** of QREs exceeding 50% of avg. QREs for prior 3 years |
| Base amount floor | Cannot be less than 50% of current year QREs |
| Payroll tax offset (small business) | Up to **$500,000** per year against payroll taxes (gross receipts <$5M, ≤5 years) |

### R&D Amortization Requirement (IRC §174, post-2022)

| Parameter | Value |
|---|---|
| Domestic R&D | Must be amortized over **5 years** (mid-year convention) |
| Foreign R&D | Must be amortized over **15 years** |
| Effective date | Tax years beginning after December 31, 2021 |
| **Impact** | R&D expenses can NO longer be fully deducted in the year incurred |

## 5. Qualified Business Income Deduction - IRC §199A

*Applies to pass-through entities (S-corps, partnerships, sole proprietors)*

| Parameter | Value |
|---|---|
| Deduction rate | **20%** of Qualified Business Income (QBI) (IRC §199A(a)) |
| Taxable income threshold (Single) | $191,950 (2024) |
| Taxable income threshold (MFJ) | $383,900 (2024) |
| Phase-out range | $50,000 (Single) / $100,000 (MFJ) |
| Specified Service Trade/Business (SSTB) | Fully phased out above threshold + phase-out (IRC §199A(d)) |
| W-2 wage / UBIA limitation | Greater of: 50% of W-2 wages OR 25% of W-2 wages + 2.5% of UBIA of qualified property (IRC §199A(b)(2)) |
| Phase-in rules | W-2/UBIA limitation phases in between threshold and threshold + phase-out range (IRC §199A(b)(3)) |
| Sunset | Scheduled to expire after **December 31, 2025** |

## 6. Charitable Contributions (IRC §170)

### Corporate Limitations

| Type | Limit |
|---|---|
| General cash contributions | **10%** of taxable income (computed before charitable deduction) (IRC §170(b)(2)(A)) |
| Food inventory donations | **15%** of taxable income (IRC §170(e)(3)(C)) |
| Conservation easements | **Not subject** to percentage limitation for qualified farmers/ranchers |
| Carry-forward | Excess contributions carry forward **5 years** |

## 7. Employee Compensation Deductions

### Salary & Wages

| Parameter | Value |
|---|---|
| General rule | Deductible if "reasonable" (IRC Section 162(a)(1)) |
| IRC Section 162(m) limitation | **$1,000,000** per "covered employee" for publicly traded companies |
| Covered employees | CEO, CFO, 3 other highest-paid officers, plus anyone previously covered |
| Performance-based exception | **Eliminated** by TCJA (no longer exempt from $1M cap) |

### Employee Benefits

| Benefit | Deductibility |
|---|---|
| Health insurance premiums | **100%** deductible |
| Retirement plan contributions | Deductible up to plan limits |
| 401(k) employer match | Deductible; total contributions ≤25% of eligible compensation |
| Group term life insurance | Deductible up to $50,000 coverage per employee |
| Educational assistance | Deductible up to $5,250 per employee per year |
| Dependent care assistance | Deductible up to $5,000 per employee |
| HSA contributions | Deductible: $4,150 (self) / $8,300 (family) for 2024 |

## 8. Meals & Entertainment (IRC §274)

| Expense Type | Deductibility |
|---|---|
| Business meals (with business discussion) | **50%** deductible (IRC §274(k), §274(n)(1)) |
| Entertainment expenses | **0%** deductible (eliminated by TCJA) (IRC §274(a)(1)) |
| Employer-provided meals (on premises, convenience of employer) | **50%** deductible |
| Holiday/annual parties for employees | **100%** deductible |
| De minimis meals (snacks, coffee) | **50%** deductible |
| Transportation fringe benefits | **0%** deductible (qualified parking, transit) |

## 9. Bad Debt Deduction (IRC Section 166)

| Method | Details |
|---|---|
| Specific charge-off method | Required for all businesses (direct write-off when debt becomes worthless) |
| Reserve method | **Not allowed** for tax purposes (except certain financial institutions) |
| Partially worthless debts | Can deduct amount charged off, with IRS consent |
| Business vs. non-business | Business bad debts = ordinary deduction; non-business = short-term capital loss |
| Documentation required | Evidence debt is bona fide, efforts to collect, determination of worthlessness |

## 10. Start-Up & Organizational Costs

### Start-Up Costs (IRC Section 195)

| Parameter | Value |
|---|---|
| Immediate deduction | Up to **$5,000** |
| Phase-out | Reduced dollar-for-dollar when total start-up costs exceed **$50,000** |
| Remaining costs | Amortized over **180 months** (15 years) beginning with month business starts |

### Organizational Costs (IRC Section 248)

| Parameter | Value |
|---|---|
| Immediate deduction | Up to **$5,000** |
| Phase-out | Reduced dollar-for-dollar when total costs exceed **$50,000** |
| Remaining costs | Amortized over **180 months** |

## 13. Home Office Deduction (IRC Section 280A)

| Method | Calculation |
|---|---|
| Regular method | Actual expenses × (office sq ft / total home sq ft) |
| Simplified method | **$5.00 per sq ft**, max 300 sq ft = **$1,500 maximum** |
| Requirements | Regular & exclusive use, principal place of business |
| Limitation | Cannot exceed gross income from business |

| Parameter | Value |
