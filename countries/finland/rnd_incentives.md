# Finland - R&D Tax Incentives

## 1. Overview

| Feature | Details |
|---|---|
| **Primary Incentive** | R&D Additional Tax Deduction |
| **Type** | Enhanced (super) deduction — not refundable |
| **Standard Rate** | 50% additional deduction (150% total deduction) |
| **Pilot Project Rate** | 100% additional deduction (200% total deduction) |
| **Maximum Additional Deduction** | €500,000 per tax year |
| **Legal Basis** | Laki tutkimus- ja kehittämistoiminnan lisävähennyksestä (Law 1286/2022) |
| **Effective From** | January 1, 2023 |
| **Duration** | 2023–2027 (initial period) |
| **Refundability** | Not refundable — requires taxable income to benefit |
| **Patent Box** | None — Finland does not operate a patent box regime |

## 2. Detailed Programs

### 2.1 R&D Additional Tax Deduction (General)

**Legal Basis:** Laki tutkimus- ja kehittämistoiminnan lisävähennyksestä verotuksessa (1286/2022) — Law on Additional Deduction for R&D Activities in Taxation

Finland introduced its first broad-based R&D tax incentive effective from the 2023 tax year. The scheme provides an enhanced deduction on qualifying R&D expenditure.

#### Deduction Rates & Limits

| Parameter | Amount |
|---|---|
| Standard additional deduction | 50% of qualifying costs |
| Total deduction (including base) | 150% of qualifying costs |
| Maximum additional deduction | €500,000 per tax year |
| Minimum threshold | €5,000 in qualifying R&D costs |
| Qualifying cost base for max benefit | €1,000,000 |

**Effective Benefit:** At Finland's 20% CIT rate, the 50% additional deduction yields an effective benefit of 10% (€500,000 × 20% = €100,000 maximum tax saving).

#### Qualifying R&D Activities

The law follows OECD Frascati Manual definitions:

- Basic research
- Applied research
- Experimental development
- Must be systematic, creative, and aimed at increasing knowledge or developing new applications
- Must involve novelty and resolution of scientific or technological uncertainty

#### Qualifying Expenditure

| Cost Category | Eligible |
|---|---|
| Wages and salaries of R&D personnel | Yes |
| Employer social contributions for R&D staff | Yes |
| Purchased R&D services (subcontracting) | Yes — from qualified entities |
| Materials consumed in R&D | No (not included under the law) |
| Capital expenditure / equipment | No |
| Overhead / indirect costs | No |
| Patent costs | No |

**Important:** The qualifying cost base is deliberately narrow, focusing on personnel costs and subcontracted R&D services.

### 2.2 Pilot Project Enhanced Deduction (200% Total)

**Legal Basis:** Same law (1286/2022), specific provisions for qualifying pilot/research collaboration projects

| Parameter | Amount |
|---|---|
| Additional deduction for pilot projects | 100% (200% total deduction) |
| Maximum additional deduction (combined) | €500,000 per tax year |
| Qualifying projects | Research collaboration with universities or research institutes |

#### Requirements for Enhanced Rate

- The R&D must be conducted in collaboration with a Finnish or EEA-based university or research institution
- The project must qualify as a defined "research collaboration" under the law
- Separate documentation requirements apply
- The enhanced and standard additional deductions share the same €500,000 annual cap

### 2.3 Subcontracting Rules

| Subcontractor Type | Eligible | Rate |
|---|---|---|
| Finnish universities and research institutes | Yes | 100% additional (pilot) or 50% (standard) |
| EEA-based universities and research institutes | Yes | 100% additional (pilot) or 50% (standard) |
| Private sector subcontractors (Finnish) | Yes | 50% additional only |
| Non-EEA subcontractors | No | Not eligible |

### 2.4 Interaction with Other Incentives

- The additional deduction is available **alongside** direct R&D grants (e.g., Business Finland funding)
- However, the portion of R&D costs covered by **tax-exempt grants** must be excluded from the qualifying cost base
- Companies receiving full grant funding for a project cannot claim the additional deduction on the same costs
- The deduction applies at the corporate income tax level and does not affect VAT treatment

## 3. Patent Box / IP Regime

Finland **does not** currently operate a patent box or preferential IP taxation regime.

| Feature | Details |
|---|---|
| Patent box regime | None |
| Standard CIT rate on IP income | 20% |
| Royalty WHT (domestic law) | 20% (reduced under tax treaties) |
| IP sale gains | Taxed at standard 20% CIT rate |
| Participation exemption for IP holding | Limited — applies to qualifying shareholdings, not IP directly |

### Policy Context

- Finland has historically focused on direct R&D support through Business Finland grants and loans
- The 2023 R&D tax deduction was the first broad-based fiscal incentive for R&D
- No patent box proposals are currently under active legislative consideration
- Finland's approach emphasizes input-based incentives over output-based IP regimes

## 4. Effective Benefit Analysis (per $1M USD R&D Spend)

*Assumptions: Exchange rate ~€0.92/USD; CIT rate 20%; company has sufficient taxable income; costs are qualifying wages/subcontracting*

| Benefit Component | Calculation | Value (USD) |
|---|---|---|
| Base R&D expenditure | — | $1,000,000 |
| Standard CIT deduction (20%) | $1,000,000 × 20% | $200,000 |
| Additional deduction (50% × 20% CIT) | $1,000,000 × 50% × 20% | $100,000 |
| **Total combined benefit** | Deduction + additional benefit | **$300,000** |
| **Effective rate of support** | Total / spend | **30.0%** |

*Note: Subject to €500,000 cap on additional deduction (~$543,000 USD equivalent). For spend above ~$1.09M, the additional benefit is capped.*

### Scenario Comparison

| Scenario | CIT Deduction | Additional Benefit | Total Benefit | Effective Rate |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Standard R&D ($1M) | $200,000 | $100,000 | $300,000 | 30.0% |
| Pilot project with university ($1M) | $200,000 | $200,000 | $400,000 | 40.0% |
| Loss-making company ($1M) | $0 (loss carried forward) | $0 (not refundable) | $0 immediate | 0.0% immediate |
| Mixed: $500K qualifying + $500K non-qualifying | $200,000 | $50,000 | $250,000 | 25.0% |

**Key Limitation:** The non-refundable nature means startups and loss-making companies receive no immediate cash benefit. Losses can be carried forward for 10 years.

## 5. Claim Process & Documentation

### Step-by-Step Claim Process

1. **Identify Qualifying R&D** — Assess activities against Frascati Manual definitions
2. **Track Qualifying Costs** — Segregate eligible wages and subcontracting costs
3. **Document R&D Projects** — Maintain contemporaneous project documentation
4. **Calculate Additional Deduction** — Apply 50% (or 100% for pilot projects) to qualifying costs, subject to €500,000 cap
5. **Corporate Tax Return** — Claim the additional deduction in the annual corporate tax return (Form 6, supplementary R&D form)
6. **Finnish Tax Administration Review** — Vero (Finnish Tax Administration) may review and audit claims
7. **No Pre-Approval Required** — The deduction is claimed self-assessed; no advance ruling needed (though available)

### Documentation Requirements

| Requirement | Details |
|---|---|
| R&D project descriptions | Written descriptions of each qualifying project |
| Personnel time records | Allocation of employee time to R&D activities |
| Payroll records | Documentation of qualifying wage costs |
| Subcontract agreements | Contracts with R&D service providers |
| Grant funding records | Documentation of any public funding received for same costs |
| Frascati Manual alignment | Demonstration that activities meet R&D definitions |
| Pilot project agreements | Collaboration agreements with research institutions (for enhanced rate) |

### Advance Ruling Option

- Companies can request a **binding advance ruling** (ennakkoratkaisu) from the Finnish Tax Administration
- Useful for confirming eligibility of specific activities or cost categories
- Fee applies; processing time typically 4-8 weeks
- Ruling is binding on the tax authority for the tax years specified

## 6. Recent Changes & Sunset Provisions

| Year | Change | Impact |
|---|---|---|
| 2023 | Law 1286/2022 enacted — first R&D tax deduction | Landmark introduction of fiscal R&D incentive |
| 2023 | Standard 50% additional deduction effective | Applies to tax years beginning on or after Jan 1, 2023 |
| 2023 | Pilot project 100% additional deduction effective | Enhanced rate for research collaboration projects |
| 2024 | First full year of claims processed | Initial compliance and interpretation guidance issued |
| 2024 | Vero publishes detailed guidance on qualifying costs | Clarification on subcontracting and wage cost eligibility |
| 2025 | Potential evaluation of scheme effectiveness | Government review expected |

### Sunset Provisions

- The law (1286/2022) initially applies to **tax years 2023–2027**
- The government has indicated intent to make the incentive **permanent** if evaluation is positive
- Parliament would need to enact extending legislation before 2028
- Parameters (rates, caps, qualifying costs) may be adjusted in extension legislation
- The Finnish Government Programme (2023) includes commitment to strengthen R&D tax incentives

### Future Outlook

- Political consensus supports maintaining and potentially expanding the incentive
- Discussions on broadening eligible cost categories (e.g., materials, equipment)
- Potential increase of the €500,000 cap
- Consideration of introducing refundability for startups (under discussion)
- No patent box regime currently under consideration
