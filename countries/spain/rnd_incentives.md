# Spain - R&D Tax Incentives

## 1. Overview

| Feature | Details |
|---|---|
| **Primary Incentive** | R&D Tax Credit (Deducción por I+D) |
| **Secondary Incentive** | Technological Innovation Tax Credit (Deducción por IT) |
| **R&D Credit Rate** | 25% base; 42% on expenditure exceeding 2-year average |
| **Researcher Bonus** | Additional 17% credit on qualifying researcher personnel costs |
| **Innovation Credit Rate** | 12% on qualifying technological innovation expenditure |
| **Patent Box** | 60% exemption on qualifying IP income (LIS Art. 23) |
| **Legal Basis** | Ley del Impuesto sobre Sociedades (LIS) Art. 35 & Art. 23 |
| **Monetizability** | Credits can be monetized at 80% face value |
| **Carry-Forward** | 18 years |
| **Refundability** | Not directly refundable, but monetizable (see below) |

## 2. Detailed Programs

### 2.1 R&D Tax Credit (Deducción por Investigación y Desarrollo)

**Legal Basis:** Ley 27/2014, del Impuesto sobre Sociedades (LIS), Artículo 35.1

Spain offers one of the most generous R&D tax credit regimes in Europe, with a dual-rate structure and additional bonuses.

#### Credit Rates

| Component | Rate | Basis |
|---|---|---|
| Base R&D credit | 25% | On all qualifying R&D expenditure |
| Incremental R&D credit | 42% | On expenditure exceeding the average of the prior 2 years |
| Researcher personnel bonus | 17% | Additional credit on costs of qualified R&D researchers |
| Investments in R&D assets | 8% | On qualifying R&D fixed assets (excluding buildings and land) |

#### Qualifying R&D Activities (I+D)

Under Spanish law, R&D (Investigación y Desarrollo) is defined more narrowly than innovation:

- Original and planned investigation aimed at discovering new knowledge
- Application of research results for the creation of new materials, products, devices, processes, systems, or services
- Substantial technological improvements to existing items
- Creation of prototypes and pilot projects (where not commercially exploitable)
- Design and creation of software that constitutes scientific or technological advancement
- Advanced technology projects commissioned by public administrations or EU entities

#### Qualifying Expenditure

| Cost Category | Eligible |
|---|---|
| Personnel costs (R&D staff) | Yes — also qualifies for 17% bonus if exclusively dedicated |
| Materials consumed in R&D | Yes |
| Subcontracted R&D services | Yes — limited to certain conditions |
| Equipment amortization (R&D use) | Yes — 8% additional credit available |
| External patent costs for R&D | Yes |
| Overhead allocation | Yes — reasonable allocation |

### 2.2 Technological Innovation Tax Credit (Deducción por Innovación Tecnológica)

**Legal Basis:** LIS Artículo 35.2

| Parameter | Details |
|---|---|
| Credit rate | 12% |
| Definition | Activities resulting in technological advances in products or processes |
| Scope | Broader than R&D — includes adaptation and improvement activities |
| Qualifying costs | Diagnostic studies, industrial design, process engineering, acquisition of advanced technology, obtaining quality certifications |

#### Key Differences: R&D vs. Innovation

| Feature | R&D (I+D) — Art. 35.1 | Innovation (IT) — Art. 35.2 |
|---|---|---|
| Credit rate | 25% / 42% | 12% |
| Personnel bonus | 17% additional | Not available |
| Definition | Scientific/technological novelty | Technological advance in products/processes |
| Asset investment credit | 8% additional | Not available |
| Monetizable | Yes | Yes |

### 2.3 Monetization Mechanism (Cash-Back Option)

**Legal Basis:** LIS Artículo 39.2

Spain offers a unique monetization mechanism allowing companies to convert unused credits to cash:

| Parameter | Details |
|---|---|
| Monetization rate | 80% of face value of the credit |
| Requirements | Binding report (informe motivado) from the Ministry of Science |
| Waiting period | Minimum 1 year from generation of the credit |
| Limitation | Cannot exceed combined annual limit |
| Annual limit | €1 million (for credits generated from €1M spend) + additional amount for excess |
| Staffing requirement | Must maintain or increase average R&D headcount |
| Discount | 20% discount from face value (receive 80%) |

**Example:** A company generating €250,000 in R&D credits but having no tax liability can apply for cash payment of €200,000 (80% × €250,000) after obtaining the binding report.

### 2.4 Application Limits and Carry-Forward

| Rule | Details |
|---|---|
| Annual application limit | Generally 25% of gross tax liability |
| Increased limit | 50% if R&D+IT credits exceed 10% of gross tax liability |
| Carry-forward period | 18 years |
| Interaction with other credits | R&D credits applied after other deductions |

### 2.5 Binding Report (Informe Motivado)

The **informe motivado** is a binding technical assessment issued by the Ministry of Science, Innovation and Universities:

- Certifies the nature of activities as qualifying R&D or innovation
- Required for monetization; highly recommended for all claims
- Provides legal certainty — binding on the tax authority
- Applied for through the CDTI (Centro para el Desarrollo Tecnológico e Industrial)
- Processing time: 3-6 months

## 3. Patent Box / IP Regime

### Spanish Patent Box (Reducción de Rentas por Cesión de Activos Intangibles)

**Legal Basis:** LIS Artículo 23

| Feature | Details |
|---|---|
| Exemption rate | 60% of qualifying IP income |
| Effective tax rate | 10% (60% exemption on 25% CIT rate) |
| Qualifying IP | Patents, utility models, legally protected designs, trade secrets, software copyright |
| Qualifying income | Licensing income, embedded royalties, IP sale proceeds |
| Nexus approach | Modified nexus approach (OECD BEPS compliant) |
| Self-developed IP requirement | IP must be at least partially created by the taxpayer |

#### Nexus Fraction Calculation

The 60% exemption applies to "qualifying income," calculated using the OECD nexus approach:

```
Qualifying Income = Total IP Income × (Qualifying R&D Expenditure × 1.3) / Total R&D Expenditure
```

| Numerator (Qualifying Expenditure) | Denominator (Total Expenditure) |
|---|---|
| In-house R&D costs | All R&D costs for the IP |
| Unrelated-party outsourcing costs | Including related-party outsourcing |
| 30% uplift (capped at total denominator) | Including acquisition costs |

#### Excluded IP

- Trademarks and brand names
- Literary and artistic works (non-software)
- Marketing intangibles
- IP acquired from related parties (unless further developed)

## 5. Claim Process & Documentation

### Step-by-Step Claim Process

1. **Identify and Classify Activities** — Distinguish R&D (Art. 35.1) from Innovation (Art. 35.2)
2. **Track Qualifying Expenditure** — Segregate costs by category (personnel, materials, subcontracting)
3. **Optional: Obtain Informe Motivado** — Apply to CDTI for binding technical report (strongly recommended)
4. **Calculate Credits** — Apply base/incremental rates, researcher bonus, and asset investment credit
5. **Corporate Tax Return (Modelo 200)** — Report credits in the annual corporate income tax return
6. **Apply Credits Against Tax Liability** — Subject to 25%/50% annual cap
7. **Carry Forward Unused Credits** — Up to 18 years
8. **Optional: Apply for Monetization** — If unable to use credits within 1+ year, apply for 80% cash-back

### Documentation Requirements

| Requirement | Details |
|---|---|
| Technical project reports | Detailed descriptions of R&D/innovation activities |
| Personnel time records | Allocation of staff time to qualifying projects |
| Cost accounting records | Segregation of qualifying vs. non-qualifying expenditure |
| Subcontracting agreements | Terms, scope, and IP ownership documentation |
| Informe motivado (recommended) | CDTI binding report certifying R&D/IT classification |
| Two-year expenditure history | For calculating incremental rate threshold |
| Researcher qualifications | Educational credentials for 17% bonus eligibility |
| R&D asset register | For 8% investment credit claims |

### Informe Motivado Application Process

1. Submit application to CDTI (online portal)
2. Provide technical and financial project documentation
3. CDTI evaluates and may request additional information
4. Binding report issued (typically 3-6 months)
5. Report is binding on the Agencia Tributaria (AEAT) for the specified tax years

## 6. Recent Changes & Sunset Provisions

| Year | Change | Impact |
|---|---|---|
| 2015 | Major reform under LIS 27/2014 | Restructured R&D/IT credits, introduced modified nexus patent box |
| 2016 | Patent box reformed to OECD nexus approach | Grandfathering of pre-2016 arrangements until 2021 |
| 2018 | Tightened informe motivado requirements | Greater scrutiny on qualifying activities |
| 2021 | Full transition to nexus patent box | No more grandfathering of old arrangements |
| 2023 | Minimum 15% effective tax rate rules (Pillar Two) | Impact on interaction between credits and minimum tax |
| 2024 | Enhanced guidance on software R&D classification | Clarification of qualifying software development |
| 2025 | Continued emphasis on binding reports for certainty | CDTI processing improvements |

### Sunset Provisions

- The R&D and Innovation tax credits under LIS Art. 35 are **permanent provisions** with no sunset date
- The patent box under LIS Art. 23 is also permanent
- The monetization mechanism under Art. 39.2 is permanent
- Rates and parameters are subject to legislative amendment
- Spain has maintained these incentives through multiple government changes

### Pillar Two Considerations

- Spain's implementation of the EU Minimum Tax Directive (Pillar Two) may affect the value of R&D credits
- Companies subject to the 15% global minimum effective tax rate should model the interaction
- Qualified refundable tax credits receive more favorable treatment under GloBE rules
- Spain's monetization mechanism may help qualify credits as "refundable" for Pillar Two purposes
