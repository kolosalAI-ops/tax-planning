# Turkey - R&D Tax Incentives

## 1. Overview

| Feature | Details |
|---|---|
| **Primary Incentive** | R&D and Design Activity Support (Law 5746) |
| **R&D Deduction** | 200% super-deduction on qualifying R&D expenditure |
| **Researcher Income Tax** | 95% income tax exemption for R&D/design personnel |
| **Social Security** | 50% employer social security exemption for R&D staff |
| **Technopark Benefits** | Technology Development Zone (TDZ) incentives under Law 4691/6550 |
| **R&D Centre Threshold** | Minimum 15 FTE R&D staff |
| **Legal Basis** | Law 5746 (R&D); Law 4691 as amended by Law 6550 (Technoparks) |
| **CIT Rate** | 25% (increased from 20% in stages) |
| **Carry-Forward** | Unlimited (for R&D deduction) |

## 2. Detailed Programs

### 2.1 R&D and Design Activity Support (Law 5746)

**Legal Basis:** Law No. 5746 on Supporting Research, Development and Design Activities (Araştırma, Geliştirme ve Tasarım Faaliyetlerinin Desteklenmesine İlişkin Kanun)

#### 2.1.1 200% R&D Super-Deduction

| Parameter | Details |
|---|---|
| Deduction rate | 200% of qualifying R&D/design expenditure |
| Additional deduction | 100% over and above the normal business deduction |
| Eligible entities | R&D centres, design centres, technopark tenants, and SMEs |
| R&D centre threshold | Minimum 15 FTE dedicated R&D personnel |
| Design centre threshold | Minimum 10 FTE dedicated design personnel |
| Carry-forward | Unlimited (no time limit on unused deductions) |
| Applicability | Corporate and individual income tax |

#### Effective Tax Benefit

| CIT Rate | Deduction | Tax Benefit per TRY 1 of R&D |
|---|---|---|
| 25% | 200% | TRY 0.50 (25% × 200%) |
| Effective support rate | | **50%** |

#### Qualifying R&D Activities

- Systematic research aimed at increasing the stock of knowledge
- Development of new products, processes, software, or services
- Technological improvement of existing products/processes
- Design activities resulting in new or improved aesthetic/functional features
- Must be conducted in certified R&D centres, design centres, or technoparks

#### Qualifying Expenditure

| Cost Category | Eligible | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Personnel costs (R&D/design staff) | Yes | Wages, salaries, bonuses, social contributions |
| Materials and supplies | Yes | Directly consumed in R&D/design |
| General operating expenses | Yes | Rent, utilities, maintenance of R&D centre |
| Depreciation of R&D equipment | Yes | Assets used in R&D activities |
| External R&D services | Yes | Contract R&D from qualified providers |
| Rights and licenses for R&D | Yes | IP acquired for R&D use |
| Consultancy and advisory | Yes | Expert services for R&D projects |

#### R&D Centre Certification

| Requirement | Details |
|---|---|
| Minimum FTE | 15 full-time equivalent R&D personnel |
| Application | Filed with the Ministry of Industry and Technology |
| Assessment | Evaluation of R&D capacity, facilities, and projects |
| Certification validity | Subject to periodic review and inspection |
| Ongoing obligation | Must maintain minimum 15 FTE at all times |
| Penalty for non-compliance | Loss of R&D centre status and clawback of benefits |

#### Design Centre Certification

| Requirement | Details |
|---|---|
| Minimum FTE | 10 full-time equivalent design personnel |
| Qualifying activities | Industrial design, product design, fashion design |
| Certification | Similar process to R&D centres |

### 2.1.2 Income Tax Exemption for R&D Personnel (95%)

**Legal Basis:** Law 5746, Article 3(2)

| Parameter | Details |
|---|---|
| Exemption rate | 95% of income tax on R&D/design personnel wages |
| Effective income tax | 5% of the normal rate |
| Eligible staff | R&D personnel working in certified R&D/design centres |
| Mechanism | Employer withholds reduced income tax; difference treated as subsidy |
| Duration | For the period of R&D centre certification |
| PhD holders | Additional benefits may apply |

#### Income Tax Exemption Example

| Component | Standard Employee | R&D Centre Employee |
|---|---|---|
| Gross salary | TRY 100,000 | TRY 100,000 |
| Income tax (approximate) | TRY 27,000 | TRY 1,350 (95% exempt) |
| Employee net benefit | — | TRY 25,650 higher take-home or employer cost saving |

**Note:** The exemption effectively functions as a wage subsidy — the employer pays less income tax on behalf of R&D employees, reducing the cost of R&D personnel.

### 2.1.3 Social Security Contribution Exemption (50%)

**Legal Basis:** Law 5746, Article 3(3)

| Parameter | Details |
|---|---|
| Exemption rate | 50% of employer's share of social security premiums |
| Standard employer SS rate | ~20.5% (employer portion) |
| Reduced rate for R&D staff | ~10.25% |
| Eligible staff | R&D/design personnel in certified centres |
| Funding | Government reimburses 50% through Social Security Institution (SGK) |
| Duration | For the period of R&D centre certification |

### 2.1.4 Stamp Duty Exemption

| Parameter | Details |
|---|---|
| Exemption | Documents related to R&D/design activities are exempt from stamp duty |
| Stamp duty rate (normal) | 0.948% of document value |
| Applicable documents | Employment contracts, procurement contracts for R&D |

### 2.2 Technology Development Zone (Technopark) Incentives — Law 4691/6550

**Legal Basis:** Law 4691 (Technology Development Zones) as amended by Law 6550

Technoparks (Teknoloji Geliştirme Bölgeleri / TGB) provide additional incentives beyond Law 5746:

#### Technopark Tax Benefits

| Benefit | Details | Period |
|---|---|---|
| Corporate income tax exemption | Profits from R&D/design activities in TDZ exempt from CIT | Extended to December 31, 2028 |
| Income tax exemption for personnel | 95% exemption (same as Law 5746) | Extended to December 31, 2028 |
| VAT exemption | Software deliveries and R&D services from TDZ are VAT-exempt | Extended to December 31, 2028 |
| Social security exemption | 50% exemption (same as Law 5746) | Extended to December 31, 2028 |
| Customs duty exemption | Imports of R&D materials and equipment | Subject to approval |

#### Key Difference: Technopark vs. R&D Centre

| Feature | R&D Centre (Law 5746) | Technopark (Law 4691/6550) |
|---|---|---|
| CIT on R&D profits | R&D costs deducted at 200% (reduces taxable income) | R&D profits fully exempt from CIT |
| Location requirement | Company's own premises | Must be physically located in a certified technopark |
| Minimum FTE | 15 R&D staff | Varies by technopark |
| VAT on software | Standard VAT applies | VAT exempt |
| Application | Ministry of Industry and Technology | Technopark management + Ministry approval |

### 2.3 SME Provisions

Companies that do not meet the 15 FTE threshold for R&D centre certification can still benefit:

| Provision | Details |
|---|---|
| SME R&D deduction | 200% deduction available for R&D projects supported by TÜBİTAK |
| TÜBİTAK project support | R&D project approved by TÜBİTAK qualifies for benefits |
| Technopark SMEs | SMEs located in technoparks benefit from TDZ incentives regardless of headcount |
| KOSGEB support | Additional SME support programs for R&D/innovation |

## 3. Patent Box / IP Regime

Turkey does not have a formal patent box regime but provides IP-related benefits through other mechanisms:

| Feature | Details |
|---|---|
| Formal patent box | None |
| Technopark IP income | Exempt from CIT if earned within TDZ (through 2028) |
| Patent sale exemption | 50% CIT exemption on gains from sale of qualifying IP (held 2+ years) under CIT Act Art. 5/1(e) |
| Royalty WHT | 20% (reduced under tax treaties) |
| Software in technoparks | VAT-exempt deliveries |

### IP Sale Exemption

**Legal Basis:** Corporate Tax Act, Article 5/1(e)

| Parameter | Details |
|---|---|
| Exemption rate | 50% of gain from sale of qualifying IP |
| Holding period | IP must be held for at least 2 years |
| Qualifying IP | Patents, utility models, and other registered IP |
| Effective CIT rate | 12.5% (50% exemption on 25% CIT) |

## 4. Effective Benefit Analysis (per $1M USD R&D Spend)

*Assumptions: Exchange rate ~TRY 32/USD; CIT rate 25%; R&D centre with 15+ FTE; 65% of costs are personnel; company is profitable*

### Scenario A: R&D Centre (Law 5746)

| Benefit Component | Calculation | Value (USD) |
|---|---|---|
| Base R&D expenditure | — | $1,000,000 |
| Standard CIT deduction (25%) | $1,000,000 × 25% | $250,000 |
| Additional R&D deduction (100% × 25%) | $1,000,000 × 100% × 25% | $250,000 |
| Income tax exemption (95% on personnel) | $650,000 × ~27% × 95% | $166,725 |
| Social security exemption (50% on personnel) | $650,000 × ~10.25% | $66,625 |
| **Total benefit** | | **$733,350** |
| **Effective rate** | | **73.3%** |

### Scenario B: Technopark (Law 4691/6550)

| Benefit Component | Calculation | Value (USD) |
|---|---|---|
| CIT exemption on R&D profits | Full exemption (variable by profit margin) | Varies |
| Income tax exemption (95% on personnel) | $650,000 × ~27% × 95% | $166,725 |
| Social security exemption (50%) | $650,000 × ~10.25% | $66,625 |
| VAT exemption on software | Depends on software deliveries | Varies |
| **Minimum guaranteed benefit (personnel only)** | | **$233,350** |

### Scenario Comparison

| Scenario | CIT Benefit | IT Exemption | SS Exemption | Total | Effective Rate |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| R&D Centre (Law 5746) | $500,000 | $166,725 | $66,625 | $733,350 | 73.3% |
| Technopark (full CIT exempt) | Full exempt | $166,725 | $66,625 | $233,350+ | 23.3%+ |
| SME with TÜBİTAK project | $500,000 | — | — | $500,000 | 50.0% |
| Loss-making R&D centre | $0 (carry fwd) | $166,725 | $66,625 | $233,350 | 23.3% |

**Key Advantage:** Turkey's combined benefits (CIT deduction + income tax + social security) make it one of the most generous R&D incentive regimes globally. The personnel-related exemptions provide immediate cash flow benefits regardless of profitability.

## 5. Claim Process & Documentation

### Step-by-Step Claim Process

#### For R&D Centre Certification

1. **Assess Eligibility** — Confirm minimum 15 FTE R&D staff
2. **Apply to Ministry** — Submit application to Ministry of Industry and Technology
3. **Ministry Evaluation** — On-site inspection and assessment of R&D capacity
4. **Certification Issued** — R&D centre status granted
5. **Ongoing Compliance** — Maintain FTE minimum; periodic inspections

#### For Claiming Benefits

1. **Track R&D Expenditure** — Detailed cost records per project
2. **Personnel Records** — R&D staff identification, time allocation, qualifications
3. **CIT Return** — Claim 200% deduction in annual corporate tax return
4. **Income Tax Exemption** — Applied monthly through payroll withholding
5. **Social Security Exemption** — Applied monthly through SGK reporting
6. **TÜBİTAK Reporting** — If project-based, submit progress reports to TÜBİTAK

### Documentation Requirements

| Requirement | Details |
|---|---|
| R&D centre certificate | Ministry of Industry and Technology certification |
| Project documentation | Detailed R&D project descriptions, objectives, methodology |
| Personnel records | FTE count, qualifications, time allocation per project |
| Cost accounting | Expenditure segregation by project and cost category |
| Monthly payroll records | For income tax and social security exemptions |
| TÜBİTAK project reports | Progress reports for approved projects |
| Annual activity report | Submitted to the Ministry |
| Equipment and material records | Documentation of R&D-specific purchases |

### Inspection and Audit

- Ministry of Industry and Technology conducts periodic inspections of R&D centres
- Failure to maintain 15 FTE threshold results in loss of certification
- Tax authorities (Revenue Administration / GİB) audit R&D deduction claims
- TÜBİTAK monitors project-based incentive compliance

## 6. Recent Changes & Sunset Provisions

| Year | Change | Impact |
|---|---|---|
| 2008 | Law 5746 enacted | Established comprehensive R&D incentive framework |
| 2016 | Design centres added to Law 5746 | Extended benefits to design activities |
| 2021 | CIT rate increased from 20% to 25% | Higher absolute benefit from 200% deduction |
| 2022 | Technopark benefits extended to December 31, 2028 | Continued CIT exemption for TDZ activities |
| 2023 | FTE threshold maintained at 15 (previously reduced temporarily) | Stabilization of R&D centre requirements |
| 2024 | Enhanced monitoring of R&D centre compliance | Stricter FTE verification procedures |
| 2025 | No significant structural changes | Existing framework continues |

### Sunset Provisions

| Incentive | Sunset Date | Status |
|---|---|---|
| Law 5746 (R&D/design centres) | December 31, 2028 | Extended; further extension expected |
| Law 4691/6550 (Technoparks) | December 31, 2028 | Extended; highly likely to be renewed |
| 200% R&D deduction | Tied to Law 5746 expiry | Expected to continue |
| Income tax exemption (95%) | Tied to Law 5746 expiry | Expected to continue |
| Social security exemption (50%) | Tied to Law 5746 expiry | Expected to continue |
| CIT exemption in technoparks | December 31, 2028 | May be modified post-2028 |

### Future Outlook

- Strong political commitment to maintaining R&D incentives for technology sector development
- Turkey aims to increase R&D spending to 3% of GDP (currently ~1.4%)
- Potential introduction of a formal patent box regime under discussion
- Global minimum tax (Pillar Two) considerations for large MNEs — Turkey monitoring but not yet implementing
- Possible tightening of technopark CIT exemption post-2028 (transition to partial exemption discussed)
