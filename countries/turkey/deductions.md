# Turkey - Taxable Income Deductions

> **Jurisdiction**: National (Revenue Administration — Gelir İdaresi Başkanlığı / GİB)
> **Governing Law**: Kurumlar Vergisi Kanunu No. 5520 (KVK — Corporate Tax Law); Vergi Usul Kanunu No. 213 (VUK — Tax Procedure Law); Gelir Vergisi Kanunu No. 193 (GVK — Income Tax Law)
> **Tax Year**: 2024/2025
> **Currency**: TRY (₺)

---

## 1. Depreciation & Capital Allowances

### General Framework (VUK Art. 313–321)

Depreciation is calculated based on the **useful life** of assets as prescribed in General Communiqué tables issued by the Ministry of Finance. Taxpayers may choose between the straight-line method and the declining-balance method.

| Method | Description | Legal Basis |
|---|---|---|
| **Straight-line (normal amortisman)** | Equal annual depreciation over useful life | VUK Art. 315 |
| **Declining-balance (azalan bakiyeler)** | Depreciation rate = 2× straight-line rate, applied to the net book value; maximum rate: **50%** | VUK Art. 315 |
| **Change of method** | Declining-balance may be switched to straight-line; reverse is not permitted | VUK Art. 320 |

### Depreciation Rates by Asset Category (VUK General Communiqué)

| Asset Category | Useful Life (years) | Straight-Line Rate | Maximum Declining-Balance Rate |
|---|---|---|---|
| Buildings — concrete/steel | **50** | **2%** | **4%** |
| Buildings — other | **20–25** | **4%–5%** | **8%–10%** |
| Machinery and equipment — general | **5–10** | **10%–20%** | **20%–40%** |
| Machinery — heavy industrial | **10–15** | **6.67%–10%** | **13.3%–20%** |
| Motor vehicles | **5** | **20%** | **40%** |
| Computers and IT equipment | **4** | **25%** | **50%** |
| Office furniture | **5–10** | **10%–20%** | **20%–40%** |
| Intangible assets — general | Per useful life | Per useful life | N/A (straight-line only) |
| Goodwill (şerefiye) | **5** | **20%** | **40%** |
| Patents and know-how | **15** | **6.67%** | **13.3%** |

> **Note:** Exact useful lives are specified in the MoF's General Communiqué on Depreciation (Genel Tebliğ). The rates above are representative; confirm the specific useful life for each asset from the communiqué.

### Inflation Adjustment — Enflasyon Düzeltmesi (VUK Mükerrer Art. 298)

| Parameter | Detail |
|---|---|
| Scope | Restatement of balance sheet items (assets and liabilities) to reflect inflation |
| Re-activation | Inflation adjustment was reactivated for balance sheets as of **31 December 2023** onwards (after being suspended since 2004) |
| Impact on depreciation | From **1 January 2024**, depreciation is calculated on the inflation-adjusted (restated) asset values |
| Inflation gain/loss | Net monetary gain or loss from inflation adjustment is included in taxable income |
| Legal basis | VUK Mükerrer Art. 298; Law No. 7440 (reactivation) |

### Low-Value Assets (VUK Art. 313)

| Parameter | Detail |
|---|---|
| Threshold | Assets with an acquisition cost below the annually updated threshold (TRY 6,900 for 2024 [unverified — confirm indexed amount]) may be fully expensed in the year of acquisition |
| Legal basis | VUK Art. 313 |

### Pre-Trading Expenditure

| Parameter | Detail |
|---|---|
| Organisation costs | Capitalised and amortised over **5 years** — VUK Art. 282 |
| Pre-operating revenue expenditure | Deductible in the year incurred if related to income-producing activities |

## 2. Business Operating Expenses

### General Deductibility (KVK Art. 8; GVK Art. 40)

All expenses incurred in connection with the conduct of business, provided they are properly documented, are deductible. The KVK incorporates GVK Art. 40 for the definition of deductible expenses.

| Expense Category | Deductibility | Legal Basis |
|---|---|---|
| Salaries, wages, and bonuses | **100%** deductible | GVK Art. 40(1) |
| Social security contributions (SGK — employer portion) | **100%** deductible | GVK Art. 40(2) |
| Rent (business premises) | **100%** deductible | GVK Art. 40(1) |
| Repairs and maintenance | **100%** deductible | GVK Art. 40(1) |
| Insurance premiums (business) | **100%** deductible | GVK Art. 40(1) |
| Professional fees (legal, accounting) | **100%** deductible | GVK Art. 40(1) |
| Travel and accommodation | **100%** deductible (business purpose, documented) | GVK Art. 40(4) |
| Vehicle expenses (company cars) | Deductible with limitations on luxury vehicles (acquisition cost and lease rental caps apply) | GVK Art. 40(5); KVK Art. 11(1)(g) |
| Advertising and marketing | **100%** deductible | GVK Art. 40(1) |
| Bad debts | Deductible when judicially proven or when debtor's insolvency is established | VUK Art. 322–323 |
| Doubtful receivables | May be provisioned when litigation has commenced or debtor's financial distress is documented | VUK Art. 323 |
| Entertainment and representation | **50%** non-deductible (only **50%** deductible) | KVK Art. 11(1)(f) [unverified — some sources cite different limitations; confirm exact percentage] |
| Charitable donations (approved) | Deductible up to **5%** of taxable income (full deduction for certain public benefit donations) | KVK Art. 10(1)(c)–(e) |

### Vehicle Expense Limitations

| Parameter | Detail |
|---|---|
| Acquisition cost cap | Vehicles with acquisition cost exceeding certain thresholds: depreciation limited to TRY amounts set annually by MoF (e.g., TRY 790,000 for 2024 [unverified — confirm indexed threshold]) |
| Lease rental cap | Monthly lease payments deductible up to TRY 26,000 (2024) [unverified — confirm indexed amount] |
| Fuel and running costs | Deductible for business-use vehicles; personal use portion non-deductible |
| Legal basis | GVK Art. 40(5); KVK Art. 11(1)(g) |

## 3. Interest & Financing Costs

### General Interest Deduction (GVK Art. 40(1); KVK Art. 8)

Interest on loans used for business purposes is generally deductible, subject to the thin capitalisation and excess borrowing restrictions below.

### Thin Capitalisation — KVK Art. 12

| Parameter | Detail |
|---|---|
| Scope | Loans obtained from **shareholders** or persons **related to shareholders** |
| Ratio | Shareholder loans must not exceed **3× shareholders' equity** at any time during the year |
| Consequence | Interest attributable to the excess (thin capital) is **non-deductible** and treated as a deemed **dividend distribution** (subject to 10% withholding tax) |
| Shareholders' equity | Measured at year-end; if equity is negative, all related-party interest is non-deductible |
| Back-to-back loans | Anti-avoidance: loans from third parties guaranteed by related parties may also be caught |
| Legal basis | KVK Art. 12 |

### Excess Borrowing Restriction (GVK Art. 40(1) — as amended from 2021)

| Parameter | Detail |
|---|---|
| Scope | Where total external debt (from all sources, including unrelated third parties) exceeds the taxpayer's **equity** |
| Restriction | **10%** of borrowing costs (interest, commission, FX differences) attributable to the excess borrowing is **non-deductible** |
| Legal basis | GVK Art. 40(1); effective from 1 January 2021 |
| Interaction | This applies in addition to the thin capitalisation rule under KVK Art. 12 |

### Transfer Pricing of Interest

| Parameter | Detail |
|---|---|
| Requirement | Interest on related-party loans must be at arm's-length rates |
| Consequence | Excess interest treated as a disguised profit distribution — KVK Art. 13 |

## 4. Research & Development

Turkey provides significant R&D deductions through Law No. 5746 on Supporting Research, Development and Design Activities, which allows a 100% additional deduction on qualifying R&D expenditure at approved R&D Centres (minimum 15 full-time researchers), and Law No. 4691 on Technology Development Zones, which exempts profits from R&D and software activities in approved Technology Development Zones from CIT (extended to 31 December 2028). From 1 January 2025, a domestic minimum tax of 10% applies on pre-deduction corporate income under Law No. 7524 (KVK Art. 32/C), limiting the effective benefit of R&D deductions and TDZ exemptions in certain cases [unverified — confirm with local counsel].

See [R&D Incentives](rnd_incentives.md) for full programme details, eligibility criteria, and application process.

## 5. Loss Carry-Forward & Carry-Back

### Net Operating Losses — KVK Art. 9

| Parameter | Detail |
|---|---|
| Carry-forward | **5 years** — KVK Art. 9(1)(a) |
| Carry-back | **Not permitted** |
| Utilisation | Losses offset against taxable income; no percentage cap on annual utilisation |
| Losses from foreign branches | May be carried forward for 5 years, but only offset against profits from the same foreign PE |
| Change of ownership | No automatic forfeiture rule, but anti-avoidance provisions may apply |

## 6. Provisions & Reserves

### General Principles (VUK Art. 322–323; KVK Art. 11)

| Item | Treatment |
|---|---|
| Doubtful receivables provision | Deductible when litigation commenced or debtor's financial distress documented — VUK Art. 323 |
| Bad debts (worthless receivables) | Deductible when judicially proven irrecoverable — VUK Art. 322 |
| General provisions | **Not deductible** |
| Contingency reserves | **Not deductible** |
| Warranty provisions | **Not deductible** unless an actual liability has crystallised |
| Employee severance pay (kıdem tazminatı) | Deductible when the obligation becomes due and is paid; accruals for future severance generally not deductible |
| Insurance technical reserves | Deductible for insurance companies per Insurance Regulation and Supervision Agency rules |
| Inventory write-downs | Deductible if documented via official valuation commission (takdir komisyonu) or court-supervised destruction — VUK Art. 267, 278 |

## 7. Industry-Specific Deductions

### Technology Development Zones (TDZ / Technoparks) — Law No. 4691

| Parameter | Detail |
|---|---|
| CIT exemption | Profits from R&D, software, and design activities in TDZs are **exempt** from corporate income tax |
| Duration | Extended to **31 December 2028** |
| Personnel incentive | Income tax withholding exemption for R&D personnel (up to a ceiling based on minimum wage multiples) |
| Social security | Employer's share of social security premiums supported by government |
| Minimum tax (2025+) | TDZ exemption subject to the 10% domestic minimum tax floor — KVK Art. 32/C |

### Free Trade Zones — Free Trade Zones Law No. 3218

| Parameter | Detail |
|---|---|
| CIT exemption | Manufacturing activities in Free Trade Zones: profits exempt from CIT (for licences obtained before 6 February 2004) |
| Current status | Post-2004 licences: only manufacturing activities receive CIT exemption; trading activities are taxable |
| Personnel | Income tax withholding exemption for FTZ employees |

### Tourism

| Parameter | Detail |
|---|---|
| Investment incentives | Tourism enterprises in designated regions may qualify for investment incentive certificates providing VAT exemption, customs duty exemption, and enhanced depreciation |
| Legal basis | Council of Ministers Decision on Investment Incentives (Yatırım Teşvik Kararı) |

### Mining and Energy

| Parameter | Detail |
|---|---|
| Exploration costs | May be capitalised and amortised over the mine life or expensed |
| Investment incentives | Strategic investment incentive scheme: CIT reduction up to 50%, VAT exemption, customs duty exemption |

## 8. Treaties & Transfer Pricing

### Transfer Pricing — KVK Art. 13; Transfer Pricing General Communiqué (Serial No. 1)

| Parameter | Detail |
|---|---|
| Standard | Arm's-length principle — KVK Art. 13 |
| Related parties | Shareholders (≥10% direct or indirect), related companies, related individuals |
| Methods | CUP, resale price, cost plus, TNMM, profit split — aligned with OECD Guidelines |
| Documentation | Annual TP documentation; Master File and Local File for large taxpayers |
| Country-by-Country Reporting | Required for Turkish-parented MNE groups with consolidated revenue ≥ TRY 7B [unverified — confirm current threshold] |
| Disguised profit distribution | Non-arm's-length transactions treated as disguised dividends: 10% withholding tax applies |
| Penalties | Understatement penalties; potential criminal liability for tax evasion |
| APA | Advance Pricing Agreements available (unilateral, bilateral, multilateral) — KVK Art. 13(5) |

### Treaty Network

Turkey has over 90 bilateral double taxation treaties. Standard treaty rates typically reduce dividends to 5%–15%, interest to 5%–10%, and royalties to 5%–10%.

## 9. Non-Deductible Items

The following are **NOT deductible** under Turkish tax law:

| Expense | Legal Basis |
|---|---|
| Corporate income tax itself | KVK Art. 11(1)(a) |
| Fines and tax penalties | KVK Art. 11(1)(d) |
| Bribes and corrupt payments | KVK Art. 11(1)(c) |
| 50% of entertainment and representation expenses | KVK Art. 11(1)(f) [unverified — confirm exact limitation] |
| Interest on thin capital (excess over 3:1) | KVK Art. 12 |
| 10% of borrowing costs where external debt > equity | GVK Art. 40(1) |
| Disguised profit distributions (non-arm's-length related-party payments) | KVK Art. 13 |
| Reserves and provisions (except doubtful receivables per VUK 323) | KVK Art. 11(1)(e) |
| Personal expenses of shareholders | KVK Art. 11(1)(b) |
| Interest on equity (imputed interest on own capital) | KVK Art. 11(1)(a) |
| Dividends and profit distributions | KVK Art. 11 |
| Excessive vehicle costs (above prescribed thresholds) | GVK Art. 40(5) |
| Donations exceeding 5% of taxable income (unless full deduction applies) | KVK Art. 10 |
