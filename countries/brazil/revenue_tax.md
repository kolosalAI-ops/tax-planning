## 1. Corporate Income Tax System Overview

Brazil has **four corporate tax regimes**. Companies must select one per fiscal year (January-December). The selection rules and regime definitions are governed by **Lei No. 9.430/1996** and **Lei No. 9.249/1995**:

| Regime | Applicability | Description |
|---|---|---|
| **Lucro Real** (Actual Profit) | Mandatory for companies with revenue >R$78M | Tax on actual accounting profit with adjustments (**Lei No. 9.430/1996, Artigos 1-8**) |
| **Lucro Presumido** (Presumed Profit) | Revenue ‚Č§R$78M | Tax on deemed profit (percentage of revenue) (**Lei No. 9.430/1996, Artigos 25-27**) |
| **Simples Nacional** | Revenue ‚Č§R$4.8M | Simplified, unified tax regime for micro/small businesses (**LC No. 123/2006, Artigos 12-41**) |
| **Lucro Arbitrado** (Assessed Profit) | When books are inadequate | Tax authorities determine profit basis (**Lei No. 9.430/1996, Artigos 47-51**) |

## 11. Income by Source Type

### 11.1 Service Income (Domestic & Cross-Border)

> **Legal Basis**: LC 116/2003 (ISS); Lei 10.168/2000 (CIDE); Lei 14.596/2023 (transfer pricing); RIR/2018 (Decreto 9.580/2018)

| Parameter | Rate / Treatment | Legal Reference |
|---|---|---|
| Domestic services under Lucro Real | Combined **34%** (IRPJ 25% + CSLL 9%) on taxable profit | **Lei No. 9.249/1995, Art. 3o; Lei No. 7.689/1988, Art. 3o** |
| ISS (municipal service tax) | **2-5%** on service revenue | **LC 116/2003, Artigo 8o** |
| Cross-border services rendered in Brazil | Subject to IRPJ/CSLL + CIDE **10%** on remittances for technical services | **Lei 10.168/2000, Artigo 2o** |
| PIS/COFINS on service revenue (non-cumulative) | **9.25%** (PIS 1.65% + COFINS 7.6%) | **Lei 10.637/2002, Art. 2o; Lei 10.833/2003, Art. 2o** |
| PIS/COFINS on service revenue (cumulative) | **3.65%** (PIS 0.65% + COFINS 3.0%) | **Lei 9.718/1998, Artigo 2o** |
| Lucro Presumido services | 32% presumed margin ‚Üí effective **~11.33%** on revenue | **Lei No. 9.249/1995, Art. 15, SS1o, III** |
| Transfer pricing (intercompany services) | New OECD-aligned arm's length rules effective 2024 | **Lei 14.596/2023** |
| IRRF on service remittances to non-residents | **15%** (general); **25%** to tax havens | **RIR/2018, Artigo 744** |

**Practical Notes:**
- Cross-border technical service remittances incur a triple layer: IRRF (15-25%) + CIDE (10%) + PIS/COFINS-Importa√ß√£o, making effective withholding burden significant.
- Lei 14.596/2023 aligns Brazil with OECD transfer pricing guidelines, replacing the prior fixed-margin system. Mandatory for fiscal years starting 1 January 2024.
- ISS rate is set by each municipality within the 2-5% band; most major cities (S√£o Paulo, Rio de Janeiro) apply the maximum 5%.

### 11.2 Royalty & IP Licensing Income (Received)

> **Legal Basis**: Lei 9.249/1995 Art. 28 (IRRF on royalties); Lei 10.168/2000 (CIDE); Portaria MF 436/1958 (deductibility limits)

| Parameter | Rate / Treatment | Legal Reference |
|---|---|---|
| Domestic royalties received | Included in Lucro Real taxable income at combined **34%** | **RIR/2018; Lei 9.249/1995** |
| IRRF on royalty payments to non-residents | **15%** (general); **25%** to tax havens | **Lei 9.249/1995, Artigo 28** |
| CIDE on royalty remittances | **10%** | **Lei 10.168/2000, Artigo 2o** |

| IP amortization | Straight-line over useful life or contract period | **RIR/2018, Artigos 324-328** |
| Patent box regime | **Not available** in Brazil | N/A |

**Practical Notes:**
- Royalty payments to foreign related parties are subject to both deductibility caps (Portaria MF 436/1958) and transfer pricing scrutiny (Lei 14.596/2023).
- INPI registration is an absolute prerequisite: without it, royalty payments are non-deductible and may be reclassified by Receita Federal.
- The CIDE 10% applies on top of the IRRF, making the total outbound burden on royalties to non-residents at least 25% (15% IRRF + 10% CIDE).

### 11.5 Dividend Income (Received by Company)

> **Legal Basis**: Lei 9.249/1995 Art. 9 (JCP) and Art. 10 (dividend exemption); CFC rules under Lei 12.973/2014

| Parameter | Rate / Treatment | Legal Reference |
|---|---|---|
| Dividends from Brazilian companies | **EXEMPT** from IRPJ and IRRF | **Lei 9.249/1995, Artigo 10** |
| Exemption scope | Applies to both domestic corporate and individual shareholders | **Lei 9.249/1995, Artigo 10, caput** |
| JCP (Interest on Equity / Juros sobre Capital Pr√≥prio) | Deductible for payer; subject to **15% IRRF** for recipient | **Lei 9.249/1995, Artigo 9o** |
| Foreign dividends received by Brazilian company | Included in worldwide income at **34%** (CFC rules apply universally) | **Lei 12.973/2014, Artigos 76-92** |
| Proposed reform (2024 discussions) | Potential reintroduction of dividend taxation at **15-20%** | Not yet enacted; under legislative discussion |

**Practical Notes:**
- Brazil's dividend exemption (since 1996) is one of the most generous globally, but has been subject to persistent reform proposals. Companies should monitor legislative developments.
- JCP is a powerful tax planning tool: the payer deducts JCP at 34%, and the recipient is taxed at only 15% IRRF, yielding a net tax benefit of approximately 19 percentage points.
- CFC rules in Brazil are among the strictest globally: all foreign subsidiary profits are taxed in Brazil regardless of whether they are distributed, with no active income exemption (Lei 12.973/2014, Art. 76-92).

### 12.1 Digital Services Taxation

**Practical Notes:**
- The 2021 STF ruling (ADIs 1.945 and 5.659) settled a long-standing dispute by determining that software transactions, including SaaS and cloud services, are subject to ISS (not ICMS), dramatically reducing the tax burden compared to ICMS rates of 17-20%.
- Non-resident platforms providing digital services to Brazilian consumers face IRRF on payments plus CIDE, though enforcement remains challenging.
- The upcoming CBS/IBS reform will unify the treatment of digital services, eliminating the current ISS vs. ICMS distinction.
