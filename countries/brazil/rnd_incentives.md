# Brazil - R&D Tax Incentives

## 1. Overview

| Parameter | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Headline Incentive** | Lei do Bem (Good Law) — R&D Super-Deduction |
| **Mechanism Type** | Super-deduction (enhanced deduction from taxable income), accelerated depreciation, IPI reduction |
| **Headline Rate** | 160% deduction of R&D expenditures (base 100% + 60% additional); up to 200% with qualifiers |
| **Refundable** | No — requires taxable profit (real profit regime — *lucro real*) |
| **Patent Box** | No formal patent box regime |
| **Legal Basis** | Law 11.196/2005 (Lei do Bem) Articles 17–26; Decree 5.798/2006; Law 8.248/1991 (Informatics Law); Law 13.969/2019 |

## 2. Detailed Programs

### 2.1 Lei do Bem — R&D Super-Deduction (Law 11.196/2005, Art. 17–26)

| Parameter | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Legal Basis** | Law 11.196/2005, Articles 17–26; regulated by Decree 5.798/2006 |
| **Base Deduction** | 160% of R&D expenditures (100% ordinary deduction + 60% additional deduction from IRPJ and CSLL tax base) |
| **Additional 20% — Researcher Hiring** | +20% (total 180%) if the company increases the number of dedicated R&D researchers by up to 5% compared to previous year |
| **Additional 20% — Patent Filing** | +20% (total 200% maximum) if a patent is granted as a result of the R&D activity |
| **Combined Maximum** | 200% deduction of qualifying R&D expenditures |
| **Qualifying Expenses** | Salaries of R&D personnel, materials consumed in R&D, third-party contracted R&D (Brazilian institutions), travel related to R&D, technical training of R&D staff |
| **Eligibility Requirement** | Company must be on the *lucro real* (real profit) tax regime and must have taxable profit in the year of claim |
| **Pre-Approval** | Not required — automatic benefit (self-assessment); annual report to MCTI (Ministry of Science, Technology and Innovation) required by July 31 of the following year |
| **Cap** | No explicit cap on deduction amount |

### 2.2 Accelerated Depreciation on R&D Equipment (Art. 17, §3)

| Parameter | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Legal Basis** | Law 11.196/2005, Art. 17, §3; Decree 5.798/2006 |
| **Benefit** | Full (100%) accelerated depreciation of new machinery, equipment, and instruments used exclusively for R&D in the year of acquisition (for IRPJ purposes) |
| **Accelerated Amortization** | Intangible assets used in R&D may be amortized in the acquisition year |
| **CSLL Treatment** | Accelerated depreciation also applies for CSLL (Social Contribution on Net Profit) purposes |

### 2.3 IPI Reduction (Art. 17, §5)

| Parameter | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Legal Basis** | Law 11.196/2005, Art. 17, §5 |
| **Benefit** | 50% reduction in IPI (*Imposto sobre Produtos Industrializados*) on equipment, machinery, and instruments purchased for R&D purposes |
| **Conditions** | Equipment must be destined exclusively for R&D activities |

### 2.4 Informatics Law (Lei de Informática — Law 8.248/1991, revised by Law 13.969/2019)

| Parameter | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Legal Basis** | Law 8.248/1991 (original); Law 13.969/2019 (reform effective April 2020) |
| **Previous Benefit** | IPI reduction/exemption for qualifying IT/ICT products manufactured in Brazil |
| **Post-2020 Benefit** | Converted to financial credit (tax credit) on R&D investments in ICT |
| **Credit Rate** | Varies by region: up to 13.65% of net revenue on eligible products (reduced rates for North/Northeast regions) |
| **R&D Investment Requirement** | Minimum 4%–5% of net revenue from incentivized products must be invested in R&D activities |
| **Eligible Products** | Hardware, automation equipment, telecommunications products — subject to PPB (*Processo Produtivo Básico*) |
| **Oversight** | MCTI approval required; annual compliance reporting |

### 2.5 PADIS — Semiconductor Support (Law 11.484/2007)

| Parameter | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Legal Basis** | Law 11.484/2007 (PADIS — Support Program for Technological Development of the Semiconductor Industry) |
| **Benefit** | Reduction to zero of PIS/COFINS, IPI, and CIDE on imports and domestic purchases of inputs for semiconductor manufacturing |
| **R&D Requirement** | Minimum 5% of gross revenue must be invested in R&D |
| **Approval** | MCTI project approval required |

## 3. Patent Box / IP Regime

**Not applicable.** Brazil does not have a formal patent box or IP box regime providing reduced tax rates on IP-derived income. However, the Lei do Bem provides an additional 20% super-deduction when a patent is granted (bringing the total to up to 200% deduction), which functions as an indirect incentive for patenting R&D outcomes.

| Parameter | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Status** | No patent box regime |
| **Related Incentive** | Lei do Bem Art. 19, §3 — additional 20% deduction upon patent grant |
| **Transfer Pricing for IP** | OECD transfer pricing rules adopted by Law 14.596/2023 (effective January 2024) |

## 4. Effective Benefit Analysis

**Assumptions:** R$5,000,000 (~US$1,000,000) R&D spend under Lei do Bem, real profit regime, profitable entity, combined IRPJ+CSLL rate of 34%.

| Component | Calculation | Tax Benefit (R$) |
|---|---|---|
| **Base Deduction (100%)** | Included in ordinary business expense — no incremental benefit | R$0 (already deducted) |
| **Additional 60% Super-Deduction** | R$5,000,000 × 60% × 34% (IRPJ 25% + CSLL 9%) | **R$1,020,000** |
| **+20% Researcher Hiring Bonus** | R$5,000,000 × 20% × 34% | **R$340,000** |
| **+20% Patent Grant Bonus** | R$5,000,000 × 20% × 34% | **R$340,000** |
| **Accelerated Depreciation (equipment)** | Timing benefit — present value depends on asset life; assume R$500,000 equipment, 5-year normal life | **~R$136,000** (NPV) |
| **IPI Reduction (50%)** | Assume R$500,000 equipment × 10% IPI rate × 50% | **R$25,000** |
| **Total Maximum Benefit** | | **R$1,861,000** |
| **Effective Benefit Rate (base 160%)** | R$1,020,000 ÷ R$5,000,000 | **20.4%** |
| **Effective Benefit Rate (maximum 200%)** | R$1,700,000 ÷ R$5,000,000 | **34.0%** |

**Per US$1,000,000 R&D Spend:**

| Scenario | Tax Benefit (USD Equivalent) |
|---|---|
| **Base (160% deduction)** | ~US$204,000 |
| **Maximum (200% + accelerated depreciation + IPI)** | ~US$372,000 |

## 5. Claim Process & Documentation

### Filing Process

| Step | Detail |
|---|---|
| **1. Verify Eligibility** | Confirm company is on *lucro real* regime and has taxable profit |
| **2. Identify Qualifying Projects** | Map R&D projects to the definition in Decree 5.798/2006, Art. 2 (basic research, applied research, experimental development) |
| **3. Track Expenditures** | Segregate and document all qualifying R&D expenses throughout the fiscal year |
| **4. Apply Deductions** | Claim deductions on IRPJ/CSLL returns (ECF — *Escrituração Contábil Fiscal*) |
| **5. File FORMP&D** | Submit the annual report (FORMP&D) to MCTI by July 31 of the year following the fiscal year |
| **6. Maintain Documentation** | Retain project documentation, financial records, and employment records for 5 years |

### Key Deadlines

| Item | Deadline |
|---|---|
| **IRPJ/CSLL Return (ECF)** | Last business day of July following the fiscal year |
| **FORMP&D Report to MCTI** | July 31 of the year following the fiscal year |
| **Pre-Approval** | Not required for Lei do Bem (automatic/self-assessment) |
| **Informatics Law Claims** | MCTI project approval required in advance |
| **Record Retention** | 5 years from the date of filing |

### Documentation Requirements

- Detailed project descriptions with scientific/technological objectives
- Evidence of technological uncertainty and innovation
- Personnel records (names, qualifications, time allocation to R&D)
- Financial records for all R&D expenditures
- Equipment acquisition records (for accelerated depreciation and IPI reduction)
- Patent applications and grants (for 20% bonus)
- Annual FORMP&D filing with MCTI

## 6. Recent Changes & Sunset Provisions

| Change | Effective Date | Detail |
|---|---|---|
| **Informatics Law Reform** | April 2020 | Law 13.969/2019 converted IPI reduction to financial credit model |
| **Transfer Pricing Reform** | January 2024 | Law 14.596/2023 adopted OECD transfer pricing framework; impacts IP-related deductions |
| **IRPJ/CSLL Rate Reform Proposals** | Under discussion | Tax reform (PEC 45/2019, converted to EC 132/2023) focuses on indirect taxes; income tax reform pending |
| **Lei do Bem Continuity** | Ongoing | No sunset provision; Lei do Bem is permanent legislation |
| **PADIS Extension** | Extended through 2029 | Semiconductor incentives extended by Law 14.302/2022 |
| **Increased MCTI Oversight** | 2023–ongoing | MCTI increasing scrutiny of FORMP&D filings and qualifying activity definitions |
| **Indirect Tax Reform (IBS/CBS)** | Phased 2026–2033 | Constitutional Amendment 132/2023; IPI being phased out — may impact Lei do Bem IPI reduction benefit |
