# Brazil - International Investors

> **Jurisdiction**: Receita Federal do Brasil (RFB) + Banco Central do Brasil (BCB)
> **Governing Law**: Constituicao Federal de 1988 (CF/1988), Lei 9.249/1995, Lei 10.833/2003, Lei 12.973/2014, Lei 14.286/2021
> **Tax Year**: 2024/2025
> **Currency**: Brazilian Real (BRL / R$)
> **Primary Legal References**: RIR/2018 (Decreto 9.580/2018), BCB Resolution 278/2022 (RDE-IED), BCB Resolution 277/2022 (FX market), Lei 5.709/1971 (rural land), Lei 10.168/2000 (CIDE), Lei 4.131/1962 (foreign capital statute), Resolucao CMN 4.373/2014 (non-resident portfolio investment)

---

## 1. Foreign Ownership Restrictions & Investment Approvals

Brazil is **generally open to foreign direct investment** across most sectors of the economy. However, the Constituicao Federal de 1988 (CF/1988) and specific legislation impose restrictions in several strategic sectors. All foreign direct investment must be registered with the Central Bank of Brazil.

### General Framework

| Parameter | Detail | Legal Reference |
|---|---|---|
| General FDI policy | Open; no prior government approval required for most sectors | **Lei 4.131/1962** (Foreign Capital Statute) |
| Registration requirement | **Mandatory** — all FDI must be registered via RDE-IED | **BCB Resolution 278/2022** |
| Registration body | Banco Central do Brasil (BCB) — Registro Declaratorio Eletronico (RDE-IED) | **BCB Resolution 278/2022, Art. 3o** |
| Registration timing | Within 30 days of the FX transaction (equity investment) | **BCB Resolution 278/2022, Art. 14** |
| Screening body | No formal screening/approval body (unlike CFIUS in the US or FIRB in Australia) | General principle under Lei 4.131/1962 |

### Sector-Specific Restrictions

| Sector | Restriction | Legal Reference |
|---|---|---|
| **Media** (newspapers, magazines, radio, TV) | Maximum **30% foreign ownership** (voting or total capital) | **CF/1988, Artigo 222** |
| **Rural land** | Foreign individuals and companies limited; municipalities capped at **25%** foreign-owned rural land; individual foreigners limited to **15-100 modulos de exploracao indefinida** depending on region | **CF/1988, Artigo 190; Lei 5.709/1971; Decreto 74.965/1974** |
| **Aviation (airlines)** | Previously restricted to 20% foreign ownership; **relaxed by Lei 13.842/2019** — now **100% foreign ownership** permitted for Brazilian airlines | **Lei 7.565/1986 (Codigo Brasileiro de Aeronautica), amended by Lei 13.842/2019** |
| **Healthcare** | Generally restricted for foreign capital; CF/1988 prohibits direct or indirect participation of foreign companies in healthcare | **CF/1988, Artigo 199, SS3o** |
| Healthcare (exceptions) | Lei 13.097/2015 opened exceptions allowing foreign participation in hospitals, clinics, and diagnostic services under certain conditions | **Lei 13.097/2015, Artigos 142-145** |
| **Nuclear energy** | State monopoly — exclusive competence of the Federal Government | **CF/1988, Artigo 21, inciso XXIII** |
| **Border regions** (150km strip) | Restrictions on foreign ownership of land and enterprises within 150km of international borders; requires authorization from the Conselho de Defesa Nacional | **Lei 6.634/1979; CF/1988, Artigo 91, SS1o, III** |
| **Postal services** | Federal Government monopoly | **CF/1988, Artigo 21, inciso X** |
| **Coastal and internal navigation (cabotage)** | Brazilian-flagged vessels; ownership restrictions apply | **Lei 9.432/1997** |

### RDE-IED Registration Process

| Step | Description |
|---|---|
| 1. CPF/CNPJ | Foreign investor must obtain a CPF (individual) or appoint a legal representative with CNPJ |
| 2. Foreign exchange | Investment funds must enter Brazil through authorized FX institutions |
| 3. RDE-IED filing | Electronic registration within 30 days of the FX transaction |
| 4. Updates | Must update RDE-IED for any changes (capital increases, reinvestment of profits, equity transfers) |
| 5. Annual declaration | BACEN Census — mandatory for entities with foreign equity participation >US$100M (quarterly) or >US$1M (annual) |

> **Note**: Failure to register does not invalidate the investment but creates regulatory penalties and prevents lawful repatriation of capital and profits through official FX channels.

## 2. Non-Resident Investor Taxation

> **Legal Basis**: Lei 10.833/2003, Art. 26 (CGT on non-residents); Lei 9.249/1995, Art. 10 (dividend exemption); Lei 9.249/1995, Art. 9o (JCP); Resolucao CMN 4.373/2014 (non-resident portfolio investors)

### Capital Gains Tax (CGT) on Share Dispositions

| Transaction Type | Tax Rate | Legal Reference |
|---|---|---|
| Sale of **non-listed shares** (general non-resident) | **15%** on capital gain | **Lei 10.833/2003, Artigo 26** |
| Sale of non-listed shares — seller in **tax haven** (jurisdicao com tributacao favorecida) | **25%** on capital gain | **Lei 10.833/2003, Artigo 26; IN RFB 1.037/2010 (tax haven list)** |
| Sale of **listed shares** (outside stock exchange, general) | **15%** on capital gain | **Lei 10.833/2003, Artigo 26** |
| Sale of listed shares (on stock exchange — **Resolucao 4.373 investor**) | Subject to **same rules as residents**: 15% on swing trade gains; 20% on day trade gains; exempt for up to R$20K/month in common shares | **Resolucao CMN 4.373/2014; Lei 11.033/2004, Art. 2o** |
| Sale of shares (progressive rates for non-4.373 non-residents) | 15% (gain up to R$5M), 17.5% (R$5-10M), 20% (R$10-30M), 22.5% (>R$30M) | **Lei 13.259/2016, Artigo 1o** |

### Withholding Tax on Income Distributions

| Payment Type | WHT Rate | Legal Reference |
|---|---|---|
| **Dividends** to non-residents | **0%** (exempt) | **Lei 9.249/1995, Artigo 10** |
| **Interest on Equity (JCP)** to non-residents | **15%** | **Lei 9.249/1995, Artigo 9o, SS2o** |
| JCP to non-residents in **tax havens** | **25%** | **Lei 9.249/1995, Artigo 9o; RIR/2018, Artigo 744** |
| Interest on loans to non-residents | **15%** (general) | **RIR/2018, Artigo 744** |
| Interest on loans — **tax haven** recipient | **25%** | **RIR/2018, Artigo 744** |
| Royalties to non-residents | **15%** | **RIR/2018, Artigo 744** |
| Royalties — **tax haven** recipient | **25%** | **RIR/2018, Artigo 744** |
| Technical/management/professional service fees | **15%** (or **25%** to tax havens) | **RIR/2018, Artigo 744** |

> **Note on dividends**: Brazil is unique among most major economies in exempting dividends from withholding tax for both residents and non-residents. This exemption has been in place since 1996 under Lei 9.249/1995, Artigo 10. However, **PL 3394/2024** (income tax reform bill) proposes introducing a **15% withholding tax on dividends**. As of early 2025, this legislation remains under Congressional consideration.

### Branch Profits

| Parameter | Detail | Legal Reference |
|---|---|---|
| Branch profits tax | **No separate branch profits tax** | General principle — no specific branch profits tax legislation |
| Branch CIT rate | **34%** (same as domestic entity: IRPJ 25% + CSLL 9%) | **Lei 9.249/1995, Art. 3o; Lei 7.689/1988, Art. 3o** |
| Remittance of branch profits | **0% WHT** (consistent with dividend exemption principle) | **Lei 9.249/1995, Artigo 10** (applied by analogy) |

### Resolucao 4.373 Investors (Non-Resident Portfolio Investment)

| Parameter | Detail | Legal Reference |
|---|---|---|
| Eligible investors | Non-resident individuals and legal entities investing in Brazilian financial markets | **Resolucao CMN 4.373/2014** |
| Registration | Must register with CVM (Comissao de Valores Mobiliarios) and appoint local representative | **Resolucao CMN 4.373/2014, Art. 3o** |
| Tax treatment | Preferential — generally **same rates as resident investors** on financial market operations | **Lei 11.033/2004; Decreto 6.306/2007** |
| Fixed-income gains | Generally **15%** (may be 0% for government bonds) | **Lei 11.033/2004, Art. 1o; Medida Provisoria 2.159-70/2001** |
| Equity gains (stock exchange) | **Same as residents** (15% swing, 20% day trade) | **Resolucao CMN 4.373/2014** |
| IOF on inflows | **0%** (general, since 2022) | **Decreto 6.306/2007, as amended** |

## 3. Repatriation of Profits

> **Legal Basis**: Lei 14.286/2021 (new FX Law, effective January 2023); BCB Resolution 277/2022 (FX market regulations); Lei 9.249/1995 (dividend/JCP treatment)

### Foreign Exchange Framework

| Parameter | Detail | Legal Reference |
|---|---|---|
| FX law | **Lei 14.286/2021** (new FX framework, effective January 2023) — replaced Lei 4.131/1962 and Decreto-Lei 9.025/1946 for FX purposes | **Lei 14.286/2021** |
| FX regime | **More liberal** than prior regime; simplified procedures for FX transactions | **Lei 14.286/2021, Artigos 5-12** |
| FX operators | Authorized by BCB — banks, exchange brokers, other authorized institutions | **BCB Resolution 277/2022** |
| Free FX market | Single FX market (previously dual market was eliminated in 2005) | **Lei 14.286/2021, Artigo 5o** |
| Reporting | FX transactions above US$50,000 require detailed reporting | **BCB Resolution 277/2022** |

### Repatriation by Payment Type

| Payment Type | WHT on Remittance | Other Taxes/Charges | Central Bank Requirement | Legal Reference |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Dividends** | **0%** | None (no CIDE, no PIS/COFINS on dividend remittance) | RDE-IED must be current | **Lei 9.249/1995, Art. 10** |
| **JCP (Interest on Equity)** | **15%** (25% for tax havens) | None | RDE-IED must be current | **Lei 9.249/1995, Art. 9o, SS2o** |
| **Interest on loans** | **15%** (25% for tax havens) | **IOF-Cambio** may apply (0-6.38% depending on loan term); PIS/COFINS-Importacao on service component | ROF registration required for loan | **RIR/2018, Art. 744; Decreto 6.306/2007** |
| **Royalties** | **15%** (25% for tax havens) | **CIDE 10%** (Contribuicao de Intervencao no Dominio Economico on technology transfers); PIS/COFINS-Importacao (9.25%); ISS (2-5%) | INPI registration required for IP; ROF for financial registration | **RIR/2018, Art. 744; Lei 10.168/2000, Art. 2o** |
| **Management/technical service fees** | **15%** (25% for tax havens) | **PIS/COFINS-Importacao** (9.25% — PIS 1.65% + COFINS 7.6%); ISS (2-5%) | No specific registration beyond FX | **RIR/2018, Art. 744; Lei 10.865/2004** |
| **Capital repatriation** (return of invested capital) | **0%** (to the extent of registered capital) | None | RDE-IED — repatriation limited to registered amount in foreign currency | **Lei 14.286/2021; BCB Resolution 278/2022** |
| **Capital gains on exit** | **15%** (or 25% for tax haven sellers) | None beyond IRRF | Buyer withholds; FX through authorized channel | **Lei 10.833/2003, Art. 26** |

### Central Bank Registration Requirements

| Investment Type | Registration System | Legal Reference |
|---|---|---|
| **Equity investment (FDI)** | **RDE-IED** (Registro Declaratorio Eletronico — Investimento Estrangeiro Direto) | **BCB Resolution 278/2022** |
| **Foreign loans** | **ROF** (Registro de Operacoes Financeiras) | **BCB Resolution 278/2022** |
| **Portfolio investment** | **RDE-ROF/4.373 registration** via CVM-registered custodian | **Resolucao CMN 4.373/2014; BCB Resolution 278/2022** |
| **Technology transfer** | INPI registration (Artigo 211 of Lei 9.279/1996) + ROF for financial flows | **Lei 9.279/1996; BCB Resolution 278/2022** |

> **Critical note**: BCB approval is **not required** for FDI transactions, but **registration is mandatory** for lawful repatriation of capital and profits. Without proper registration, the foreign investor cannot remit funds through the official FX market.

## 4. Branch vs. Subsidiary

> **Legal Basis**: Codigo Civil (Lei 10.406/2002); Lei 6.404/1976 (S.A. law); RIR/2018 (tax treatment)

Foreign companies investing in Brazil typically establish a **Brazilian subsidiary** rather than a branch. While branches are legally permitted, they are uncommon due to regulatory complexity and the absence of liability limitation.

### Entity Type Comparison

| Feature | Branch (Filial) | Subsidiary — Ltda. (Sociedade Limitada) | Subsidiary — S.A. (Sociedade Anonima) |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Legal personality** | Extension of foreign parent; no separate legal personality | Separate legal entity | Separate legal entity |
| **Liability** | Unlimited — parent fully liable | Limited to capital contribution (with exceptions) | Limited to share subscription price |
| **Governing law** | **Codigo Civil, Art. 1.134-1.141** | **Codigo Civil, Art. 1.052-1.087** | **Lei 6.404/1976** |
| **CIT rate** | **34%** (IRPJ 25% + CSLL 9%) | **34%** (IRPJ 25% + CSLL 9%) | **34%** (IRPJ 25% + CSLL 9%) |
| **Dividend distribution WHT** | **0%** (profit remittance to head office) | **0%** | **0%** |
| **Registration** | Junta Comercial (State Board of Trade); requires DNRC authorization | Junta Comercial | Junta Comercial (or CVM for publicly traded) |
| **Governance** | Managed by permanent legal representative in Brazil | Managed by quotaholders (socios) and administrators | Board of Directors (optional for closed S.A.); Board of Executive Officers required |
| **Minimum capital** | No statutory minimum | No statutory minimum | No statutory minimum (but CVM rules for public S.A.) |
| **Minimum partners/shareholders** | N/A (branch of single entity) | Minimum **2 quotaholders** (but single-member Ltda. — EIRELI/SLU — is possible since 2019) | Minimum **2 shareholders** (7 for public S.A.) |
| **Common use** | Rare — typically for specific regulated activities | **Most common** for foreign investors; simpler governance | Required for **public companies** or **capital markets access** |
| **FX registration** | Required (RDE-IED) | Required (RDE-IED) | Required (RDE-IED) |
| **Transfer pricing** | Applies (head office vs. branch transactions) | Applies (parent-subsidiary transactions) | Applies (parent-subsidiary transactions) |
| **Annual filing** | Same corporate tax returns (ECF, ECD, etc.) | Same corporate tax returns (ECF, ECD, etc.) | Same corporate tax returns + CVM filings if public |

### Practical Considerations

| Factor | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Visa/immigration** | Permanent visa (VITEM V) for foreign administrators may require minimum capital (typically R$600K for simplified process, though not a legal minimum for entity formation) |
| **Operational complexity** | Branch requires full authentication/apostille of parent company documents; subsidiary formation is more straightforward |
| **Liability protection** | Subsidiary provides limited liability shield; branch does not |
| **Profit repatriation** | Identical treatment — 0% WHT on dividends/profit remittance regardless of structure |
| **Regulatory perception** | Brazilian authorities and counterparties generally prefer dealing with a locally incorporated entity |
| **JCP benefit** | Available to both branches and subsidiaries (deductible interest on equity reduces effective tax burden) |

### S.A. Sub-Types

| Type | Description | Legal Reference |
|---|---|---|
| **S.A. Aberta (Open)** | Publicly traded; securities listed on B3 stock exchange; CVM registration mandatory | **Lei 6.404/1976, Art. 4o** |
| **S.A. Fechada (Closed)** | Privately held; no public offering of securities; simpler compliance | **Lei 6.404/1976, Art. 4o** |

## 5. Foreign Tax Credit

> **Legal Basis**: RIR/2018, Artigo 298 (foreign tax credit); Lei 9.249/1995, Artigo 26 (reciprocity requirement)

Brazil allows a **credit method** for the avoidance of double taxation. Foreign income taxes paid may be credited against Brazilian IRPJ and CSLL, subject to important limitations.

### Foreign Tax Credit Rules

| Parameter | Detail | Legal Reference |
|---|---|---|
| Method | **Credit** (not exemption) | **RIR/2018, Artigo 298** |
| Eligibility | Income tax (or equivalent) paid to foreign governments on income also taxed in Brazil | **RIR/2018, Artigo 298, caput** |
| **Reciprocity requirement** | Credit only allowed if the foreign country grants **equivalent treatment** to Brazilian residents (reciprocidade) or if there is a tax treaty in force | **Lei 9.249/1995, Artigo 26** |
| Limitation | Credit limited to the **Brazilian tax attributable to the foreign income** (i.e., cannot exceed the Brazilian tax that would have been paid on the same income) | **RIR/2018, Artigo 298, SS1o** |
| Limitation method | **Per-country** (separate calculation for each source country) | **RIR/2018, Artigo 298** |
| Carry-forward of excess credits | **Not allowed** — excess credits in one year cannot be carried forward | **RIR/2018, Artigo 298** (no carry-forward provision) |
| Carry-back of excess credits | **Not allowed** | **RIR/2018, Artigo 298** |
| Underlying tax credit (indirect) | Available for dividends received from subsidiaries where Brazil has a treaty providing for such credit | **Treaty-specific provisions** |
| Documentation | Must provide proof of foreign tax payment (translated and apostilled documentation) | **RIR/2018, Artigo 298; IN RFB 1.520/2014** |

### Reciprocity in Practice

| Scenario | FTC Available? | Explanation |
|---|---|---|
| Country with DTA with Brazil | **Yes** | Treaty provides the legal basis for credit |
| Country without DTA but granting reciprocity | **Yes** | Receita Federal maintains reciprocity determinations |
| Country without DTA and **no reciprocity** | **No** | No credit available — results in full double taxation |
| Tax haven jurisdictions | **Limited** | Tax haven countries rarely qualify for reciprocity; credits generally not available |

### Practical Limitations

| Issue | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Timing** | Foreign tax must have been paid or withheld in the same fiscal year as the Brazilian inclusion |
| **Currency conversion** | Foreign tax converted to BRL at the exchange rate on the date of payment |
| **CFC income** | Foreign tax credit available on CFC income (Lei 12.973/2014, Art. 87), but subject to the per-country limitation |
| **Indirect taxes** | Foreign VAT/GST and similar indirect taxes are **not creditable** |
| **Sub-national taxes** | Foreign state/provincial income taxes may qualify if they are income taxes in nature |

## 6. CFC Rules (Controlled Foreign Company Rules)

> **Legal Basis**: Lei 12.973/2014, Artigos 76-92 (CFC rules — tributacao em bases universais)

Brazil operates one of the **most aggressive CFC regimes globally**. Under the "universal basis" taxation principle (tributacao em bases universais), **all profits** of foreign controlled entities are taxed in Brazil on an accrual basis, regardless of whether those profits are actually distributed.

### Core CFC Principles

| Parameter | Detail | Legal Reference |
|---|---|---|
| Regime name | **Tributacao em Bases Universais** (Universal Basis Taxation) | **Lei 12.973/2014, Artigos 76-92** |
| Scope | **ALL profits** of foreign subsidiaries taxed annually in Brazil | **Lei 12.973/2014, Artigo 77** |
| Active vs. passive income | **No distinction** — both active and passive income included | **Lei 12.973/2014, Artigo 77** (no active income exemption) |
| Substance carve-out | **None** — profits taxed regardless of economic substance in the foreign jurisdiction | **Lei 12.973/2014** (no substance exception) |
| Tax rate on CFC income | **34%** (IRPJ 25% + CSLL 9%) on the Brazilian parent's proportional share of CFC profit | **Lei 12.973/2014, Artigo 77; Lei 9.249/1995, Art. 3o; Lei 7.689/1988, Art. 3o** |
| Timing of inclusion | December 31 of each year (annual accrual) | **Lei 12.973/2014, Artigo 77** |
| Profit determination | Based on the CFC's financial statements, adjusted to Brazilian accounting/tax standards | **Lei 12.973/2014, Artigo 76** |

### CFC Categories and Thresholds

| Category | Ownership Threshold | Treatment | Legal Reference |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Controlada (Controlled entity)** | Direct or indirect control (>50% voting rights or equity, or predominant influence over corporate decisions) | ALL profits taxed annually in Brazil on a proportional basis | **Lei 12.973/2014, Artigo 77** |
| **Coligada (Affiliate/Associated entity)** | Significant influence (generally >20% equity) in the same jurisdiction or connected entities | Profits taxed annually IF: (a) entity is in a tax haven, OR (b) entity is subject to an effective tax rate <20%, OR (c) Brazilian parent has >50% income from the affiliate | **Lei 12.973/2014, Artigos 81-82** |
| **Coligada with active income exception (limited)** | >20% equity in non-tax-haven, non-low-tax jurisdiction | May defer taxation until actual distribution (very limited applicability) | **Lei 12.973/2014, Artigo 81, SS3o-SS5o** |

### Consolidation of CFC Results

| Parameter | Detail | Legal Reference |
|---|---|---|
| Consolidation | Losses of one CFC can **offset profits** of another CFC (consolidacao) | **Lei 12.973/2014, Artigo 78** |
| Conditions for consolidation | (a) CFC not in tax haven; (b) CFC not subject to beneficial regime; (c) CFC provides audited financial statements; (d) Brazilian entity has comprehensive tax exchange agreement with CFC jurisdiction; (e) CFC not subject to effective rate <20% | **Lei 12.973/2014, Artigo 78, incisos I-V** |
| Limitation on consolidation | Net loss offset limited to the **proportional positive results** of other CFCs in the consolidated computation | **Lei 12.973/2014, Artigo 78, SS1o** |
| Loss carryover within consolidation | Unused CFC losses may be carried forward within the consolidation computation (subject to conditions) | **Lei 12.973/2014, Artigo 78, SS2o** |
| Deadline | Consolidation regime available through 2024 (subject to extension by legislation) | **Lei 12.973/2014, Artigo 78** |

### Foreign Tax Credit on CFC Income

| Parameter | Detail | Legal Reference |
|---|---|---|
| Direct credit | Tax paid by the CFC in its jurisdiction creditable against Brazilian IRPJ/CSLL on CFC income | **Lei 12.973/2014, Artigo 87** |
| Indirect credit | Tax paid by lower-tier subsidiaries of the CFC also creditable (underlying tax credit) | **Lei 12.973/2014, Artigo 87, SS1o** |
| Limitation | Credit limited to the Brazilian tax attributable to the CFC income (per-country basis) | **Lei 12.973/2014, Artigo 87, SS3o** |
| Tax haven CFCs | Foreign tax credit available but practically limited due to low foreign tax burden | General application |

> **Strategic implication**: Brazil's CFC regime is significantly broader than most OECD countries. Unlike jurisdictions that exempt active business income earned by CFCs (e.g., the US GILTI high-tax exclusion, or European Directive exemptions), Brazil taxes **all CFC income** without regard to nature or substance. This makes Brazil one of the most challenging jurisdictions for outbound investment structuring.

## 7. Tax Treaty Network

> **Legal Basis**: CF/1988, Artigo 84, VIII (presidential power to enter treaties); CF/1988, Artigo 49, I (Congressional approval); Decreto Legislativo (ratification)

Brazil maintains a **moderately sized double tax agreement (DTA) network** with approximately **35+ comprehensive treaties** in force. This is smaller than the networks of most other G20 countries. Notably, Brazil lacks treaties with several major trading partners.

### Treaty Network Overview

| Parameter | Detail |
|---|---|
| Total comprehensive DTAs in force | **~35** |
| Model used | Generally based on the **UN Model** (developing country preference) rather than OECD Model |
| Credit vs. exemption | Brazil uniformly applies the **credit method** (not exemption) for foreign income |
| **MLI (Multilateral Instrument)** | Brazil has **not signed** the OECD/G20 MLI (BEPS Multilateral Convention) |
| LOB (Limitation on Benefits) | **Not present** in existing treaties |
| PPT (Principal Purpose Test) | **Not present** in current treaties (may appear in future treaties if Brazil engages with MLI) |
| Tax-sparing clauses | Present in several older treaties (e.g., Japan, France, Spain) — allows credit for tax that would have been paid but was reduced by Brazilian incentives |

| **United Kingdom** | DTA **terminated** (denounced by Brazil, effective 2023) | Previous 1967 treaty no longer applicable; UK investors now subject to full domestic WHT rates |
| **Germany** | DTA **terminated** (denounced by Germany, effective 2006) | Significant double taxation risk; German investors face full Brazilian WHT without treaty reduction |
| **Australia** | No DTA | Australian investors subject to domestic rates |

> **Strategic note**: The absence of DTAs with the US, UK, and Germany — three of the largest sources of global FDI — creates significant double taxation costs for investors from these countries. Cross-border structuring through treaty jurisdictions (e.g., Netherlands, Luxembourg, Spain) has historically been used to mitigate this exposure, though anti-avoidance measures are increasingly scrutinized.

## 8. Permanent Establishment (PE) Risk

> **Legal Basis**: RIR/2018, Artigos 161-162 (estabelecimento permanente); Individual treaty PE articles (typically based on Art. 5 of UN/OECD Models)

### Domestic PE Concept

| Parameter | Detail | Legal Reference |
|---|---|---|
| PE definition | **Estabelecimento permanente** — broadly defined as any fixed place of business through which business is wholly or partly carried on | **RIR/2018, Artigos 161-162** |
| Examples of PE | Office, branch, factory, workshop, warehouse, mine, quarry, oil/gas well, any place of extraction of natural resources | **RIR/2018, Artigo 161** |
| Agent PE | A person acting in Brazil with authority to **conclude contracts** on behalf of a non-resident may constitute a PE | **RIR/2018, Artigo 162; Treaty Art. 5(5) equivalents** |
| Subsidiary as PE | A subsidiary is generally **not** treated as a PE of its parent solely by reason of the parent-subsidiary relationship | General principle under treaties |

### Treaty PE Thresholds

| PE Type | Typical Treaty Threshold | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| **Construction PE** | **6-12 months** (varies by treaty) | Most Brazilian treaties use 6 months (UN Model preference); some use 12 months (OECD Model) |
| **Service PE** | **183 days** in any 12-month period (most treaties) | Threshold for furnishing services through employees or personnel |
| **Agent PE (dependent)** | Person with authority to conclude contracts | Present in virtually all Brazilian treaties |
| **Agent PE (independent)** | Independent agent acting in ordinary course of business | Generally excluded from PE definition |

### PE Taxation

| Parameter | Detail | Legal Reference |
|---|---|---|
| CIT on PE | **34%** (IRPJ 25% + CSLL 9%) | Same as domestic entities |
| Profit attribution | Business profits attributable to the PE are taxed in Brazil; arm's length attribution applies | **Treaty Art. 7 equivalents; RIR/2018** |
| Deemed dividend on PE profit remittance | **0%** — no additional tax on remittance of PE profits to head office (consistent with dividend exemption) | **Lei 9.249/1995, Art. 10** (applied by analogy) |
| PIS/COFINS | PE subject to PIS/COFINS on Brazilian revenues | **Lei 10.637/2002; Lei 10.833/2003** |
| Transfer pricing | Transactions between PE and head office subject to TP rules | **Lei 14.596/2023** |

### Digital PE

| Parameter | Detail |
|---|---|
| Digital PE in domestic law | **Not addressed** — Brazilian domestic law does not contain a specific digital PE concept |
| Digital PE in treaties | **Not present** in any current Brazilian DTA |
| Digital services tax | Brazil does not have a separate digital services tax (DST), though the 2024/2025 tax reform may address digital economy taxation |
| Withholding on digital payments | Payments to non-residents for digital services generally subject to **15% WHT** (as technical services) + PIS/COFINS-Importacao |

### Common PE Risk Scenarios in Brazil

| Scenario | PE Risk Level | Analysis |
|---|---|---|
| Foreign company with sales office in Brazil | **High** | Fixed place of business = PE |
| Foreign company sending employees for 3-month project | **Low to Medium** | Below most treaty service PE thresholds (183 days) |
| Foreign company with dependent agent concluding contracts | **High** | Agent PE under both domestic law and treaties |
| Foreign company with warehouse only | **Medium** | Depends on treaty (some exclude preparatory/auxiliary activities) |
| Foreign company providing digital services remotely (no presence) | **Low** | No digital PE concept; but WHT may apply on payments |
| Foreign company with construction project lasting 8 months | **High** (6-month treaty) / **Low** (12-month treaty) | Depends on applicable treaty threshold |
