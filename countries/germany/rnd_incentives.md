# Germany - R&D Tax Incentives

## 1. Overview

| Feature | Details |
|---|---|
| **Headline Incentive** | Forschungszulage (Research Allowance) — 25% tax credit |
| **Type** | Direct tax credit against assessed income tax / corporate tax |
| **Rate** | 25% of eligible R&D personnel and subcontractor costs |
| **Refundable** | Yes — fully refundable if credit exceeds tax liability |
| **Patent Box** | No — Germany does not operate a patent box or IP box regime |
| **Legal Basis** | Forschungszulagengesetz (FZulG) 2019, amended 2020 and 2024; EStG / KStG |

---

## 2. Detailed Programs

### 2.1 Forschungszulage (Research Allowance)

**Legal Basis:** Gesetz zur steuerlichen Förderung von Forschung und Entwicklung (Forschungszulagengesetz — FZulG), enacted 14 December 2019, effective 1 January 2020. Amended by Wachstumschancengesetz 2024.

| Parameter | Value |
|---|---|
| Credit Rate | 25% of qualifying R&D expenditure |
| Maximum Assessment Base (from 2024) | €4,000,000 per fiscal year (up from €2M pre-2024) |
| Maximum Credit Per Year | €1,000,000 (25% × €4M) |
| Affiliated Group Cap | €4M assessment base applies per group of affiliated companies (verbundene Unternehmen) |
| Qualifying Costs — In-house R&D | Wages and salaries of R&D employees (including employer social security contributions, capped at calculation) |
| Qualifying Costs — Contract R&D | 60% of amounts paid to third-party contractors for R&D services |
| Eligible Entities | All corporate income tax (KSt) and income tax (ESt) payers in Germany |
| SME Enhancement (from 2024) | SMEs (per EU definition) receive 35% rate on first €4M (max credit €1.4M) |
| Fiscal Year Application | Applied per assessment period; claimed on income/corporate tax return |

**Eligible R&D Activities (per FZulG §2):**

Activities must fall within one or more of the following Frascati Manual categories:

| Category | Description |
|---|---|
| Basic Research (Grundlagenforschung) | Experimental or theoretical work to acquire new knowledge without direct commercial application |
| Applied Research (Angewandte Forschung) | Original investigation directed toward a specific practical aim |
| Experimental Development (Experimentelle Entwicklung) | Systematic work using existing knowledge to produce new/improved products, processes, or services |

**Exclusions:**
- Market research and market development
- Quality control and routine testing
- Prospecting, exploring, or drilling for minerals/hydrocarbons
- Commercially motivated adaptation of existing products
- Aesthetic design changes
- Routine software development

### 2.2 BSFZ Certification Process

**Legal Basis:** FZulG §6; Forschungszulagen-Bescheinigungsverfahrensverordnung (FZulBV).

The Bescheinigungsstelle Forschungszulage (BSFZ) at the Förderbank is the designated certification body.

| Step | Details |
|---|---|
| 1. Application | Submit electronic application to BSFZ describing R&D projects |
| 2. Project Description | Technical description of each project: objectives, novelty, uncertainties, methods |
| 3. Assessment | BSFZ evaluates whether activities qualify as R&D per Frascati Manual criteria |
| 4. Certificate Issued | Bescheinigung issued confirming qualifying R&D projects and eligible period |
| 5. Tax Authority Claim | Submit certificate with income/corporate tax return; Finanzamt applies credit |

| BSFZ Processing | Details |
|---|---|
| Application Fee | Free of charge |
| Processing Time | Typically 2–4 months |
| Validity | Certificate covers the specified assessment period |
| Appeals | Objection (Einspruch) possible; then Finanzgericht |
| Retroactive Claims | Possible for FY 2020 onwards (statute of limitations applies) |

### 2.3 Contract Research Rules

| Parameter | Value |
|---|---|
| Qualifying Base | 60% of contract payments to R&D service providers |
| Contractor Location | Must be within EU/EEA or a country with a mutual assistance agreement |
| Documentation | Contract must clearly identify R&D activities; contractor must be identified |
| Commissioner vs. Contractor | Only the commissioning entity (Auftraggeber) claims the credit; contractor cannot double-claim |
| Related Party Contracts | Qualifying base reduced to 60% of actual R&D personnel costs incurred by the contractor |

### 2.4 Wage Cost Calculation

| Component | Included |
|---|---|
| Gross salary | Yes |
| Employer social security contributions | Yes (capped per Beitragsbemessungsgrenze) |
| Flat-rate approach | Employer contributions can be calculated at a flat rate of the salary |
| Time Recording | Must document hours spent on qualifying R&D vs. other activities |
| Maximum per employee | Pro-rata share of time spent on qualifying R&D projects |

## 3. Patent Box / IP Regime

**Germany does not have a patent box or intellectual property box regime.**

| Aspect | Status |
|---|---|
| Patent Box | Not available |
| IP Box | Not available |
| License Box | Not available |
| Historical Proposals | Discussed but not implemented; political preference for direct R&D funding |
| Alternative IP Benefits | R&D costs are fully deductible as business expenses under general tax principles |
| Transfer Pricing | Arm's length principle applies to IP licensing; DEMPE functions analysis required |

Germany's approach to innovation support relies on direct R&D subsidies (e.g., ZIM, BMBF programs) combined with the Forschungszulage, rather than preferential IP taxation.

## 4. Effective Benefit Analysis (per $1M USD R&D Spend)

*Assumes $1M USD ≈ €920,000 (illustrative rate); combined corporate tax rate ~30% (15% KSt + 5.5% SolZ + ~14% GewSt).*

| Scenario | Calculation | Benefit |
|---|---|---|
| **In-house R&D (standard rate)** | 25% × €920,000 = €230,000 credit | €230,000 |
| **In-house R&D (SME rate, from 2024)** | 35% × €920,000 = €322,000 credit | €322,000 |
| **Contract R&D (standard)** | 25% × (60% × €920,000) = €138,000 credit | €138,000 |
| **Contract R&D (SME)** | 35% × (60% × €920,000) = €193,200 credit | €193,200 |
| **Cap impact (if >€4M base)** | Maximum credit: €1,000,000 (standard) / €1,400,000 (SME) | Capped |
| **Full deductibility of R&D costs** | €920,000 × ~30% combined rate = €276,000 tax saving | €276,000 |
| **Combined (in-house, standard, sub-cap)** | €230,000 credit + €276,000 deduction = €506,000 | €506,000 |
| **Combined (in-house, SME, sub-cap)** | €322,000 credit + €276,000 deduction = €598,000 | €598,000 |

> **Note:** The Forschungszulage credit is itself not subject to income/corporate tax, making it a true 25% (or 35%) benefit on qualifying costs.

## 5. Claim Process & Documentation

### 5.1 Two-Stage Process

| Stage | Authority | Action |
|---|---|---|
| **Stage 1: Certification** | BSFZ (Bescheinigungsstelle Forschungszulage) | Apply for R&D project certification |
| **Stage 2: Tax Claim** | Finanzamt (local tax authority) | Claim credit on tax return with BSFZ certificate |

### 5.2 Filing Timeline

| Event | Deadline |
|---|---|
| BSFZ application | Can be filed during or after the fiscal year |
| Tax return filing | Standard corporate tax return deadline (typically 31 July of following year; extensions possible via Steuerberater to 28/29 February of second following year) |
| Forschungszulage application | Filed with or as part of the tax assessment; Form "Antrag auf Forschungszulage" |
| Refund/credit | Applied against assessed tax; excess refunded by Finanzamt |

### 5.3 Documentation Requirements

| Document | Purpose |
|---|---|
| R&D project descriptions | Submitted to BSFZ; must detail novelty, uncertainty, systematic approach |
| Time tracking records | Employee hours allocated to qualifying R&D projects (contemporaneous) |
| Employment contracts/payroll | Verify qualifying wage costs |
| Contractor agreements | Identify R&D scope, contractor details, costs |
| BSFZ Bescheinigung (certificate) | Proof of qualifying R&D status; required by Finanzamt |
| Financial statements | Substantiate expenditure amounts |
| Project progress reports | Demonstrate ongoing R&D activity and systematic approach |

### 5.4 Compliance Considerations

- The Finanzamt independently verifies the financial aspects of the claim; it does not re-assess the technical R&D qualification (that is BSFZ's role)
- Tax audits (Betriebsprüfung) may review R&D claims
- Penalties for incorrect claims under general tax penalty provisions (AO §370 et seq.)
- Statute of limitations: general 4-year assessment period; 10 years for tax evasion

## 6. Recent Changes & Sunset Provisions

| Change | Effective Date | Details |
|---|---|---|
| FZulG enacted | 1 January 2020 | Introduction of first-ever R&D tax credit in Germany |
| Assessment base doubled to €4M | 1 July 2020 (COVID measure) | Temporarily increased, later made permanent |
| €4M base made permanent | 2024 (Wachstumschancengesetz) | Permanent increase from original €2M |
| SME enhanced rate (35%) | 2024 | Higher rate for EU-definition SMEs |
| Inclusion of depreciable assets | Under discussion | Capital expenditure on R&D equipment may become eligible |
| Digital application process | Ongoing | BSFZ increasingly digitized |

**Sunset Provisions:**
- The FZulG has **no sunset clause**; it is permanent legislation
- Assessment base and rate changes require legislative amendment
- The SME enhanced rate (35%) is subject to evaluation but currently has no expiry
- Germany committed to R&D incentives in coalition agreements; expansion more likely than reduction

**Anticipated Developments:**
- Potential inclusion of depreciation on R&D equipment in the qualifying base
- Discussion of further increases to the assessment base cap
- Possible expansion of qualifying cost categories to include materials and overhead
