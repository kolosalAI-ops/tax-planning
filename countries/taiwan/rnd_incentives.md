# Taiwan - R&D Tax Incentives

## 1. Overview

| Feature | Details |
|---|---|
| **Primary Incentive** | R&D Investment Tax Credit (15% current year or 10% over 3 years) |
| **Smart Machinery Credit** | 5% of qualifying smart machinery investment |
| **Tax Credit Cap** | 30% of corporate income tax liability |
| **Governing Authority** | Ministry of Economic Affairs (MOEA); Ministry of Finance (MOF); Industrial Development Administration (IDA) |
| **Key Legislation** | Act for Industrial Innovation (AII) Art. 10; AII Enforcement Rules |
| **Corporate Tax Rate** | 20% (standard); 12% surtax on undistributed earnings |

## 2. Detailed Programs

### 2.1 R&D Investment Tax Credit - AII Art. 10

**Legal Basis:** Act for Industrial Innovation (Chan Ye Chuang Xin Tiao Li) Article 10

Companies may choose one of two credit methods (election is irrevocable for the tax year):

**Option A: Current Year Credit**

| Parameter | Details |
|---|---|
| **Credit Rate** | 15% of qualifying R&D expenditure |
| **Utilization** | Must be used in current tax year only |
| **Cap** | 30% of income tax liability for the year |
| **Carry-Forward** | None (unused credit expires) |

**Option B: Three-Year Spread Credit**

| Parameter | Details |
|---|---|
| **Credit Rate** | 10% of qualifying R&D expenditure |
| **Utilization** | Spread over 3 consecutive tax years |
| **Cap** | 30% of income tax liability for each year |
| **Carry-Forward** | Unused portion available in subsequent 2 years within the 3-year window |

### 2.2 Qualifying R&D Expenditure

| Category | Examples | Included |
|---|---|---|
| **Personnel costs** | Salaries of full-time R&D researchers | Yes |
| **Consumable materials** | Raw materials, reagents used in R&D | Yes |
| **Patent fees** | Application, maintenance, and licensing fees for R&D-generated IP | Yes |
| **Professional/consulting fees** | External technical consulting for R&D projects | Yes |
| **Equipment usage fees** | Rental/depreciation of R&D-dedicated equipment | Yes (limited) |
| **Outsourced R&D** | Payments to universities/research institutions | Yes (to approved entities) |

**Non-Qualifying Expenditure:**
- General administrative overhead
- Market research and marketing
- Quality control (routine)
- Land and building costs
- Entertainment expenses

### 2.3 Smart Machinery Investment Credit - AII Art. 10-1

**Legal Basis:** Act for Industrial Innovation Article 10-1

| Parameter | Details |
|---|---|
| **Credit Rate** | 5% of qualifying investment (current year) or 3% over 3 years |
| **Qualifying Assets** | Smart machinery and equipment, 5G systems, IoT equipment |
| **Minimum Investment** | NT$1M per asset |
| **Cap** | Combined with R&D credit, subject to 30% of tax liability |
| **Validity** | Extended through December 31, 2029 |

### 2.4 R&D-Focused Personnel Credit

| Parameter | Details |
|---|---|
| **Scope** | Enhanced credit for salary increases to R&D personnel |
| **Benefit** | Additional deduction for salary increases exceeding statutory minimum wage increases |
| **Conditions** | Must be permanent salary increases (not bonuses) |

### 2.5 Tax Credit Cap Rules

| Parameter | Details |
|---|---|
| **Overall Cap** | 30% of income tax payable for the year |
| **Stacking** | R&D credit + smart machinery credit share the 30% cap |
| **Minimum Tax** | Alternative Minimum Tax (AMT) at 12% may apply |
| **AMT Interaction** | R&D credits can reduce regular tax but AMT sets a floor |

## 3. Patent Box / IP Regime

Taiwan does not have a formal patent box regime.

| Aspect | Status |
|---|---|
| **Patent Box** | Not available |
| **IP Amortization** | Patents: useful life (typically 10-20 years); Software: 3-5 years |
| **IP Transfer Tax** | Subject to income tax on gains |
| **Royalty WHT** | 20% on payments to non-residents (reduced under limited treaty network) |
| **IP Holding** | No specific IP holding company regime |
| **Alternative** | R&D tax credit effectively incentivizes IP creation |

**Note:** Taiwan has a limited tax treaty network (approximately 34 comprehensive agreements and limited other arrangements). This affects the efficiency of cross-border IP structures.

## 4. Effective Benefit Analysis (per $1M USD R&D Spend)

*Assuming TWD/USD exchange rate of 31; NT$31M equivalent spend; 20% CIT rate*

| Scenario | Credit Rate | Tax Credit (TWD) | Tax Credit (USD) | Effective Subsidy |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Option A (15% current year)** | 15% | NT$4.65M | ~$150,000 | 15.0% |
| **Option B (10% over 3 years)** | 10% | NT$3.1M | ~$100,000 | 10.0% |
| **Smart Machinery (5%)** | 5% | NT$1.55M | ~$50,000 | 5.0% |
| **Combined (R&D + Smart Machinery)** | Up to 20% | NT$6.2M | ~$200,000 | 20.0% |

**Cap Impact Example:**
- Assume NT$100M taxable income, tax liability = NT$20M
- 30% cap = NT$6M maximum credit
- If R&D credit calculated at NT$4.65M (within cap): full credit available
- If combined credits exceed NT$6M: excess under Option A is lost; under Option B, can carry to next 2 years

**Key Insight:** Option A (15%) provides a higher rate but no carry-forward, making it suitable for companies with sufficient tax liability. Option B (10%) is safer for companies uncertain about future profitability.

## 5. Claim Process & Documentation

### 5.1 Filing Process

| Step | Action | Timeline |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Identify and document qualifying R&D projects | During tax year |
| 2 | Categorize expenditure per AII Art. 10 requirements | Year-end |
| 3 | Elect Option A or Option B (irrevocable) | With tax return |
| 4 | File corporate income tax return with R&D credit form | Within 5 months of fiscal year-end (May 31 for calendar year) |
| 5 | Obtain CPA attestation (if required) | Before filing |
| 6 | MOEA/IDA review (if selected for examination) | Post-filing |

### 5.2 Documentation Requirements

- **R&D project plans:** Objectives, methodology, technical content, personnel assignments
- **Expenditure records:** Itemized costs by project and category
- **Personnel records:** Full-time R&D staff qualifications, employment contracts
- **Patent records:** Patent applications and grants related to R&D activities
- **Consumable records:** Purchase records, usage logs for R&D materials
- **CPA attestation:** Independent verification of qualifying expenditure (for claims above threshold)
- **Progress reports:** R&D project status and outcomes documentation

### 5.3 Examination by Authorities

- MOEA Industrial Development Administration may examine R&D activity eligibility
- Tax authorities examine expenditure qualification and credit calculation
- Cross-referencing between MOEA technical review and MOF tax review
- Record retention: minimum 5 years from filing date

## 6. Recent Changes & Sunset Provisions

| Change | Effective Date | Details |
|---|---|---|
| **AII Art. 10 Extension** | 2019 amendments | R&D credit extended with no fixed sunset date |
| **Smart Machinery Credit Extension** | 2023 | Extended through December 31, 2029 |
| **AMT Rate** | 2024 | AMT rate under review; potential increase |
| **Salary Increase Credit** | 2022 | Additional credit for R&D personnel salary increases |
| **5G Investment Credit** | 2020-2025 | Special credit for 5G infrastructure investment |
| **Undistributed Earnings Surtax** | 2018 | Reduced from 10% to 5% (encourages reinvestment in R&D) |

**Sunset Provisions:**
- AII Art. 10 R&D credit: **No explicit sunset** - permanent feature of the Act
- Smart machinery credit: Authorized through December 31, 2029
- 5G investment credit: Authorized through 2025
- Periodic legislative review may adjust rates and caps
- CHIPS Act-influenced provisions for semiconductor R&D under consideration
