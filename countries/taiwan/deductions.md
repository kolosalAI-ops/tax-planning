# Taiwan - Taxable Income Deductions

> **Jurisdiction**: National (Ministry of Finance — MOF; National Taxation Bureau)
> **Governing Law**: Income Tax Act (所得稅法 — ITA); Statute for Industrial Innovation (產業創新條例 — SII)
> **Tax Year**: 2024/2025
> **Currency**: TWD (NT$)

---

## 1. Depreciation & Capital Allowances

### 1.1 General Depreciation Rules

> **Legal Basis**: ITA Art. 51; ITA Art. 54

Fixed assets other than land are depreciable for tax purposes. The taxpayer may choose from several methods, applied consistently.

#### Methods Available

| Method | Description |
|---|---|
| **Straight-Line** | Default and most common |
| **Fixed Percentage on Diminishing Value** | Declining balance method |
| **Sum-of-Years-Digits** | Accelerated method |
| **Unit-of-Production** | Output-based |
| **Working-Hour** | Usage-based |

#### Key Asset Useful Lives (MOF Tables)

| Asset Category | Useful Life Range | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| **Reinforced concrete buildings** | **35–60 years** | SL most common |
| **Steel-frame buildings** | **20–40 years** | |
| **Other buildings** | **10–25 years** | |
| **General machinery & equipment** | **5–15 years** | By industry type |
| **Motor vehicles** | **5–8 years** | |
| **Computers and IT equipment** | **3–5 years** | |
| **Furniture and fixtures** | **5–10 years** | |
| **Software (purchased)** | **3 years** | |
| **Patents** | Legal remaining life | |
| **Other intangibles** | Contract term or useful life | |

#### Salvage Value

A salvage value of **10%** of cost was traditionally required under ITA Art. 54; however, recent practice allows **0%** salvage value where justified. Confirm current treatment with local counsel.

### 1.2 Revaluation of Fixed Assets

> **Legal Basis**: ITA Art. 61

A company may revalue its fixed assets when the wholesale price index has increased by **25%** over the base period since purchase or last revaluation. The resulting increase in book value is depreciable.

## 2. Business Operating Expenses

### 2.1 General Deductibility

> **Legal Basis**: ITA Art. 24

All costs and expenses necessary for income generation are deductible.

| Expense Category | Deductibility | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Salaries, wages, bonuses | **100%** | Must be reasonable |
| Labour insurance and health insurance (employer) | **100%** | Statutory contributions |
| Labour pension fund (employer 6%+) | **100%** | Per Labour Pension Act |
| Rent and lease payments | **100%** | Business premises |
| Repairs and maintenance | **100%** | Revenue nature |
| Insurance premiums (business) | **100%** | |
| Travel expenses | **100%** | Business-related; with documentation |
| Bad debts written off | **100%** | Must meet prescribed conditions (ITA Art. 49) |
| Advertising and marketing | **100%** | |
| Training expenses | **100%** | |

### 2.2 Entertainment Expenses (交際費)

> **Legal Basis**: ITA Art. 37; MOF Enforcement Rules

Entertainment expenses are subject to caps based on revenue:

| Revenue Bracket | Deductible Limit |
|---|---|
| First NT$50 million of revenue | **0.45%** |
| NT$50 million — NT$500 million | **0.25%** |
| Above NT$500 million | **0.15%** |
| Export businesses | Additional **10%** of above limits |

Amounts exceeding the cap are **non-deductible**.

### 2.3 Charitable Donations

> **Legal Basis**: ITA Art. 36

| Donation Type | Limit |
|---|---|
| Government bodies / military / educational institutions | **100%** deductible (no cap) |
| Approved charitable and educational institutions | **10%** of taxable income |
| Political donations | Subject to Political Donations Act; capped amounts |
| Non-qualifying donations | **Non-deductible** |

## 3. Interest & Financing Costs

### 3.1 General Deductibility

> **Legal Basis**: ITA Art. 24; ITA Art. 38

Interest on borrowings for business purposes is deductible. Excess interest not related to income generation is non-deductible (ITA Art. 38).

### 3.2 Thin Capitalisation

> **Legal Basis**: ITA Art. 43-2

| Parameter | Value |
|---|---|
| Debt-to-equity ratio | **3:1** for related-party borrowings from overseas entities |
| Excess interest | **Non-deductible** |
| Application | Interest on inter-company loans from related parties exceeding the DER |
| Transfer pricing | Related-party interest must also satisfy arm's length standard |

### 3.3 Interest on Construction

Interest on construction-period borrowings must be capitalised into the cost of the asset and depreciated over the asset's useful life.

## 4. Research & Development

Taiwan provides R&D tax credits under the Statute for Industrial Innovation (SII) Art. 10. Companies may choose either a **15% credit** on current-year qualifying R&D expenditure (usable in current year only) or a **10% credit** spread over 3 years. The maximum credit is capped at 30% of CIT liability in the applicable year. Qualifying activities include research in new products, production techniques, and service delivery methods.

See [R&D Incentives](rnd_incentives.md) for full program detail.

## 5. Loss Carry-Forward & Carry-Back

### 5.1 Loss Carry-Forward

> **Legal Basis**: ITA Art. 39

| Parameter | Value |
|---|---|
| Duration | **10 years** forward |
| Carry-back | **Not available** |
| Requirement | Company must maintain a complete set of accounting books and file a "blue return" or have CPA-certified financial statements |
| Limitation | 100% of taxable income (no percentage cap) |

### 5.2 Blue Return Requirement

The loss carry-forward is only available if the taxpayer files a "blue return" (CPA-audited return or properly maintained books) for both the loss year and the utilisation year.

## 6. Provisions & Reserves

| Provision Type | Deductibility | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Specific bad debts written off | **Deductible** | Must meet ITA Art. 49 conditions (2 years overdue, legal action exhausted, debtor bankrupt) |
| General bad debt provisions | **Deductible** | Up to **1%** of total receivables outstanding at year-end — ITA Art. 49 |
| Employee retirement reserves | **Deductible** | Old Labour Standards Act: up to 15% of total salaries; funded through approved account |
| Warranty provisions | **Not deductible** | Until paid |
| Insurance reserves | **Deductible** | Industry-specific for licensed insurers |

## 7. Industry-Specific Deductions

### 7.1 Statute for Industrial Innovation (SII) Incentives

> **Legal Basis**: SII Art. 10; SII Art. 10-1; SII Art. 23-3

| Incentive | Benefit |
|---|---|
| R&D tax credit | 15% (1-year) or 10% (3-year) of qualifying R&D expenditure — SII Art. 10 |
| Smart machinery/5G investment credit | 5% of investment (1-year) or 3% (3-year) — SII Art. 10-1 [unverified — confirm current status] |
| Undistributed earnings surtax reduction | Retained earnings invested in specified assets may reduce the 5% surtax — SII Art. 23-3 |

### 7.2 Biotech and New Drug Development

> **Legal Basis**: Act for the Development of Biotech and Pharmaceutical Industry

| Parameter | Value |
|---|---|
| R&D tax credit | 25% of qualifying R&D expenditure |
| Personnel training credit | 25% of qualifying training expenditure |
| Limit | Combined credits capped at 50% of CIT liability |

### 7.3 Alternative Minimum Tax (AMT)

> **Legal Basis**: Income Basic Tax Act (所得基本稅額條例)

| Parameter | Value |
|---|---|
| AMT rate | **12%** for profit-seeking enterprises |
| Exemption | NT$600,000 of basic income |
| Interaction | Certain tax incentives (SII credits, tax-exempt income) are added back for AMT purposes |
| Effect | Taxpayer pays the higher of regular CIT or AMT |

## 8. Treaties & Transfer Pricing

### 8.1 Transfer Pricing

> **Legal Basis**: ITA Art. 43-1; MOF Transfer Pricing Regulations

| Parameter | Value |
|---|---|
| Arm's length standard | Mandatory for all related-party transactions |
| Methods | CUP, resale price, cost plus, comparable profit method, profit split, other reasonable methods |
| Documentation | Contemporaneous TP documentation required for material transactions |
| CbC reporting | Required for groups with consolidated revenue ≥ NT$27 billion |
| Penalties | Understatement penalties; additional tax plus interest |
| APA | Available (unilateral and bilateral) |

### 8.2 Treaty Network

Taiwan has over 30 comprehensive income tax agreements (due to diplomatic constraints, these are often titled "avoidance of double taxation agreements" rather than formal treaties). Benefits include reduced withholding rates and dispute resolution.
