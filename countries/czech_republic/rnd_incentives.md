# Czech Republic - R&D Tax Incentives

## 1. Overview

| Feature | Details |
|---|---|
| **Primary Incentive** | R&D Tax Allowance (Odpočet na podporu výzkumu a vývoje) |
| **Base Rate** | 100% additional deduction (200% total deduction) |
| **Incremental Bonus** | 110% on year-over-year increase in R&D expenditure |
| **Legal Basis** | Zákon o daních z příjmů (ZDP) §34(4)-(5) — Income Tax Act |
| **Carry-Forward** | 3 tax years |
| **Patent Box** | None — Czech Republic does not operate a patent box regime |
| **CIT Rate** | 21% (19% from 2024 for certain taxpayers; increasing to 21% under consolidation) |
| **Pre-Documentation** | R&D project must be documented **before** activities begin |
| **Refundability** | Not refundable — requires taxable income |

## 2. Detailed Programs

### 2.1 R&D Tax Allowance (Odpočet na podporu VaV)

**Legal Basis:** Zákon č. 586/1992 Sb., o daních z příjmů (ZDP), §34 odst. 4 a 5

The Czech R&D allowance provides a 100% additional deduction on qualifying R&D expenditure, effectively allowing the same costs to be deducted twice.

#### Deduction Rates

| Component | Rate | Total Deduction Effect |
|---|---|---|
| Base additional deduction | 100% of qualifying R&D costs | 200% total (100% standard + 100% additional) |
| Incremental bonus | 110% on increase vs. prior year | 210% on the incremental portion |
| First-time claimant | 100% on all qualifying costs (no comparison year) | 200% total |

#### How the Incremental Bonus Works

- If qualifying R&D costs **exceed** the prior year's qualifying costs, the excess is deducted at **110%** (instead of 100%)
- If costs are equal to or lower than the prior year, the standard 100% additional deduction applies
- The incremental portion receives a 10% premium to incentivize growing R&D investment

**Example:**
| Year | Qualifying Costs | Prior Year | Increment | Base Deduction | Incremental Deduction |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Year 1 | CZK 10,000,000 | N/A (first year) | N/A | CZK 10,000,000 (100%) | N/A |
| Year 2 | CZK 13,000,000 | CZK 10,000,000 | CZK 3,000,000 | CZK 10,000,000 (100%) | CZK 3,300,000 (110%) |

#### Qualifying R&D Activities

Activities must meet the definition of R&D under Czech law (aligned with Frascati Manual):

- Basic research (základní výzkum)
- Applied research (aplikovaný výzkum)
- Experimental development (experimentální vývoj)
- Must involve an appreciable element of **novelty** and resolution of **scientific or technical uncertainty**
- Must be systematic, creative, and reproducible
- Routine testing, quality control, and minor modifications are excluded

#### Qualifying Expenditure

| Cost Category | Eligible | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Personnel costs (wages, salaries, social/health contributions) | Yes | R&D staff directly involved |
| Materials and supplies consumed in R&D | Yes | Must be directly attributable |
| Depreciation of tangible R&D assets | Yes | Proportional to R&D use |
| Overhead costs | Yes | Only directly allocable overhead |
| Travel costs related to R&D | Yes | If directly connected to R&D project |
| Services from public research organizations | Yes | Universities, Academy of Sciences |
| Contract R&D from private entities | **No** | Major limitation — private subcontracting excluded |
| Certification and patent costs | No | Not qualifying expenditure |

**Critical Limitation:** Costs of R&D services purchased from **private sector entities** (subcontracting) are **not eligible** for the R&D allowance. Only services from public research organizations (universities, public research institutes) qualify.

### 2.2 Project Documentation Requirement

**One of the strictest documentation requirements in Europe.**

**Legal Basis:** ZDP §34(5) — R&D project documentation must be prepared and signed by the company's authorized representative **before** R&D activities commence.

#### Mandatory Project Document Contents

| Element | Description |
|---|---|
| Project identification | Name, start date, planned end date |
| R&D objectives | Clear statement of research/development goals |
| Description of novelty | What new knowledge or capability is being sought |
| Planned activities | Detailed description of R&D work to be performed |
| Cost budget | Estimated qualifying R&D costs |
| Method of cost accounting | How R&D costs will be tracked and allocated |
| Personnel identification | R&D staff assigned to the project |
| Time schedule | Project milestones and timeline |
| Evaluation criteria | How results will be assessed |
| Date and signature | Signed by authorized representative **before project start** |

**Failure to prepare documentation before the project starts will result in denial of the entire allowance for that project.** This is the most commonly cited reason for claim rejection.

### 2.3 Limitations and Restrictions

| Restriction | Details |
|---|---|
| Private subcontracting | Not eligible — only public research organization services qualify |
| Pre-documentation | Must be prepared before R&D activities begin |
| Grant-funded costs | Costs covered by public grants/subsidies are excluded |
| Carry-forward | 3 years only (relatively short) |
| No refundability | Cannot be refunded — only reduces tax liability |
| No R&D centre enhancement | No enhanced rate for certified R&D centres (unlike Poland) |

## 3. Patent Box / IP Regime

The Czech Republic **does not** operate a patent box or preferential IP taxation regime.

| Feature | Details |
|---|---|
| Patent box regime | None |
| Standard CIT rate on IP income | 21% |
| Royalty WHT | 15% (reduced under tax treaties) |
| IP sale gains | Standard CIT rate |
| Policy status | No legislative proposals for patent box currently under consideration |

### Impact of No Patent Box

- Companies generating significant IP income receive no preferential treatment in the Czech Republic
- IP holding structures may benefit from locating IP in jurisdictions with patent box regimes
- The absence of a patent box makes the Czech Republic less attractive for IP-intensive headquartering
- The R&D allowance (input-based) is the sole fiscal incentive for R&D

## 4. Effective Benefit Analysis (per $1M USD R&D Spend)

*Assumptions: Exchange rate ~CZK 23/USD; CIT rate 21%; company is profitable; 20% year-over-year increase in R&D spend; no private subcontracting*

### Scenario A: Base R&D Allowance (No Increment)

| Benefit Component | Calculation | Value (USD) |
|---|---|---|
| Base R&D expenditure | — | $1,000,000 |
| Standard CIT deduction (21%) | $1,000,000 × 21% | $210,000 |
| Additional R&D deduction (100% × 21%) | $1,000,000 × 100% × 21% | $210,000 |
| **Total benefit** | | **$420,000** |
| **Effective rate** | | **42.0%** |

### Scenario B: With Incremental Bonus

| Benefit Component | Calculation | Value (USD) |
|---|---|---|
| Prior year R&D spend | — | $833,333 |
| Current year R&D spend | — | $1,000,000 |
| Increment | $1,000,000 - $833,333 | $166,667 |
| Standard CIT deduction (21%) | $1,000,000 × 21% | $210,000 |
| Base additional (100% on $833,333) | $833,333 × 100% × 21% | $175,000 |
| Incremental (110% on $166,667) | $166,667 × 110% × 21% | $38,500 |
| **Total benefit** | | **$423,500** |
| **Effective rate** | | **42.4%** |

### Scenario Comparison

| Scenario | CIT Deduction | R&D Allowance | Total | Effective Rate |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Base allowance (no increment) | $210,000 | $210,000 | $420,000 | 42.0% |
| With 20% incremental bonus | $210,000 | $213,500 | $423,500 | 42.4% |
| Loss-making company | $0 | $0 (carry fwd 3 yrs) | $0 immediate | 0% immediate |
| 50% costs are private subcontracting | $210,000 | $105,000 | $315,000 | 31.5% |

**Key Limitation:** The exclusion of private subcontracting costs significantly reduces the effective benefit for companies that outsource R&D to private providers.

## 5. Claim Process & Documentation

### Step-by-Step Claim Process

1. **Prepare R&D Project Documentation** — **BEFORE starting R&D activities** (mandatory)
2. **Conduct R&D Activities** — Execute the documented project
3. **Track Qualifying Costs** — Maintain detailed cost records per project
4. **Complete the R&D Allowance Form** — Prepare schedule showing qualifying costs
5. **File CIT Return (Přiznání k dani z příjmů PO)** — Include R&D allowance claim
6. **Retain Documentation** — Keep all records available for potential tax audit
7. **Carry Forward (if needed)** — Unused allowance carried forward up to 3 years

### Documentation Requirements

| Requirement | Details |
|---|---|
| **Pre-project documentation** | **Mandatory — must exist before R&D starts** |
| Project descriptions | Objectives, novelty, uncertainty, planned activities |
| Personnel records | Time allocation to specific R&D projects |
| Cost records | Detailed breakdown per qualifying category |
| Results documentation | Outcomes achieved vs. planned objectives |
| Accounting records | Separation of R&D costs from other business costs |
| Public research contracts | Agreements with universities/research institutes |
| Year-over-year comparison | Prior year costs for incremental calculation |

### Audit Risk and Common Issues

Czech tax authorities actively audit R&D allowance claims. Common issues include:

- **Missing or late pre-project documentation** — single most common reason for denial
- Insufficient evidence of novelty or technical uncertainty
- Inadequate time records for personnel cost allocation
- Inclusion of ineligible costs (private subcontracting, grant-funded costs)
- Failure to separate R&D from routine activities
- Lack of documentation of R&D results and outcomes

## 6. Recent Changes & Sunset Provisions

| Year | Change | Impact |
|---|---|---|
| 2005 | R&D allowance introduced in Czech tax law | Original 100% additional deduction |
| 2014 | Incremental bonus (110%) introduced | Reward for increasing R&D investment |
| 2020 | Enhanced guidance from tax authorities | Greater clarity on qualifying activities |
| 2021 | Consolidation package discussions | CIT rate changes discussed |
| 2024 | CIT rate increased to 21% (from 19%) | Higher nominal benefit from R&D allowance |
| 2024 | Tightened audit procedures for R&D claims | Increased scrutiny by Finanční správa |
| 2025 | No significant changes to R&D allowance | Scheme continues with existing parameters |

### Sunset Provisions

- The R&D allowance under ZDP §34(4)-(5) is a **permanent provision** — no sunset date
- Parameters have remained largely stable since introduction
- No plans to introduce a patent box or significantly modify the scheme
- The 3-year carry-forward is relatively short compared to peer countries
- No refundability mechanism is under consideration

### Policy Discussion

- Industry associations have advocated for extending the carry-forward period
- Inclusion of private subcontracting costs has been discussed but not enacted
- Patent box introduction has been proposed but lacks political momentum
- Czech R&D incentive is considered less competitive than neighboring countries (Poland, Hungary)
