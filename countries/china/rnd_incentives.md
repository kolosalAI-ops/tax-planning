# China - R&D Tax Incentives

## 1. Overview

| Feature | Details |
|---|---|
| **Primary Incentive** | 200% Additional Deduction (300% total, permanent from 2023) |
| **HNTE Status** | 15% reduced CIT rate (vs. 25% standard) |
| **TASE Status** | 15% reduced CIT rate for Technologically Advanced Service Enterprises |
| **IC Industry** | CIT exemptions up to 10 years for integrated circuit enterprises |
| **Governing Authority** | State Taxation Administration (STA); Ministry of Science and Technology (MOST); Ministry of Finance (MOF) |
| **Key Legislation** | Enterprise Income Tax (EIT) Law Art. 30; EIT Implementing Regulations Art. 95; Cai Shui [2023] No. 7 |
| **Corporate Tax Rate** | 25% (standard); 15% (HNTE/TASE); 0-12.5% (IC/software) |

## 2. Detailed Programs

### 2.1 Super Deduction for R&D Expenses - EIT Law Art. 30

**Legal Basis:** Enterprise Income Tax Law Art. 30(1); EIT Implementing Regulations Art. 95; Cai Shui [2023] No. 7

| Parameter | Details |
|---|---|
| **Base Deduction** | 100% (standard expense deduction) |
| **Additional Deduction** | 200% of qualifying R&D expenses |
| **Total Deduction** | 300% of qualifying R&D expenses |
| **Status** | Permanent from January 1, 2023 (previously temporary, repeatedly extended) |
| **Eligible Entities** | All enterprises (except negative list industries) |
| **Cap** | No cap on qualifying expenditure |

**Evolution of the Super Deduction:**

| Period | Additional Deduction Rate | Total Deduction |
|---|---|---|
| Before 2018 | 50% | 150% |
| 2018-2020 | 75% | 175% |
| 2021-2022 | 100% (manufacturing/SMEs) | 200% |
| 2023 onwards | 200% (all enterprises) | 300% (permanent) |

**Negative List Industries (Not Eligible):**
- Tobacco manufacturing
- Accommodation and catering
- Wholesale and retail
- Real estate
- Leasing and business services
- Entertainment

### 2.2 High and New Technology Enterprise (HNTE) - EIT Law Art. 28

**Legal Basis:** EIT Law Art. 28; Guo Ke Fa Huo [2016] No. 32

| Parameter | Details |
|---|---|
| **CIT Rate** | 15% (vs. 25% standard) |
| **Duration** | 3-year certification, renewable |
| **Certification Body** | Joint certification by MOST, MOF, and STA |
| **IP Requirement** | Must own core proprietary IP rights |
| **R&D Ratio Requirement** | Revenue < RMB 50M: ≥ 6%; RMB 50-200M: ≥ 4%; > RMB 200M: ≥ 3% |
| **Revenue from Hi-Tech** | ≥ 60% from hi-tech products/services |
| **Personnel** | ≥ 10% of total employees in R&D |

**Eight Qualifying Technology Fields:**
1. Electronic information technology
2. Biotechnology and new medicine
3. Aerospace
4. New materials
5. High-tech services
6. New energy and energy conservation
7. Resources and environmental technology
8. Advanced manufacturing and automation

### 2.3 Technologically Advanced Service Enterprise (TASE)

**Legal Basis:** Cai Shui [2017] No. 79

| Parameter | Details |
|---|---|
| **CIT Rate** | 15% |
| **Qualifying Services** | Technology-advanced service outsourcing, R&D and design services |
| **Revenue Requirement** | ≥ 50% from qualifying service activities |
| **Education Requirement** | ≥ 50% of employees with college degree or above |

### 2.4 Integrated Circuit (IC) and Software Industry

**Legal Basis:** Cai Shui [2020] No. 45; State Council Document [2020] No. 8

| Linewidth / Investment | CIT Incentive |
|---|---|
| **IC (≤ 28nm, investment > RMB 15B)** | 10-year exemption, followed by 10% for 5 years |
| **IC (≤ 65nm, investment > RMB 15B)** | 5-year exemption, followed by 12.5% for 5 years |
| **IC (≤ 130nm, investment > RMB 10B)** | 2-year exemption, followed by 12.5% for 3 years |
| **IC Design / Software** | 2-year exemption, followed by 12.5% for 3 years |
| **Key IC Design** | 10% CIT rate for up to 5 years |

### 2.5 Accelerated Depreciation

| Parameter | Details |
|---|---|
| **R&D Equipment** | Equipment valued ≤ RMB 5M: one-time deduction in current year |
| **Equipment > RMB 5M** | Accelerated depreciation (double-declining or sum-of-years) |
| **New Equipment (2024)** | 100% bonus depreciation for equipment purchased in designated sectors |

## 3. Patent Box / IP Regime

China does not have a formal OECD-style patent box regime. However, several IP-related incentives exist.

| Aspect | Status |
|---|---|
| **Patent Box** | Not available as a standalone regime |
| **HNTE as Proxy** | 15% rate for IP-rich companies functions similarly |
| **Technology Transfer Exemption** | First RMB 5M of qualifying tech transfer income exempt; excess at 50% |
| **IP Amortization** | Self-developed IP: generally 10 years; software: 2-3 years |
| **Royalty WHT** | 10% on payments to non-residents (reduced under treaties) |
| **Tech Transfer to Domestic** | Exempt if technology transfer within China for < RMB 5M |

### Technology Transfer Income Exemption - EIT Law Art. 27

| Parameter | Details |
|---|---|
| **Full Exemption** | First RMB 5M of qualifying technology transfer income |
| **50% Exemption** | Income exceeding RMB 5M taxed at 50% of applicable rate |
| **Qualifying Transfers** | Patents, computer software copyrights, IC layout designs, biological varieties, plant new varieties; 5+ year exclusive licensing |

## 4. Effective Benefit Analysis (per $1M USD R&D Spend)

*Assuming RMB/USD exchange rate of 7.2; RMB 7.2M equivalent spend*

| Scenario | Tax Treatment | Tax Saving (RMB) | Tax Saving (USD) |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Super Deduction (25% CIT)** | RMB 7.2M × 200% × 25% | RMB 3.6M | ~$500,000 |
| **Super Deduction (15% HNTE)** | RMB 7.2M × 200% × 15% | RMB 2.16M | ~$300,000 |
| **HNTE Rate Reduction** | 10% savings on taxable income | Varies by income | Varies |
| **IC Industry (exempt)** | Full CIT exemption | Full CIT savings | Full CIT savings |
| **Combined (Super Deduction + HNTE)** | 300% deduction at 15% rate | Effective benefit significant | ~$500,000+ |

**Comprehensive Example (HNTE with RMB 50M profit, RMB 7.2M R&D):**
- Super deduction: RMB 7.2M × 200% = RMB 14.4M additional deduction
- Taxable income reduced to: RMB 50M - RMB 14.4M = RMB 35.6M
- Tax at HNTE 15%: RMB 5.34M
- Versus standard 25% on RMB 50M: RMB 12.5M
- **Total saving: RMB 7.16M (~$994,000)**

## 5. Claim Process & Documentation

### 5.1 Filing Process

| Step | Action | Timeline |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Maintain R&D project ledger (auxiliary account) | Ongoing |
| 2 | Prepare R&D expenditure auxiliary account summary | Year-end |
| 3 | Self-assess and claim super deduction on annual CIT return | May 31 of following year |
| 4 | Apply for HNTE certification (if applicable) | Triennial application to MOST |
| 5 | Retain documentation for audit | Minimum 10 years |

### 5.2 R&D Auxiliary Account (Mandatory)

- **Since 2023:** Simplified auxiliary account format per STA Announcement [2023] No. 7
- Must separately track R&D expenditure by project
- Must distinguish between costs that qualify for super deduction and those that do not
- Must be maintained contemporaneously (not reconstructed at year-end)

### 5.3 Documentation Requirements

- **Project approval documents:** Internal R&D project approval and budget
- **R&D auxiliary account:** Detailed by project, by expense category
- **Personnel list:** Dedicated R&D staff with employment records
- **Expense vouchers:** Invoices (fapiao), contracts, payment records
- **Results documentation:** Patents, test reports, prototypes, research papers
- **Outsourced R&D contracts:** 80% of outsourced costs qualify for super deduction

### 5.4 HNTE Certification Documentation

- IP ownership certificates
- R&D expenditure audited by qualified CPA firm
- Revenue from hi-tech products/services audited
- Science and technology personnel records
- Innovation capability scoring (IP, technology conversion, R&D management, growth indicators)

## 6. Recent Changes & Sunset Provisions

| Change | Effective Date | Details |
|---|---|---|
| **300% Permanent** | January 1, 2023 | Super deduction made permanent at 200% additional (Cai Shui [2023] No. 7) |
| **Quarterly Pre-Payment Claims** | 2023 | Can claim super deduction in quarterly pre-payment filings |
| **Simplified Auxiliary Account** | 2023 | Streamlined record-keeping requirements |
| **IC Incentive Extension** | 2020-2030 | 10-year framework for advanced IC manufacturers |
| **Equipment Bonus Depreciation** | 2024 | 100% first-year deduction for qualifying equipment |
| **HNTE Certification Reform** | 2024 | Streamlined online certification process |

**Sunset Provisions:**
- Super deduction at 300%: **Permanent** - no sunset clause
- HNTE 15% rate: Permanent feature of EIT Law; 3-year certification renewable
- IC/Software incentives: Authorized through 2030 under State Council policy
- Equipment bonus depreciation: Subject to periodic extension
- Technology transfer exemption: Permanent feature of EIT Law
