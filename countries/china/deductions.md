# China - Taxable Income Deductions

> **Jurisdiction**: National (State Taxation Administration — STA/SAT)
> **Governing Law**: Enterprise Income Tax Law 2007 (EITL); EITL Implementation Regulations (IR)
> **Tax Year**: 2024/2025
> **Currency**: CNY / RMB (¥)

---

## 1. Depreciation & Capital Allowances

### 1.1 General Depreciation Rules

> **Legal Basis**: EITL Art. 11; IR Art. 57–60

Depreciation is calculated using the straight-line method as the default. Accelerated depreciation (declining balance or sum-of-years-digits) is permitted for assets subject to rapid technological change or high vibration/corrosion (EITL Art. 32).

#### Minimum Useful Lives (IR Art. 60)

| Asset Category | Minimum Useful Life | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| **Buildings & structures** | **20 years** | SL only (default) |
| **Aircraft, trains, ships, machinery, other production equipment** | **10 years** | |
| **Appliances, tools, furniture related to production & business** | **5 years** | |
| **Transport vehicles (other than aircraft/trains/ships)** | **4 years** | |
| **Electronic equipment** | **3 years** | Computers, servers, etc. |

Residual value is determined by the enterprise; typically 5% of cost (common practice, not statutory).

### 1.2 Accelerated Depreciation

> **Legal Basis**: EITL Art. 32; Caishui [2014] No. 75; Announcement 12/2023 [unverified — confirm with local counsel]

| Method | Description |
|---|---|
| **Declining balance (双倍余额递减法)** | 200% of SL rate applied to net book value |
| **Sum-of-years-digits (年数总和法)** | Accelerated write-off |
| **100% expensing** | Available for qualifying manufacturing SMEs on newly acquired equipment (Announcement 12/2023) |

#### 100% Immediate Expensing (Manufacturing SMEs)

| Parameter | Value |
|---|---|
| Eligible enterprises | Manufacturing SMEs (small and micro) |
| Qualifying assets | Newly acquired equipment and appliances |
| Unit value threshold | **CNY 5 million** (100% immediate write-off for equipment ≤ CNY 5 million) |
| Above CNY 5 million | Shortened useful life (not less than 60% of statutory minimum) or accelerated method |
| Effective period | Extended periodically; verify current status for 2024/2025 [unverified — confirm with local counsel] |

### 1.3 Amortisation of Intangible Assets

> **Legal Basis**: EITL Art. 12; IR Art. 67

| Parameter | Value |
|---|---|
| Method | Straight-line |
| Minimum life | **10 years** (general intangibles) |
| Exception | If contractual/legal term is shorter, use that term |
| Self-developed intangibles | Cost capitalised and amortised from the date available for use |
| Goodwill | Not amortised unless impaired; deductible on disposal of the investee |
| Land use rights | Amortised over the term of the right |

## 2. Business Operating Expenses

### 2.1 General Deductibility

> **Legal Basis**: EITL Art. 8; IR Art. 27–55

Expenses that are **actual, relevant, and reasonable** and incurred in deriving taxable revenue are deductible.

| Expense Category | Deductibility | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Salaries and wages | **100%** | Reasonable compensation — IR Art. 34 |
| Social insurance (employer: pension, medical, unemployment, injury, maternity) | **100%** | Within statutory rates — IR Art. 35 |
| Housing fund (住房公积金) | **100%** | Within statutory caps — IR Art. 35 |
| Supplementary pension/medical insurance | Deductible up to **5%** of total wages each | IR Art. 35(2) |
| Rent and lease payments | **100%** | Operating leases; finance lease principal not deductible (depreciation instead) |
| Repairs and maintenance | **100%** | Revenue nature |
| Insurance premiums (commercial) | **100%** | For business assets |
| Travel expenses | **100%** | With proper documentation |
| Training expenses | **Deductible** up to **8%** of total wages | Excess carries forward indefinitely — IR Art. 42 |
| Union fees (工会经费) | Deductible up to **2%** of total wages | IR Art. 41 |
| Employee welfare (福利费) | Deductible up to **14%** of total wages | IR Art. 40 |

### 2.2 Entertainment Expenses (业务招待费)

> **Legal Basis**: IR Art. 43

| Parameter | Value |
|---|---|
| Deductible amount | **60%** of actual entertainment expenses incurred |
| Maximum cap | **0.5%** of annual revenue (sales + other business revenue) |
| Effective rule | The **lower** of 60% of actual expenses or 0.5% of revenue |

### 2.3 Advertising & Business Promotion Expenses

> **Legal Basis**: IR Art. 44

| Parameter | Value |
|---|---|
| General enterprises | Deductible up to **15%** of annual revenue |
| Cosmetics manufacturing, pharmaceutical, and beverage industries | Deductible up to **30%** of annual revenue |
| Excess | Carries forward to subsequent years indefinitely |
| Tobacco advertising | **Not deductible** |

### 2.4 Charitable Donations

> **Legal Basis**: EITL Art. 9; IR Art. 51–53

| Parameter | Value |
|---|---|
| Limit | **12%** of annual accounting profit |
| Excess carry-forward | **3 years** (from 2017) |
| Qualifying donations | Through eligible public welfare social organisations or government bodies |
| Targeted poverty alleviation donations | **100%** deductible (special policy) |
| Non-qualifying donations | **Non-deductible** |

## 3. Interest & Financing Costs

### 3.1 General Deductibility

> **Legal Basis**: EITL Art. 8; IR Art. 37–38

| Parameter | Value |
|---|---|
| Third-party bank loans | Fully deductible |
| Non-financial enterprise to non-financial enterprise loans | Deductible up to the rate for similar bank loans for the same period — IR Art. 38 |
| Related-party interest | Subject to thin capitalisation and TP rules |

### 3.2 Thin Capitalisation

> **Legal Basis**: EITL Art. 46; Caishui [2008] No. 121

| Parameter | Value |
|---|---|
| General enterprises | **2:1** debt-to-equity ratio |
| Financial institutions | **5:1** debt-to-equity ratio |
| Excess interest | **Non-deductible** |
| GAAR backstop | STA may adjust if arrangement lacks commercial substance |
| Safe harbour | If enterprise can demonstrate arm's length terms, actual DER may exceed 2:1 |

### 3.3 Related-Party Loan Interest

Interest on related-party loans is deductible only if:
1. The DER does not exceed 2:1 (or 5:1 for financial institutions)
2. The interest rate is at arm's length
3. Both conditions must be satisfied; excess is disallowed

## 4. Research & Development

China offers a 200% super deduction for qualifying R&D expenses (100% base + 100% additional deduction) under EITL Art. 30 and Caishui [2023] No. 7. This applies to all enterprises (not limited to specific industries from 2023 onward). High-New Technology Enterprises (HNTEs) additionally benefit from a reduced 15% CIT rate.

See [R&D Incentives](rnd_incentives.md) for full program detail.

## 5. Loss Carry-Forward & Carry-Back

> **Legal Basis**: EITL Art. 18

| Parameter | Standard | HNTE / Encouraged Industries |
|---|---|---|
| Carry-forward period | **5 years** | **10 years** (from 2018 for qualifying HNTEs and technology-based SMEs) |
| Carry-back | **Not allowed** | Not allowed |
| Utilisation limit | **100%** of taxable income | 100% |
| Condition | Losses must be verified by tax authorities | Same |

## 6. Provisions & Reserves

> **Legal Basis**: IR Art. 55; EITL Art. 10

| Provision Type | Deductibility | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| General provisions/reserves | **Not deductible** | EITL Art. 10(7) |
| Bank loan loss reserves | **Deductible** | 1% of risk asset balance for qualifying financial institutions — Caishui [2012] No. 25 |
| Insurance claim reserves | **Deductible** | Within statutory limits for licensed insurers |
| Securities firms reserves | **Deductible** | Specific provisions per regulation |
| Asset impairment losses | **Not deductible** as provisions | Only deductible on actual disposal |
| Specific bad debt write-offs | **Deductible** | With supporting evidence and approval |

## 7. Industry-Specific Deductions

### 7.1 High-New Technology Enterprises (HNTE)

> **Legal Basis**: EITL Art. 28; Guofa [2016] No. 32

| Parameter | Value |
|---|---|
| CIT rate | **15%** (reduced from 25%) |
| R&D spending threshold | 3%–6% of revenue (depending on revenue bracket) |
| IP ownership | Must own core IP in China |
| Validity | 3-year certification; renewable |

### 7.2 Western Development Strategy

> **Legal Basis**: Caishui [2020] No. 23

| Parameter | Value |
|---|---|
| CIT rate | **15%** for encouraged industries in western China |
| Duration | Extended through 2030 |
| Qualifying industries | Must derive ≥ 60% of revenue from encouraged catalogue sectors |

### 7.3 Software & Integrated Circuit Enterprises

> **Legal Basis**: Caishui [2020] No. 45

| Parameter | Value |
|---|---|
| Qualifying IC enterprises | 5-year CIT exemption + 5-year 50% reduction (from first profit year) |
| Key software enterprises | 5-year CIT exemption + follow-on 10% rate |
| Conditions | Revenue, IP, and R&D thresholds apply |

### 7.4 Environmental Protection / Energy & Water Conservation

> **Legal Basis**: EITL Art. 34

Qualifying environmental protection and energy/water conservation equipment: **10%** tax credit on equipment investment (CTA equivalent).

## 8. Treaties & Transfer Pricing

### 8.1 Transfer Pricing

> **Legal Basis**: EITL Art. 41–44; SAT Announcement [2017] No. 6 (TP documentation)

| Parameter | Value |
|---|---|
| Arm's length standard | Mandatory for all related-party transactions |
| Methods | CUP, resale price, cost plus, TNMM, profit split |
| Documentation tiers | Master File, Local File, Special Issue File |
| Master File threshold | Related-party transactions > CNY 1 billion |
| Local File threshold | Related-party transactions > specified amounts (varies: CNY 200M for tangible goods, CNY 40M for services) |
| CbC reporting | Groups with consolidated revenue ≥ CNY 5.5 billion |
| Penalties | Underpayment + interest; 5-year statute on adjustments (10 years for major cases) |
| APA | Available — unilateral, bilateral, multilateral |

### 8.2 Treaty Network

China has over 110 double tax agreements. China adopted certain MLI provisions selectively.

### 8.3 General Anti-Avoidance Rule (GAAR)

> **Legal Basis**: EITL Art. 47; IR Art. 120

The STA may adjust taxable income for arrangements that lack reasonable business purpose and have a principal purpose of obtaining tax benefits.
