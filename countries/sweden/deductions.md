# Sweden - Taxable Income Deductions

> **Jurisdiction**: National (Skatteverket)
> **Governing Law**: Inkomstskattelag 1999:1229 (IL)
> **Tax Year**: 2024/2025
> **Currency**: SEK (kr)

---

## 1. Depreciation & Capital Allowances

Sweden allows tax depreciation on tangible and intangible fixed assets used in business operations. The rules are set out in Chapters 18-20 of the Income Tax Act (IL).

### 1.1 Machinery & Equipment — IL Ch.18

Taxpayers may choose between two methods for each category of assets:

| Method | Rate | Statutory Basis |
|---|---|---|
| **Declining balance (räkenskapsenlig avskrivning)** | 30% of book value per year | IL Ch.18 §13 |
| **Straight-line (restvärdesavskrivning)** | 20% of acquisition cost per year | IL Ch.18 §17 |

**Key rules:**

| Parameter | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Pooling** | All machinery and equipment are pooled together for declining balance — IL Ch.18 §13 |
| **Complementary rule** | Assets held for 5+ years may be written down to residual value under the 5-year rule — IL Ch.18 §14 |
| **Low-value assets** | Assets with acquisition cost below SEK 26,250 (half a price base amount) may be fully expensed in the year of acquisition — IL Ch.18 §4 |
| **Short useful life** | Assets with useful life of 3 years or less may be fully expensed — IL Ch.18 §4 |

### 1.2 Buildings — IL Ch.19

Buildings are depreciated on a straight-line basis at rates determined by building type:

| Building Type | Annual Rate | Statutory Basis |
|---|---|---|
| **Industrial buildings** | 4% | IL Ch.19 §5 |
| **Office buildings** | 2% | IL Ch.19 §5 |
| **Commercial buildings (retail)** | 3% | IL Ch.19 §5 |
| **Warehouses** | 4% | IL Ch.19 §5 |
| **Hotels & restaurants** | 3% | IL Ch.19 §5 |
| **Residential rental buildings** | 2% | IL Ch.19 §5 |
| **Timber-frame buildings** | 5% | IL Ch.19 §5 |

**Note:** Land is not depreciable. Land improvements are depreciated at 5% per year — IL Ch.20 §5.

### 1.3 Intangible Assets — IL Ch.18 §1

| Asset Type | Method | Rate |
|---|---|---|
| **Patents, licenses, trademarks** | Pooled with machinery | 30% declining balance or 20% straight-line |
| **Goodwill (acquired)** | Pooled with machinery | 30% declining balance or 20% straight-line |
| **Self-developed intangibles** | Expensed as incurred | N/A |

## 2. Business Operating Expenses

General deductibility of business expenses is governed by IL Ch.16 §1 — expenses must be incurred for the purpose of acquiring or maintaining income.

### 2.1 Fully Deductible Expenses — IL Ch.16 §1

| Expense Category | Deductibility | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| **Employee salaries & wages** | 100% | Including bonuses |
| **Social security contributions (arbetsgivaravgifter)** | 100% | 31.42% employer rate — Socialavgiftslagen 2000:980 §2:26 |
| **Rent (business premises)** | 100% | Must be for business use |
| **Insurance premiums** | 100% | Business-related risks |
| **Professional fees** | 100% | Legal, accounting, tax advisory |
| **Advertising & marketing** | 100% | Business purpose required |
| **Travel expenses** | 100% | Business travel; daily allowance rules apply |
| **Training expenses** | 100% | Job-related employee training |
| **Bad debts (realized)** | 100% | Must be definitively established as uncollectible — IL Ch.16 §7 |

### 2.2 Partially Deductible / Restricted

| Expense Category | Limit | Statutory Basis |
|---|---|---|
| **Meals & entertainment** | Deductible only if directly business-related; SEK 300 per person limit for simpler meals [unverified — confirm with local counsel] | IL Ch.16 §2 |
| **Gifts to employees** | SEK 500 per occasion (tax-free for recipient) | Skatteverket administrative guidance |
| **Company cars** | Benefit-in-kind calculated on employee; cost deductible for employer | IL Ch.11 §§1-4 |
| **Pension contributions** | Deductible within special rules — max 35% of salary or SEK 10 price base amounts | IL Ch.28 |
| **Representation** | Non-deductible for income tax purposes (VAT-deductible portion may differ) | IL Ch.16 §2 (as amended 2017) |

## 3. Interest & Financing Costs

### 3.1 General Deductibility — IL Ch.16 §1

Interest expenses are generally deductible if incurred for business purposes. However, Sweden has introduced strict interest limitation rules targeting intra-group debt.

### 3.2 EBITDA-Based Interest Limitation — IL Ch.24 §§21-29

| Parameter | Detail |
|---|---|
| **General rule** | Net interest expenses deductible up to 30% of tax EBITDA — IL Ch.24 §24 |
| **De minimis** | SEK 5M threshold — net interest expenses up to SEK 5M are always deductible — IL Ch.24 §23 |
| **Scope** | Applies to all net interest expenses (not limited to related-party) — IL Ch.24 §21 |
| **Carry-forward** | Non-deductible interest may be carried forward for up to 6 years — IL Ch.24 §28 |
| **Group ratio rule** | Alternative: deduction up to group's net interest/EBITDA ratio — IL Ch.24 §25 |
| **Election** | Taxpayer may choose between the 30% EBITDA rule and the group ratio rule annually — IL Ch.24 §29 |

### 3.3 Targeted Anti-Avoidance — IL Ch.24 §§10a-10f

| Parameter | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Hybrid mismatches** | Interest deductions denied for payments creating deduction/no-inclusion outcomes — IL Ch.24 §§10a-10f (implementing ATAD) |
| **Back-to-back loans** | Look-through rules apply to conduit arrangements |

## 4. Research & Development

Sweden provides an R&D wage tax reduction (employer social security contribution reduction) for companies conducting qualifying R&D activities. The reduction effectively lowers the cost of employing R&D personnel. Additionally, there is an enhanced deduction for R&D costs under IL Ch.16 §37a, allowing companies to deduct an additional percentage of qualifying R&D expenditures.

See [R&D Incentives](rnd_incentives.md) for full program detail.

## 5. Loss Carry-Forward & Carry-Back

### 5.1 Loss Carry-Forward — IL Ch.40

| Parameter | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Duration** | **Indefinite** carry-forward — IL Ch.40 §2 |
| **Annual cap** | **No cap** — 100% of taxable income may be offset |
| **Ownership change restriction** | Losses may be forfeited or restricted upon change of control (>50% ownership change) — IL Ch.40 §§10-15 |
| **Group contribution offset** | Losses in one group company can be offset via group contributions (koncernbidrag) — IL Ch.35 |
| **Merger rule** | Losses may transfer in qualifying mergers subject to limitations — IL Ch.40 §18 |

### 5.2 Loss Carry-Back

| Parameter | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Carry-back** | **Not permitted** under Swedish law |

### 5.3 Group Contributions (Koncernbidrag) — IL Ch.35

| Parameter | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Availability** | Between Swedish parent and ≥90%-owned Swedish subsidiaries — IL Ch.35 §2 |
| **Effect** | Deductible for payer, taxable for recipient |
| **Requirement** | Both entities must have been in the group for the entire fiscal year — IL Ch.35 §3 |
| **Cross-border** | Not available to foreign group entities (Swedish entities only) |

## 6. Provisions & Reserves

### 6.1 Tax Allocation Reserve (Periodiseringsfond) — IL Ch.30

| Parameter | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Maximum allocation** | 25% of taxable income per year — IL Ch.30 §5 |
| **Reversal** | Must be reversed within 6 years (FIFO basis) — IL Ch.30 §7 |
| **Standard income charge** | A notional income (schablonintäkt) of 100% of the government borrowing rate is charged annually on the reserve balance — IL Ch.30 §6a |
| **Purpose** | Tax deferral mechanism — effectively allows deferral of 25% of annual profits |

### 6.2 Bad Debt Provisions — IL Ch.16 §7

| Parameter | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Actual loss** | Fully deductible when loss is definitively established |
| **Provision** | Estimated bad debt provisions deductible if sufficiently supported (individual assessment) |
| **Factoring** | Losses on factored receivables follow the same rules |

### 6.3 Inventory Write-Down — IL Ch.17

| Parameter | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Lowest value principle** | Inventory valued at lower of cost or net realizable value — IL Ch.17 §3 |
| **Collective write-down** | 3% collective write-down allowed on total inventory value (the "97% rule") — IL Ch.17 §4 |
| **Obsolete inventory** | Additional write-down permitted for demonstrably obsolete items |

### 6.4 Warranty Provisions

| Parameter | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Deductibility** | Warranty provisions are deductible if based on historical experience and contractual obligations |
| **Documentation** | Must be supported by statistical data on warranty claims |

## 7. Industry-Specific Deductions

### 7.1 Forestry & Agriculture

| Deduction | Detail | Statutory Basis |
|---|---|---|
| **Forest account (skogskonto)** | Up to 60% (individual) or 40% (corporate) of forest income may be set aside — deferred taxation | IL Ch.21 §§21-40 |
| **Depletion of forest assets** | Deductions for the cost of harvested timber based on acquisition cost | IL Ch.21 |

### 7.2 Shipping

| Deduction | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Tonnage taxation** | Optional tonnage-based taxation regime for qualifying shipping companies — Tonnageskattelagen (2016:1067) |
| **Ring-fencing** | Income/losses from tonnage-taxed activities are ring-fenced from other income |

### 7.3 Real Estate

| Deduction | Detail |
|---|---|

## 8. Treaties & Transfer Pricing

### 8.1 Transfer Pricing — IL Ch.14 §19-20

| Parameter | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Arm's length principle** | Mandatory for all related-party transactions — IL Ch.14 §19 |
| **Documentation** | Contemporaneous documentation required for entities exceeding thresholds — Skatteförfarandelagen (SFL) Ch.39 §15-16 |
| **Country-by-Country Reporting** | Required for groups with consolidated revenue ≥ SEK 7Bn — SFL Ch.33a |
| **Master File / Local File** | Required following OECD BEPS Action 13 guidelines |
| **Penalties** | Tax surcharge (skattetillägg) of 40% on adjustments — SFL Ch.49 |

### 8.2 Accepted Methods

1. Comparable Uncontrolled Price (CUP)
2. Resale Price Method
3. Cost Plus Method
4. Transactional Net Margin Method (TNMM)
5. Profit Split Method

### 8.3 Tax Treaties

| Parameter | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Treaty network** | Sweden has 90+ double taxation treaties |
| **Model** | Primarily based on OECD Model Tax Convention |
| **MAP** | Mutual Agreement Procedure available for treaty disputes |
| **Arbitration** | Available under select treaties and the EU Arbitration Convention |

### 8.4 Withholding Tax (Outbound) — Kupongskattelagen

| Payment Type | Domestic Rate | Treaty-Reduced Rate |
|---|---|---|
| **Dividends** | 30% | Typically 0-15% |
| **Interest** | 0% | 0% (no WHT on interest under domestic law) |
| **Royalties** | 0% | 0% (no WHT on royalties under domestic law) |

## 9. Non-Deductible Items

### 9.1 Expressly Non-Deductible — IL Ch.9 §2; IL Ch.16

| Expense | Reason | Statutory Basis |
|---|---|---|
| **Fines and penalties** | Not incurred for business purposes | IL Ch.9 §2 |
| **Bribes and corrupt payments** | Prohibited by law | IL Ch.9 §2 |
| **Income taxes (Swedish and foreign)** | Not a deductible expense | IL Ch.9 §4 |
| **Dividends paid** | Distribution of profits, not an expense | IL Ch.24 |
| **Capital losses on substantial holdings (participation exemption)** | Non-deductible if related shares qualify for exemption | IL Ch.25a |
| **Representation costs** | Non-deductible for income tax since 2017 | IL Ch.16 §2 |
| **Private/personal expenses** | Not business-related | IL Ch.9 §2 |
| **Donations and gifts (non-business)** | Not incurred for income production | IL Ch.9 §2 |
| **Provisions for future obligations (general)** | Only specific, documented provisions allowed | IL Ch.16 |

### 9.2 Participation Exemption — IL Ch.25a

| Parameter | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Qualifying holdings** | Shares representing ≥10% of voting rights, held ≥1 year, in unlisted companies (or listed companies if business connection) — IL Ch.25a §§3-5 |
| **Effect** | Capital gains and dividends on qualifying shares are tax-exempt; losses are non-deductible |
