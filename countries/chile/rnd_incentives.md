# Chile - R&D Tax Incentives

## 1. Overview

| Parameter | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Headline Incentive** | R&D Tax Credit (*Incentivo Tributario a la I+D*) |
| **Mechanism Type** | Tax credit (against first-category income tax) and enhanced deduction |
| **Headline Rate** | 35% tax credit on qualifying R&D expenditures |
| **Refundable** | No — non-refundable credit against first-category income tax; carry-forward available (10 years) |
| **Patent Box** | No |
| **Legal Basis** | Law 20,241 (2008); modified by Law 20,570 (2012); regulated by Decree 68/2013 (Ministry of Economy); CORFO certification |

## 2. Detailed Programs

### 2.1 R&D Tax Credit (Law 20,241 as modified by Law 20,570)

| Parameter | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Legal Basis** | Law 20,241 (D.O. 19/01/2008); substantially modified by Law 20,570 (D.O. 06/03/2012); Decree 68/2013 (Ministry of Economy, Development and Tourism) |
| **Credit Rate** | 35% of qualifying R&D expenditures |
| **Deduction Component** | The remaining 65% of qualifying R&D expenditures is deductible as an ordinary business expense (i.e., 65% deduction against taxable income) |
| **Combined Treatment** | 35% direct tax credit + 65% deductible expense = 100% of R&D expenditure receives tax benefit |
| **Annual Cap** | No annual monetary cap on the credit amount (uncapped) |
| **Daily Rate Cap** | Intramural researcher costs: maximum 15 UTM per researcher per day (*Unidad Tributaria Mensual*) |
| **Intramural R&D** | R&D conducted within the company's own facilities using its own personnel |
| **Extramural R&D** | R&D contracted to CORFO-registered research centers, universities, or technology institutes |
| **Extramural Rate** | Same 35% credit rate applies to extramural R&D expenditures |
| **Eligible Taxpayers** | All first-category income tax payers (*contribuyentes de primera categoría*) conducting or contracting qualifying R&D in Chile |
| **Carry-Forward** | Unused credit may be carried forward for 10 years |
| **Carry-Back** | Not available |

### 2.2 Qualifying R&D Activities

| Parameter | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Definition** | Systematic and creative activities aimed at increasing knowledge, including basic research, applied research, and experimental development |
| **Frascati Manual Alignment** | Chile's definition aligns with the OECD Frascati Manual (2015) definition of R&D |
| **Qualifying Expenses — Intramural** | Salaries and benefits of R&D personnel, materials and supplies consumed in R&D, overhead directly attributable to R&D, depreciation of R&D equipment, R&D-related travel expenses |
| **Qualifying Expenses — Extramural** | Payments to CORFO-certified research centers, universities, and technological institutes for contracted R&D services |
| **Exclusions** | Routine engineering, market research, quality control, software customization (non-developmental), social sciences (unless integral to a qualifying project), mining exploration |

### 2.3 CORFO Certification Process

| Parameter | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Certifying Body** | Corporación de Fomento de la Producción (CORFO) — Chilean Economic Development Agency |
| **Certification Requirement** | All R&D projects must be certified by CORFO before the tax credit can be claimed |
| **Intramural Certification** | CORFO certifies the company's R&D project, including technical merit and expenditure eligibility |
| **Extramural Certification** | CORFO must register and certify the external research center or institution |
| **Evaluation Criteria** | Scientific/technological novelty, systematic methodology, presence of uncertainty, qualified personnel |
| **Certification Duration** | Project-specific; typically covers the project period (1–3 years) |
| **Appeal Mechanism** | Applicants may appeal CORFO decisions to the Ministry of Economy |

### 2.4 Additional Innovation Incentives

| Parameter | Detail |
|---|---|
| **CORFO Grants** | Various co-financing programs for innovation, technology transfer, and startup support (non-tax incentives) |
| **Start-Up Chile** | Government accelerator program offering equity-free funding (not a tax incentive) |
| **Free Trade Zone (Zona Franca)** | Reduced or zero customs duties in designated zones (Iquique, Punta Arenas); may benefit R&D-related imports |

## 3. Patent Box / IP Regime

**Not applicable.** Chile does not have a patent box or IP box regime. Income from intellectual property (patents, trademarks, copyrights) is taxed at the standard first-category income tax rate.

| Parameter | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Status** | No patent box regime |
| **Standard CIT Rate** | 27% (general regime — *régimen semi-integrado*) or 25% (*régimen Pro PyME* for SMEs) |
| **Withholding on Royalties** | 15% for payments to treaty-country residents; 30% for non-treaty (general rule under DL 824, Art. 59) |
| **Treaty Network** | Chile has 35+ tax treaties |
| **OECD BEPS Compliance** | Chile is an OECD member; adheres to BEPS Action 5 (harmful tax practices) |

## 4. Effective Benefit Analysis

**Assumptions:** CLP equivalent of US$1,000,000 R&D spend, first-category taxpayer under general regime (27% CIT rate), project certified by CORFO, profitable entity.

| Component | Calculation | Tax Benefit |
|---|---|---|
| **35% Tax Credit** | US$1,000,000 × 35% | **US$350,000** |
| **65% Deduction** | US$1,000,000 × 65% × 27% CIT rate | **US$175,500** |
| **Total Tax Benefit** | US$350,000 + US$175,500 | **US$525,500** |
| **Effective Benefit Rate** | US$525,500 ÷ US$1,000,000 | **52.6%** |

**Under Pro PyME Regime (25% CIT rate):**

| Component | Calculation | Tax Benefit |
|---|---|---|
| **35% Tax Credit** | US$1,000,000 × 35% | **US$350,000** |
| **65% Deduction** | US$1,000,000 × 65% × 25% | **US$162,500** |
| **Total Tax Benefit** | | **US$512,500** |
| **Effective Benefit Rate** | | **51.3%** |

> **Note:** Chile's R&D tax credit is among the most generous in Latin America. The absence of an annual monetary cap makes it particularly attractive for large R&D spenders. However, the mandatory CORFO certification process can add time and administrative burden.

## 5. Claim Process & Documentation

### Filing Process

| Step | Detail |
|---|---|
| **1. Design R&D Project** | Define project scope, objectives, methodology, timeline, and budget per Frascati Manual criteria |
| **2. Apply for CORFO Certification** | Submit project application to CORFO through the online portal (*Plataforma de Incentivo Tributario I+D*) |
| **3. CORFO Technical Evaluation** | CORFO evaluates the project using external peer reviewers; assess scientific merit and qualifying expenditures |
| **4. Obtain Certification** | Receive CORFO certification (*certificado de I+D*) confirming the project qualifies |
| **5. Execute R&D Project** | Conduct the R&D activities, tracking all expenditures per CORFO-approved budget |
| **6. CORFO Completion Report** | Submit project completion and expenditure report to CORFO for final certification of actual expenditures |
| **7. Claim Credit on Tax Return** | Apply the 35% credit and 65% deduction on the annual income tax return (Form 22) filed with SII (*Servicio de Impuestos Internos*) |

### Key Deadlines

| Item | Deadline |
|---|---|
| **CORFO Application** | Open enrollment (applications accepted on a rolling basis) |
| **CORFO Evaluation Period** | Approximately 40–60 business days |
| **Annual Tax Return (Form 22)** | April of the year following the fiscal year |
| **Completion Report to CORFO** | Within 60 days of project completion |
| **Pre-Approval** | **Mandatory** — CORFO certification required before claiming the credit |
| **Carry-Forward Period** | 10 years from the year the credit is generated |

### Documentation Requirements

- Detailed project proposal with scientific/technological objectives
- Budget breakdown aligned with qualifying expense categories
- Researcher CVs and qualifications
- Time allocation records for R&D personnel
- Financial records for all claimed expenditures (invoices, contracts, payroll records)
- CORFO certification documentation
- Project progress and completion reports
- Contracts with external research institutions (for extramural R&D)
- Annual tax return supporting schedules

## 6. Recent Changes & Sunset Provisions

| Change | Effective Date | Detail |
|---|---|---|
| **Original Law 20,241** | January 2008 | Established the R&D tax credit at 35% for extramural R&D only |
| **Law 20,570 Amendment** | March 2012 | Major expansion: extended credit to intramural R&D, removed annual cap, improved credit/deduction structure |
| **Decree 68/2013** | 2013 | Implementing regulations for the modified R&D credit |
| **Law 20,570 Made Permanent** | 2012 | The law as amended has no sunset provision (permanent incentive) |
| **CORFO Process Improvements** | 2020–ongoing | Digitalization of application process, reduced evaluation timelines |
| **Tax Reform (Law 21,210)** | February 2020 | Modernization of tax system; CIT rates confirmed at 27% (general) and 25% (SME); no changes to R&D credit |
| **Pending Proposals** | Under discussion | Potential expansion of qualifying activities and streamlining of CORFO process |
| **Sunset Provisions** | None | R&D tax credit is permanent legislation |
