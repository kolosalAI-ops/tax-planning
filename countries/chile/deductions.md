# Chile - Taxable Income Deductions

> **Jurisdiction**: National (SII — Servicio de Impuestos Internos)
> **Governing Law**: Ley sobre Impuesto a la Renta (LIR), Decreto Ley 824/1974, as amended
> **Tax Year**: 2024/2025
> **Currency**: CLP ($)

---

## 1. Depreciation & Capital Allowances

> **Legal Basis**: LIR Art. 31 N°5 (depreciation of fixed assets — normal and accelerated); SII useful life tables (Resolución Exenta)

### Standard (Normal) Depreciation

| Parameter | Value | Legal Reference |
|---|---|---|
| Method | **Straight-line** over SII-prescribed useful life | LIR Art. 31 N°5, first paragraph |
| Basis | Acquisition or construction cost, adjusted for inflation (CPI correction — corrección monetaria) | LIR Art. 41 |
| Residual value | Generally **1 peso** (nominal) | SII administrative practice |

### Standard Useful Life Tables (SII)

| Asset Category | Normal Useful Life | Annual Rate |
|---|---|---|
| Buildings (concrete/steel) | **40–80 years** | 1.25–2.5% |
| Buildings (wood/mixed) | **20–30 years** | 3.3–5% |
| Factory / industrial buildings | **20–40 years** | 2.5–5% |
| Heavy machinery | **15 years** | 6.67% |
| General machinery and equipment | **10–15 years** | 6.67–10% |
| Furniture and fixtures | **7–10 years** | 10–14.3% |
| Vehicles (passenger) | **7 years** | 14.3% |
| Trucks | **7 years** | 14.3% |
| Computers and IT equipment | **6 years** | 16.7% |
| Software | **3–6 years** | 16.7–33.3% |
| Tools | **3–5 years** | 20–33.3% |

### Accelerated Depreciation — LIR Art. 31 N°5 (second paragraph)

| Parameter | Value | Legal Reference |
|---|---|---|
| Method | Straight-line over **1/3 of normal useful life** (rounded down to nearest whole year) | LIR Art. 31 N°5, second paragraph |
| Minimum normal life to qualify | **3 years** or more | LIR Art. 31 N°5 |
| Eligible assets | **New or imported** fixed assets | LIR Art. 31 N°5 |
| Used assets | **Not eligible** for accelerated depreciation | |
| Effect on FCT vs. shareholders | Accelerated depreciation reduces FCT (First Category Tax) but excess depreciation added back for distributed profit (FUT/STUT) purposes | LIR Art. 31 N°5, final paragraph |

**Example**: Machinery with 15-year normal useful life → accelerated life = 15 / 3 = **5 years** (20% annual rate).

### Instantaneous Depreciation (expired)

| Parameter | Value |
|---|---|
| Status | Expired — was available for assets acquired through **May 2020** under Ley 21.210 |
| Benefit | 100% depreciation in year of acquisition for certain SME assets |

### Mining Royalty Impact on Depreciation — Ley 21.591

| Parameter | Value |
|---|---|
| Rule | For mining royalty tax base calculation, accelerated depreciation is **added back** and only normal depreciation is deductible |
| Effect | Taxpayers who elect accelerated depreciation for income tax purposes must adjust their mining royalty calculation |

## 2. Business Operating Expenses

> **Legal Basis**: LIR Art. 31 (list of deductible expenses — necessary to produce taxable income)

### General Deductibility — LIR Art. 31

| Parameter | Value |
|---|---|
| Standard | Expenses are deductible if they are **necessary to produce taxable income**, paid or owed during the relevant tax year, properly substantiated, and not classified as non-deductible |
| Documentation | Must be supported by invoices, receipts, contracts, or other lawful documentation |

### Deductible Expenses per LIR Art. 31

| Expense Type | Art. 31 Number | Deductibility |
|---|---|---|
| Interest on business debt | N°1 | **100%** deductible (subject to thin-cap rules) |
| Taxes (except income tax) | N°2 | **100%** deductible |
| Losses | N°3 | Carry-forward indefinitely (see Section 5) |
| Bad debts | N°4 | Deductible once established as uncollectable; reasonable collection efforts required; debts unpaid >365 days are deductible for non-related parties |
| Depreciation | N°5 | Normal or accelerated (see Section 1) |
| Employee salaries and wages | N°6 | **100%** deductible |
| Social security contributions (employer) | N°6 | **100%** deductible |
| Severance (indemnización por años de servicio) | N°6 bis | Deductible when provisioned annually (up to legal formula) |
| Donations (educational, cultural, social) | N°7 | Subject to specific limits per special laws (see below) |
| Rent and lease payments | N°8 [unverified — confirm with local counsel] | **100%** deductible if related to income-producing activity |
| Insurance premiums | N°8 [unverified — confirm with local counsel] | **100%** deductible if business-related |
| Royalties | N°9 | Deductible; subject to WHT on cross-border payments |
| Promotion and advertising | N°10 [unverified — confirm with local counsel] | **100%** deductible |
| Training expenses | N°12 | Deductible; also eligible for tax credit under Ley 19.518 (SENCE) |

### Charitable Donations

| Type | Limit | Legal Reference |
|---|---|---|
| Donations to educational institutions | Tax credit of **50%** of donation + deduction of remaining 50%; total limited to 5% of net taxable income or 4.5‰ of equity | Ley 19.247, Art. 3 |
| Social/public benefit donations | Tax credit of **50%** of donation; balance deductible | Ley 19.885 |
| Cultural donations (Ley Valdés) | Tax credit of **50%**; limited to 2% of net taxable income | Ley 18.985 |
| Global absolute limit | Total donations (all categories) may not exceed a combined limit | LIR Art. 31 N°7; various special laws |

## 3. Interest & Financing Costs

> **Legal Basis**: LIR Art. 31 N°1 (interest deduction); LIR Art. 41 F (thin capitalization — excess indebtedness)

### General Interest Deduction — LIR Art. 31 N°1

| Parameter | Value |
|---|---|
| Basic rule | Interest paid or accrued on debts related to income-producing activity is **100%** deductible |
| Arm's length | Interest rates must reflect market conditions for related-party debt |
| Inflation adjustment | Monetary correction on debt balances is deductible |

### Thin Capitalization — LIR Art. 41 F (Excess Indebtedness)

| Parameter | Value | Legal Reference |
|---|---|---|
| Debt-to-equity ratio | **3:1** (related-party cross-border debt to financial equity) | LIR Art. 41 F |
| Scope | Applies to interest, commissions, and other financial costs on debt with related parties abroad | LIR Art. 41 F |
| Excess interest treatment | Subject to **35% WHT** (instead of the reduced treaty/general rate) on the portion of interest attributable to excess debt | LIR Art. 41 F |
| Financial equity | Net equity per financial statements (adjusted for certain items) | LIR Art. 41 F |
| Exemption | Does not apply to debts with non-related foreign lenders | LIR Art. 41 F |

## 4. Research & Development

Chile provides an R&D tax incentive under Ley 20.241 (as amended by Ley 20.570), allowing a **35% tax credit** on eligible R&D project expenditures (capped at approximately 15,000 UTM per year) for projects certified by CORFO. The remaining 65% of the expenditure is deductible as a normal business expense.

See [R&D Incentives](rnd_incentives.md) for full program detail.

## 5. Loss Carry-Forward & Carry-Back

> **Legal Basis**: LIR Art. 31 N°3 (loss carry-forward — indefinite)

| Parameter | Value | Legal Reference |
|---|---|---|
| Carry-forward period | **Indefinite** | LIR Art. 31 N°3 |
| Carry-back | **Not allowed** | LIR (no carry-back provision) |
| Annual limitation | **100%** of taxable income (no percentage cap) | LIR Art. 31 N°3 |
| Inflation adjustment | Losses are adjusted by CPI (corrección monetaria) each year | LIR Art. 41 |
| Merger/absorption | Losses of absorbed company are **not** transferable to surviving entity | LIR Art. 31 N°3, final paragraph |
| Change of ownership | No specific loss restriction on change of ownership (unlike some jurisdictions) | |

## 6. Provisions & Reserves

> **Legal Basis**: LIR Art. 31 (specific deductible provisions); general principle — provisions are deductible only if specifically authorised

| Provision Type | Deductibility | Legal Reference |
|---|---|---|
| Bad debts (established as uncollectable) | **Deductible** — must demonstrate reasonable collection efforts | LIR Art. 31 N°4 |
| Bad debts (non-related party, unpaid >365 days) | **Deductible** | LIR Art. 31 N°4 |
| Severance provision (annual accrual) | **Deductible** (up to legal formula limits) | LIR Art. 31 N°6 bis |
| Vacation accruals | **Deductible** when accrued | LIR Art. 31 N°6 |
| General contingency reserves | **Not deductible** until liability is crystallised | |
| Warranty provisions | **Not deductible** until actual claim is paid | |
| Environmental remediation | **Not deductible** until payment | |

## 7. Industry-Specific Deductions

### Mining — Ley 21.591 (Mining Royalty, effective January 1, 2024)

| Parameter | Value | Legal Reference |
|---|---|---|
| Ad valorem component | **1%** on gross copper sales (for large mining operators, >50,000 MT annual production) | Ley 21.591 |
| Mining margin component | Marginal rates from **8% to 26%** based on operating margin | Ley 21.591 |
| Deductibility of royalty | The mining royalty is **deductible** as an expense for FCT purposes | Ley 21.591 |
| Exploration expenses | Deductible as incurred | LIR Art. 31 N°5; Código de Minería |
| Development costs | Capitalised and depreciated or amortised | |
| Total effective tax cap | Combined mining royalty + FCT capped at **46.5%** of operating income | Ley 21.591 |

### Real Estate

| Parameter | Value |
|---|---|
| Construction costs | Capitalised and depreciated (buildings per useful life tables) |
| Land | **Not depreciable** |
| Property tax (contribuciones de bienes raíces) | Deductible as operating expense; also available as credit against FCT (LIR Art. 20 N°1) |

### Agriculture — LIR Art. 20 N°1

| Parameter | Value |
|---|---|
| Presumptive income regime | Available for agricultural taxpayers below revenue threshold (10% of fiscal valuation) |
| Actual regime | Standard deduction rules apply |

## 8. Treaties & Transfer Pricing

### Transfer Pricing — LIR Art. 41 E

| Parameter | Value | Legal Reference |
|---|---|---|
| Standard | Arm's length principle | LIR Art. 41 E |
| Methods | CUP, RPM, CPM, TNMM, PSM | LIR Art. 41 E N°2 |
| Documentation | Transfer pricing study required for related-party cross-border transactions | LIR Art. 41 E |
| Sworn declaration (DJ 1907) | Annual filing for related-party transactions | SII Resolución |
| Country-by-Country reporting | Required for MNE groups with consolidated revenue ≥ €750M | LIR Art. 41 E, N°7 |
| Penalties | Adjustments plus fines; potential surcharges per Código Tributario | Código Tributario Art. 97 |

### Treaty Network

Chile has over 30 bilateral tax treaties in force (including with the US, Canada, Spain, UK, France, Germany, Australia, Japan, China, South Korea). Treaties generally follow the OECD model.

## 9. Non-Deductible Items

> **Legal Basis**: LIR Art. 31 (inverse — expenses not meeting deductibility requirements); LIR Art. 33 (adjustments to taxable income)

| Expense | Notes |
|---|---|
| Income tax (First Category Tax — FCT) | Not deductible per LIR Art. 33 N°1 |
| Expenses not necessary to produce taxable income | LIR Art. 31 (general principle) |
| Personal expenses of shareholders/partners | LIR Art. 33 N°1 |
| Fines and penalties (governmental) | Not deductible |
| Donations exceeding legal limits | LIR Art. 31 N°7; excess is non-deductible |
| Expenses related to exempt or non-taxable income | Not deductible — proportional allocation required |
| Excessive remuneration to related parties | LIR Art. 31 N°6 (must be arm's length) |
| Interest on excess debt (thin capitalization) | Subject to 35% WHT instead — LIR Art. 41 F |
| Entertainment not related to business | Not deductible |
| Land | Not depreciable/not deductible |
