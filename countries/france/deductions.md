# France - Taxable Income Deductions

> **Jurisdiction**: National (DGFiP — Direction Générale des Finances Publiques)
> **Governing Law**: Code Général des Impôts (CGI)
> **Tax Year**: 2024/2025
> **Currency**: EUR (€)

---

## 1. Depreciation & Capital Allowances

### General Principles — Art. 39 B CGI

France requires a minimum annuity depreciation (amortissement minimum) — taxpayers must record at least the straight-line depreciation amount each year; any shortfall is permanently lost (Art. 39 B CGI).

### Depreciation Methods

| Method | Availability | Statutory Basis |
|---|---|---|
| Straight-line (linéaire) | Default for all assets | Art. 39-1-2° CGI |
| Declining balance (dégressif) | Available for qualifying new assets with useful life ≥3 years | Art. 39 A CGI |
| Components approach (approche par composants) | Required for buildings and complex assets | Art. 237 septies CGI |

### Declining Balance Coefficients — Art. 39 A CGI

| Useful Life | Coefficient (× straight-line rate) |
|---|---|
| 3–4 years | **1.25** |
| 5–6 years | **1.75** |
| ≥7 years | **2.25** |

### Key Depreciation Rates

| Asset Category | Useful Life | Straight-Line Rate | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| Commercial buildings | 20–50 years | 2–5% | Components depreciated separately |
| Industrial buildings | 20 years | 5% | |
| Machinery and equipment | 5–10 years | 10–20% | Declining balance eligible |
| Office furniture | 10 years | 10% | |
| Vehicles | 4–5 years | 20–25% | Subject to amortisation cap (see §9) |
| Computer equipment | 3 years | 33.3% | Declining balance eligible |
| Software (acquired) | 12 months | **100%** immediate deduction option | Art. 236 CGI |
| Patents | 5 years (or useful life) | 20% | Art. 39-1-2° CGI |

### Exceptional Depreciation (Amortissement Exceptionnel)

| Category | Rate/Period | Statutory Basis |
|---|---|---|
| Energy-saving equipment | **100%** over 12 months | Art. 39 AB CGI [unverified — confirm with local counsel] |
| Electric/hydrogen vehicles | Accelerated allowance | Art. 39 AC CGI |
| Anti-pollution equipment | Accelerated over 12 months | Art. 39 quinquies E CGI |

## 2. Business Operating Expenses

### General Deductibility — Art. 39-1 CGI

Expenses are deductible if they are (i) incurred in the interest of the business, (ii) supported by documentation, (iii) result in a decrease in net assets, and (iv) included in the expenses of the relevant fiscal year.

### Key Deductible Operating Expenses

| Expense Category | Deductibility | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Employee salaries and wages | Fully deductible | Must be arm's-length; excessive remuneration reclassified as hidden distribution |
| Employer social charges | Fully deductible | CSG, CRDS employer portion |
| Rent and lease payments | Fully deductible | Arm's-length for related parties |
| Professional fees | Fully deductible | |
| Repairs and maintenance | Deductible if revenue nature | Capital improvements must be depreciated |
| Bad debts | Deductible when specifically provided for | Art. 39-1-5° CGI |
| Insurance premiums | Fully deductible | |
| Training expenses (formation professionnelle) | Fully deductible | |
| Pre-incorporation expenses | Deductible if assumed by the company within first fiscal year | Art. 210 A CGI context |

### Charitable Donations — Art. 238 bis CGI

| Parameter | Value |
|---|---|
| Deduction rate | **60%** tax reduction on donations up to **€2,000,000** |
| Rate above €2M | **40%** tax reduction |
| Cap | 0.5% of revenue (chiffre d'affaires HT) per year |
| Carry-forward of excess | **5 years** |
| Food aid donations | **60%** on first €2M (additional carve-out — Art. 238 bis-1 CGI) |

## 3. Interest & Financing Costs

### General Interest Deduction — Art. 39-1-3° CGI

Interest on business borrowings is generally deductible. However, several limitations apply.

### Thin Capitalisation Rules — Art. 212 bis CGI

Three cumulative conditions; if any one is breached, excess interest on related-party debt is limited:

| Condition | Threshold |
|---|---|
| **1. Debt-to-equity** | Related-party debt > **1.5×** equity |
| **2. Interest coverage** | Net interest paid to related parties > **10%** of EBITDA (reducing to **30%** by ATAD alignment) |
| **3. Arm's-length** | Interest rate exceeds arm's-length rate |

### ATAD Interest Limitation — Art. 212 bis I bis CGI (from 2019)

| Parameter | Value |
|---|---|
| Deductible limit | Net interest expense up to **30%** of tax-EBITDA |
| De minimis | **€3,000,000** net interest expense |
| Group ratio rule | Alternative based on group external interest/EBITDA ratio |
| Carry-forward | Disallowed interest carried forward indefinitely |
| EBITDA carry-forward | Unused EBITDA capacity carried forward **5 years** |

### Shareholder Loan Interest — Art. 39-1-3° CGI / Art. 212 CGI

| Parameter | Value |
|---|---|
| Maximum deductible rate | Annual average rate of bank loans (taux effectif moyen pratiqué — TEMP) published by DGFiP |
| 2024 rate | Published annually (approximately 5.57% for 2023) [unverified — confirm with local counsel] |
| Capital fully paid up | Required for interest deduction on shareholder advances |

## 4. Research & Development

France operates the Crédit d'Impôt Recherche (CIR), one of Europe's most generous R&D tax credit schemes. The CIR provides a volume-based tax credit on qualifying R&D expenditure, and is complemented by the Crédit d'Impôt Innovation (CII) for SMEs on innovation expenses beyond pure R&D.

See [R&D Incentives](rnd_incentives.md) for full program detail.

## 5. Loss Carry-Forward & Carry-Back

### Loss Carry-Forward — Art. 209-I CGI

| Parameter | Value |
|---|---|
| Duration | **Indefinite** |
| Restriction | Losses offset freely up to **€1,000,000** per year; above that, only **50%** of remaining taxable profit may be offset |
| Change of activity | Losses forfeited if substantial change in activity (Art. 221-5 CGI) unless ministerial approval obtained |

### Loss Carry-Back (Report en Arrière) — Art. 220 quinquies CGI

| Parameter | Value |
|---|---|
| Duration | **1 year** |
| Maximum | **€1,000,000** |
| Result | Creates a tax receivable (créance sur le Trésor) usable against future CIT or refunded after 5 years |
| Limitations | Cannot carry back against profits distributed as dividends or qualifying for reduced rates |

## 6. Provisions & Reserves

### Tax-Deductible Provisions — Art. 39-1-5° CGI

| Provision Type | Deductibility | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Specific bad debt provisions | Deductible | Must relate to identified risk |
| General bad debt provisions | **Not deductible** | Too uncertain |
| Warranty provisions | Deductible | If based on reliable historical data |
| Litigation provisions | Deductible | If proceedings initiated or probable |
| Provisions for price increases (PHA) | Deductible under specific conditions | Art. 39 bis CGI (press sector) |
| Pension commitments (provision for retirement indemnities — IDR) | Deductible if actual obligation under collective agreement | Art. 39-1-5° CGI |
| Environmental provisions (site restoration) | Deductible | If statutory obligation exists |
| Restructuring provisions | Generally **not deductible** until costs actually incurred | |

### Regulated Provisions (Provisions Réglementées)

| Provision | Detail |
|---|---|
| Provision for price fluctuations | Art. 39-1-5° bis CGI — for inventory price increases exceeding 10% |
| Provision for investments (historical) | Largely abolished; limited surviving categories |
| Amortissement dérogatoire | Excess depreciation over straight-line recorded as regulated provision |

## 8. Treaties & Transfer Pricing

### Transfer Pricing — Art. 57 CGI / Art. L13 AA–L13 AB LPF

| Parameter | Detail |
|---|---|
| Standard | Arm's-length principle — Art. 57 CGI |
| Documentation | Master file and local file required for entities meeting thresholds — Art. L13 AA LPF |
| Thresholds | Revenue or assets ≥ €400M, or transactions with related parties ≥ €50M, or controlled by entity meeting these thresholds |
| Country-by-Country Reporting | Required for MNE groups with consolidated revenue ≥ €750M — Art. 223 quinquies C CGI |
| Penalties | 5% of adjustments (minimum €10,000) for documentation failures — Art. 1735 ter CGI |
| APAs | Available (bilateral, multilateral) — Art. L80 B-7° LPF |

### Participation Exemption — Art. 216 / Art. 145 CGI

| Parameter | Value |
|---|---|
| Exemption | 95% of dividends from qualifying subsidiaries exempt |
| Quote-part (add-back) | **5%** of gross dividends added back as non-deductible expenses |
| Minimum holding | **5%** of capital for at least **2 years** |
| Capital gains on qualifying participations | **88%** exempt (12% taxed — Art. 219-I-a quinquies CGI) |

### Treaty Network

France has over **120** double taxation agreements.

| Income Type | Typical Treaty Rate |
|---|---|
| Dividends (substantial holding) | 0–5% |
| Dividends (portfolio) | 15% |
| Interest | 0–10% |
| Royalties | 0–5% |

## 9. Non-Deductible Items

| Expense | Statutory Reference |
|---|---|
| Corporate income tax (IS) | Not deductible |
| Vehicle depreciation exceeding caps | Art. 39-4 CGI — cap €18,300 for CO₂ >130g/km; €30,000 for CO₂ ≤20g/km; €20,300 for 20–50g/km; €9,900 for >160g/km [unverified — confirm with local counsel] |
| Lease payments on vehicles exceeding cap | Art. 39-4 CGI — same caps applied to excess lease portion |
| Hunting, fishing, yachts, pleasure boats | **Fully non-deductible** — Art. 39-4 CGI |
| Luxury residences (châteaux) | **Fully non-deductible** — Art. 39-4 CGI |
| Fines and penalties | **Non-deductible** — Art. 39-2 CGI |
| Bribes | **Non-deductible** — Art. 39-2 bis CGI |
| Excessive remuneration | Non-deductible portion reclassified as hidden distribution — Art. 39-1-1° CGI |
| Dividends paid | Not an expense |
| Contribution sociale de solidarité (C3S) | Deductible (unlike IS itself) |
| Non-arm's-length management fees | Non-deductible to extent of excess — Art. 57 CGI |
| Capital expenditure | Must be depreciated |
| Tax on company vehicles (TVS) | **Deductible** from 2024 (Taxe sur les Émissions de CO₂ + Taxe sur l'Ancienneté) [unverified — confirm with local counsel] |
