# France - R&D Tax Incentives

## 1. Overview

| Feature | Details |
|---|---|
| **Headline Incentive** | Crédit d'Impôt Recherche (CIR) — 30% R&D Tax Credit |
| **Type** | Direct tax credit against corporate income tax (IS) |
| **Rate** | 30% on first €100M of qualifying R&D expenditure; 5% above €100M |
| **Refundable** | Yes — after 3 years of carry-forward; immediate for SMEs, JEIs, and new companies |
| **Patent Box** | Yes — 10% effective rate on qualifying IP income |
| **Legal Basis** | Code Général des Impôts (CGI) Art. 244 quater B (CIR); CGI Art. 238 (Patent Box); CGI Art. 244 quater B II (CII) |

---

## 2. Detailed Programs

### 2.1 Crédit d'Impôt Recherche (CIR)

**Legal Basis:** CGI Art. 244 quater B, introduced 1983, substantially reformed 2008.

The CIR is one of the most generous and well-established R&D tax incentive programs globally.

| Parameter | Value |
|---|---|
| Credit Rate — First €100M | 30% of qualifying R&D expenditure |
| Credit Rate — Above €100M | 5% of qualifying R&D expenditure |
| Carry-Forward | 3 years; excess refunded after 3 years if not fully utilized |
| Immediate Refund Eligibility | SMEs (EU definition), JEIs, new companies (<5 years), companies in difficulty, JECA |
| Qualifying Activities | Basic research, applied research, experimental development (per Frascati Manual) |
| Subcontracted R&D | Eligible; payments to approved public bodies (organismes agréés) at 100%; private contractors at 100% (cap €2M per contractor if unrelated; €10M total) |
| Double Counting Prevention | If contractor claims CIR, the commissioner's base is reduced accordingly |

**Qualifying Expenditure Categories:**

| Category | Treatment |
|---|---|
| R&D personnel costs | Actual salary costs + mandatory social charges; flat-rate 43% overhead option on personnel costs abolished — now actual costs |
| Operating expenditure (forfait) | Flat-rate 75% of depreciation on R&D equipment + 43% of eligible personnel costs (replaced by actual costs method in recent reforms) |
| Depreciation of R&D equipment | Depreciation charges on assets used for R&D |
| Patent costs | Filing, maintenance, and defense of patents linked to R&D |
| Technology watch (veille technologique) | Capped at €60,000 per year |
| Standardization costs | 50% of participation in standardization activities |
| Subcontracted R&D | As above; must be to approved (agréé) organizations |
| Young doctorate researchers | 200% of salary costs for first 24 months of first CDI contract (doubled for CIR base) |

### 2.2 Crédit d'Impôt Innovation (CII)

**Legal Basis:** CGI Art. 244 quater B II (innovation component).

| Parameter | Value |
|---|---|
| Eligible Companies | SMEs only (EU definition: <250 employees, <€50M turnover or <€43M total assets) |
| Credit Rate | 30% (increased from 20% for 2023+) |
| Qualifying Expenditure Cap | €400,000 per year |
| Maximum Credit | €120,000 per year (30% × €400,000) |
| Qualifying Activities | Design of prototypes or pilot installations of new products (beyond R&D; innovation activities) |
| "New Product" Definition | Superior performance in technical characteristics, eco-design, ergonomics, or functionality compared to existing market offerings |
| Refundable | Yes, for SMEs (immediate) |
| Sunset | Extended through 2024; renewal subject to Finance Act provisions |

### 2.3 Jeune Entreprise Innovante (JEI) Status

**Legal Basis:** CGI Art. 44 sexies-0 A; Code de la Sécurité Sociale Art. L. 131-4-2.

| Parameter | Value |
|---|---|
| Eligibility — Age | Less than 11 years old (extended from 8 years) |
| Eligibility — Size | SME (EU definition) |
| Eligibility — R&D Intensity | R&D expenditure ≥15% of total charges (fiscally deductible expenses) |
| Eligibility — Independence | ≥50% held by natural persons, other JEIs, public research institutions, or certain funds |
| Corporate Tax Exemption | 100% exemption for first profitable fiscal year; 50% for the second |
| Social Charges Exemption | Partial exemption from employer social security contributions on R&D personnel, up to €231,840 per employee per year (2024) |
| CFE/CVAE Exemption | 7-year exemption from local business taxes (on deliberation by local authorities) |
| Duration | Benefits apply for the duration of JEI eligibility (up to 11 years) |

### 2.4 Rescrit CIR (Advance Ruling)

**Legal Basis:** Livre des Procédures Fiscales (LPF) Art. L. 80 B 3° and 3° bis.

| Parameter | Value |
|---|---|
| Purpose | Obtain advance confirmation from tax authorities that planned R&D activities qualify for CIR |
| Filing | Submit rescrit request to the Direction Générale des Finances Publiques (DGFiP) or to the Ministère de l'Enseignement Supérieur et de la Recherche (MESR) |
| Response Time | 3 months (DGFiP) or 4 months (MESR); silence = acceptance |
| Binding Effect | Tax authority is bound by its favorable ruling for the specified activities and period |
| Recommended For | Complex or novel R&D activities; first-time claimants; activities with uncertain qualification |

## 3. Patent Box / IP Regime

**Legal Basis:** CGI Art. 238, as reformed by Finance Act 2019 (nexus approach, OECD BEPS Action 5 compliant).

| Parameter | Value |
|---|---|
| Effective Tax Rate | 10% on qualifying IP income (vs. standard 25% IS rate) |
| Tax Saving | 15 percentage points (25% – 10%) |
| Qualifying IP | Patents, patentable inventions (certificats d'utilité), plant variety certificates, certain software copyrights |
| Nexus Ratio | Qualifying R&D expenditure / Total R&D expenditure (including acquisitions, related-party R&D) × 1.3 (30% uplift) |
| Qualifying Income | Royalties, license fees, sales of qualifying IP, embedded IP income, compensation for infringement |
| Regime Application | Optional; election made per qualifying IP asset or group |
| Net Income Calculation | Qualifying IP income minus directly attributable costs |
| Documentation | Must track R&D expenditure per IP asset; maintain nexus ratio calculation |

**Interaction with CIR:**
- CIR can be claimed on R&D expenditure that generates IP qualifying for the patent box
- However, the CIR credit reduces the qualifying expenditure base for the nexus ratio calculation
- Both incentives are available simultaneously, but combined benefit is moderated

## 4. Effective Benefit Analysis (per $1M USD R&D Spend)

*Assumes $1M USD ≈ €920,000 (illustrative rate); standard IS rate 25%.*

| Scenario | Calculation | Net Benefit |
|---|---|---|
| **CIR — Profitable company** | 30% × €920,000 = €276,000 credit (offsets IS liability) | €276,000 |
| **CIR — Loss-making SME** | Same credit, immediately refundable | €276,000 cash |
| **CIR — Loss-making large co.** | Carry-forward 3 years, then refunded | €276,000 (deferred) |
| **CII — SME (innovation)** | 30% × €400,000 cap = €120,000 | €120,000 |
| **CIR + CII combined (SME)** | €276,000 + €120,000 = €396,000 (if both apply) | €396,000 |
| **JEI — Social charges saving** | ~€50,000–€100,000 annually (depending on R&D headcount) | Additional savings |
| **Young doctorate (first 24 months)** | 200% × salary in CIR base → additional ~€30,000–€60,000 credit | Additional credit |
| **Patent Box (on €920k IP income)** | 15% saving × €920,000 = €138,000 | €138,000 |
| **Full stack: CIR + JEI + Patent Box** | Up to ~€500,000+ combined | ~€500,000+ |

## 5. Claim Process & Documentation

### 5.1 CIR Filing Process

| Step | Action | Deadline |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Identify qualifying R&D projects and expenditure | During fiscal year |
| 2 | Prepare Dossier Justificatif (technical documentation) | Maintain contemporaneously |
| 3 | Complete Formulaire 2069-A-SD (CIR declaration) | With annual tax return (within 3 months of fiscal year end) |
| 4 | File CIR claim with corporate IS return | Same deadline as IS return |
| 5 | If refund sought (SME): file Formulaire 2573-SD | After filing IS return |
| 6 | Receive credit offset or cash refund | Typically within 3–6 months for SME refunds |

### 5.2 Dossier Justificatif (Technical File)

The dossier justificatif is the cornerstone of CIR compliance and must be prepared for each R&D project:

| Section | Content |
|---|---|
| State of the Art | Description of existing scientific/technical knowledge at project start |
| Scientific/Technical Objective | The advance sought beyond the state of the art |
| Uncertainties | Specific scientific or technical uncertainties that could not be resolved by a competent professional |
| R&D Work Performed | Detailed description of experimental and analytical work |
| Results | Outcomes, including failures and partial successes |
| Personnel Involved | Named researchers, qualifications, time allocation |
| Financial Summary | Expenditure breakdown by category, tied to the project |

### 5.3 Audit & Controls

- MESR (Ministry of Research) experts conduct technical audits on behalf of tax authorities
- Tax audit prescription period: 3 years (general) or 10 years (fraud)
- Common audit triggers: first-time claims, large claims, high CIR-to-revenue ratios, subcontracting-heavy claims
- Rescrit CIR provides legal certainty if obtained in advance
- Expert contradictoire procedure available if technical disagreement during audit

## 6. Recent Changes & Sunset Provisions

| Change | Effective Date | Details |
|---|---|---|
| CII rate increased to 30% | 2023 | Up from 20% |
| Patent Box reformed (nexus approach) | 2019 | OECD BEPS Action 5 compliance; 10% rate |
| JEI age limit extended to 11 years | 2024 | Up from 8 years |
| Flat-rate overhead calculation reformed | 2023 | Simplified overhead allocation |
| Operating expenditure forfait adjusted | Ongoing | Trend toward actual costs |
| CIR rate unchanged at 30%/5% | Stable since 2008 | No changes anticipated |
| IS rate reduced to 25% | 2022 | Full effect; enhances relative value of CIR |
| Digital filing requirements | 2024 | Increasing digitization of claims and documentation |
| CII extended | 2024 | Subject to Finance Act; check annual renewal |

**Sunset Provisions:**
- The **CIR has no sunset clause**; it is a permanent feature of French tax law
- The **CII** has historically required periodic renewal in Finance Acts; check latest PLF
- **JEI** status benefits are subject to time limits (age of company) but the regime itself is permanent
- **Patent Box** is permanent following 2019 reform
