# Philippines - Taxable Income Deductions

> **Jurisdiction**: National (Bureau of Internal Revenue — BIR)
> **Governing Law**: National Internal Revenue Code (NIRC), as amended by the CREATE Act (Republic Act No. 11534) and CREATE MORE Act (Republic Act No. 12066)
> **Tax Year**: 2024/2025
> **Currency**: PHP (₱)

---

## 1. Depreciation & Capital Allowances

### 1.1 General Depreciation Rules

> **Legal Basis**: NIRC §34(F)

The Philippines allows several depreciation methods. There is no official government-published asset-life table; useful lives are based on reasonable estimates consistent with industry practice.

#### Methods Available

| Method | Description |
|---|---|
| **Straight-Line** | Equal annual deductions over useful life |
| **Declining Balance** | Accelerated; applied to net book value |
| **Sum-of-Years-Digits** | Accelerated; based on remaining life fraction |
| **Units of Production** | Output-based |

#### Common Useful Lives (Industry Practice)

| Asset Category | Typical Useful Life | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| **Buildings (reinforced concrete)** | **20–40 years** | No official table; BIR accepts reasonable estimates |
| **Buildings (wooden / semi-permanent)** | **10–20 years** | |
| **Machinery & equipment** | **5–15 years** | Industry-dependent |
| **Motor vehicles** | **5 years** | |
| **Computers & IT equipment** | **3–5 years** | |
| **Office furniture & fixtures** | **5–10 years** | |
| **Software** | **3–5 years** | |
| **Leasehold improvements** | Shorter of useful life or lease term | |

### 1.2 Depreciation of Revalued Assets

Depreciation based on the appraised/revalued amount is not deductible for tax purposes. Tax depreciation is based on historical cost only.

## 2. Business Operating Expenses

### 2.1 General Deductions

> **Legal Basis**: NIRC §34(A)–(L)

| Expense Category | Deductibility | Legal Basis |
|---|---|---|
| Salaries, wages, bonuses | **100%** | NIRC §34(A)(1)(a) — ordinary and necessary |
| SSS / PhilHealth / Pag-IBIG (employer share) | **100%** | Statutory contributions |
| 13th-month pay and mandatory benefits | **100%** | Labour Code mandated |
| Rent and lease payments | **100%** | NIRC §34(A)(1)(a) |
| Repairs and maintenance | **100%** | Revenue nature |
| Insurance premiums | **100%** | NIRC §34(A)(1)(b) — business-related |
| Travel expenses (business) | **100%** | Must be ordinary and necessary |
| Advertising and marketing | **100%** | |
| Legal and professional fees | **100%** | Revenue nature |
| Taxes paid (business taxes, real property tax, etc.) | **100%** | NIRC §34(C)(1) — except income tax and estate tax |

### 2.2 Bad Debts

> **Legal Basis**: NIRC §34(E)

| Parameter | Value |
|---|---|
| Deductible | Debts ascertained to be worthless and actually charged off within the taxable year |
| Conditions | Must have been previously included in income; taxpayer must show debt is uncollectible |
| Recovery | If subsequently recovered, taxable as income |

### 2.3 Charitable Contributions

> **Legal Basis**: NIRC §34(H)

| Donation Type | Limit |
|---|---|
| Government, accredited NGOs (priority activities) | **100%** deductible — NIRC §34(H)(2) |
| Other accredited donee institutions | **5%** of taxable income (individuals: 10%) — NIRC §34(H)(1) |
| Non-accredited donees | **Non-deductible** |

### 2.4 Optional Standard Deduction (OSD)

> **Legal Basis**: NIRC §34(L)

| Parameter | Value |
|---|---|
| Rate | **40%** of gross income (for corporations) |
| Election | Irrevocable for the taxable year; signified in the return |
| Effect | In lieu of itemised deductions (Sections 34(A)–(K)) |
| NOLCO | Not available if OSD elected |

## 3. Interest & Financing Costs

### 3.1 General Deductibility

> **Legal Basis**: NIRC §34(B)

Interest on business indebtedness is deductible, subject to the reduction rule for interest income earned.

### 3.2 Interest Reduction Rule

> **Legal Basis**: NIRC §34(B)(1)

| Parameter | Value |
|---|---|
| Reduction | Deductible interest expense reduced by **20%** of interest income subjected to final withholding tax (CREATE MORE Act; previously 33% under CREATE Act) [unverified — confirm implementing Revenue Regulations are in force] |
| Purpose | Prevent double benefit from tax-free interest income and interest expense deduction |

### 3.3 Thin Capitalisation

| Parameter | Value |
|---|---|
| Statutory thin-cap rules | **None** — the Philippines has no formal thin capitalisation statute |
| BIR approach | Related-party interest tested under arm's length / transfer pricing rules |
| Excessive interest | BIR may recharacterise as dividends if debt is in substance equity |

### 3.4 Related-Party Interest

Transfer pricing rules (RR No. 2-2013; CREATE MORE enhanced provisions) apply. Interest rates must be at arm's length; BIR may adjust.

## 4. Research & Development

The Philippines offers enhanced R&D deductions under the CREATE MORE Act (RA 12066). Qualifying R&D expenditure may receive enhanced deductions of up to 200% for micro and small enterprises. The R&D programme is administered by the Department of Science and Technology (DOST) and the Department of Trade and Industry (DTI).

See [R&D Incentives](rnd_incentives.md) for full program detail.

## 5. Loss Carry-Forward & Carry-Back

### 5.1 Net Operating Loss Carry-Over (NOLCO)

> **Legal Basis**: NIRC §34(D)(3)

| Parameter | Value |
|---|---|
| Carry-forward period | **3 years** (standard) |
| Extended carry-forward | **5 years** for losses incurred in 2020 and 2021 (CREATE Act COVID provision) |
| Carry-back | **Not allowed** |
| Utilisation | Against taxable income in subsequent years |
| OSD | NOLCO not available if Optional Standard Deduction is elected |
| PEZA / BOI registered | NOLCO may be limited during incentive periods |

### 5.2 Capital Losses

> **Legal Basis**: NIRC §39

| Parameter | Value |
|---|---|
| Deductibility | Capital losses deductible only against capital gains |
| Carry-forward | **Not permitted** — net capital loss must be absorbed in the same year against capital gains |
| Exception | Securities dealer losses treated as ordinary losses |

## 6. Provisions & Reserves

| Provision Type | Deductibility | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Specific bad debts written off | **Deductible** | NIRC §34(E) — must be worthless and charged off |
| General bad debt provisions | **Not deductible** | Only actual write-offs |
| Employee benefit accruals | **Deductible** when paid | Unless specifically allowed |
| Warranty provisions | **Not deductible** | Until actual claim |
| General reserves | **Not deductible** | |
| Insurance reserves | **Deductible** for licensed insurers | Per Insurance Commission regulations |

## 7. Industry-Specific Deductions

### 7.1 PEZA-Registered Enterprises

> **Legal Basis**: Republic Act No. 7916 (Special Economic Zone Act); CREATE Act; CREATE MORE Act

| Parameter | Value |
|---|---|
| Income tax holiday (ITH) | **4–7 years** depending on industry and location |
| Post-ITH rate | **5%** Special Corporate Income Tax (SCIT) on gross income earned (in lieu of all national and local taxes) |
| Enhanced deductions (CREATE MORE) | Additional deductions during ITH and post-ITH periods (training, infrastructure, R&D) |

### 7.2 BOI-Registered Enterprises

> **Legal Basis**: Omnibus Investments Code; CREATE Act

| Parameter | Value |
|---|---|
| ITH | **4–7 years** |
| Post-ITH | Enhanced deductions (up to 200% for qualifying expenses) |
| Net Operating Loss Carry-Over | 5 years during ITH period |

### 7.3 Minimum Corporate Income Tax (MCIT)

> **Legal Basis**: NIRC §27(E); CREATE Act

| Parameter | Value |
|---|---|
| MCIT rate | **1%** of gross income (CREATE Act; was 2% pre-CREATE) |
| Application | Payable when MCIT exceeds regular CIT (25% of taxable income) |
| Starts | Beginning 4th taxable year of operations |
| Excess MCIT credit | Carried forward and credited against normal CIT for **3 succeeding years** |

## 8. Treaties & Transfer Pricing

### 8.1 Transfer Pricing

> **Legal Basis**: NIRC §50; RR No. 2-2013; CREATE MORE Act provisions

| Parameter | Value |
|---|---|
| Arm's length standard | Mandatory for all related-party transactions — NIRC §50 |
| Methods | CUP, resale price, cost plus, TNMM, profit split, comparable profit method |
| Documentation | TP documentation required; three-tier approach (Master File, Local File, CbC) for large MNEs |
| CbC reporting | Required for Philippine parent entities of MNE groups with consolidated revenue ≥ PHP 75 billion |
| Penalties | Deficiency assessments; 25%–50% surcharge; 12% annual interest |
| APA | Formal APA programme expected under CREATE MORE; bilateral APAs through treaty MAP |

### 8.2 Treaty Network

The Philippines has over 40 double tax agreements.

## 9. Non-Deductible Items

> **Legal Basis**: NIRC §36

| Expense | Legal Basis |
|---|---|
| Personal, living, or family expenses | NIRC §36(A)(1) |
| Amounts paid for new buildings, permanent improvements (capital) | NIRC §36(A)(2) — unless depreciated |
| Premiums on life insurance of officers/employees where company is beneficiary | NIRC §36(A)(4) |
| Losses from sales between related parties | NIRC §36(B) |
| Income tax | NIRC §34(C)(1) — specifically excluded |
| Estate and donor's tax | NIRC §34(C)(1) |
| Fines, penalties, and surcharges | Not deductible — contrary to public policy |
| Bribes and illegal payments | Not deductible |
| Capital losses beyond capital gains | NIRC §39 |
| Non-business expenses | NIRC §34(A) — must be ordinary and necessary |
| Lobbying / political campaign expenses | Not ordinary and necessary |
