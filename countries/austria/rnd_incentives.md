# Austria - R&D Tax Incentives

## 1. Overview

| Feature | Details |
|---|---|
| **Headline Incentive** | Forschungsprämie (Research Premium) — 14% tax credit |
| **Type** | Direct refundable tax premium (credit against assessed tax; excess refunded) |
| **Rate** | 14% of qualifying R&D expenditure |
| **Refundable** | Yes — fully refundable; no cap on qualifying expenditure |
| **Patent Box** | No — Austria does not operate a patent box or IP box regime |
| **Legal Basis** | Einkommensteuergesetz (EStG) §108c; Körperschaftsteuergesetz (KStG); Forschungsprämienverordnung |

---

## 2. Detailed Programs

### 2.1 Forschungsprämie (Research Premium)

**Legal Basis:** EStG §108c, as amended. Rate increased to 14% by Steuerreformgesetz 2020 (effective 2018 assessment year onward, initially at 12%, increased to 14% from 2018).

| Parameter | Value |
|---|---|
| Premium Rate | 14% of qualifying R&D expenditure |
| Cap | **No cap** — unlimited qualifying expenditure base |
| Refundable | Yes — fully refundable; premium applied against assessed income/corporate tax; excess refunded in cash |
| Eligible Entities | All income tax (ESt) and corporate income tax (KSt) payers conducting R&D in Austria |
| In-House R&D | Qualifying expenditure on R&D activities performed by the taxpayer's own employees |
| Contract R&D | Qualifying expenditure on R&D commissioned to third parties (limited provisions — see below) |
| Assessment Basis | Actual R&D expenditure (personnel costs, material costs, overhead, depreciation of R&D assets) |
| Taxability of Premium | The Forschungsprämie itself is **not taxable** — it is a tax-free government premium |

**Qualifying R&D Activities (per EStG §108c and Frascati Manual):**

| Category | Eligible |
|---|---|
| Basic research (Grundlagenforschung) | Yes |
| Applied research (Angewandte Forschung) | Yes |
| Experimental development (Experimentelle Entwicklung) | Yes |
| Systematic investigation | Must involve systematic, creative work to increase knowledge |
| Novelty requirement | Must aim to develop new knowledge, products, processes, or services |
| Uncertainty | Must involve scientific/technical uncertainty |

**Excluded Activities:**

| Activity | Status |
|---|---|
| Routine testing and analysis | Not eligible |
| Quality control | Not eligible |
| Market research | Not eligible |
| Routine software development | Not eligible |
| Prospecting and exploration | Not eligible |
| Social science research | Not eligible (for Forschungsprämie; may qualify for other programs) |

### 2.2 Qualifying Expenditure Categories

| Category | Details | Eligible |
|---|---|---|
| R&D personnel costs | Gross salaries, wages, employer social security contributions for R&D employees | Yes — pro-rata for time spent on R&D |
| Material costs | Consumables, raw materials, prototyping materials used in R&D | Yes |
| Overhead allocation | General overhead attributable to R&D activities | Yes — reasonable allocation method required |
| Depreciation of R&D assets | Depreciation charges on equipment, instruments, and facilities used for R&D | Yes |
| External R&D services | Payments to contractors, consultants, or research institutions for R&D services | Yes — subject to contract R&D rules |
| Patent costs | Filing, prosecution, and maintenance of patents arising from R&D | Yes |
| Software and licenses | Software licenses used in R&D activities | Yes |

### 2.3 Contract R&D (Auftragsforschung)

| Parameter | Value |
|---|---|
| Eligibility | R&D commissioned by the taxpayer to third parties |
| Qualifying Base | Actual payments for contract R&D services |
| Cap on Contract R&D | €1,000,000 per fiscal year (for contract R&D specifically) |
| Contractor Requirements | Contractor must be identified; R&D nature of activities must be documented |
| Location | Contract R&D can be performed domestically or internationally |
| Double Claiming | Commissioner claims the premium; contractor cannot also claim on the same expenditure |
| FFG Certification | FFG (Forschungsförderungsgesellschaft) certification may be required for contract R&D |

### 2.4 FFG Certification Process

**Legal Basis:** EStG §108c(8); FFG certification pursuant to Forschungsprämienverordnung.

| Step | Details |
|---|---|
| 1. Application | Submit R&D project description to FFG (Austrian Research Promotion Agency) |
| 2. Assessment | FFG evaluates whether activities constitute qualifying R&D per Frascati Manual criteria |
| 3. Certificate (Gutachten) | FFG issues positive or negative assessment (Gutachten) |
| 4. Finanzamt Claim | Submit FFG certificate with annual tax return; Finanzamt applies premium |
| 5. Processing | Premium applied against assessed tax; excess refunded |

| FFG Processing Details | Information |
|---|---|
| Application Method | Electronic application via FFG portal (eCall) |
| Processing Time | Typically 6–12 weeks |
| Fee | No application fee |
| Validity | Covers the assessment period specified in the application |
| In-House R&D | FFG certification required for in-house R&D claims |
| Contract R&D | FFG certification also advisable for contract R&D claims |
| Appeals | Objection to FFG assessment possible; ultimately Finanzamt/BFG determines tax treatment |

### 2.5 Interaction with Direct R&D Grants

| Parameter | Details |
|---|---|
| Grant Funding | R&D expenditure funded by government grants (FFG, EU Horizon, etc.) must be excluded from the Forschungsprämie base |
| Net Expenditure Principle | Only the taxpayer's own net R&D expenditure qualifies |
| Partial Grants | If only part of a project is grant-funded, the non-funded portion qualifies |
| Documentation | Must clearly separate grant-funded and self-funded R&D expenditure |

## 3. Patent Box / IP Regime

**Austria does not have a patent box or intellectual property box regime.**

| Aspect | Status |
|---|---|
| Patent Box | Not available |
| IP Box | Not available |
| License Box | Not available |
| Historical Discussions | Discussed at EU level; Austria has not adopted |
| R&D Cost Deductibility | R&D expenditure fully deductible as business expenses under general tax principles |
| IP Transfer Pricing | Arm's length principle applies to IP licensing; Austrian transfer pricing guidelines follow OECD |
| IP Holding | No special IP holding regime; standard CIT rate of 23% applies to IP income |

> **Note:** Austria compensates for the lack of a patent box through its generous, uncapped, fully refundable Forschungsprämie, which provides significant front-end R&D benefits.

## 4. Effective Benefit Analysis (per $1M USD R&D Spend)

*Assumes $1M USD ≈ €920,000 (illustrative rate); CIT rate 23% (reduced from 25% in 2024).*

| Scenario | Calculation | Net Benefit |
|---|---|---|
| **Forschungsprämie (in-house R&D)** | 14% × €920,000 = €128,800 (tax-free, fully refundable) | **€128,800** |
| **Forschungsprämie (contract R&D, within €1M cap)** | 14% × €920,000 = €128,800 | **€128,800** |
| **Forschungsprämie (contract R&D, €1.5M spend)** | 14% × €1,000,000 (cap) = €140,000 | **€140,000** (capped) |
| **Full deductibility of R&D costs** | €920,000 × 23% CIT = €211,600 tax saving | €211,600 |
| **Combined: Premium + Deductibility** | €128,800 + €211,600 = €340,400 | **€340,400** |
| **Loss-making company** | €128,800 premium (refunded in cash) + loss carry-forward on R&D costs | **€128,800 cash + future deduction** |
| **Large R&D spender (€10M, in-house)** | 14% × €10,000,000 = €1,400,000 (no cap for in-house) | **€1,400,000** |

> **Key Advantage:** The absence of a cap on in-house R&D expenditure combined with full refundability makes Austria's Forschungsprämie exceptionally valuable for large R&D spenders, regardless of profitability.

## 5. Claim Process & Documentation

### 5.1 Annual Claim Process

| Step | Action | Deadline |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Conduct qualifying R&D activities during fiscal year | During fiscal year |
| 2 | Apply for FFG certification (Gutachten) | Typically filed with or before tax return |
| 3 | Calculate qualifying R&D expenditure | After fiscal year end |
| 4 | File annual tax return (ESt or KSt) with Forschungsprämie claim | Standard tax return deadline (corporate: 30 June following year; extended with Steuerberater to 30 April of second following year) |
| 5 | Attach FFG certificate and form E108c | With tax return |
| 6 | Finanzamt assessment | Premium applied against assessed tax; excess refunded |
| 7 | Cash refund received | Typically within 2–4 months of assessment |

### 5.2 Documentation Requirements

| Document | Purpose |
|---|---|
| FFG Gutachten (certificate) | Confirms qualifying R&D nature of activities |
| R&D project documentation | Technical descriptions: objectives, methods, novelty, uncertainties, results |
| Time tracking records | Employee hours allocated to qualifying R&D (contemporaneous records) |
| Cost allocation workpapers | Breakdown of R&D costs by category; overhead allocation methodology |
| Employment records | Contracts, payroll records for R&D personnel |
| Contract R&D agreements | Scope of work, contractor identification, R&D nature |
| Grant documentation | Evidence of any grant funding to exclude from Forschungsprämie base |
| Financial statements | Audited accounts supporting expenditure claims |
| Form E108c | Official claim form for Forschungsprämie |

### 5.3 Audit & Compliance

| Aspect | Details |
|---|---|
| Finanzamt Review | Tax office may review Forschungsprämie claims during general tax assessment or audit |
| FFG Role | FFG certification provides strong presumption of qualifying R&D; Finanzamt focuses on financial aspects |
| Betriebsprüfung | Standard tax audit may include Forschungsprämie review |
| Penalties | General tax penalty provisions (BAO §§ 111 et seq.) apply for incorrect claims |
| Retention Period | 7 years (standard Austrian tax document retention) |

## 6. Recent Changes & Sunset Provisions

| Change | Effective Date | Details |
|---|---|---|
| Premium rate increased to 14% | 2018 (assessment year) | Up from 12% (2016), 10% (2012), 8% (earlier) |
| CIT rate reduced to 23% | 1 January 2024 | Down from 25%; affects deductibility value but not premium rate |
| CIT rate was 24% transitionally | 2023 | Stepped reduction: 25% → 24% (2023) → 23% (2024) |
| FFG certification requirement formalized | Ongoing | Increasingly important for audit defense |
| Contract R&D cap at €1M | Stable | Has not been recently adjusted |
| No cap on in-house R&D | Ongoing | Distinguishing feature of Austrian system |
| Digital filing | Ongoing | Electronic filing via FinanzOnline |

**Sunset Provisions:**
- The **Forschungsprämie (EStG §108c)** is permanent legislation; no sunset clause
- Premium rate of 14% is established by law; changes require legislative amendment
- No announced plans to reduce or eliminate the premium
- Austria views the Forschungsprämie as a cornerstone of its innovation policy
- Contract R&D cap may be adjusted in future fiscal packages

**Anticipated Developments:**
- Potential increase in premium rate discussed in political circles
- Possible adjustment of contract R&D cap
- Enhanced digital reporting and FFG process improvements
- Pillar Two (15% global minimum tax) may interact with effective tax rate calculations
