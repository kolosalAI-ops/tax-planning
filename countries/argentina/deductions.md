# Argentina - Taxable Income Deductions

> **Jurisdiction**: Federal (AFIP — Administración Federal de Ingresos Públicos)
> **Governing Law**: Ley de Impuesto a las Ganancias (LIG), Ley No. 20.628 (consolidated text per Decreto 649/1997, as amended)
> **Tax Year**: 2024/2025
> **Currency**: ARS ($)

---

## 1. Depreciation & Capital Allowances

> **Legal Basis**: Ley 20.628, Artículos 87–89 (depreciation of fixed assets); Decreto Reglamentario 1.344/1998 (implementing regulations, useful life tables)

### Standard Depreciation Method

| Parameter | Value | Legal Reference |
|---|---|---|
| Method | **Straight-line** over estimated useful life | LIG Art. 87 |
| Basis | Historical cost, adjusted for inflation (see Section below) | LIG Art. 87; Arts. 94–98 (inflation adjustment) |
| Residual value | Not prescribed; generally depreciated to zero | |
| Conformity | Book/tax conformity not required | |

### Standard Useful Life and Rates

| Asset Category | Useful Life | Annual Rate | Legal Reference |
|---|---|---|---|
| Buildings and constructions | **50 years** | **2%** | LIG Art. 87; Decreto 1.344/1998 |
| Buildings (proven shorter life) | Per technical report | Per report | LIG Art. 87 (taxpayer may prove shorter life) |
| Machinery and equipment (general) | **10 years** | **10%** | Decreto 1.344/1998 |
| Furniture and fixtures | **10 years** | **10%** | Decreto 1.344/1998 |
| Vehicles (passenger) | **5 years** | **20%** | Decreto 1.344/1998 |
| Trucks and transport equipment | **5 years** | **20%** | Decreto 1.344/1998 |
| Computers and IT equipment | **3 years** | **33.3%** | Decreto 1.344/1998 |
| Software | **3 years** | **33.3%** | Decreto 1.344/1998 |
| Tools | **5 years** | **20%** | Decreto 1.344/1998 |
| Aircraft | **10–20 years** | **5–10%** | Decreto 1.344/1998 |
| Patents and trademarks | Over legal life or **5 years** minimum | Variable | LIG Art. 87 |

### Automobile Deduction Limits

| Parameter | Value | Legal Reference |
|---|---|---|
| Maximum depreciable cost | **ARS 20,000** (nominal, not inflation-adjusted — effectively negligible for new vehicles) | LIG Art. 92 [unverified — confirm with local counsel; this historic cap has not been updated legislatively and in practice the inflation adjustment may apply] |
| Related expense deduction cap | **ARS 7,200/year** per vehicle (gasoline, insurance, repairs, maintenance) | LIG Art. 92 |

> **Note**: The automobile deduction limits under Art. 92 have not been legislatively updated for inflation in many years. In practice, AFIP administrative guidance and the inflation adjustment mechanism may affect the effective thresholds. Confirm current applicable amounts with local counsel.

### Accelerated Depreciation — RIGI (Ley 27.742, effective July 8, 2024)

| Parameter | Value | Legal Reference |
|---|---|---|
| Regime | Régimen de Incentivo para Grandes Inversiones (RIGI) | Ley 27.742 (Ley de Bases), Título V |
| Minimum investment | **USD 200 million** (up to USD 900M for certain sectors) | Ley 27.742 |
| Movable property | Amortized in **2 equal annual instalments** | Ley 27.742 |
| Infrastructure / mines / quarries / forests | Amortized over **60% of estimated useful life** | Ley 27.742 |
| Stability period | **30 years** from registration date | Ley 27.742 |
| Income tax rate under RIGI | **25%** (reduced from general 35% rate) | Ley 27.742 |
| Application deadline | July 8, 2027 (extended by one year) [unverified — confirm decree number with local counsel] | Decreto 2025 [decree number unconfirmed] |

## 2. Business Operating Expenses

> **Legal Basis**: LIG Art. 83 (general deductibility — expenses necessary to obtain, maintain, and preserve taxable income)

### General Deductibility Rule — LIG Art. 83

| Parameter | Value |
|---|---|
| Standard | Expenses are deductible if they are **necessary to obtain, maintain, and preserve** taxable income |
| Documentation | Must be supported by invoices, receipts, or equivalent documentation |
| Accrual method | Expenses are generally deductible on the accrual basis |

### Common Deductible Expenses

| Expense Type | Deductibility | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Employee salaries and wages | **100%** | Including mandatory 13th-month salary (aguinaldo) |
| Employer social security contributions | **100%** | SUSS/SIPA employer contributions |
| Rent and lease payments | **100%** | Arm's length; related-party scrutiny applies |
| Utilities (electricity, gas, water, telecom) | **100%** | Business use |
| Professional services (legal, accounting, consulting) | **100%** | |
| Insurance premiums (business) | **100%** | |
| Advertising and marketing | **100%** | Must relate to income-producing activity |
| Maintenance and repairs | **100%** | Current-year repairs; improvements must be capitalised |
| Travel expenses | **100%** | Must be business-related with documentation |
| Training and education | **100%** | Employee-related |
| Bank fees and financial charges | **100%** | |
| Non-income taxes (provincial gross income tax, municipal charges) | **100%** | |

### Charitable Donations — LIG Art. 85

| Parameter | Value | Legal Reference |
|---|---|---|
| Maximum deduction | **5%** of net taxable income | LIG Art. 85, inciso c) |
| Eligible recipients | Registered non-profit entities (religious, educational, scientific, cultural organisations) | LIG Art. 85, inciso c) |
| Carry-forward of excess | Not allowed | |

### Meals & Entertainment

| Expense Type | Deductibility |
|---|---|
| Business meals | Deductible if necessary for business; must be properly documented |
| Entertainment | Deductible to the extent related to income-producing activity |
| Representational expenses | Deductible if reasonable and documented |

## 3. Interest & Financing Costs

> **Legal Basis**: LIG Art. 85, inciso a) (general interest deductibility); LIG Art. 81 quater (interest limitation — 30% of EBITDA); LIG Art. 91 (thin capitalization for non-resident related-party loans)

### General Interest Deduction — LIG Art. 85(a)

| Parameter | Value | Legal Reference |
|---|---|---|
| Basic rule | Interest on borrowed capital is deductible if related to taxable income | LIG Art. 85, inciso a) |
| Arm's length | Interest must not exceed arm's length rates | LIG Art. 17 (transfer pricing) |

### Interest Limitation (BEPS-Aligned) — LIG Art. 81 quater

| Parameter | Value | Legal Reference |
|---|---|---|
| Limitation | **30%** of tax-EBITDA (net interest and financing expenses) | LIG Art. 81 quater (introduced by Ley 27.430) |
| Carry-forward of denied interest | **5 years** | LIG Art. 81 quater |
| Carry-forward of unused capacity | **3 years** | LIG Art. 81 quater |
| Exemption — threshold | Entities with net interest expense ≤ threshold set by regulation | LIG Art. 81 quater |
| Exemption — banks | Financial institutions supervised by BCRA | LIG Art. 81 quater |

### Thin Capitalization — LIG Art. 91

| Parameter | Value | Legal Reference |
|---|---|---|
| Debt-to-equity ratio | **2:1** for related-party loans from **non-resident** lenders | LIG Art. 91 |
| Excess interest | Non-deductible; treated as a non-computable dividend for WHT purposes | LIG Art. 91 |
| Scope | Applies to interest, commissions, and other financial charges paid to non-resident related parties | LIG Art. 91 |

## 4. Research & Development

Argentina provides R&D incentives primarily through Ley 23.877 (Promotion of Technological Innovation) and the R&D tax credit established under Ley 27.431. Qualifying taxpayers may obtain a tax credit certificate of up to 80% of eligible R&D project expenditures, subject to an annual cap set by the enforcement authority (currently the Ministry of Science, Technology and Innovation).

See [R&D Incentives](rnd_incentives.md) for full program detail.

## 5. Loss Carry-Forward & Carry-Back

> **Legal Basis**: LIG Art. 19 (loss carry-forward — 5 years); LIG Arts. 94–98 (inflation adjustment of losses)

### Net Operating Losses

| Parameter | Value | Legal Reference |
|---|---|---|
| Carry-forward period | **5 years** | LIG Art. 19 |
| Carry-back | **Not allowed** | LIG Art. 19 (no carry-back provision) |
| Annual limitation | **100%** of taxable income (no percentage cap) | LIG Art. 19 |
| Inflation adjustment | Losses are adjusted for inflation using CPI indices | LIG Arts. 94–98 |
| Loss classification | Losses from Argentine-source income and foreign-source income tracked separately | LIG Art. 19 |
| Foreign-source losses | Can only offset foreign-source income | LIG Art. 19 |
| Merger/acquisition | Losses generally **not** transferable to surviving entity | LIG Art. 19 |

### RIGI Special Rule

| Parameter | Value |
|---|---|
| Under RIGI regime | Losses may be carried forward **without time limit** |
| Transfer to third parties | Permitted from the 5th year of the loss | Ley 27.742 |

## 7. Industry-Specific Deductions

### Hydrocarbons (Oil & Gas) — Ley 17.319

| Parameter | Value |
|---|---|
| Exploration expenses | Deductible as incurred or capitalised and amortised |
| Development costs | Amortised based on units of production or straight-line |
| Royalties | Generally **12%** of wellhead value; deductible as business expense |
| Windfall tax | Subject to export duties (retenciones) — not deductible from income tax |

### Agriculture

| Parameter | Value |
|---|---|
| Livestock (breeding stock) | Depreciated over estimated useful life |
| Annual crops | Costs deductible in the harvest year |
| Perennial crops (vineyards, orchards) | Capitalised and depreciated over productive life |

### Mining — Ley 24.196 (Mining Investment Law)

| Parameter | Value |
|---|---|
| Exploration expenditures | **100%** deductible in year incurred | Ley 24.196, Art. 12 |
| Feasibility studies | Deductible in year incurred |
| Capital investments | Accelerated depreciation available |
| Royalties | Provincial royalties capped at **3%** of mine-mouth value |
| Fiscal stability | **30-year** fiscal stability for qualifying projects |

## 8. Treaties & Transfer Pricing

### Transfer Pricing — LIG Art. 17; Ley 27.430

| Parameter | Value | Legal Reference |
|---|---|---|
| Standard | Arm's length principle | LIG Art. 17 |
| Methods | CUP, RPM, CPM, TNMM, PSM | LIG Art. 17; Decreto Reglamentario |
| Documentation | Annual transfer pricing study (Form F.2668) required | RG AFIP 4717/2020 |
| Country-by-Country reporting | Required for MNE groups with consolidated revenue ≥ ARS equivalent of €750M | RG AFIP 4130/2017 |
| Master File / Local File | Required for related-party transactions exceeding ARS threshold | RG AFIP 4717/2020 |
| Penalties | Fines for late filing, omission, or misrepresentation | Ley 11.683, Arts. 38–39 |

### Treaty Network

Argentina has approximately 20 bilateral tax treaties in force (including with Germany, France, Spain, UK, Brazil, Chile). Treaties follow a mix of OECD and UN model conventions.
