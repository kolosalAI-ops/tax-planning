# Argentina - R&D Tax Incentives

## 1. Overview

| Parameter | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Headline Incentive** | Knowledge Economy Regime (*Régimen de Economía del Conocimiento*) |
| **Mechanism Type** | CIT reduction, fiscal credit on employer social contributions, tax stability |
| **Headline Rate** | 60% CIT reduction; 70%–80% fiscal credit on employer contributions |
| **Refundable** | Partially — fiscal credit on social contributions is a transferable/applicable credit |
| **Patent Box** | No |
| **Legal Basis** | Law 27,506 (Knowledge Economy Law, 2019); Decree 1,034/2020; Law 25,922 (Software Promotion Law, 2004); Law 26,692 (extension, 2011) |

## 2. Detailed Programs

### 2.1 Knowledge Economy Regime (Law 27,506)

| Parameter | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Legal Basis** | Law 27,506 (published B.O. 10/06/2019); amended by Law 27,570 (2020); regulated by Decree 1,034/2020 and Resolution 4/2021 (SEPyME) |
| **Duration** | 10-year regime (through 2029) |
| **CIT Reduction** | 60% reduction in corporate income tax rate (from 35% to 14% effective rate for micro/small enterprises; graduated reduction for larger companies) |
| **Fiscal Credit on Social Contributions** | Credit applicable against federal taxes (excluding income tax) on employer social security contributions: **70%** for software and IT services; **80%** for other knowledge economy activities |
| **Tax Stability** | Fiscal stability guaranteed for the duration of the regime (10 years); no increase in overall national tax burden |
| **Withholding Tax Exemption** | Reduced/exempted from withholding and collection regimes for qualifying entities |
| **Eligible Activities** | Software development and IT services, audiovisual production, biotechnology, nanotechnology, satellite and aerospace industries, nuclear engineering, industrial automation, AI and robotics |
| **R&D Investment Requirement** | Minimum 3% of total revenue must be invested in R&D activities |
| **Quality Requirement** | Must hold quality certification (e.g., ISO, CMMI) or equivalent |
| **Employment Requirement** | Must maintain or increase payroll; compliance with labor regulations |
| **Export Reporting** | Entities with export revenue must comply with foreign exchange regulations |

### 2.2 Software Promotion Law (Law 25,922 — Predecessor Regime)

| Parameter | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Legal Basis** | Law 25,922 (2004); extended by Law 26,692 (2011); effectively superseded by Law 27,506 (2019) |
| **Status** | Transitional — entities registered under Law 25,922 were migrated to Knowledge Economy Regime |
| **Historical Benefits** | 60% CIT reduction; fiscal credit equal to 70% of employer social contributions; tax stability for 10 years |
| **Qualifying Activities** | Software development, IT services, software production |
| **R&D Requirement** | Minimum 3% of revenue invested in R&D or training |
| **Transition** | Law 27,506 Art. 19 provided transition mechanism for Law 25,922 beneficiaries |

### 2.3 R&D Specific Provisions

| Parameter | Detail |
|---|---|
| **R&D Investment Threshold** | Minimum 3% of total billing (revenue) must be allocated to R&D |
| **Qualifying R&D Activities** | Basic research, applied research, experimental development, technology transfer, innovation processes |
| **R&D Documentation** | Must maintain detailed records of R&D expenditures, personnel, and project outcomes |
| **Enforcement Authority** | Subsecretaría de Economía del Conocimiento (Ministry of Economy) |
| **Audit and Compliance** | Annual compliance audit; beneficiaries must demonstrate ongoing adherence to all requirements |

### 2.4 Provincial Incentives

| Parameter | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Buenos Aires Province** | Software industry exemptions from provincial gross income tax (*Ingresos Brutos*) |
| **City of Buenos Aires (CABA)** | Technology district incentives in Parque Patricios (gross income tax exemptions, property tax benefits) |
| **Córdoba** | Knowledge economy hub incentives; provincial tax reductions for IT companies |
| **Mendoza** | IT industry promotion regime with provincial tax benefits |
| **Note** | Provincial incentives vary and are subject to local legislation; can be stacked with national regime |

## 3. Patent Box / IP Regime

**Not applicable.** Argentina does not have a patent box or IP box regime. Income derived from intellectual property (patents, software copyright, etc.) is taxed at the standard corporate income tax rate. The Knowledge Economy Regime provides general R&D incentives but does not specifically provide preferential rates on IP-derived income.

| Parameter | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Status** | No patent box regime |
| **Standard CIT Rate** | 35% (Law 20,628, as amended) |
| **Withholding on Royalties** | 21%–31.5% effective rate on royalty payments to non-residents (depending on deductibility presumption) |
| **Treaty Network** | Limited (approximately 20 tax treaties) |

## 4. Effective Benefit Analysis

**Assumptions:** ARS equivalent of US$1,000,000 R&D spend, company registered under Knowledge Economy Regime (Law 27,506), small/medium enterprise, 35% standard CIT rate.

| Component | Calculation | Tax Benefit |
|---|---|---|
| **CIT Reduction (60%)** | 35% × 60% reduction = 14% effective CIT rate; savings = 21% of taxable income | **21% of taxable income** |
| **Fiscal Credit on Social Contributions (70% — software)** | Assume employer contributions ~23% of payroll; 70% credit = 16.1% of payroll as fiscal credit | **16.1% of R&D payroll** |
| **Fiscal Credit on Social Contributions (80% — other)** | 23% × 80% = 18.4% of payroll as fiscal credit | **18.4% of R&D payroll** |
| **Tax Stability** | Protection against tax increases for 10 years | **Qualitative benefit** |

**Illustrative Calculation per US$1,000,000 R&D Spend (assuming 70% is payroll):**

| Component | Calculation | USD Benefit |
|---|---|---|
| **CIT Reduction** | US$1,000,000 profit × 21% savings | **US$210,000** |
| **Social Contribution Credit (software, 70%)** | US$700,000 × 23% × 70% | **US$112,700** |
| **Total Annual Benefit** | | **~US$322,700** |
| **Effective Benefit Rate** | | **~32.3%** |

> **Note:** The actual benefit varies significantly based on the company's profitability, payroll structure, and the applicable exchange rate. Argentina's macroeconomic volatility and currency controls can substantially impact the real value of these incentives.

## 5. Claim Process & Documentation

### Filing Process

| Step | Detail |
|---|---|
| **1. Registry Application** | Apply for registration in the National Registry of Knowledge Economy Beneficiaries (*Registro Nacional de Beneficiarios del Régimen de Promoción de la Economía del Conocimiento*) |
| **2. Compliance Verification** | Demonstrate compliance with all requirements: R&D investment (3%), quality certification, employment conditions |
| **3. Obtain Certificate** | Receive beneficiary certificate from the Subsecretaría de Economía del Conocimiento |
| **4. Apply CIT Reduction** | Reflect the 60% CIT reduction in annual income tax returns |
| **5. Claim Social Contribution Credit** | Apply fiscal credit against federal tax obligations (e.g., VAT) through AFIP (Federal Tax Authority) |
| **6. Annual Compliance Report** | Submit annual affidavit (*declaración jurada*) demonstrating continued compliance |
| **7. Periodic Audits** | Subject to compliance audits by the enforcement authority |

### Key Deadlines

| Item | Deadline |
|---|---|
| **Registration Application** | Open enrollment (subject to administrative processing times) |
| **Annual Income Tax Return** | 5th month after fiscal year-end (generally May for calendar-year taxpayers) |
| **Annual Compliance Affidavit** | As specified by the Subsecretaría (typically within 4 months of fiscal year-end) |
| **Pre-Approval** | **Mandatory** — must be registered as a beneficiary before claiming benefits |
| **Quality Certification** | Must be obtained or maintained throughout the benefit period |

### Documentation Requirements

- Company registration documents and tax identification (CUIT)
- R&D investment records demonstrating 3% of revenue threshold
- Quality certification (ISO 9001, CMMI, or equivalent)
- Payroll records and employment contracts for R&D personnel
- Detailed project descriptions and R&D expenditure reports
- Annual compliance affidavits
- Financial statements audited by certified public accountant
- Evidence of qualifying knowledge economy activities

## 6. Recent Changes & Sunset Provisions

| Change | Effective Date | Detail |
|---|---|---|
| **Knowledge Economy Law Enacted** | June 2019 | Law 27,506 replaced and expanded the Software Promotion Law (Law 25,922) |
| **Law 27,570 Amendments** | October 2020 | Modified benefits structure, adjusted CIT reduction schedule, reinforced employment requirements |
| **Decree 1,034/2020** | December 2020 | Regulatory decree implementing Law 27,506 as amended |
| **Resolution 4/2021** | 2021 | Operational rules for registration and compliance |
| **Regime Expiration** | 2029 | Knowledge Economy Regime is scheduled to expire; potential renewal subject to future legislation |
| **CIT Rate Changes** | 2021 | Law 27,630 established graduated CIT rates (25%–35% based on income level); interacts with 60% reduction |
| **Currency Controls Impact** | Ongoing | BCRA foreign exchange regulations affect repatriation of benefits for foreign-owned entities |
| **AFIP → ARCA Reorganization** | 2024 | Federal tax authority reorganization may affect administrative procedures |
