# Netherlands - Taxable Income Deductions

> **Jurisdiction**: National (Belastingdienst)
> **Governing Law**: Wet op de Vennootschapsbelasting 1969 (Vpb); Wet inkomstenbelasting 2001 (Wet IB 2001)
> **Tax Year**: 2024/2025
> **Currency**: EUR (€)

---

## 1. Depreciation & Capital Allowances

### General Principles — Art. 8 Vpb / Art. 3.30 Wet IB 2001

Tax depreciation follows the principle of "goed koopmansgebruik" (sound business practice). Both straight-line and declining balance methods are accepted.

### Key Depreciation Rates

| Asset Category | Typical Rate | Method | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| Buildings (business use) | **Varies** — limited by WOZ floor | Straight-line or declining balance | Art. 3.30a Wet IB 2001 |
| Machinery and equipment | **20%** | Declining balance | |
| Office equipment and furniture | **20%** | Declining balance | |
| Vehicles | **20%** | Declining balance | |
| Computer equipment | **20–33%** | Declining balance | |
| Goodwill | Max **10%** per year (min 10-year life) | Straight-line | Art. 3.30 Wet IB 2001 |
| Other intangibles | Over useful life | Straight-line | |

### Building Depreciation Floor — Art. 3.30a Wet IB 2001

| Building Type | Minimum Tax Book Value |
|---|---|
| Buildings used in own business | **50%** of WOZ value (Waardering Onroerende Zaken — official valuation) |
| Investment properties (not own use) | **100%** of WOZ value (no depreciation below WOZ) |

### Arbitrary Depreciation (Willekeurige Afschrijving)

| Parameter | Value |
|---|---|
| Standard | Not generally available |
| Environmental investments (VAMIL) | Qualifying environmental assets may be depreciated freely (up to 75% arbitrary in year of choice) — Art. 3.31 Wet IB 2001 |
| Start-up companies | Arbitrary depreciation on qualifying investments up to €300,000 during first 3 years [unverified — confirm with local counsel] |

### Small-Scale Investment Deduction (Kleinschaligheidsinvesteringsaftrek — KIA) — Art. 3.41 Wet IB 2001

| Investment Amount | Deduction |
|---|---|
| €2,801–€69,764 | **28%** of investment |
| €69,764–€110,998 | Fixed €19,534 |
| €110,998–€332,994 | Declining (€19,534 minus 7.56% of amount above €110,998) |
| Above €332,994 | €0 |

*Note: Thresholds are 2024 values [unverified — confirm with local counsel].*

### Energy Investment Allowance (EIA) — Art. 3.42 Wet IB 2001

| Parameter | Value |
|---|---|
| Deduction | **45.5%** of qualifying energy investment (2024) |
| Maximum | €136M per taxpayer |
| Eligible | Investments on the official Energy List (Energielijst) |

### Environmental Investment Allowance (MIA) — Art. 3.42a Wet IB 2001

| Category | Deduction Rate (2024) |
|---|---|
| Category I | **45%** |
| Category II | **36%** |
| Category III | **27%** |

## 2. Business Operating Expenses

### General Deductibility

Business expenses are deductible if incurred for the purpose of the enterprise, applying the "goed koopmansgebruik" standard.

### Key Deductible Operating Expenses

| Expense Category | Deductibility | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Employee salaries and wages | Fully deductible | Arm's-length for related parties |
| Employer social security contributions | Fully deductible | |
| Rent and lease payments | Fully deductible | |
| Professional fees | Fully deductible | |
| Repairs and maintenance | Deductible if revenue nature | |
| Bad debts | Deductible when specifically provided for or written off | |
| Insurance premiums | Fully deductible | |
| Training expenses | Fully deductible | |
| Start-up expenses | Deductible in year incurred | |

### Charitable Donations — Art. 16 Vpb

| Parameter | Value |
|---|---|
| Deductible | Donations to qualifying public benefit organisations (ANBI) and social interest organisations (SBBI) |
| Cap | Maximum **50%** of taxable profit; minimum €227 |
| Periodic gifts | Fully deductible (no percentage cap) if documented in a notarial deed or via official form |

## 3. Interest & Financing Costs

### ATAD1 Interest Limitation — Art. 15b Vpb

| Parameter | Value |
|---|---|
| Deductible limit | Net interest expense up to **20%** of tax-EBITDA (FY 2024); increased to **24.5%** from 1 January 2025 — Art. 15b Vpb |
| De minimis | **€1,000,000** net interest expense |
| Group ratio rule | Alternative: group net interest/EBITDA ratio if higher |
| Carry-forward | Disallowed interest carried forward **indefinitely** |
| EBITDA carry-forward | Unused EBITDA capacity carried forward for up to **5 years** — Art. 15b Vpb (ATAD standard, consistent with peer EU jurisdictions) |

### Anti-Base Erosion Rules — Art. 10a Vpb

| Parameter | Value |
|---|---|
| Target | Interest on loans from related parties for (i) internal acquisitions, (ii) capital contributions, or (iii) profit distributions |
| Effect | Interest **non-deductible** unless taxpayer demonstrates commercial substance (dubbele zakelijkheidstoets — double business motive test) |

### Hybrid Mismatch Rules (ATAD2) — Art. 12aa–12ag Vpb

| Parameter | Value |
|---|---|
| Scope | Denies deduction for payments resulting in double deduction or deduction/no-inclusion mismatches |
| Effective | From 1 January 2020 (extended to reverse hybrids from 1 January 2022) |

### Withholding Tax on Interest/Royalties (Conditional WHT)

| Parameter | Value |
|---|---|
| Rate | **25.8%** (2024) on interest and royalty payments to low-tax jurisdictions and abusive structures |
| Effective from | 1 January 2021 (interest and royalties) |

## 4. Research & Development

The Netherlands operates the Innovation Box (Innovatiebox — Art. 12b Vpb), providing an effective **9%** tax rate on qualifying IP income. Additionally, the WBSO (Wet Bevordering Speur- en Ontwikkelingswerk) provides a wage tax credit for R&D personnel costs, effectively reducing the cost of R&D employees.

See [R&D Incentives](rnd_incentives.md) for full program detail.

## 5. Loss Carry-Forward & Carry-Back

### Loss Carry-Forward — Art. 20 Vpb

| Parameter | Value |
|---|---|
| Duration | **Indefinite** (from fiscal years starting on or after 1 January 2022) |
| Restriction | Losses offset freely up to **€1,000,000**; above that, only **50%** of remaining taxable profit |

### Loss Carry-Back — Art. 20 Abs. 2 Vpb

| Parameter | Value |
|---|---|
| Duration | **1 year** |
| Restriction | Same €1M + 50% cap applies |

### Change of Ownership — Art. 20a Vpb

| Parameter | Value |
|---|---|
| >30% ownership change | Losses forfeited if activities/assets change by >70% within a 3-year window |
| Holding company rule | Stricter test for companies whose assets consist ≥50% of low-taxed participations |

## 6. Provisions & Reserves

### Tax-Deductible Provisions

| Provision Type | Deductibility | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Specific bad debt provisions | Deductible | Based on individual assessment of receivables |
| General bad debt provisions | Deductible if based on historical default rates | Unlike many countries, Netherlands allows statistical provisions |
| Warranty provisions | Deductible | Based on historical data |
| Litigation provisions | Deductible | If probable and estimable |
| Environmental provisions | Deductible | If legal obligation exists |
| Restructuring provisions | Deductible if detailed plan approved and announced | |
| Employee-related provisions (holiday, bonus) | Deductible | When obligation arises |
| Pension provisions | Generally not on balance sheet (externalised via pension funds) | |

### Equalisation Reserve (Egalisatiereserve)

| Parameter | Value |
|---|---|
| Purpose | Smoothing cyclical expenditure (e.g., major maintenance) |
| Deductibility | Deductible additions provided prudent commercial practice followed |

### Reinvestment Reserve (Herinvesteringsreserve — HIR) — Art. 3.54 Wet IB 2001

| Parameter | Value |
|---|---|
| Purpose | Deferral of capital gains on disposal of business assets |
| Condition | Intention to reinvest in replacement asset |
| Period | Must reinvest within **3 years** (extendable) |
| Effect | Gain reduces cost base of replacement asset |

## 8. Treaties & Transfer Pricing

### Transfer Pricing — Art. 8b Vpb

| Parameter | Detail |
|---|---|
| Standard | Arm's-length principle — Art. 8b Vpb |
| Documentation | Master file and local file required — Decree of 14 November 2022 |
| Thresholds | Revenue ≥ €50M (master file and local file) |
| Country-by-Country Reporting | Required for MNE groups with consolidated revenue ≥ €750M |
| Penalties | Up to 100% of additional tax for intentional non-compliance; documentation penalties apply |
| APAs | Bilateral and unilateral available through Belastingdienst |

### Participation Exemption (Deelnemingsvrijstelling) — Art. 13 Vpb

| Parameter | Value |
|---|---|
| Exemption | **100%** of dividends and capital gains from qualifying participations |
| Minimum holding | **5%** of nominal paid-up capital |
| Conditions | Not a "portfolio investment" or a low-taxed passive holding |
| Liquidation losses | Deductible under specific conditions — Art. 13d Vpb (restricted from 2021) |

### Treaty Network

The Netherlands has over **95** double taxation agreements.

| Income Type | Typical Treaty Rate |
|---|---|
| Dividends (substantial holding) | 0–5% |
| Dividends (portfolio) | 10–15% |
| Interest | 0% (most treaties) |
| Royalties | 0% (most treaties) |

## 9. Non-Deductible Items

| Expense | Statutory Reference |
|---|---|
| Corporate income tax (Vpb) | Not deductible |
| Dividend tax (dividendbelasting) borne by company | Not deductible (creditable) |
| Fines and penalties (criminal, tax, administrative) | Art. 10 Abs. 1d Vpb |
| Bribes | Non-deductible — Art. 10 Abs. 1e Vpb |
| Interest on hybrid instruments (treated as equity) | Art. 10 Abs. 1d Vpb |
| Hidden profit distributions | Non-deductible — established by case law |
| Excessive remuneration to related parties | Non-deductible to the extent above arm's-length |
| Interest subject to Art. 10a anti-abuse | Non-deductible |
| Costs relating to exempt participation income | Non-deductible (but 100% exemption means no add-back system like France's 5% quote-part) |
| Capital expenditure | Must be depreciated |
| 0.4% of taxable wages — non-deductible portion of mixed costs | Art. 3.15/3.16 Wet IB 2001 (food/drink, business gifts) |
| Sponsoring with personal benefit element | Non-deductible to the extent of personal benefit |
